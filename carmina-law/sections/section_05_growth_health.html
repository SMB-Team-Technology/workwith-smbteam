Pillar Health Scorecard

Lead Generation

  • Google Maps organic and referrals are the only lead sources — no paid advertising of any kind has ever been run
  • Website carminalaw.com does not appear to be indexed by Google — the site contributes no organic traffic
  • GBP primary category is set to “General Practice Attorney” instead of “Immigration Attorney,” actively suppressing Map Pack visibility for the highest-value practice area
Without a predictable lead system, every week you are away is a week where the pipeline thins out — and that is the opposite of the freedom you need.

Intake

  • No dedicated receptionist — all staff pick up calls “as able,” with no structure or accountability
  • No call tracking, no CRM, no after-hours answering — leads that come in after hours go nowhere
  • No defined follow-up process — leads who do not sign immediately are rarely re-contacted
Every missed call during a closing emergency, every voicemail that never gets returned, is revenue the firm already paid to generate — and lost anyway.

Self-Managing Team

  • Some support staff exist but operate without KPIs, defined roles, or accountability structures
  • Owner is still the default for intake, operations, and case emergencies — no manager layer exists
  • Independent contractors (paralegal) operate without communication standards, creating fire drills that pull the owner back in at the worst moments
A team that requires your oversight for every decision is not a team — it is a second full-time job that travels with you everywhere you go.

Profit

  • Monthly financial reporting has improved but bookkeeping is split between two systems with a transition in progress
  • Estate planning cases at $950–$1,000 with heavy attorney education time are consuming capacity that immigration cases worth $3,000–$7,000 could fill
  • No practice-area-level profitability tracking — unclear which work pays and which drains time without proportional return
Growing revenue without knowing which cases actually make money is how you end up working harder for the same take-home — which is not what freedom looks like.
