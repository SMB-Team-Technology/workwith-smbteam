Intake Assessment

The Law Offices of Carmina Fernandes has no dedicated intake process — calls are answered by whoever is available, there is no after-hours coverage, and no tracking exists to know how many leads come in or what happens to them. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
“No dedicated receptionist; everyone answers phones as able.”
The Problem
  • No dedicated intake or receptionist role — all staff answer phones “as able,” with no ownership, no script, and no accountability for follow-up
  • No call tracking (no CallRail or equivalent) — the firm cannot confirm how many inbound calls it receives, where they come from, or how many are missed
  • No after-hours answering coverage — calls that come in evenings and weekends go to voicemail, and there is no defined process for returning them promptly
  • No lead management system — without a CRM or intake tracking tool, prospects who do not sign immediately fall out of the pipeline with no follow-up
Blind Spot: The firm’s busiest inbound lead times may be evenings and weekends — when immigrant families have time to make calls they cannot make during work hours. Without 24/7 bilingual answering, those calls are going to voicemail and, in many cases, to a competitor who picks up.
The Positive
  • Carmina has already identified intake as a core problem and is open to a phased solution starting with a bilingual answering service — the awareness and willingness to fix this are in place
The Gap
  • Every missed call during a closing emergency, a travel day, or a busy afternoon is a $3,000–$7,000 case that called once and never called back — the revenue cost of weak intake is invisible until it is measured
  • Without a scheduling link and 24/7 bilingual answering, the firm cannot capture the after-hours demand from Spanish and Portuguese-speaking immigrant families who work during business hours
  • Without call tracking, launching Google Ads would be like spending money in the dark — no ability to see which campaigns generate calls, which convert, and which do not
A firm with a bilingual answering service, a scheduling link, and a call tracking system keeps working at 11 PM on a Tuesday — so you do not have to be awake for it.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact form placement and functionality could not be verified due to the site’s crawl restrictions — whether a form exists and where it appears on the page is unknown
  • No scheduling link or online booking tool confirmed — prospects who visit the website after hours have no self-service path to request a consultation
  • Adding a scheduling link (Calendly or equivalent) to the website and GBP listing would allow prospects to book directly from any channel without requiring a phone call

These findings are also referenced in the Website Audit section.

Team Assessment

Carmina has some staff in place, but every key process — intake, case management, vendor coordination — runs through her personally, with no one empowered or trained to handle problems without her involvement. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
“Unreliable contractors and internal bandwidth gaps causing missed closings, fires, and owner distraction.”
The Problem
  • No dedicated intake or office manager — Carmina is the default point of escalation for every operational problem, including mid-closing paralegal failures during client calls
  • No KPI framework for any role — staff compensation and accountability are not tied to performance metrics, so there is no mechanism for the team to self-manage
  • No structured hiring process — no vivid vision, no defined job scorecards, no interview framework — meaning the next hire will be as unpredictable as the last
  • Independent contractors operate without communication standards or deliverable deadlines, creating recurring emergencies that interrupt casework and personal time
Blind Spot: The real cost of owner-dependency is not measured in hours — it is measured in decisions deferred, opportunities missed, and the inability to ever fully disconnect. A team that needs you to supervise every outcome is not a safety net; it is a ceiling.
The Gap
  • Without a trusted intake person who owns the front of the pipeline, every lead that comes in when Carmina is traveling is at risk — the team cannot convert what it cannot catch
  • Without KPIs tied to compensation, there is no self-correcting mechanism — problems surface only when they become emergencies, and emergencies require Carmina to fix them
  • Without a documented hiring process, filling the next critical role will take longer and produce a less qualified result than the firm needs to hit its revenue goals
Building a team that holds the firm together while you are away is not a luxury — given your situation, it is the single most important thing this firm can do right now.

Profit Plan Assessment

The firm is generating $325,000–$350,000 in annual revenue with monthly reporting that has improved recently, but without clear visibility into which practice areas are profitable and which are consuming time at below-market rates. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Estate planning cases priced at $950–$1,000 with heavy attorney education time are consuming capacity that could be generating $3,000–$7,000 immigration cases — the practice mix is not optimized for revenue per hour
  • Bookkeeping is split between two systems (QuickBooks Desktop with husband, QuickBooks Online with external firm) during an active transition — financial reporting is improving but still inconsistent
  • Invoicing remains weak — no automated or systematic billing process means revenue recognition lags behind work completed
  • No practice-area-level profitability analysis — the firm does not track cost per case type, time per case, or revenue per attorney hour by practice area
Blind Spot: Estate planning at $950 per plan with significant client education time may be the firm’s least profitable practice area per hour — but it feels productive because the volume is there. Until the numbers are tracked by case type, the firm may be unknowingly subsidizing low-margin work with the margins from immigration.
The Positive
  • Monthly financial reporting has recently improved — Carmina has made meaningful progress toward better financial visibility
  • Carmina has clearly identified the profitability gap herself and is actively seeking a bilingual CPA/bookkeeper to consolidate and improve her financial management
The Gap
  • Without practice-area-level profitability data, every decision about which cases to take and how to price them is based on instinct rather than margin — and instinct is not scalable
  • Growing from $350K to $1M requires knowing exactly which revenue streams to expand, which to deprioritize, and what each hire costs versus what it generates
  • The transition to a unified bookkeeping system with a bilingual CPA is the right move — until it is complete, financial decisions are being made with incomplete data
Knowing exactly what you make, what you keep, and which cases make you the most per hour is what lets you choose your work instead of taking whatever comes in — and that is what real freedom looks like.
