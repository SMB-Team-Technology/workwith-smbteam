Here Is Where RLB Legal Stands Today

Reagan Butts founded RLB Legal a little over a year ago after nearly a decade as General Counsel for a private oil & gas company, building a commercial litigation and transactional practice (60/40 split, with a distinct oil & gas emphasis) that hit $500K in its first year almost entirely on word-of-mouth. That same referral engine that got the firm off the ground is now its biggest constraint: with 95%+ of clients coming from personal networking and zero paid marketing ever run, new matters arrive in an unpredictable 0-to-6-per-month feast-or-famine pattern that makes it impossible for Reagan to hire proactively instead of reactively.

Stage 6 is where a close-knit, 3-5 attorney boutique firm runs on predictable systems instead of Reagan's personal network — exactly the firm he described wanting to build over the next three years.

Where You Are Today

STAGE 3: SOLO PRACTITIONER
  • Nearly all new matters come from word-of-mouth referrals and personal networking — new business ranges from 0 to 6 matters a month with no way to predict which
  • Reagan personally handles case work, client intake, and business development, with no scalable system standing behind him
  • The firm has never run a single paid ad on any platform — referrals are the only lever for growth today
  • Hiring has been reactive, driven by workload spikes out of necessity, rather than a deliberate step toward the 3-5 attorney firm targeted within three years

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
Closing that gap does not require Reagan to work harder than the 50-60 hours he already puts in each week — it requires a predictable acquisition system to finally stand alongside the referral network he has already built.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
RLB Legal
RLB Legal is operating at Stage 3 — a Solo Practitioner firm whose founder built a genuinely impressive $500K first year entirely on reputation and referrals, with no marketing system behind it. The single biggest thing holding Reagan back is the complete absence of a predictable, paid client acquisition channel: every hiring decision remains reactive because there is no way to know if next month brings zero new matters or six. Until that changes, the $2-3M, 3-5 attorney boutique firm Reagan is building toward will keep depending on the same unpredictable pipeline that makes it so hard to plan for today.
