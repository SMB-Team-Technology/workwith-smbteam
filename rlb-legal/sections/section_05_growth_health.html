Pillar Health Scorecard

Lead Generation

  • 95%+ of new clients come from word-of-mouth referrals and personal networking — no paid marketing has ever been run on any platform
  • The only paid channel under consideration is a local agency's not-yet-signed $10K/mo proposal, targeting just 4-6 cold leads a month at a projected 10-15% close rate
  • No geo-targeted landing pages exist for Midland, Dallas, or Houston — the three markets outside Tyler where RLB Legal's actual and targeted clients are
Every month referrals stay the only lever is a month further from the proactive hiring decisions the 3-5 attorney firm you're building will require.

Intake

  • No scalable intake process exists yet — Reagan personally fields inbound interest alongside casework and business development
  • No embedded contact form appears on the homepage; the CTA instead routes to a scheduling flow, adding friction in a market where prospects can just as easily call an established competitor
  • No after-hours coverage was confirmed — a real risk for a firm built on protecting a "white glove" reputation
Fixing intake means converting more of the referrals already coming in the door — before a single new marketing dollar is spent.

Self-Managing Team

  • A team of six exists (1 W-2 attorney, 1 W-2 support staff, 2 contract attorneys, 2 contract paralegals), but Reagan still personally manages nearly every client relationship and business decision
  • Reagan is already working 50-60 hours a week, with hiring driven reactively by workload spikes rather than by a deliberate plan
  • No documented growth plan yet exists to guide the next round of hiring toward the targeted 3-5 attorney structure
Until hiring becomes proactive instead of reactive, Reagan cannot build the close-knit boutique firm he described — he can only keep reacting to it.

Profit

  • Revenue reached $500K in year one, but the "feast or famine" flow of 0-6 new matters a month makes financial planning and hiring decisions genuinely difficult
  • No cost-per-lead or cost-per-case tracking currently exists to evaluate whether the $10K/mo ad agency proposal under review would even pay off
  • No profitability figures were discussed on the call — revenue growth alone does not yet confirm whether growth is translating into predictable take-home profit
Without predictable numbers, Reagan cannot know whether the next hire pays for itself — turning growth into a guess instead of a plan.
