Pillar Health Scorecard

Lead Generation

  • 95–98% of cases come from referrals — no controlled marketing system exists
  • Near-zero Google reviews means the firm is invisible in local 3-pack searches for any PI keyword
  • $2–3K/month in FindLaw and social posting produces no measurable return
Without a predictable lead source, Adam's income will remain hostage to referral volatility — and the CEO role he is building toward will remain out of reach.

Intake

  • Adam must personally sign every new client — a Utah State Bar requirement that creates a hard cap on signed cases per month
  • No after-hours coverage — cases calling after 5pm or on weekends go uncaptured
  • No defined intake system or follow-up sequence for leads who do not sign immediately
Every hour Adam spends personally signing clients is an hour he cannot spend building the systems that would let him step back.

Self-Managing Team

  • Adam is now the sole owner, sole attorney, and sole decision-maker — every key function runs through him
  • Support staff handle ~60 active cases but cannot sign new clients or represent the firm independently
  • No second attorney, no management layer, and no documented processes to support a transition to CEO
A firm that cannot run without Adam at every step cannot give Adam the time and freedom he has described.

Profit

  • Revenue has been flat at ~$1.1M for five years — growth has stalled while costs continue
  • Average case fee has declined from ~$13K to ~$11K this year — a trend that erodes margin without growth
  • Marketing spend of $2–3K/month on directories produces no measurable return — invisible cost of stagnation
Growing revenue without growing profit is just growing the grind — Adam needs a profit plan alongside his growth plan to reach the financial security he described.
