Lead Generation

Larson Law has built a respected personal injury practice in South Jordan on nearly four decades of referrals — but with 95 to 98 percent of cases coming from referrals and no controlled marketing system in place, the firm's revenue has been flat for five consecutive years while competitors with full digital campaigns capture every case that begins with a Google search.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Website larsonlawutah.com was confirmed live through directory data, but the site's bot-protection configuration prevented direct access — homepage headline, CTA placement, and load performance could not be confirmed during this audit
  • PageSpeed scores could not be retrieved — the site blocked all PageSpeed analysis tools; a manual test at pagespeed.web.dev is strongly recommended before Friday's call to understand mobile load performance
  • No geo-targeting landing pages observed for surrounding cities (Draper, Riverton, Herriman, Sandy, Murray, West Jordan) — every PI search with a city modifier returns competitors instead of Larson Law
  • No Spanish-language pages observed — a significant missed opportunity in Salt Lake County's growing Hispanic market
Blind Spot: A site that blocks research tools may also be inadvertently blocking Google's crawlers — if the site's bot-protection affects Googlebot, it could be silently suppressing organic rankings without any visible error or warning in Search Console.
The Positive
  • The site is indexed and confirmed live — multiple pages (practice areas, team, contact, resources) appear in Google's search index
  • Attorney bio pages exist (larsonlawutah.com/team) — a credibility signal that many competing PI firms omit entirely
The Gap
  • Without confirmed PageSpeed scores, there is no way to know whether mobile visitors — the primary channel for PI searches — are bouncing before the site fully loads
  • Without geo-targeting pages, Larson Law is invisible for searches like "personal injury attorney Draper UT" and "car accident lawyer Riverton Utah" — searches that go to Parker & McConkie and Kramer Law Group every day
  • Without a confirmed above-fold CTA and contact form, every paid traffic dollar invested sends visitors to a site that may not capture them
A website that cannot be found, measured, or confirmed to convert is not an asset — it is a barrier between Adam and the CEO role and financial security he is building toward.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Google Business Profile review count appears near-zero — Wheree.com, a Google data aggregator, shows no reviews indexed for Larson Law, placing the firm at the bottom of local ranking signals for any PI keyword
  • Larson Law does not appear in the local 3-pack for "personal injury lawyer South Jordan Utah," "car accident attorney South Jordan Utah," or any broader Utah PI search observed during this audit
  • No geo-targeting landing pages for Draper, Riverton, Herriman, Sandy, Murray, or West Jordan — leaving all city-specific PI searches to better-positioned competitors
Blind Spot: The local 3-pack is effectively a review competition in high-value PI markets — and with near-zero Google reviews, Larson Law is starting that competition from last place while Kramer Law Group (300+ reviews) and Parker & McConkie (1,000+ reviews) are already at the finish line. There is no amount of ad spend that replaces this signal.
The Positive
  • NAP (name, address, phone) is consistent across FindLaw, Yelp, Lawyers.com, Martindale, and other directories — a clean citation foundation to build on once the review campaign launches
The Gap
  • Without Google reviews, the GBP cannot rank competitively — every PI case that starts with a Google search goes to a competitor regardless of how good the underlying website is
  • The Garner Law Firm, a direct South Jordan PI competitor, is newer to the market but already holds a 5.0-star Google Maps rating — without an aggressive review campaign, Larson Law risks being permanently outranked by a younger firm in their own city
Kramer Law Group — 300+ Google reviews at 4.9 stars, 8 miles away in West Jordan, actively capturing the same South Jordan metro PI market through local SEO dominance and paid search.
Building to 200+ Google reviews is not a vanity exercise — it is the single most important prerequisite for everything else: 3-pack rankings, LSA eligibility, and the online credibility that converts a first-time visitor into a signed case.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Larson Law does not appear to be running Google search ads for "personal injury South Jordan Utah," "car accident attorney South Jordan Utah," or "personal injury lawyer Utah" — the firm may not have campaigns configured for these terms, or may be getting outbid by the major regional firms that dominate paid results in this market
  • Current marketing spend of ~$2–3K/month is allocated to FindLaw directory listings and social posting — not paid search, which is where the highest-intent PI prospects are looking
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Siegfried & Jensen — almost certainly running paid search for all primary PI keywords in Salt Lake County; their "$1.2B+ recovered" messaging and massive review base give them a quality score advantage that raises CPCs for everyone in the market
  • Parker & McConkie — running paid ads across Utah PI keywords from four office locations; "40+ years of experience" and 1,000+ five-star reviews are their ad messaging anchors
  • Kramer Law Group — active PPC targeting West Jordan truck and car accident keywords, capturing the same South Jordan metro audience
The Opportunity
  • "Personal injury attorney South Jordan" and "car accident lawyer South Jordan" — high-intent, geographically specific terms with lower CPCs than broad Salt Lake City keywords; Larson Law's local address is a quality score advantage here
  • "Catastrophic injury attorney Utah" — a specific practice area term where Larson Law has documented experience and where competition is less saturated
  • Surrounding city terms — "personal injury attorney Draper," "car accident lawyer Herriman," "Riverton UT injury attorney" — represent untapped geographic reach at manageable CPCs
  • Spanish-language PI keywords in the Salt Lake County market — a largely underserved paid search segment where Larson Law could capture cases at lower cost per lead than English-language campaigns
Blind Spot: Adam's own stated CAC target of ~$2,500 per signed case means that at $12,500/month in ad spend, the firm adds 5 signed cases — $55,000 in expected revenue from a $12,500 investment. That same amount is currently going to FindLaw and social posting with no measurable return.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • Larson Law does not appear to be running Local Service Ads for personal injury searches — no Google Screened badge was observed for the firm during this audit
  • LSA eligibility is tied in part to Google review count — the same review gap that limits organic rankings also limits LSA access and quality score
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Siegfried & Jensen — given their review volume (~1,558 reviews at 4.8 stars) and marketing investment, LSA participation is highly probable; they have the review count to achieve and maintain top LSA positioning
  • Parker & McConkie — 1,000+ five-star reviews across four locations make them a natural LSA market leader; their review advantage translates directly into LSA ranking priority
The Opportunity
  • Getting Google Screened is a one-time verification process — once earned, Larson Law would appear above all organic results, above the map pack, and above all standard Google Ads for PI searches in South Jordan
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • As the review campaign builds toward 200+ Google reviews, LSA eligibility and ranking quality both improve — making the review campaign the first and most critical step to unlocking this channel entirely

Facebook & Instagram Ads

Ad Presence
  • No active Meta ads from Larson Law were found in the Facebook Ads Library — the firm has 604 Facebook followers and 24 Facebook reviews from organic activity, but paid social advertising does not appear to be running
  • Current social media spend is going toward organic posting only, which reaches an existing audience of 604 followers — not the new accident victims who are searching for representation right now
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Siegfried & Jensen — running Meta ads with major Utah brand presence; multiple ad formats and sustained campaigns reach PI prospects across Facebook and Instagram before they ever run a Google search
  • Kramer Law Group — active on Facebook with a digital-first presence; likely running Meta retargeting given their 300+ review base and systematic marketing approach
The Opportunity
  • Retargeting — website visitors who did not convert are leaving with no follow-up
  • Video ads featuring the attorney — builds trust before a prospect ever calls; Adam's youth, energy, and 12 years of experience are differentiators against older competition
  • Educational content ads — capture leads earlier in the decision process (what to do after a car accident in South Jordan)
  • Spanish-language Meta ads targeting Salt Lake County's Hispanic community — a PI audience that is underserved by most competitors and reachable at lower CPL than English-language campaigns
