Intake Assessment

With roughly 80 leads coming in every month but only about 20 of them actually getting a response, Rebel Brown Law Group's intake process — not its lead volume — is the single biggest thing standing between the firm and the growth Marianne is asking for. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Only about 1 in 4 monthly leads — roughly 20 of 80 — are actually being responded to
  • No described process exists for triaging or following up with leads that aren't reached on the first attempt
  • No after-hours or overflow coverage is described for handling call and lead volume
  • Once a lead is actually contacted, close rate is strong at 80% — proof the breakdown happens entirely before that conversation
Blind Spot: The 60 leads a month that aren't being answered are not disappearing — they're calling the next name on their list, which, given the local search gap identified earlier, is very likely one of your named competitors.
The Positive
  • Once your team does reach a lead, they close at an 80% rate — a strong number that proves the sales process itself already works
The Gap
  • At 80 leads a month with only 20 worked, roughly 60 leads a month go untouched — at an 80% close rate and a $4,000 average case value, that is an estimated $200,000 a year in fees left on the table
  • Without a system, this gap does not close on its own as ad spend or SEO improves — new leads will suffer the same fate as current ones
Fixing intake is the single fastest way to buy back the time you're spending working harder for a business that isn't yet working for you.

Team Assessment

With Marianne limited to 2–3 billable hours a day by non-billable operational work, the firm's growth plan currently runs through her personal bandwidth instead of a team that can absorb it. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Marianne spends roughly 70% of her time on non-billable administrative "babysitting" work rather than casework or strategy
  • The stated goal of a lean, 2.5-person team for the SSD practice does not yet have defined roles or a delegation structure in place
  • No management or accountability layer exists between Marianne and day-to-day operations
  • The firm is simultaneously winding down Family Law and scaling SSD with the same small team, splitting focus at the exact moment focus matters most
Blind Spot: The fact that you're still personally in the day-to-day is the blind spot itself — it means you haven't yet experienced what SSD growth looks like when it isn't limited by your own calendar.
The Positive
  • Your staff member Meg is already being named specifically in client reviews for strong SSD case outcomes — proof the team has real talent to build a delegation structure around
The Gap
  • Every hour Marianne spends on non-billable operations is an hour not spent on billable casework or growing the SSD practice
  • Without delegation, hitting 1,000 SSD clients means multiplying Marianne's current workload rather than reducing it
Real freedom starts the day your team can run SSD intake and casework without you personally holding it all together.

Profit Plan Assessment

Beyond the $400,000 SSD revenue projection and the roughly $200,000 a year in identified missed-lead revenue, Rebel Brown Law Group has no stated system for tracking profitability by practice area during this transition. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No stated profitability tracking separates Family Law from SSD during the wind-down and build-up happening at the same time
  • The firm's own missed-lead math — roughly $200,000 a year — was calculated on this call, not previously known or tracked internally
  • No stated visibility into cost per case or cost per lead for either practice
  • The SSD growth goal is set as a client count (1,000 clients) rather than an explicit revenue target
Blind Spot: Scaling SSD lead volume before fixing intake means paying to generate leads you're already failing to convert — profit gets worse, not better, until that sequence is reversed.
The Positive
  • The SSD practice's average case value ($4,000) and close rate (80%) are both known numbers — a stronger starting point for profit planning than most firms have
The Gap
  • Without practice-level profitability tracking, growth in gross revenue does not guarantee growth in what Marianne actually keeps
  • The $200,000 a year in missed revenue is unrealized profit, not just unrealized revenue, since those leads are already paid for
You already know what a paying SSD case is worth to you — the missing piece is knowing whether growth is building you a bigger business or just more work.
