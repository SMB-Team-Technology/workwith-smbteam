Lead Generation

Rebel Brown Law Group is running two disconnected web properties during a practice pivot — a stale legacy Family Law site and a modern Social Security Disability site — with no paid lead generation confirmed on any channel and no local landing pages built for the eight counties the SSD practice claims to serve.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Two separate websites are competing for the firm's search authority — rebelbrownlawgroup.com (Family Law, the practice being wound down) and njdisabilitylaw.com (SSD, the designated growth practice)
  • PageSpeed scores could not be captured this session (API quota exhausted) — mobile and desktop scores must be re-run manually before this number is used in the proposal
  • The Family Law site's blog has not been updated since May 15, 2023 — roughly three years of stale content on the firm's older, still-indexed web property
  • Neither site carries video content, and the SSD site's contact form sits mid-page rather than above the fold
Blind Spot: The SSD site — the one actually driving your growth practice — is the stronger of your two web assets, but it currently has no blog and no city-specific pages, so none of that design quality is being used to actually rank for the searches your next 1,000 clients are typing.
The Positive
  • The SSD site (njdisabilitylaw.com) is modern, clean, and professionally built, with a strong attorney bio, an Avvo 10.0 rating badge, and a clear "no fee unless you win" guarantee above the fold
  • The primary phone number and "Free Consultation" CTA are prominent above the fold on both sites
The Gap
  • Two separate web properties split search authority instead of concentrating it behind the SSD practice you are trying to scale
  • No city or county landing pages means the SSD site cannot be found for the location-specific searches your named competitors are already winning
  • No blog and no video means zero ongoing content is being produced to build authority or capture non-branded search traffic
The SSD site already has the credibility — it just doesn't yet have the content or structure telling Google, and prospects, that this is where you want your next 1,000 clients to find you.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Google Business Profile shows 48 Google reviews at a blended 3.3–3.4 star rating — behind Hoffman DiMuzio's 84 reviews at a 4.9 rating in the same market
  • Primary GBP category is still listed as "Family Law Attorney," not SSD, even though SSD is the designated growth practice
  • No city or county landing pages exist despite the SSD site claiming coverage across Gloucester, Camden, Burlington, Cumberland, Salem, Ocean, Atlantic, and Cape May counties
  • NAP inconsistency: one directory citation lists (856) 485-0062 instead of the primary (856) 881-5000, and a third office (Wall, NJ) is referenced in site copy with no listed address
Blind Spot: Review sentiment is polarized in a specific way — your strongest 5-star reviews call out SSD case outcomes and staff by name, while 1-star reviews cluster around Family Law billing and responsiveness complaints. Google and prospects can both see that split, and it is already reinforcing that SSD, not Family Law, is where your reputation is strongest.
The Positive
  • Review velocity is healthy — the most recent review was posted May 18, 2026, so the profile is actively earning trust rather than sitting stagnant
The Gap
  • Organic results for "social security disability lawyer [South Jersey city]" are dominated by named competitors, not Rebel Brown, meaning the firm is invisible at the exact moment a prospect is searching
  • NAP inconsistencies dilute the local-ranking signals Google uses to decide who appears in the map pack
Hoffman DiMuzio — more reviews, a stronger rating, and multiple physical offices positioned across the same counties Rebel Brown claims to serve, giving Google more reasons to rank them first.
Every SSD search you don't win is a client who ends up at a competitor's office instead of yours — one step further from the 1,000-client practice you're building.

  • No budget, platform, or result from any past Google Ads campaign was mentioned on the call
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Hoffman DiMuzio, Bross & Frankel, and Stopper Lopez — ad presence could not be directly confirmed in this session either (also blocked), but their dominant organic visibility for SSD search terms across South Jersey makes it reasonable to expect they are also competing for paid placement; verify manually before the proposal call
The Opportunity

  • Mobile-intent, urgency-driven terms like "SSD attorney near me"
  • Bidding on competitor brand names — Hoffman DiMuzio, Bross & Frankel, Stopper Lopez — to capture prospects already comparing SSD attorneys
  • Neighboring, currently-unclaimed cities across all 8 counties the SSD site claims to cover but does not yet target
Blind Spot: Competitors bidding on your own firm name would let them capture people already searching for Rebel Brown Law Group by name — this should be checked directly in Google before the proposal call.
