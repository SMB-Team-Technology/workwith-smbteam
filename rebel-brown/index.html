Here Is Where Rebel Brown Law Group Stands Today

Marianne Rebel Brown has spent nearly 30 years building a South Jersey family law practice out of Glassboro and Haddon Heights — but she is now deliberately winding that practice down, handing it off to another firm, to focus entirely on Social Security Disability, the practice she has grown to a projected $400,000 this year with a stated goal of scaling to 1,000 clients on a lean, 2.5-person team. The single most important finding from this audit: of the roughly 80 leads the firm receives every month, only about 20 are actually being responded to — meaning the firm's real ceiling right now is not lead volume, it is an intake bottleneck currently costing an estimated $200,000 a year in cases that are never worked.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Intake Assessment

With roughly 80 leads coming in every month but only about 20 of them actually getting a response, Rebel Brown Law Group's intake process — not its lead volume — is the single biggest thing standing between the firm and the growth Marianne is asking for. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Only about 1 in 4 monthly leads — roughly 20 of 80 — are actually being responded to
  • No described process exists for triaging or following up with leads that aren't reached on the first attempt
  • No after-hours or overflow coverage is described for handling call and lead volume
  • Once a lead is actually contacted, close rate is strong at 80% — proof the breakdown happens entirely before that conversation
Blind Spot: The 60 leads a month that aren't being answered are not disappearing — they're calling the next name on their list, which, given the local search gap identified earlier, is very likely one of your named competitors.
The Positive
  • Once your team does reach a lead, they close at an 80% rate — a strong number that proves the sales process itself already works
The Gap
  • At 80 leads a month with only 20 worked, roughly 60 leads a month go untouched — at an 80% close rate and a $4,000 average case value, that is an estimated $200,000 a year in fees left on the table
  • Without a system, this gap does not close on its own as ad spend or SEO improves — new leads will suffer the same fate as current ones
Fixing intake is the single fastest way to buy back the time you're spending working harder for a business that isn't yet working for you.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • The SSD site's contact form sits mid-page, under "Start with a free consultation," rather than being visible above the fold
  • The Family Law site's contact form is reachable only through nav and CTA buttons, not embedded directly on the homepage
