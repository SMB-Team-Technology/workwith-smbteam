Lead Generation

Leibowitz Law Firm PLLC has a 16-year-old practice specializing in some of the highest-value personal injury cases in Texas — car accidents, commercial truck crashes, and drunk driving injuries — yet it does not appear to be generating a single paid digital lead across any channel in San Antonio or the four South Texas counties it serves. Every high-intent prospect searching for a truck accident or personal injury attorney in this market is being captured by competitors who are spending heavily on every channel Jake has left completely uncontested.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Built on Squarespace — the platform imposes structural limitations on SEO customization, page speed optimization, and landing page flexibility that are critical for a competitive PI market
  • PageSpeed scores: Not retrieved due to API rate limiting — Squarespace-hosted sites commonly score 40–65 on mobile and 70–85 on desktop; recommend verifying at pagespeed.web.dev before the proposal call
  • No practice area sub-pages observed — the site has a homepage, an attorney bio page, and a contact page, but no dedicated pages for car accidents, truck accidents, or drunk driving injuries; competitors rank on these terms because they have dedicated pages for each
  • Contact form did not render during testing on the contact page — the page prompts visitors to "fill out the form" but the form itself was not visible, meaning motivated prospects may be hitting a dead end
  • No geo-targeting pages for Edinburg, Brownsville, or Laredo — Jake serves Hidalgo, Cameron, and Webb Counties but has no location-specific content to capture searchers in those markets
Blind Spot: Competitors may be running Google Ads bidding on "Leibowitz Law Firm" as a keyword — capturing prospects who search for Jake by name and redirecting them to a competing firm before they reach Jake's site. This is a common tactic in high-competition PI markets and would be invisible without an ad audit.
The Positive
  • Modern, professional Squarespace design with a clear homepage headline — the site does not look dated or unprofessional on first impression
  • Attorney bio page with credentials, bar admissions, personal story, and association memberships (SATLA, AAJ, TTLA, ATAA) — the trust signals are there; they just need to be surfaced more prominently and expanded into practice area pages
The Gap
  • Without dedicated practice area pages, the site cannot rank organically for "truck accident attorney San Antonio" or "car accident lawyer San Antonio" — the terms that drive the highest-intent traffic in this market
  • No geo-landing pages means the firm's four-county South Texas reach is entirely invisible to digital searchers in Edinburg, Brownsville, and Laredo — an untapped bilingual market that Jake is uniquely positioned to serve
  • A broken contact form means a percentage of motivated website visitors are leaving without any way to reach the firm — potential cases lost before Jake ever knows they existed
A website that ranks for the right terms and converts visitors who land on it is the foundation Jake needs to stop relying on whoever happens to call — and start building the high-value caseload that frees up his time and grows his income predictably.

  • Estimated fewer than 10 Google reviews — the primary barrier to local pack ranking in a market where the nearest small competitor (Ryan Orsatti Law) has 125+ reviews and the dominant competitor (Carabin Shaw) has 1,207+
  • Three confirmed NAP inconsistencies across directories: Yellow Pages lists a wrong address (Saint Ann / 78201) and wrong phone number ((210) 225-8787); FindLaw shows a stale listing under Mack Injury Attorneys with a wrong zip (78230); LawyerLegion lists a wrong address (6243 I-10 / 78201) and is unclaimed — each inconsistency erodes Google's confidence in the firm's location signals
  • No geo-targeting landing pages for Edinburg (Hidalgo County), Brownsville (Cameron County), or Laredo (Webb County) — Jake serves all four counties but has no digital footprint in three of them
Blind Spot: Google uses NAP consistency across the web as a local ranking signal. With three directories showing different addresses and phone numbers for this firm, Google's confidence in the business location is compromised — which directly suppresses local pack visibility even if other signals improve. Fixing these inconsistencies is one of the fastest wins available and costs nothing except time.
The Positive
  • Avvo profile is claimed and active with an 8.9/10 Excellent rating, 1 client review, and 3 peer endorsements — a foundation to build from, even if the review count needs significant growth
The Gap
  • Without 3-pack visibility, the firm is invisible to the majority of personal injury prospects in San Antonio — an estimated 40–60% of PI case searches click directly from the map pack without scrolling to organic results
  • Competitors with 400+ reviews are generating a steady flow of new reviews at a rate that widens the gap every month Jake waits to implement a review acquisition program
Carabin Shaw — 1,207+ Google reviews at 4.9 stars, active Google Business Profile with regular posts and photos, appearing in the local 3-pack across every major PI search term in San Antonio. Their review velocity alone makes it nearly impossible for a firm with fewer than 10 reviews to compete for map pack placement without a dedicated review program.
Local SEO visibility is what turns Jake's 16 years of experience and bilingual capability into a lead machine — one that generates high-value cases from four South Texas counties without Jake personally making a single referral call.
