Intake Assessment

Soltesz Law Group is a recently launched solo practice where all intake appears to fall on one person — there is no delegation, no after-hours coverage, and no follow-up system for leads who do not connect on the first call. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Roland appears to handle all consultations and intake personally — no dedicated intake team member has been identified, meaning every incoming inquiry competes for his attention alongside active casework
  • No after-hours coverage mechanism identified — criminal defense leads are time-critical; a DUI arrest at midnight generates a search and a call within hours, and if that call goes to voicemail the prospect will reach the next attorney on the list before morning
  • No intake tracking or case management system observed — leads that do not convert on the first contact likely fall through with no structured follow-up sequence to bring them back
  • No observed online booking system or web-based intake form — prospects who prefer to initiate contact digitally have no pathway to engage outside of a direct phone call
Blind Spot: Criminal defense leads are among the most time-sensitive in law — arrest and arraignment timelines create genuine urgency on the client's side. The attorney who answers first gets retained first. Every unanswered call from a recently arrested prospect is not just a missed opportunity — it is a case that walks directly to a competitor who did answer.
The Gap
  • If even two criminal defense leads per month are lost to missed calls or slow follow-up — at a blended average case value of $4,000 — that is $8,000 in lost monthly revenue, or nearly $100,000 per year walking out the door before advertising spend even begins
  • Without a defined intake process, every dollar invested in marketing generates leads that have no systematic path to becoming signed clients — the return on advertising spend is limited by the intake gap, not the ad quality
  • Leads who express interest but do not sign immediately — the "I need to think about it" calls — have no follow-up sequence to bring them back; they will hire whoever follows up first, and right now that is not Soltesz Law Group
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

  • No live chat or chatbot was observed in any directory listing or search snippet referencing the Soltesz Law Group website

Team Assessment

Soltesz Law Group appears to be a one-person operation — Roland handles the legal work, the business development, and the client relationships, with no team to carry any portion of the load. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • All firm functions — legal work, intake, client communication, and business development — appear to fall on Roland alone; there is no division of labor and no bandwidth buffer
  • A serious felony case requiring 60+ hours of preparation will consume the entire firm's capacity — there is no team structure that can absorb that load while other cases and inquiries continue
  • No accountability structure, management layer, or documented processes identified — nothing that would allow another person to step in and run any part of the firm without Roland's direct involvement
  • The firm's growth ceiling is Roland's personal hours — every new case requires more of him, which means growth makes the problem worse, not better
Blind Spot: The independence of owning your own firm can feel like freedom — but a firm that only runs when Roland is present is not a firm he owns. It is a job he built himself. True ownership means the firm generates cases, processes clients, and functions on its own, whether Roland is in the office or not.
The Gap
  • As marketing brings in more leads, Roland will face a binary choice: turn away cases and leave revenue on the table, or accept every case and burn out on the volume — neither is a path to the firm he set out to build
  • Every hour Roland spends on admin tasks, intake calls, and non-billable operations is an hour he cannot spend on high-value casework or on the life he built this firm to support
  • Without team infrastructure in place before advertising scales, growth will accelerate the problem — more leads, more cases, more of everything falling on one person with no more hours in the day
Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Profit Plan Assessment

For a recently launched solo criminal defense practice, financial visibility is the foundation that makes every other decision — how to price cases, which cases to take, and how much to invest in marketing — make sense rather than being guesswork. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Revenue, case volume, and profit margins were not available for this audit — establishing a financial baseline requires a discovery conversation before any planning can begin
  • No marketing attribution or case tracking system observed — without knowing which cases came from which source, it is impossible to calculate the return on any marketing investment
  • No visibility into case profitability by type — serious felony and homicide cases are high-prestige but also highly time-intensive; without tracking, they may be generating less per hour than volume DUI or misdemeanor work
  • Without a profit plan, revenue growth does not automatically mean more take-home — expenses can expand to absorb new income, leaving Roland working harder for the same result
Blind Spot: Criminal defense attorneys often take on their most complex, time-intensive cases — homicide, sex crimes, multi-defendant cases — because of their professional significance. Without tracking true cost-per-case including attorney hours and overhead, those high-profile cases may actually be the least profitable in the portfolio.
The Positive
  • Roland's premium credentials — 100+ jury trials, death penalty qualification, and 22+ years of experience — position him to command above-market rates for high-stakes criminal defense work that other attorneys cannot take on
The Gap
  • Without financial visibility, Roland cannot know whether to raise rates, change his case mix, or reduce overhead — every major business decision is made without data
  • Investing in marketing without ROI tracking means it is impossible to know whether growth is genuinely profitable or simply creating more billable hours with thinner margins
  • A solo practitioner without a profit plan often discovers that doubling revenue means doubling work — not doubling take-home; a profit plan prevents that trap before it forms
Profit is not what is left after the bills — it is what you plan for. Building a profit plan now means that as revenue grows, the money Roland keeps grows with it.
