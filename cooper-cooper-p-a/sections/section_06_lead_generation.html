Lead Generation

Cooper & Cooper doubled its marketing budget from $5K–$7.5K/mo to roughly $20K/mo this year, yet new client acquisition has stayed flat at about 15/mo. The current marketing agency also represents a direct competitor in the same market — a conflict of interest that is likely capping both strategy and results.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed scores: Mobile 47/100 — Desktop 89/100 (best-effort assessment; recommend a manual re-check before final delivery)
  • No blog or educational content published — the site relies entirely on static service and geo pages, ceding every "how does custody work in Florida" style search to competitors
  • Contact form lives on a dedicated /contact/ page — not confirmed as duplicated above the fold on the homepage, adding friction for time-pressed visitors
  • No video content on the site — prospects facing a divorce or custody decision have no way to see or hear from Anthony or Charlean before ever calling
Blind Spot: Geo-targeted landing pages currently exist for only one confirmed area outside Fleming Island itself (Green Cove Springs). Prospects searching from Duval, St. Johns, or Nassau County — all served by the firm — may never find a page built for their specific town.
The Positive
  • Mobile-responsive design with a clear consultation CTA above the fold
  • Attorney bios present for both Anthony and Charlean Cooper, including Florida Supreme Court certified mediator credentials
The Gap
  • A 47/100 mobile PageSpeed score likely means prospects searching on their phones — the majority of family law searches — are bouncing before the page finishes loading
  • Zero blog content means competitors like Head, Moss, Fulton & Griffin, P.A. can claim informational searches the firm should be winning
  • Limited geo-page coverage leaves the firm effectively invisible in three of the four counties it serves
A faster, broader-reaching website is not a technical nice-to-have — it is the foundation that makes every other marketing dollar work harder, which is exactly what frees up the time you are trying to reclaim by October.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Review profile is fragmented across platforms — 4.9 stars and 50+ reviews in aggregate, but only 29 reviews reflected on Yelp specifically
  • No GBP Posts activity observed — a free, no-cost visibility tool is going unused
  • Geo-page coverage exists for only one confirmed town beyond Fleming Island, limiting local pack visibility across Clay, Duval, St. Johns, and Nassau counties
Blind Spot: Your review count and rating (4.9 stars, 50+ reviews) already outperform every named local competitor in this audit — Fleming Island Law Group has 3, Florida Women's Law Group has 15. Most firms in your position assume they are behind on reputation; you are actually ahead. The gap is visibility, not trust.
The Positive
  • Review velocity is active and recent — most recent review within the last 30 days, with no NAP inconsistencies found across Avvo, Justia, Yelp, BBB, LawCrossing, or Facebook
The Gap
  • Without broader geo-page coverage, competitors capture nearly every "family law attorney [nearby town]" search outside Fleming Island itself
  • An already-strong reputation is not being converted into local pack dominance the way it should be
Owenby Law, P.A. — BBB A+ accredited and appears consistently in the local pack across every search performed in this audit, from divorce to custody to probate, backed by strong overall review volume.
Your reputation is already better than the firms beating you in the map pack — local SEO is the fastest, lowest-cost way to turn that advantage into the steady flow of cases that lets you step back.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Per the transcript, the firm runs active PPC campaigns for Family Law as part of its ~$20K/mo combined PPC/LSA spend — but new client acquisition has remained flat at ~15/mo despite the increase
  • Google Ads spend does not appear to extend to Probate/Estate Planning — Family Law is absorbing effectively all of the budget, leaving the new Probate division without a dedicated growth engine
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Owenby Law, P.A. — established local paid presence in Family Law across the same Fleming Island/Clay County market
  • Head, Moss, Fulton & Griffin, P.A. — visible ad presence specifically in Probate/Estate Planning, the exact practice area Cooper & Cooper is trying to grow
The Opportunity
  • Direct-intent keywords already proven to have local demand in this audit's own local pack tests — "divorce lawyer Fleming Island," "child custody attorney Fleming Island," "family law attorney Clay County"
  • Dedicated Probate keyword coverage the firm is not currently bidding on — "probate attorney Fleming Island FL," "wills and trusts attorney Clay County"
  • Competitor conquesting terms to intercept prospects actively comparison-shopping against Owenby Law and Florida Women's Law Group
  • Urgency-driven terms — "emergency custody attorney," "same day divorce consultation Fleming Island" — to capture prospects at their moment of highest intent
Blind Spot: Your current agency also manages the paid strategy for Florida Women's Law Group — a direct family law competitor in the same market. Any competitive bidding insight or creative advantage they develop for that account works directly against you in the same auction.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • Firm appears in LSA as part of the current $20K/mo combined spend, but lead quality is described by the prospect as low — a signal that targeting or budget configuration needs attention, not that the channel itself is broken
  • LSA review count is consistent with the firm's overall GBP profile (50+), which should be a meaningful advantage in LSA ranking
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Owenby Law, P.A. — LSA presence backed by strong overall review volume and BBB accreditation
  • Fleming Island Law Group — smaller review footprint (3 reviews, 5.0 stars) but still competing for the same LSA placements
The Opportunity
  • With more reviews than Fleming Island Law Group and a rating on par with Owenby Law, Cooper & Cooper is positioned to outperform both in LSA placement once the account is properly tuned
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • Correcting the low lead-quality issue could redirect a meaningful share of the existing $20K/mo spend toward leads that actually convert, without adding a single new dollar to the budget
Blind Spot: Low LSA lead quality is usually a service-area or budget-cap targeting problem, not a channel problem. If the current agency has set the service radius too wide, the firm may be paying for leads far outside a practical drive time to Fleming Island.

Ad Presence

  • Direct Meta Ad Library confirmation was not available in this research session; this finding is based on the prospect's own statement on the call

The Opportunity

  • Video ads featuring Anthony and Charlean — builds trust before a prospect ever calls, especially valuable in family law where the decision is emotional
  • Educational content ads — capture leads earlier in the decision process, before they are ready to pick up the phone
  • Even a modest increase to the current $1K/mo, paired with retargeting, could meaningfully move the "flat at 15/mo" number without a large jump in total spend
