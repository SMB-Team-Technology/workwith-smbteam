Intake Assessment

Cooper & Cooper is converting only 20–25% of consults into retainers against a stated goal of 50%, with no intake automation live yet. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Consult-to-retainer conversion sits at 20–25%, roughly half of the firm's own 50% goal
  • No CRM or intake automation is live yet — Lawmatics implementation is still about three weeks out
  • PPC leads convert better than LSA leads but often cannot afford the firm's fees, creating friction and lost time during consultations
  • LSA leads are described by the prospect as low-quality, adding volume without adding retained clients
Blind Spot: Every point below your 50% goal on a 15-consult-per-month flow is roughly 2–4 signed cases walking out the door each month — cases you already paid marketing dollars to generate.
The Positive
  • The firm has already identified the exact gap (20–25% vs. 50% goal) and is actively moving to close it with Lawmatics
  • PPC lead quality, while imperfect on fee fit, confirms the firm's marketing is reaching genuinely motivated prospects
The Gap
  • At current conversion rates, doubling ad spend without fixing intake simply doubles the number of leads that walk away unconverted
  • Without a defined follow-up sequence, PPC leads who hesitate on fees today may retain with a competitor tomorrow
  • No after-hours or fee-qualification process means the firm cannot pre-screen for fit before burning attorney time in consultation
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • No live chat presence observed on the site, removing a low-friction entry point for prospects who are not yet ready to call

Team Assessment

With 11 employees and a 12th attorney joining next week, Cooper & Cooper has real team scale — but Charlean remains stuck in casework instead of leading the firm, and the business cannot yet run without both owners' constant oversight. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
"Charlean is stuck in casework, preventing her from focusing on management."
The Problem
  • Charlean is still handling casework personally, with no clear path yet to a full-time management role
  • No senior attorney is currently in place to absorb casework and free Charlean for leadership — hiring one is a stated goal, not yet a reality
  • The firm cannot run without Anthony and Charlean's constant, direct involvement
  • A vacation without disruption, targeted for October, is not achievable under the firm's current structure
Blind Spot: Adding a 12th attorney without first building a management layer risks adding more casework for Charlean to oversee personally, rather than less — growth alone will not solve this without a structural change.
The Positive
  • The firm already has the headcount (11 employees, soon 12) to support a management structure — the constraint is structure, not size
  • Both owners have clearly named the goal — hiring a senior attorney and training Charlean into management — which means the firm is aligned on the fix it needs
The Gap
  • Every week Charlean spends on casework instead of management is a week the firm's growth depends entirely on the owners' personal hours
  • Without a trained management layer, the planned Florida-wide expansion (Tampa, St. Augustine, Boca, Fort Lauderdale, Panhandle) has no one to run it but Anthony and Charlean themselves
  • The October vacation goal has no structural support behind it yet
Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Profit Plan Assessment

Cooper & Cooper hit $1M in revenue this year, but profit is currently murky, and the firm is only now hiring a bookkeeper to clean up its financials. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: RED
"Profit is currently murky."
The Problem
  • No clean profit margin has been established despite reaching $1M in revenue this year
  • A bookkeeper is only now being brought on to clean up the firm's financials
  • Marketing spend doubled to ~$20K/mo with no clear read on what return that spend actually produced
  • A new, high-cost Probate attorney is being carried without a clear picture of whether that division is currently profitable
Blind Spot: Without cost-per-case visibility by practice area, the firm cannot yet tell whether the Probate division — carrying a high-cost attorney on 3% of caseload — is being subsidized by Family Law, or whether it is on track to pay for itself.
The Positive
  • The firm has already taken the first concrete step toward financial visibility by hiring a bookkeeper
  • Hitting $1M in revenue in the firm's first full year is a strong foundation to build a real profit plan on top of
The Gap
  • Without a profit plan, the firm cannot confirm whether doubled marketing spend is actually paying for itself
  • No visibility into cost per case by practice area makes it impossible to know whether growth is adding wealth or just adding work
  • Decisions about the Probate division, staffing, and expansion are currently being made without the numbers to support them
You cannot plan the October vacation, the next hire, or the Florida-wide expansion off numbers you cannot see — a profit plan turns "murky" into a number you can actually trust.
