Here Is Where The Finn Law Firm Stands Today

Larry Finn has spent over 40 years building a respected practice in medical malpractice, nursing home neglect and abuse, and sexual abuse and civil assault litigation — complex, high-value cases that routinely resolve for $400,000 or more. The firm ran on referrals alone for most of that history and only began digital marketing 2.5 years ago, but Google Ads have been paused for six months and the website is mid-rebuild, leaving The Finn Law Firm with no active lead generation system and no organic visibility in any of its three core practice areas.

Stage 6 is the stage where the pre-intake bottleneck disappears for good — where workflows run on their own and Larry's time goes to the cases that matter most, not to manually triaging every lead that comes in.

Where You Are Today

STAGE 3: SOLO PRACTITIONER
  • Built almost entirely on referrals for most of the firm's 40+ year history
  • No additional attorneys or dedicated staff — Larry personally handles case evaluation and the pre-intake bottleneck
  • Google Ads paused for six months, and the only active marketing spend ($6k/mo to an outside agency) is currently tied up in a website rebuild rather than lead generation
  • Cash flow is volatile month to month because revenue depends on the slow resolution timelines of a small number of large, complex cases

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between a referral-only solo practice and a firm that runs on predictable systems is exactly what stands between Larry and the stable, expandable firm he described wanting to build — and closing it starts with fixing the bottleneck at the very front of the funnel.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • The blog has gone stale — the most recent post is from August 22, 2025, roughly 11 months old, with no active content program behind it
  • PageSpeed mobile and desktop scores could not be retrieved live during this research pass (rate-limited API access) — flagged for manual verification at pagespeed.web.dev before this report is finalized
  • The live site is a known-outdated version currently being replaced by an outside agency — meaning today's site is a placeholder, not a finished asset
  • No geo-targeted landing pages exist in the current site navigation for the outlying Northern Illinois counties the firm serves
Blind Spot: The firm's own blog already proved geo-content works — three Rockford-specific posts show a past effort to target the outlying counties the firm serves. That effort went dormant almost a year ago and was never extended to the other counties served.
The Positive
  • A strong attorney bio sits above the fold — 40+ years of experience, a Super Lawyers selection, and specific case-volume stats (650 personal injury cases, 200 nursing home abuse cases, 250 wrongful death cases, 150 medical malpractice cases)
  • Clear calls to action above the fold ("FREE CASE EVALUATION" plus a visible phone number) with a contact form repeated in the footer
The Gap
  • An aging site with a stale blog cannot rank or convert while ads are paused — leaving zero active lead sources running today
  • Without geo-targeted pages for the outlying counties served, the firm is invisible to prospects searching from those markets
  • Every month the rebuild continues without a parallel content and SEO plan is a month of lost organic visibility stacked on top of the paused ad spend
A rebuilt website only pays off once it is paired with a lead system that runs on its own — otherwise the bottleneck simply moves from the old site to the new one.

Profit Plan Assessment

Revenue is $1M+ annually, but with resolution timelines running long on complex, high-value litigation, Larry has described real cash-flow volatility rather than predictable profit. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

"High-value ($400k+), complex cases (med mal, nursing home abuse) create significant cash-flow volatility."
The Problem
  • Revenue is $1M+ annually, but no profitability figures by practice area were discussed on the call
  • Cash flow is described as significantly volatile, driven by the long resolution timelines on complex cases
  • No revenue goal was stated on the call — this audit uses a standard 2x-current-revenue default (~$2M+) in its place
  • Average case value is known only as a range ($400k+), not tracked as a specific, predictable number
Blind Spot: Because your highest-value cases are also your slowest to resolve, revenue and cash flow can tell two very different stories in the same month — without separating the two, it is easy to feel stretched even in a strong year.
The Positive
  • A $1M+ revenue base built almost entirely on complex, high-value litigation shows real selectivity in the cases the firm takes on — a discipline a profit plan can build directly on
The Gap
  • Without profitability visibility by case type, there is no way to know which practice areas are actually the most profitable, not just the largest
  • Cash-flow volatility makes it harder to commit confidently to new marketing or staffing investment, even when the underlying revenue trend is healthy
  • A stated growth goal without a matching profit plan risks the firm working harder for the same — or lower — take-home
Stabilizing revenue was the outcome Larry named directly on the call — and that starts with a profit plan that turns high-value cases into predictable income, not just impressive case results.
