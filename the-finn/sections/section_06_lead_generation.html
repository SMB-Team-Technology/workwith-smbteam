Lead Generation

The Finn Law Firm has no active lead generation system running today: Google Ads have been paused for six months, and the website is mid-rebuild by an outside $6k/mo agency. What was once a firm growing through referrals and, more recently, digital marketing now has no channel producing new leads at all.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • The blog has gone stale — the most recent post is from August 22, 2025, roughly 11 months old, with no active content program behind it
  • PageSpeed mobile and desktop scores could not be retrieved live during this research pass (rate-limited API access) — flagged for manual verification at pagespeed.web.dev before this report is finalized
  • The live site is a known-outdated version currently being replaced by an outside agency — meaning today's site is a placeholder, not a finished asset
  • No geo-targeted landing pages exist in the current site navigation for the outlying Northern Illinois counties the firm serves
Blind Spot: The firm's own blog already proved geo-content works — three Rockford-specific posts show a past effort to target the outlying counties the firm serves. That effort went dormant almost a year ago and was never extended to the other counties served.
The Positive
  • A strong attorney bio sits above the fold — 40+ years of experience, a Super Lawyers selection, and specific case-volume stats (650 personal injury cases, 200 nursing home abuse cases, 250 wrongful death cases, 150 medical malpractice cases)
  • Clear calls to action above the fold ("FREE CASE EVALUATION" plus a visible phone number) with a contact form repeated in the footer
The Gap
  • An aging site with a stale blog cannot rank or convert while ads are paused — leaving zero active lead sources running today
  • Without geo-targeted pages for the outlying counties served, the firm is invisible to prospects searching from those markets
  • Every month the rebuild continues without a parallel content and SEO plan is a month of lost organic visibility stacked on top of the paused ad spend
A rebuilt website only pays off once it is paired with a lead system that runs on its own — otherwise the bottleneck simply moves from the old site to the new one.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Cross-referenced directory data shows a small review base — 4.8 stars/11 reviews on Lawyers.com, 5.0/8 client reviews on Avvo — a live Google Business Profile pull was not accessible this research pass and should be spot-checked before delivery
  • The Finn Law Firm did not appear organically in any of 9 local-visibility searches run across medical malpractice, nursing home abuse, and sexual abuse — its three core practice areas
  • No geo-targeted landing pages currently exist in the site's navigation for the outlying counties the firm serves
Blind Spot: Malman Law and Levin & Perconti aren't just outranking Finn in generic searches — they're capturing the exact nursing home abuse and medical malpractice terms the firm specializes in, meaning every lead lost is a lead this firm was uniquely positioned to win.
The Positive
  • NAP (name, address, phone) is consistent across every directory checked — Avvo, BBB, Martindale-Hubbell, Lawyers.com, and Yellow Pages — a clean foundation to build local SEO on
The Gap
  • An 11-month-stale blog and no county-specific pages mean the firm's organic footprint has not grown while named competitors expanded theirs
  • A review base of 8–11 leaves prospects with far less social proof than competitors with hundreds of reviews at the exact moment they are comparing firms
Malman Law — sustained, multi-channel local SEO investment (TV commercials plus dedicated nursing home abuse and nursing home sexual abuse landing pages) directly overlapping Finn's core practice area.
Local visibility is the foundation every other lead channel builds on — without it, Larry's 40 years of earned reputation stays trapped in word of mouth instead of reaching the prospects searching for help right now.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • This firm does not appear to be running Google search ads for medical malpractice, nursing home abuse, or sexual abuse terms in Chicago — consistent with the transcript's confirmation that Google Ads were paused six months ago
  • Ads were run in the past, so the firm has existing account history and performance data to relaunch from rather than starting cold
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Clifford Law Offices & Levin & Perconti — dedicated medical malpractice sub-practice landing pages by injury type, typical of sustained PPC investment
  • Malman Law — dedicated nursing home abuse and nursing home sexual abuse landing pages plus confirmed TV commercial investment
  • Rosenfeld Injury Lawyers — dedicated clergy abuse and sexual abuse landing pages with "$450 million recovered" messaging
The Opportunity
  • "medical malpractice lawyer chicago" and "chicago medical malpractice attorney" — high-value terms with zero current organic or paid presence for the firm
  • "nursing home abuse lawyer chicago" and "elder abuse attorney illinois nursing home neglect lawyer" — direct overlap with the firm's stated specialty
  • Conquesting campaigns on Malman Law, Levin & Perconti, and Rosenfeld Injury Lawyers brand terms — capturing prospects already comparing firms
  • Rockford and other outlying Northern Illinois county terms — building on the geo-intent the firm's own dormant blog posts already proved existed
Blind Spot: Because Google Ads were paused for "sufficient lead volume" rather than poor performance, this is a paused system with a track record — not a rebuild from zero. Relaunching should be faster and lower-risk than the phrase "starting ads" usually implies.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • The Finn Law Firm does not appear in Local Service Ads; Google Screened status could not be confirmed via a live-rendered search this research pass — this is an unconfirmed gap, not a verified absence, and should be spot-checked before delivery
  • No LSA review count currently exists for the firm
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Named local competitors in personal injury and abuse litigation generally treat LSAs as a well-established, competitive channel — specific firm-level LSA confirmation was not available via tools in this research session, and should not be read as evidence that competitors are absent from this channel
The Opportunity
  • LSAs would put The Finn Law Firm above every paid and organic result for its highest-value practice areas — the exact $400k+ cases the firm specializes in
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • With a real, credentialed reputation (40+ years, Super Lawyers) but a small review count, LSA's pay-per-lead model reduces the risk of the firm's low current search visibility while the broader digital presence is rebuilt
Blind Spot: Even without a live confirmation this session, LSAs are a well-established, competitive channel in personal injury and abuse-litigation verticals generally — the firm's absence from this channel should not be read as absence of competition for these slots.
