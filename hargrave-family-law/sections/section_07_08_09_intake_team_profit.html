Intake Assessment

No discovery call transcript was available for this audit — Hargrave Family Law's intake process cannot be directly verified from public information, but observable website signals point to specific conversion gaps that are almost certain to be leaving signed cases on the table. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • The contact form is not visible above the fold on the homepage — prospects arriving after hours or on mobile with an urgent question have to navigate away to find a contact option, and many will not
  • No live chat or real-time engagement tool is present on the website — family law prospects frequently have late-night concerns and need an immediate low-pressure way to start a conversation without committing to a phone call
  • With two offices across Dallas and McKinney, intake consistency across both locations is a documented challenge for multi-site firms — without a verified intake protocol, the experience a prospect receives likely varies by who answers
  • Without a transcript, the firm's after-hours coverage, follow-up cadence, and speed-to-response cannot be confirmed — all of which are critical in a market where family law decisions are emotionally urgent and prospects compare multiple firms before signing
Blind Spot: Family law prospects are emotionally activated at the moment they first reach out — they are not comparison shopping calmly. A slow response or voicemail after hours is not a minor inconvenience; it is often the reason they sign with the next firm that picks up.
The Positive
  • Two visible CTAs on the homepage ("Schedule" and "Call Now") give prospects a clear next step — the issue is not the absence of contact options but their placement and after-hours coverage
  • The firm's 4.9-star reputation and "Divorce Without Destruction™" brand mean that prospects who do reach intake arrive with trust already built — a well-run intake process will convert at a very high rate given that head start
The Gap
  • Every prospect who reaches the website after business hours and finds no immediate way to engage is making a decision — and without after-hours coverage, that decision defaults to whoever answers first, which is often a competitor
  • Without a defined follow-up sequence for leads who submit a form but do not immediately schedule, the firm is almost certainly losing warm prospects who needed one more touchpoint before committing
  • A multi-office firm without a documented intake protocol runs the risk of inconsistent client experience — which, in a referral-driven market, directly affects the quality and volume of word-of-mouth that has built this firm's reputation
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Profit Plan Assessment

Revenue, margins, and financial targets for Hargrave Family Law are not publicly available and no discovery call transcript was provided — but the firm's profile (seven attorneys, two offices, Inc. 5000 recognition) indicates a business at the scale where profit planning is no longer optional. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Without a transcript, the firm's financial targets, margins, and what Jennifer actually takes home relative to gross revenue cannot be verified — these are the numbers that determine whether growth is building wealth or just adding overhead
  • A two-office, seven-attorney firm carries significant fixed overhead — lease obligations, staff salaries, insurance, and technology costs that must be planned against revenue targets with precision
  • Inc. 5000 growth recognition is a meaningful achievement, but firms that grow revenue without a profit plan often find that gross revenue increases while owner distributions remain flat — more clients, same pay
  • Without knowing cost per case by practice area and case type, it is impossible to know whether the firm's most time-intensive cases are also its most profitable ones — or whether certain case types are subsidizing others
Blind Spot: The most common financial trap for growing boutique law firms is mistaking revenue milestones for profit milestones. A firm that reaches $1M or $2M in gross revenue but runs at thin margins is not financially secure — it is highly leveraged against its next slow month.
The Positive
  • Inc. 5000 recognition confirms that the firm is on a documented growth trajectory — which means there is real revenue to work with once a profit plan is in place to retain more of it
  • A boutique firm with Jennifer Hargrave's board certification and brand can command above-market case values — which means margin improvement is a real possibility if billing and case selection are optimized
The Gap
  • Without financial visibility — what comes in, what goes out, and what Jennifer keeps — growth decisions are made on instinct rather than data, which is how overhead quietly outpaces revenue
  • A firm at this size without a formal profit plan is almost certainly leaving money on the table in billing efficiency, case mix optimization, or overhead management — and will not know it until the numbers are mapped
  • As the firm grows into its next revenue tier, the gap between gross revenue and owner income will widen unless a deliberate financial structure is in place to manage it
The firm Jennifer Hargrave has built deserves a financial engine that turns revenue growth into personal wealth — not just a bigger payroll and more overhead to manage.
