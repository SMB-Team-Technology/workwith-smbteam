Lead Generation

Hargrave Family Law has built a strong organic foundation — geo-targeted landing pages, an active blog, client video testimonials, and 211 Google reviews at 4.9 stars — but the firm does not appear to be running paid ads on any channel, meaning its entire lead flow depends on prospects finding it without any paid assistance. In the Dallas family law market, where competitors are actively purchasing top-of-page visibility across Google Search, Local Service Ads, and social media, relying on organic alone is leaving a significant volume of high-intent leads to firms with paid presence.

The Gap

A website that works for Jennifer — not just looks good for Jennifer — converts visitors around the clock and feeds the firm without her personal involvement.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Hargrave Family Law does not appear to be running Google Search Ads for divorce, family law, child custody, or collaborative divorce searches in the Dallas market at time of research — the firm may be configured to show for terms not researched, or may be relying entirely on organic traffic
  • The collaborative divorce keyword category — which maps directly to the firm's "Divorce Without Destruction™" brand — is a particularly notable paid gap, as it carries lower competition and higher conversion intent than broad divorce terms
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Powell Law Offices — actively running Google Search Ads with "FREE Consultation" messaging for Dallas divorce and family law terms, capturing high-intent prospects at the moment of search
  • DebnamRust, P.C. — running consultation scheduling-focused ads in the Dallas family law market, creating direct paid competition for the same clients Hargrave serves
  • Modern Family Law — national firm with a Dallas location running active Google Ads, bringing out-of-market spend and infrastructure to compete locally
The Opportunity
  • "Divorce attorney Dallas TX," "collaborative divorce Dallas," and "child custody attorney Dallas" — high-intent searches where the firm has no paid presence but a directly relevant offering
  • McKinney and Collin County family law terms — the McKinney office and geo-targeted pages give Hargrave a geographic authenticity advantage over Dallas-only firms, which translates to lower CPCs and higher Quality Scores in a less competitive sub-market
  • "Collaborative divorce Dallas" is a differentiated search term with lower competition and higher intent — and it maps directly to "Divorce Without Destruction™," giving Hargrave a Quality Score and conversion rate advantage no competitor can easily replicate
  • Competitor name bidding — prospects researching Goranson Bain Ausley or McClure Law Group who see a Hargrave ad during their comparison phase can be pulled into the funnel at a critical decision point
Blind Spot: The Dallas family law market carries a $20–$75 CPC range for search terms — but the "collaborative divorce" category is significantly less competitive and maps directly to the firm's brand. Launching ads in this category first would generate leads at lower cost while establishing paid presence before competitors recognize the opportunity.
