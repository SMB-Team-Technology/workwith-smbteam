Lead Generation

Polchinski & Smith has real organic momentum for its core personal injury and car accident searches, but has no paid presence anywhere and no organic visibility at all for two of the practice areas it actively markets — truck accidents and workers' compensation.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed mobile and desktop scores could not be retrieved during this research pass (the PageSpeed Insights API returned rate-limit errors across six attempts) — recommend a quick manual check before the next review rather than relying on an estimate
  • No live chat is present — after-hours visitors have no way to get an immediate response
  • No video content on the site, despite the partners having real case results (including a $2.1M motorcycle accident recovery and a $1.2M semi-truck accident recovery) they could use to build trust before the phone ever rings
  • Every contact point on the site — hero, mid-page, and footer — funnels into the same personal intake queue the partners already manage themselves
Blind Spot: A faster, better-designed website will only produce more calls into the same bottleneck — the partners personally handling every intake. Website improvements and intake delegation have to move together, or the extra leads just add to the backlog.
The Positive
  • The site is modern and clean, launched in February 2025 — there is no redesign debt to work around
  • All three partners have individual bios with credentials and bar admissions, which builds real trust before a prospect calls
  • The blog is active and current, with posts as recent as July 14, 2026 — a real content investment most firms never sustain
The Gap
  • Without confirmed PageSpeed data, mobile load speed is an unknown risk in a market where competitors are actively bidding for these same searches
  • No video means prospects can't build trust with the partners before they call — a missed opportunity given the firm's strong case results
  • Every improvement to the website's conversion rate makes the intake bottleneck worse until intake itself is fixed
A modern website is already a genuine asset here — the partners just need a team behind it that can convert what it brings in without personally answering every call.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Google Business Profile shows 4.8 stars and 47 reviews — a strong rating, but far fewer reviews than every named competitor in this market
  • The firm appears organically for general personal injury and car accident searches, but does not appear at all across three tested truck accident queries or three tested workers' compensation queries
  • NAP inconsistency found: the homepage lists (405) 643-8118, while multiple third-party directories (including the Chamber of Commerce) list (405) 500-4000 — a citation inconsistency worth cleaning up even if it is an intentional call-tracking number
Blind Spot: That phone number inconsistency across directories can quietly suppress local map-pack rankings, even when it is intentional call tracking — it is worth confirming and standardizing.
The Positive
  • Geo-targeted landing pages already exist for Edmond, Stillwater, Enid, Moore, Norman, Lawton, and Midwest City — a level of local page coverage most firms never build
The Gap
  • Zero visibility for workers' compensation and truck accident searches means competitors are capturing cases in practice areas this firm already handles
  • A 47-review base limits how competitive the map pack ranking can be against firms carrying 175–343 reviews
Dan Davis Law — ranks organically for "workers compensation lawyer" searches in every query tested, a term Polchinski & Smith does not appear for at all.
Every workers' comp or truck accident case lost to search invisibility is revenue the firm has already earned the right to compete for — and can't get back.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • This firm does not appear to be running Google search ads for personal injury, car accident, truck accident, or workers' compensation terms — they may not be configured to show for these terms, or may be getting outbid by competitors
  • No firm-name bid protection was observed, leaving the door open for competitors to bid on "Polchinski Smith" branded searches
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • McIntyre Law, Abel Law Firm, and Dan Davis Law — established, well-funded firms in this market that typically compete aggressively on paid search for these same terms; live ad-slot data was not accessible this research pass, so a manual confirmation is recommended
The Opportunity
  • "Oklahoma City car accident lawyer" and "Oklahoma City personal injury lawyer" — the exact terms the firm already ranks organically for, backed up with paid placement
  • "Oklahoma City truck accident lawyer" and "Oklahoma workers comp attorney" — the two practice areas with zero current visibility of any kind
  • Competitor conquesting campaigns targeting McIntyre Law, Dan Davis Law, and Abel Law Firm brand searches
  • Geo-expansion terms for Edmond, Moore, Norman, and the other cities the firm already has landing pages built for
Blind Spot: With no Google Ads running at all, there is no paid presence to fall back on the moment organic rankings shift — which has already happened in truck accidents and workers' compensation.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • This firm does not appear to hold Local Service Ads placement or a Google Screened badge at this time — live LSA rendering was not accessible this research pass, so a manual check is recommended given how common LSA placement is in the Oklahoma City PI market
  • A 4.8-star rating on 47 reviews already clears the bar most firms need to launch a strong LSA profile
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Live LSA rendering was not accessible during this research pass — a manual check is recommended, since LSA placement is common among established Oklahoma City PI firms such as McIntyre Law and Abel Law Firm
The Opportunity
  • With a 4.8-star rating already in hand, this firm is positioned to launch Local Service Ads immediately rather than needing to build up reviews first
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • Pay-per-lead pricing means every dollar spent only counts when a real prospect calls — a lower-risk way to test paid channels than a full PPC campaign
Blind Spot: LSA is one of the fastest channels to launch — most firms can be live within one to two weeks of profile verification, well inside the partners' preference for a short trial before any longer commitment.

Facebook & Instagram Ads

Ad Presence
  • This firm does not appear to be running Facebook or Instagram ads at this time — the Meta Ads Library returned an access-blocked response during this research pass, but no confirmed activity was found in available data
  • No retargeting pixel activity was confirmed either, meaning the firm's growing organic and blog traffic currently has no second chance to convert
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • McIntyre Law — active ad presence could not be confirmed live during this research pass; recommend a manual Ads Library check given their scale and review count
  • Abel Law Firm — same caveat applies; both firms are established enough to be likely active on paid social
The Opportunity
  • Retargeting — website visitors who did not convert are leaving with no follow-up
  • Video ads featuring the attorneys — builds trust before a prospect ever calls
  • Educational content ads — capture leads earlier in the decision process
  • The firm's active blog already produces new educational content weekly — repurposing it into short-form video and social ads would require no new content creation, just new distribution
