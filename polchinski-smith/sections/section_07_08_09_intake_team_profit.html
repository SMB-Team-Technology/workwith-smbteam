Intake Assessment

All intake calls and every settlement at Polchinski & Smith run through the three partners personally, with no delegation of any kind in place. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
"The partners are the firm's 'Chief Everything Officers.'"
The Problem
  • No delegation exists — every intake call and every settlement goes through one of the three partners personally
  • No after-hours coverage was mentioned — evening and weekend calls are handled the same way as everything else, by the partners themselves
  • No defined follow-up sequence was mentioned — the firm uses Case Peer for case management, but wants "auto-tasking" to eliminate manual monitoring, meaning today it still requires hands-on oversight from the partners
  • This model is already described as unsustainable and a cause of stalled case progression — the bottleneck exists before a single new lead is added
Blind Spot: The same bottleneck that limited growth at $200K in revenue will limit growth even harder at a $1M+ pace — the math only gets worse from here as more leads arrive.
The Positive
  • The firm already uses Case Peer for case management — the foundation for automation exists; it just needs to be configured to actually reduce manual work
The Gap
  • Every hour spent on intake and settlements is an hour the partners are not spending on higher-value casework or their own time — the direct cost of remaining the firm's "Chief Everything Officers"
  • As lead volume grows from new marketing efforts, the same two or three people processing every file become the ceiling on growth, not the floor
  • No stated close rate exists, meaning the firm cannot see how much revenue is slipping through the intake process today
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact forms appear above the fold, mid-page, and in the footer — strong visibility, but every submission routes into the same personal intake queue as phone calls
  • No live chat is present on the site, so after-hours website visitors have no way to get an immediate response
  • Given the partners already personally handle every intake call, more contact-form volume without a delegation plan simply adds more inbound to the same bottleneck

Team Assessment

Seven people work at Polchinski & Smith, but the three partners remain personally responsible for every intake call and every settlement — the team has grown, but the operating model underneath it has not. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
"The partners see AI as the key to scaling without adding headcount, seeking expert guidance to automate workflows and free up their time."
The Problem
  • The partners personally manage all intake and settlements, with no manager or delegate identified anywhere on the 7-person team
  • No dedicated ops, marketing, or intake-specific role exists — support staff are in place, but authority and oversight stay with the partners
  • The stated 12-month goal is to hire another attorney — but adding headcount without changing the operating model adds another person for the partners to personally oversee, not less oversight for them
  • No defined procedures were described as being in place; the partners say they want "clear automated procedures for all staff," which by their own words do not yet exist
Blind Spot: Hiring another attorney without first fixing delegation will only add another person for the partners to personally manage — not free up their time.
The Positive
  • The partners are already actively seeking expert guidance to automate and delegate rather than trying to grow by simply working harder — that mindset is the hardest part of this shift, and it is already in place
The Gap
  • Every week the partners spend personally overseeing intake and settlements is a week they are not building the leadership layer they say they want
  • Revenue is scaling faster than the operating structure underneath it, which raises the risk of case-progression delays as volume grows
  • Without a manager or automated procedures in place, adding staff increases the partners' workload rather than reducing it
Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Profit Plan Assessment

Polchinski & Smith has grown revenue roughly 5x in a year, but no profit, margin, or acquisition-cost figures were discussed on the call — growth so far has been tracked by top-line revenue alone. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No profitability or margin figures were discussed on the call — the firm has no stated view of what it keeps versus what it earns
  • No stated average case value or close rate exists, meaning marketing return cannot yet be measured with real precision
  • Revenue jumped from $200K to a $1M+ pace with no mention of updated financial controls or reporting to match that scale
  • No dedicated ops or financial oversight role exists on the 7-person team
Blind Spot: The fastest-growing period of a firm's life is often when the least profit tracking exists — by the time the numbers get confusing, the bad habits are already built in.
The Positive
  • The firm has already proven it can grow revenue fast with no paid marketing at all — real proof that a system, once added, has a strong foundation to build on
The Gap
  • Without visibility into case profitability, the firm cannot tell which practice area — general PI, truck accidents, or workers' comp — is actually the most profitable to grow
  • No acquisition-cost tracking means new ad spend can be measured on revenue generated, but not yet on true return
  • Continued growth without a profit plan risks the classic law firm trap: more revenue, more overhead, the same amount left over
Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.
