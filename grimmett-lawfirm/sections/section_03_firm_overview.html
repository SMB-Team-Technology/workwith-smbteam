Here Is Where Grimmett Law Firm Stands Today

David Grimmett has spent 17 years building a Middle Tennessee personal injury, bad faith, and child custody practice — but nearly every new client still arrives through a single referral relationship with "Shannara" in Alabama, at a referral fee of roughly 50% per case. With a 156-case load that is 80% low-value, and attorney's fees of $2,500–$5,000 running against referral costs of $3,000–$4,000, the firm is often working cases at a loss under its current model. The single most important finding: Grimmett Law Firm has no working digital lead generation system of its own, which means its growth is capped by what one outside referral source decides to send it.

You told us the goal is $10M in revenue, 10 lawyers, and a 15% profit margin — a firm built to scale and dominate its market. Stage 6 is where that ambition becomes reality, and it starts with building the systems Stage 3 firms don't yet have.

Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Grimmett Law Firm
Grimmett Law Firm is operating at Stage 3 — a Solo Practitioner model where one attorney and a lean support team (one associate, one EA, one paralegal, one VA) carry a 156-case load sourced almost entirely from a single outside referral relationship. The single most urgent gap limiting growth is the absence of any independent lead generation system: with referral costs running $3,000–$4,000 against attorney's fees of $2,500–$5,000, much of the current caseload is break-even or worse before overhead is even considered. Until Grimmett Law Firm builds its own reliable channels for new clients, the stated goal of $10M in revenue, 10 lawyers, and a 15% profit margin within 3–5 years will remain structurally out of reach — no matter how hard the current team works.
