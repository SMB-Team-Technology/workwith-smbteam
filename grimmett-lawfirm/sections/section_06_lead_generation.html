Lead Generation

Grimmett Law Firm has built a 17-year reputation across personal injury, bad faith, and child custody work — but nearly every new client still arrives through a single outside referral relationship rather than any system the firm controls itself. With no working digital lead generation and named local competitors dominating organic search, the firm's growth is capped by what one referral source decides to send it.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed scores could not be pulled this session (API quota limitation) — a manual mobile and desktop check is recommended before assuming performance is not costing leads
  • The contact form ("Get a Free Case Evaluation") is buried at the bottom of the homepage rather than above the fold
  • No live video content on the site, despite David's 17 years of trial experience being a strong trust asset
  • Blog has not been updated in roughly 18 months — the most recent post (January 2025) covers Bad Faith, one of the firm's three practice areas, but no follow-up content has been published since
Blind Spot: The site's blog is stale exactly where the firm has real subject-matter authority — Bad Faith — meaning competitors like McWherter Scott & Bobbitt, who maintain an active Tennessee-wide bad faith practice, have more current content in front of prospects at the exact moment they're searching.
The Positive
  • David Grimmett's attorney bio appears above the fold, describing him as a "seasoned trial lawyer with 17 years of experience" — a strong trust signal most competitors bury deeper in their sites
  • The homepage headline ("You Deserve The Win") and free consultation CTA both appear above the fold in the header navigation
The Gap
  • A buried contact form means visitors who are ready to act have to scroll past the entire homepage to reach it — some percentage of them leave instead
  • 18 months of blog silence on Bad Faith means the firm is not capturing organic search traffic for a practice area it has already demonstrated expertise in
  • Without confirmed PageSpeed data, it is not possible to rule out load-time issues as a silent source of lost mobile leads
A website that captures and converts visitors on its own is one less thing standing between Grimmett Law Firm and the client volume it needs to reach $10M.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Google Business Profile is mis-categorized as "General Law" rather than "Personal Injury Attorney" — the firm's actual core practice area
  • Yelp listing is separately mis-categorized under "Divorce & Family Law"
  • Directory NAP is inconsistent: FindLaw lists a Nashville address (315 Deaderick Street, Regions Center) while the firm's own site and Yelp listing use the Franklin, TN address (3326 Aspen Grove Dr)
  • Grimmett Law Firm was not observed in top organic results for "personal injury lawyer Franklin TN," "bad faith insurance lawyer Franklin TN," or "child custody lawyer Franklin TN" this session
Blind Spot: The GBP and Yelp category mismatches mean Google and Yelp themselves may be actively steering searchers looking for personal injury help toward competitors, since the platforms don't associate Grimmett Law Firm with that category in the first place.
The Positive
  • The firm's GBP proxy shows a 4.4-star rating, a solid foundation to build from once category and NAP issues are resolved
The Gap
  • Category and NAP inconsistencies are likely suppressing visibility for the firm's highest-value practice area at the exact moment prospects are searching for it
  • A ~27-review profile is well behind the review counts competitors are showing in the same market
Puryear, Newman & Morton, PLLC — 66 reviews at 4.9 stars, nearly three decades established, with multi-practice depth spanning family and injury work that lets them show up for more of the searches Grimmett Law Firm's own three practice areas should be capturing.
Fixing the category and NAP issues costs nothing but attention — and it is standing directly between Grimmett Law Firm and the searches its ideal clients are already running.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Grimmett Law Firm does not appear to be running Google search ads for personal injury, bad faith, or child custody terms at present — this session's tools could not render the live sponsored SERP block, so this reads as an opportunity gap rather than a confirmed absence
  • No ad budget, platform, or campaign history was described in the discovery call for any practice area
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Griffith Law — active ad presence not independently confirmed this session, but their strong organic visibility for "personal injury lawyer Franklin TN" suggests an established digital footprint worth a manual SERP check
  • The Williams Firm — active ad presence not independently confirmed this session; their 5.0-star, 81-review profile positions them well if they are running paid search alongside organic visibility
The Opportunity
  • "personal injury lawyer Franklin TN" and "car accident lawyer Nashville" — obvious, high-intent keywords the firm is not currently bidding on
  • "bad faith insurance lawyer Franklin TN" — directly aligned with the firm's own recent blog content, but not currently captured through paid search
  • Competitor-name conquesting (Griffith Law, The Williams Firm) — capturing prospects who search a competitor by name but haven't committed yet
  • Neighboring-city terms (Brentwood, Murfreesboro, Nashville) — capturing regional searches beyond Franklin proper
Blind Spot: Every month without Google Ads is a month the firm relies entirely on one referral relationship — while competitors capture the paid search traffic actively looking for a personal injury attorney right now.
