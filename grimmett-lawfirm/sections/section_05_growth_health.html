Pillar Health Scorecard

Lead Generation

  • New clients come almost entirely through one referral source ("Shannara," Alabama) at a roughly 50% referral fee
  • No paid Google Ads, LSA, or Meta Ads activity currently produces new clients
  • GBP is mis-categorized as "General Law" instead of "Personal Injury Attorney," actively suppressing visibility for the firm's core practice area
Every case that comes through a 50% referral fee is a case that could have been full-margin — the fuel a firm needs to scale to $10M does not come from a single outside source.

Intake

  • Roughly 20% of 260–300 weekly inbound calls go unanswered
  • Contact form on the website is buried at the bottom of the homepage rather than above the fold
  • No live chat or after-hours capture mechanism observed
A firm that can't answer one in five calls cannot reliably grow revenue — no amount of new lead flow fixes a leak this size.

Self-Managing Team

  • A support team exists — one associate, one EA, one paralegal, one VA — but David remains the central figure in the practice
  • The stated goal of 10 lawyers within 3–5 years requires a leadership structure that does not yet exist
  • No mention of managers or delegated authority beyond the current small team
Scaling to 10 lawyers means building a team that can run cases without David personally carrying each one.

Profit

  • Annual revenue is roughly $900,000 and described as "on track," but the goal is $10M within 3–5 years
  • Case economics are slim to negative per case: $2,500–$5,000 attorney's fee against a $3,000–$4,000 referral cost
  • Target profit margin (15%) has not yet been mapped against a concrete plan
Revenue growth means little toward a 15% margin goal if a large share of cases are being paid away in referral fees before they ever reach the bottom line.
