Here Is Where Grimmett Law Firm Stands Today

David Grimmett has spent 17 years building a Middle Tennessee personal injury, bad faith, and child custody practice — but nearly every new client still arrives through a single referral relationship with "Shannara" in Alabama, at a referral fee of roughly 50% per case. With a 156-case load that is 80% low-value, and attorney's fees of $2,500–$5,000 running against referral costs of $3,000–$4,000, the firm is often working cases at a loss under its current model. The single most important finding: Grimmett Law Firm has no working digital lead generation system of its own, which means its growth is capped by what one outside referral source decides to send it.

You told us the goal is $10M in revenue, 10 lawyers, and a 15% profit margin — a firm built to scale and dominate its market. Stage 6 is where that ambition becomes reality, and it starts with building the systems Stage 3 firms don't yet have.

Where You Are Today

Stage 3: Solo Practitioner
  • New clients come almost entirely through one referral source, at a roughly 50% fee

  • 156 active cases, 80% of them low-value, with attorney's fees ($2,500–$5,000) often undercut by referral costs ($3,000–$4,000)
  • Roughly 20% of 260–300 weekly inbound calls go unanswered

Where You Need To Be

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Profit Plan Assessment

Grimmett Law Firm's annual revenue is roughly $900,000 and described as "on track" toward a $10M goal — but with attorney's fees of $2,500–$5,000 running against referral costs of $3,000–$4,000 per case, growth under the current model may not translate into more profit.
