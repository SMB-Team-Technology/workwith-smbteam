Intake Assessment

Moore Christoff & Siddiqui converts at an exceptional rate once they reach a prospect — Sam stated 80–90% of consults result in a signed client. The gap is not in the close, it is in the speed and reliability of the first contact. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Speed-to-lead gaps during trial weeks — when partners are unavailable, inbound criminal defense inquiries wait while PNA Law and Price Benowitz offer 24/7 attorney availability as a direct competitive message
  • No after-hours intake coverage — a DUI or criminal arrest happens at midnight or on weekends, and a prospect who cannot reach anyone immediately calls the next firm on the list
  • Contact form is only accessible from the dedicated Contact page, not from the homepage or practice area pages — reducing the number of inquiries the website captures
  • No structured follow-up sequence for leads who inquire but do not immediately schedule a consultation — without a follow-up protocol, warm leads go cold and cases are lost without ever being counted
Blind Spot: DUI and serious criminal charges are emotional emergencies — the moment of arrest, bond hearing, or court notice is when a prospect's willingness to hire is highest. That moment often happens after 9 p.m. or on weekends. The firm that answers first — not the most credentialed firm — gets that case. PNA Law built its "24/7 attorney availability" message around this exact vulnerability.
The Positive
  • Sam stated an 80–90% close rate when the firm actually speaks with a prospect — this is an exceptional conversion rate that means intake fixes have enormous leverage
  • The website includes case-type categorization in the contact form, which helps route inquiries — the infrastructure for better intake exists and can be built on
The Gap
  • An 80–90% close rate is irrelevant for any lead that does not get a timely first response — every delayed callback is a case the firm already had and gave away
  • Without after-hours coverage, the firm is only competing for cases during business hours in a practice area where emergencies happen around the clock
  • Without a follow-up protocol for unconverted inquiries, the firm has no visibility into how many leads came in, how many were reached, and how many were lost — making it impossible to improve
Until intake is systematized, Sam cannot hand off client acquisition responsibility — a firm that depends on partner availability to convert leads is not a firm he can run as CEO.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact form is located on a dedicated Contact page only — not embedded on the homepage, practice area pages, or attorney bio pages where a prospect would naturally look
  • The form categorizes by case type, which is useful for routing — but requires the prospect to navigate off their landing page before they can submit an inquiry
  • No immediate engagement mechanism on the homepage above the fold — the only CTAs above the fold are "About Us," "Practice Areas," and "Meet Our Team," which send the prospect deeper into research rather than toward a consultation request

These findings are also referenced in the Website Audit section.

Team Assessment

Moore Christoff & Siddiqui has 11–12 staff and 8 attorneys — more infrastructure than most firms at this revenue level. The challenge is structural: three co-equal partners are each managing legal work and firm operations simultaneously, with no single person owning growth strategy or day-to-day administration. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Three co-equal partners means firm decisions — including growth investments, operational changes, and the Fairfax office expansion — require consensus rather than having a single decision-maker who can move quickly
  • LSA account setup was handled in-house by the partners rather than delegated, taking attorney time away from casework and firm strategy to manage an administrative task
  • No dedicated intake, marketing, or operations manager confirmed — administrative and growth functions are absorbed by partner and attorney time rather than staff responsibility
  • Opening the Fairfax office at the end of 2025 required significant partner attention — the firm is managing two physical locations without the operational infrastructure to scale both independently
Blind Spot: The fact that three experienced attorneys have built a $2M firm by raw effort and individual excellence is genuinely impressive — but it is also the problem. The firm works because of the partners, not despite them, which means any of the three stepping back creates a gap. A firm that requires its partners to function is a firm that will always require its partners. That is the ceiling Sam is approaching.
The Positive
  • With 11–12 total staff and 8 attorneys, the firm has more human infrastructure than most practices at this revenue level — the foundation for a self-managing structure is partially in place
  • Three partners with complementary expertise (criminal defense, family law, civil litigation) gives the firm the depth to support a managing partner structure without losing practice area coverage
The Gap
  • Without a managing partner or dedicated operations leader owning growth strategy, the firm's growth rate will continue to be limited by the partners' willingness to add more responsibilities to already full plates
  • The Fairfax office is a real growth opportunity in a priority market — but capturing it fully requires leadership bandwidth and administrative support the current structure does not have in reserve
  • A firm that cannot run at full capacity for 30 days without all three partners present is still a partnership of attorneys, not yet a business with independent operating systems
Sam wants to run this firm as a CEO, not as its hardest-working employee — and that transition becomes possible only when the team structure exists to support it.

Profit Plan Assessment

Moore Christoff & Siddiqui is approaching $2 million in revenue with consistent year-over-year growth — but revenue without profit visibility is not a plan, it is a hope. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No confirmed system for tracking profit by practice area, case type, or attorney — reinvestment decisions are being made without data on what each case type actually costs and returns
  • DUI cases average $4,800 while reckless driving cases average $1,200 — a 4:1 revenue difference with major profit implications, but without case-type tracking, the firm cannot confirm whether it is optimizing for its most profitable work
  • Reinvestment decisions, including the Fairfax office opening and the in-house LSA setup, were made without established ROI benchmarks to measure performance against
  • The $2.8M–$3.0M revenue target for end of 2027 has not been paired with a corresponding profit goal — hitting revenue without protecting margins means more cases and more overhead, not necessarily more owner income
Blind Spot: Reckless driving cases at $1,200 require meaningful attorney time relative to their fee. Without tracking time-per-case and revenue-per-case-type side by side, the firm could be filling attorney capacity with its least profitable work while higher-value DUI and felony leads go to competitors. Knowing the answer to this question changes which cases to prioritize in marketing, which the firm does not currently track.
The Positive
  • Sam's explicit expectation that any investment must pay for itself near-term reflects real financial discipline — this is the beginning of a profit mindset and a foundation that makes a profit plan achievable quickly
  • Consistent year-over-year growth toward $2M shows the firm is generating real revenue — the problem to solve is not growth, it is converting that growth into predictable owner wealth
The Gap
  • Without a profit plan tied to the revenue goal, growing from $2M to $3M could mean more cases, more staff, and more overhead — with no improvement in what each partner actually takes home
  • Sam's stated requirement for near-term ROI cannot be measured or validated without baseline data on cost-per-lead and cost-per-case — making marketing investment decisions feel like guesswork
  • Without profit tracking by case type, the firm cannot confidently allocate its marketing budget toward the cases that generate the highest return — a direct obstacle to hitting the 2027 revenue goal profitably
The path from $2M to $3M runs through profit clarity — knowing what each case actually earns means Sam can grow the right cases, not just more cases.
