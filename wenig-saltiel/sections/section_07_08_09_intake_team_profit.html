Team Assessment

Jeffrey Saltiel is semi-retired, yet he is still the one personally reviewing every pre-bill by hand, twice a month, three hours at a time. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
"3-hour, bi-weekly manual review of paper pre-bills" — reduced to "~1 hour" is the explicit target Jeffrey described for this engagement.
The Problem
  • Jeffrey personally performs a 3-hour, bi-weekly manual review of a 3-inch stack of paper pre-bills — with no staff delegation or automation in place
  • Document assembly is also handled manually, adding further hands-on time to his workload
  • Billing runs through the firm's legacy ProLaw system with no modern workflow layered on top of it
  • No process exists today to catch pre-bill errors before they reach Jeffrey's desk — he is the entire review system himself
Blind Spot: Because Jeffrey has always done this review himself, it can be easy to treat 3 hours every two weeks as simply the cost of running the firm — rather than as roughly 78 hours a year that could be given back to him.
The Gap
  • Every bi-weekly cycle spent manually reviewing paper pre-bills is time Jeffrey does not get back in his semi-retirement
  • Manual document assembly compounds the same time cost across the rest of the firm's caseload
  • A legacy ProLaw billing workflow with no automation layered in means this bottleneck will not resolve itself as the firm continues operating exactly as it always has
This is the single biggest thing standing between Jeffrey and the free time he told SMB Team he wants back — not necessarily to cut costs or grow revenue, but to reclaim his own hours.
