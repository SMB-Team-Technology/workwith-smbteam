Here Is Where Wenig Saltiel LLP Stands Today

Founded by Meryl L. Wenig and Jeffrey L. Saltiel, Wenig Saltiel LLP has represented landlords, foreclosure defense clients, commercial tenants, and real estate litigation clients across Manhattan and Staten Island for more than 25 years, built almost entirely on referrals and reputation. The firm has grown to 8 attorneys with zero paid marketing and no lead generation systems ever deployed — but the single most important finding from this research is not about growth at all: founding partner Jeffrey Saltiel, now semi-retired, still personally performs a 3-hour, bi-weekly manual review of a 3-inch stack of paper pre-bills, with no delegation or automation in place anywhere in the firm's operations.

Jeffrey did not build this firm to spend his semi-retirement buried in a stack of paper pre-bills — closing that gap is exactly where a firm that runs itself begins.

Where You Are Today

  • Document assembly and billing inside the firm's legacy ProLaw system remain manual, with no automation in place

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you

The distance between a 3-hour pre-bill review and a firm that truly runs itself is not about hiring more people — it is about putting the right AI system in place to do the manual work Jeffrey is still doing himself.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Wenig Saltiel LLP
Wenig Saltiel LLP sits at Stage 3 — a firm whose 25-year reputation and 8-attorney roster have never required a single paid marketing system, because referrals alone have always been enough. But the most urgent gap limiting Jeffrey Saltiel's semi-retirement right now has nothing to do with lead volume: it is that he still personally reviews every pre-bill by hand, twice a month, three hours at a time, with no automation standing between him and that stack of paper. Until that changes, adding more clients or more revenue will not give Jeffrey the one thing he actually said he wants — his time back.
