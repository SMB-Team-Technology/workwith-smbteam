The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Intake Assessment

Kemp Injury Law already has real intake coverage in place — but with no way to measure what it's actually catching or losing. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: AMBER
"No formal system tracks total monthly lead volume."
The Problem
  • No formal system tracks total monthly lead volume — nobody can say how many leads are coming in or how many are being lost
  • Adam acknowledges some lead leakage each month, treated as secondary to the bigger problem of insufficient lead volume overall
  • No CRM was mentioned to sit behind the phone/answering-service setup and give visibility into what happens to each lead
  • With Adam treating the firm as a side hustle relative to his other businesses, intake oversight likely competes for his attention with casework and those primary businesses
Blind Spot: Without lead-volume tracking, Adam has no way to know whether the intake system he already built is working well or quietly losing prospects — the marketing rebuild will only pay off if the leads it generates are actually being counted and converted.
The Positive
  • A dedicated staff member carries a phone 24/7, backed by a lead case manager and a call answering service — real after-hours coverage most firms this size do not have
The Gap
  • Every unconverted lead right now is scarcer and more valuable, since overall volume has collapsed to zero — unmeasured leakage costs more than it used to
  • Without lead-volume tracking, Adam cannot tell whether the marketing rebuild is actually increasing signed cases or just increasing calls that still leak out
  • Manual, untracked handoffs between the answering service, the case manager, and casework staff are where leads are most likely lost
Fixing lead generation only matters if the leads it produces are actually converted — otherwise Adam is paying to generate cases he still has to chase personally.
