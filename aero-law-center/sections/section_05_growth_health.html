Lead Generation

Intake

  • The homepage contact form is buried at the bottom of the page, inside the "Free Consultation" section — not visible above the fold where a national prospect first lands
  • No live chat is present on the site, and no after-hours intake coverage was described on the call

A national platform can't afford to let a prospect who found the firm from across the country slip away because the contact form was buried at the bottom of the page.

Self-Managing Team

  • A 13-person team is in place with a dedicated Finance/Revenue Manager (Sean Anglim) — real structural progress most firms this size do not yet have
  • The firm's highest-value, highest-rate work still concentrates on one board-certified attorney billing $700/hour, and he was not available for this discovery call
  • GBP access itself is tied to the principal's personal Gmail account — a small but telling sign of how much still runs through one person
A team large enough to run a national brand cannot yet run it without the principal in every important room — closing that gap is what turns a growing firm into a category-dominant platform that doesn't depend on one calendar.

Profit

  • Revenue is known and stated with confidence (~$3M last year, $5–7M goal this year, $10M long-term) — a level of financial visibility many firms this size lack
  • No stated close rate or cost-per-acquisition benchmark exists outside the PPC-specific historical numbers, making it hard to know which channels are actually profitable
  • Prior PPC spend (~$17,000 in one month) was judged a disappointment in part because of lead mix, not because the underlying math was reviewed against blended case value
Knowing the revenue goal is only half the plan — without visibility into which channels and case types actually drive profit, hitting $5–7M could mean a lot more work without a lot more to show for it.
