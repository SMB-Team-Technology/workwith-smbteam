Here Is Where Aero Law Center Stands Today

Aero Law Center is a Fort Lauderdale-based aviation law firm — litigation, transactional, and regulatory work for aircraft owners, MROs, and Part 135 operators nationally — that grew to roughly $3M in revenue last year on relationships, referrals, conferences, and weekly AeroShield webinars, with a 13-person remote team already in place. The single most important finding: the firm just ended its only paid SEO program over poor ROI, its Google Ads account is currently paused on a payment issue, and its most recent blog content is over a year old — meaning the firm is trying to reach a $5–7M goal this year with fewer active lead generation systems than it had twelve months ago.

Stage 6 is the stage where a firm built to be "the largest aviation law firm in the United States" actually runs like one — a national platform that dominates its category without every matter routing back through one attorney's calendar.

Where You Are Today

STAGE 4: SMALL BUSINESS MANAGER
  • Growth to date has come almost entirely from relationships, referrals, conferences, PR, and the AeroShield webinar funnel — not from a paid, repeatable lead generation system
  • The one paid channel that was tested at scale (PPC) is remembered as a disappointment, in part because many of those leads converted into lower-value demand-letter matters rather than the firm's higher-value transactional work
  • A 13-person team and a dedicated Finance/Revenue Manager are in place, but the firm's highest-value work still runs through one board-certified attorney billing $700/hour
  • Seven state pages and four city pages are live, yet the two cities named as priority targets on the call — Orlando and Atlanta — have no page at all

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
Closing the gap between a firm growing on referrals and webinars and a firm that dominates its category nationally is not about working harder — it is about rebuilding the paid lead generation systems the firm just let lapse, before the goal of $5–7M this year quietly slips out of reach.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Aero Law Center
Aero Law Center is operating at Stage 4 — a Small Business Manager with a real team and a real brand, but without a scalable, currently-active lead generation engine to match its ambitions. The single most urgent gap limiting growth right now is lead generation: the paid SEO retainer ended at the new year, the Google Ads account is paused on a payment issue, and the firm's most recently indexed blog content is over a year old. Until that gap closes, the jump from ~$3M to the stated $5–7M goal — let alone the $10M long-term target — has no engine capable of producing it.
