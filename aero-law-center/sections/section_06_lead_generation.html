Lead Generation

Aero Law Center has grown to roughly $3M on relationships, referrals, conferences, and the AeroShield webinar funnel — with SEO never core to the strategy and now, at the exact moment the firm is targeting $5–7M, its two paid channels are either recently ended or paused.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed mobile and desktop scores could not be retrieved this session (API quota and rendering limitations) — a manual pagespeed.web.dev check is recommended before this is cited as a specific number in future reporting
  • The primary contact form is buried at the bottom of the homepage inside the "Free Consultation" section — not visible above the fold
  • Two of the prospect's own named priority expansion cities — Orlando and Atlanta — have no landing page at all, despite 7 state pages and 4 city pages already being live
  • No dedicated Aircraft Lien page exists; lien and title issues are addressed only inside the broader Aircraft Transaction page, despite liens and demand letters being described as a common litigation entry point
Blind Spot: The firm's own blog was a paid deliverable — 6 articles/month was part of the $8,600/mo SEO contract — but only about 5 posts total were ever indexed, and the most recent is over a year old. That gap between what was paid for and what was delivered may still be costing search visibility today, even though the relationship itself has ended.
The Positive
  • The site carries a 2025/2026 copyright, has a clear above-the-fold CTA ("Start Your Free Consultation Today"), embedded video, and individual bio pages for all 13 team members — the foundation of a credible, modern site is already in place
  • Geo-targeting pages already exist for 7 states and 4 cities, which is more built-out than a "thin city coverage" firm typically has at this stage
The Gap
  • Every visitor who searches for "aircraft lien lawyer" and lands on a page without a dedicated lien page is being sent to generic transaction content instead of the exact answer they searched for
  • Every prospect from Orlando or Atlanta — the two cities named as priorities on this call — currently has no page built for their market at all
  • A buried contact form means some percentage of visitors who are ready to reach out simply never scroll far enough to find how
A national platform needs a website built to catch every state, every city, and every practice area it wants to dominate — right now, the firm's own stated priority markets are the exact gaps in its own site.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Google Business Profile access is tied to the principal's personal Gmail, and GBP category/services are likely incomplete per the transcript
  • GBP photos and posts activity could not be confirmed live this session — flagged for a manual check, including whether the cover photo has been updated (transcript notes it is not currently optimized)
  • Two named legal entities (Aero Law Center and Ewing Legal Group, P.A.) and two different phone numbers appear across BBB, Yelp, and the firm's own site — a NAP inconsistency that can confuse directory and GBP matching
Blind Spot: The firm's 190 Google reviews at 4.9 stars is a genuinely strong, verifiable number — but none of the three named competitors researched this session (Tripp Scott, Aviation Legal Group, Lapayowker Jet Counsel) show an aggregated Google review count on any indexed platform. That means the firm may already be sitting on its single best differentiator without using it anywhere in paid marketing.
The Positive
  • 190 Google reviews at a 4.9-star average, with the most recent review posted April 29, 2026 — a real, current, and verifiable reputation asset
The Gap
  • A strong review profile that isn't reflected in a clean, consistent GBP listing (correct category, current photos, consistent NAP) is not doing the work it could be doing to win local and national aviation-law searches
  • Aero Law Center appears alongside Aviation Legal Group, Tripp Scott, and Lapayowker Jet Counsel in nearly every practice-area search performed this session — meaning it is already competing in these results, just without a fully optimized profile behind it
Aviation Legal Group, P.A. — nearly 30 years of tenure (AV Rated Preeminent since 1997) gives it a trust signal in search and directory listings that a firm with an incomplete GBP profile and inconsistent NAP data can't yet match, regardless of review count.
A national category leader's local presence has to be as clean and consistent as its ambition — right now, two business names and two phone numbers across public listings is the kind of gap competitors like Aviation Legal Group don't have.
