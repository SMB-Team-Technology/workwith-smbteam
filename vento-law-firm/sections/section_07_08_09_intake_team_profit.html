Intake Assessment

Vento Law Firm has no intake system in place — Idalis personally handles every inquiry, the website contact form is buried below the fold, and there is no after-hours coverage in a practice area where clients call during an active criminal emergency. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Idalis personally handles every intake call — when she is in court, with a client, or unavailable, calls go unanswered with no backup system
  • No after-hours coverage of any kind — criminal defense arrests happen at all hours; a call at 11pm that reaches voicemail will be a client calling a competitor by morning
  • Website contact form is below the fold — prospects in crisis who do not scroll never see a path to contact the firm
  • Prior attempt with Docket Flow produced zero clients from approximately eight leads — unqualified leads expecting free consultations suggest no intake qualification process was in place to convert them
Blind Spot: Criminal defense clients are not shopping — they are in crisis. The average person arrested at 10pm is not waiting until business hours to find an attorney; they are calling the first firm they can reach right now. Every unanswered after-hours call is not a delayed conversion — it is a permanent loss to whoever answers first.
The Gap
  • Every missed call in a $3,000–$15,000+ practice area represents a direct revenue loss — at a 15% close rate, even 5 missed calls per month is a potential $2,250–$11,250 in uncaptured monthly revenue
  • Without a defined intake process and qualification script, the firm cannot distinguish between a qualified DUI client and an unqualified lead expecting a free consultation — leading to wasted time and skewed conversion metrics
  • No intake data means no way to measure which lead sources are actually converting — making it impossible to optimize marketing spend as ad campaigns launch
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact form is located mid-to-lower page in a section titled "Schedule Your Confidential Consultation" — not visible on homepage arrival without scrolling
  • No live chat or chat widget observed — for a criminal defense practice that explicitly markets 24/7 availability, there is no real-time digital contact option outside business hours
  • No click-to-call button visible above the fold — a prospect on mobile in a crisis situation has no one-tap path to reach the firm on first view of the site

Team Assessment

Vento Law Firm is a single-attorney operation with no support staff of any kind — Idalis handles every client call, every court appearance, every piece of casework, and every administrative function of the firm herself. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Zero staff — there is no separation between attorney and firm; the business stops when Idalis stops
  • Every intake call, consultation, court appearance, and administrative task competes for the same finite hours — there is no capacity to grow without adding people
  • No accountability structure, no defined roles, and no hiring plan currently in place — growth requires hiring, and hiring requires a plan
  • Idalis has stated a clear goal to hire a full-time assistant and dedicated intake staff — but that goal cannot be executed until the firm first generates enough consistent revenue to fund the hires
Blind Spot: The instinct to hire after revenue is stable is exactly right — but the sequence matters. Hiring intake first, before a general assistant, multiplies the return on every marketing dollar spent. An intake coordinator converts more of the leads coming in; a general assistant helps after the firm is already converting well.
The Positive
  • Idalis has a clear vision of the team she needs — intake coordinator, assistant, eventually multiple attorneys — and has articulated a realistic two-year hiring roadmap
The Gap
  • A solo attorney handling every function cannot increase case volume without increasing personal hours — there is a hard ceiling on growth without staff
  • Without dedicated intake support, every new marketing-generated lead lands in the same bottleneck: Idalis personally answering a call while also managing active cases
  • The longer hiring is deferred, the longer Idalis remains personally responsible for every function — and the longer the firm runs on hours rather than systems
Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Profit Plan Assessment

Vento Law Firm is operating in a revenue contraction — active case volume has fallen from approximately 25 matters to 10, producing a financial situation Idalis described as unsustainable, with no predictable revenue floor and no profit plan in place. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Case volume has dropped from approximately 25 active matters to roughly 10 — the revenue trajectory is downward without intervention
  • Revenue is entirely dependent on referrals, which are unpredictable by nature — there is no financial planning horizon because there is no reliable lead source to forecast from
  • No financial tracking or profit planning system in place — without knowing revenue by case type, cost per client acquired, and overhead against collections, profit is discovered rather than planned
  • Without average case value data by type, it is impossible to know whether felony cases, DUI cases, or misdemeanor cases are driving the most profit relative to time invested — making case selection and marketing prioritization guesswork
Blind Spot: The goal to purchase an office building requires a specific and predictable profit margin over a sustained period — not just higher revenue. Without a profit plan that tracks what is kept after overhead, taxes, and operating costs, higher revenue from more cases could still fall short of what is needed for a meaningful real estate investment. Knowing the numbers is what makes the building purchase a goal with a date, not a dream.
The Gap
  • Operating without a revenue floor means every business decision — hiring, marketing, office — is made under financial uncertainty rather than from a position of clarity
  • Without profit tracking by case type, case selection is based on availability rather than profitability — the firm may be taking its most time-consuming cases without knowing they generate the lowest return per hour
  • The gap between $180K current revenue and the sustained profitability needed to fund a building purchase cannot be closed without first building a system that makes revenue predictable
A clear profit plan is what turns revenue growth into the financial security and independence Idalis built this firm to achieve — not just more cases, but the right cases, priced right, with the overhead clarity to know exactly what she is keeping.
