Intake Assessment

Wil Alexander is still personally handling pre-suit negotiations rather than delegating them to an associate or intake staff, and the firm has no documented tracking system for measuring how leads move through the pipeline. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Pre-suit negotiations are handled personally by Wil rather than by a delegated team member — a task he has explicitly identified as one to hand off to a future associate attorney
  • No CRM or intake tracking system confirmed — the firm could not measure ROI on its own past paid advertising because of this same gap
  • No documented after-hours coverage identified for inbound leads generated by billboards or Facebook
  • No defined follow-up sequence confirmed for leads who do not sign immediately
Blind Spot: Without a tracking system, Wil has no way of knowing how many of the leads generated by billboards and Facebook are being lost simply because no one followed up quickly enough.
The Gap
  • Every pre-suit negotiation Wil handles personally is an hour not spent on higher-value casework or stepping back from the business — directly tied to the ~$520K/yr opportunity cost he estimated on the call
  • Without tracking, leads generated by the firm's strong reputation may be quietly falling through the cracks with no visibility into where
  • Inconsistent follow-up on a lead base this strong (121 five-star reviews) means the firm is very likely leaving signed cases on the table it already paid to generate
Fixing intake is the fastest way to get Wil out of pre-suit negotiations and back the hours he needs for a real two-week vacation.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

Team Assessment

Alexander & Alexander has grown to 5 total team members — Wil and Carson Alexander plus 3 support staff — but Wil is still working a 60-hour week with an estimated 20 hours/week tied up in low-value operational tasks. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • 5-person team (2 attorneys, 3 support staff) is a real foundation, but Wil is still the one absorbing pre-suit negotiation work rather than a delegated associate
  • Wil estimated roughly 20 hours/week on low-value operational tasks at an assumed $500/hr blended value — approximately $520K/year in opportunity cost
  • No associate attorney currently in place to take pre-suit negotiations off Wil's plate
  • No accountability structure or management layer described between Wil and the rest of the team
Blind Spot: Because Wil has run the firm this way for years, the 60-hour week may feel normal rather than optional — until he sees what a properly delegated pre-suit process actually frees up.
The Positive
  • Carson Alexander already shares attorney-level responsibility, giving the firm a real leadership foundation to build a self-managing structure on top of
The Gap
  • Roughly $520K/year in Wil's time is tied up in tasks that do not require his personal involvement
  • Without a delegated intake and negotiation process, adding more leads only adds more hours to Wil's week rather than more freedom
  • No documented path yet for a future associate attorney to absorb pre-suit negotiations
Building a team that can run pre-suit negotiations and daily operations without Wil is the single biggest unlock for the two-week vacation he described wanting.

Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

"~$520k/yr" opportunity cost estimate on Wil's own low-value hours, at an assumed $500/hr value
