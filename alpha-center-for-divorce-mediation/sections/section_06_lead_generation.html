Lead Generation

Alpha Center for Divorce Mediation has invested meaningfully in digital infrastructure over its 30+ year history — an active Google Ads campaign with a dedicated call-tracking subdomain, 57+ geo-targeted web pages, and a modern responsive site — but key channels like Local Service Ads and Facebook advertising remain entirely untapped, and the firm's review momentum has stalled while competitors with higher star ratings pull ahead in local search results.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Contact form is located on a separate /contact-us/ page and is not embedded on the homepage — every additional click creates a drop-off point for emotionally-driven divorce inquirers
  • PageSpeed scores: Manual check required — sales rep should run pagespeed.web.dev for alpha-divorce.com before the call; scores were not obtainable during research
  • No pricing information displayed on the website — while common in legal services, competitors like SnapDivorce actively promote flat-fee pricing on Thumbtack, attracting cost-conscious clients who self-select before calling
  • Founder Keila M. Gilbert Esq. does not appear in the current /teams/ active staff listing, which may cause confusion about who leads the firm for first-time visitors researching credibility
Blind Spot: The firm operates two separate domains — alpha-divorce.com (the primary mediation site) and alpharesourcecenter.com, which 301-redirects to divorcerecoverycenter.org, a sister brand focused on divorce recovery counseling. Anyone who searches for "Alpha Resource Center" looking for mediation services may land on the divorce recovery site instead, with no clear pathway back to the law firm — costing leads that the firm's own brand recognition generated.
The Positive
  • Modern, mobile-responsive website with an active blog (last post May 14, 2026) and YouTube video testimonials — meaningful trust signals that build credibility before a prospect ever calls
  • 57+ city-specific landing pages at /locations/ provide strong geographic coverage across Bucks, Montgomery, Chester, Delaware, and Philadelphia counties — a genuine SEO asset most competitors lack
The Gap
  • A homepage with no embedded contact form means the firm is paying for clicks that reach a wall — every dollar of PPC spend that drives a visitor who does not see an immediate form is a dollar with unnecessary friction attached
  • Prospects who arrive after hours have no way to self-qualify — no form above the fold, no live chat — which means the PPC campaign continues to run while leads go uncaptured until the next business day
  • The NAP discrepancy on Martindale ("55 N Main Street" vs. the correct "155 N Main Street") is an active local SEO risk — inconsistent business address data across directories can suppress Google Business Profile rankings and map pack visibility
Every improvement to the website's conversion rate means more families are served — and more revenue generated without adding a single dollar of additional ad spend.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • 39 Google reviews at 4.3 stars — while the highest raw review count among dedicated mediation firms in Doylestown, the rating trails all major competitors: SnapDivorce (22 reviews, 4.8 stars) and Rubin Glickman (69 reviews, 4.8 stars)
  • Review velocity appears stalled — the most recent visible review in the Birdeye-aggregated profile is approximately 4 years old, which is actively hurting map pack rankings as Google's algorithm heavily weights recency
  • No confirmed regular Google Business Profile post activity — a simple monthly content cadence could meaningfully improve local ranking signals at no additional ad cost
Blind Spot: Google's local ranking algorithm heavily weights review recency. A firm with 39 older reviews can be outranked in the map pack by a competitor with 15 recent reviews. SnapDivorce operates from the same Doylestown ZIP code with 22 reviews at 4.8 stars — if their reviews are more recent than Alpha Center's, they may already be ranking above the firm for "divorce mediator near me" searches despite having fewer total reviews.
The Positive
  • A+ BBB rating (accredited June 2025), four active Yelp listings (updated May 2026), and a Martindale profile with a 5.0 peer rating confirm a strong multi-directory presence — the foundation for a systematic review generation program is already in place
The Gap
  • Without a systematic review generation campaign, the gap between Alpha Center's 4.3 stars and competitors' 4.8 stars will only widen — and star rating is the first data point a prospective client sees in a Google Maps result
  • The Martindale address discrepancy (55 vs. 155 N Main) is a quick fix with immediate SEO value — inconsistent NAP data is a known suppressor of local search rankings
Rubin, Glickman, Steinberg & Gifford P.C. — 69 reviews at 4.8 stars with a review confirmed as recently as April 14, 2026. Their review velocity is active and current; Alpha Center's has gone quiet for approximately four years. In local search, recency wins.
A consistent stream of new five-star reviews is how 30 years of reputation becomes visible to someone Googling "divorce mediator near me" for the very first time — and it is one of the highest-return, lowest-cost growth levers available to the firm right now.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Strong evidence of an active Google Ads campaign — the firm operates a dedicated call-tracking subdomain (call.alpha-divorce.com) with dozens of city-specific landing pages using unique local tracking numbers, a hallmark of a structured PPC campaign with conversion tracking already configured
  • Landing page headline confirmed: "Trusted Divorce Attorney in Doylestown, Pennsylvania" — ads appear to target attorney-focused terms rather than mediation-specific terms, which may attract clients expecting traditional litigation and then convert them at a lower rate
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Rubin, Glickman, Steinberg & Gifford P.C. — confirmed appearing in Doylestown divorce attorney searches; their litigation positioning may intercept clients who were initially open to mediation
  • Petrelli Previtera (petrellilaw.com) — confirmed in Doylestown divorce lawyer searches and broader Bucks County family law queries, competing for the same high-intent local traffic
The Opportunity
  • Mediation-specific keywords ("divorce mediator Doylestown," "divorce mediation Bucks County," "affordable divorce Pennsylvania") likely carry lower CPCs than general "divorce attorney" terms while attracting more qualified, higher-converting mediation prospects
  • Expanding campaign coverage to neighboring Bucks County cities (Newtown, Horsham, Lansdale) and the Philadelphia metro market where the firm has active offices — currently underserved by the paid campaign relative to the firm's physical footprint
  • Competitor name targeting — bidding on "SnapDivorce" and local mediation competitor terms captures high-intent prospects who are already comparison-shopping and have shown intent to use mediation specifically
  • The existing call-tracking infrastructure is already built and configured — SMB Team can optimize and expand the current campaign structure rather than starting from scratch, accelerating time to results
Blind Spot: The firm's current PPC landing pages use "divorce attorney" headline language for a service that is fundamentally about avoiding adversarial attorneys. A prospect who clicks an ad promising a "Trusted Divorce Attorney" and lands on a mediation-focused site may experience a mismatch that reduces conversion rate — and increases the cost per acquired case for every dollar spent.

Local Service Ads

  • The firm's BBB A+ rating (accredited since June 2025) and team of licensed attorney-mediators make Google Screened verification straightforward — the credentialing work is largely already done
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Doylestown and Bucks County family law firms — LSA positions for family law and mediator categories in the Philadelphia-area market may currently be held by local litigation firms who are not direct mediation competitors, but are occupying prime real estate
  • SnapDivorce LLC — no confirmed LSA presence; neither Alpha Center nor its closest direct mediation competitor appears to be running LSAs, creating an open first-mover window
The Opportunity
  • Alpha Center's BBB A+ accreditation and licensed attorneys create a direct path to Google Screened approval — this could be one of the fastest available routes to capturing top-of-page placement for "divorce mediator near me" searches
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • In a market where no confirmed mediation competitor is running LSAs, being the first dedicated mediation firm to claim this position could establish category dominance across the entire Bucks County and Philadelphia metro market at a pay-per-lead cost structure
