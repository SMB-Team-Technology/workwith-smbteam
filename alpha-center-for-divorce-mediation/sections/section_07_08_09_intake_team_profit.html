Intake Assessment

Alpha Center has a free consultation CTA prominently displayed on the homepage and a contact form available on the /contact-us/ page — but the intake process has visible gaps that are costing the firm leads it has already paid to generate. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Contact form is on a separate /contact-us/ page and not embedded on the homepage — divorce prospects who land on the site and cannot find a form above the fold are likely to call a competitor rather than navigate to a second page
  • No live chat observed on the website — emotionally-driven divorce inquirers who visit after hours have no way to self-identify, ask questions, or begin the intake process before the next business day
  • After-hours response coverage is not confirmed across four or more offices — for a multi-office practice, the question of who answers calls at 7pm or on weekends has significant conversion implications that compound across every office location
  • No intake tracking or CRM data is available from research — without knowing how many leads are being lost between website visit and booked consultation, the firm cannot measure or systematically improve intake efficiency
Blind Spot: Divorce inquiries are frequently triggered by an emotional event — a confrontation, a discovery, a final decision — that happens outside business hours. The firms that win these clients are the ones that engage first. Without after-hours intake coverage and a homepage contact form, Alpha Center is likely losing its most motivated prospects to whichever competitor responds within the hour.
The Positive
  • Free consultation CTA is above the fold on the homepage with "100% FREE & Confidential Consultations" messaging — a compelling offer that reduces initial friction and positions the firm as accessible to cost-sensitive prospects
  • Multi-office presence across Doylestown, Philadelphia, Allentown, and Exton gives the firm the geographic flexibility to meet clients close to home, which is a meaningful intake advantage when properly communicated
The Gap
  • Every lead that arrives on the homepage and does not find a form is a lead that paid ad spend generated but intake failed to capture — even a 10% improvement in homepage conversion rate could mean 5–10 additional consultations per month at the current traffic levels
  • Without a defined follow-up sequence for leads who do not schedule immediately after their first visit, the firm is relying on prospects to re-engage on their own — and most will not
  • After-hours inquiries that go to voicemail with no same-day response are not waiting — divorce prospects make decisions quickly when emotions are high, and the first firm to respond typically wins the case
Fixing intake is the fastest path to serving more families without spending more money — and every additional family Alpha Center helps is the mission Keila built this organization to fulfill.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact form confirmed on /contact-us/ page (Name, Email, Phone, Location, Message fields) — not visible on the homepage; requires an extra navigation step from every CTA button click
  • No live chat or chat widget was observed on the alpha-divorce.com website during research
  • City-specific landing pages on call.alpha-divorce.com (PPC subdomain) use unique phone tracking numbers — these may convert differently than the main domain, and optimizing both conversion paths independently is important for maximizing return on the existing ad spend

These findings are also referenced in the Website Audit section.

Team Assessment

Alpha Center has built a substantial 14+ person team across four or more active offices — a real organizational asset for a mediation practice that demands professional expertise across legal, financial, and family domains. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No evidence of a dedicated operations manager or COO-level role — a multi-office structure spanning Doylestown, Philadelphia, Allentown, and Exton requires daily coordination that likely pulls the founder into operational decisions rather than strategic ones
  • 14+ professionals across four office locations — attorney-mediators, parenting mediators, accountants, and financial counselors — operate without confirmed accountability systems, role clarity, or documented performance metrics
  • Founder Keila M. Gilbert Esq. does not appear in the current active staff listing on the /teams/ page — this may indicate she has stepped back from casework, but without operational infrastructure to replace her oversight, the firm's day-to-day may still depend on her decisions
  • A multi-professional team of this size without defined KPIs and structured role ownership creates an invisible dependency on the founder for quality control, direction, and conflict resolution across all offices
Blind Spot: A firm that has run successfully for 30+ years with the same founder often creates a false sense of organizational stability. The real test is not whether the firm can sustain its current performance — it can. The question is whether it can grow by 50% in the next two years without requiring significantly more of Keila's time. If the answer is no, the ceiling on the firm's growth is Keila's bandwidth, not market demand.
The Positive
  • The existing team depth — four attorney-mediators, eight parenting mediators, three accountants, and a financial counselor — is a genuine competitive advantage that few mediation firms in Eastern PA can match. The raw talent and professional infrastructure to scale are already in place.
The Gap
  • Without an operations structure, adding a fifth or sixth office requires Keila to personally oversee each new location — which is not scalable and runs directly counter to the freedom the firm was built to create
  • Without documented workflows and accountability systems, the quality of client experience across offices depends entirely on individual practitioners — and varies by person rather than by a repeatable, trainable process
  • The non-profit arm (Alpha Community Education Foundation, launched 2021) demands mission-focused time from leadership — time that is impossible to give when day-to-day firm operations still require constant owner involvement
Building the operational layer that lets this team run without constant oversight is what transforms a 30-year practice into the legacy organization Keila set out to build — one that serves families and advances the mission long after she steps back.

Profit Plan Assessment

For a multi-office firm with 14+ professionals serving thousands of families across Eastern Pennsylvania, Alpha Center's financial performance almost certainly reflects the scale and longevity of the business — but without visibility into margins by office, by service line, and by case type, growth is happening without a clear picture of what it is actually worth. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Revenue, margins, and profitability data were not available from research or a discovery call transcript — no financial visibility has been confirmed for a multi-office operation of this size and complexity
  • Operating four or more offices with 14+ staff creates significant fixed overhead — rent across multiple locations, salaries for a diverse professional team, insurance, and administrative costs — all of which must be covered before a single dollar of profit is realized
  • No evidence of cost-per-case or profit-by-service-type tracking — divorce mediation, parenting mediation, and financial counseling likely carry very different margins, yet without data the firm cannot make strategic decisions about where to invest
  • Without knowing the cost to acquire each case through the existing Google Ads campaign, the firm cannot calculate the true ROI of its marketing investment — or determine whether the ad spend is generating profit or just revenue
Blind Spot: Multi-service firms frequently subsidize lower-margin services without realizing it. If parenting mediation or financial counseling cases require more professional hours than divorce mediation but generate the same or lower revenue per case, the firm may be working harder in those service lines for proportionately less profit — and will never know it without case-level tracking.
The Positive
  • The firm's 30+ year longevity and scale — 5,000+ families served, 4+ active offices — strongly suggest the underlying business model is financially sound. The opportunity is to measure and optimize it, not rebuild it from scratch.
The Gap
  • Without tracking profit per case type and per office, the firm cannot make evidence-based decisions about which services to prioritize, which offices to expand, and where to focus marketing resources for the highest return
  • A growing marketing investment — Google Ads plus future SMB Team services — with no measurement framework means the firm will not be able to attribute revenue to specific channels, making it impossible to optimize over time
  • Without a clear profit plan, Keila cannot calculate what the firm is actually worth — which matters whether the goal is to scale, step back, or eventually transfer ownership of the practice she spent 30 years building
A clear profit plan is what turns 30 years of mission-driven work into a firm with real, measurable, lasting value — the kind of value that outlasts any individual and sustains the mission long after Keila steps back.
