Lead Generation

MBD Law is actively investing $12,500 per month in Google advertising — a meaningful commitment — but a single structural issue in the firm's Google Business Profile is preventing that investment from delivering its full return. The firm does not appear consistently in Bergen County's most competitive local family law search results despite having more Google reviews than several of its primary competitors.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Contact form is buried at the very bottom of the homepage — visitors who do not scroll past multiple sections and attorney bios have no visible way to start a conversation with the firm
  • PageSpeed scores: Not retrieved — must be run manually at pagespeed.web.dev before the proposal call (multiple automated retrieval attempts failed; scores should be added prior to presenting this report)
  • No video content appears on the homepage despite a "Blogs | Video" navigation link — an established firm with 5 credentialed attorneys is missing a significant trust-building opportunity above the fold
  • No above-fold contact form or phone number in a persistent sticky header — the only above-fold action available is the "Schedule a Consultation" button, which directs visitors to a booking flow rather than a low-friction inquiry path
Blind Spot: Competitors may be bidding on "MBD Law" and "Marotta Blazini Dunleavy" as branded keywords in Google Ads — capturing prospects who have already heard of the firm and are searching for it by name before they can reach the website.
The Positive
  • The site is modern, mobile-responsive, and uses current image formats (.webp) — it was last redesigned within the past 2–3 years and does not have the dated appearance that undermines credibility for many law firm websites
  • Active blog section with recent posts (June 2026) on high-value topics including high-asset divorce, domestic violence in divorce, and child support — strong content foundation for SEO
  • Geo-targeting landing pages exist for Maywood, Ridgewood, Paramus, Hackensack, Teaneck, and Fort Lee combining city + practice area — a solid local SEO infrastructure already in place
The Gap
  • A buried contact form means prospective clients who visit from paid ads but don't immediately see how to contact the firm will leave — every dollar of the $12,500/month ad budget that drives a visitor to a page with no visible contact path is partially wasted
  • No video presence means the attorneys' credentials, certifications (NJ Supreme Court Certified Matrimonial Law), and years of experience are not building trust visually — the single most powerful trust signal for family law prospects is never deployed
  • Without confirmed PageSpeed scores, it is not possible to know whether load time is contributing to bounce rates and lower Quality Scores in Google Ads — poor mobile performance can silently inflate cost-per-click across the entire $12,500/month campaign
A website that is not converting visitors into consultations is costing MBD Law clients — and every client lost at the website level is a client that will not be in the firm's caseload when the $10M target is being calculated.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • The Google Business Profile is categorized as "General Law Firm" — a miscategorization that directly suppresses LSA eligibility and local pack rankings for family law and divorce searches, which are the firm's highest-value traffic sources
  • MBD Law does not appear consistently in the top 3-pack for high-volume Bergen County searches including "family law attorney Bergen County NJ," "divorce attorney Bergen County," and "child custody attorney Bergen County NJ" — despite 86 reviews at 4.8 stars, which exceeds several local competitors
  • No systematic review collection process is in place — at 86 reviews, the firm is already ahead of Burke Williams (69 reviews), but Jonathan's goal of 200+ reviews requires a deliberate, automated ask system rather than relying on satisfied clients to leave reviews organically
  • FindLaw listing does not appear prominently in Bergen County family law directory results — a likely unclaimed or unlisted profile that competitors may be using to capture high-intent directory traffic
Blind Spot: The GBP category mismatch is not just a local pack issue — LSA visibility, eligibility, and cost-per-lead are all directly tied to GBP category. Every dollar spent on LSA right now is working at a fraction of its potential because the platform cannot correctly associate MBD Law with the family law searches that trigger LSA placements.
The Positive
  • 86 reviews at 4.8 stars is a genuinely strong GBP position — MBD Law already has more reviews than Burke Williams (69 reviews at 4.9 stars) and likely more than Weinberger's Hackensack location, meaning the firm is one category fix away from a significant competitive advantage in local pack and LSA rankings
The Gap
  • Fixing the GBP category from "General Law Firm" to "Family Law Firm" is a zero-cost, five-minute change — but every day it remains miscategorized is a day that $10,000 in LSA spend is delivering at suppressed capacity while competitors capture the family law search volume MBD Law is paying to reach
  • At the current review accumulation rate, reaching 200+ reviews will take years without a systematic process — a templated post-matter review request, a QR code in the office, and a brief automated email sequence could reach 200 reviews within months
Weinberger Divorce & Family Law Group — Correctly categorized as a family law firm, running Google Screened LSA, dominating Bergen County content results with dedicated practice area pages, and maintaining a multi-location NJ presence that gives them a structural advantage in local authority signals that MBD Law's single-location profile must overcome through review volume and category optimization.
A firm that wants to reach $10M cannot afford to have its #1 paid channel running at half capacity because of a five-minute configuration fix that has not been made yet.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • MBD Law is actively running Google Ads per the discovery call — $12,500/month total budget (approximately 80% on LSA / $10,000 and 20% on PPC / $2,500). Live ad observation during research could not independently confirm specific ad copy, consistent with being outbid in competitive auctions during the research window.
  • The PPC allocation ($2,500) is low for Bergen County's competitive family law market — this budget may be insufficient to maintain consistent visibility across multiple practice area and geo combinations, particularly against larger firms with dedicated digital marketing departments.
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Weinberger Divorce & Family Law Group — Active PPC and content presence across Bergen County family law and divorce keywords; as a dedicated family law firm with multiple NJ locations, they have the budget and category authority to maintain top ad positions for the terms MBD Law is targeting.
  • Burke Williams, LLC — Active family law marketing in Bergen County; recently awarded Top 4 Law Firm in Bergen County at the 2026 Best of Bergen Awards, likely using that recognition in ad copy and landing pages to drive credibility-based conversions.
  • Weiner Law Group LLP — Established Bergen County family law and high-asset divorce presence; dedicated landing pages and 35+ years of brand recognition create a strong quality score foundation for their paid campaigns.
The Opportunity
  • Expanding PPC coverage beyond the current $2,500/month allocation would capture high-intent searches like "high-asset divorce attorney Bergen County," "divorce mediation NJ," and "child custody attorney Hackensack NJ" where MBD Law's certified matrimonial attorney (NJ Supreme Court Certified) is a unique differentiator
  • Practice area specific campaigns — separate ad groups for divorce, child custody, alimony, domestic violence, and divorce mediation — allow for more targeted messaging and higher Quality Scores than a single broad family law campaign
  • Geo-expansion campaigns targeting Ridgewood, Paramus, Hackensack, and Fort Lee (where landing pages already exist) would capitalize on MBD Law's existing local SEO infrastructure
  • Competitor name bidding (Weinberger, Burke Williams, Weiner Law) captures high-intent prospects who are actively comparing firms and may not yet be committed to a competitor
Blind Spot: The current 80/20 LSA-to-PPC allocation made sense when the LSA was performing — but with the GBP category mismatch suppressing LSA delivery, the firm is effectively running $10,000/month into a suppressed channel while underinvesting in PPC, where the category mismatch does not apply. Rebalancing while fixing the GBP issue could significantly improve total lead volume without increasing overall spend.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • MBD Law is actively spending approximately $10,000/month on Local Service Ads per the discovery call — but the GBP's "General Law Firm" category directly suppresses LSA eligibility and delivery for family law searches, which is the most likely cause of the 10-day complete lead drought the firm experienced before this engagement.
  • Google Screened status was not confirmed — LSA performance depends on both category alignment and Google Screened certification, and it is unclear whether the firm's current LSA profile is fully optimized for the family law trigger terms that drive lead volume.
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Weinberger Divorce & Family Law Group — Correctly categorized as a family law firm with Google Screened status likely in place; their category alignment means they receive the full benefit of LSA delivery on family law and divorce searches that MBD Law's miscategorized profile is not currently capturing.
  • Burke Williams, LLC — Strong GBP presence (4.9 stars, 69 reviews) and correct family law categorization gives them LSA delivery advantages — their fewer reviews than MBD Law (69 vs. 86) are being offset by having the correct category configuration.
The Opportunity
  • Fixing the GBP category from "General Law Firm" to "Family Law Firm" is a zero-cost change that could immediately unlock the full lead delivery potential of the $10,000/month MBD Law is already spending on LSA — this is the single highest-ROI action available to the firm right now
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • With 86 reviews at 4.8 stars, MBD Law has more Google reviews than Burke Williams (69) and likely more than Weinberger's Hackensack LSA profile — a properly configured LSA could position MBD Law at the top of Bergen County's family law results ahead of competitors who currently appear there with fewer reviews

Facebook & Instagram Ads

Ad Presence

  • The firm maintains active social profiles (@mbdlawnj on Facebook, @mbdnjlaw on Instagram) with real engagement potential — the infrastructure to run paid Meta campaigns is in place; it simply has not been activated.
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Burke Williams, LLC — Active Facebook page (349+ likes) with organic social presence; active Meta ad campaigns were not confirmed during the research window, suggesting this channel may not be heavily contested by MBD Law's nearest Bergen County competitors.
  • Weinberger Divorce & Family Law Group — Facebook presence active; no aggressive Meta ad campaigns confirmed for the Hackensack location during the research window — this represents a market-wide opportunity gap that the first firm to activate can own.
The Opportunity
  • Retargeting — website visitors who viewed practice area pages (divorce, child custody, high-asset divorce) but did not contact the firm are leaving with no follow-up; a retargeting campaign can bring those prospects back before they call a competitor
  • Video ads featuring Genevieve Blazini and the MBD Law attorneys — as an NJ Supreme Court Certified Matrimonial Law Attorney, Genevieve's credentials are a powerful differentiator that video can communicate before a prospect ever calls
  • Educational content ads targeting Bergen County residents facing divorce, child custody disputes, and domestic violence situations — family law clients begin their decision process weeks before they search; Meta captures them at the beginning of that journey, before they search Google
  • Life event targeting on Meta (separation, recent divorce, family changes) allows MBD Law to reach Bergen County prospects at the exact moment their family law needs begin — an audience Google search cannot reach because they have not yet started searching
