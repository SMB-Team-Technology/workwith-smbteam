Here Is Where MBD Law Stands Today

MBD Law — Marotta Blazini Dunleavy LLC — is a Bergen County family law firm with five attorneys and a Director of Operations, having grown from $1.2M in 2024 to $2.0M in 2025, with a $2.4M revenue run rate in 2026 and an aggressive target of $3.0M by year-end and $10M within five years. The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Intake Assessment

MBD Law's 75% consultation-to-signing rate is one of the strongest indicators of attorney and intake quality in Bergen County family law — but that rate only applies after a lead has successfully navigated to a booked consultation, and the online pathway to get there has meaningful gaps. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • The website contact form is buried at the bottom of the homepage — visitors arriving from paid ads, organic search, or referral who do not scroll past multiple sections and attorney bios have no visible prompt to reach out
  • No above-fold secondary contact path (callback request, text-to-start, or inquiry form) exists alongside the "Schedule a Consultation" button — prospects who are not ready to commit to a formal consultation have no low-friction way to initiate contact
  • After-hours lead capture was not confirmed — calls and form submissions received outside of business hours may not receive a response until the following business day, which is enough time for a motivated prospect to contact a competitor
  • No systematic review collection process is in place — every client who leaves without being asked for a review is a missed opportunity to build toward the 200+ review threshold that strengthens both GBP ranking and LSA lead delivery
Blind Spot: The 75% consultation close rate is so strong that it may be obscuring an upstream problem: how many leads are contacting the firm, not reaching anyone, and never becoming a consultation in the first place? Without tracking lead-to-consultation conversion, the full cost of intake gaps is invisible.
The Positive
  • A 75% consultation-to-signing close rate is genuinely exceptional — MBD Law's attorneys are converting three out of four consultations into retained clients, which means every optimization to the intake funnel upstream of the consultation has an outsized impact on revenue
  • Natalie Barone as Director of Operations provides a dedicated infrastructure owner who can build, test, and manage intake systems — most firms at this stage do not have that leadership in place
The Gap
  • At a 75% close rate, every additional consultation booked generates approximately $7,500 in expected case revenue — a contact form above the fold, an after-hours callback prompt, and a consistent follow-up sequence for non-converted leads could realistically add 2–5 consultations per month without any increase in ad spend
  • Without a review collection system, the firm's current lead over Burke Williams (86 vs. 69 reviews) will narrow as competitors grow their GBP profiles while MBD Law's review count stays static
  • A defined intake protocol and qualification script would allow Natalie and any future intake staff to replicate the quality of a partner-led consultation at scale — protecting the 75% close rate as the firm adds clients and volume
MBD Law's intake advantage — a 75% consultation close rate — is only as valuable as the number of consultations the system is actually producing; fixing the upstream gaps is how that advantage gets fully monetized on the path to $10M.

Profit Plan Assessment

MBD Law has strong top-line revenue momentum and a reported 40–45% net profit margin — real financial indicators that the firm is generating meaningful profit from its growth. But rapid scaling, increasing marketing investment, and new operational hires all compress margins in ways that are difficult to anticipate without systematic profit planning. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
"A healthy 40–45% net profit margin provides ample room for investment without jeopardizing the business."
The Problem
  • Marketing attribution is currently at a high level — total YTD spend ($57,000) tracked against total YTD litigation revenue ($706,000) — but cost per acquired client, cost per case type, and channel-level ROI (LSA vs. PPC vs. agency vs. SEO) are not being tracked separately
  • The firm is investing $57,000 YTD in marketing (approximately 8% of YTD revenue), which is a sound investment ratio — but without attribution data, it is not possible to know which channels are generating the best-quality cases and which are underperforming relative to their spend
  • Rapid scaling from $1.2M to $2.0M to $2.4M creates compounding overhead — new attorneys, the Director of Operations hire, agency fees, and increasing ad spend all add fixed and variable costs that can silently compress the 40–45% margin if not actively monitored
  • No mention of a dedicated financial reporting cadence, profit target by quarter, or CFO-level oversight — at $2.4M in annual revenue and targeting $3M by year-end, the financial complexity of the business has outgrown informal tracking
Blind Spot: A 67% revenue growth year (2024 to 2025) almost always comes with hidden margin compression — new hires, expanded marketing, and operational investments that were needed to drive the growth also cost money. Without quarterly profit reviews, MBD Law may be reporting strong top-line numbers while profit dollars-per-owner are not growing at the same rate.
The Positive
  • The firm is actively tracking revenue milestones ($1.2M, $2.0M, $2.4M run rate) and knows its YTD marketing spend — this level of financial awareness is better than many firms at this stage, and forms the foundation for a more sophisticated profit planning system
  • A stated 40–45% net profit margin, if accurate, gives the firm meaningful room to invest in the systems and staff needed to scale to $3M and beyond without jeopardizing financial health
The Gap
  • The path from $2.4M to $10M will require decisions about when to hire, how much to invest in marketing, and which practice areas to prioritize — those decisions need a profit plan and financial model, not just an awareness of total revenue
  • Without marketing attribution data, MBD Law cannot know whether the $10,000/month in LSA spend is generating more revenue than the $2,500/month in PPC — and cannot make a data-driven decision about how to rebalance that budget to maximize return
  • A Fractional CFO engagement would provide the financial architecture — KPIs, profit targets, cash flow forecasting, and investment thresholds — that translates MBD Law's aggressive growth goals into a financially sustainable plan rather than a revenue-first chase
Reaching $10M in revenue without a profit plan is just doing more work for an unknown return — the firms that build real wealth at scale are the ones that treat profit as a design decision, not a discovery at year-end.
