Intake Assessment

Guardian Legal has one dedicated intake person — but that person is hourly, with no after-hours coverage, while the website actively promises 24/7 availability to every prospect who lands on it. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • The intake employee is hourly — calls and web inquiries that arrive after hours, on weekends, or during lunch go unanswered, and MVA clients in pain do not wait until Monday morning
  • The website prominently advertises 24/7 service; when a prospect calls at 10 PM and reaches voicemail, they call the next firm on the list — Guardian Legal pays $2,000 for that retainer and loses it
  • No CRM or intake tracking system confirmed — without a structured pipeline, there is no way to know how many leads are falling through, how fast follow-up is happening, or which lead sources are converting
  • No defined follow-up sequence for leads who do not sign immediately — a prospect who calls on Tuesday and needs to "think about it" will be lost by Friday without an automated nurture process
Blind Spot: At $2,000 per purchased retainer, every missed after-hours call is a $2,000 loss before the firm has earned a single dollar from that lead. Once ad spend begins, this problem multiplies — every call that goes unanswered is money spent on advertising that the firm cannot recover. Closing the intake gap before launching ads is not a nice-to-have; it is a prerequisite for positive ROI.
The Positive
  • A dedicated intake role exists — the firm has made the right structural decision by separating intake from casework; now the role needs the right coverage model and systems to support it
The Gap
  • If 30% of leads arrive outside business hours — a conservative estimate for accident victims who call when they need help, not when it is convenient — and the firm is paying $2,000 per retainer, every 10 missed calls is $20,000 in lost investment
  • Without a CRM pipeline, there is no visibility into lead-to-case conversion rate — the firm cannot improve what it cannot measure, and it cannot build toward 20–25 cases per month without knowing where the gaps are
  • When ad spend launches, slow or inconsistent follow-up will directly reduce the return on that investment — intake efficiency is the multiplier on every marketing dollar spent
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing. An automated intake system is one of the first things that lets Mariano step back, because the firm starts following up on its own.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact form location on the website could not be verified directly — the site's bot protection blocked automated review; the form should be visible above the fold on the homepage with a response time expectation set
  • The 24/7 claim on the website creates an expectation the firm must fulfill — prospects who submit a contact form after hours need an immediate automated acknowledgment and a guaranteed response window
  • Any after-hours web inquiry that goes unacknowledged until the next business day will likely result in a prospect who has already signed with another firm — automated acknowledgment and text follow-up within 5 minutes of form submission is the industry standard for converting PI leads

These findings are also referenced in the Website Audit section.

Team Assessment

Guardian Legal has a functional three-person support team in place — intake, case manager, and paralegal — but at four months old, the firm has no documented workflows, no accountability systems, and no management layer between the team and the owner. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No documented workflows for any team role — when a case manager or paralegal is out sick, there is no system to ensure continuity; everything exists in Mariano's head
  • No manager or team lead between Mariano and the three staff members — all decisions, all escalations, and all quality control currently flow through the owner
  • No performance accountability framework — without defined KPIs for each role, the team has no way to know if they are succeeding, and Mariano has no way to identify where the bottlenecks are
  • The firm's stated goal is 20–25 new cases per month; at that volume, the current three-person structure will require significant hiring guidance — without a plan for when to hire, who to hire, and how to onboard them, growth will outpace the team's capacity
Blind Spot: The fact that the firm is only four months old and the team is already in place is actually a sign of strong instincts — but instincts without systems do not scale. The firms that hit $5–$10M annual revenue all have one thing in common: documented processes that allow new team members to plug in without slowing the firm down. The time to build those systems is now, before scale makes it harder.
The Positive
  • The right functional roles are in place for the current case volume — intake, case management, and paralegal support are the foundational positions a pre-litigation PI firm needs
  • Mariano identified hiring guidance and workflow automation as explicit goals on the call — this awareness is the first step toward building a firm that runs without him
The Gap
  • Without documented workflows, every staff departure is a crisis — the firm's operational knowledge lives in people, not in systems, which makes it fragile and unscalable
  • Mariano described a 5-year goal of "$5–$10M with an automated, system-driven firm" — that firm does not exist without a management structure that operates independently of the owner, and that structure takes 18–24 months to build
  • Hiring without a plan — wrong role, wrong timing, or wrong onboarding — is one of the most common ways fast-growing firms stall; guidance on when and how to hire is as valuable as the hire itself
Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows. The automated firm Mariano wants to build starts with systems, and systems start now.

Profit Plan Assessment

Guardian Legal is four months into operations with no collected settlement revenue yet — all capital is currently funding lead purchasing and overhead while the first cases move toward resolution. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • The firm is pre-settlement — no attorney fees have been collected; the $20,000–$25,000 monthly lead purchasing spend is currently entirely funded by startup capital, not revenue
  • No tracking of cost per case — at $2,000 per signed retainer, the firm knows what it pays for a retainer but not what it costs to take a case through to settlement when overhead, staff, and time are factored in
  • No profit plan or financial visibility framework — when the first settlements start arriving, there is no system to determine how much to reinvest in marketing, how much to hold as operating reserve, and how much represents actual owner take-home
  • The revenue goal is $3.36M per year — but without a profit model, high revenue can coexist with low take-home; knowing what 25 cases per month actually puts in Mariano's pocket requires a structured financial framework
Blind Spot: The shift from lead buying to owned marketing is not just a marketing decision — it is a financial one. Owned marketing requires upfront investment that takes 3–6 months to generate ROI. Understanding cash flow, break-even, and reinvestment ratios before that transition starts is what prevents the firm from running out of runway before the marketing engine takes hold.
The Gap
  • Without financial visibility, the firm cannot make data-driven decisions about when to hire, when to upgrade marketing spend, or when the business model has become sustainable — every major decision is a guess
  • Mariano's 5-year goal of a $5–$10M automated firm requires building financial systems today — firms that hit that number always know their numbers; firms that do not usually plateau when they hit capacity
  • The transition from purchased leads to owned marketing will temporarily increase spend before it reduces cost per case — planning for that transition with a clear financial model is the difference between a smooth scale and a cash flow crisis
Profit clarity is not about watching expenses — it is about building the financial intelligence that lets Mariano make confident decisions about growth, reinvestment, and the path to the automated $5–$10M firm he described.
