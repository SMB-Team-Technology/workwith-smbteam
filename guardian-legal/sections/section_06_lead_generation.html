Lead Generation

Guardian Legal PLLC is currently generating every one of its cases by purchasing leads at $2,000 per signed retainer — a model that is expensive, entirely dependent on third-party vendors, and builds no long-term marketing asset for the firm. The firm has no owned online presence of any kind: no Google visibility, no paid advertising, and no SEO foundation in either its Arizona or Florida markets.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Site is brand new — estimated 4 months old, consistent with the firm's founding — but new does not mean optimized
  • PageSpeed scores: Mobile and desktop scores could not be retrieved during research (site uses bot protection); actual scores must be run at pagespeed.web.dev before presenting — slow mobile load speeds are common on new PI sites and directly cost rankings and conversions
  • The website promises 24/7 intake service that the firm cannot currently deliver — an hourly intake employee creates a gap in after-hours coverage that will cause paid leads to be missed the moment ad spend begins
  • No confirmed attorney bio, no case results page, and no video content — in a market where prospects are evaluating multiple firms simultaneously, the absence of trust signals sends them to a competitor
Blind Spot: The 24/7 promise on the website is not a feature — it is currently a liability. When ad spend drives new leads to the site at 9 PM and no one answers, the firm pays for the lead and loses the case. Closing this gap before launching ads is not optional.
The Positive
  • The site is live and indexed by Google as "Arizona Personal Injury Lawyers" — the foundational URL structure is in place
  • A free consultation CTA appears above the fold, which is the correct conversion-oriented structure for a PI site
The Gap
  • A website with no authority, no reviews, no content, and unverified page speed will not rank organically for any PI keyword in Phoenix — competitors like Phillips Law Group and Shapiro Law Team have years of SEO investment this site cannot overcome without a structured content and optimization strategy
  • Every $2,000 spent on purchased leads sends the prospect to a website that has not been optimized to convert — any friction at the site level means the firm pays for a lead it cannot close
  • Without geo-targeted landing pages for surrounding cities (Scottsdale, Mesa, Tempe, Chandler, Glendale), the firm leaves significant organic traffic on the table once SEO investment begins
A high-converting, fast-loading website is the foundation of every lead gen system — without it, ad spend, SEO, and LSAs all underperform, and Mariano cannot build the automated firm he described.

  • Zero Google reviews — every major Phoenix competitor has hundreds to thousands: Phillips Law Group has 2,500+, Shapiro Law Team has 700+, Lamber Goodnow has 400–700

Phillips Law Group — 2,500+ Google reviews, established Google Screened LSA badge, dominant in every local 3-pack for Phoenix PI searches, with years of structured content and citation authority that compounds monthly.
Building a local SEO foundation today is not optional — it is the asset that will eventually replace the $2,000-per-retainer lead cost and give Mariano a marketing engine that runs without his daily involvement.
