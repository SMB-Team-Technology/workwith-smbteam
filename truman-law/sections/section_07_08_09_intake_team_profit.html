Intake Assessment

As a solo practitioner, Harkirat Singh Kang is almost certainly the person prospects reach when they contact Truman Law — which means the firm's intake speed and consistency is tied directly to the attorney's personal availability at any given moment. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • No above-fold contact form or immediate conversion mechanism — prospects who arrive on the homepage must navigate to a separate "Get Started" page to make contact, creating avoidable drop-off for high-intent visitors
  • No after-hours inquiry capture — calls and messages received outside business hours have no visible system to ensure prompt next-morning follow-up, and prospective clients do not wait
  • Solo attorney model means all initial client contact depends on Harkirat's personal availability — response speed slows during court appearances, real estate closings, and high-volume periods
  • No documented intake process that a support staff member could follow independently — the firm cannot delegate first contact without a defined protocol in place
Blind Spot: Research consistently shows that law firm prospects who contact multiple firms sign with whichever one responds first — often within the hour. In real estate and business law, where timing is frequently critical (offer deadlines, closing dates, business decisions), a same-day or after-hours response system is not a luxury. Without it, Truman Law is likely losing prospects to firms that call back before Harkirat can.
The Gap
  • Every missed call or delayed response that results in a prospect choosing a competitor is a lost file that was already paid for in referral cost or community reputation — and there is currently no system to track how often this happens
  • Without intake tracking, there is no way to know how many inquiries are converting to consultations, where the drop-off is occurring, or what it is costing the firm each month in lost revenue
  • The lack of after-hours coverage means the firm is effectively unavailable to a significant portion of prospective clients who research legal services in the evenings and on weekends
A faster, more consistent intake process would immediately increase the number of clients Harkirat converts from the referrals and searches the firm is already receiving — without changing a single thing about marketing spend.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • The contact form is accessible only via the "Get Started" navigation link — there is no embedded form on the homepage for visitors who want to submit details immediately without navigating away from the page
  • No live chat widget or automated chatbot observed on the website — high-intent visitors who have a quick question before committing to a consultation have no immediate interaction option and must wait for a callback
  • These structural friction points create measurable drop-off in inquiry-to-consultation conversion, particularly on mobile devices where any extra navigation step amplifies abandonment rates

Team Assessment

Truman Law is a solo practice where the attorney is also the business operator — handling files, managing operations, and fielding every inquiry without a management layer to share the load. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • The firm operates without a defined team structure — all work passes through Harkirat personally, creating a single point of failure for every file and every operational decision
  • No visible admin, intake, or paralegal support on the website — the expansion into seven practice areas has happened without a corresponding team to handle the volume at scale
  • Without at least one dedicated support staff member, Harkirat's time is permanently split between legal work and the administrative overhead that a non-attorney could handle just as effectively
  • No accountability structure or documented processes are visible — all institutional knowledge lives with the attorney, making delegation difficult even when the right person is eventually hired
Blind Spot: The pattern at Stage 3 is that attorneys often become more personally efficient — handling more files, working faster — without realizing that individual efficiency alone cannot get the firm past this stage. The ceiling on one attorney's capacity is fixed. The only path through it is building a team that multiplies that capacity, and that requires putting systems in place before the team is hired.
The Positive
  • The expansion from civil litigation into real estate law in 2021 and further into private lending, wills, and business law demonstrates that Harkirat has already proven he can grow the firm's capabilities — the appetite for growth is real and the execution track record supports it
The Gap
  • Without a team, revenue growth translates directly to more personal hours — there is no leverage in the current structure and no ceiling on how much the firm demands from its one attorney
  • The inability to delegate client-facing tasks means Harkirat cannot absorb the additional lead volume that marketing would generate without sacrificing response speed or service quality
  • Each hour Harkirat spends on administrative or intake tasks is an hour unavailable for billable client work — the opportunity cost compounds daily
Building even a small, structured team — starting with an intake coordinator — would allow Truman Law to handle significantly more volume without adding a single hour to Harkirat's personal workload.

Profit Plan Assessment

Without a discovery call transcript, detailed financial data for Truman Law is not available — but the patterns of a growing solo practice across seven practice areas in a competitive market are well understood. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Revenue, margins, and cost-per-matter figures are not confirmed — there is no baseline from which to measure profitability, set growth targets, or evaluate where marketing investment will generate the highest return
  • With seven practice areas spanning low-margin residential conveyancing ($900–$1,800/file) and high-margin civil litigation ($5,000–$25,000+), the firm risks spending the majority of its capacity on its least profitable work without a tracking system to identify the difference
  • Solo practice economics tie personal income directly to hours worked — without a profit plan, there is no mechanism to grow income without also growing the number of hours Harkirat personally bills
  • As marketing generates more leads and the firm scales, cash flow predictability and margin control become increasingly difficult without financial systems already in place
Blind Spot: Real estate conveyancing is high-volume but typically generates $900–$1,800 per file — some of the lowest per-matter revenue in the practice area mix. Without tracking profitability by practice area, the firm risks investing marketing dollars into the work that consumes the most time for the least return. A profit plan would clarify which practice areas to grow aggressively and which to price differently.
The Positive
  • Truman Law has operated continuously since 2020 and expanded its practice area coverage over five years — a stable, growing practice is the foundation that a profit plan can optimize and accelerate
The Gap
  • Without visibility into margins by practice area, marketing investment decisions are made blind — there is no way to know which work generates the highest return on marketing spend or attorney time
  • As the firm adds staff, technology, and marketing costs, profit margins could compress significantly without a financial tracking system to manage them in real time
  • The absence of financial forecasting means revenue targets and growth goals lack the concrete profit plan needed to actually achieve them and know when they have been reached
The firms that build real wealth are the ones that track it — knowing which cases make money, which practice areas to grow, and which to price differently is what turns a busy solo practice into a genuinely profitable business.
