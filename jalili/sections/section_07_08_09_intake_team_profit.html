Intake Assessment

The firm's only intake step today is the principal attorney personally taking every free 30-minute consultation — including prospects who were never a fit for the private-pay caseload the firm needs. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • The principal attorney personally handles every consultation — no qualification step screens for private-pay fit before the call happens
  • No defined process separates Legal Aid-eligible inquiries from the higher-value private clients the firm needs to hit its $2M goal
  • Three different phone numbers across the firm's web properties likely delay or misroute some callers before they ever reach intake
  • No stated follow-up sequence exists for leads who don't convert on the first call
Blind Spot: You may not realize how much of the attorney's calendar is being consumed by prospects who were never going to become paying private clients — time that could go directly toward the cases that fund the U.S. expansion.
The Positive
  • The volume problem is already solved — 103 leads came in during July alone. The gap is entirely in what happens after the lead arrives
The Gap
  • Every unqualified consultation is an hour the attorney isn't spending on higher-value private cases or firm strategy
  • Inconsistent phone numbers likely cost the firm calls that never get properly logged or followed up
The fastest path to your $2M goal isn't more leads — it's converting more of the 103 you're already getting each month into paying private clients.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • The "Free Case Evaluation" contact form sits above the fold on the homepage — a real strength
  • No live chat is present, meaning after-hours visitors have no way to engage before calling a competitor
  • The three-phone-number inconsistency likely affects which number prospects use to follow up after submitting the form

Team Assessment

A COO is already in place and the team has grown to 6-7 staff — but the principal attorney still personally screens and consults with every prospective client. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • The attorney personally handles every consultation, regardless of practice area or case value
  • The planned U.S. expansion is entirely gated on one person — the COO's immigration approval — with no backup path if that timeline shifts
  • No intake or case-screening role currently exists to take consultations off the attorney's plate
  • Team roles beyond "staff" and COO were not itemized on the call, so it's unclear how work is distributed today beyond casework
Blind Spot: Having a COO already in place may be masking how much of the client-facing workload still runs through you personally — a COO handling operations doesn't free up the hours you're spending on intake.
The Positive
  • A COO already exists — a real structural head start most firms at this stage don't have
  • The team has grown to 6-7 staff, giving the firm real capacity to take on more casework
The Gap
  • Every hour spent on consultations that don't convert is an hour the attorney isn't spending building the U.S. firm
  • With the expansion gated on one person's immigration status, there is no redundancy if that plan changes
A team that runs without your personal involvement in every consultation is what actually lets you build the second firm — not just fund it.

Profit Plan Assessment

The firm is tracking revenue against a clear growth target, but no profit-margin or case-profitability figures were shared on the call. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No profitability or margin figures were discussed on the call — only revenue and lead volume
  • Average case value was not stated for any practice area, making it difficult to know which cases are most worth pursuing
  • The firm has capacity for more Legal Aid work, but that volume competes for the same attorney hours as the higher-value private cases the $2M goal requires
  • Doubling revenue without a profitability view means the firm can't yet tell if it's chasing the right caseload
Blind Spot: You may not realize that your most time-consuming cases are not necessarily your most profitable ones — without tracking cost and value by case type, doubling revenue could mean doubling the wrong caseload.
The Positive
  • Revenue is being tracked and a clear, ambitious goal exists — $2M, double 2024's ~$950K — which gives a real target to plan against
The Gap
  • Without margin visibility, it's impossible to know how much of the U.S. expansion's funding will actually be available once the Canadian firm hits $2M
  • Chasing revenue without a profit lens risks growing the top line while take-home pay stays flat
The U.S. firm isn't funded by revenue — it's funded by the profit left over after the work is done, and that's the number nobody is tracking yet.
