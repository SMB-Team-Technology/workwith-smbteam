Lead Generation

Jalili Law Group's lead generation runs almost entirely through one paid agency channel — Google and Meta ads that generate real volume, but without the local SEO, directory, and city-page presence that would turn that volume into a predictable flow of higher-value private clients.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • The site is organized by charge and practice type rather than by city — no dedicated landing pages exist for North York, Downtown Toronto, Scarborough, Vaughan, or Mississauga, despite the firm actively serving all five
  • Live PageSpeed performance data could not be captured this research cycle and is pending a technical re-check before final sign-off — no score is being estimated in its place
  • Three different phone numbers appear across the firm's own web properties — (905) 597-0887 on the main site, (647) 696-8989 and (647) 696-5615 on the defence.jalililawfirm.ca subdomain — a real NAP inconsistency
  • No confirmed Avvo, Justia, or FindLaw profile exists for the Ontario firm, while competitors like Daniel Brown Law and Kazandji Law carry broader directory footprints
Blind Spot: Three different phone numbers across your own web properties likely mean your marketing spend is being tracked and attributed inconsistently — you may be under-crediting the channels that are actually working.
The Positive
  • An active, timely blog — including a recent post on Bill C-14's bail reform — is a genuine content asset most firms at this stage don't have
  • The homepage leads with a clear above-the-fold consultation CTA, phone number, and attorney bio with video
The Gap
  • Without city-specific landing pages, the firm is invisible in search to prospects in neighboring markets it already serves
  • The phone number inconsistency likely fragments call tracking, making it harder to know which channels are actually producing the private-pay clients the $2M goal depends on
Fixing the technical and structural gaps on your own site is what makes every dollar already being spent on ads work harder toward the U.S. expansion you're funding.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Neither the firm's own Google review count and rating, nor competitors' live counts, could be confirmed this cycle — a manual spot-check is still outstanding before these figures should be used in a client conversation
  • The Google Business Profile's primary category is listed as "Criminal Defense Law" only, while the firm actively markets family law and immigration work as well
  • No confirmed presence on Avvo, Justia, or FindLaw for the Ontario firm — a real citation gap next to firms like Daniel Brown Law and Kazandji Law, which carry broader directory footprints
Blind Spot: The main site and the defence.jalililawfirm.ca subdomain likely compete against each other in search rather than reinforcing one authority — Google may be splitting ranking signal between two properties instead of consolidating it behind one.
The Positive
  • The firm carries an active Yelp listing with 25 photos — a real foundation to build a broader directory presence from
The Gap
  • Without a confirmed review advantage or full directory presence, prospects comparing options may default to the more visibly established Daniel Brown Law or Kazandji Law
Kazandji Law — direct structural overlap with a dual criminal + family law practice and offices in both Toronto and Thornhill, backed by a free-consultation offer that surfaces prominently across the same searches your firm is competing in.
Winning the local visibility fight is what lets your firm capture the higher-value private clients you need — without paying for every one of them through ads.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Google Ads are running today through Grow Club.Marketing as part of a ~$10K/month combined Google & Meta budget ($4.2K spent in July to date, generating 103 leads) — specific ad creative and keyword targeting could not be independently confirmed via available research tools this cycle
  • Both criminal defense and family law campaigns appear active per the transcript, though visible SERP ad placement for either practice area does not appear in the searches captured this session — this may reflect a tool limitation rather than an actual absence of campaigns
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Kazandji Law — a prominent free-consultation offer surfacing across multiple criminal defense and family law searches
  • Daniel Brown Law — deep organic and content authority reinforced by national recognition as a Top 10 Criminal Law Boutique, competing for the same higher-value criminal clients
The Opportunity
  • High-intent local searches like "criminal defence lawyer Thornhill Ontario" and "criminal lawyer near me Thornhill" your firm should own outright
  • City-specific campaigns for North York, Scarborough, and Mississauga — markets you already serve but do not currently target directly
  • Bidding on competitor names like Kazandji Law and Daniel Brown Law to capture prospects actively comparing options
  • Urgency-driven terms ("bail hearing lawyer," "arrested today") that match how urgently criminal defense prospects are searching
Blind Spot: Your current $10K/month agency spend is being measured against lead volume rather than case value — without knowing cost per qualified private-pay client, it's difficult to know whether that spend is actually moving you toward your $2M goal.
