Intake Assessment

The Eichholz Law Firm has a delegated intake team in place — but a structural imbalance has left some case managers at 40% over ideal capacity while others are under-utilized, creating an inconsistent client experience and a real risk that qualified leads fall through the cracks. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Case managers carrying 135–143 active cases are at 40% over the 100-case ideal — at that load, there is not enough bandwidth for timely follow-up on new inbound leads, and response speed is a primary driver of PI intake conversion
  • Other case managers are operating at 93–94 cases — below capacity — indicating the problem is not a staffing shortage but a workflow and assignment failure that can be fixed without adding headcount
  • An attorney's decision to reject cases from the main referral source has created under-capacity in parts of the pipeline while other areas are overwhelmed — a strategic disagreement that is having a direct operational cost
  • No after-hours intake coverage is confirmed — in a PI market where accidents happen at all hours, missed after-hours calls are cases signed by a competitor the same night
Blind Spot: Overloaded case managers are not just slow to follow up on new leads — they are also more likely to under-service existing clients, increasing the risk of complaints, bad reviews, and referral attrition. The case load imbalance is simultaneously a lead conversion problem and a client retention problem.
The Positive
  • Intake is delegated — the attorneys are not personally handling every intake call, which means there is a team structure to optimize rather than a process to build from scratch
  • The website has a prominent contact form above the fold with a clear "Get Your Free Case Evaluation" CTA — the digital intake path exists and is accessible
The Gap
  • At 135–143 cases per manager, follow-up speed on new leads is compromised — every hour of delay in responding to a PI inquiry is a measurable decrease in conversion probability, and at this load that delay is systematic
  • The referral source rejection has created a pipeline gap that no amount of advertising can fill — cases that should be converting are being turned away, reducing revenue without reducing the fixed costs that created the profit problem
  • Without a defined after-hours coverage protocol, the firm is losing leads every evening to competitors who respond first
Getting intake right means more cases from every dollar already spent on marketing — and more cases means a faster path back to the market dominance that defines what this firm is supposed to be.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • The homepage contact form is embedded above the fold with a visible "Send" button — a strong conversion signal that removes friction for prospects ready to reach out
  • No live chat widget was detected on the homepage — meaning after-hours visitors who want an instant response have no digital option and must choose between calling (which may go unanswered) or leaving
  • The "Get Your Free Case Evaluation" CTA is clearly visible above the fold — but without a live chat or callback option, after-hours traffic has only one conversion path, and a slow-to-load mobile site reduces the chance they complete the form

These findings are also referenced in the Website Audit section.

Team Assessment

The Eichholz Law Firm has a COO, a Managing Partner, and multiple attorneys — more management infrastructure than most firms at this revenue level. But a fundamental governance failure has left the leadership team unable to execute on any unified direction, and the owner remains the central authority in a firm that needs to run without him. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • A strategic stalemate between the owner (growth path) and the COO (lean contraction path) has blocked execution on any unified direction — the firm is neither growing nor contracting, it is drifting
  • Without a clear decision-making framework, the leadership team cannot resolve strategic disagreements internally, which means David must be in every room for any major decision to move forward
  • The attorney who rejected the primary referral source created a case load imbalance that the management team has not resolved — a sign that authority and accountability structures are not clear enough to enforce operational decisions
  • Revenue has declined from $13M to $5.2M during a period when the COO and Managing Partner were both in place — indicating the existing team structure has not been sufficient to arrest the decline
Blind Spot: Having a COO who advocates for a smaller firm is not inherently wrong — it becomes a problem when that disagreement prevents the owner from executing his own vision. The real issue is not the COO's opinion; it is that there is no governance framework that lets David make the final call clearly and have the team execute behind it. Without that structure, every strategic disagreement becomes a stalemate.
The Positive
  • A COO and Managing Partner are in place — the org chart exists, which means the path to a self-managing firm is about alignment and accountability, not about hiring from scratch
  • The team includes multiple attorneys and case managers with defined roles — there is a functional operating team that can be organized around a clear growth plan
The Gap
  • The owner cannot execute a $13M growth vision while simultaneously managing a stalemate with his own COO — until the leadership team is aligned behind a single direction, every growth initiative competes with internal resistance
  • A firm where strategic direction requires the owner's involvement in every decision cannot scale — the next level of growth requires a team that executes the plan without David in every conversation
  • The case load imbalance that no one has resolved is costing the firm revenue right now — and it signals an accountability gap at the management level that will block operational improvement across every other pillar
Proving the growth path was right requires a team that can execute it — and that means getting the leadership structure aligned before adding more marketing fuel to a machine that is not yet pointed in one direction.

Profit Plan Assessment

The Eichholz Law Firm is generating $5.2 million in annual revenue while projecting a net loss — a structure where payroll has risen to 42% of revenue and a $2.1 million mortgage balloon looms at the end of 2027, creating a financial deadline that cannot be resolved with cost-cutting alone. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • The firm is projecting a -$130,000 net loss on $5.2M revenue — unprofitable at a scale where healthy PI firms typically target 23–31% net margin, representing a $1.2M–$1.6M annual profit gap versus industry standard
  • Payroll has risen from 25% to 42% of revenue as the firm shrank — the fixed cost base was built for a $13M firm and has not been restructured to match the current revenue level, making every dollar of revenue less profitable than it should be
  • A $2.1 million mortgage balloon payment is due at the end of 2027, a prior $500K line of credit was called in on seven days' notice, and the firm is currently pursuing a new line of credit — the financial position is fragile and the runway is limited
  • Advertising spend remained at $3.5M–$4.5M annually even as revenue declined from $13M — meaning the firm continued spending for growth while the revenue it was designed to generate was falling away
Blind Spot: The decision to cut advertising to address the -$130K net loss may make the 2027 financial crisis worse, not better. PI cases signed today resolve in 12–18 months. Cutting ads now means the case pipeline that would fund 2027 revenue is not being built. By the time the balloon payment arrives, the pipeline will be at its thinnest — precisely when the firm needs it to be strongest.
The Positive
  • David is aware of the exact problem — the -$130K net loss, the payroll percentage, the balloon payment, and the industry benchmarks. This level of financial clarity is more than most PI firm owners have, and it means the plan can be built on facts rather than assumptions.
  • Recent large settlements in the $100K–$400K range have improved cash flow — demonstrating that the firm's case mix includes high-value matters that, if grown systematically, can significantly improve profitability without adding headcount
The Gap
  • Knowing the numbers is not the same as having a plan to change them — the firm needs a structured profit target, a payroll percentage goal, and a monthly review process that treats profitability as an operational output rather than a year-end discovery
  • The 2027 balloon payment creates a hard deadline — without a clear financial plan that builds reserves over the next 18 months, the firm will arrive at that deadline in the same or worse financial position it is in today
  • Growing revenue without fixing the cost structure will not solve the profitability problem — $7M in revenue at 42% payroll still produces a loss; the cost structure and the growth plan must be addressed together
David can prove the growth path was right — but only if the firm is profitable enough to fund it. A clear profit plan is not the opposite of ambition; it is what makes the ambition possible.
