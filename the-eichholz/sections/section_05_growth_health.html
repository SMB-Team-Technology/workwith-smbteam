Lead Generation

  • Paid advertising — the firm's primary historical lead source — has been reduced or paused, leaving competitors to capture searches the firm once dominated

Restoring a reliable lead generation system is the only path back to the $13M revenue David built — and without it, every other improvement in the firm produces less than it should.

Intake

  • Case managers are carrying 135–143 active cases — 40% over the 100-case ideal — meaning follow-up on new leads is inconsistent at best
  • Other case managers are operating at 93–94 cases — below capacity — indicating a load-balancing failure rather than a pure staffing gap
  • A decision by one attorney to reject cases from the main referral source has created under-capacity in parts of the intake pipeline while other areas are overwhelmed
Until intake is balanced and every inbound lead gets the same consistent follow-up, the firm is leaving revenue on the table from leads it has already paid to generate.

Self-Managing Team

  • A COO and Managing Partner are in place — but a strategic stalemate between the owner and COO is blocking execution on any growth or restructuring path
  • Without a clear decision-making framework, every strategic conversation becomes a debate rather than a direction, and the firm drifts while competitors advance
  • The owner remains the final authority on major decisions, meaning the firm's trajectory is tied to David's bandwidth — not to a team that can execute independently
A firm that requires the owner's involvement in every strategic decision cannot grow back to $13M — it needs a leadership structure that can execute the plan without him in every room.

Profit

  • The firm is projecting a -$130,000 net loss on $5.2M revenue — unprofitable at a revenue level where healthy PI firms target 23–31% net margin
  • Payroll has risen from 25% to 42% of revenue as revenue declined, creating a fixed cost structure that makes profitability nearly impossible without significant restructuring
  • A $2.1 million mortgage balloon payment is due at the end of 2027, and a prior $500K line of credit was called in — creating a financial deadline that cannot be ignored
Proving that the growth path was right requires more than revenue — it requires a firm that is visibly profitable, with a clear plan to handle the 2027 balloon before it becomes a crisis.
