Here Is Where The Eichholz Law Firm Stands Today

The Eichholz Law Firm is a multi-location personal injury practice based in Savannah, Georgia — one of the most recognized PI brands in the region, built on decades of TV advertising, a dominant Google Ads presence, and a peak revenue of $13 million. Today the firm operates at $5.2M in annual revenue while projecting a net loss of -$130,000, having pulled back from the advertising spend that drove its peak growth — and the gap between what this firm once was and what it is today represents both its greatest risk and its greatest opportunity.

Stage 6 is where The Eichholz Law Firm belongs — where the marketing engine drives predictable revenue, the team executes without constant owner direction, and David can finally stand back and prove that his vision to dominate this market was right all along.

Where You Are Today

Stage 4: Small Business Manager
  • Revenue has fallen from a $13M peak to $5.2M, with the firm now projecting a -$130K net loss
  • Paid advertising has been reduced or paused, ceding ground to competitors who are actively buying the market
  • A strategic conflict between the owner and COO is blocking execution on either a growth or contraction path
  • Case managers are operating at 135–143 cases — 40% over the 100-case ideal — while others are under-utilized

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between where this firm is today and where David knows it can be is not a gap in reputation or talent — it is a gap in unified strategy, disciplined cost structure, and a marketing system that runs whether or not the owner is watching.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
The Eichholz Law Firm
The Eichholz Law Firm is operating at Stage 4 — a firm with meaningful infrastructure, a recognizable brand, and a management team in place, but one where the cost structure has spiraled as revenue declined and where the owner remains the central decision-maker on every strategic question. The most urgent limiting factor is not any single channel or tactic — it is the absence of a clear, unified growth plan that addresses both the revenue gap and the looming 2027 financial deadline before options close. The window to return this firm to its dominant position is real — but it requires moving now, not waiting for internal consensus that may never come.
