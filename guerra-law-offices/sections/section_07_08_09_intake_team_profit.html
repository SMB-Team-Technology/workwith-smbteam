Intake Assessment

With no CRM, no contact form, and no delegation of any kind, every new client conversation at Guerra Law Offices runs through Stella personally. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Stella personally handles every intake call and client conversation across three practice areas — no delegation in place
  • No CRM or intake tracking system of any kind — leads are not systematically followed up
  • No contact form on the website — visitors can only call, email, or fax
  • No after-hours coverage — inquiries outside business hours simply wait for Stella
Blind Spot: You may not realize how many personal injury leads go to whichever firm answers the phone first — every hour a call sits unanswered is an hour a competitor's intake team can pick it up instead.
The Positive
  • Referral leads — arriving pre-vetted by trusted realtors, loan officers, and past clients — convert at a naturally high rate, showing Stella's casework and client relationships are strong once someone reaches her
The Gap
  • Every unanswered after-hours call is a client who may call Rob Levine Law or d'Oliveira instead before morning
  • Without a tracking system, there is no way to know how many inquiries are being lost or how much revenue that represents
  • Real estate leads from realtors and loan officers depend entirely on Stella's personal availability to respond fast — a slow response risks the referral relationship itself
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • No contact form exists anywhere on guerralaw.org — the Contact page offers only a mailto link, phone, fax, and a static map image
  • No live chat or any real-time engagement option is present on the site
  • Every website visitor who isn't ready to pick up the phone currently has no way to leave their information

Team Assessment

Guerra Law Offices is a team of one — Stella handles casework, intake, and every operational decision with no support staff to share the load. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
"A vacation requires pausing new business."
The Problem
  • The firm has no support staff of any kind — Stella is the entire operation
  • There is no one to hand a case to, so time away from the office means turning away new business
  • No organizational structure exists to delegate intake, marketing, or administrative work
  • Growth to $1M+ under the current structure would mean Stella personally doing twice the work, not the firm doing more
Blind Spot: Stella's goal to scale as a solo practitioner and avoid partnerships is a clear, deliberate choice — but without any team at all, "solo" doesn't mean independent, it means the firm's growth ceiling is exactly Stella's personal capacity.
The Positive
  • Stella has deliberately chosen to scale without partners — a values-driven decision that keeps full control and full upside in her hands, which SMB Team's systems are built to support without requiring her to add a partner
The Gap
  • Every week Stella cannot step away is a week the firm generates zero new business
  • There is no leadership layer to absorb growth, meaning doubling revenue today would double Stella's workload, not free up her time
  • No delegation path exists for the added intake and follow-up work that fixing lead generation will generate
A self-managing team — even a small one — is what turns "more revenue" into "more freedom" instead of just "more hours."

Profit Plan Assessment

Guerra Law Offices knows its top-line revenue — $500,000 — and has set an ambitious goal of $1M+, but no profitability, margin, or cost-per-case data was discussed on the call. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No profitability or margin figures were shared or appear to be tracked
  • No stated cost-per-case or cost-per-lead benchmark exists for either practice area
  • The $1M+ revenue goal was set without a corresponding plan for the marketing or staffing spend required to reach it
  • No system was described for knowing which practice area — personal injury, real estate, or family law — is actually the most profitable
Blind Spot: Personal injury is being prioritized for its higher payouts, but without cost-per-case tracking by practice area, there is no way to confirm it is actually the more profitable line to scale.
The Positive
  • Stella has already identified personal injury and real estate as the two practice areas to prioritize for growth — a clear strategic starting point for a profit plan, even without the underlying numbers yet
The Gap
  • Without margin visibility, doubling revenue could just as easily double costs and leave take-home pay flat
  • There is no way to currently forecast what return a future marketing investment needs to produce to be worthwhile
  • Family Law is explicitly deprioritized for growth, but without cost data there is no confirmation it isn't quietly the most profitable line today
Knowing the real numbers behind $500,000 is what will tell Stella whether doubling revenue actually funds the freedom she's building toward — or just doubles the workload.
