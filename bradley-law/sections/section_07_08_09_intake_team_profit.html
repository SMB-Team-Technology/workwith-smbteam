Intake Assessment

The Bradley Law Firm uses an AI phone receptionist to capture inbound calls — an important first step toward delegation — but the intake process has significant gaps in follow-up, document logistics, and conversion that are costing Anthony cases he has already earned the right to win. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Manual follow-up for VA/elder law matters — high volume of document collection reminders and client follow-ups are handled personally by Anthony or staff rather than through an automated system
  • Wet-signature requirement for all estate planning, elder law, and probate documents extends case cycle time and creates friction for non-local clients at the Southaven, MS office and beyond
  • No defined follow-up sequence confirmed — leads that do not convert on first contact may not receive consistent, systematic outreach before choosing a competitor
  • Part-time paralegal with quality and reliability issues creates unreliable intake support during periods of higher call volume
Blind Spot: The wet-signature requirement, while legally driven by the practice areas, means every non-local client interaction has a logistics gap between engagement and signing. Without a structured system for managing signing appointments — including mobile notary coordination — some clients will disengage during that gap, and Anthony will never know they left.
The Positive
  • AI phone receptionist ("Sean") in place at $600/month — inbound calls are captured and initial intake questions are asked, providing after-hours coverage and reducing the number of leads that go entirely unanswered
The Gap
  • Each unanswered VA/elder law document request is a delay in a case that is already signed — slow case velocity directly reduces Anthony's throughput and effective earnings per hour
  • Without a defined follow-up sequence, warm leads who do not sign immediately are being lost to competitors who respond faster and follow up more consistently
  • Wet-signature logistics without a systematic process for managing signing appointments elongate every case by days or weeks, reducing the number of cases Anthony can complete in a month even when fully booked
Intake is where working smarter begins — a system that converts and manages clients without Anthony's direct involvement frees him from being the bottleneck between a new lead and a signed client.

  • No live chat observed on the website — after-hours visitors who reach the site outside business hours have no immediate engagement option beyond the contact form

Team Assessment

The Bradley Law Firm runs on Anthony Bradley's personal effort — with one part-time paralegal as the only support and a recently vacated full-time position adding to the capacity strain, there is no team structure in place to support growth without the owner doing more. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Solo attorney plus one part-time paralegal — not enough capacity to delegate meaningful work with confidence
  • Recently lost a key full-time staff member, increasing Anthony's direct workload at the exact moment the firm needs to scale
  • Paralegal quality issues — missed instructions on probate and conservatorship pleadings require Anthony to review and redo work, adding oversight burden that erodes his trust in delegation
  • Duplicative data entry between PracticePanther and WealthCounsel adds administrative overhead to every case, consuming staff time that could be spent on higher-value tasks
Blind Spot: The quality issues with the current paralegal are not just an efficiency problem — they are a trust problem. When delegation produces unreliable output, owners instinctively pull work back and do it themselves. That instinct, while understandable, will make it harder to delegate even after the right people are hired.
The Positive
  • Anthony's investment in the AI phone receptionist demonstrates a willingness to adopt systems that work without him — this is the mindset that makes a self-managing firm possible
The Gap
  • Without a reliable team, Anthony cannot take on more volume, cannot take time away from the office, and cannot grow revenue without growing his personal hours — the exact constraint he has identified as the problem
  • Every hour Anthony spends reviewing paralegal rework or handling tasks that should be delegated is an hour not spent on billable work, business development, or strategic decisions
  • A firm that cannot run without the owner is a job, not a business — and a job cannot be scaled, sold, or stepped away from on Anthony's terms
Building the right team with the right systems is how Anthony transforms from the firm's most indispensable employee into its most valuable asset.

Profit Plan Assessment

Anthony stated clearly that he wants to increase his earnings by "working smarter, not just raising prices" — meaning the path to more profit runs through better systems and visibility, not just more revenue. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No revenue figure stated during the call — financial visibility is not yet formalized to the level of tracking key metrics like cost per acquisition, revenue per case type, or profit margin
  • Capacity ceiling reached with earnings tied directly to Anthony's hours — the firm is generating revenue, but that revenue cannot grow without Anthony working more
  • Duplicative data entry between PracticePanther and WealthCounsel is a confirmed, named inefficiency — every duplicated entry is time that Anthony or staff spend not earning
  • AI receptionist at $600/month is the only confirmed marketing spend — no visibility into cost per acquisition or return on that investment
Blind Spot: Being fully booked sounds like a success metric — but without tracking cost per case by practice area, Anthony may be filling his calendar with lower-margin work when higher-margin opportunities exist. Estate planning, VA/elder law, and probate have meaningfully different revenue and time profiles; knowing which cases to prioritize is a profit decision, not just a scheduling one.
The Positive
  • 28 years of consistent practice with a stable client base confirms the firm generates real, recurring revenue — the foundation for a meaningful profit plan already exists
The Gap
  • Without a formal profit plan, growth in revenue tends to mean growth in overhead and workload, not growth in what Anthony actually takes home
  • No tracking of marketing spend ROI means it is impossible to know whether the current $600/month AI receptionist investment is generating cases or just capturing calls
  • The duplicative data entry between PracticePanther and WealthCounsel is a known drag on capacity — in a solo practice, every wasted hour is directly subtracted from billable output
Working smarter with money means knowing exactly where every dollar goes and which levers to pull to earn more of it — because you cannot optimize what you have not measured.
