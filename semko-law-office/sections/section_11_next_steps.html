Implement a 3-Step Lead Follow-Up Sequence
Intake Long-Term Play
Why It Matters
Without a defined follow-up system, a web lead who submits a form but does not get a same-day response will likely retain another firm. Criminal defense prospects are comparing multiple attorneys simultaneously — speed and consistency of follow-up is often the deciding factor, not price or reputation.
The Opportunity
A 3-step follow-up sequence — immediate text confirmation upon form submission, a call within 30 minutes, and a next-day follow-up for non-responses — would significantly increase the percentage of web leads that convert to retained clients without generating a single additional lead.

Why It Matters
Every hour an attorney spends on intake screening, answering basic inquiries, or qualifying new leads is an hour not spent on billable casework. As marketing brings in more leads, attorney-handled intake becomes the bottleneck that prevents conversion and caps revenue regardless of how much is spent on advertising.
Who Is Doing It Better
Stracci Law Group — a 7-attorney firm with a full support infrastructure means their intake team converts leads around the clock while their attorneys focus exclusively on casework and client results.
The Opportunity
A trained intake coordinator who handles first contact, qualification, and scheduling frees both attorneys to focus on billable work — and at the revenue levels marketing is designed to produce, the coordinator's salary is recovered within the first retained case each month.
