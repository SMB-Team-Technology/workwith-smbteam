Here Is Where Farias Firm (David Farias) Stands Today

David Farias launched Farias Trial Law in early 2026 as a solo PI litigation firm in San Antonio, immediately generating referral cases through his Texas Trial Lawyers Association network by positioning the firm as a trial-ready resource for pre-litigation firms that lack courtroom capacity. The firm's single most urgent challenge is that it has zero digital infrastructure — no functional website, no Google Business Profile, no reviews, and no paid advertising — entering one of the most competitive PI markets in Texas while established competitors already dominate every visible digital channel.

Stage 6 is the foundation David needs to reach to stop running a job and start leading a firm — so his best years in the courtroom are spent pursuing board certification and jury trials, not chasing records and answering after-hours calls at 70 hours a week.

Where You Are Today

Stage 3: Solo Practitioner
  • All cases come from TTLA referrals — no scalable or predictable lead generation system in place
  • David handles every intake call, client contact, casework, and administrative task personally
  • No staff on payroll; contract paralegals at $60–$110/hr are cost-prohibitive for routine tasks
  • Self-funding all litigation costs with cash flow entirely dependent on future settlements

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
David has the reputation and the referrals to prove the firm has real demand — but without systems, every case he takes comes at the cost of 60–70 hours of his own time, with no path to the focused litigation career he is building the firm to support.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Farias Firm (David Farias)
David is operating at Stage 3 — a Solo Practitioner whose strong network credibility has produced immediate referral flow, but whose business infrastructure is not yet built to support any of the growth he is describing. The single most urgent gap is the complete absence of a digital presence: no functional website, no Google Business Profile, no reviews, and no paid advertising while established competitors have spent years building the review counts, 3-pack rankings, and multi-channel campaigns that generate consistent inbound volume independent of any referral network. Until a digital foundation is built and staffing begins, growth depends entirely on David's personal connections — leaving the firm's future as unpredictable as its current cash flow, and David as its only operating asset.
