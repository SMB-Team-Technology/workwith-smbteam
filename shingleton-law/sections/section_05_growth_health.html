Lead Generation

Intake

  • Adam personally handles every initial consultation and his ~90% close rate is a genuine strength — but it means the entire intake conversion system is built around one person and cannot scale
  • No defined follow-up sequence exists for leads who do not book immediately — inquiries that do not convert on first contact are likely being left uncontacted

If intake cannot run without Adam on every call, then statewide scale requires him to be in multiple places at once — which defeats the entire purpose of building a self-managing firm.

Self-Managing Team

  • The team includes a tech co-founder (Zach Milne) who has already built the efficiency platform — this is a meaningful structural advantage over a typical solo practitioner

  • No intake coordinator, no documented SOPs, and no system for training new hires — geographic expansion will stall without these in place
The technology platform Zach built is the foundation for a self-managing firm — but the team structure to operate it at scale does not exist yet.

Profit

  • At ~$125,000 per year, revenue is below sustainable scale for a PLLC with statewide ambitions — the transition from estate planning to probate-only is still in progress and creates short-term revenue uncertainty
  • The technology platform dramatically reduces the time cost of each probate case, which means margin per case is genuinely higher than competitors — but no financial model has been built to track or project this advantage
  • No confirmed profit visibility model — Adam has not described tracking case acquisition cost, profit per case type, or monthly cash flow targets against his growth milestones
The path from $125K to $10M requires knowing exactly what each case costs to acquire, what it earns, and what milestone triggers the next phase — without that model, growth is guesswork.
