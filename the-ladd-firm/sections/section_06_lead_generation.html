Lead Generation

The Ladd Firm already runs Google PPC and LSA at roughly $6,000/month and has built out 20+ city-specific landing pages across Alabama and Mississippi — real infrastructure most local competitors do not have. What is missing is a cohesive, data-driven plan connecting these channels into one system instead of several separate efforts.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • A live mobile and desktop speed test could not complete during this research session due to a rate-limited testing tool — a full technical performance audit is recommended as part of onboarding to confirm load times
  • Contact form sits below the fold, with name, phone, email, and message fields — visitors have to scroll and commit before reaching it
  • No live chat to capture visitors who are not ready to call
  • Directory listings show a NAP (Name/Address/Phone) inconsistency: FindLaw still lists the old "51 Tacon St Ste E" address while the current site and Yelp list "41 W. I-65 Service Rd. N., Suite 305" — a mismatch that can quietly cost local search ranking
Blind Spot: Prospects cross-referencing your address on FindLaw versus your current site or Yelp see two different locations — small inconsistencies like this are exactly what erodes trust and local search visibility without ever showing up as an obvious complaint.
The Positive
  • Strong above-the-fold CTA ("Call Now to Request a Free Telephone Consultation!") with the phone number repeated for easy access
  • Attorney bio, educational video content, and an active blog are all in place — the blog's posting cadence has visibly increased in 2026, with the most recent post dated July 14, 2026
The Gap
  • Every visitor who has to scroll past the fold to find the contact form is a visitor who may leave and call a competitor instead
  • A NAP inconsistency across directories can quietly suppress local map rankings — the exact channel driving much of the firm's current traffic
  • Without a live chat option, after-hours and hesitant visitors have no lower-commitment way to engage before deciding to call
A website that captures every visitor — not just the ones ready to call immediately — is what turns Banks's existing traffic into meetings without adding a single hour to his day.

The Positive

The Gap

Southern Estate Lawyers — markets "200+ five-star reviews" firm-wide as a brand claim and already holds physical locations in the Foley/Daphne/Gulf Coast markets The Ladd Firm is discussing expanding into.
The reputation is already built — turning it into a system that runs on its own is what frees up the hours Banks currently spends producing new business himself.
