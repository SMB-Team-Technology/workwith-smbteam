Here Is Where The Ladd Firm, LLC Stands Today

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • A live mobile and desktop speed test could not complete during this research session due to a rate-limited testing tool — a full technical performance audit is recommended as part of onboarding to confirm load times
  • Contact form sits below the fold, with name, phone, email, and message fields — visitors have to scroll and commit before reaching it

  • Directory listings show a NAP (Name/Address/Phone) inconsistency: FindLaw still lists the old "51 Tacon St Ste E" address while the current site and Yelp list "41 W. I-65 Service Rd. N., Suite 305" — a mismatch that can quietly cost local search ranking
Blind Spot: Prospects cross-referencing your address on FindLaw versus your current site or Yelp see two different locations — small inconsistencies like this are exactly what erodes trust and local search visibility without ever showing up as an obvious complaint.
The Positive

  • Attorney bio, educational video content, and an active blog are all in place — the blog's posting cadence has visibly increased in 2026, with the most recent post dated July 14, 2026
The Gap

  • A NAP inconsistency across directories can quietly suppress local map rankings — the exact channel driving much of the firm's current traffic
