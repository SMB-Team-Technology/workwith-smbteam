Lead Generation

The Thomson Law Firm has built 29 years of exceptional results on referrals and organic search alone — but in Roanoke's personal injury market, competitors are running full-channel paid campaigns that capture the clients who need a catastrophic injury attorney before they ever encounter Paul Thomson. The firm's credibility and credentials far exceed its digital visibility, and that gap costs the firm qualified catastrophic cases every single day.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Contact form is buried below the fold on the homepage and mid-page on the dedicated contact page — visitors arriving ready to inquire must scroll before they can act
  • PageSpeed scores: unavailable at time of research — PageSpeed Insights API returned a rate-limit error; manual scores should be obtained at pagespeed.web.dev before the sales call
  • No video content on the homepage — a brief introduction from Paul speaking to his background as a former insurance company claims representative would be a uniquely powerful trust signal no competitor can replicate
  • Blog post dates are not displayed, and the most recent post appears to date to approximately June 2025 — stale content signals inactivity to Google's crawler and can suppress organic rankings
Blind Spot: The homepage headline "Your Serious Injury, Our Priority" accurately reflects the firm's focus, but does not immediately surface the $21 million verdict or Paul's former insurance company background — the two differentiators most likely to convert a serious injury prospect who is comparing multiple attorneys at once.
The Positive
  • Attorney bio page is prominent and comprehensive — 29+ years experience, Avvo 10.0, Super Lawyers, former Senior Claims Representative background all present above the fold on the bio page
  • Results page featuring verdicts from $21M down to $100K is a powerful trust signal for catastrophic injury prospects — few competitors in the Roanoke market can show comparable case results
The Gap
  • Without an above-fold contact form and clear primary CTA visible immediately, visitors from paid ads or organic search leave without converting — each lost conversion is a potential catastrophic case gone to a competitor
  • Stale blog content (last observed post ~June 2025) sends a diminishing freshness signal to Google, which can suppress organic rankings at the exact moment competitor content is climbing
  • No geo-specific landing pages for Salem, Lynchburg, Blacksburg, Christiansburg, or Abingdon — the firm serves Southwest Virginia with two offices but only ranks for generic Roanoke terms
A website built to convert at its full potential means Paul stops losing qualified catastrophic injury prospects to competitors with lower credentials and fewer results — and every new case from existing traffic costs zero additional marketing spend.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • ~101 Google reviews at 5.0 stars — strong quality, but mathematically disadvantaged in the 3-pack against Crandall & Katt's 316–336 reviews; Google's algorithm heavily weights review volume in local rankings
  • Entity name inconsistency across directories: "The Thomson Law Firm, PC" in some listings and "Thomson Law Firm, PLLC" in others (Yelp, Nextdoor) — this PC vs. PLLC discrepancy creates citation confusion that suppresses local SEO rankings
  • FindLaw profile has zero reviews, a generic placeholder photo, and no populated practice area descriptions — a weak presence on a high-authority legal directory that Google references in local rankings
  • Duplicate Yelp and Nextdoor listings with different entity names create conflicting citation signals that Google interprets as data quality issues
Blind Spot: The PC vs. PLLC naming inconsistency across Yelp and Nextdoor may appear minor, but Google's local ranking algorithm treats citation consistency as a trust signal. In a market where Crandall & Katt holds a 3× review advantage, any additional ranking suppression from inconsistent citations further widens the gap on every search that matters.
The Positive
  • 5.0-star Google rating across 101 reviews is a genuine differentiator — the quality and credibility are already there; the volume simply needs to be systematically grown to match
The Gap
  • Without consistent, high-volume reviews — especially at the pace needed to close a 215+ review gap with Crandall & Katt — the firm will not break into the 3-pack for competitive PI terms in Roanoke
  • The duplicate listings and PC/PLLC inconsistency, if left unresolved, will continue suppressing rankings at the exact searches where Thomson's credentials should make him the clear choice
Crandall & Katt — 316–336 Google reviews at 4.5 stars, holds the top 3-pack position on nearly every personal injury keyword in Roanoke. Their review volume advantage is not closing on its own — it requires a systematic, post-settlement review request process to overtake.
Winning the local 3-pack on catastrophic injury searches in Roanoke and Southwest Virginia means the highest-value prospects find Paul Thomson first — before Crandall & Katt, Monge & Associates, or any other competitor gets the call.
