Intake Assessment

The Thomson Law Firm's website has no live chat, a contact form buried below the fold, and no confirmed after-hours coverage — which means high-value catastrophic injury prospects who cannot immediately reach someone are calling a competitor who picks up first. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Contact form is not visible above the fold on the homepage or the dedicated contact page — visitors who arrive ready to act must scroll before they can submit an inquiry
  • No live chat functionality on the website — prospects who prefer immediate digital contact during business hours have no way to reach the firm online
  • No confirmed after-hours intake coverage — calls arriving evenings or weekends are likely going to voicemail, and catastrophic injury prospects in the immediate aftermath of an accident will call the next attorney who answers
  • No observed intake qualification process to pre-screen incoming leads against the firm's catastrophic PI focus — lower-value cases may be consuming time that should be reserved for Thomson's highest-value work
Blind Spot: For a catastrophic injury firm, intake speed matters exponentially more than for a commodity PI practice. The first attorney a victim's family reaches in the hours after a serious accident is almost always the attorney who gets retained. A competitor with 24/7 intake coverage and an immediate callback protocol is systematically capturing the most motivated — and most valuable — prospects before Paul's office opens the next morning.
The Positive
  • Two office locations with dedicated contact information in both Roanoke and Abingdon create multiple geographic points of access for prospects across Southwest Virginia
The Gap
  • Every un-captured after-hours call or buried contact form abandonment is a potential catastrophic case lost — with average case values in the range Paul's results page demonstrates, each missed case represents a significant and unrecoverable revenue gap
  • Competitors running 24/7 intake protocols — including those with far lower credentials and smaller case results — are systematically winning the cases Paul should be getting first, simply by answering sooner
  • Without intake tracking, there is no way to quantify how many qualified catastrophic injury prospects are lost monthly — which means the problem is invisible even as it compounds
Systematized intake means Paul receives only the highest-value, pre-qualified catastrophic cases — without needing to personally manage every inquiry or be the first call that gets answered at midnight.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Homepage contact form requires scrolling to the lower portion of the page — not positioned for conversion from paid traffic or organic search visitors arriving with high intent
  • Dedicated contact page at /contact-us/ places the form mid-page rather than at the top — a missed opportunity to convert visitors at the highest-intent moment of their visit
  • No live chat widget is present anywhere on the site — a growing expectation among personal injury prospects who prefer immediate digital response, especially after hours

Team Assessment

Paul Thomson III has built a two-office practice over 29 years, but the firm's revenue, client relationships, and daily operations appear to remain centered on Paul personally — with no observed systems for delegation, performance accountability, or independent team function. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • The firm's reputation, lead flow, and client relationships are centered on Paul personally — his name and credentials are the brand, with no observed system that operates independently of his involvement
  • No observed dedicated intake coordinator, case manager, or operations role to handle lead qualification and client onboarding separately from Paul's case work
  • Two office locations without a confirmed management or accountability structure means Paul is likely the primary decision hub for both Roanoke and Abingdon — doubling the operational demands on a single person
  • No visible evidence of documented processes, performance metrics, or SOPs that would allow a team to maintain the firm's service standard without Paul's direct oversight
Blind Spot: The greatest risk in a reputation-based practice built on one attorney's credentials is that the brand equity Paul has invested 29 years building is entirely non-transferable without systems. If Paul steps back from day-to-day involvement tomorrow, the firm has no documented infrastructure to maintain the client experience, lead qualification process, or case intake standard independently.
The Positive
  • Two office locations and 16 years of continuous operation indicate that Paul has built real operational infrastructure — this is not a pure solo practice with zero support
The Gap
  • Without a self-managing intake and operations function, Paul cannot focus exclusively on the highest-value catastrophic cases — administrative and triage responsibilities consume time he should be spending on the work that generates his best results
  • The firm cannot sustainably handle higher case volume without Paul's direct involvement at every stage — growth becomes a ceiling rather than an opportunity
  • Paul cannot take extended leave or step back from day-to-day decisions without revenue and client service deteriorating — the firm's income remains dependent on his daily presence
Building a team that handles intake, operations, and client communication without Paul's constant involvement is the only path to focusing exclusively on catastrophic cases — and to the financial freedom that comes with building a firm that runs itself.

Profit Plan Assessment

Without paid advertising channels, confirmed intake tracking, or observable financial visibility infrastructure, The Thomson Law Firm's revenue is driven entirely by whatever referrals and organic search happen to deliver — making profitability dependent on case settlement timing rather than deliberate strategy. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No active paid advertising means the firm has no controllable marketing variable — revenue cannot be reliably increased by investing more in what works because no system tracks what works
  • Without intake tracking or case source data, there is no way to calculate cost per case, conversion rate from lead to retained client, or marketing ROI across any channel
  • Catastrophic PI settlement timing creates unpredictable revenue cycles — large cases resolve irregularly, creating income volatility that systematic case pipeline management would reduce
  • No observed financial visibility infrastructure (dashboards, KPI tracking, profit-by-case-type analysis) to identify which case types or referral sources drive the most profitable work
Blind Spot: In catastrophic personal injury, high average case values can create the illusion of profitability even when overhead, contingency expenses, and operational inefficiencies are eroding margins. Without case-level profitability tracking, Paul may not know which types of cases — TBI, wrongful death, truck accident, medical malpractice — are actually most profitable per hour invested, and may be accepting work that costs more than it returns.
The Positive
  • Catastrophic PI with $21M+ verdicts creates the potential for exceptional case-level profitability — the revenue capacity is real; the systems to capture and track it consistently are what is missing
The Gap
  • Without marketing ROI data, every dollar spent on overhead and operations is unoptimized — the firm cannot reinvest intelligently in the channels and case types that generate the highest returns
  • Unpredictable case settlement timing creates cash flow volatility that a systematic pipeline approach and financial forecasting would stabilize
  • The firm cannot make informed decisions about hiring, second-office investment, or marketing expansion without visibility into where revenue actually comes from and what it costs to generate
The financial security Paul has spent 29 years building toward — the ability to choose which cases to take, step back from operations, and know exactly what the firm earns — requires knowing where every dollar comes from and where every dollar goes.
