Intake Assessment

Rose Harper Law has the right instincts — an above-fold contact form, visible phone numbers, and a free consultation offer — but without a confirmed intake team, after-hours coverage, or a defined follow-up system across three office locations, those instincts are not translating into the case conversion rate the firm deserves. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • No live chat or confirmed after-hours intake coverage — prospects who visit the website in the evening or on weekends, often the most urgent injury leads, have no immediate option to connect with anyone at the firm
  • No confirmed intake team or dedicated coordinator across three offices in three states — with a solo or small-team structure, intake calls most likely route directly to Rose or go unanswered outside business hours
  • No confirmed follow-up sequence for form submissions — prospects who submit the contact form but do not immediately sign are likely receiving no structured outreach to bring them back to conversion
  • Three offices across three states without a centralized intake system creates inconsistency in how prospects are handled depending on which location they contact, which practice area they need, and what time of day they reach out
Blind Spot: Personal injury leads are among the fastest-decaying lead types in legal — studies consistently show that firms that respond within five minutes of a form submission are dramatically more likely to qualify the lead than those who respond after 30 minutes. Every evening and weekend without after-hours coverage, those leads are going to the first firm that answers — and they are not calling back.
The Positive
  • Above-fold contact form with SMS consent and a clear free consultation CTA demonstrates the right instinct to capture leads at the moment of first visit — most PI firms bury their forms
  • Multiple visible phone numbers across all three office locations give prospects an immediate call option, reducing friction for the subset of leads who prefer to call over fill out a form
The Gap
  • Every after-hours lead that goes to voicemail is potentially a case lost to a competitor who answered — in personal injury, leads do not wait until morning, and they do not call back when they find another firm that was available
  • Without a defined follow-up sequence for form submissions, the firm is effectively treating every non-immediate signup as a dead lead — when the data consistently shows that most cases sign after 2 to 5 follow-up touchpoints
  • Without centralized intake tracking across three offices, there is no way to know which location, which channel, or which referral source is producing the most qualified cases — making it impossible to double down on what is working
Rose cannot step back from the day-to-day until someone else is consistently converting inbound leads into signed clients — intake is the system that makes everything else Rose builds actually pay off.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • The above-fold contact form requests name, email, phone, practice area, accident date, and message — comprehensive for lead qualification but high friction for an injured prospect who needs help immediately and may abandon mid-form
  • No live chat widget observed on the website — a significant gap for after-hours lead capture in a practice area where injury urgency drives immediate decision-making; prospects who cannot reach a live person will call the next firm on the list
  • Phone numbers are prominently displayed across the site with a "Call Now" CTA — this immediate-contact option is well-positioned for prospects ready to call, but those who prefer digital contact have no real-time option outside business hours

These findings are also referenced in the Website Audit section.

Team Assessment

Rose Harper Law has expanded to three offices across three states, which required building some operational structure — but the evidence suggests Rose remains the central driver of firm growth, client acquisition, and operational decisions, without a confirmed management layer capable of running the firm independently. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Team size not confirmed — the website has a /team-members/ page suggesting staff exists, but no team structure, roles, or accountability systems are visible from the outside
  • Rose is prominently featured as founding attorney across all marketing materials, all three office locations, and all practice areas — creating a brand and operation that depends on her personal presence and credibility to function
  • Operating across NY, NJ, and PA simultaneously without a confirmed operations manager means Rose likely coordinates significant cross-state decisions personally, compounding the bottleneck across jurisdictions
  • No confirmed intake manager, marketing coordinator, or dedicated operations lead — the roles that would allow Rose to step back from daily client-facing and administrative decisions are not confirmed
Blind Spot: Expanding to three offices is evidence of real ambition — but more offices without more delegation means more complexity, not more freedom. The infrastructure that could support a self-managing team already exists; the systems and roles that activate it do not.
The Positive
  • Three-office, three-state operation demonstrates that Rose has already built beyond a true solo practice — the infrastructure and geographic reach exist to support a significant team once roles are defined and accountability is established
The Gap
  • Without a team that can manage intake, client communications, and case coordination independently, Rose's caseload capacity is limited by her personal hours — capping both revenue and the time she can reclaim for family and teaching
  • Every hour Rose spends on operational coordination or intake decisions is an hour she is not practicing law, building the firm strategically, or being present for her three children and her students at Mercy University
  • Competitors like Brandon J. Broderick operate multi-location PI firms with dedicated intake teams and operational management — allowing them to capture and convert far more leads per marketing dollar than a firm where the founder is in the flow of every decision
The version of Rose Harper Law that gives Rose real freedom — time for her children, her teaching, and her own life — requires a team that makes decisions without waiting for her, and systems that ensure those decisions are the right ones.

Profit Plan Assessment

With no revenue or financial data confirmed from a discovery call, this assessment is based on the structural realities of a contingency-fee PI firm operating across three offices in three states — a model that requires consistent case volume to generate consistent profit. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Revenue, take-home income, and profit margins are not available — without a financial picture from the discovery call, there is no baseline to measure growth against or plan profit around
  • Contingency-fee PI model creates inherently variable cash flow — cases settle unpredictably, creating revenue spikes and valleys that are difficult to manage without a clear profit plan and cash reserve strategy
  • Three-office overhead across NY, NJ, and PA — including separate rents, multi-state bar compliance costs, and staff — requires consistent case flow to sustain, and any revenue gap compounds quickly across three locations
  • Without tracking revenue and profitability by office location, practice area, and referral source, there is no data to guide decisions about where to invest marketing dollars and where to cut costs
Blind Spot: Without tracking profitability by practice area and office location, some of the firm's most profitable opportunities may be systematically under-resourced while high-overhead, lower-margin areas consume budget and attention. Growing revenue without this visibility means the firm could be scaling the wrong things.
The Gap
  • Without visibility into which cases and which channels are most profitable, marketing spend cannot be optimized — meaning some ad dollars will inevitably fund the wrong case types or the wrong markets
  • A predictable revenue system built on paid lead generation and strong intake is the first prerequisite for a profit plan — without consistent case volume, there is no financial data stable enough to plan around
  • The gap between what Rose is currently earning and what she could take home with proper financial visibility and a structured profit plan is likely substantial — and impossible to close without knowing the current numbers
Building a firm that generates the kind of take-home wealth that lets Rose be fully present for her family and her teaching — without financial anxiety — starts with knowing exactly where every dollar is coming from and going.
