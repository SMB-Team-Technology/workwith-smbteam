Pillar Health Scorecard

Lead Generation

  • Google Ads and LSAs — the firm's only lead generation channel — are currently paused while recovering from a website malware incident; the new campaign has not yet launched
  • All major directories (Yelp, Google, BBB, FindLaw) still show the pre-fire address (14401 Sylvan St), creating a NAP inconsistency that suppresses local search rankings and misleads potential clients
  • No social media presence, no content strategy, no retargeting — while Eisner Gorin and Shouse Law Group actively dominate multiple channels including paid search, organic, and geo-targeted landing pages
A predictable flow of high-quality misdemeanor and DUI cases near the San Fernando and Burbank courthouses is the foundation — without it, the "autopilot and profit" model never gets built.

Intake

  • Dan personally handles every intake inquiry — no intake coordinator, no defined process, no CRM to track leads or follow up with prospects who did not sign immediately
  • No after-hours coverage identified — a criminal defense prospect who cannot reach someone after 5 PM will call the next firm on the list
  • No defined follow-up sequence for prospects who do not retain immediately, meaning the firm is paying for leads it is not fully converting
Fixing intake is the fastest way to increase revenue from the marketing dollars already being spent — and the first step toward delegating work so Dan can step back.

Self-Managing Team

  • Dan is the entire firm — no full-time associate, no intake staff, no operations support; he personally handles intake, casework, court appearances, and business decisions
  • Uses outside appearance counsel for some court dates, but this is not a delegation structure — it is a workaround that still requires Dan to manage, brief, and oversee every case
  • No accountability structure, no job descriptions, no team to hand work to — which means the firm cannot grow without Dan working more hours
Until Dan has a team that can run cases and intake without him, he cannot step back — and the "hand it off to a younger attorney" exit he wants is impossible to execute.

Profit

  • Revenue is not confirmed but the firm has a meaningful history of ad spend (historical Google Ads campaigns significant enough to generate ~$300K in debt), indicating real revenue — but financial clarity and planning are absent
  • No confirmed profit plan, no visibility into cost per case or margins by case type — the shift toward higher-volume misdemeanor work has not been financially modeled
  • The $300K Google Ads debt (nearly paid off) resulted from an unmonitored campaign running without oversight — a symptom of the absence of financial controls and accountability
Dan needs the firm to be a financial asset he can sell or hand off — that requires building a business with clear, predictable profit, not just revenue that depends on his hours.
