Lead Generation

Granados Law Group is actively investing in paid search through Rise Up Marketing — but ads pause at night and on weekends, ROI is unmeasured, and the firm has no system to capture the after-hours DWI and criminal arrest calls that represent its highest-value opportunities. The infrastructure is in place; what is missing is the management, measurement, and 24/7 coverage that turns spending into consistent growth.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Contact form is buried below the fold — visitors must scroll past hero imagery, practice area links, and testimonials before reaching a way to contact the firm; leads who don't see an immediate CTA will leave
  • PageSpeed scores: Mobile and Desktop scores not retrieved due to API quota limitations — verify at pagespeed.web.dev before final delivery
  • No video above the fold — attorney credibility content (Lindsey's trial experience, case results) requires visitors to dig deeper into the site rather than building trust immediately on arrival
  • No visible trust signals above the fold — no bar admissions, no notable case results, no credentials displayed at the top of the homepage where first impressions are made
The Positive
  • Site is mobile responsive with a modern design, .webp image optimization, and a hamburger menu — solid technical foundation
  • Separate geo-targeting pages exist for Raleigh, Cary, and Apex, and the blog features a current 2026 post — the site is being maintained
The Gap
  • Every visitor who cannot find a contact form above the fold and leaves without converting is a potential case going to Saad Law or Amy Whinery Osborne — at $4,000–$50,000 per case, this is not a small number
  • Without a conversion-optimized above-fold experience, paid ad spend is generating clicks that the website is not capturing — every unoptimized landing page is wasted ad budget
  • Competitors who have moved CTA buttons, click-to-call, and attorney credentials above the fold consistently convert at higher rates than sites that make visitors search for a way to connect
A website that converts is the foundation of every lead generation system — and a firm whose website does not convert cannot step back from the business, because every missed lead requires someone to chase it down manually.

  • Critical gap: ads are paused at night, per Lindsey's own statement — meaning the firm goes dark during the hours when DWI arrests, criminal incidents, and high-value felony emergencies are most likely to generate urgent search activity
  • Marketing ROI from these campaigns is unknown — Lindsey stated she "doesn't know if they're performing," meaning budget is being spent without a measured return
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Saad Law — runs aggressive 24/7 Google Ads campaigns in Wake County with former DA credentialing as the central message; dominates high-intent searches like "DWI attorney Raleigh" and "criminal defense Wake County" around the clock
  • Minick Law — multi-office NC DWI specialist running consistent DWI-specific campaigns; captures the high-intent DWI searcher segment that Granados Law Group's after-hours pause misses entirely

The Opportunity
  • Enable 24/7 campaign coverage — particularly Thursday through Sunday evenings, when DWI traffic stops and criminal arrests spike; this single change could generate 2–4 additional qualified cases per month from currently missed after-hours calls
  • Expand to felony-specific ad groups targeting high-value keywords ("felony defense attorney Cary," "weapons charges Wake County," "sex crimes attorney NC") to attract the higher-value case mix Lindsey is strategically pursuing

Blind Spot: The after-hours ad pause is not a cost-saving measure — it is a revenue-destroying one. DWI arrests happen between 10pm and 3am on weekends. The family members and defendants searching for a DWI attorney at midnight on a Saturday are looking for the first credible firm that answers. Saad Law and Minick Law are answering. Granados Law Group is not.
