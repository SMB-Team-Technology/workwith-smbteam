Intake Assessment

Granados Law Group has an exceptional 80% consult-to-sign rate — but that rate only applies to prospects who actually reach someone, and every evening and weekend the firm goes completely dark with no answering service and no ads running. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
"I work 11-12 hours/day and fear revenue will stop during my upcoming 2-week vacation."
The Problem
  • No after-hours answering service — the firm previously used one at approximately $1,000/month, decided it was not worth the cost, and now all evening and weekend calls go unanswered or to voicemail
  • LSA ads and Google Ads pause at night, compounding the after-hours gap — not only is no one available to answer, but the firm is not even visible to prospects searching during those hours
  • Lindsey personally handles all high-value felony intake consultations, charging a $350 consult fee and quoting cases herself; when she is unavailable, those cases do not get quoted
  • No structured intake protocol for the associate-handled cases — quoting, follow-up timing, and conversion processes are inconsistent and undocumented
Blind Spot: A criminal defense firm's highest-value leads arrive on Friday and Saturday nights. DWI arrests happen between 10pm and 3am, and the family calls that same night looking for an attorney. Those calls are going directly to Saad Law, Coolidge Law Firm, and every other firm that has a 24/7 answering system — and they are signing those clients before Monday morning arrives.
The Positive
  • An 80% consult-to-sign rate is exceptional — the team is described as sales-minded and coachable, which means the raw conversion skill is already there
  • The associate handles lower-value case intake, which means there is already some delegation of the intake function — the structure for further delegation exists
The Gap
  • At $4,000–$50,000 per case, every missed after-hours call represents a significant uncaptured revenue opportunity — even 2 missed DWI cases per month at $7,500 average represents $15,000/month in recoverable revenue on a relatively small answering service investment
  • Lindsey's bottleneck on high-value felony intake ties the firm's highest-margin revenue directly to her personal availability — any absence, vacation, or illness creates an immediate revenue gap
  • Without a 24/7 intake system, the firm's exceptional 80% close rate is only being applied to a fraction of the potential leads that are searching for help
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact form is buried below the fold on the homepage — a visitor arriving from a Google ad or LSA click who does not immediately see a way to reach the firm is likely to leave without converting
  • No live chat or immediate callback option is visible — a prospect in a high-stress criminal situation wants instant acknowledgment, and a form that promises a response "within 24 hours" will lose to a competitor who answers immediately
  • The firm has a Clio payment integration embedded on the site, which suggests some digital client infrastructure exists — but intake conversion and payment are not the same problem

Team Assessment

Granados Law Group has a team of six that is only 3–4 months into its most stable period in three years — real progress after a long revolving door — but Lindsey remains the decision-maker and bottleneck for every high-value function in the firm. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
"I need to rebuild a stable team so the firm can run without me."
The Problem
  • Lindsey is the sole decision-maker for all high-value felony cases — intake, quoting, and case strategy run through her personally, regardless of whether the associate or support staff could handle elements of that work
  • No documented processes, playbooks, or standard operating procedures — the team functions on Lindsey's institutional knowledge, meaning her absence is a firm-wide systems failure
  • Lindsey spends 1–2 hours per day on low-value administrative tasks that represent approximately $3,000/week in lost billable opportunity at her $350/hour consult rate — this is time that should be spent on high-value client work or firm development
  • Three years of staff turnover prior to the current team created justified reluctance to delegate — the firm has been conditioned to run through Lindsey because trusting staff has historically led to problems
Blind Spot: The stability of the last 3–4 months is genuine progress and should not be dismissed — but it is also fragile. Without documented systems and a clear management structure, the current stability depends on circumstances staying the same. One bad hire or key departure puts the firm back into the chaos of the prior three years.
The Positive
  • Team is described as "sales-minded and coachable" — the raw material for a self-managing team is already present in the people Lindsey has assembled
  • Three to four months of team stability after firing the toxic office manager is the longest stable stretch in three years — the foundation is being laid
The Gap
  • Lindsey cannot take a two-week vacation without fearing the firm will stop — this is not a hypothetical concern, it is the reality she described on the call, and it is the direct personal cost of owner dependency
  • A firm that runs through its owner has no market value beyond the owner's personal willingness to keep showing up — it cannot be sold, cannot be scaled, and cannot fund college or retirement without Lindsey's continued presence
  • Every hour Lindsey spends on administrative tasks is an hour she is not developing the team, building client relationships, or working on cases that pay $15,000–$50,000 — the opportunity cost is measured in the gap between $750K and $1.25M
Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Profit Plan Assessment

Granados Law Group generated approximately $750,000 last year — down from a $1.25 million peak before the 2023 rebrand — and Lindsey has no financial plan connecting today's revenue to the goal she has described. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: RED
"We're in the black."
The Problem
  • No revenue forecasting, budget planning, or profit tracking — Lindsey described financial management as "fly by the seat of my pants," meaning the firm has no visibility into what is coming or where the money is going
  • No tracking of client acquisition cost or marketing ROI — an unknown amount is going to Rise Up Marketing every month with no measured return; Lindsey cannot say what a signed client costs to acquire from her ad spend
  • No profitability analysis by practice area or case type — the firm may be doing more low-margin misdemeanor and traffic cases than the revenue mix justifies, without knowing it
  • Despite a clear stated goal to return to $1.25 million, there is no plan, no milestones, and no financial roadmap connecting today's $750K baseline to that target
Blind Spot: The firm's strategic pivot toward higher-value felony cases is exactly the right move — but without financial tracking by case type, there is no way to know how much of the current revenue is actually coming from high-value felonies versus low-margin misdemeanors and traffic tickets. Data-driven strategy requires data. Right now, the firm is flying blind.
The Positive
  • "We're in the black" — the firm is profitable, which means there is a solid and real foundation to build a financial plan on top of
  • The strategic pivot toward higher-value felony cases ($15,000–$50,000 each) is the correct direction for margin improvement — the instinct is right, even without the data to confirm it
The Gap
  • Without knowing cost per acquisition, every marketing dollar is being spent blind — the firm cannot optimize what it cannot measure, and cannot confidently scale what it has not validated
  • The goal of returning to $1.25 million is achievable — but without a revenue plan with milestones, it will remain aspirational rather than actionable, and Lindsey will continue working 11–12 hours per day hoping the number eventually changes
  • Lindsey's stated personal goals — college funding and retirement — require a predictable profit plan, not just the hope that "we're in the black" stays true as expenses scale with growth
Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.
