Intake Assessment

Sumner & Associates, P.C. has a dedicated Legal Intake & Client Services Coordinator on staff — a meaningful advantage that most practices at this stage do not have. But a strong intake team is only as effective as the digital and process infrastructure that funnels prospects to them. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Website contact form is buried in the lower section of the homepage — visitors must scroll past multiple content sections before encountering any way to connect, reducing conversions significantly
  • No after-hours contact mechanism is visible — calls and web visits after business hours go unattended until the next business day
  • No live chat or text-based contact option anywhere on the site — off-hours visitors who research evenings and weekends have no path to immediate engagement
  • No observable automated follow-up sequence for prospects who submit a form but do not immediately schedule — warm leads generated by organic or referral traffic likely go cold overnight
Blind Spot: A disproportionate share of family law and estate planning research happens in the evenings — when both spouses are home and can discuss next steps. Without after-hours contact capability, the firm misses its best conversion window every single day. A prospect who cannot reach anyone at 9pm will call a competitor before morning.
The Positive
  • Maryanne Hakeem, Legal Intake & Client Services Coordinator — a dedicated staff member for intake signals organizational maturity and a commitment to the client experience that most firms at this stage have not yet made
  • Sarah Aldred, Director of Legal Operations — a management layer above intake creates accountability for client flow and conversion that is rare and valuable at this firm size
The Gap
  • Every prospect who visits the website after business hours and finds no way to connect immediately is a potential client who calls a competitor before morning — a revenue loss that compounds every day
  • A buried contact form converts at a fraction of an above-fold form's rate — even the most motivated visitors face unnecessary friction before reaching the intake team
  • Without a defined follow-up sequence for form submissions and voicemails, leads who don't commit on first contact are almost certainly being lost — there is no system to re-engage them
A tighter intake system means the firm converts more of the people it already reaches — and every converted lead is revenue that does not require a new marketing dollar to create.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact form appears in the "Get In Touch" section in the lower portion of the homepage — requires visitors to scroll past the full hero section, practice area listings, attorney profiles, and blog preview before finding a way to reach the firm
  • No click-to-call button or prominently positioned phone number in the hero section on mobile — mobile visitors, who make up the majority of local legal searches, must scroll to find contact information
  • No text-a-question or live response option — the only digital contact mechanism is a standard form submission with no indication of response time, creating uncertainty that reduces conversion rate

These findings are also referenced in the Website Audit section.

Team Assessment

Sumner & Associates, P.C. has more team infrastructure than most practices its size — a Director of Legal Operations, a Senior Paralegal, a dedicated intake coordinator, and an Associate Attorney represent a meaningful organizational investment. But infrastructure alone does not equal a self-managing firm — and without the systems and documentation that allow the team to operate independently, Scott remains the firm's critical dependency. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No confirmation that Scott Sumner can be absent from the firm for a week or more without operational impact — at Stage 4, founder dependency is common even when a full team is in place
  • Four office locations without a systematic lead gen engine means that maintaining case volume across all four sites likely requires Scott's personal involvement in relationship and referral management
  • No visible evidence of documented intake scripts, consultation workflows, or operational playbooks that would allow each team member to operate consistently without founder direction
  • Ron R. Sumner's Of Counsel role rather than partner status suggests Scott remains the firm's primary legal authority and final decision-maker — a single point of dependency that limits what the team can handle independently
Blind Spot: The firm's biggest team risk is not a lack of people — it is the absence of documented systems. Without SOPs for intake, consultation, and case management, every team member is operating from institutional knowledge that only functions reliably while the founder is present. The day Scott is unavailable is the day the gaps show.
The Positive
  • Sarah Aldred as Director of Legal Operations means there is someone other than Scott managing firm operations — one of the most important structural hires a growing firm can make, and a genuine sign of organizational maturity
  • Meghan Schuler Todd as Associate Attorney means legal work can be distributed beyond the founding attorney — a prerequisite for scaling case volume without the founder's personal hours becoming the bottleneck
The Gap
  • Until Scott's presence is not required for the firm to operate at full capacity, his income and freedom remain directly tied to how many hours he personally works — revenue growth without systems growth just means more work, not more freedom
  • Scaling through paid advertising without first establishing that the team can independently handle intake and case volume will create a bottleneck at exactly the moment the firm's growth starts accelerating
  • A multi-location firm without systematic lead generation means the owner must personally maintain referral relationships across all four markets — geographic expansion amplifies founder dependency rather than reducing it
The goal is a firm where Scott's presence is a strategic choice, not an operational requirement — where the team handles cases, intake handles prospects, and Scott runs the vision.

Profit Plan Assessment

No financial data, revenue goals, or practice-area profitability information was available for this audit — the firm's financial picture cannot be fully assessed from external research alone. Based on the firm's six decades of operation, four-location footprint, and established team structure, Sumner & Associates, P.C. almost certainly generates meaningful revenue. But without visibility into margins by practice area or a defined profit target, growth investment cannot be calibrated efficiently. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No revenue figures were available — marketing recommendations cannot be precisely calibrated against current revenue and margin targets without this baseline
  • Four office locations add fixed overhead that may not be proportionate to the revenue generated at each location — satellite office ROI is unknown, and each unproductive location is a direct reduction in owner take-home
  • The firm operates across four practice areas — divorce/family law, estate planning, business law, and personal injury — each with different billing models, case timelines, and margins; without tracking economics by area, optimizing for profitability is not possible
  • No stated revenue goal means there is no defined outcome to plan marketing spend against — without a target, growth is reactive rather than strategic
Blind Spot: A firm with both Personal Injury (contingency-based, long cycle, lumpy revenue) and Estate Planning (flat-fee/hourly, faster cycle, predictable revenue) is managing two dramatically different cash flow profiles simultaneously. Without tracking each practice area separately, profit planning and cash management are working from an incomplete picture — and marketing spend may be optimizing for the wrong outcomes.
The Positive
  • Six decades of operating history means the firm has survived multiple economic cycles and carries an established client base with strong referral economics — a recurring revenue foundation that most practices never build
  • AV Preeminent Rating and Gold Client Champion 2026 designation from Martindale indicate consistent high-value client relationships and strong retention — signals of a practice where clients return and refer
The Gap
  • Without case-level profitability data, every dollar of marketing spend is going toward volume rather than return — the firm cannot know whether it is investing in its most profitable practice areas or its least profitable ones
  • Four office locations each carry fixed overhead — rent, staffing, utilities — that comes directly out of margin; a single underperforming location can meaningfully reduce owner take-home without appearing in gross revenue figures
  • Without a defined profit target, there is no mechanism to distinguish revenue growth from actual wealth building — the firm could be growing its top line while holding its take-home flat
A clear profit plan is what separates growing a firm from building real wealth — and it starts with knowing the numbers that actually matter.
