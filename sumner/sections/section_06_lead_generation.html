Website Audit

The Problem
  • Contact form is buried in the lower section of the homepage — not visible above the fold where the majority of visitors engage and decide whether to stay
  • PageSpeed scores: Not retrieved during audit (Google API returned HTTP 429 — must be verified live at pagespeed.web.dev before final delivery); site age and layout structure suggest mobile performance gaps consistent with law firm sites of this era
  • No video content anywhere on the site — attorneys are not visible through any media beyond static photos, which limits the trust-building a visitor can do before calling
  • No live chat or text-based contact option — off-hours visitors who browse evenings and weekends have no mechanism for immediate connection
Blind Spot: Competitors may be bidding on branded search variations such as "Sumner law firm Rochester Hills" and "Sumner attorney Michigan" — capturing prospects who search for the firm by name and land on a competitor's paid ad before ever finding the website.
The Positive
  • Dedicated city landing pages for Rochester Hills, Novi, Clinton Township, and Clarkston — solid local SEO structure that supports geographic targeting across the firm's four office locations
  • Practice area pages are well-organized with specific sub-topics (custody, alimony, Lady Bird Deed, brain injury) that align with how prospects actually search and strengthen topical relevance signals
The Gap
  • A buried contact form means the firm is converting only the most determined visitors — prospects who don't immediately see a way to connect leave without reaching out, and most never return
  • Without a confirmed mobile PageSpeed score, mobile visitors — who make up 60%+ of local legal searches — may be abandoning the site before it finishes loading, handing those leads to competitors
  • The absence of video or trust-building content above the fold means every visitor must decide whether to call based on text alone — a higher friction experience than competing firms that feature attorney-led video
A website that converts is what turns the firm's existing credibility and organic traffic into appointments — and that is the first step toward a firm that generates clients on command, not by chance.
