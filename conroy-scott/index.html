Here Is Where Conroy Scott LLP Stands Today

Conroy Scott LLP is a 68-year-old general practice firm in Sudbury, Ontario, serving Northern Ontario clients across corporate/commercial law, real estate transactions, wills & estates, and civil litigation. At $1.25M in annual revenue with a 13-person team, the firm has real infrastructure and a defined growth plan — but Adam Castonguay, the only senior experienced lawyer, is personally carrying more than half the firm's revenue generation while simultaneously overseeing all day-to-day operations, making him the single point of failure standing between the firm's current reality and the scalable business he is trying to build.

Stage 6 is where the firm runs without Adam at the center of every decision — and it is exactly the firm he is working to build.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Lead source tracking is inconsistent — the firm cannot identify which channels produce its best clients, which makes every future marketing decision a guess
  • No defined intake process or follow-up sequence for leads who do not sign immediately; prospects who reach out and don't hear back within hours frequently call someone else
  • No confirmed after-hours intake coverage — leads arriving via the website or phone after business hours likely reach voicemail and are not followed up systematically

The Positive

  • At 1–2 leads per day on the current ad spend, a 15% conversion gap — losing just 1 in 7 leads due to slow follow-up or friction — represents multiple cases per month left on the table before any new marketing investment is made
  • Without intake tracking, the firm cannot tell whether its best corporate and real estate clients came from ads, referrals, or the website — making it impossible to know what to do more of
  • Every month the intake system remains inconsistent is a month that future ad spend underperforms its potential

Profit Plan Assessment

Conroy Scott LLP is generating $1.25M in annual revenue and projecting $1.5M in 2026 — but without visibility into margins, the firm cannot confirm whether that growth is making Adam wealthier or just busier. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No profit margin data was disclosed on the call — the firm tracks revenue but has no confirmed visibility into what it actually keeps after expenses, staff costs, and overhead
  • A prior SMB Team proposal of $10K USD/month was rejected because it equaled the firm's entire profit margin — indicating that the gap between revenue and profitability has historically been very narrow
  • The firm has no confirmed practice area profitability data — it is unknown whether corporate general counsel retainers or real estate transactions are the higher-margin growth path
  • The corporate general counsel pricing model (annuity/fixed-fee retainer) is still under development — the firm is growing into a revenue model it has not yet fully priced
Blind Spot: The firm's revenue ceiling is stated as $2.5M at current team capacity — but without knowing the profit margin at $1.25M, it is impossible to know whether scaling to $2.5M doubles profit or doubles overhead. Growing into a revenue ceiling without a profit plan means the number at the top may not reflect what Adam actually takes home.
The Positive
  • Revenue trajectory is strong and consistent — $1.25M actual in 2025, $1.5M projected in 2026, with a clear capacity ceiling that defines the growth target
  • The move toward fixed-fee corporate retainers is strategically sound — annuity revenue is the most predictable and profitable model in law firm economics
The Gap
  • Without practice area profitability data, marketing investment decisions are based on revenue potential rather than profit potential — the firm may be growing the wrong practice areas
  • The corporate retainer model has significant profit upside — 200 new clients at a fixed monthly fee could be transformative — but without pricing it properly from the start, the model can become as margin-thin as hourly billing
  • Every year without a profit plan is a year Adam cannot answer the question: "Am I building wealth, or just building a bigger firm?"
Profit clarity is what turns revenue growth into personal wealth — and personal wealth is what gives Adam the real option to step back. The goal is not just a firm that runs without him. It is a firm that pays him whether he is there or not.
