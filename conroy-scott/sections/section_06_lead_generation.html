Lead Generation

Conroy Scott LLP is running Google Ads — but targeting the wrong practice area. The firm's $30/day paid search investment is generating Wills & Estates leads in a practice area already at full capacity, while Corporate general counsel and Real Estate transactions — the two declared growth targets — have no active digital lead generation across any channel.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • The primary above-fold CTA is "Discover More" — a directional button pointing to the services section, not a book-a-consultation prompt; this captures curiosity, not intent
  • PageSpeed scores: Not confirmed at time of research (Google API rate limited) — verify at pagespeed.web.dev before presenting; site is a contemporary build estimated to perform at moderate levels
  • The contact form is hidden behind a sliding panel — not visible on the homepage, creating significant friction for visitors ready to reach out
  • No video content, no blog, and no authority-building content for the corporate or real estate practice areas the firm is trying to grow
Blind Spot: The website positions the firm as a full-service general practice — which is accurate — but this makes it invisible for specific-intent searches like "corporate lawyer Sudbury" or "real estate lawyer Sudbury." A visitor searching for a corporate counsel solution lands on a page about 14 practice areas and has no clear reason to believe this firm is the specialist they need.
The Positive
  • Clean, professional contemporary design that reflects the firm's 68-year legacy without feeling dated
  • Attorney profiles are present and accessible, providing social proof and personal credibility once a visitor finds them
The Gap
  • A hidden contact form means that visitors who cannot find a way to reach the firm in under 5 seconds leave — and the firm never knows they were there
  • No dedicated landing pages for Corporate or Real Estate means Google has nothing to rank for these terms, and paid ad quality scores suffer without matching landing pages
  • Without a conversion-optimized site to send paid traffic to, every dollar spent on future advertising underperforms — the website is the foundation everything else builds on
A website rebuild is not cosmetic — it is the infrastructure that turns ad spend into client calls, and it is the first step toward a firm that generates revenue without Adam doing outreach personally.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Conroy Scott has 27 Google reviews at 4.6 stars — a respectable rating but critically low review volume for a 68-year-old firm with 13 staff members and thousands of past clients
  • The firm does not appear to hold a dominant position in the local 3-pack for "corporate lawyer Sudbury," "business lawyer Sudbury," or "real estate lawyer Sudbury" — the searches most likely to generate its target clients
  • No geo-targeting landing pages exist for surrounding communities (Chelmsford, Espanola, North Bay) or for the Toronto satellite office, leaving significant local search opportunity uncaptured
  • No presence confirmed on Avvo, Justia, or FindLaw — three high-authority legal directories that influence both local search rankings and referral traffic
Blind Spot: The firm's existing ad spend is generating Wills & Estates leads at approximately 1–2 per day — but the GBP is likely categorized under "General Litigation" (per Yelp data), not "Corporate Lawyer" or "Real Estate Attorney." Google uses the primary GBP category as a ranking signal. If the GBP category does not match the firm's growth targets, paid and organic visibility for those searches is suppressed before any other factor.
The Positive
  • 4.6-star average rating is the highest confirmed among all named local competitors with verified review counts — a genuine quality signal that converts once visitors find the firm
The Gap
  • 27 reviews for a 68-year-old firm dramatically undersells its reputation and limits local pack ranking power — firms with 75–100+ reviews consistently outrank comparable practices in Sudbury searches
  • Without category optimization, geo landing pages, and directory citations aligned to Corporate and Real Estate, the firm is invisible for the searches that generate its most valuable clients
Weaver Simmons LLP — holds an estimated 50–169 aggregate reviews across platforms at 4.5 stars, making them the default visible choice for any Sudbury business owner who searches and does not specifically know Conroy Scott by name.
A firm that cannot be found for its target practice areas cannot grow those practice areas — and Adam will stay the primary source of new corporate and real estate clients until local SEO is fixed.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Conroy Scott is confirmed running Google Ads — $30/day budget (~$900/month), active for approximately 3 months, generating 1–2 leads per day primarily for Wills & Estates
  • The firm does not appear to be running ads for corporate/business law or real estate search terms — the two declared growth practice areas appear to be absent from the firm's paid search strategy
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • RealEstateLawyers.ca LLP — a technology-forward flat-rate real estate firm with 15,500+ completed transactions actively targeting Sudbury real estate search terms; their budget and national scale means they can outbid local firms on every relevant keyword
  • Deeded.ca — virtual closing platform appearing in top organic and likely paid results for Sudbury real estate search queries; targets the same $1,300 transaction market Conroy Scott is trying to grow
The Opportunity
  • "Corporate lawyer Sudbury," "business lawyer Sudbury Ontario," and "general counsel Sudbury" — high-intent searches with no confirmed dominant local advertiser, representing an uncrowded paid search opportunity
  • "Real estate lawyer Sudbury," "real estate closing Sudbury" — searches currently dominated by tech-forward competitors who can be countered with local trust signals and a competitive service offer
  • Competing firm names as keywords — bidding on searches for Fortier Law Firm and B&B Law captures business owners who are comparison shopping and have already decided to hire a local Sudbury lawyer
  • Redirecting the existing $30/day from Wills & Estates to Corporate and Real Estate terms costs nothing additional and immediately aligns the firm's spend with its stated growth goals
Blind Spot: The firm is spending roughly $900/month on leads it cannot convert — because Wills & Estates is at full capacity. Every lead that comes in from the current campaign is either turned away or accepted at the expense of a higher-value corporate or real estate matter. The misalignment is not just a marketing problem — it is an opportunity cost problem.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • Conroy Scott does not appear to be running Google Local Service Ads — no Google Screened badge was observed for the firm in Sudbury search results
  • This channel appears underutilized across the Sudbury legal market broadly — representing an early-mover advantage for the first firm to activate it
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Local Sudbury competitors — no specific local competitor has been confirmed running LSAs for corporate or real estate search terms; this is an uncrowded channel in this market
  • National/tech competitors — Deeded and RealEstateLawyers.ca may hold LSA positions in broader Ontario markets; local LSA activation by Conroy Scott would outposition them in Sudbury-specific searches
The Opportunity
  • At 27 reviews and 4.6 stars, Conroy Scott already meets the threshold to qualify for Google Screened — the review base is there; activation is the only step missing
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • Being the first Sudbury corporate and real estate firm to hold a Google Screened badge gives Conroy Scott a visible trust advantage over every local competitor who has not yet activated the channel

Facebook & Instagram Ads

Ad Presence
  • Conroy Scott does not appear to be running active Meta/Facebook Ads — no confirmed active ads were found in the Meta Ads Library; the firm has a Facebook presence at facebook.com/sudburylegal but no confirmed paid boosting
  • This is consistent with the broader Sudbury legal market — none of the primary local competitors (Weaver Simmons, Desmarais Keenan, B&B Law) appear to be running consistent paid social campaigns, making this an uncrowded channel
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Weaver Simmons LLP — Facebook presence confirmed; no active paid ads confirmed — not leveraging social advertising despite their market dominance
  • B&B Law — Facebook presence confirmed; no active paid ads confirmed — their perfect 5.0 rating suggests strong word-of-mouth, not paid social investment
The Opportunity
  • Retargeting — website visitors who did not convert are leaving with no follow-up
  • Video ads featuring the attorney — builds trust before a prospect ever calls
  • Educational content ads — capture leads earlier in the decision process
  • Targeted Facebook ads reaching Sudbury business owners (by employer size, industry, and job title) for corporate general counsel services — an audience that exists on social media and is not yet being reached by any local competitor
