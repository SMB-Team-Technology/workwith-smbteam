Here Is Where Conroy Scott LLP Stands Today

Conroy Scott LLP is a 68-year-old general practice firm in Sudbury, Ontario, serving Northern Ontario clients across corporate/commercial law, real estate transactions, wills & estates, and civil litigation. At $1.25M in annual revenue with a 13-person team, the firm has real infrastructure and a defined growth plan — but Adam Castonguay, the only senior experienced lawyer, is personally carrying more than half the firm's revenue generation while simultaneously overseeing all day-to-day operations, making him the single point of failure standing between the firm's current reality and the scalable business he is trying to build.

Stage 6 is where the firm runs without Adam at the center of every decision — and it is exactly the firm he is working to build.

Where You Are Today

Stage 4: Small Business Manager
  • Adam Castonguay generates 50%+ of revenue and personally oversees all daily operations
  • Google Ads budget ($30/day) drives Wills & Estates leads — the one practice area the firm is not trying to grow
  • Corporate general counsel and real estate transactions — the two declared growth targets — have no active digital lead generation
  • Intake data is inconsistent; a consultant has been engaged to fix the problem but the process is not yet in place

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between Stage 4 and Stage 6 is not talent or reputation — Conroy Scott has both — it is the systems, aligned marketing, and leadership structure the firm needs to grow without Adam being the bottleneck.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Conroy Scott LLP
Conroy Scott LLP is operating at Stage 4 — a Small Business Manager with a real team, a 68-year legacy, and a leader who knows exactly what needs to change. The firm's most urgent gap is lead generation: the current $30/day Google Ads investment is generating leads for the one practice area not targeted for growth, while corporate general counsel and real estate transactions — both declared priorities — have no digital presence driving new client inquiries. Until lead generation is redirected and scaled toward the firm's actual goals, the growth Adam wants will remain dependent on referrals and timing — neither of which he can control.
