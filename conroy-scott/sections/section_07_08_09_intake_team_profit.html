Intake Assessment

Conroy Scott LLP knows intake is broken — a consultant has been engaged to address it — but the fix is not yet in place and the firm is scaling lead generation into a system that cannot consistently convert. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Lead source tracking is inconsistent — the firm cannot identify which channels produce its best clients, which makes every future marketing decision a guess
  • No defined intake process or follow-up sequence for leads who do not sign immediately; prospects who reach out and don't hear back within hours frequently call someone else
  • No confirmed after-hours intake coverage — leads arriving via the website or phone after business hours likely reach voicemail and are not followed up systematically
  • The website contact form is hidden in a sliding drawer panel, creating friction that filters out all but the most motivated prospects before they even make contact
Blind Spot: The firm is aware of the intake problem and has hired a consultant — which means the gap is acknowledged but not yet closed. In the interim, every lead being generated by the current $30/day ad spend is entering a process that cannot consistently convert it. Scaling ad spend before intake is fixed amplifies the loss, not just the gain.
The Positive
  • The decision to engage a consultant signals that the firm is ready to fix intake — it is in motion, not in denial
The Gap
  • At 1–2 leads per day on the current ad spend, a 15% conversion gap — losing just 1 in 7 leads due to slow follow-up or friction — represents multiple cases per month left on the table before any new marketing investment is made
  • Without intake tracking, the firm cannot tell whether its best corporate and real estate clients came from ads, referrals, or the website — making it impossible to know what to do more of
  • Every month the intake system remains inconsistent is a month that future ad spend underperforms its potential
Intake is the multiplier. A better intake system does not just convert more leads — it is the foundation that makes every other investment in marketing pay off, and it is the system that allows the firm to grow without Adam personally fielding every inquiry.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • The contact form is accessible only through a sliding panel drawer — it is not visible on the homepage without a deliberate click sequence, creating friction that filters out all but the most motivated visitors
  • No above-fold consultation CTA exists — the primary action prompt ("Discover More") directs visitors to service information rather than capturing intent to hire
  • Attorney profiles are not featured on the homepage — trust signals that could convert visitors are buried rather than leading the conversion path

These findings are also referenced in the Website Audit section.

Team Assessment

Conroy Scott LLP has a 13-person team — 5 lawyers and 8 support staff — but Adam Castonguay is personally responsible for generating more than half the firm's revenue while simultaneously making every significant operational decision. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
"Difficult to find qualified lawyers in the Sudbury market"
The Problem
  • Adam generates 50%+ of the firm's revenue personally — meaning the firm's revenue ceiling is Adam's personal billing capacity, not market demand
  • Adam also handles all operational oversight — making him responsible for both producing revenue and running the business that produces it; this is the definition of a bottleneck
  • The Sudbury legal market makes talent acquisition difficult — qualified lawyers are scarce, which limits the firm's ability to delegate casework and reduce Adam's dependency
  • No accountability layer exists between Adam and day-to-day decisions; the firm runs through Adam, not beyond him
Blind Spot: The hardest part of building a self-managing firm is not hiring — it is building the systems, processes, and accountability structures that allow someone other than Adam to make decisions confidently. The talent scarcity problem in Sudbury is real, but it is secondary: even if Conroy Scott hired two experienced lawyers tomorrow, without documented processes and a defined leadership layer, Adam would still be the person everyone turns to when something unexpected happens.
The Positive
  • A 13-person team already exists — the foundation for a self-managing firm is in place; what is missing is structure, not headcount
  • The firm has the revenue base ($1.25M and growing) to invest in the coaching, systems, and leadership development that builds the next layer
The Gap
  • Without an operational layer between Adam and the team, every new client, every intake call, and every hiring decision flows back to Adam — and the firm's maximum revenue remains capped at his personal hours
  • The firm's stated maximum capacity at current team size is $2.5M — but reaching that ceiling without removing Adam from the operational center means he works more, not less
  • Every month without a self-managing structure is a month Adam spends maintaining the status quo instead of building the firm that replaces him
The goal is not a bigger firm — it is a firm that works without Adam at the center. That requires deliberate investment in systems and accountability structures, not just more revenue. This is precisely what the coaching engagement is designed to build.

Profit Plan Assessment

Conroy Scott LLP is generating $1.25M in annual revenue and projecting $1.5M in 2026 — but without visibility into margins, the firm cannot confirm whether that growth is making Adam wealthier or just busier. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No profit margin data was disclosed on the call — the firm tracks revenue but has no confirmed visibility into what it actually keeps after expenses, staff costs, and overhead
  • A prior SMB Team proposal of $10K USD/month was rejected because it equaled the firm's entire profit margin — indicating that the gap between revenue and profitability has historically been very narrow
  • The firm has no confirmed practice area profitability data — it is unknown whether corporate general counsel retainers or real estate transactions are the higher-margin growth path
  • The corporate general counsel pricing model (annuity/fixed-fee retainer) is still under development — the firm is growing into a revenue model it has not yet fully priced
Blind Spot: The firm's revenue ceiling is stated as $2.5M at current team capacity — but without knowing the profit margin at $1.25M, it is impossible to know whether scaling to $2.5M doubles profit or doubles overhead. Growing into a revenue ceiling without a profit plan means the number at the top may not reflect what Adam actually takes home.
The Positive
  • Revenue trajectory is strong and consistent — $1.25M actual in 2025, $1.5M projected in 2026, with a clear capacity ceiling that defines the growth target
  • The move toward fixed-fee corporate retainers is strategically sound — annuity revenue is the most predictable and profitable model in law firm economics
The Gap
  • Without practice area profitability data, marketing investment decisions are based on revenue potential rather than profit potential — the firm may be growing the wrong practice areas
  • The corporate retainer model has significant profit upside — 200 new clients at a fixed monthly fee could be transformative — but without pricing it properly from the start, the model can become as margin-thin as hourly billing
  • Every year without a profit plan is a year Adam cannot answer the question: "Am I building wealth, or just building a bigger firm?"
Profit clarity is what turns revenue growth into personal wealth — and personal wealth is what gives Adam the real option to step back. The goal is not just a firm that runs without him. It is a firm that pays him whether he is there or not.
