Here Is Where Couch Law Group Stands Today

Jordan and Annie Couch launched Couch Law Group just three weeks ago in the Cashiers/Highlands area of Western North Carolina — bringing Jordan's background in med-mal defense and M&A from Nashville into one of the most underserved legal markets in the state, where just two litigators serve a five-county region. The firm has already generated approximately $20,000 in billables in its first three weeks, which is a genuinely strong start — but with no digital presence, no Google Business Profile, no marketing infrastructure, and no financial systems in place, every lead the firm is winning right now is being left entirely to chance.

Stage 6 is where Jordan and Annie build the firm that earns at the highest level and still lets them live the life they came home to Western NC to have — and getting there starts with the systems that do not yet exist.

Where You Are Today

Stage 3: Solo Practitioner
  • Three weeks old — leads come from attorney referrals and word of mouth only
  • Jordan is the bottleneck for intake, case review, and every decision
  • No GBP, no website visibility, no paid advertising on any channel
  • No bookkeeper, no accountant, no financial clarity on what the firm keeps

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The opportunity in front of Couch Law Group is real and rare — a wide-open market, a talented attorney, and a clear vision — but none of that converts to the life Jordan and Annie want without systems built deliberately to support it.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

  • PageSpeed scores: Not obtainable — the site returned a 403 error during research, which typically indicates limited public availability or crawler-blocking on a new site; scores should be checked and optimized before any SEO or ad traffic is sent to the site

Blind Spot: The website being new is not the same as being optimized. Many firms launch a site and assume the work is done — but a site that is not structured for conversion, not loaded with geo-targeted practice area content, and not technically fast will produce almost no organic leads even after six to twelve months of aging. The window to build it correctly is right now, before bad SEO habits get indexed.
The Positive

  • Being new means there is no legacy code, outdated design, or entrenched bad structure to undo — the site can be built right from the start
The Gap

  • Competitors like Coward, Hicks & Siler have had decades to build domain authority in this market — without deliberate SEO investment starting now, Couch Law Group will be years behind on organic rankings

Intake Assessment

Couch Law Group's intake process is entirely manual and entirely dependent on Jordan — a structure that works at three weeks of age but will become a hard ceiling on growth the moment lead volume increases. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Paralegal Vanessa collects initial information via a Microsoft Form, but Jordan personally reviews every intake and makes the final call on whether to take the case — he is the bottleneck at every step
  • Follow-up after initial contact is manual phone calls only — there are no automated email or SMS sequences in Clio to re-engage leads who do not respond immediately
  • No defined response time standard exists — leads who contact the firm outside business hours have no after-hours coverage and no automated acknowledgment
  • Vanessa's intake role has no scripts, no qualification criteria, and no KPIs — she is doing administrative work without a defined process to turn inquiries into signed clients
Blind Spot: Jordan's 80% close rate on qualified leads is exceptional — but the bottleneck is not conversion, it is qualification speed and lead volume. Every hour Jordan spends reviewing an intake form is an hour he is not billing. If paid ads bring in 10–15 leads per month, the current system will collapse under the volume unless Vanessa's role is defined and automated before advertising begins.
The Positive
  • Jordan's close rate of approximately 80% for qualified leads is significantly above the industry average — the conversion skill is there; the system around it is not
  • The firm already uses Clio as a CRM, which has native intake automation, workflow triggers, and follow-up tools that can be configured without purchasing additional software
The Gap
  • Without a defined intake workflow, every new lead that comes in from a paid ad or a Google search requires Jordan's personal time to process — at 15 leads per month, that is a second job on top of casework
  • Leads who do not receive a response within the first five minutes of contacting a firm are 21 times less likely to convert — a manual phone callback system cannot compete with that window
  • Jordan's goal of taking home $250k–$300k depends on the firm running cases, not Jordan running the phone — without intake delegation, revenue scales only as fast as Jordan does
A firm where Jordan can step back starts with an intake system that qualifies, follows up, and converts without Jordan being involved in every step — and that system can be built in Clio, right now, before ads even launch.
