Here Is Where Couch Law Group Stands Today

Jordan and Annie Couch launched Couch Law Group just three weeks ago in the Cashiers/Highlands area of Western North Carolina — bringing Jordan's background in med-mal defense and M&A from Nashville into one of the most underserved legal markets in the state, where just two litigators serve a five-county region. The firm has already generated approximately $20,000 in billables in its first three weeks, which is a genuinely strong start — but with no digital presence, no Google Business Profile, no marketing infrastructure, and no financial systems in place, every lead the firm is winning right now is being left entirely to chance.

Stage 6 is where Jordan and Annie build the firm that earns at the highest level and still lets them live the life they came home to Western NC to have — and getting there starts with the systems that do not yet exist.

Where You Are Today

Stage 3: Solo Practitioner
  • Three weeks old — leads come from attorney referrals and word of mouth only
  • Jordan is the bottleneck for intake, case review, and every decision
  • No GBP, no website visibility, no paid advertising on any channel
  • No bookkeeper, no accountant, no financial clarity on what the firm keeps

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The opportunity in front of Couch Law Group is real and rare — a wide-open market, a talented attorney, and a clear vision — but none of that converts to the life Jordan and Annie want without systems built deliberately to support it.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Couch Law Group
Couch Law Group is operating at Stage 3 — a Solo Practitioner firm in its earliest weeks, with revenue entirely dependent on Jordan's personal relationships and referrals, and no infrastructure yet to capture or convert any lead that arrives from outside those channels. The single most urgent gap is the complete absence of digital presence: no Google Business Profile, no organic rankings, no paid ads, and no directory listings under the firm's name — which means that anyone in the five-county region searching for a litigator right now will find Coward, Hicks & Siler before they ever find Couch Law Group. With a goal of $300,000 in Year 1 and $600,000 by Year 2, closing that visibility gap is not optional — it is the precondition for everything else.
