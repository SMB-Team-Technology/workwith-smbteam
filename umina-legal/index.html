Here Is Where Umina Legal Stands Today

Ryan Umina is a trial attorney running Umina Legal PLLC out of Morgantown, WV — a boutique criminal defense firm that has grown to approximately $1.2M in annual revenue almost entirely through a single, self-managed Google Ads campaign. The firm operates as a two-person shop where Ryan personally handles every intake call, every case, and every strategic decision, creating a firm that is performing at a high level financially but is entirely dependent on the owner to function. The single most important finding: the revenue ceiling is not a marketing problem — it is a structural one, and the burnout Ryan experienced before will return unless the systems are built differently this time.

Stage 6 is where you scale to $2M and still work 30 hours a week — where the firm runs on trained people and proven systems, not on you personally answering every call.

Where You Are Today

Stage 4: Small Business Manager
  • Google Ads is generating strong lead flow at $3,000/month — but it is the firm's only lead source
  • Ryan personally handles every intake call, acting as attorney and intake specialist simultaneously
  • Case management runs on spreadsheets with an unusable Practice Panther instance in the background
  • A prior scaling attempt resulted in burnout — growth without the right systems will repeat that cycle

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between where you are and where you want to be is not about capability — Ryan has already proven he can build a $1.2M criminal defense firm. The gap is about systems, and systems can be built.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Contact form requires clicking "Request a Call Back" — no embedded above-fold form captures visitors who do not scroll or click through
  • PageSpeed scores: Mobile 50/100 — Desktop 83/100. A mobile score of 50 means roughly half of paid traffic on mobile devices encounters slow load times before seeing the firm's message
  • No full attorney bio page with education, career history, or personal story — prospects evaluating high-stakes criminal matters have no detailed profile to build trust before calling
  • No blog content and no video on the homepage — both are trust signals that competing firms with established digital presence are leveraging to differentiate
Blind Spot: The firm operates two distinct web properties — uminalegal.com (main site) and umina-legal.com (Google Ads landing page). If these two URLs carry different contact information, business name formatting, or service descriptions, they may create NAP inconsistency that undermines local SEO authority and confuses directory platforms that are trying to verify the firm's legitimacy.
The Positive
  • Modern, mobile-responsive design with both desktop and mobile logo variants — the site does not look outdated and reflects the boutique positioning Ryan has built
  • Trust credentials are displayed (Super Lawyers and Multi-Million Dollar Advocates Forum), and live chat is active — both are genuine conversion assets already in place
The Gap
  • A mobile PageSpeed score of 50 on a site receiving paid traffic means a meaningful share of ad-driven mobile visitors bounce before the page fully loads — those are paid clicks that convert to nothing
  • Without a detailed attorney bio, prospective criminal defense clients facing serious charges have no way to vet Ryan's background beyond the homepage headline — many will find a competing attorney with a full profile and choose them instead
  • No practice area landing pages for the satellite markets (Parkersburg, Charleston, Waynesburg) means ad traffic from those regions lands on a homepage that does not speak to their specific location — reducing conversion rates outside Morgantown
Every visitor who bounces due to a slow mobile load or a missing credibility signal is a case Ryan could have converted without spending another dollar on ads — fixing the website is the highest-leverage, lowest-cost lead generation improvement available right now.

Intake Assessment

Ryan Umina personally handles every intake call for a $1.2M firm — routing each prospect through Zoom Phone to his cell so he can personally guarantee speed to lead, which he views as a competitive advantage. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Ryan handles every intake call personally — when he is in court, in trial prep, or otherwise unavailable, every incoming lead either waits or loses patience and calls a competitor
  • All calls route through Zoom Phone directly to Ryan's cell — there is no after-hours process, no backup contact, and no defined protocol for missed calls
  • No trained intake coordinator and no defined intake script — the entire intake process lives in Ryan's head and cannot be delegated or replicated without him
  • Practice Panther exists but is described as unusable ("years of unorganized data") — without a working CRM, there is no system for tracking leads from first call to signed agreement, meaning lead pipeline health is invisible
Blind Spot: The speed-to-lead advantage Ryan has built is real — but it is only as fast as his personal availability. Any period of trial, deposition, court travel, or time off creates a complete intake black hole. Every hour Ryan spends in court is an hour where the phone rings and no one qualified is answering it, which means the cases with the highest stakes and the highest fees are exactly when the intake system is most likely to fail.
The Positive
  • Ryan's personal attention on intake reflects high standards and ensures first impressions are handled by the attorney who will take the case — this is genuinely valued by clients facing serious criminal matters and should be preserved in spirit as intake is delegated
The Gap
  • Every call Ryan cannot answer during trial prep, court, or after hours is a potential $5,000–$10,000 criminal defense case walking to Slavey & Shumaker or The Moore Law Firm — firms that have the staff to answer every call
  • Without a CRM tracking prospects from first contact to signed retainer, Ryan has no visibility into how many qualified leads are slipping through or which ad spend is actually converting to cases
  • As the firm grows toward $1.5M–$2M, personal intake handling scales linearly with revenue — more leads means more calls Ryan must personally take, which is the exact mechanism that caused the previous burnout
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing. A trained intake coordinator with a defined process and a working CRM is not a cost; it is the single highest-return investment Ryan can make right now.
