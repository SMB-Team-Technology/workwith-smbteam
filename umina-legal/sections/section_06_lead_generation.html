Lead Generation

Umina Legal is generating $100K/month almost entirely through a single self-managed Google Ads campaign — a strong foundation, but a single point of failure that leaves significant organic, LSA, and social territory unclaimed while competitors build multi-channel presence. The firm's current growth is real; the risk is that it all depends on one channel, one budget level, and one person answering the phone.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Contact form requires clicking "Request a Call Back" — no embedded above-fold form captures visitors who do not scroll or click through
  • PageSpeed scores: Mobile 50/100 — Desktop 83/100. A mobile score of 50 means roughly half of paid traffic on mobile devices encounters slow load times before seeing the firm's message
  • No full attorney bio page with education, career history, or personal story — prospects evaluating high-stakes criminal matters have no detailed profile to build trust before calling
  • No blog content and no video on the homepage — both are trust signals that competing firms with established digital presence are leveraging to differentiate
Blind Spot: The firm operates two distinct web properties — uminalegal.com (main site) and umina-legal.com (Google Ads landing page). If these two URLs carry different contact information, business name formatting, or service descriptions, they may create NAP inconsistency that undermines local SEO authority and confuses directory platforms that are trying to verify the firm's legitimacy.
The Positive
  • Modern, mobile-responsive design with both desktop and mobile logo variants — the site does not look outdated and reflects the boutique positioning Ryan has built
  • Trust credentials are displayed (Super Lawyers and Multi-Million Dollar Advocates Forum), and live chat is active — both are genuine conversion assets already in place
The Gap
  • A mobile PageSpeed score of 50 on a site receiving paid traffic means a meaningful share of ad-driven mobile visitors bounce before the page fully loads — those are paid clicks that convert to nothing
  • Without a detailed attorney bio, prospective criminal defense clients facing serious charges have no way to vet Ryan's background beyond the homepage headline — many will find a competing attorney with a full profile and choose them instead
  • No practice area landing pages for the satellite markets (Parkersburg, Charleston, Waynesburg) means ad traffic from those regions lands on a homepage that does not speak to their specific location — reducing conversion rates outside Morgantown
Every visitor who bounces due to a slow mobile load or a missing credibility signal is a case Ryan could have converted without spending another dollar on ads — fixing the website is the highest-leverage, lowest-cost lead generation improvement available right now.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Google Business Profile status could not be confirmed live — the firm does not appear in Yellow Pages or BBB directory searches for Morgantown WV criminal defense, suggesting either an unoptimized or low-visibility GBP profile
  • Umina Legal does not appear in Avvo's main criminal defense attorney rankings for Morgantown — only listed in the "Newest to the area" section, which carries no credibility weight with prospective clients who are comparison shopping
  • Not listed in Yellow Pages, BBB, FindLaw, or Justia for this market — competitors with presence across all of these directories have a compounding organic authority advantage that grows wider every month
Blind Spot: Two different URLs (uminalegal.com and umina-legal.com) appearing in different places across the web may send conflicting signals to Google about the firm's address, phone number, and business name — NAP inconsistency is one of the primary reasons firms fail to rank in the local map pack even with strong websites and ad spend.
The Positive
  • The main website uses Yext Knowledge Tags, suggesting the firm has already invested in directory infrastructure — this is a stronger starting point than firms with no syndication technology in place and means directory cleanup can happen at scale rather than listing by listing
The Gap
  • Without a strong GBP profile and verified review count, Umina Legal captures zero organic local search traffic — every searcher who finds the firm does so through paid ads, meaning the firm pays for every single visit when competitors are earning organic visibility for free
  • Competitors with 20+ Avvo reviews have a trust advantage in every organic search where both firms could appear — a prospect who finds Slavey & Shumaker's 10.0 Avvo rating alongside Umina Legal's "Newest to area" label will make an obvious credibility comparison
Slavey & Shumaker — Avvo 10.0 with 21 reviews and active directory presence across multiple platforms. While Umina Legal has the revenue to compete, Slavey & Shumaker has the online credibility footprint that wins comparison searches — and those are the leads that call without needing to be convinced.
Organic local SEO is the only lead generation channel that compounds in value over time without requiring ongoing spend — building it now means Ryan's $2M goal gets easier and cheaper to reach each year, not harder.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Confirmed running per transcript — Google Ads campaign at $100/day (~$3,000/month) driving to the umina-legal.com custom landing page, routing through Zoom Phone directly to Ryan's cell
  • Ad creative and headline not observed live; the firm reports strong results at current spend ("highly effective Google Ads strategy"), but campaign structure, keyword targeting, and quality scores were not accessible for direct review
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Slavey & Shumaker — strong Avvo profile (10.0, 21 reviews) suggests active digital marketing investment; likely bidding on the same criminal defense and DUI terms in Morgantown
  • Frank Walker Law — Pittsburgh-area criminal defense firm known to serve the WV market; actively present across directory platforms with Avvo 10.0 and 12 reviews, indicating paid and organic market investment
The Opportunity
  • Expanding from ~$3,000/month to a managed budget with professional optimization — criminal defense in competitive markets typically requires $7,500+ to maintain consistent top-position visibility across all relevant terms
  • Geo-targeted satellite market campaigns for Parkersburg WV, Charleston WV, and Waynesburg PA — the firm has physical presence in all three markets but appears to be running a single Morgantown-focused campaign
  • Specific charge-type targeting beyond general criminal defense — DUI, drug charges, assault, federal charges, and violent crimes all carry distinct search intent and convert at different rates
  • Competitor name bidding — capturing searchers who look up Slavey & Shumaker or The Moore Law Firm by name and showing them Umina Legal as an alternative before they call a competitor
Blind Spot: At $3,000/month in a competitive criminal defense market with multiple established firms, the current budget may be insufficient to maintain top-position visibility for all high-value search terms simultaneously — competitors with larger budgets can outbid Ryan for specific high-value queries (federal charges, violent crimes) while he maintains presence on more general terms, creating invisible coverage gaps in the most valuable case categories.
