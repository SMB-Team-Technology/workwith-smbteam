Intake Assessment

Ryan Umina personally handles every intake call for a $1.2M firm — routing each prospect through Zoom Phone to his cell so he can personally guarantee speed to lead, which he views as a competitive advantage. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Ryan handles every intake call personally — when he is in court, in trial prep, or otherwise unavailable, every incoming lead either waits or loses patience and calls a competitor
  • All calls route through Zoom Phone directly to Ryan's cell — there is no after-hours process, no backup contact, and no defined protocol for missed calls
  • No trained intake coordinator and no defined intake script — the entire intake process lives in Ryan's head and cannot be delegated or replicated without him
  • Practice Panther exists but is described as unusable ("years of unorganized data") — without a working CRM, there is no system for tracking leads from first call to signed agreement, meaning lead pipeline health is invisible
Blind Spot: The speed-to-lead advantage Ryan has built is real — but it is only as fast as his personal availability. Any period of trial, deposition, court travel, or time off creates a complete intake black hole. Every hour Ryan spends in court is an hour where the phone rings and no one qualified is answering it, which means the cases with the highest stakes and the highest fees are exactly when the intake system is most likely to fail.
The Positive
  • Ryan's personal attention on intake reflects high standards and ensures first impressions are handled by the attorney who will take the case — this is genuinely valued by clients facing serious criminal matters and should be preserved in spirit as intake is delegated
The Gap
  • Every call Ryan cannot answer during trial prep, court, or after hours is a potential $5,000–$10,000 criminal defense case walking to Slavey & Shumaker or The Moore Law Firm — firms that have the staff to answer every call
  • Without a CRM tracking prospects from first contact to signed retainer, Ryan has no visibility into how many qualified leads are slipping through or which ad spend is actually converting to cases
  • As the firm grows toward $1.5M–$2M, personal intake handling scales linearly with revenue — more leads means more calls Ryan must personally take, which is the exact mechanism that caused the previous burnout
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing. A trained intake coordinator with a defined process and a working CRM is not a cost; it is the single highest-return investment Ryan can make right now.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact forms require a deliberate click to "Request a Call Back" — no above-fold embedded form captures visitors who land and do not scroll, meaning the site's conversion rate depends entirely on the visitor's initiative
  • Live chat is active on the site ("Hi. Do you need any help?" dialog confirmed) — a genuine positive, but its effectiveness depends entirely on who is monitoring and responding, which is unclear given that Ryan handles intake personally
  • With no after-hours intake process in place, visitors who reach the site outside business hours and use the contact form or chat have no guarantee of a timely response — the speed-to-lead advantage Ryan values disappears completely after hours

Team Assessment

Umina Legal runs on two people — Ryan and one assistant — with Ryan personally carrying intake, casework, and strategic direction for a $1.2M firm that is outperforming its infrastructure. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Two-person operation (Ryan + 1 assistant) with no management layer — every decision, every client call, and every operational function runs through Ryan personally
  • No dedicated intake coordinator, paralegal, or case manager — the roles that would allow Ryan to focus on high-value attorney work simply do not exist yet
  • A prior scaling attempt led to burnout — this history makes Ryan understandably cautious, but caution without structure does not prevent burnout; it just limits the revenue ceiling
  • Case management on spreadsheets creates no accountability, no visibility into firm health, and no foundation for the next hire to step into a functional system
Blind Spot: The prior burnout may be pointing at the wrong lesson. The problem was not growth — it was growth without the systems and team to support it. Slowing growth to avoid burnout is the instinctive response, but it does not address the root cause. The right answer is building the team structure that allows growth to happen without adding to Ryan's personal load.
The Positive
  • Ryan's commitment to personal quality control on intake shows he has built high standards into the firm's culture — those standards can be documented and trained into the next hire rather than lost in delegation
The Gap
  • A $1.2M firm with no management layer and one support person cannot scale to $1.5M–$2M without breaking the attorney — the math simply does not work without adding capacity
  • Without a trained intake coordinator, every additional dollar of ad spend that generates more leads multiplies Ryan's personal workload rather than the firm's revenue capacity
  • The 30-hour workweek Ryan described requires delegation of at least intake and case management — neither function is delegated today, meaning the goal is structurally blocked regardless of revenue
Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows. The next hire Ryan makes is not an expense; it is the structural requirement for the 30-hour week he described.

Profit Plan Assessment

Umina Legal generates $100K/month in revenue, but Ryan's own acknowledgment that bookkeeping is a weakness requiring professional oversight signals that the firm's real profitability picture is unclear — and growing without that clarity is how a $1.2M firm can feel like it is not generating the wealth it should be. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Bookkeeping is acknowledged as a weakness requiring professional oversight — without clean financial records, Ryan cannot know actual profit margins, cash flow position, or the true cost of running the firm
  • Case management on spreadsheets provides no financial visibility into revenue per case type, cost per acquisition, or write-off rates — the firm is managing a $1.2M business with the financial tools of a startup
  • Practice Panther's unusable state means financial data that should be systematically tracked is dark — there is no platform generating the reports needed to make informed scaling decisions
  • No stated attribution tracking on Google Ads — at $3,000/month in spend with no clear accounting of how many signed cases came from ads versus referrals, the ROI on the firm's primary marketing investment is unknown
Blind Spot: Criminal defense cases vary significantly in fee structure — from flat-fee misdemeanors to multi-month felony retainers. Without tracking profitability by case type, it is possible that Ryan's highest-volume case types are also his least profitable per hour invested, while the high-stakes matters he specializes in are the real profit drivers. Growing toward $2M without this data could mean working harder for a lower effective hourly rate.
The Positive
  • LawPay is deployed for payments, and Claude AI is used for fee agreement drafting and email routing — both reflect a genuine openness to technology that creates a solid foundation for building more sophisticated financial systems
The Gap
  • Without knowing the actual cost to acquire a criminal defense client, scaling ad spend from $3,000 to $10,000+ per month carries financial risk that is currently unquantified — an informed scaling decision requires knowing what a signed case actually costs to generate
  • Spreadsheet-based case management cannot generate the P&L visibility needed to reach $2M with confidence or to make the presentation to a coaching program, a lender, or a potential future buyer
  • Growing to $1.5M–$2M without a financial plan risks replicating the burnout cycle at a higher revenue level — more income does not automatically mean more take-home if expenses, overhead, and write-offs are growing at the same rate
The 30-hour week and $2M goal Ryan described require a financial plan, not just more revenue — and that plan starts with knowing exactly what the firm earns, what it spends, and what it actually keeps after every case is closed.
