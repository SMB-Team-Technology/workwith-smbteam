Lead Generation

Silversmith Legal currently operates with zero digital marketing — every client acquisition flows through referrals and practice acquisitions in a Vail Valley market where the window for first-mover digital advantage is still open. That window is not permanent: the Front Range expansion Chris is planning runs directly into a market where well-funded, review-heavy competitors have already claimed the top digital positions across paid ads, local SEO, and social media.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Website launched on Squarespace in 2024 with a clean design but thin content — no blog, no service-area landing pages, no video, and no FAQ content that builds organic search authority
  • PageSpeed scores: Not obtained — Google PageSpeed API was rate-limited during research; scores must be verified manually at pagespeed.web.dev before the proposal call
  • No contact form anywhere on the site — the contact page lists only an email address (admin@silversmithlegal.com) and phone number (970-306-6449); there is no way to submit an inquiry without leaving the browser
  • The primary above-fold CTA ("Learn more") links to the attorney biography page rather than to a contact or intake step — visitors who are ready to act have no direct path to becoming a lead
Blind Spot: The homepage headline — "Planning Not Just Done, But Done Right" — is brand-oriented rather than search-intent-oriented. It will not rank for any estate planning or probate search term in Vail, Avon, or Denver. The site is built for people who already know Silversmith Legal, not for people who are searching for what Chris does. Every organic search opportunity is invisible to this site as it currently exists.
The Positive
  • Clean, modern design built in 2024 — visually professional and mobile-responsive with a working navigation menu
  • Christopher Silversmith has a dedicated attorney bio page with professional credentials; Amy is being added to the site this week
The Gap
  • Without a contact form, every potential online inquiry requires a manual extra step — prospects who cannot immediately call will bounce to a competitor who makes contact easier
  • Without landing pages for Vail, Avon, Edwards, Glenwood Springs, or any Denver suburb, the site cannot rank for any local estate planning or probate search query in any of the markets the firm serves or wants to enter
  • A website that cannot convert traffic makes every other marketing investment less effective — ads, SEO, and LSA all depend on a destination page that captures inquiries
A website built to generate and capture leads is the foundation everything else builds on — without it, Amy cannot have a full pipeline, and Chris cannot step back from being the firm's only source of new business.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Silversmith Legal has approximately 1–3 Google reviews at 5.0 stars (per aggregator data) — Legacy Law Group Colorado has 245 reviews at 4.9 stars and Colorado Estate Planning Law Center has 66 reviews at 4.8 stars; this gap makes ranking in the local 3-pack against these competitors essentially impossible without a major review-building effort
  • The firm does not appear confirmed in the local 3-pack for "estate planning attorney Vail Colorado" — Heckman & O'Connor, P.C. holds the incumbent position in the Vail Valley 3-pack despite only approximately 3 Google reviews
  • No geo-targeted landing pages exist for any city served — Vail, Avon, Edwards, Glenwood Springs, Denver, or any Front Range suburb — so there is no organic content to support local pack visibility in any market
  • NAP inconsistencies across directories: Avvo lists an old Edwards address; YellowPages shows all hours as "CLOSED"; the Glenwood Springs office does not appear in any directory listing reviewed
Blind Spot: The YellowPages listing showing all hours as "CLOSED" is not just a data error — it signals to both Google and potential clients that the firm may not be operational. These types of NAP inconsistencies actively suppress Google Business Profile visibility by reducing Google's confidence in the firm's location signals. They are silent and easy to miss, but they are costing the firm ranking positions it should already own.
The Positive
  • 5.0-star average rating across available reviews — the reviews that do exist are excellent; the foundation for a credible profile is there, it just needs volume
  • Listed on Avvo (7.7 rating, Rising Stars 2021–2022), Justia, FindLaw, SuperLawyers, BBB (A rating), Yelp, Nextdoor, and other major directories — the directory footprint exists and needs cleanup, not rebuilding from scratch
The Gap
  • With fewer than 5 Google reviews, Silversmith Legal cannot compete for local pack visibility against any established competitor — in the Denver market especially, the review gap is so large that local pack entry requires a sustained multi-month review campaign before paid ads alone can close it
  • The NAP inconsistencies across directories compound the local SEO problem: Google cross-references these signals to confirm legitimacy, and conflicting information degrades rankings across all locations
Legacy Law Group Colorado — 245 Google reviews at 4.9 stars. Their GBP presence alone disqualifies most competitors from appearing in the Denver 3-pack for estate planning — before a single dollar of ad spend is considered. Silversmith Legal entering the Denver market without a comparable review profile means starting every campaign in a structural disadvantage.
Local SEO is how probate clients find an attorney in a moment of need — if Silversmith Legal does not appear when they search, Amy will never get the call, and Chris's vision of a full, self-sustaining practice remains out of reach.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Silversmith Legal does not appear to be running paid search ads for estate planning or probate in the Vail Valley, Avon, Denver, or Front Range markets — the firm may not be configured to show for these terms, or no campaign is currently active
  • Confirmed on call: the firm has run "minimal to no paid digital" marketing — this is Silversmith Legal's first digital marketing initiative
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Legacy Law Group Colorado — maintains strong paid and organic presence in Front Range estate planning searches; KDVR news feature video embedded on site signals an active media outreach and brand investment strategy
  • Colorado Estate Planning Law Center — well-established in Front Range organic and paid search; positioned in the exact Denver-to-mountains corridor Silversmith Legal wants to enter
  • Glatstein & O'Brien LLP — operates denverprobatelaw.com (a domain that signals strong probate PPC intent); Burr Law running "Free Consultation — Denver Probate Attorney" messaging in Denver probate searches
The Opportunity
  • "Estate planning attorney Vail Colorado," "probate attorney Avon Colorado," and "estate planning attorney Glenwood Springs" — the mountain market has low paid search competition; Silversmith Legal could claim top-of-page position at significantly lower CPCs than any Denver campaign
  • "Denver probate attorney" and "estate planning attorney Denver" — the Front Range market is more competitive but represents the growth trajectory Chris described; establishing a presence here now is significantly cheaper than entering after competitors consolidate
  • Competitor name targeting — bidding on "Heckman O'Connor estate planning" and similar searches captures high-intent prospects who are already comparing attorneys, typically at lower CPCs than primary keywords
  • Probate urgency keywords — "how to start probate in Colorado," "who handles probate in Eagle County," "probate attorney near me Colorado" — capture prospects in crisis mode before they call the first number they find
Blind Spot: The Vail Valley mountain market has been described as having very low paid search competition — the same dynamic that makes LSA leads cost approximately $50/call likely applies to Google Search as well. Every month without a campaign active is a month that first-mover pricing advantage goes unclaimed. Once a funded competitor enters this market, CPCs will rise and that advantage disappears permanently.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • Silversmith Legal does not appear to be running Local Service Ads in the Vail/Avon market or the Denver Front Range — the firm has not yet obtained Google Screened verification, which is required to appear
  • The mountain valley LSA market is reportedly very low competition — per the sales rep's assessment, approximately $50 per qualified call with no dominant LSA holders observed in the Vail/Avon area
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Vail Valley / Eagle County — LSA positions do not appear to be held by dominant local competitors; this market has a genuine early-mover opening that is not yet claimed
  • Denver / Front Range — multiple established firms appear to maintain LSA presence in the Denver estate planning and probate market; specific firms and review counts were not confirmed live during this research session
The Opportunity
  • The Vail Valley LSA market is reportedly untapped — at approximately $50 per qualified call, this is one of the lowest-cost, highest-intent lead sources available to the firm today, and there is no competitor currently blocking the top position
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • Achieving Google Screened status adds a trust badge to the listing — especially valuable for estate planning and probate clients who are making high-stakes legal decisions about their family's assets and are looking for signals of legitimacy
