Lead Generation

The Immigration Law Office of Ronen Kurzfeld has spent 20+ years building one of Toronto's most credible immigration brands — but that brand is now invisible on every major digital channel at the same time. Organic rankings have collapsed, local Maps presence is inconsistent, and Google PPC at $5,000 CAD/month is no longer generating effective results, leaving a 15-person firm scrambling for the 4–8 consultations per day it needs to sustain itself.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed scores were not retrievable during this research pass (Google API rate-limited) — scores must be obtained at pagespeed.web.dev before the sales call; based on site characteristics (WordPress CMS, active blog, no video, responsive layout), mobile performance is likely in the 45–65 range
  • Two active websites — immigrationway.com and kurzfeldlawfirm.com — cover identical practice areas and split every backlink, every domain authority signal, and every Google trust metric the firm has built over 20 years; this structural dilution is a primary contributor to the organic ranking collapse
  • No live chat or immediate response option — prospects who arrive outside business hours or prefer instant contact have no way to connect without submitting a form and waiting for a reply
  • Dual about.me profiles (two separate slugs for the same attorney) and three different phone numbers across listings create additional brand confusion that erodes trust signals
Blind Spot: Every backlink pointing to kurzfeldlawfirm.com is link equity that is NOT powering immigrationway.com — the domain the firm presumably wants to rank. Until the second domain is 301-redirected to the primary, the firm is essentially running two separate SEO campaigns for the same practice and winning neither.
The Positive
  • Active blog with recent posts (May 5, April 25, April 15, 2026) signals content freshness to Google — a real SEO asset that many competitors neglect
  • 13 geo-targeted landing pages covering Toronto-area cities (Mississauga, Brampton, Markham, Scarborough, Richmond Hill, and more) create a meaningful Local SEO footprint that competitors would need years to replicate
  • Multilingual support in English, French, Hebrew, and Tagalog — a genuine differentiator in Toronto's immigration market and a foundation for multilingual advertising
  • Modern, responsive design with clear CTAs ("Get Immigration Assessment," "Book a Consultation") above the fold
The Gap
  • The dual-domain structure is an active drag on rankings that cannot be fixed with content, links, or any other SEO tactic — until the domains are consolidated, every dollar spent on SEO is working at half efficiency
  • Without live chat or after-hours contact capture, the firm is losing prospects who arrive outside business hours — in a competitive market, the firm that responds first typically wins the client
  • Geo-targeted landing pages are an asset only if they have substantive, unique content — thin location pages can suppress rankings rather than improve them, and the quality of each page needs to be assessed before the SEO recovery plan is built
A website that is invisible and cannot immediately convert the visitors it does attract is not an asset — fixing it is the first step toward giving Ronen the consistent, predictable lead flow that makes delegation possible.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Primary keyword "immigration lawyer Toronto" — firm dropped from page 1 to page 6+, confirmed by Ronen on the discovery call; at page 6+, the firm is effectively invisible to the vast majority of prospects who never scroll past the first page
  • Google Maps (local 3-pack) visibility is inconsistent — the firm is not reliably appearing for any of its core immigration search terms, meaning even prospects who are looking specifically for the firm may not find it
  • NAP (Name, Address, Phone) inconsistencies across platforms: three different phone numbers observed across directories, and Birdeye shows 170 Sheppard Avenue East #101 while the firm's website and BBB list 110 Sheppard Ave E Suite #630 — Google uses NAP consistency as a local ranking signal and actively penalizes contradictions
  • Two active about.me profiles with slightly different slugs (about.me/RonenKurzfeldImmigrationLawyer and about.me/ronenkurzfeldimmigration) add to the brand inconsistency signal
Blind Spot: The address discrepancy — 170 Sheppard Ave E #101 on Birdeye versus 110 Sheppard Ave E #630 on the firm's own website — is not just a data error; Google reads this as two different business locations, which directly suppresses Maps confidence in which listing is authoritative. This may be a legacy address from a former office that was never cleaned up, but it is actively costing the firm Maps rankings right now.
The Positive
  • 371 Google reviews at 4.9 stars is a genuine competitive asset — significantly stronger than Bellissimo Law Group (100 reviews) and Guberman Appleby (38 reviews); this review profile gives the firm a real foundation to qualify for Google Screened status and LSA enrollment
  • 13 geo-targeted landing pages covering major Toronto-area cities represent years of SEO groundwork that competitors would need significant time to replicate
The Gap
  • Mamann Sandaluk holds 783 reviews and Matkowsky holds 600+ — the review gap is not just a social proof problem, it is an active Local SEO ranking factor; every week the firm is not soliciting reviews, the gap widens
  • Until NAP inconsistencies are corrected across all platforms, no amount of new content or backlinks will restore Maps rankings — the structural trust problem must be fixed first
  • GBP posts are not confirmed active — firms that post consistently to Google Business Profile maintain freshness signals that directly support Maps placement
Mamann Sandaluk LLP — 783 Google reviews, consistent NAP across all platforms, and 30+ years of sustained digital marketing has built the domain authority and Maps trust that now dominates Toronto immigration search results for the firm's most valuable keywords.
Ronen cannot delegate a firm that has no leads coming in — restoring Local SEO is the foundation that everything else is built on, and the 371 reviews already in the bank are the fastest path to getting it back.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Firm confirmed running Google PPC at $5,000 CAD/month — Ronen stated directly on the call that this spend is "no longer effective"
  • The effectiveness problem is likely a combination of factors: declining Quality Score from the organic authority collapse raises CPCs; larger competitors with established domain authority are outbidding the firm for the same terms; and without practice-area-specific landing pages, campaign conversion rates are likely well below market benchmarks
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Mamann Sandaluk LLP — 30+ years in the Toronto immigration market with an established digital operation; their domain authority and Quality Scores are built on decades of consistent investment, which means they pay less per click for the same keywords and their ads are shown more often
  • Matkowsky Immigration Law — confirmed running Google and Microsoft display remarketing, a complete paid funnel that recaptures every bounced visitor across the web; while this firm's PPC budget is concentrated in search, Matkowsky is capturing leads at every stage of the decision process
The Opportunity
  • Rebuild campaign structure around specific practice area keywords — "express entry lawyer Toronto," "spousal sponsorship lawyer," "refugee claim lawyer," "work permit LMIA" — with dedicated landing pages for each; practice-area campaigns achieve 3–5× higher conversion rates than broad "immigration lawyer" campaigns
  • Run branded protection campaigns on "Kurzfeld," "Ronen Kurzfeld," and "Kurzfeld law" — competitors may be bidding on these terms to intercept prospects who are already looking specifically for this firm
  • Target neighboring city terms where competition is lower and CPCs are reduced — "immigration lawyer Mississauga," "immigration lawyer North York," "immigration lawyer Markham" — capitalizing on the firm's existing geo-targeted landing pages
  • Add Google Local Service Ads as a complement to traditional PPC — LSAs appear above all search ads at a pay-per-lead rate, and the firm's 371 reviews at 4.9 stars give it the profile to qualify immediately
Blind Spot: At $5,000 CAD/month (approximately $3,650–$3,800 USD), and with Toronto legal CPCs routinely running $30–$100+ CAD per click, the firm is generating fewer than 200 clicks per month at best. A single consultation page converting at 2–3% yields 4–6 consultations per month — well below the 4–8 consultations per day the firm needs to meet its revenue minimum. The spend is not the primary problem; the infrastructure and strategy behind the spend is.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • LSA enrollment for this firm was not confirmed during research — the firm does not appear to be actively running Local Service Ads
  • With 371 Google reviews at 4.9 stars, this firm meets or exceeds the review threshold required for Google Verified status in most Canadian markets — LSA enrollment appears immediately viable
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Mamann Sandaluk LLP — with 783 reviews and a mature digital marketing operation, they are almost certainly enrolled in LSA, holding the top placement above all paid search and organic results for immigration terms
  • Matkowsky Immigration Law — 600+ reviews and confirmed paid digital investment strongly suggests LSA enrollment; at this review count, they likely hold the top 1–2 LSA positions for key immigration terms in Toronto
The Opportunity
  • With 371 reviews at 4.9 stars, the firm has one of the strongest review profiles in the Toronto immigration market — Google Screened verification appears immediately achievable and would unlock LSA eligibility
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • LSA enrollment could restore top-of-page visibility for Toronto immigration searches while the organic recovery is still underway — giving the firm a paid channel that works on review strength rather than budget size, where this firm already has an edge over most competitors
Blind Spot: Unlike Google PPC where ranking is determined largely by bid amount, LSA placement is determined primarily by review count, recency, and Google Verified status — areas where 371 reviews at 4.9★ give this firm a legitimate competitive advantage that it is currently not using.

  • Educational content ads — capture leads earlier in the decision process
  • Multilingual targeting — French, Hebrew, and Tagalog-speaking communities in the Toronto area are underserved segments that this firm can reach more credibly than any competitor, at lower CPCs due to reduced audience competition in those language segments
