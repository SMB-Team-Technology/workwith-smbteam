Intake Assessment

The Immigration Law Office of Ronen Kurzfeld is generating consultations — but Ronen personally handles both the intake conversation and the retainer follow-up, making him the single point of failure for the firm's most critical revenue function. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
"Implement systems that enable delegation of intake and billing, which Ronen currently handles."
The Problem
  • Ronen personally handles both intake and billing — the two most time-consuming non-legal functions in the firm, and the two functions he most wants to delegate
  • No automated follow-up sequence exists for leads who attend a consultation but do not sign a retainer — these prospects simply fall out of the pipeline with no recovery attempt
  • Monday.com retainer follow-up workflow is broken — qualified leads who do not sign immediately are lost because the system that should trigger follow-up does not reliably do so
  • No documented after-hours coverage process — prospects who call evenings or weekends may reach voicemail and call a competitor before business hours
Blind Spot: Every lead who attends a consultation and does not sign is a warm prospect with real intent who has already invested time in the process. Without an automated retainer follow-up sequence, the firm is spending money to generate leads it then allows to hire competitors — and the cost of each lost lead includes not just the acquisition cost but the lifetime value of the case.
The Positive
  • Multilingual consultation capability (English, French, Hebrew, Tagalog) is a genuine intake differentiator — the firm can serve communities that many Toronto immigration firms cannot, which increases both the breadth of the lead pool and the trust of prospects in those communities
  • Multiple contact entry points on the website (consultation form with clear fields, toll-free number 1-855-999-0224) create multiple paths to first contact
The Gap
  • If the firm needs 4–8 scheduled consultations per day to hit its $220,000 CAD/month revenue minimum, losing even 20–30% of post-consultation prospects to no follow-up represents tens of thousands of dollars in monthly revenue left uncaptured
  • With Ronen as the intake bottleneck, the firm's consultation capacity is capped by his personal calendar — which is already full managing cases, operations, and billing
  • Delegating intake without a documented process and tracking system risks inconsistency in how prospects are qualified, the retainer value they are quoted, and the timeline in which they are followed up
Delegating intake is not just an operational improvement — it is the single action that most directly frees Ronen from the role he has explicitly said he most wants to step out of.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Multiple consultation request forms on the website (homepage and inner pages) with fields for name, email, phone, client status, and message — the structure is solid, but all submissions route to email, which is slower than live response
  • No live chat is available — prospects who arrive outside business hours or prefer instant communication have no way to get an immediate response, and in a competitive market, speed of response is the single biggest driver of conversion rate
  • The toll-free number (1-855-999-0224) is visible, but without documented call routing or defined after-hours coverage, incoming calls during off-hours may go unanswered — representing lost prospects who were ready to engage

These findings are also referenced in the Website Audit section.

Team Assessment

The Immigration Law Office of Ronen Kurzfeld has a 15-person team — 7 lawyers and 8 offshore staff — that gives the firm real operational capacity, but the case management system is broken, no leadership layer exists between the team and the owner, and Ronen remains the default decision-maker for anything that falls outside a clearly defined workflow. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
"Implement systems that enable delegation of intake and billing, which Ronen currently handles."
The Problem
  • Monday.com case management has failed as a reliable system — broken notification workflows caused at least one lawyer to miss a filing deadline, a direct risk to client outcomes and the firm's professional standing
  • No leadership layer exists between the attorneys and the owner — 15 people coordinate informally, which means Ronen becomes the default resolution point for every operational gap
  • AI is being used ad hoc across the firm without a structured policy or verification framework — in a legal context, unvetted AI use creates the risk of fabricated case citations that could expose the firm to professional discipline and client harm
  • Intake and billing remain personally managed by Ronen — functions that require a designated staff owner with a documented process before Ronen can meaningfully step back
Blind Spot: The Monday.com notification failure that caused a missed filing deadline is not primarily a software problem — it is a people-and-process problem. When accountability for critical deadlines rests on an automated system that no one is auditing, a single configuration failure becomes a malpractice exposure. The question is not whether this will happen again; it is when — and whether the firm will have a human check in place before it does.
The Positive
  • A 15-person team with 7 licensed lawyers represents meaningful operational capacity — the raw infrastructure to scale to $4–5M CAD exists; the missing element is the structure to deploy it without Ronen in the middle
  • Offshore staff provides cost-effective support for case administration and document processing, which is a genuine advantage in a practice area with high document volume
The Gap
  • A team without defined accountability and clear function ownership means Ronen is the safety net for every process that breaks — and right now, multiple processes are breaking simultaneously
  • Without a designated intake lead and a documented process, Ronen cannot take uninterrupted time away without the firm's consultation intake pausing entirely
  • The absence of an AI use policy is not a minor operational gap — it is a professional liability exposure that sits quietly in every brief, every filing, and every AI-generated document the team produces without verification
Ronen's goal is to step back from the day-to-day — but a team without delegation structures and clear ownership cannot run without him, regardless of how capable the individual lawyers are.

Profit Plan Assessment

The Immigration Law Office of Ronen Kurzfeld is generating $2–3M CAD in annual revenue with a 15-person team — but collections are leaking through broken follow-up on unpaid invoices and expired credit cards, and there is no profit clarity guiding the gap between revenue earned and revenue kept. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Follow-up on unpaid invoices and expired credit cards is poor — revenue is being billed but not being fully collected, a direct and ongoing drain on profitability
  • No profit visibility was shared on the call — the firm tracks revenue targets ($220,000 CAD/month minimum) but there is no evidence of a structured profit plan that shows what Ronen actually keeps after covering a 15-person team and operating costs
  • Revenue goal of $4–5M CAD represents a 67–150% increase from current levels — scaling at that rate without a profit plan creates real risk of growing overhead faster than margin, ending up with more revenue and less take-home
  • The dual-domain website structure (two active websites for the same firm) represents unnecessary maintenance cost with no revenue benefit
Blind Spot: At $2–3M CAD annual revenue with a 15-person team in Toronto, the firm's break-even point is likely in the $1.8–2.2M CAD range. If collections are leaking even 5–10% of billed revenue, the firm could be netting less profit than it appears on paper — and Ronen may not have the financial clarity to know it is happening.
The Positive
  • Revenue of $2–3M CAD with a defined goal of $4–5M CAD indicates a firm generating meaningful income and thinking ambitiously about its future — the baseline is strong, and the ambition is real
  • A broad immigration practice covering Express Entry, spousal sponsorship, refugee claims, work permits, business immigration, and Federal Court appeals diversifies revenue across multiple immigration demand cycles and client types
The Gap
  • Every month with poor collections follow-up is a month where revenue earned on paper does not land in the bank — at 5% leakage on $2–3M CAD annual billings, that is $100,000–$150,000 CAD per year walking out the door uncollected
  • Without a structured profit plan, scaling to $4–5M CAD will likely require proportional team growth — which, without margin clarity, could compress or eliminate the profit improvement that growth should deliver
  • Ronen's ultimate goal is not just revenue growth — it is financial freedom — and a firm where profit is not predictable cannot safely delegate, cannot plan compensation, and cannot build the kind of enterprise value that creates wealth beyond a salary
A firm that earns $4–5M CAD but has no clarity on what it keeps is not the firm Ronen is working toward — a profit plan is what turns revenue growth into actual personal wealth and the freedom to step back.
