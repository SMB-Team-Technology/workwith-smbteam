Intake Assessment

With no live chat, no confirmed intake specialist, and no automated follow-up on the website, Maiden Law Firm is likely relying on the attorney to personally manage every new inquiry — creating a bottleneck that limits case volume and leaves after-hours prospects with nowhere to turn. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • No live chat or chatbot on the website — prospects arriving outside of business hours find no immediate way to start the intake process and are likely moving on to the first competitor who is available
  • With a two-attorney firm and limited confirmed support staff, new inquiries likely fall to the attorney personally — the most expensive and time-constrained person in the firm handling the most routine function
  • No confirmed CRM or intake tracking system observed — leads who contact the firm and don't immediately book can fall through the cracks with no structured follow-up sequence to bring them back
  • No after-hours answering service or automated form response visible — prospects submitting forms or calling at night or on weekends may wait until the next business day, by which time they may have already retained a competitor
Blind Spot: The highest-value personal injury cases — serious accidents, traumatic injuries — are often signed on the day of the accident or within the first 24 hours. Every hour without a response is a case that is already being lost to the first attorney who calls back. In a market where Joye Law Firm advertises "Call 24/7," after-hours availability is not a differentiator — it is the minimum standard.
The Positive
  • Contact form is prominently placed above the fold with clear fields — the capture point exists and is easy to find; the follow-up system after that capture is what needs to be built
The Gap
  • A PI firm without after-hours intake coverage is functionally closed for the majority of hours in a day — accident victims call when they are in crisis, not during business hours, and competitors with 24/7 availability are getting those cases
  • Without a CRM or lead tracking system, there is no visibility into how many inquiries are coming in, what percentage are converting, or how many are being lost to slow follow-up
  • The attorney personally handling intake creates a hard ceiling on case volume — every inquiry that requires Brooke's direct attention is time she is not spending on billable casework
Systematized intake is the only thing that lets Brooke take a day off without cases slipping through the cracks — and it is the first step toward the freedom she is building toward.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact form on the homepage captures name, email, phone, and case description — the fields are correct and the placement is strong, but no confirmation email or automated follow-up appears to be configured after submission
  • No live chat tool present on the website — visitors who have a quick question but are not ready to call have no way to engage, and mobile visitors in particular expect to be able to message immediately
  • SMS/text line (843-375-6262) is referenced in the website's consent language but is not prominently featured as a contact option — a missed opportunity to capture the large percentage of PI prospects who prefer texting over calling

These findings are also referenced in the Website Audit section.

Team Assessment

Maiden Law Firm appears to operate with Brooke Maiden as principal attorney, Graham Maiden as co-attorney, and minimal confirmed support staff — a structure that places the owner at the center of every function the firm needs to run. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • With one confirmed employee per BBB and two attorneys, casework, intake, client communication, and business development are almost certainly being handled by the attorneys themselves with limited delegation possible
  • No visible operations manager, intake coordinator, or office administrator role — every administrative function falls to the attorneys by default, consuming time that should be on billable work
  • No org chart, formal accountability system, or team meeting structure visible — without structure, growth adds workload to the owner rather than distributing it
  • The firm's production capacity is bounded by how many hours Brooke and Graham personally work — there is no leverage in the current structure
Blind Spot: When both attorneys are the firm's only production capacity and its only management structure, there is no redundancy. A vacation, illness, a personal commitment, or a complex case that demands full attention can bring the entire firm's intake and follow-up to a halt. That is not a firm — that is a job.
The Positive
  • Having two attorneys rather than one provides more coverage than a true solo practice — this is a real foundation to build a delegation and team structure from, not a starting point of zero
The Gap
  • Without at least one trained non-attorney team member managing intake, scheduling, and client communication independently, there is no pathway for Brooke to step back from day-to-day operations
  • The gap between the current team structure and a self-managing firm is a hiring and training problem — not a capability problem; the right team structure is within reach in 12–18 months with the right coaching framework
  • Every month without a dedicated intake and support role is a month where new marketing investment drives more leads into a bottleneck that the attorneys are personally managing — defeating the purpose of growth
The team structure Maiden Law Firm builds in the next 12 months will determine whether growth creates freedom or just more work.

Profit Plan Assessment

With no stated revenue figures, no confirmed financial tracking system, and a contingency-only fee structure that creates uneven cash flow cycles, Maiden Law Firm currently operates without the financial visibility needed to know whether growth efforts are actually creating wealth. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Revenue not disclosed and financial performance not confirmed — without a baseline, there is no way to measure whether growth investments are producing real returns or just keeping the firm busy
  • Contingency fee structure creates feast-or-famine cash flow — multi-month gaps between case sign and settlement make consistent owner compensation and budget planning difficult without a formal financial framework
  • Mixed practice areas (car accident + mesothelioma) carry dramatically different case values and time-to-settle cycles — without tracking by practice area, it is impossible to know which cases are actually most profitable
  • No confirmed cost-per-case tracking or acquisition cost metrics — without knowing what it costs to get a case, there is no reliable way to calculate whether new marketing investment will generate a positive return
Blind Spot: PI firms with mixed practice areas — especially those handling both car accident volume cases and mesothelioma matters — can easily under-account for the overhead, referral fees, expert costs, and extended case cycles associated with their highest-revenue case type. Mesothelioma settlements are larger but so are the costs; without separate profit tracking, what looks like a high-value case may net less than a straightforward MVA after expenses.
The Gap
  • Without knowing the current revenue, cost structure, and profit margin, there is no way to calculate whether the recommended marketing investment will accelerate profitability or simply maintain the current trajectory with more overhead
  • Owner compensation in PI firms is often irregular and reactive — a profit plan converts that into a predictable monthly draw with a clear annual income target and a roadmap to reach it
  • Without practice-area profit tracking, the firm cannot determine whether to push harder on car accident marketing (higher volume, shorter cycle) or mesothelioma referral development (lower volume, dramatically higher per-case value) — both require different investment levels and produce different financial outcomes
A clear profit plan is what converts revenue growth into an actual ownership income — and ownership income is what buys back Brooke's time and builds the financial freedom she is working toward.
