Intake Assessment

Mandall is generating 600 leads a month, but the process behind turning a signed client into a completed, paid file is slow and entirely manual. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Files take 75–90 days to complete versus an ideal 2–3 weeks, caused by inefficient client follow-up
  • The second retainer payment is due at submission — the 75–90 day delay directly delays this collection, creating a real cash flow drag
  • Sales are tracked manually and inaccurately in Apple Notes — there is no system giving visibility into where leads stall
  • Austin personally handles payment collection and file opening rather than a defined intake role
Blind Spot: the 75–90 day file delay is not just a client-experience issue — it is quietly capping how fast the firm can reinvest its own cash into growth, since every delayed submission pushes real revenue further out.
The Gap
  • Every file that sits for 90 instead of 21 days delays real cash collection by roughly two months — money that could otherwise be funding the ad spend needed to hit the $500K/mo goal
  • Manual tracking in Apple Notes means leads can fall through the cracks with no way to catch it until a client complains
  • Without a defined follow-up process, faster file completion is left entirely up to how much personal attention Austin has left to give each file
Fixing intake is the fastest path to the freedom Austin is after — every file that moves off his desk and through a real process is time back in his life without giving up a dollar of revenue.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • The contact form is not above the fold — it sits mid-page after intro content, with a duplicate in the footer
  • No live chat is present, so visitors who are not ready to call or fill out a form have no lower-commitment way to start a conversation
  • The strong above-the-fold "Book a Consultation" and click-to-call CTAs are doing the heavy lifting intake-wise right now — but they only capture visitors who are already ready to commit

Team Assessment

Mandall has grown to a 10-person team in under two years, but the core admin work of running the firm still routes through Austin personally. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Austin personally handles payment collection and file opening — no delegation of this work exists despite a 10-person team
  • The firm is slow to fire underperforming staff, letting accountability gaps sit unresolved
  • No dedicated ops, marketing, or intake owner has emerged as the team has grown — decisions and low-value admin work both still land on Austin
  • Sales tracking lives in Apple Notes rather than any shared system the team can be held accountable to
Blind Spot: the fact that Austin has kept the firm running this well while still doing admin work himself is actually the risk — it means the firm has never been tested without him in that seat, and there is no proof yet that it can run without him.
The Positive
  • A 10-person team is real infrastructure most 22-month-old firms do not have yet — the raw material for delegation already exists
The Gap
  • Every hour Austin spends on payment collection and file opening is an hour he is not spending on the strategy and travel he built the firm to afford
  • Slow accountability on underperformance means the team is not yet operating at the standard a self-managing firm requires
  • Without a defined ops owner, growth just adds more work to Austin's plate instead of taking work off it
The financial freedom and travel Austin is working toward do not arrive at a higher revenue number — they arrive the day admin work has a real owner other than him.

Profit Plan Assessment

Mandall runs healthy 55–60% margins, but those profits are not being strategically reinvested toward the firm's own growth goals. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
"Profitability: 55–60% margins, but profits are not strategically reinvested"
The Problem
  • Margins are known and healthy at 55–60%, but there is no strategic plan for reinvesting that profit toward the firm's growth targets
  • Manual sales tracking in Apple Notes makes it difficult to connect specific ad spend or practice areas to actual profitability
  • The 75–90 day file completion delay creates a cash flow drag that sits on top of an already unpredictable weekly revenue picture
  • No performance reporting from the current marketing agency means ad dollars cannot be evaluated against the return they actually produce
Blind Spot: knowing the margin number is not the same as having a plan — 55–60% margins sitting idle instead of funding growth is the exact gap between where Mandall is and the $500K/mo target Austin has set.
The Gap
  • Without a reinvestment plan, healthy margins do not compound into the $100M/year vision — they just accumulate without direction
  • Cash flow drag from slow file completion limits how confidently the firm can commit to scaling ad spend
  • Unreported ad performance means profit decisions are being made without the data to back them
A real profit plan is what turns Mandall's 55–60% margins into the fuel for the aggressive growth and eventual freedom Austin is building toward — not just a number he already knows.
