Lead Generation

Mandall Immigration Law has scaled to a ~$2M revenue run rate almost entirely on one Google Ads account and organic search — but that single paid channel is inconsistent, unreported, and unsupported by any diversified mix of the other channels its established Toronto competitors are already running.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Mobile and desktop PageSpeed scores could not be independently confirmed during this audit and should be verified in onboarding — early signals suggest a modern, well-built site, so this is a validation step rather than a known red flag
  • The contact form sits mid-page after intro content and repeats in the footer — present, but not literally above the fold where the "Book a Consultation" and phone-number CTAs already live
  • No live chat is present, and no video content exists anywhere on the site — both are trust-building, conversion-lifting tools competitors increasingly use
  • Attorney bio for Austin Mandall Rooplall is present but brief — thin relative to the trust signals a 40-year firm like Green & Spiegel can lean on
Blind Spot: the site's actual build quality is stronger than how it was described on the call — it is a modern, multi-location site with dedicated geo-service pages and an active blog, not the "basic Fiverr build" it was characterized as. That gap is worth closing directly: either the site was upgraded since that impression formed, or expectations are being benchmarked against a fully custom, higher-end build. Either way, the site is a stronger asset than the current internal narrative gives it credit for.
The Positive
  • Clear, above-the-fold CTAs ("Book a Consultation" and a click-to-call phone number) in the hero section
  • Dedicated geo-targeting landing pages already live for Ottawa, Scarborough, and Mississauga, plus service-specific pages for work permits, visitor visas, express entry, and study permits
  • An active blog with recent, relevant posts on refusal rates and IRCC's new AI strategy — real content investment already happening
The Gap
  • Without confirmed Core Web Vitals data, it is impossible to know how many mobile visitors are abandoning the site before the contact form ever loads
  • No live chat and no video mean visitors who are not ready to call have no lower-commitment way to engage — those visitors likely convert with a competitor instead
  • A thin attorney bio undersells the "all files personally reviewed by Austin" trust signal the firm already has
A website that converts more of its existing traffic is capital Austin does not have to spend twice — every fix here compounds the return on the ad spend already going into the site.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Mandall does not appear in organic results for "immigration lawyer Toronto near me" or "express entry lawyer Toronto" — the two highest-intent generic searches in the market
  • The firm's exact Google review count could not be independently confirmed, but given the firm's 22-month age it is almost certainly well below the 390–1,182 reviews held by its three named competitors
  • The firm does show up organically for "work permit lawyer Toronto" via its dedicated work-permits page — proof the content strategy works when a page exists, it simply does not exist everywhere yet
Blind Spot: a 5.0-star rating with a small review count can actually be riskier than a 4.8 with a thousand reviews — a single bad review swings the average hard at low volume, and competitors know this is the moment to out-review a young firm before its base compounds.
The Positive
  • 5.0-star rating confirmed consistently across the firm's own site badges and third-party listings
  • Geo-targeting pages already live for Ottawa, Scarborough, and Mississauga, plus an active BBB profile under the Ottawa office
The Gap
  • Every month without a visible organic presence for "immigration lawyer Toronto" is a month Green & Spiegel's 40-year head start gets harder to close
  • A thin review base limits how much local pack and map visibility the firm can win no matter how good the service is
Green & Spiegel — dominates organic visibility for the exact generic searches Mandall is missing, on the strength of 1,182 reviews and 40+ years of accumulated authority.
Closing the review and organic-visibility gap is what turns Mandall from "found if you already know the work-permit page" into "found first" — the difference between chasing leads and having them come to you.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Currently spending $28K/mo on Google Ads plus $7K/mo in management fees, generating 550 paid leads/mo — but with no performance reporting from the current agency, there is no way to know which campaigns, keywords, or practice areas are actually driving that volume
  • Results are highly inconsistent week to week ($37K in revenue one week, $111K the next), and a prior $4K/mo Vancouver campaign produced only $20K in revenue over six months — a clear misallocation a recent audit already flagged
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Matkowsky Immigration Law — actively runs Google and Microsoft display remarketing campaigns, a diversified paid strategy layered on top of an 846+ review organic base
The Opportunity
  • A rebuilt account with practice-area-specific campaigns (work permits, PR/Express Entry, study permits, visitor visas, citizenship) replaces the current single-bucket approach with visibility into what each service line actually returns
  • Real weekly and monthly performance reporting turns "$37K one week, $111K the next" from a mystery into a managed, explainable number
  • Toronto-market immigration keywords run CAD $35–$85 CPC per industry benchmarks — a highly competitive, expensive category where poor account structure is the most expensive mistake a firm can make
  • Correcting the kind of geographic misallocation that produced the $4K/mo Vancouver campaign's weak return frees up budget to reinvest in the channels actually converting
Blind Spot: a $500K/mo revenue goal built on top of an ad account with zero reporting is a plan with no steering wheel — Austin cannot know what to scale until he can see what is actually working.

Facebook & Instagram Ads

Ad Presence
  • Mandall does not appear to be running Meta ads currently — the firm ran a Meta campaign in 2025 that returned a strong 3x ROAS but produced low-quality leads that burned out the intake team, and the channel was discontinued
  • That prior experience was a targeting and lead-quality problem, not proof Meta cannot work for this firm — a properly configured retargeting-first approach addresses exactly what went wrong last time
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Matkowsky Immigration Law — active ad presence not confirmed live this session, but the firm's confirmed Google/Microsoft remarketing investment suggests a firm actively testing paid channels beyond search
  • Green & Spiegel — active ad presence not confirmed live this session; recommend a live Meta Ads Library check to confirm before the next call
The Opportunity
  • Retargeting — the website visitors who did not convert during the 2025 campaign's failure are exactly the audience a retargeting-first Meta approach is built to recapture, without repeating the cold-lead mistake
  • Video ads featuring Austin — builds the personal trust signal the site's brief bio does not yet carry, before a prospect ever calls
  • Educational content ads addressing common Express Entry and work permit questions — capture leads earlier in the decision process than a "Book a Consultation" ad alone
  • A warm-audience-only relaunch protects the intake team from the lead-quality problem that shut the channel down the first time
