Lead Generation

Albizu Law has grown to $1.8M almost entirely on referrals and reputation, with no active paid advertising currently running in either immigration or family law. That growth has been real — but it has also left the firm with zero visibility in local search at a time when its top competitors dominate every relevant channel.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • No on-page contact form on the homepage — visitors are routed to a separate "Schedule Now!" page rather than converting immediately above the fold
  • PageSpeed scores could not be obtained — the PageSpeed Insights API was rate-limited across 6 separate attempts; recommend a manual re-check before the proposal call
  • No video present on the homepage to build trust with a prospect before they ever call
  • No live chat present — hesitant or after-hours visitors have no lower-friction way to reach out
Blind Spot: The family law Google Ads landing page already built and not currently in use is a sunk cost sitting idle — the site is already built to convert paid traffic, it just is not receiving any right now.
The Positive
  • Mobile responsive with a modern, consistent template and active 2025-2026 blog posts showing ongoing maintenance
  • Dedicated attorney bio page for Iris Albizu with photo builds trust and credibility
The Gap
  • The buried contact path likely costs the firm hand-raising leads who want to act immediately, not click through to another page
  • No live chat means hesitant or after-hours visitors — common in a bilingual, relationship-driven practice — have no way to start a conversation outside business hours
  • Missing video means prospects cannot connect with Iris personally before they ever pick up the phone
Every visitor who leaves because the site made it too hard to reach you is a client Iris has to replace with more of her own hours — closing this gap costs nothing extra to acquire.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Google Business Profile shows ~40 reviews at 4.7 stars — the lowest review count of any competitor identified in this audit, across both immigration and family law
  • Zero appearance in the local 3-pack across all six searches performed (three immigration, three family law) for Austin
  • A live citation conflict was found: four major directories (Justia, LawInfo, FindLaw, and BBB) all list the firm's prior address (111 W. Anderson Ln.) instead of the current address used on Yelp, GBP, and the firm's own site (8500 Shoal Creek Blvd) — with Avvo compounding the problem by listing a third address entirely — this kind of NAP inconsistency actively works against local map rankings
Blind Spot: Google uses name, address, and phone (NAP) consistency as a direct local ranking signal — with four separate directories pointing to a stale address, this mismatch could quietly be capping how high Albizu Law can rank in the map pack, no matter how many other signals improve.
The Positive
  • The firm has already built dedicated geo-targeted landing pages for 16 surrounding cities, including Cedar Park, Round Rock, San Antonio, Georgetown, Killeen, Waco, Leander, Pflugerville, San Marcos, Kyle, Manor, Harker Heights, Elgin, Copperas Cove, Buda, and Bastrop — a real local SEO asset most firms this size have not invested in
The Gap
  • Without appearing in the map pack, none of the sixteen geo-targeted landing pages are getting the visibility they were built for
  • Every named competitor in this audit outranks Albizu Law in local search despite the firm offering a bilingual differentiator none of them have
Lincoln-Goldfinch Law — with a review count in the thousands (Birdeye-aggregated at 4,460 and climbing) at roughly 4.9 stars, consistently occupies the top local-pack positions for every immigration search performed in this audit.
Correcting the citation conflict and building review volume is the fastest, lowest-cost path to finally showing up where your ideal bilingual clients are already searching.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Immigration Law — this firm does not appear to be running Google search ads for immigration-related terms; it may not be configured to show for these terms, or may be getting outbid by competitors with larger budgets
  • Family Law — the firm has its own dedicated Google Ads landing page built for family law (/family-law-lp/), indicating past or planned paid search investment, but ads were not observed live in current search results, so the campaign does not appear to be active right now
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Lincoln-Goldfinch Law & Alonso & Alonso Attorneys at Law — both appear prominently and repeatedly across organic and paid immigration search results, consistent with active paid search investment
  • Kirker Davis LLP & Modern Family Law — both show up consistently across paid-intent search queries for Austin divorce and child custody terms
The Opportunity
  • "immigration lawyer Austin" and "family law attorney Austin" — the obvious head terms neither campaign is currently capturing
  • "deportation defense lawyer Austin" and "child custody attorney Austin" — case-type specific terms with real buyer intent
  • Bidding on competitor names (Lincoln-Goldfinch Law, Kirker Davis) to intercept prospects already comparing firms
  • Spanish-language campaign variants — the firm's bilingual identity is a differentiator none of the named competitors are positioned to match
Blind Spot: The family law landing page already built and not currently receiving traffic means turning ads back on could start converting immediately, without waiting on new page development.
