Here Is Where Albizu Law Stands Today

Iris Albizu built Albizu Law into a bilingual, Latina-led immigration and family law firm in Austin, growing revenue from roughly $800K in 2023 to $1.8M in 2025 almost entirely on referrals and reputation. The single most important finding: this is a bandwidth problem, not a lead generation problem — Iris personally absorbs about 85% of the casework across a 70-hour week, and the firm's next stage of growth depends on building capacity, not chasing more volume.

Stage 6 is where the firm runs without you personally carrying 85% of the casework — where the 40-hour week and the time with your daughter before she leaves for college are no longer a trade-off against the firm's growth.

Where You Are Today

STAGE 3: SOLO PRACTITIONER
  • Growth has come almost entirely from referrals and reputation, not a built lead generation system
  • Iris personally handles roughly 85% of casework across a ~70-hour week
  • A family law Google Ads landing page exists but no active paid campaign is currently running
  • The 2026 revenue projection was revised down due to immigration policy shifts, with no system in place to absorb that kind of external shock

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
You have already proven the demand exists — the firm has more than doubled revenue in two years without a formal growth system. The gap between here and Stage 6 is not more clients; it is the team and structure to serve the clients you already have without it costing you your family time.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Albizu Law
Albizu Law is operating at Stage 3 — a Solo Practitioner firm whose revenue has grown faster than its systems, built almost entirely on referrals, reputation, and Iris's own hours. The single most urgent gap limiting growth right now is capacity: with Iris absorbing 85% of casework at 70 hours a week, there is no structural way to reach the $5M–$6M goal without either burning out or bringing on the strategic hires and workflows to support it. Until that gap closes, every additional client the firm's strong reputation attracts adds more weight to Iris's shoulders rather than more freedom to her life.
