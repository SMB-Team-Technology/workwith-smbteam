Intake Assessment

Iris personally absorbs the vast majority of client intake on top of an already full caseload, with no dedicated intake team or defined follow-up process described on the call. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • No dedicated intake team or process — the owner personally handles client-facing follow-up on top of an already full caseload
  • No CRM or intake tracking system mentioned, so leads have no structured follow-up sequence
  • No live chat or after-hours coverage — the homepage routes all inquiries to a separate scheduling page with no lower-friction option
  • No defined process for following up with leads who do not sign on the first contact
Blind Spot: You may not realize how many after-hours inquiries — especially from Spanish-speaking families researching options at night — reach the buried contact page and simply never convert because no one responds before a competitor does.
The Gap
  • Every lead lost to slow or missing follow-up is revenue the firm already earned through referrals and reputation, wasted
  • Iris's time spent on intake directly reduces the hours available for higher-value case work or personal time
  • Without a system, any improvement in lead volume would only add more pressure to an already overloaded intake process
Fixing intake first means every future marketing dollar converts more efficiently — and gives Iris back hours she is currently spending chasing leads instead of her daughter.

Team Assessment

Iris personally handles roughly 85% of casework across a ~70-hour week, with no manager or delegated leadership layer described. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Iris personally handles roughly 85% of casework across a ~70-hour week
  • No manager, associate attorney, or paralegal layer described that could absorb casework independently
  • The stated hiring plan (4-5 strategic hires) has not yet materialized, and finding qualified bilingual candidates has been the bottleneck
  • No leadership or delegation structure exists to let the firm run without Iris's direct involvement in each case
Blind Spot: The fact that referrals keep flowing in even without a dedicated growth system may be masking how close the firm is to a breaking point — Iris's current pace has no slack left to absorb the four to five hires the growth plan requires.
The Positive
  • Iris has already defined a clear hiring roadmap (2-3 attorneys, 2 paralegals) rather than treating team growth as an afterthought
The Gap
  • Every hour Iris spends on casework rather than leadership is an hour the growth plan sits unbuilt
  • Owner-dependency at this scale means revenue growth and personal freedom are currently working against each other, not together
  • Without delegation, the firm cannot safely absorb more leads even if the lead generation gaps identified in this audit were fixed tomorrow
Until Iris has a team that runs cases without her in the room, the sustainable 40-hour week and the time with her daughter will stay exactly as far away as they are today.

Profit Plan Assessment

Albizu Law has grown revenue from ~$800K to $1.8M in two years, but no profit margin, cost structure, or financial plan was discussed on the call. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No profitability commentary was made on the call — revenue figures were shared, but nothing about margin or take-home
  • The 2026 revenue projection was already revised downward due to external immigration policy shifts, with no described plan for managing that kind of swing
  • No fractional CFO or financial oversight currently in place
  • No stated visibility into cost per case, client acquisition cost, or margin by practice area
Blind Spot: Growing revenue while your own hours are the primary input can hide a profit problem — the firm's 2025 revenue growth does not by itself tell Iris what she is actually keeping.
The Positive
  • The firm has more than doubled revenue in two years (~$800K to $1.8M), proving the underlying demand and pricing model work
The Gap
  • Without profit visibility, Iris cannot tell which growth investments actually improve her take-home versus just add more work
  • The stated $5M-$6M goal has no financial plan behind it yet to confirm it would produce meaningfully more profit, not just more revenue moving through the firm
  • No buffer or plan was described for absorbing revenue swings like the one already seen in the 2026 projection
A clear profit plan is what turns the $5M-$6M goal into more freedom for Iris — not just a bigger number that requires more of her time to sustain.
