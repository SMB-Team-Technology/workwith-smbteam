The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Intake Assessment

Every prospective client conversation currently reaches Brandon directly — there is no delegated intake process standing between a referral and his personal calendar. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Brandon personally handles every prospective client conversation — no delegation exists at any stage
  • No CRM or intake tracking system — referrals and inquiries are managed informally
  • No after-hours coverage — evening or weekend inquiries have no defined path to a response
  • No defined follow-up sequence exists for a prospect who doesn't retain the firm on the first conversation
Blind Spot: Every hour Brandon spends personally screening and following up with prospects is an hour not spent on billable transactional work or the next referral relationship — intake is quietly capping the firm's growth, not just its client experience.
The Positive
  • Institutional and corporate prospects at this deal size often expect and value speaking directly with a name partner early on — Brandon's personal involvement is a credibility asset at the firm's current stage, not purely a bottleneck
The Gap
  • A referral who calls after hours or during a closing crunch has no path to a response but voicemail — a lost referral is a lost, hard-won relationship in this practice, not just a lost lead
  • Without tracking, there's no way to know how many warm referrals go cold waiting for Brandon's attention
  • As deal volume grows, intake capacity will hit its ceiling before revenue does — the firm's growth rate is already outpacing what one person can personally manage
Fixing intake is the fastest way to protect your time before you formally invest in lead generation — you cannot become the rainmaker while you are also the entire intake department.
