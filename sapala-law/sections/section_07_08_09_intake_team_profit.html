Intake Assessment

Every prospective client conversation currently reaches Brandon directly — there is no delegated intake process standing between a referral and his personal calendar. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Brandon personally handles every prospective client conversation — no delegation exists at any stage
  • No CRM or intake tracking system — referrals and inquiries are managed informally
  • No after-hours coverage — evening or weekend inquiries have no defined path to a response
  • No defined follow-up sequence exists for a prospect who doesn't retain the firm on the first conversation
Blind Spot: Every hour Brandon spends personally screening and following up with prospects is an hour not spent on billable transactional work or the next referral relationship — intake is quietly capping the firm's growth, not just its client experience.
The Positive
  • Institutional and corporate prospects at this deal size often expect and value speaking directly with a name partner early on — Brandon's personal involvement is a credibility asset at the firm's current stage, not purely a bottleneck
The Gap
  • A referral who calls after hours or during a closing crunch has no path to a response but voicemail — a lost referral is a lost, hard-won relationship in this practice, not just a lost lead
  • Without tracking, there's no way to know how many warm referrals go cold waiting for Brandon's attention
  • As deal volume grows, intake capacity will hit its ceiling before revenue does — the firm's growth rate is already outpacing what one person can personally manage
Fixing intake is the fastest way to protect your time before you formally invest in lead generation — you cannot become the rainmaker while you are also the entire intake department.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • No contact form is visible above the fold on the homepage — only a physical address is shown in the observed content
  • No phone number was captured in the observed homepage content, creating friction for a referral ready to reach out immediately
  • No live chat is present on the site

Team Assessment

Sapala LLP is a firm of one — Brandon is the founder, the closer, the deal-runner, and the entire operations team, five months into the business. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
"Transition from operator to rainmaker within 3-5 years."
The Problem
  • Brandon is the firm's only attorney and operator — every deal, client relationship, and dollar collected runs through him alone
  • No delegated ownership of any function — marketing, intake, operations, or finance — exists five months in
  • No accountability structure or leadership layer exists to develop as the firm grows
  • The website lists a second partner, Donald Sapala, but the operative reality described on the call is a solo, one-person operation
Blind Spot: The fact that the firm is already growing fast on Brandon's personal effort alone is itself the blind spot — it means the ceiling on growth right now is not the market, it's how many hours are in Brandon's week.
The Positive
  • Donald Sapala's decades of litigation and risk-management experience, including as former Managing Counsel at a Fortune 250 company, is a real bench asset the firm can build a leadership structure around
The Gap
  • Every hour spent on operations and intake is an hour not spent on billable transactional work or high-value relationship-building
  • There is no one to hand deal execution, client communication, or firm operations to if Brandon is unavailable
  • The residential title agency and Miami commercial finance visions both require leadership capacity that does not yet exist — they cannot be run by the same one person running the current practice
You told us you want to transition from operator to rainmaker within three to five years — that transition starts with building a team now, while the firm is still small enough to build it deliberately.

Profit Plan Assessment

Sapala LLP's collections have grown from $28K to $43K a month in under five months — real, fast traction for a five-month-old firm. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
"Collected: $28k (Apr), $30s (May), $43k (June). Billed: >$50k (June)."
The Problem
  • No margin, overhead, or profit-percentage figures were shared — revenue is being tracked, but profitability is not
  • Billings (over $50K in June) are already running ahead of collections ($43K) — a gap between what's earned and what's actually collected that hasn't been addressed
  • No formal profit plan or coaching relationship exists to translate this early growth into predictable take-home income
  • As a five-month-old firm, there is no full-year financial picture yet to plan against — decisions are being made month to month
Blind Spot: A widening gap between what you bill and what you collect is often the first sign of a receivables problem — worth watching closely as deal volume grows, before it becomes a cash flow problem instead of a paperwork one.
The Positive
  • Collections have grown from $28K to $43K in three months without any paid marketing spend — the underlying practice is genuinely in demand; the gap is in planning, not production
The Gap
  • Without margin visibility, Brandon can't tell whether growing revenue is translating into more personal income or just more work
  • The billed-versus-collected gap (over $50K billed vs. $43K collected in June) has no visibility into why it exists or whether it's growing
  • There's no financial plan connecting today's numbers to the "operator to owner" transition Brandon says he wants
You can't plan your transition from operator to owner without first knowing exactly what the firm keeps — a profit plan turns "growth" into a number you can actually build a future on.
