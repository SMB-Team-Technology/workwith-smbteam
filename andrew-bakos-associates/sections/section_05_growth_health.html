Pillar Health Scorecard

Lead Generation

  • Referral flow has collapsed from 10-20 leads per month to just 1-2 — active cases down from 150-200 to approximately 30
  • No paid advertising observed across any channel — Google, LSA, or Meta — while competitors spend aggressively in the same market
  • Firm does not appear in the local 3-pack for any primary practice area search across Folsom or Sacramento
Without a reliable lead flow, Andrew cannot select which cases he takes — the market selects for him.

Intake

  • Attorney personally handles all PI intake calls — no delegation, no defined process, no backup coverage
  • No after-hours coverage — prospects who call evenings or weekends have no way to reach anyone
  • No documented follow-up sequence — leads who do not sign on the first call have no system to re-engage them
Personally handling every intake call is the opposite of practicing selectively — it is being required in every conversation.

Self-Managing Team

  • Firm operates with two people — Andrew and one assistant — with no intake, operations, or marketing staff
  • Andrew is the single point of failure for intake, case strategy, and client relationships
  • Adding lead volume through paid advertising without adding team capacity creates a bottleneck at the attorney level
A two-person firm where the attorney is still the intake system cannot be the foundation for the legacy practice Andrew wants to build.

Profit

  • Revenue has declined as case volume dropped 90% — no stated financial target or revenue goal for recovery
  • No stated awareness of cost per acquisition or marketing ROI metrics to guide the investment decision
  • No evidence of a formal profit plan or financial tracking system beyond case billings
Without knowing the numbers behind each case type, it is impossible to plan the selective, high-value exit Andrew is working toward.
