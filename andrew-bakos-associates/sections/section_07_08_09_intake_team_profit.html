Intake Assessment

For Andrew Bakos & Associates, intake is entirely dependent on Andrew himself — every PI inquiry comes through him personally, which built trust and conversion when referrals were plentiful, but becomes a critical bottleneck the moment paid advertising creates a consistent flow of new leads. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
"I need leads yesterday."
The Problem
  • Attorney personally handles all PI intake calls — no delegation in place, no backup, and no system that functions when Andrew is unavailable
  • No after-hours coverage — prospects who call evenings, weekends, or in the immediate aftermath of a catastrophic accident have no way to reach anyone at the firm
  • No documented intake process or follow-up sequence — if a lead does not convert on the first call, there is no system to re-engage them before they hire a competitor
  • No intake tracking — no visibility into lead source, close rate, or which call types convert, making it impossible to measure or improve what is working
Blind Spot: Once paid advertising launches, lead volume will increase significantly — potentially 40-70 new inquiries per month at the conservative spend level. Without a structured intake system, the bottleneck is not the advertising; it is Andrew's personal bandwidth to answer every single call. The cost of that bottleneck is not measured in time — it is measured in cases signed by competitors who pick up.
The Positive
  • Andrew's direct involvement in intake has historically produced strong conversion rates — a personal approach that built his 35-year reputation is worth documenting and systematizing so it can be delegated without losing the quality
The Gap
  • Every catastrophic injury prospect who calls after 5pm or on a weekend with no answer is a potential high-value case — and a potential $500K+ settlement — given to a competitor who picks up
  • Without a follow-up sequence, leads who do not sign on first contact are permanently lost, even when the initial lead cost $300 or more to generate through paid advertising
  • If Andrew cannot personally take every intake call when ad volume increases, conversion rates drop and the marketing investment is wasted before it has a chance to prove its ROI
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • A "Quick Contact Form" (name, email, phone, message) is present on the homepage but positioned mid-page — below the fold for most visitors — meaning prospects who do not scroll past the primary content area miss it entirely
  • No after-hours contact option is available — prospects who visit the website outside business hours have no real-time path to engage, request a callback, or schedule a consultation
  • No auto-response or confirmation system observed for form submissions — a prospect who completes the form has no indication their inquiry was received, which reduces confidence and increases the likelihood they will call a competitor while waiting

Team Assessment

Andrew Bakos & Associates operates with two people — Andrew and one assistant — making it a firm where the attorney is the sole person qualified to handle intake, case strategy, and client relationships across every active matter. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Firm operates with two staff members — Andrew and one assistant — with no intake coordinator, case manager, operations, or marketing personnel
  • Andrew is the sole attorney and the sole intake handler — there is no redundancy in the two most critical firm functions, and no one who can step in if he is in trial or unavailable
  • Adding lead volume through paid advertising without adding team capacity creates a direct bottleneck at the attorney level — more leads does not equal more cases without people to qualify and convert them
  • The firm's current capacity ceiling is Andrew's personal hours — not market demand, not case quality, not reputation
Blind Spot: The hands-on approach that built Andrew's 35-year reputation is genuinely valuable — but it also means the firm's growth ceiling has always been his personal bandwidth. Adding paid advertising without structural support does not expand that ceiling; it exposes it. The investment in marketing only compounds if there is someone to convert the leads it generates.
The Gap
  • Adding 40-70 leads per month through paid advertising creates an intake burden that one attorney cannot manage alongside active casework — and catastrophic injury cases demand full attention from the attorney who knows how to try them
  • Without a case coordinator or intake specialist, Andrew will constantly be choosing between new business development and the high-stakes case work he is uniquely qualified to do
  • The selective, high-value practice Andrew envisions for the next 5-7 years requires a structure that supports it — a system where Andrew does the courtroom work while someone else handles the pipeline
Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Profit Plan Assessment

Andrew Bakos & Associates has experienced a significant revenue decline as case volume dropped from 150-200 active cases to approximately 30 — yet there is no evidence of a formal financial tracking system, revenue target, or profit plan to quantify where the firm stands and what recovery looks like. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No stated revenue figure or financial target — without a specific number, there is no plan for what "success" in the final chapter looks like in financial terms
  • Case volume has dropped approximately 90% over recent years, but no known cost-per-acquisition or marketing efficiency benchmark exists to guide the recovery investment
  • Catastrophic injury and TBI cases have settlement timelines of 2-4 years — cash flow planning is critical for a firm with a smaller active docket, and there is no evidence of a formal system for managing this
  • No stated awareness of what the "selective final chapter" requires financially — how many cases at what average value equals the exit on Andrew's terms
Blind Spot: Catastrophic injury cases can take 2-4 years to resolve. With approximately 30 active cases and no new lead generation system, the revenue runway may be shorter than the active docket suggests — and without tracking case-level financials and expected resolution timelines, Andrew may not see the shortfall coming until it has already arrived.
The Positive
  • Catastrophic injury, TBI, and trucking cases command significantly higher settlements than standard PI — the active cases represent substantial potential revenue when they resolve, which provides a foundation to build from
The Gap
  • Without a profit plan, revenue recovery depends entirely on referrals returning — which the data shows they will not at prior levels
  • No understanding of marketing ROI means no way to evaluate whether the investment in paid advertising is generating returns before it is too late to course-correct
  • The exit Andrew envisions — practicing selectively and stepping away on his own terms — is a financial destination, and without knowing the number and a plan to reach it, "on my own terms" is hope rather than plan
The final chapter Andrew wants is a financial destination — and without a profit plan, there is no map to get there.
