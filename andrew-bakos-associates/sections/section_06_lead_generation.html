Lead Generation

Andrew Bakos & Associates has built a 35-year reputation as a tenacious trial attorney — but that reputation is invisible online at the exact moment potential catastrophic injury and TBI clients are searching for help. With referrals down 90% and no paid advertising of any kind in place, the firm is competing in one of Northern California's most aggressively marketed PI markets without a single digital lead generation system.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Site is estimated 5-10+ years old with a dated template layout — an outdated design signals to prospective high-value clients that the firm may not be as sharp as its reputation suggests
  • PageSpeed scores: Mobile and desktop scores could not be retrieved via automated fetch — manual verification required at pagespeed.web.dev before presentation; based on site characteristics (older template, no CDN references, no lazy loading), mobile score likely in the 40-65 range, which is below competitive thresholds
  • No blog or fresh content section — the site provides no ongoing SEO signal and gives Google no reason to rank it for new search queries
  • No video present — in catastrophic injury and TBI cases where trust is the primary decision factor, video is the fastest way to establish credibility before a prospect ever calls
Blind Spot: The site's meta and title tags reference a "New Version" — suggesting an older version may still be indexed by Google, creating a situation where two versions of the site compete for authority. This kind of duplicate indexing suppresses rankings and is invisible to most firm owners.
The Positive
  • Attorney bio page exists — important for trust and credibility in high-stakes catastrophic injury cases where prospective clients research the attorney before calling
  • Free consultation CTA with phone number is visible above the fold — the right structure is in place, even if the execution needs improvement
The Gap
  • Without a modern, fast-loading site optimized for conversions, every dollar spent on paid advertising drives traffic to a page that may be losing prospects before they reach the phone number
  • No geo-targeted landing pages for Folsom, Sacramento, or specific case types means no SEO capture for high-intent searches like "catastrophic injury attorney Folsom" or "TBI lawyer Sacramento"
  • The contact form is mid-page — prospects under the stress of a serious accident who do not scroll will leave without any path to connect
A website that converts is the foundation of everything — without it, Andrew cannot attract the caliber of catastrophic injury clients he is targeting for the years ahead, no matter how much is spent on advertising.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Only 8 Google reviews at 3.9 stars — the lowest review presence of any firm appearing in Sacramento and Folsom PI searches; competitors range from 76 to 900+ reviews
  • Firm does not appear in the local 3-pack for any primary practice area search — personal injury, catastrophic injury, TBI, trucking, or wrongful death — across both Folsom and Sacramento
  • NAP inconsistency detected: FindLaw lists a Sacramento address (1420 River Park Dr., Ste. 110) while all other directories and the website show the Folsom address (6611 Folsom-Auburn Road, Suite A) — this conflict suppresses local search trust signals
Blind Spot: Google's local algorithm uses NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency across directories as a credibility signal. The conflicting Sacramento address on FindLaw — almost certainly a legacy listing from an old location — may be actively suppressing the firm's local rankings right now. Fixing it costs nothing and is one of the fastest SEO wins available.
The Positive
  • Listed on 10+ directories including Avvo, Justia, FindLaw, Yelp, Lawyers.com, and Martindale-Hubbell — directory coverage is broad even if the listings need cleanup
The Gap
  • With 8 reviews versus Demas Law Group's 900+, the firm cannot compete for local 3-pack placement or LSA eligibility without a systematic review generation campaign — it is a structural gap, not a content gap
  • No geo-targeted landing pages for practice areas means zero SEO capture for the specific, high-intent searches that catastrophic injury prospects use when they are ready to hire
Demas Law Group — 900+ Google reviews at 4.9 stars; consistently appears in the local 3-pack for every catastrophic injury, TBI, and wrongful death search Andrew needs to win. Their review volume is the primary reason they hold top local placement while the firm remains invisible.
Dominating local search is the difference between Andrew choosing his next catastrophic case from a qualified pipeline and waiting for a referral that may not come.

Blind Spot: Andrew demonstrated sophistication about Sacramento PPC costs of $625-$1,500 per call — but this assumes a generic PI campaign competing on broad terms. A precisely targeted catastrophic injury and TBI campaign with dedicated, conversion-optimized landing pages can reduce cost-per-qualified-case while filtering out the smaller matters he does not want. The right campaign structure is not about spending less — it is about spending on exactly the right prospects.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • Andrew Bakos & Associates does not appear to have a Google Screened profile or active Local Service Ads presence for personal injury in Sacramento or Folsom — no verified LSA listing was observed
  • LSA requires Google Screened verification (bar status check, insurance, background — typically 3-4 weeks to complete); this is a one-time setup that unlocks top-of-page placement above every other Google result
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Demas Law Group — strong LSA presence supported by 900+ Google reviews at 4.9 stars; the review volume positions them for top LSA placement in Sacramento PI searches
  • AutoAccident.com (Edward A. Smith) — 24/7 availability and four decades of Sacramento brand presence support LSA dominance; active across Sacramento, Stockton, Elk Grove, and Modesto market areas
The Opportunity
  • A systematic review generation campaign targeting current and former satisfied clients can build from 8 to 30+ Google reviews within 90 days — the threshold that makes LSA competitive positioning viable
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • A Google Screened verification badge adds instant credibility for prospective catastrophic injury clients making a high-stakes attorney hiring decision — the green checkmark signals the kind of trustworthiness that Andrew's 35-year track record has earned
