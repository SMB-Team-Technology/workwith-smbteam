Lead Generation

McAdoo Estate Planning already runs active Google Ads and Local Service Ads campaigns and holds a review profile that beats every named competitor in Jamaica and Queens — but the website driving that traffic still sells probate as hard as estate planning, and the firm's local search footprint has real, fixable gaps.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • The homepage gives estate planning, Medicaid planning, probate, and wills/trusts roughly equal visual weight — directly contradicting the firm's own goal of leading with estate planning and de-emphasizing probate
  • PageSpeed scores could not be captured this session (public PageSpeed API quota was exhausted on repeated attempts) — a manual mobile and desktop check is needed before this is finalized
  • No attorney bio or credentials appear on the site, even though Kim is the firm's face and principal attorney
  • No video content appears anywhere on the homepage
Blind Spot: The prior web vendor, Non-Stuffy Attorney, has been terminated — meaning nobody is currently monitoring this site's technical health, uptime, or performance, and any issue that comes up could go unnoticed for weeks.
The Positive
  • A clear "Schedule a Consultation" call-to-action appears above the fold in both the navigation and the hero section
  • The blog is active, with a recent post published in January 2026
The Gap
  • A homepage that still sells probate as hard as estate planning is actively working against the practice mix the firm wants to grow into
  • Without an attorney bio, prospects researching a major decision like estate planning have no way to build trust in Kim before they call
  • The contact path requires an extra click through to a scheduling flow rather than an embedded above-the-fold form, adding friction at the exact moment a prospect is ready to act
Until the site reflects the estate-planning-first firm you're building, every dollar of ad spend is working against your own message — and Scott will keep manually patching a site that undermines the brand he's trying to build.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • The firm was not observed in top organic/directory results for "estate planning attorney Jamaica NY," "wills and trusts lawyer Queens NY," or "elder law attorney Jamaica Queens NY" — matching the transcript's own flagged concern about weak organic visibility
  • At least two distinct incorrect addresses and phone numbers are currently in circulation across Avvo, Justia, and Yelp — none of which match the firm's real Jamaica office or its site-listed phone number
  • The Yelp/Manhattan address has now started surfacing inside Google-sourced review aggregation itself — this is no longer a static directory problem, it is actively spreading into how the firm's own Google-linked profile gets read by aggregators
  • No geo-targeted landing pages exist for surrounding neighborhoods like Queens Village, Hollis, St. Albans, or Springfield Gardens
Blind Spot: FindLaw has no profile at all for the firm or for Kim McAdoo — a real directory gap, not just a weak listing.
The Positive
  • The firm's Google Business Profile shows a 4.9-star rating across 275 reviews — stronger on both volume and rating than every named local competitor, and still growing at a normal, healthy pace
The Gap
  • NAP inconsistency across major directories undermines the local search trust signals the firm's strong review profile has already earned — and the fact that it is now bleeding into Google-sourced aggregation makes this a worsening problem, not a static one
  • Without geo-targeted pages, the firm is invisible in hyper-local searches that competitors are actively capturing
Morgan Legal Group P.C. — now at 157 Google reviews (up from 154), and maintains dedicated geo + practice-area landing pages ("queens-elder-law-attorney," "wills-and-trusts-queens") that give it structural visibility McAdoo's site does not have.
Your reputation already outperforms the competition — the only thing standing in the way of it showing up in search is data cleanup and content Scott shouldn't have to build by hand.
