Intake Assessment

With every call routed through a lean 3-person team and AI answering tools filling the gaps, John Tatone has no dedicated intake process today. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No contact form found above the fold on your website — visitors have to search for a way to reach the firm before ever speaking to anyone
  • No dedicated intake team member — calls and follow-up are split between a remote paralegal, an in-house legal secretary, and AI call-answering tools
  • No live chat for after-hours website visitors — they either call, email, or leave
  • No confirmed follow-up sequence for leads who don't convert on the first phone call
Blind Spot: You may not realize how many people who need estate planning or probate help visit your site after hours, find no contact form and no chat, and simply call the next firm on the list instead.
The Positive
  • AI call answering (RingCentral) means even missed calls get an automated response instead of going straight to voicemail
  • GoHighLevel CRM is already in place to track and nurture leads — most firms this size have no CRM at all
The Gap
  • Every lead that calls after hours and gets no live chat or clear form is a case that may sign with a competitor by morning
  • With no dedicated intake role, follow-up quality and speed depend entirely on how much bandwidth the paralegal and legal secretary have that day
  • Without a defined follow-up sequence, the firm can't consistently tell which leads were lost to slow response versus lost to price or fit
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Team Assessment

Just three people run this entire practice — John, a remote paralegal, and an in-house legal secretary — with no manager, no dedicated ops or marketing role, and no layer between John and daily casework. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • No manager or team lead — every operational decision runs through John personally
  • No accountability structure beyond a 3-person team handling casework
  • No dedicated marketing, ops, or intake position — those responsibilities are patched together with AI tools instead of people
  • No evidence John has stepped away from the practice for any extended period since the 2023 pivot
Blind Spot: The heavy automation you've built — AI call answering, automated CRM nurturing — may be masking how dependent the practice still is on you personally. Automation replaces some tasks, but it hasn't replaced the decisions only you can make today.
The Positive
  • Choosing to automate (RingCentral, GoHighLevel) rather than simply add headcount shows real intent to build a leaner, more scalable operation
  • The team that does exist — a paralegal and a legal secretary — is stable enough to support day-to-day casework
The Gap
  • Every hour John spends managing tasks a team member or system could own is an hour he isn't spending building the freedom he pivoted this practice to create
  • With revenue projected to double to $500K in 2026, the current 3-person structure has no built-in room to absorb that growth without adding strain on John directly
  • No accountability layer means growth depends entirely on John's personal bandwidth, not on a system that scales
Building a self-managing team is what finally lets you take back the time a hearing loss once forced you to fight for.

Profit Plan Assessment

Revenue is on track to nearly double, from $270K to a projected $500K in 2026, but John tracks profitability channel-by-channel on marketing spend, not across the practice as a whole. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No confirmed close rate — defaulting to a 15% industry-standard estimate because none was stated
  • No stated average case value for estate planning or probate matters — a practice area default will be used for ROI projections
  • Financial visibility is limited to marketing channel ROI (LSA profitable, Access Legal ~3x, Facebook low ROI) — no confirmed view of overall firm margin
  • No profit plan in place to translate revenue growth into predictable take-home income
Blind Spot: Tracking marketing ROI channel-by-channel is valuable, but it is not the same as knowing your overall margin — a firm can grow revenue and still not know if it's actually more profitable.
The Positive
  • Already tracking ROI by channel (LSA profitable, Access Legal ~3x, Facebook low ROI) shows more financial discipline than most firms this size
The Gap
  • Without knowing average case value or true margin, it's impossible to confirm whether the projected jump to $500K in 2026 will translate into meaningfully more profit
  • No profit plan means growth could just mean more overhead and more work, not more wealth
A real profit plan is what proves the growth you're already seeing translates into the freedom you rebuilt this practice for — not just a bigger number on the revenue line.
