Here Is Where Morris, Andrews, Talmadge & Driggers LLC Stands Today

Morris, Andrews, Talmadge & Driggers LLC has practiced personal injury law across Dothan and Mobile, Alabama since 1999, building a $4-6M firm almost entirely on referrals from former clients and other attorneys. The four partners are now targeting an aggressive move to $10M+ in annual revenue, but the firm has no tracked digital marketing history, a broken primary domain (mcatlaw.com), and an unverified Google Business Profile in Dothan that blocks that office from local search entirely.

Stage 6 is where MCAT's four partners finally see the firm running as one unified growth engine instead of four individual practices sharing overhead — with the systems in place to prove the ROI they need before scaling further.

Where You Are Today

STAGE 4: SMALL BUSINESS MANAGER
  • 80% of new clients come from referrals — no predictable, tracked lead generation system exists
  • Four partners each run their own practice under one shared-overhead structure, with no single operator unifying systems
  • Paralegals field intake calls because the intake specialist role sits vacant, delaying lawsuits and demands on cases already signed
  • The only paid marketing history — two billboards and a past, untracked Google LSA campaign — never produced a measurable return

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
Closing the gap from where MCAT stands today to Stage 6 does not require the partners to work harder — it requires building the lead generation, intake, and operating systems that turn four independent practices into one dominant regional brand.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Morris, Andrews, Talmadge & Driggers LLC
Morris, Andrews, Talmadge & Driggers is operating at Stage 4 — a firm with real scale (5 attorneys, two offices, $4-6M in revenue) that has never had to build a true growth engine because referrals have carried it for over 25 years. The single biggest gap limiting growth right now is the near-total absence of a lead generation system: with 80% of business referral-dependent and no tracked paid marketing history, the firm has no lever to pull to generate the extra $2M/year needed to prove ROI to the partners and unlock the path to $10M+. Until that changes, the ambition to dominate the Black Belt market will remain aspirational rather than actionable.
