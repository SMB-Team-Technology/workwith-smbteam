Pillar Health Scorecard

Lead Generation

  • 80% of new clients come from referrals — there is no predictable, tracked lead generation system
  • The firm's own primary domain, mcatlaw.com, is broken and serves an error page
  • Only paid marketing history: two billboards ($2.5-3K/mo) and a past, untracked Google LSA campaign — no attribution, no way to know what worked
Every referral-only month is a month MCAT cannot prove the ROI the partners need to unlock the growth investment that gets them to $10M+.

Intake

  • Paralegals handle intake calls because the intake specialist role sits vacant, pulling them from filing lawsuits and sending demands
  • No dedicated after-hours or overflow coverage exists for intake calls in either office
  • No system to track or measure lead-to-client conversion across either office
Every lead lost to slow or inconsistent intake is revenue the partners never see — and proof of ROI they cannot show.

Self-Managing Team

  • The firm operates as "four chiefs" — four partners running individual practices under shared overhead, with no unified operating system
  • No fractional or in-house COO exists to build systems, hire, or manage the team day to day
  • 6.5 support staff across two offices are stretched thin covering roles, like intake, that should be dedicated positions
Until MCAT has an operator running systems day to day, the partners cannot step back enough to prove — or scale — the growth investment they are asking each other to make.

Profit

  • A fixed 35% of revenue goes to overhead by design, with the remainder paid directly to attorneys — leaving little capital available for new growth investment
  • No profit plan exists to fund the $2M/year growth push without cutting into partner compensation
  • Growth investment currently has to compete directly with take-home pay, which raises the bar for internal buy-in
A clear profit plan is what turns "this comes out of our pockets" into "this pays for itself" — the exact proof partners need before saying yes to bigger investment.
