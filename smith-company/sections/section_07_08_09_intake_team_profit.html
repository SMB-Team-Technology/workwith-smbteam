Intake Assessment

At Smith & Company PLLC, Eon handles every intake call himself — on top of handling every case, every court appearance, and every client relationship. There is no intake process, no delegation in place, and no system to ensure a prospect who calls after hours or during a trial is followed up before they call someone else. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Eon personally handles all intake calls with no delegation to Kim or part-time staff — when he is in court or working a case, inbound prospects wait or move on
  • No defined intake script, qualification process, or follow-up sequence — each new inquiry is handled differently based on Eon's availability, not a system
  • No after-hours coverage — calls and web inquiries after business hours have no guaranteed response timeline, and immigration and criminal matters often arise outside of 9-to-5
  • As paid ads begin producing volume, a broken intake system will convert a fraction of the leads being paid for — every unqualified or unanswered lead is money spent and cases lost
Blind Spot: Immigration and criminal prospects are often making contact during a crisis moment — a family member has been detained, an indictment just came down, a court date is tomorrow. These calls require an immediate, professional response or the prospect calls the next firm on the list. At current intake capacity, the firm may be losing its highest-urgency — and highest-value — cases at the first point of contact.
The Gap
  • At a 15% close rate and a blended case value of $5,100, every 10 leads that fall through intake unanswered represents over $7,500 in potential revenue permanently lost — and at the current intake setup, that number will only grow as marketing starts producing volume
  • Kim is already present and available — the gap is not headcount, it is process; a trained intake script and defined role for Kim would immediately increase conversion without adding cost
  • Every week without an intake system is a week where Eon's time is consumed by tasks that do not require his level of expertise — and a week where the 60-hour grind continues with no structural relief in sight
A functioning intake system is what frees Eon from being the first person every prospect talks to — and it is the prerequisite for every other piece of growth to work.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • A contact page exists at smithelaw.com/contact-us but its placement, form design, and above-fold visibility could not be directly assessed — the site blocked automated access during research
  • No live chat or chat widget was observed on the site; prospects who visit outside business hours have no way to start a conversation without making a phone call
  • With three separate websites, it is unclear which site a prospect lands on after a search, and whether each site has equivalent contact conversion infrastructure — this is a significant gap that consolidation and a website rebuild would resolve

Team Assessment

Smith & Company has Eon, Kim, and part-time staff — but the firm runs entirely on Eon. There are no defined roles, no accountability structure, and no system that would allow a single case to move forward without Eon's direct involvement. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Kim's role is informal — she is present and available, but without a defined job description, KPIs, or accountability, her capacity is not being used systematically
  • Eon is the sole point of contact for all clients, all cases, and all intake — there is no backup and no delegation protocol if he is unavailable
  • A full-time hire is a stated 2026 goal, but without a hiring blueprint — job description, compensation structure, onboarding plan — it cannot happen on a predictable timeline
  • The part-time staff capacity exists but is not being leveraged; without clear processes, additional staff creates coordination overhead rather than relief
Blind Spot: The fact that the firm has managed to reach $250,000 in revenue on referrals alone, with Eon working 60+ hours per week, is actually evidence of the problem rather than the solution. The plateau is not bad luck — it is structural. A firm where everything flows through one person cannot grow past the limit of what that one person can personally handle. The ceiling is not a market problem; it is an org chart problem.
The Positive
  • Kim's presence on the team means a capable person is already available to own the intake function — this is a significant head start compared to firms that need to hire their first team member before they can delegate anything
The Gap
  • Every hour Eon spends on intake, client coordination, and administrative tasks is an hour not spent on the federal criminal and removal cases that generate the highest revenue per hour of attorney time
  • Without a team structure, adding marketing will increase case volume but not Eon's capacity — revenue may grow while the 60-hour week grows with it, which is the opposite of the stated goal
  • A business coaching engagement that builds Kim's intake role, defines the first full-time hire, and creates an accountability structure is the prerequisite for Eon to work fewer hours as the firm earns more
The "Law Firm CEO" status Eon described is not a title — it is the point at which the firm produces income that is not directly dependent on his personal hours. That starts with Kim owning intake and ends with Eon managing a team rather than being the team.

Profit Plan Assessment

Smith & Company PLLC is generating approximately $250,000 in annual revenue — but on the discovery call, Eon did not know last year's exact revenue and had to confirm the figure with Kim. A firm working toward $600,000 in the next two years cannot be managed by feel. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Revenue was not confirmed on the call — Eon needed to verify the figure with Kim, which indicates that financial visibility is not a daily or weekly practice in this firm
  • No tracking by practice area means the firm does not know whether immigration or criminal defense produces more profit per hour of attorney time — this is the data that should drive where marketing dollars go first
  • The revenue goals (+$350k in 2026, 2x by 2027) are clearly articulated but not backed by a financial model: what does marketing cost, what does a full-time hire cost, what is the case value per practice area, and what is the margin after each new hire?
  • Without a client acquisition cost baseline established now, there will be no way to measure whether the marketing investment is working once it starts — the firm will be flying blind
Blind Spot: Federal criminal cases and removal defense cases have very different economics — different average fees, different case durations, and different referral multipliers. Without tracking revenue and cost by case type, Eon is making marketing decisions based on volume goals ("2 federal criminal cases + 3 removal cases per month") without knowing which case type produces the most profit per hour. Knowing that number changes everything about where to invest first.
The Positive
  • Eon's revenue goal is specific ($350K increase in 2026) and his target case types are precise (2 federal criminal + 3 removal per month) — that level of goal clarity is uncommon and gives a coaching engagement a concrete baseline to build a financial model around
The Gap
  • At $250K current revenue and a $600K goal, the firm needs to roughly 2.4× its current revenue in two years — that requires knowing exactly what each case type costs to acquire, what each produces in margin, and what team structure is financially sustainable at each growth milestone
  • A full-time hire by EOY 2026 is a significant financial commitment — without a model showing what revenue level supports that hire, it is a bet rather than a business decision
  • Growth without financial visibility means Eon could double revenue and still not take home meaningfully more — every dollar that goes to untracked expenses, unnecessary overhead, or underpriced cases is a dollar that does not reach his personal income
The Law Firm CEO that Eon described does not just have more revenue — he has more of his revenue landing in his pocket, on a schedule he can predict and plan around.
