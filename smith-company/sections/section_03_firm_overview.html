Here Is Where Smith & Company PLLC Stands Today

Attorney Eon R. Smith — a former federal prosecutor and U.S. Justice Advisor — operates Smith & Company PLLC out of Hartford, CT and New York City, handling immigration and criminal defense cases at a 50/50 split with no paid marketing and no formal lead generation system of any kind. The firm is built entirely on word-of-mouth referrals, and while Eon's credentials and courtroom record are genuinely exceptional, the single most important finding from this audit is stark: with approximately 2 total reviews across all platforms, the firm is functionally invisible online in a market where Norte Immigration Law carries 228 reviews and Ruane Attorneys carries over 1,000.

Stage 6 is what it looks like when the firm runs without you — when your income is no longer tied to how many hours you personally work, and when the 60-hour weeks become a choice rather than a requirement.

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you

The gap between 60-hour weeks on referrals and a firm that generates cases, converts leads, and runs with a trained team is real — but it is a systems gap, not a talent gap, and systems can be built.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Smith & Company PLLC

The single most urgent gap is the complete absence of any lead generation system: the firm has never run a paid ad, does not appear in the local 3-pack for immigration or criminal defense searches, and has approximately 2 total reviews in a market where leading competitors have hundreds to over a thousand.
