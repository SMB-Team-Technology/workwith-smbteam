Lead Generation

Smith & Company PLLC has built its entire $250,000 practice on referrals alone — with no paid advertising, no local SEO strategy, no Google Business Profile presence to speak of, and three separate websites that dilute rather than reinforce each other. Every case Eon has today came through a personal relationship, not a system — and that is the ceiling that is keeping this firm at Stage 3.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • The firm operates three separate websites — smithelaw.com (NYC & Hartford general), smithelawct.com (CT criminal and immigration), and elegaldefender.com (federal criminal) — each competing with the others for the same keywords and splitting the domain authority that should be concentrated in one place
  • PageSpeed scores could not be retrieved automatically — the site blocks external access. Scores must be run manually at pagespeed.web.dev before finalizing. Based on the site's structure and age, performance is likely below competitive benchmarks.
  • No visible CTA above the fold, no contact form confirmed accessible on the homepage, and no live chat — visitors who land on the site have no immediate, frictionless way to start a conversation
  • The site has a blog (at minimum one article confirmed: "Right to a Jury Trial"), but no consistent content publishing cadence is evident — search engines reward fresh, relevant content and this firm has none in production
Blind Spot: Having three websites is not a strength — it is a liability. Search engines consolidate trust signals around a single authoritative domain. By spreading content, backlinks, and reviews across smithelaw.com, smithelawct.com, and elegaldefender.com, the firm has made it structurally impossible for any one site to compete for top rankings. Consolidating to one domain would immediately increase the organic authority of every page on that site.
The Positive
  • The firm does have a live, indexed website with confirmed attorney bio content and practice area pages — the foundation is there, it just needs to be consolidated and optimized
  • Eon's background — former prosecutor, U.S. Justice Advisor — is the kind of credential that converts skeptical criminal defense and immigration prospects into retained clients when it is prominently featured
The Gap
  • Every visitor who lands on the site without a clear CTA or contact form above the fold is a lead that cost Eon nothing but gets lost — and at current traffic levels, every lost visitor is a case that goes to Norte or Ruane instead
  • Three competing websites mean the firm has no single authoritative digital home, and Google has no clear signal about which site to rank — consolidation alone could produce meaningful organic improvement with no additional spend
  • Without a conversion-optimized website, paid ads will produce lower returns — traffic without a landing page built to convert is money poured into a leaky bucket
A website that works as a 24/7 intake machine — capturing leads while Eon is in court, sleeping, or spending time away from the office — is what makes the 60-hour week optional rather than required.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Google Business Profile shows approximately 2 total reviews across all platforms — at this level, the firm cannot rank in the local 3-pack for any competitive immigration or criminal defense keyword in Hartford
  • A verified NAP inconsistency exists: Yelp lists the firm at 124 Jefferson Street, Hartford, CT 06106 — while the website and other directories show 100 Pearl Street, 14th Floor, Hartford, CT 06103. Inconsistent Name, Address, and Phone data is a direct ranking penalty in Google's local algorithm
  • The firm does not appear in any observed local 3-pack search for "immigration lawyer Hartford CT," "removal defense attorney Hartford CT," "federal criminal defense Hartford CT," or "criminal defense lawyer Hartford CT" — it is effectively invisible for the searches that produce the most high-intent inbound inquiries
  • No geo-targeting landing pages exist for surrounding cities — no East Hartford, West Hartford, New Britain, New Haven, or Bristol pages — leaving significant local search volume completely uncaptured
Blind Spot: The Hartford Immigration Court handles every removal, asylum, and deportation case in Connecticut — with over 41,500 pending cases as of late 2025. Families across the entire state are actively searching for removal defense attorneys right now. The firm's total digital footprint — two reviews and three competing websites — means it is capturing essentially none of that demand, which is flowing directly to Norte Immigration Law and Musani Law instead.
The Positive
  • The firm has existing listings on Avvo, Justia, Birdeye, and ReachAttorneys — the directory foundation is started, it simply needs to be corrected, expanded, and made consistent
The Gap
  • With only ~2 reviews, the firm needs a systematic review generation strategy to close the gap with Norte (228 reviews) and Musani (128 reviews) — even reaching 30–40 reviews would meaningfully improve 3-pack visibility for lower-competition keywords
  • Every month without consistent NAP data, every month without a review generation system, and every month without geo-targeted landing pages is another month the firm falls further behind competitors who are actively building those signals
Norte Immigration Law LLC — 228 verified reviews at 4.8 stars, active directory listings with consistent NAP, and a dedicated removal defense page targeting the exact case type Eon wants to build. Norte is capturing every Hartford-area family searching for deportation defense that Smith & Company is not.
Local SEO is the only marketing channel that keeps producing leads after the work is done — unlike paid ads, a top-three Google Maps ranking generates calls without spending money every month.

Google Ads

Immigration — Ad Presence
  • Smith & Company does not appear to be running Google Ads for immigration searches — consistent with what Eon confirmed on the call (the firm has never run paid advertising of any kind)
  • For searches like "immigration attorney Hartford CT" and "removal defense attorney Hartford CT," the paid ad slots are occupied by competitors while the firm has no presence above or within organic results
Immigration — Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Norte Immigration Law LLC — strong search presence for removal and family immigration terms; their directory rankings and review volume suggest an active paid and organic investment strategy capturing high-intent immigration searches
  • Musani Law — 128 Google reviews and a focused immigration-only practice with a multilingual team; their review velocity and web presence suggest active lead generation investment for the same case types Eon is targeting
Immigration — The Opportunity
  • High-intent keywords: "removal defense attorney Hartford," "immigration lawyer Hartford CT," "deportation defense Connecticut" — these searches represent people with an active legal problem who are ready to hire today
  • Specific case type targeting: "federal removal proceedings CT," "DACA attorney Hartford," "family petition lawyer Hartford" — these terms have lower competition and higher specificity than broad immigration searches
  • Non-obvious: Spanish-language campaigns targeting "abogado de inmigración Hartford" — the Hartford immigration court serves a heavily Spanish-speaking population and bilingual search volume is often underserved by English-only competitors
  • Non-obvious: Surrounding city targeting for East Hartford, New Britain, Waterbury, and New Haven — the firm's dual NY/CT licensing and Hartford immigration court access make it uniquely positioned to serve clients across the state, but it is currently capturing none of that geographic demand
Criminal Defense — Ad Presence
  • Smith & Company does not appear to be running Google Ads for criminal defense or federal criminal searches — consistent with the firm's referral-only history
  • For searches like "federal criminal defense Hartford CT" and "criminal defense lawyer Hartford CT," the paid results are occupied by well-funded competitors while this firm is absent
Criminal Defense — Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Ruane DUI & Criminal Defense Attorneys — statewide presence with geo-targeted pages for Hartford, East Hartford, West Hartford, and beyond; their 1,000+ review count and multiple locations indicate substantial marketing investment across paid and organic channels
  • Woolf & Ross Law Firm — 4.9-star rating, licensed in federal courts for CT, NY Southern and Eastern Districts, and actively advertising free consultations — directly targeting the federal criminal clients Eon wants to attract
Criminal Defense — The Opportunity
  • High-intent keywords: "federal criminal defense attorney Hartford," "federal drug crime lawyer CT," "federal weapons charge attorney Connecticut" — federal criminal searches have fewer advertisers than state criminal and tend to attract higher case values
  • Differentiation angle: "former federal prosecutor Hartford" — Eon's prosecutorial background is a legitimate conversion advantage that no competitor can replicate; ads leading with this credential would stand out in a field of generic "experienced criminal defense" copy
  • Non-obvious: Federal court proximity targeting — "Connecticut federal court criminal defense" and "District of Connecticut criminal attorney" — terms that capture defendants already in federal proceedings who need immediate representation
  • Non-obvious: Nighttime and weekend scheduling — criminal arrests and federal indictments do not happen on a 9-to-5 schedule; ads running 24/7 with a strong CTA capture the high-urgency moments competitors miss outside business hours
Blind Spot: Eon's former-prosecutor credential is his single most powerful conversion asset — and it is currently being used for nothing. A Google Ads campaign leading with "Former Federal Prosecutor Now Defending Clients" would immediately differentiate this firm from every generic criminal defense ad in Hartford and command significantly higher click-through rates and case quality.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • Smith & Company does not appear to be in the LSA program for immigration or criminal defense — the firm has not been Google Screened and is not appearing in the top-of-page LSA positions for observed Hartford searches
  • With approximately 2 total reviews, the firm currently lacks the review foundation needed for a competitive LSA profile — building to 15–25 Google reviews should happen in parallel with the LSA application process
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Ruane DUI & Criminal Defense Attorneys — with 1,000+ Google reviews, Ruane is almost certainly active in the LSA program for criminal defense and has the review volume to hold top LSA positions in Hartford
  • Norte Immigration Law LLC — 228 reviews and dedicated immigration focus positions them as the likely LSA leader for Hartford immigration searches; they are capturing pay-per-lead calls at the very top of Google before any organic result appears
The Opportunity
  • LSA is the only ad product where smaller firms can appear above larger competitors based on review quality and responsiveness rather than pure budget — this is a meaningful equalizer for a firm like Smith & Company that is starting from zero
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • Getting Google Screened for both immigration and criminal defense establishes credibility with prospects before they ever read a single word on the website — it is the digital equivalent of a trusted referral from Google itself
Blind Spot: LSA for immigration law is available and growing — but many immigration firms in secondary markets like Hartford have not yet activated it, which means early movers face less competition for the top slots. Getting in now, before the rest of the market catches up, is significantly cheaper per lead than entering a crowded LSA market later.
