Pillar Health Scorecard

Lead Generation

  • Zero paid advertising — Google Ads, LSA, and Meta have never been activated
  • Approximately 2 total reviews across all platforms; firm does not appear in the local 3-pack for any immigration or criminal defense search
  • Three separate websites fragment the firm's digital authority and make consistent SEO impossible
Without a lead generation system, every case Eon takes is one he had to personally find — and there is no path to 60-hour relief without one.

Intake

  • Eon personally handles all intake calls on top of all casework — there is no separation
  • No defined intake process, no delegation to Kim or part-time staff, no after-hours coverage
  • As lead volume grows, a broken intake system means paying for leads that do not convert
A structured intake system is what allows Kim to handle the front end so Eon can focus on the casework that actually generates revenue.

Self-Managing Team

  • Kim and part-time staff exist but have no defined roles or accountability structure
  • Eon is the sole decision-maker and point of contact for all cases — stepping away for a day creates operational risk
  • A full-time hire by EOY 2026 is the stated goal but no hiring blueprint exists yet
The team Eon has is the raw material — what it lacks is structure, which is what separates a support staff from a self-managing operation.

Profit

  • Annual revenue was not confirmed on the call — Eon was asked to confirm with Kim before the follow-up
  • No financial tracking by case type means the firm does not know which practice area is more profitable
  • Revenue goals (+$350k in 2026, 2x by 2027) are set without a financial model to work backward from
Knowing which cases produce the most profit — not just revenue — is what tells Eon where to focus the marketing dollars and which cases to prioritize.
