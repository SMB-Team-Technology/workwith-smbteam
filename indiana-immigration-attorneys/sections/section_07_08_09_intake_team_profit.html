Intake Assessment

With a two-person team and no documented intake process, Indiana Immigration Attorneys is almost certainly leaving cases on the table every week — not from a lack of leads, but from a lack of systems to convert them. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • No documented intake process — with only 2 team members, Libby is almost certainly the primary point of contact for all initial consultations and new client inquiries
  • No after-hours intake coverage confirmed — calls and web inquiries received in the evening or on weekends go unanswered until the next business day
  • No intake tracking or follow-up system identified — leads who do not sign immediately may never be contacted again
  • No intake specialist or dedicated staff to triage and qualify leads before they reach the attorney — every new inquiry consumes attorney time regardless of fit or urgency
Blind Spot: The families who need immigration help most urgently — an asylum seeker with a court date, a spouse waiting on a green card decision, a DACA recipient facing a renewal deadline — often cannot wait until the next business day. Without after-hours response capability, those families call the next option on their list and never come back.
The Gap
  • Every intake inquiry that goes unanswered for more than a few hours converts at a fraction of the rate of a same-day response — at $3,500 average case value, even 2–3 missed cases per month represents $7,000–$10,500 in monthly revenue that disappears silently
  • Without intake tracking, IIA has no visibility into how many potential clients contacted the office and never signed — that number is almost certainly larger than it appears
  • As lead volume increases with paid advertising, an unstructured intake process becomes a bottleneck that limits the ROI of every marketing dollar spent
A structured intake system means Libby spends her time practicing immigration law — not fielding initial calls — while the firm serves more families than she can reach on her own today.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact form location not confirmed — automated research was blocked by Cloudflare; verify manually whether a form is visible above the fold or buried on a contact page
  • No live chat solution observed on the website — visitors who arrive outside business hours have no immediate way to express interest or get a response commitment
  • No automated form confirmation or response sequence confirmed — a prospect who submits a form after hours may receive no communication until the next business day, well past the window when they are most likely to still be available

Team Assessment

Indiana Immigration Attorneys is a 2-person operation where the attorney is the firm — handling casework, consultations, and almost certainly intake and day-to-day operations. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Team consists of Libby Nelson (attorney/founder) and Karla Chavez (interpreter/translator) — no operational, admin, or intake coverage beyond these two roles
  • All casework, client communication, and almost certainly intake flow through Libby — the firm stops when she stops
  • No defined roles, accountability structure, or documented procedures that would allow staff to operate without constant attorney direction
  • No capacity to hire additional staff until lead flow and revenue grow sufficiently — creating a growth ceiling where the firm cannot scale without Libby working more hours first
Blind Spot: The fact that Libby's work is meaningful and community-driven can mask the cumulative toll of being the sole operational driver of the firm. Purpose-driven solo practitioners often push past the signals of burnout because the work matters — but a firm that requires the owner's constant presence is not yet a firm; it is a job with overhead.
The Positive
  • Karla Chavez's bilingual Spanish/English capability is a genuine competitive advantage — Spanish-speaking clients receive immediate service without a third-party interpreter, which builds trust and closes cases faster
  • Two conference rooms with video conferencing infrastructure enable the firm to serve clients statewide virtually — a scalable delivery model that does not require more physical space to grow
The Gap
  • Without an intake team, lead volume cannot grow without Libby spending more time on calls she is not paid for — marketing investment without intake delegation produces diminishing returns
  • Without operational staff, adding a second attorney or paralegal is difficult — there is no infrastructure to onboard or support them
  • Libby cannot take extended time away from the firm without active cases stalling — the personal cost of that dependency grows with every new client added
Until the team has the structure to handle intake, operations, and client communication without Libby, the firm's ability to serve north Indiana families is permanently capped by her personal capacity.

Profit Plan Assessment

With no revenue figures available and no financial planning systems visible from research, Indiana Immigration Attorneys is in the earliest stage of building financial clarity. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Annual revenue not stated and could not be confirmed — financial visibility into the firm's actual performance is unknown
  • No evidence of cost-per-case tracking or profitability by matter type — DACA renewals and citizenship matters may generate high volume but lower margins than complex asylum or family petition cases
  • As a 13-month-old firm, formal profit planning, cash flow management, and financial forecasting are likely not yet in place
  • Without financial visibility, every growth decision — hiring, advertising, adding services — carries higher risk and has no clear financial benchmark for success
Blind Spot: DACA renewals and basic citizenship filings are often the highest-volume intake calls for immigration firms, but they also carry the lowest fees. Without tracking profit by case type, Libby could be investing the most time and overhead in the work that returns the least — and not yet know it.
The Gap
  • Without profit planning, revenue growth may not translate to personal income growth — more cases can mean more overhead, more staff costs, and the same take-home pay
  • No financial visibility means no clear answer to the most important growth question: "Can I afford to hire someone?" — and the inability to hire is what keeps the firm stuck at Stage 3
  • A formal profit plan creates the foundation for every growth decision: what to charge, which cases to prioritize, when to hire, and how much to invest in marketing
Serving the Logansport community for a generation requires a financially sustainable firm — not just a busy one — and that starts with knowing exactly what the firm earns, what it keeps, and what it needs to grow.
