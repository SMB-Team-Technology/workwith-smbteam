Intake Assessment

Just Jurist Law Firm has no defined intake process for new association inquiries — inbound interest is handled reactively, with no tracking, no delegation, and no protocol for converting interest into signed clients. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • No defined intake process for new association inquiries — each inbound contact is handled ad hoc with no protocol for who responds, in what timeframe, and what happens next
  • Pablo personally handles all prospective client conversations — a three-person team with a second attorney and a paralegal has no one designated to qualify and route new association inquiries
  • No after-hours coverage — board members and property managers who search for HOA counsel in the evenings, the same hours Pablo is at board meetings, reach voicemail or a footer contact form with no immediate response
  • No tracking or follow-up system — prospective associations that contact the firm but do not immediately sign have no structured follow-up sequence; opportunities are lost through inaction rather than disqualification
Blind Spot: Most HOA board meetings happen in the evenings — which is exactly when Pablo is unavailable and when prospective association inquiries go unanswered. A board member who searches for an HOA attorney at 9pm, gets no response until the next morning, has already called two other firms before Pablo's office opens.
The Gap
  • Every inbound inquiry that is not acknowledged within the first hour converts at a fraction of the rate of one that receives a fast response — as lead generation scales through paid advertising, a broken intake process becomes a direct revenue leak, not a manageable gap
  • Without a defined qualification and handoff process, Pablo is personally invested in every new inquiry — time that belongs to client work, strategic decisions, or personal freedom is consumed by conversations that a trained associate could handle
  • As Just Jurist grows its digital presence and inbound volume increases, an ad hoc intake approach means more leads entering a broken funnel — more associations lost, not more clients signed
A defined intake process that converts inbound association inquiries without Pablo's personal involvement is the first system that turns marketing spend into firm revenue while he is not in the room — and it is the foundation that makes every other growth investment work.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • "Book a Consult" above-the-fold CTA links to Clio's scheduling tool — this is the firm's strongest intake conversion element and a genuine strength in an otherwise ad hoc intake structure
  • Contact form is in the footer only — visitors who are not ready to book a consultation must scroll to the bottom of the page to find the secondary contact path; a significant share of interested visitors never make it there
  • No after-hours or weekend contact coverage on the website — the scheduling tool is the only path, and if Pablo's calendar is not open during the hours a prospect searches, there is no fallback engagement mechanism

Team Assessment

Just Jurist has a three-person team — Pablo, an experienced second attorney, and a paralegal — but Pablo is still the operational center of every client relationship, every board meeting, and every decision the team cannot handle without his direct involvement. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Pablo personally attends HOA and condo association board meetings most evenings — a task that could be systematically delegated to the second attorney once defined protocols and client communication standards are in place
  • The team lacks documented protocols, decision frameworks, or escalation rules — everything runs on Pablo's institutional knowledge rather than systems that others can follow independently
  • No accountability structure exists — without defined roles and ownership, the team defaults to Pablo for decisions that should be handled without him
  • Pablo's concern that a hired associate will leave and start a competing firm is preventing the team expansion that would directly enable the delegation he needs — a solvable problem with the right hiring model and onboarding structure
Blind Spot: Pablo's willingness to personally absorb the workload — including evening board meetings — has removed the urgency to build the systems that would replace it. His capacity to manage it all is precisely what has allowed the firm to run without autonomy. The day Pablo is unavailable for any reason, the firm stalls — because there is no system that runs in his absence.
The Positive
  • Second attorney Jessica Anne Elliott has 25+ years of experience — the capability for senior-level delegation already exists within the team; the barrier is defined protocols and autonomy, not attorney quality
  • The existing team structure (attorney + paralegal) is the operational foundation for a self-managing department once SOPs are documented and role ownership is defined
The Gap
  • Pablo's calendar is filled every evening with board meeting obligations — time that can never be reclaimed until a structured delegation protocol systematically transfers those meetings to an associate attorney with defined escalation rules
  • Without documented SOPs, every new client matter requires Pablo's personal judgment — the firm's capacity is capped at whatever Pablo can personally handle, and that cap is already filled
  • The goal Pablo stated — stepping out of the nucleus — requires a team that is trained, empowered, and accountable without his daily presence; building that team is not a passive outcome of growth, it is an intentional systems-building project that requires a framework
The only path to Pablo attending fewer board meetings — and eventually none — is a team operating on defined systems with clear accountability, not on his personal availability every evening. That is exactly what we help build.

Profit Plan Assessment

Just Jurist Law Firm is profitable — Pablo confirmed that on the call — and the retainer model of 40 association clients creates a stable recurring revenue base that most growing law firms never achieve. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No stated revenue figure shared on the call — Pablo did not provide financial details, making growth planning, investment sizing, and hiring ROI analysis impossible to run without that baseline
  • No financial model for the hiring decision Pablo needs to make — his hesitation to bring on an experienced associate is rooted in cost uncertainty, not in a calculated analysis that shows whether the hire pays for itself
  • No defined profit target — Pablo expressed willingness to "invest at the right price point" but without a revenue-to-profit model, there is no benchmark to evaluate any investment against
  • No visibility into cost per association, margin per matter type, or the financial impact of adding one attorney — decisions about the firm's biggest costs are being made on instinct rather than data
Blind Spot: Pablo's concern about ad spend — framing $8,000 per month as excessive — may be based on an emotional reaction rather than a financial model. A revenue-per-dollar-invested analysis of a targeted $2,500–$5,000/month HOA campaign would replace that concern with math: one new association retainer at $750–$1,500/month pays back the ad investment in weeks, not years.
The Positive
  • Forty association retainer clients create a stable, predictable monthly revenue base — a financially sound foundation that most growing firms lack and that makes investment in growth significantly lower risk
  • Pablo confirmed having the capital to invest — the constraint is ROI clarity and confidence, not cash availability
The Gap
  • Without a hiring model, Pablo is making a $120,000–$150,000 salary decision on instinct rather than math — and that instinct is saying "wait" when the revenue data from 40 retainer clients would very likely say "hire now"
  • Without a profit plan, growth in client volume could mean more work without proportionally more net income — a scenario Pablo explicitly described wanting to escape, and one that accelerates without financial guardrails
  • The next step toward autonomy is blocked by a financial question Pablo cannot currently answer — how much does a senior associate cost relative to what they generate — and that question is solvable with the right analysis
Profit clarity is not an accounting exercise — it is the evidence Pablo needs to make the hiring decision that gives him his evenings back. The math almost certainly supports the hire; the question is whether the model exists to show it.
