Intake Assessment

Just Jurist Law Firm has no defined intake protocol — new association inquiries are handled reactively by Pablo personally, with no standardized process, no dedicated intake role, and no after-hours coverage for boards that reach out outside business hours. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No defined intake protocol — new association inquiries are handled reactively by Pablo personally, without a standardized qualification process or documented handoff to the team
  • Website contact form is buried in the footer — there is no above-fold form or scheduling widget, and no way for an interested board to book a consultation without scrolling to the very bottom of the page
  • No after-hours intake coverage — board members who search for legal counsel after work hours (when most board decisions happen) find no one available and no automated response
  • No intake tracking or follow-up system — leads who do not convert on first contact are unlikely to receive a structured follow-up sequence; there is no visibility into how many potential association clients reach out and are never re-engaged
Blind Spot: Pablo's personal firm receives 3 inbound calls per day and has a waitlist — there is documented, real demand for his legal expertise. Just Jurist is capturing none of that overflow demand because there is no system to route, qualify, or respond to inbound interest on the Just Jurist side. Every one of those calls that goes elsewhere is a potential long-term association retainer walking out the door.
The Gap
  • HOA boards considering switching from Association Law Group (2.9 stars, large impersonal firm) need a response within the hour to convert — without a defined intake process, Just Jurist is not positioned to capture these high-value switcher leads when they are most motivated to act
  • Every new association client that fails to receive a same-day response is a lost recurring retainer worth $9,000+ in first-year billings alone — the intake gap compounds every month marketing spend increases
  • Without intake metrics, Pablo has no visibility into how many potential association clients are reaching the website, failing to find a contact option, and calling a competitor instead
The fastest path to stepping back is not working more — it is having a system that responds, qualifies, and books consultations without Pablo touching it.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • The only contact form on the Just Jurist website is located in the footer — a visitor must scroll through the entire page before encountering any way to reach the firm
  • No scheduling tool is present — there is no way for a prospective association client to book a consultation directly without emailing or calling during business hours
  • No live chat or automated response mechanism — after-hours inquiries receive no acknowledgment, no automatic response, and no path forward until Pablo or a team member manually follows up

These findings are also referenced in the Website Audit section.

Team Assessment

The Just Jurist team of two attorneys and a paralegal is functional but entirely dependent on Pablo — he personally leads every board meeting, manages every client relationship, and makes every consequential decision. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
"Current attorney and paralegal are not 'A players' and require hand-holding; lack of self-managed culture."
The Problem
  • Pablo personally attends HOA and condo board meetings most evenings — this is time-consuming, owner-dependent work that the current junior attorney is not equipped to take over
  • The current attorney and paralegal require active direction and do not operate self-sufficiently — there is no self-managing culture, no accountability framework, and no leadership layer below Pablo
  • No organizational structure or defined roles with KPIs — the team functions as support staff for Pablo rather than as an independent operational unit
  • Key-man risk is severe: if Pablo stepped back, client relationships built on his personal credibility and presence would be at risk — the firm has no systems to transfer those relationships to the team
Blind Spot: The fact that Pablo is willing to keep working evenings — and that the firm remains profitable — actually enables the problem to persist. There is no forcing function to delegate, so the team's dependency on Pablo deepens each month. The longer this continues, the harder it becomes to change, because clients increasingly associate Just Jurist with Pablo personally rather than with the firm as an institution.
The Positive
  • Pablo has a precise, articulate vision for what a self-managing firm looks like — he described it specifically as "operations autonomous via programming/systems," which means the goal is clear even if the path is not yet built
  • The firm's stable base of 40 associations provides a reliable revenue floor to fund team development, training, and the eventual hire of a senior attorney capable of running client relationships autonomously
The Gap
  • Pablo cannot take a vacation, reduce his evening hours, or expand to new associations without personally absorbing the additional workload — growth currently means more of Pablo, not more of the team
  • The inability to hire and retain "A player" talent (fear of training and losing an associate to competition) means the team dependency problem compounds over time without outside help structuring the process
  • Without a senior attorney who can run client relationships and board meetings autonomously, no amount of revenue growth will free Pablo from the day-to-day operations of the firm
Pablo's goal is a firm that runs without him — and building the team structure that makes that possible is not optional; it is the single most direct path to the freedom he described on the call.

Profit Plan Assessment

Just Jurist is profitable — Pablo confirmed this — but the financial picture lacks the detail needed to make confident growth decisions; revenue could not be stated on the call and key metrics like cost per new client, hiring ROI, and profit margin by service type are not being tracked. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Revenue figure is unknown at the firm level — Pablo could not provide a ballpark number on the call, indicating that financial tracking is not a regular management practice
  • No profit planning framework — decisions about ad spend, hiring, and expansion are evaluated through a lens of concern and risk rather than through a clear cost-per-client or ROI model
  • No defined budget for growth: Pablo cited a peer spending "$8,000 per month on ads" as concerning — without a reference ROI model, that number has no context, and every marketing investment remains emotionally evaluated rather than analytically approved
  • Two-firm structure (Just Jurist + personal firm) likely obscures true revenue, cost allocation, and profit margins — without consolidated financial visibility, it is impossible to make accurate growth decisions for either entity
Blind Spot: The HOA/condo general counsel retainer model is one of the most financially predictable revenue models in law — 40 associations each paying a monthly retainer represents substantial contracted recurring revenue. Pablo may significantly underestimate his own financial leverage and the capacity he already has to fund strategic hires and marketing, because he has never modeled it in a profit plan.
The Positive
  • The firm is profitable and cash-flow positive — this is a meaningful foundation and confirms the business model works; the goal is not to fix what is broken but to build the visibility needed to grow it strategically
  • The retainer-based model with 40 associations provides a degree of revenue predictability that most law firms do not have — monthly retainer income is far more stable than contingency or flat-fee case flow
The Gap
  • Without knowing the cost per new association client, Pablo cannot evaluate whether $2,500/month in Google Ads is a good or bad investment — every marketing decision is made emotionally rather than analytically, and that leads to underspending on high-ROI channels
  • The concern about "affording Shaq-level talent" is fundamentally a financial planning problem, not a revenue problem — the retainer model likely already supports a senior hire if the math is modeled clearly; without that model, the hire never happens and Pablo stays in the nucleus
  • Without a profit plan, revenue growth from new marketing will be consumed by reactive spending rather than channeled into the team development that creates Pablo's long-term freedom
Pablo already has the revenue foundation to fund his own freedom — what he lacks is the financial clarity to see it clearly and act on it confidently.
