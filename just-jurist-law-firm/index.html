Here Is Where Just Jurist Law Firm Stands Today

Just Jurist Law Firm, PLLC is a Miami-based HOA and condo association law firm representing approximately 40 condominium associations — a client base built through referrals and inherited books, not marketing. Founder Pablo Gonzalez Zepeda leads a three-person team in a profitable retainer-based practice that is functionally invisible online: the firm does not appear in any Google local pack for its own practice area, has no verified Google reviews, and runs zero paid advertising while well-funded competitors actively capture every inbound inquiry from Miami association boards searching for legal counsel.

Stage 6 is the stage where Just Jurist runs without Pablo having to be in every board meeting, on every call, and at every decision his team cannot handle — it is the destination he described on this call, and it is exactly where we are taking him.

Where You Are Today

Stage 4: Small Business Manager
  • 40 association retainer clients built through inherited books and referrals — no digital system to attract new ones
  • Pablo personally attends HOA/condo board meetings most evenings, leaving no room for delegation or personal freedom
  • Three-person team lacks defined autonomy — every decision and client matter escalates back to Pablo
  • Zero digital presence: no GBP, no reviews, no paid ads, not appearing in any local search result for their own practice area

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The path from Pablo attending every board meeting himself to having a firm that runs without him runs directly through predictable lead generation, a properly structured team, and the systems that make both work — and none of those things are in place yet.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

The Problem
  • No defined intake process for new association inquiries — each inbound contact is handled ad hoc with no protocol for who responds, in what timeframe, and what happens next

The Gap
  • Every inbound inquiry that is not acknowledged within the first hour converts at a fraction of the rate of one that receives a fast response — as lead generation scales through paid advertising, a broken intake process becomes a direct revenue leak, not a manageable gap
  • Without a defined qualification and handoff process, Pablo is personally invested in every new inquiry — time that belongs to client work, strategic decisions, or personal freedom is consumed by conversations that a trained associate could handle
  • As Just Jurist grows its digital presence and inbound volume increases, an ad hoc intake approach means more leads entering a broken funnel — more associations lost, not more clients signed

Profit Plan Assessment

Just Jurist Law Firm is profitable — Pablo confirmed that on the call — and the retainer model of 40 association clients creates a stable recurring revenue base that most growing law firms never achieve. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No stated revenue figure shared on the call — Pablo did not provide financial details, making growth planning, investment sizing, and hiring ROI analysis impossible to run without that baseline
  • No financial model for the hiring decision Pablo needs to make — his hesitation to bring on an experienced associate is rooted in cost uncertainty, not in a calculated analysis that shows whether the hire pays for itself
  • No defined profit target — Pablo expressed willingness to "invest at the right price point" but without a revenue-to-profit model, there is no benchmark to evaluate any investment against
  • No visibility into cost per association, margin per matter type, or the financial impact of adding one attorney — decisions about the firm's biggest costs are being made on instinct rather than data
Blind Spot: Pablo's concern about ad spend — framing $8,000 per month as excessive — may be based on an emotional reaction rather than a financial model. A revenue-per-dollar-invested analysis of a targeted $2,500–$5,000/month HOA campaign would replace that concern with math: one new association retainer at $750–$1,500/month pays back the ad investment in weeks, not years.
The Positive
  • Forty association retainer clients create a stable, predictable monthly revenue base — a financially sound foundation that most growing firms lack and that makes investment in growth significantly lower risk
  • Pablo confirmed having the capital to invest — the constraint is ROI clarity and confidence, not cash availability
The Gap
  • Without a hiring model, Pablo is making a $120,000–$150,000 salary decision on instinct rather than math — and that instinct is saying "wait" when the revenue data from 40 retainer clients would very likely say "hire now"
  • Without a profit plan, growth in client volume could mean more work without proportionally more net income — a scenario Pablo explicitly described wanting to escape, and one that accelerates without financial guardrails
  • The next step toward autonomy is blocked by a financial question Pablo cannot currently answer — how much does a senior associate cost relative to what they generate — and that question is solvable with the right analysis
Profit clarity is not an accounting exercise — it is the evidence Pablo needs to make the hiring decision that gives him his evenings back. The math almost certainly supports the hire; the question is whether the model exists to show it.
