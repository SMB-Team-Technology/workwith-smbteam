Lead Generation

Florida Law Advisors has built a professional multi-attorney presence across three Florida offices — but the firm does not appear to be running active paid advertising on any channel, and it is absent from the local 3-pack in every one of its primary practice areas. In a competitive Tampa market where established competitors are spending aggressively on Google Ads, LSAs, and Facebook, this firm's entire growth depends on clients finding them organically — a fragile foundation for a firm with five attorneys and three locations to support.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed scores could not be retrieved during this audit due to API limitations — website loading performance requires manual verification before any paid campaign launches, as slow load times directly reduce ad conversion rates
  • The primary consultation form requires six fields (name, phone, email, practice area, county, and description) — this is high friction compared to competitors using simple name + phone CTAs that maximize lead capture
  • No live chat feature observed — prospects who visit the site outside business hours or want immediate engagement have no real-time option to connect
  • NAP inconsistency identified: the firm appears as "Florida Law Advisers" on its own website but "Florida Law Advisors" on the BBB and other directory platforms — Google treats these as signals pointing to different entities, suppressing local rankings across all three office locations
Blind Spot: The firm has geo-targeted landing pages for 20+ Florida cities and three GBP listings — but those pages and listings are likely underperforming because the name inconsistency ("Advisers" vs. "Advisors") reduces the consolidated authority that Google uses to determine 3-pack eligibility. Fixing this one discrepancy can lift rankings across all three locations simultaneously.
The Positive
  • Website underwent a full redesign in 2024 with mobile-responsive layouts, separate mobile and desktop image assets, and clear CTAs above the fold — a solid technical foundation that will support paid traffic conversion
  • Five attorney bios with Super Lawyers, Best Lawyers, Martindale-Hubbell, and ABA credentials prominently displayed, along with press mentions in the Miami Herald and Bradenton Herald — strong trust signals that competing firms cannot easily replicate
The Gap
  • Without verified PageSpeed scores, loading performance may be silently reducing conversions — every additional second of load time reduces conversion rates by 7%, and paid traffic on a slow page burns budget without producing results
  • A six-field intake form is creating abandonment that simpler competitor CTAs avoid — reducing the number of leads captured from every visitor the site receives
  • NAP inconsistency is actively suppressing local SEO authority across Tampa, Orlando, and Dade City — a fixable problem that is quietly costing the firm 3-pack eligibility in every market it serves
A conversion-optimized website is the foundation every paid campaign depends on — without it, every dollar spent on ads works harder than it should to produce results.

Blind Spot: Because this firm has no existing Google Ads quality score history, initial CPCs will run higher than established competitors — but this also means a properly structured campaign will have less history-based competition to overcome, and every week of running builds quality score that permanently lowers future costs. Starting now is always cheaper than starting later in a market where competitors are actively accumulating quality score advantage.
