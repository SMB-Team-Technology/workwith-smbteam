Intake Assessment

Etehad Law has one intake person handling all inbound leads with no scripts, no defined process, and no measurement of results — meaning there is no way to know how many qualified prospects are calling and not becoming clients. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • No intake scripts or structured training — the intake person operates on instinct with no consistent process, no qualifying framework, and no defined pitch
  • Conversion metrics are completely unknown — the firm has no idea what percentage of inbound leads become signed clients, what the cost per signed case is, or where prospects drop off
  • After-hours coverage only just launched with RapidSign — calls before this change were going unanswered, and the RapidSign process has not yet been evaluated for effectiveness or integration with the firm's intake system
  • No follow-up cadence for leads who do not sign on the first call — in PI, prospects often contact multiple firms before deciding; without systematic follow-up, cases are being won by competitors
Blind Spot: At $4 million in annual revenue, even a 5-percentage-point improvement in close rate — from 15% to 20% — would add hundreds of thousands of dollars per year without any increase in marketing spend. The intake problem is almost certainly the single highest-ROI fix available right now, and it requires zero additional ad budget.
The Positive
  • The firm recognized the after-hours gap and took action by hiring RapidSign — that awareness of the problem is a good starting point for building a stronger intake system
The Gap
  • With no close rate data, the firm cannot calculate how much revenue is being lost to poor intake — and cannot know whether any improvement is working
  • A firm that is about to invest in paid advertising with a broken intake process will spend marketing dollars to generate leads that the intake system then fails to convert — burning budget without building revenue
  • Every week without a trained, scripted intake process is a week where qualified prospects call Etehad Law and end up signing with Setareh Law or Omega Law Group instead
A functioning intake system is what turns marketing investment into actual revenue — and it is what allows Simon to eventually step back knowing that leads are being handled professionally without his involvement.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • No confirmed above-fold contact form on the homepage — website visitors who want to reach out must navigate to a separate contact page, creating friction that reduces conversion rates
  • No confirmed live chat or chat widget on the website — a significant gap given that many PI prospects prefer to start with a quick question rather than a phone call
  • After-hours website leads are now partially handled by RapidSign, but the integration between website form submissions and the live response service needs to be confirmed and optimized

Team Assessment

Simon Etehad is the Chief Everything Officer of a 15-person firm — personally approving every check, managing operations, handling client relationships, and running the legal work — with no management layer between him and the daily chaos. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
"Simon is the Chief Everything Officer, working 8+ hours daily. He must personally approve all checks daily to prevent fraud."
The Problem
  • Simon must personally approve every check — this single bottleneck means the firm cannot function for even one day without his direct involvement, regardless of how much it grows
  • No KPIs or performance scorecards — the 15-person team operates without any measurable accountability standard, making it impossible to identify poor performers or reward strong ones
  • Hiring system is broken — 19 of 20 paralegals hired were described as poor fits, resulting in high turnover and the owner being forced to train staff from scratch repeatedly
  • No management layer between Simon and the team — there is no operations manager, no lead attorney who runs the firm in Simon's absence, and no defined chain of command
Blind Spot: The fact that Simon has successfully run the firm through all of this — 30 years, $4 million in revenue — is actually the problem. His ability to hold it all together personally has masked the absence of systems. The firm runs because Simon shows up every day. The moment he does not, it stops. That is not a firm — that is a job.
The Positive
  • The firm has 15 staff including 3 attorneys, case managers, and an intake person — there is enough organizational depth to build a self-managing structure; it just needs roles, accountability, and leadership to activate it
The Gap
  • Simon cannot take a vacation, cannot get sick, and cannot step back — every hour of his time is consumed by operations that a properly structured team and leadership layer should be handling
  • Without KPIs and scorecards, the firm cannot grow to $8 million — at that scale, the owner must be able to manage by metrics, not by being physically present for every decision
  • Every bad hire that is not caught quickly costs the firm in wasted salary, training time, and Simon's personal bandwidth — a hiring system and trial protocol would have prevented most of the 19 failed paralegals
The self-managing firm Simon described — one that operates without him as the daily bottleneck — requires a management structure that does not yet exist, and building it is the only path to the personal freedom he is working toward.

Profit Plan Assessment

Etehad Law generates approximately $4 million in annual revenue but has no monthly financial reporting system, no cost-per-case tracking, and no mechanism to distinguish which practice areas or case types are actually driving profit. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No monthly review of key financial metrics — revenue, profit, cash flow, and cost per case are not tracked or reviewed on a regular basis
  • Decision-making is reactive rather than data-driven — without financial dashboards, the firm responds to problems rather than preventing them
  • No cost-per-case analysis by practice area — the firm does not know whether personal injury, employment law, or business litigation cases are the most profitable, making strategic resource allocation impossible
  • The firm is about to commit $10,000/month to an SEO contract without knowing the current cost per case or the expected return — a significant financial decision made without financial data
Blind Spot: At $4 million in revenue, even a modest improvement in financial visibility — knowing which cases cost the most to acquire and generate the most profit — could meaningfully change how marketing budget is allocated. Without this data, the firm may be investing heavily in the least profitable work while underinvesting in the most profitable.
The Positive
  • With $4 million in annual revenue, Etehad Law has a real financial base to work from — the revenue exists; the visibility into where it goes does not yet, making this a solvable problem
The Gap
  • A firm heading toward $8 million in revenue cannot be managed without financial systems — at that scale, decisions made without cost-per-case data and profit visibility will cost far more than the systems would have
  • Without knowing current profitability, the firm cannot answer the most important question in growth planning: what is one new client actually worth, and how much should we be willing to spend to acquire one?
  • Financial opacity also creates risk — without regular reporting, overspending, cash flow problems, and underperforming staff can go undetected for months before becoming crises
Financial clarity is what allows Simon to eventually step out of daily operations with confidence — knowing the firm is profitable, sustainable, and growing without requiring his personal supervision of every dollar.
