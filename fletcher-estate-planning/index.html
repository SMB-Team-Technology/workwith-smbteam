The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Fletcher Estate Planning has built one of the most effective consultation-to-close processes in the market — a 79% conversion rate that reflects Jim's expertise, warmth, and deep trust with prospects. But with no formal intake infrastructure and no after-hours coverage, the firm is almost certainly losing leads that never reach Jim at all. Status: AMBER
"I'm considering charging a fee — maybe $99 — to get people to commit so they don't no-show."

The Positive

Profit Plan Assessment

Jim has clear visibility into his revenue — $420,000 projected this year, $840,000 as the goal — and a disciplined approach to pricing his services at $2,000–$3,000 per client. What is less clear is whether the path to that goal includes a tracked profit strategy and a cost structure built to handle paid advertising. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No financial dashboard or profit tracking system was mentioned on the call — revenue is known but margin by service type and take-home as a percentage of revenue are not tracked at a granular level
  • Adding paid advertising introduces a new cost category (management fees + ad spend) that must be managed against margin targets — without a profit plan in place, scaling ad investment can compress take-home rather than grow it
  • Drafting costs at scale ($300–$400 per plan) represent a significant variable expense that grows with volume — at 100+ plans per year, this exceeds $35,000 annually before overhead, and without tracking it, profitability can erode invisibly
  • No mention of a cost-per-acquisition target or margin floor — without these benchmarks, it is impossible to evaluate whether a given marketing channel is actually profitable at the case level
Blind Spot: At Jim's current revenue of $420,000 and outsourced drafting costs of $300–$400 per plan, a significant portion of gross revenue flows out before a single overhead expense is paid. If plan volume doubles to hit $840,000, so does that cost — potentially adding $35,000–$40,000 in drafting expenses alone. Without a profit plan that models this, doubling revenue does not guarantee doubling take-home.
The Positive
  • Revenue metrics are clearly tracked — $144,000 YTD at the time of the call and a clear annual projection of $420,000 shows Jim monitors his top-line performance closely
  • Single-service focus on estate planning creates a clean, predictable revenue model — one practice area, one primary package price, one client type — which is the simplest foundation for building a profit plan
The Gap
  • Doubling revenue to $840,000 will not automatically double take-home — without a profit plan that accounts for rising variable costs (drafting, advertising, additional team), net income growth may significantly lag revenue growth
  • Marketing ROI cannot be optimized without cost-per-lead and cost-per-acquisition data — knowing that an ad campaign brought in 10 clients means little without knowing what those 10 clients cost to acquire versus what they returned
  • Without financial visibility at the case level, it is impossible to know which service offerings, client sources, or geographic markets are the most profitable — and therefore which to scale
Jim's goal is not just $840,000 in revenue — it is $840,000 that translates to real financial freedom, and that requires a clear view of what actually stays in the firm after every cost is paid.
