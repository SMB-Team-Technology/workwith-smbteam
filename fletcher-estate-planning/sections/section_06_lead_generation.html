Lead Generation

Fletcher Estate Planning has generated approximately $420,000 in annual revenue entirely through referrals and organic directory listings — a testament to Jim's reputation, but a fragile foundation for a firm with a $840,000 growth goal. There is no paid advertising, no predictable pipeline, and no system to replace or amplify word-of-mouth when referral volume falls short.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Website returned access errors during automated research — direct design observation was not possible; all findings are sourced from search metadata, directory listings, and crawl data
  • PageSpeed scores: Not obtained during research — must be verified manually at pagespeed.web.dev before final presentation
  • Two active domains (fletcherestateplanning.com and jimfletcherlaw.com) split Google's authority signals between two separate sites, reducing the ranking potential of both
  • No geo-targeting landing pages observed for surrounding Atlanta submarkets (Buckhead, Alpharetta, Roswell, Marietta) — limiting organic reach beyond Sandy Springs despite Jim serving those areas by Zoom and in-home visits
Blind Spot: Running two separate domains means Google may not know which one to rank — and may split link authority between them. Consolidating to a single domain could meaningfully improve the firm's competitive position for estate planning searches without spending a dollar on advertising.
The Positive
  • Website offers free online webinars — a trust-building lead magnet that educates prospects and creates a low-friction first step before a consultation call
  • Active blog with estate planning content observed (trust planning topics) — provides the foundation for content-driven SEO that compounds over time
The Gap
  • Without a high-converting website built to receive paid traffic, any Google Ads investment risks a lower return — every ad dollar sends prospects to a site that may not be optimized to convert them into consultation requests
  • No visible geographic landing pages means the firm is not competing for searches from Buckhead, Marietta, Roswell, or Alpharetta — suburban Atlanta markets well within Jim's service area that represent meaningful additional revenue opportunity
  • Missing an attorney video means Jim's warmth and credibility — his primary competitive advantage over larger firms — never reaches prospects until they are already on the phone; competitors who use video build trust before the first call
A high-converting website is not just a marketing asset — it is the foundation that turns every ad dollar into a consultation booked, and every consultation into progress toward $840k.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Firm ranks #2 in the local map pack for estate planning searches in Sandy Springs — one position away from the #1 spot that captures a disproportionately higher share of clicks and calls
  • Google Business Profile hours are not set to 24/7 — the firm is filtered out of after-hours search results every evening and weekend, exactly when many prospects research estate planning from home
  • Google review count is estimated below 30 based on practice size — significantly behind Perigon Legal Services (~58 reviews) and Morgan & DiSalvo (125+ reviews), creating a social proof gap that affects both rankings and prospect confidence
Blind Spot: Google filters businesses marked as "closed" from local search results during non-business hours. Every night and weekend, estate planning prospects searching from home see competitors but not Fletcher Estate Planning — even though Jim is available for inquiries. Updating GBP hours to 24/7 is a zero-cost fix that makes the firm immediately visible to after-hours searchers.
The Positive
  • Confirmed Google Business Profile active and ranking #2 in the local map pack — a strong organic foundation that paid strategies can amplify immediately
The Gap
  • Perigon Legal Services' ~58 reviews signal greater social proof to both Google's ranking algorithm and prospects evaluating multiple firms — every month without a review generation system widens this gap further
  • Without city-specific landing pages for suburbs Jim serves (Buckhead, Dunwoody, Alpharetta, Marietta), the firm cannot rank organically for estate planning searches in those ZIP codes despite already serving those clients
Perigon Legal Services — operates dedicated geographic landing pages for Sandy Springs, Alpharetta, and Marietta; maintains two Facebook pages and an active Instagram account; has a Social Media Manager on staff; and runs a dedicated PPC landing page (landing.perigonlegal.com) targeting estate planning searches in Atlanta — accumulating reviews and local visibility at a rate Jim's current approach cannot match.
Owning the #1 map pack position and building a review count that signals clear market leadership is what turns organic traffic into a consistent stream of $3,000 trust plan clients — month after month.
