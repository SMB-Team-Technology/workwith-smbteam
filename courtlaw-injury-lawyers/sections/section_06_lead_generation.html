Lead Generation

CourtLaw Injury Lawyers built a $5M peak year almost entirely on referral reputation and a run of high-value Uber/Lyft cases — not on a repeatable lead generation system — and now that new client intake has fallen to roughly 5 per month, there is no paid channel currently working to fill the gap.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed mobile and desktop scores could not be obtained this session (API quota exhausted) — recommend a manual PageSpeed run before finalizing technical scope
  • No live chat observed — with lead flow already this thin, every additional touchpoint for capturing visitor intent matters
  • No Spanish-language site content was confirmed, despite roughly 90% of the client base being Spanish-speaking per Karim
Blind Spot: The site was rebuilt within the last 2-3 years with a modern, responsive design, six attorney bios, and calls-to-action repeated above the fold, mid-page, and near the footer — this is not a website quality problem. That makes it easy to assume the fix is a new website, when the real gap is that too few people ever reach the site in the first place.
The Positive
  • Modern, mobile-responsive design with clear calls-to-action in multiple locations, including a phone number marked "Available 24/7"
  • Six attorney bios and an active blog, including a recent post on a $1.265M rideshare settlement directly relevant to the firm's highest-value case type
The Gap
  • A well-built site sitting with almost no paid traffic behind it means the investment already made in design and content is producing far less than it could
  • Relevant, share-worthy content like the rideshare settlement post is not being amplified through paid social, so it reaches almost no one beyond organic search
  • Without confirmed Spanish-language content, the site may be underserving the majority of the firm's own client base at the exact moment they are searching for help
The site itself is not what's standing between CourtLaw and its next 20 clients — getting people to it is.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • CourtLaw does not appear in top organic results for "car accident lawyer Perth Amboy NJ" or "personal injury lawyer Perth Amboy NJ best rated" — competitors surface instead
  • The firm's own geo-targeted pages surface only for narrower searches (New Brunswick injury lawyer, Uber/Lyft-specific terms) — not the broader, higher-volume practice area searches
  • No GBP posting activity was observed, and the NAP phone number should be confirmed consistent across all directory listings
Blind Spot: CourtLaw has already built a real SEO asset — geo-targeted landing pages for New Brunswick, East Brunswick, Perth Amboy, and Perth Amboy/New Brunswick Uber-Lyft searches — that simply is not producing lead volume yet. This isn't a rebuild project; it's an optimization and amplification project on top of work already paid for.
The Positive
  • ~203 Google reviews at 4.6 stars — more than every named local competitor found this session, some by a wide margin. Reputation is a genuine strength here, not a gap.
The Gap
  • With a review profile this strong, CourtLaw should be leading the local pack for every practice-area-plus-city search in its service area — right now it is not
  • Every month that reputation isn't converted into local pack visibility is a month a lower-rated competitor captures the click instead
Lombardi & Lombardi, P.A. — weaker reviews (28-42 reviews, 3.1-4.1★) but sits roughly 10 miles away in Edison and, at nearly 50 years in practice, likely has the budget and scale to be capturing local pack visibility that CourtLaw's stronger reputation should be winning instead.
You already earned the reputation that wins this market — right now, firms with weaker reviews may be capturing the search visibility that should be yours.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Per the discovery call, CourtLaw is not currently running Google search ads by choice — a $40k campaign in the past produced zero clients, creating a strong and understandable aversion to paid search
  • This applies across both primary case types: general car accidents and the firm's highest-value focus, Uber/Lyft rideshare accidents
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Not confirmed live this session — recommend a manual check of "car accident lawyer Perth Amboy NJ" and "car accident lawyer New Brunswick NJ" before the call
  • Lombardi & Lombardi and Bramnick Rodriguez Grabas Arnold & Mangan — the two named competitors with the scale most likely to be running paid search in this market today
The Opportunity
  • "Car accident lawyer" and "personal injury lawyer" plus city name across Perth Amboy, New Brunswick, Trenton, Jersey City, Roselle, and East Orange
  • Uber/Lyft rideshare accident keywords specifically — the firm's highest historical case value and least contested keyword set among named local competitors
  • Spanish-language search campaigns — roughly 90% of the client base is Spanish-speaking, and this audience is almost certainly underserved by every competitor in this comp set
  • Competitor conquesting and urgency-driven terms ("no fee unless we win," "free consultation")
Blind Spot: The $40k campaign that failed almost certainly reflects broad, poorly targeted keywords and no dedicated landing page strategy — not that PPC doesn't work for personal injury. Rebuilt around rideshare-specific ad groups, a Spanish-language track, and the geo-pages already in place, this channel does not have to repeat that history.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • CourtLaw does not appear to be running Local Service Ads or carrying the Google Screened badge — not confirmed as a live absence this session, but not observed
  • No LSA review count was found for the firm
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Not confirmed live this session — recommend a manual check before the call
  • Personal injury requires additional background and license verification for LSA eligibility, so presence in this vertical is inconsistent even among larger local players
The Opportunity
  • With ~203 reviews at 4.6 stars, CourtLaw would enter the LSA auction with a stronger review profile than every named local competitor — an immediate credibility edge
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • A pay-per-lead model means no wasted spend on clicks that don't convert — a meaningful reassurance for a firm still wary of repeating its earlier Google Ads experience
