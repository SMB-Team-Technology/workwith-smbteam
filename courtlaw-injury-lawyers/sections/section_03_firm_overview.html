Here Is Where CourtLaw Injury Lawyers Stands Today

CourtLaw Injury Lawyers is a 40-year-old New Jersey personal injury firm with 5 attorneys and roughly 20 staff, built almost entirely on referral reputation and a run of high-value Uber/Lyft rideshare cases that pushed annual revenue to a $5M peak during COVID. That peak was never the product of a repeatable lead generation system — it was reputation and case timing — and now that new client intake has fallen from roughly 5 per day to roughly 5 per month, the firm has no working system to fall back on. Founder Karim Arzadi is personally handling every financial decision himself, prioritizing payroll over strategy, while a $10-12k/month SEO retainer produces "garbage calls" and zero new cases.

Stage 6 is where CourtLaw stops being one bad month away from a payroll crisis and becomes a firm that runs — and protects its 40-year legacy and 20 jobs — without Karim carrying every financial and marketing decision alone.

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner

  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between a firm that once hit $5M on reputation alone and a firm that hits $5M every year on purpose is a predictable lead generation system — and closing that gap is what takes the payroll pressure off your shoulders for good.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
CourtLaw Injury Lawyers
Despite 40 years in business and a staff of roughly 20, CourtLaw is operating at Stage 3 — because the growth engine has always been Karim's personal reputation and referral network, not a system, and he still personally carries every financial decision with no dedicated ops or financial support in place. The single biggest gap limiting the firm right now is the absence of any working lead generation system: the paid SEO retainer is not converting, the firm carries a strong aversion to Google Ads after a $40k campaign produced zero clients, and its highest-value practice area — Uber/Lyft rideshare cases — has no dedicated paid channel behind it at all. Until that changes, the firm's stated goal of stabilizing and getting back to predictable growth will keep slipping further away every month intake stays this low.
