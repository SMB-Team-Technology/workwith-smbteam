Here Is Where Gunn & Gunn P.C. Stands Today

Gunn & Gunn P.C. is a third-generation family criminal defense firm in Salem, Oregon, with nearly 90 years of combined experience across three attorneys — and a 40-year reputation that most firms will never build. The firm handles criminal defense, DUI/DUII, bankruptcy, and personal injury cases throughout Marion and Polk County, and has earned 69 reviews at 4.5 stars on Google. The single most urgent finding: the firm's own founders are its biggest bottleneck — and without systems to change that, reaching $1.2M in revenue while Marcus gets his family time back is impossible.

Stage 6 is where the firm runs without Marcus — where he can triple his income, move his family into the home they deserve, and be present for his four kids without the business collapsing the moment he steps away from it.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • The website (gunnlawfirm.com) blocks search engine crawlers and automated tools — a technical configuration that limits how well Google can index and rank the site's content
  • Blog last updated March 2025 — over 15 months without fresh content signals an inactive site to both Google and prospective clients who visit looking for recent activity
  • The firm operates at least four separate web properties (gunnlawfirm.com, salemdui.com, gunndefense.com, salemcriminalattorney.com) — without a unified strategy, these micro-sites can split authority and create NAP inconsistencies that hurt local rankings
  • No visible contact form above the fold or live chat capability observed — prospects who arrive after-hours or on weekends have no immediate way to connect
Blind Spot: The multiple micro-sites Gunn & Gunn has built over the years (salemdui.com, gunndefense.com, salemcriminalattorney.com) may feel like a marketing advantage — but without a coordinated SEO strategy linking them, they are more likely diluting the firm's authority than multiplying it. A competitor with one focused, well-optimized site will often outrank three unfocused ones.
The Positive
  • Dedicated practice area pages exist for criminal defense, DUI/DUII, and bankruptcy — this is a strong foundation for SEO when properly optimized
  • Separate attorney bio pages for all three attorneys demonstrate credibility and personal connection that many competitor sites lack
The Gap
  • A website that blocks crawlers and has 15 months of stale content cannot rank competitively — every month without optimization is a month more leads go to Gilroy Napoli Short or Reynolds Defense instead
  • The absence of above-fold conversion tools (chat, immediate call-to-action form) means site visitors who are ready to hire right now are leaving without connecting
  • Criminal defense clients often make contact decisions within minutes of their arrest or charge — a website that cannot be found or does not capture urgently is invisible at the moment it matters most
A high-converting, fully optimized website is how Gunn & Gunn's 40-year reputation translates into the revenue that lets Marcus triple his income and stop being the firm's only salesperson.

Intake Assessment

Gunn & Gunn's intake function currently runs on one person — an A-player who is leaving, with no trained replacement and no system to cover the gap. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • The firm's only trained intake specialist is departing — there is no backfill plan and no system that operates independently of a single person
  • If the intake person is sick or unavailable, there is no one who can do the role at the same level — leads fall through with no follow-up
  • No after-hours or weekend coverage — criminal defense and DUI clients frequently need to connect immediately after an arrest, often late at night or on weekends, and unanswered calls go directly to competitors
  • No defined intake process, CRM, or follow-up sequence — prospects who do not sign immediately are lost, with no system to nurture them to a decision
Blind Spot: The departing intake person likely handles far more than just answering phones — scheduling, follow-up, consultation booking, and first impressions all go through this role. Without documenting what that person actually does before they leave, the firm will not know what broke until leads start disappearing.
The Positive
  • The firm already recognizes what an A-player intake hire looks like — they have seen one perform, which makes it easier to hire and train the right replacement
  • The firm was already referred to Attorney Assistant for after-hours coverage — they are aware of the gap and have taken a first step toward addressing it
The Gap
  • Every lead that arrives after hours or while intake is unavailable and goes unanswered is a case the firm paid to generate and then gave away — at $4,000 per average criminal defense case, even two missed calls per week represents over $400,000 in lost annual revenue
  • A single-point-of-failure intake system means the firm's revenue is directly tied to one person's health, schedule, and employment status — that is not a business, it is a dependency
  • Competitors like Reynolds Defense Firm have built intake processes specifically designed to capture the urgent, late-night call from someone just arrested — Gunn & Gunn is competing for those same clients with no system in place to catch them
A reliable intake system is what converts the firm's 40-year reputation and growing lead flow into the consistent revenue that gets Marcus to $300K personally — without him ever picking up another intake call himself.

Without a clear profit plan, the gap between how hard the firm works and what it pays Marcus will not close on its own. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

The Problem
  • Marcus is currently earning approximately $100K per year — against a personal income goal of $300K — with no structured plan for how to close that gap
  • No financial visibility system in place — the firm does not appear to track cost of client acquisition, profit by case type, or marketing ROI, which makes it impossible to make data-driven decisions about where to invest

The Positive
  • Marcus has named a specific income target ($300K) — that kind of clarity is the starting point for building a profit plan that actually achieves it
