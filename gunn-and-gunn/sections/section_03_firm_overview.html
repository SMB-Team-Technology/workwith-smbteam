Here Is Where Gunn & Gunn P.C. Stands Today

Gunn & Gunn P.C. is a third-generation family criminal defense firm in Salem, Oregon, with nearly 90 years of combined experience across three attorneys — and a 40-year reputation that most firms will never build. The firm handles criminal defense, DUI/DUII, bankruptcy, and personal injury cases throughout Marion and Polk County, and has earned 69 reviews at 4.5 stars on Google. The single most urgent finding: the firm's own founders are its biggest bottleneck — and without systems to change that, reaching $1.2M in revenue while Marcus gets his family time back is impossible.

Stage 6 is where the firm runs without Marcus — where he can triple his income, move his family into the home they deserve, and be present for his four kids without the business collapsing the moment he steps away from it.

Where You Are Today

Stage 4: Small Business Manager
  • Both Mark and Marcus personally run all day-to-day operations — the firm cannot function without either of them
  • A-player intake person is leaving with no trained replacement and no system to cover the gap
  • No SOPs and no KPIs — there is no framework for delegation even if there were someone to delegate to
  • Marcus could not take a single week off when his wife had their newborn in February — the firm demanded he stay

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between where Gunn & Gunn is today and where Marcus needs it to be is not about talent or reputation — it is about systems, and that is exactly what this roadmap is built to fix.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Gunn & Gunn P.C.
Gunn & Gunn P.C. is operating at Stage 4 — a Small Business Manager stage where some lead generation infrastructure exists through an established marketing provider, but where the owners remain personally essential to every function of the firm. The single most urgent gap is the complete absence of systems for delegation: no SOPs, no KPIs, and no intake redundancy mean that every time Marcus or Mark steps back, something breaks. Until that changes, the $1.2M revenue goal and the personal freedom Marcus wants will remain out of reach — not because the market is not there, but because the firm is not built to capture it.
