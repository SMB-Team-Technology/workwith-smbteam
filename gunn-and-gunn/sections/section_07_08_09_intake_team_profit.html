Intake Assessment

Gunn & Gunn's intake function currently runs on one person — an A-player who is leaving, with no trained replacement and no system to cover the gap. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • The firm's only trained intake specialist is departing — there is no backfill plan and no system that operates independently of a single person
  • If the intake person is sick or unavailable, there is no one who can do the role at the same level — leads fall through with no follow-up
  • No after-hours or weekend coverage — criminal defense and DUI clients frequently need to connect immediately after an arrest, often late at night or on weekends, and unanswered calls go directly to competitors
  • No defined intake process, CRM, or follow-up sequence — prospects who do not sign immediately are lost, with no system to nurture them to a decision
Blind Spot: The departing intake person likely handles far more than just answering phones — scheduling, follow-up, consultation booking, and first impressions all go through this role. Without documenting what that person actually does before they leave, the firm will not know what broke until leads start disappearing.
The Positive
  • The firm already recognizes what an A-player intake hire looks like — they have seen one perform, which makes it easier to hire and train the right replacement
  • The firm was already referred to Attorney Assistant for after-hours coverage — they are aware of the gap and have taken a first step toward addressing it
The Gap
  • Every lead that arrives after hours or while intake is unavailable and goes unanswered is a case the firm paid to generate and then gave away — at $4,000 per average criminal defense case, even two missed calls per week represents over $400,000 in lost annual revenue
  • A single-point-of-failure intake system means the firm's revenue is directly tied to one person's health, schedule, and employment status — that is not a business, it is a dependency
  • Competitors like Reynolds Defense Firm have built intake processes specifically designed to capture the urgent, late-night call from someone just arrested — Gunn & Gunn is competing for those same clients with no system in place to catch them
A reliable intake system is what converts the firm's 40-year reputation and growing lead flow into the consistent revenue that gets Marcus to $300K personally — without him ever picking up another intake call himself.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • The website has a contact page at /contact-us/ but no evidence of a contact form or chat tool visible above the fold on the homepage — prospects arriving from a search or ad who want to connect immediately have no obvious path to do so
  • No live chat or automated response tool was observed on the site — for a criminal defense firm where the urgency of a prospective client's situation is often at a peak, the absence of an instant connection option directly costs consultations
  • Attorney Assistant, which has already been recommended to the firm, could address the after-hours coverage gap immediately — this is the fastest way to stop bleeding leads while a permanent intake hire is recruited and trained

Team Assessment

Mark and Marcus Gunn are personally running every major function of the firm — and both of them know it. They have acknowledged out loud that they are the bottleneck. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Mark and Marcus personally manage all day-to-day operations — if either is absent, the other absorbs the full weight of the firm alone
  • No SOPs exist — there is no documented process for any function, which means nothing can be reliably handed off to anyone
  • No KPIs — the firm has no way to measure how the team is performing, which makes accountability and improvement impossible
  • Marcus could not take even a week off when his wife had their newborn daughter in February — a firm that cannot survive one week without its owner is not a business that can grow
Blind Spot: The fact that both Mark and Marcus are capable attorneys who handle everything themselves can feel like a strength — but it is the firm's single biggest ceiling. The more they handle personally, the harder it is for anyone else to grow into those roles. The longer this continues, the further away a self-managing firm becomes.
The Positive
  • Marcus is self-aware — he has named the bottleneck clearly and is actively motivated to solve it. That level of honesty is rare and is exactly the starting point needed for real change
  • The firm has a third generation of legal talent in Marcus — a long-term foundation for building a firm that can outlast any one person's direct involvement
The Gap
  • A firm where both owners are the bottleneck cannot scale — every new case is absorbed by people who are already at capacity, which means growth just means more hours, not more income
  • Without SOPs, no new hire — attorney, intake, or admin — can be onboarded and performing independently. That makes the upcoming intake backfill harder and any future attorney hire more dependent on Marcus's direct time than it needs to be
  • The personal cost is already happening: missed family time, absorbed workload, and a ceiling on income that cannot be raised without systems. Marcus's four children are growing up while he is at the office absorbing Mark's workload
Building a self-managing team is not just a business goal for Marcus — it is how he gets to be present for his family without the firm punishing him for stepping away.

Profit Plan Assessment

Marcus is working at the level of a managing partner but earning at the level of a mid-level associate — approximately $100K per year against a target of $300K. Without a clear profit plan, the gap between how hard the firm works and what it pays Marcus will not close on its own. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Marcus is currently earning approximately $100K per year — against a personal income goal of $300K — with no structured plan for how to close that gap
  • No financial visibility system in place — the firm does not appear to track cost of client acquisition, profit by case type, or marketing ROI, which makes it impossible to make data-driven decisions about where to invest
  • No contingency financial plan exists despite Mark's stated concern about economic downturns and a potential pivot to bankruptcy work — the firm would be reacting instead of preparing
  • Revenue goal of $1.2M next year is clearly defined, but there is no structured financial roadmap for how increased revenue translates into increased owner distributions
Blind Spot: Growing revenue without a profit plan often means growing expenses at the same rate — or faster. Hiring a new attorney, investing in marketing, and backfilling intake all have costs. Without financial visibility, Marcus could hit $1.2M in revenue and still be taking home $100K because the money went somewhere else first.
The Positive
  • Marcus has named a specific income target ($300K) — that kind of clarity is the starting point for building a profit plan that actually achieves it
  • The firm has survived and operated profitably for over 40 years, which means there is a working business underneath the systems gap — the foundation is real
The Gap
  • Without knowing cost per acquired client or ROI by practice area, the firm cannot make confident decisions about where to spend marketing dollars — which makes every growth investment a guess
  • Marcus's personal income goal of $300K is $200K away from where he is today. Without a structured distribution plan tied to firm revenue milestones, that gap does not close automatically — it requires design
  • The absence of a recession contingency plan is a specific risk: if a downturn arrives and the firm needs to pivot toward bankruptcy work, they have no playbook, no marketing infrastructure for that practice area, and no prepared team
A clear profit plan is what turns a $1.2M revenue goal into $300K in Marcus's pocket — and into the bigger home, the real vacations, and the financial freedom that this firm's reputation has already earned.
