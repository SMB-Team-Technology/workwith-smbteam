Lead Generation

Davidek Law Firm generates enough lead flow to keep appointments booked into late August, driven by strong organic search visibility and an outside agency managing paid campaigns. But the firm cannot see into how that agency spend is actually performing, and it has no dedicated pages in place yet for the Austin market it plans to launch into by January 2027.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Live PageSpeed mobile and desktop scores could not be captured this pass — the tooling used hit a quota block, and a manual pagespeed.web.dev check should be run before load-time claims are finalized with the client
  • The homepage phone number (830) 515-5854 does not match the dedicated Contact page number (830) 217-1544 — a live, confirmed NAP inconsistency that can quietly cost calls and confuse tracking on any paid campaign
  • No contact form appears above the fold on the homepage — phone and email are the only options surfaced to a visitor
  • No individual attorney bio module surfaces on the homepage despite "31 years of combined legal experience" messaging — bios for Beverly R. Davidek, Robert S. Harrison, Keith McMahon, and Amanda Batsche exist on a separate About page instead
Blind Spot: You may not realize the two different phone numbers listed across your own site and directories are actively splitting your call volume and confusing which channel gets credit for a lead — including any paid campaigns your agency is running.
The Positive
  • The blog is genuinely active — five posts in the two weeks before this audit, most recently July 17, 2026 — a real, underused SEO and trust asset
  • The site uses a modern layout, responsive navigation, and contemporary design conventions, without the dated look that hurts several competitor sites
The Gap
  • Every visitor who can't find a contact form or dials the wrong number is a lead that may quietly go to J.M. Dickerson, Carroll Law Group, or Cludius Law instead
  • Without confirmed PageSpeed data, mobile load time is an unverified risk sitting on top of an already-splitting phone number problem
  • Zero geo-targeted pages means the Austin launch currently has no digital foundation to launch from
Fixing the site's foundation — one phone number, a visible contact form, and dedicated Austin pages — protects the lead flow you already have while building the digital footprint your 2027 expansion needs.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Google Business Profile shows a strong 4.9-star rating and roughly 156 aggregated reviews (Birdeye-sourced), but this has not yet been confirmed against a live Google Maps panel
  • The same six competitors — J.M. Dickerson, Cludius Law, Carroll Law Group, Texas Horizons Law Group, Weisinger Law Firm, and Mazurek Belden & Burke — recur across every estate planning, asset protection, and probate search run this pass
  • No geo-landing pages exist for Seguin, San Antonio, or Austin, despite the planned January 2027 Austin rebrand and launch
Blind Spot: Appearing organically today does not guarantee 3-pack map-pack placement — those are two different games, and only a live Maps check can confirm which one you're actually winning.
The Positive
  • Davidek's own review count (~156) already outpaces J.M. Dickerson (59) and Carroll Law Group (39) — a real trust advantage worth protecting and promoting, not just maintaining
The Gap
  • Every practice-area search where a named competitor holds a stronger local pack position is a searcher handed to that firm instead of Davidek
  • Without geo-pages for the Austin market, the January 2027 launch starts from zero on local search — a market Davidek is entering fresh, not with the reputation advantage it holds in New Braunfels
The J.M. Dickerson Law Firm — Central Texas — a perfect 5.0 rating and the single most consistently-surfacing name across every estate planning, probate, and business succession search run this pass, making it the closest thing to an incumbent threat in local SEO.
Protecting and extending the organic advantage you've already built in New Braunfels — and building an equivalent footprint in Austin before you launch there — is what turns local reputation into the predictable growth your $4M goal requires.

Competitor Ad Landscape

The Opportunity

  • "estate planning attorney Austin" / "asset protection attorney Austin" — building paid presence ahead of the January 2027 rebrand instead of starting from zero
Blind Spot: Because your paid ads are fully agency-managed, you may not have visibility into whether that spend is actually reaching the searches above — worth confirming directly rather than assuming.
