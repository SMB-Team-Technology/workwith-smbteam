Intake Assessment

For a solo practitioner handling immigration cases, intake is the most vulnerable point in the client journey — a prospective client who cannot reach someone immediately will often call the next firm on their list. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Karla handles intake personally as the sole attorney — every new inquiry competes for attention with active casework, hearings, and client obligations
  • No contact form or clear CTA confirmed above the fold on the website — online visitors who are ready to reach out face unnecessary friction before they can connect
  • No after-hours coverage observed — the immigrant community Karla serves often calls evenings and weekends when work schedules allow, and those calls likely reach voicemail
  • No defined intake process or follow-up system identified — leads who do not sign on first contact have no structured pathway to bring them back
Blind Spot: Immigration clients — particularly those facing ICE matters, removal proceedings, or urgent visa situations — are making calls in moments of high stress. If the first call goes to voicemail and there is no follow-up within minutes, they will call another firm. The urgency of their situation creates a very short intake window that most solo practitioners cannot cover without a system.
The Gap
  • Every unanswered or slow-followed-up inquiry is a lost case — at an average immigration case value of $2,500–$5,000, even one missed intake per week represents $10,000–$20,000 in lost monthly revenue
  • Without a structured follow-up sequence, leads who expressed interest but did not immediately sign are never re-engaged — they go to a competitor who called them back first
  • Karla's personal time spent on intake is time not spent on casework, strategy, or building the firm — a structural drain on both productivity and personal bandwidth
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • A contact form was not confirmed above the fold or on the homepage — visitors most likely to convert (those who found the site through search) have no immediate pathway to connect
  • No scheduling tool or instant consultation booking was observed — adding an online appointment booking option would allow Spanish-speaking prospects to initiate the relationship at any hour without needing to call
  • No live chat was observed — for immigration clients who may be uncertain about calling (language barriers, fear of documentation questions), a text-based contact option significantly lowers the barrier to first contact

Team Assessment

Santiago Legal LLC operates as a solo practice where Karla is the firm — handling casework, intake, client communication, and every operational decision without a dedicated team structure to distribute the load. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • No dedicated intake, operations, or marketing staff identified — Karla is the only confirmed team member handling all firm functions simultaneously
  • The firm's revenue ceiling is Karla's personal capacity — there is no way to grow beyond what one attorney can personally execute
  • No accountability structure or defined roles observed — without a team with clear responsibilities, every decision and client issue routes back to Karla
  • Karla cannot step away from the firm without cases stalling — there is no one else who can manage client matters, handle calls, or maintain momentum in her absence
Blind Spot: Many solo practitioners believe they need to wait until revenue grows before hiring — but the inverse is often true. Without someone to handle intake, calls, and admin, a solo attorney cannot take on enough cases to justify the hire. The first team member is what unlocks the next revenue level, not the other way around.
The Gap
  • Every hour Karla spends on intake, scheduling, and administrative work is an hour she cannot spend on casework or practice development — the firm is paying attorney-rate time for non-attorney-rate tasks
  • Without a team, adding marketing spend only adds pressure — more leads mean more calls to answer, more intake conversations to have, and more cases to manage, all without additional capacity
  • The firm cannot scale, cannot take on complex or high-value cases that require more bandwidth, and cannot give Karla any meaningful time off without shutting down operations
Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Profit Plan Assessment

With 16 years of practice, Karla has invested substantially in building Santiago Legal LLC — but without a formal profit plan, there is no mechanism to ensure that investment is generating the financial return and personal income it deserves. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Revenue figures were not available for this audit — without knowing the starting number, it is impossible to determine whether the firm is profitable or simply busy
  • No stated profit plan, financial visibility system, or cost-per-case-type tracking observed — the firm is likely operating on a reactive financial basis rather than a planned one
  • Solo practitioner income is typically variable and tied to case completion cycles — immigration cases can have irregular billing timelines that create unpredictable cash flow
  • No visible mechanism to separate business revenue from owner compensation — a common solo practitioner pattern that makes true profitability difficult to measure
Blind Spot: Immigration case types vary dramatically in profitability per hour invested. A removal defense case billed at $3,000–$10,000 may take 40–80 hours, while a simpler green card petition at $2,500–$5,000 might take 10–15 hours. Without tracking profitability by case type, a firm can be "growing" in revenue while actually working more for less money per hour.
The Gap
  • Without a profit plan, growth adds workload without guaranteed proportional income gains — every new case creates more work, but the financial yield is unclear
  • Without financial visibility, Karla cannot make informed decisions about hiring, marketing investment, or practice area focus — every major decision is made without the data it needs
  • Without knowing the most profitable case types, Santiago Legal may be taking on high-effort, lower-margin work when higher-value opportunities exist in the same market
A profit plan is how 16 years of dedication becomes real financial freedom — not just more hours, but more of what those hours are actually worth.
