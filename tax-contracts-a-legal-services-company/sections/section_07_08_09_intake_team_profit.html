Intake Assessment

Tax & Contracts is generating leads through paid advertising and webinars — but the conversion data reveals a critical breakdown: one agency generated 57 leads and converted only 1 booking, a rate so low it indicates the intake process is not built to handle paid traffic at all. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • One paid ad campaign generated 57 leads that yielded only 1 booking — a 1.75% conversion rate that indicates no structured follow-up system is in place to nurture paid leads to a signed consultation
  • No evidence of a defined multi-step intake sequence: same-day acknowledgment, follow-up calls, email touchpoints, and a closing conversation are not confirmed as a standard operating process
  • The firm is evaluating 3–4 marketing agencies simultaneously without a unified intake tracking system — leads from different sources cannot be attributed, measured, or managed consistently
  • Chrystan is the sole attorney, meaning any consultation that requires legal judgment flows through her personally — limiting the volume of intake conversations the firm can handle without her direct involvement
Blind Spot: The 57-lead-to-1-booking data point is being framed as a marketing problem — bad leads from a bad agency. But even with imperfect leads, a structured intake process with fast follow-up, clear qualification scripts, and persistent outreach typically converts 10–15% of paid ad leads. The issue is almost certainly split between lead quality AND intake process — and the firm may be firing vendors for a problem that is partly internal.
The Positive
  • Webinar attendees convert at approximately 80% of booked participants — proof that when prospects are properly warmed up and expectations are set, the firm closes at an exceptional rate
  • Referrals convert at nearly 100% — the attorney's reputation produces the highest-quality leads available, and the close rate reflects that
The Gap
  • At 1.75% conversion from paid ads, the firm is effectively paying acquisition costs 6–8× higher than what a functional intake process would produce — thousands of dollars in ad spend are generating almost no return
  • Without a defined intake sequence, the gap between the webinar experience (warm, educated prospects who convert at 80%) and cold paid ad leads (who need nurturing to get there) will never close
  • Every unbooked lead represents a prospect who was already interested enough to raise their hand — and then was not followed up with consistently enough to become a client
Intake is where the leverage lives — a firm that converts 10% of paid leads instead of 1.75% generates 5× the clients from the same ad spend, which is the fastest path to giving Chrystan her time back without adding more hours.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • The primary contact form is located on a dedicated Contact page (taxandcontracts.com/contact) — it is not confirmed as visible above the fold on the homepage, which means a significant percentage of visitors who do not scroll or navigate further never reach a conversion point
  • "Book a Consultation" CTA is present on the homepage, but the path from click to completed form adds friction that reduces the percentage of visitors who follow through
  • No live chat widget was observed — after-hours website visitors who have a question have no immediate way to engage, creating a gap in the late-night and weekend lead capture window that is often when small business owners are researching attorneys

These findings are also referenced in the Website Audit section.

Team Assessment

Tax & Contracts has four full-time team members and a returning part-time assistant — real infrastructure for a boutique firm — but every single person on the team, including Chrystan, is working 45+ hours per week, which means the team is at capacity and Chrystan is still the only person who can generate legal revenue. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Chrystan is the sole attorney — no other person at the firm can handle a client matter, which means every legal task, every review, and every billable decision requires her personally
  • All team members are already working 45+ hours per week — the team has no spare capacity to absorb the volume that would come with meaningful revenue growth
  • The firm's goal of returning to 8–10 new clients per month cannot be achieved at its current attorney capacity without Chrystan working significantly more hours than she already is
  • No operational leadership layer exists below Chrystan — when she is unavailable, client matters wait
Blind Spot: The team Chrystan has built is talented and committed — but talent without structure means each person is managing their own workload independently rather than operating as an integrated system. A bilingual ops coordinator, a tech lead, and a senior paralegal are the right ingredients for a self-managing team. What is missing is the documented workflow, clear accountability, and delegation framework that turns individual contributors into a team that runs without the owner's constant direction.
The Positive
  • The team composition is strong for this stage: a bilingual ops coordinator (Paulina Cerrato) creates a genuine Spanish-language service capability that opens a distinct market segment
  • A dedicated tech lead (Andrik Acevedo) provides internal capacity to manage digital tools and systems — a resource most boutique firms at this stage do not have
The Gap
  • A firm where the owner is the only attorney is not a business — it is a job. Until there is a second attorney or a structured delegation framework that removes Chrystan from the day-to-day of every matter, the firm's capacity is capped at her personal bandwidth
  • With no management layer, scaling means Chrystan manages more people, more cases, and more decisions — the opposite of the freedom she is building toward
  • Every team member working 45+ hours at current revenue levels means adding revenue requires either adding people or adding Chrystan's hours — neither of which is sustainable
The team Chrystan has is the foundation — but without a system that delegates ownership clearly and removes her from the critical path of every decision, the firm can only grow as fast as she can personally manage.

Profit Plan Assessment

Tax & Contracts is investing in marketing through multiple agencies simultaneously, generating revenue across 70 active matters with 30 retainer clients — but no financial metrics were shared on the discovery call, and the evidence suggests the firm does not yet have clear visibility into what each marketing dollar is returning or what profit looks like by channel or practice area. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No revenue figure was shared on the discovery call — the firm may not have clear, real-time visibility into monthly revenue, or did not feel it was trackable with confidence
  • Paying 3–4 marketing agencies with no unified attribution tracking means the firm cannot identify which dollars are generating clients and which are generating nothing — marketing spend is happening without accountability
  • One agency produced 57 leads with 1 booking — a cost-per-acquisition figure that, if it reflects the broader marketing spend, would indicate that current marketing ROI is deeply negative
  • The $6M revenue goal in three years is a 10× or greater growth target from a boutique firm's current size — achieving it requires not just more revenue, but deliberate profit planning to ensure growth does not simply produce more expenses
Blind Spot: With 30 retainer clients and 40 active project matters, it is possible that some of the highest-revenue cases are also the highest-cost ones to serve — particularly federal tax disputes, which require significant attorney time. Without tracking profitability by matter type, the firm may be optimizing for revenue in the cases that deliver the lowest net profit per hour of attorney time.
The Positive
  • A retainer client base of 30 clients represents predictable, recurring revenue — a financial foundation that most solo and small firm practitioners never build
  • The firm's late 2025 growth demonstrates that when the right lead source is active (a webinar campaign or lead-gen platform), revenue responds — the mechanism works when it is pointed in the right direction
The Gap
  • Without knowing cost per case by channel and practice area, the firm cannot make smart decisions about which agencies to keep, which to fire, or where to increase investment
  • A $6M goal without a profit plan is a revenue target, not a wealth target — growth without margin clarity can mean working harder for the same or smaller take-home
  • Every month of agency spend without attribution is waste that compounds — the sooner a tracking framework is in place, the sooner the firm can redirect those dollars to what is actually working
Chrystan's goal is not just $6M in revenue — it is a firm that generates that revenue profitably, with clear visibility into what each dollar is doing, so she can build real wealth alongside the practice she has already built.
