Lead Generation

Tax & Contracts, A Legal Services Company has a proven attorney, a bilingual team, and a track record of results for small business owners — but the firm's digital presence under its current brand is nearly invisible, with no confirmed Google Business Profile, zero local 3-pack appearances across all target practice areas, and multiple paid advertising agencies producing almost no client conversions. The leads exist in this market. The system to capture them does not yet exist for this firm.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Site is built on Wix — a platform that limits technical SEO control, custom performance optimization, and conversion-focused design customization
  • PageSpeed scores pending manual verification at pagespeed.web.dev — automated retrieval was not possible; scores should be confirmed before the call
  • A broken navigation link (FlatRate Trusts) returns a 404 error — a visible trust signal problem for any prospect exploring the site
  • No blog is present — the /blog URL returns a 404 — eliminating any opportunity for organic search traffic through content
  • Contact form is located on a separate contact page, not confirmed above the fold on the homepage, reducing immediate conversion opportunities
Blind Spot: The firm recently rebranded from "Carlton Law, Ltd." to "Tax & Contracts, A Legal Services Company" — but the website domain (taxandcontracts.com) may not yet carry the domain authority or search history of the legacy brand. Any SEO equity built under carltonlawltd.com is not automatically transferred, meaning the firm may have reset its organic search standing without realizing it.
The Positive
  • The homepage headline is specific and compelling — "high-caliber, bespoke legal solutions for small-business owners, social entrepreneurs, and nonprofit founders" clearly communicates a target audience
  • Attorney bio and headshot are present, and the site includes trust badges from ISBA, BWLA, and ABA — meaningful credibility signals for prospective clients
  • Multiple CTAs are present above the fold, including "Book a Consultation" — the firm is attempting to convert visitors, even if the form placement needs work
The Gap
  • A site that cannot be found in search — whether due to PageSpeed penalties, missing practice area pages, or Wix platform limitations — is spending money on paid ads that drive traffic to a page that won't convert
  • No blog means no organic content engine — competitors who publish regularly are building domain authority month over month while this firm stands still
  • The broken FlatRate Trusts link signals to prospects that the firm's digital presence is not maintained — damaging credibility before a consultation even begins
A website that converts is the foundation everything else is built on — without it, every marketing dollar spent on ads and SEO is working at a fraction of its potential.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • The Google Business Profile does not appear to be listed under "Tax & Contracts, A Legal Services Company" — the GBP is likely under the legacy "Carlton Law, Ltd." name, which means the firm is invisible to anyone searching under the new brand
  • Tax & Contracts does not appear in the local 3-pack for any practice area searched: "federal tax attorney Chicago," "tax lawyer Chicago," "IRS tax dispute attorney Chicago," "business contract lawyer Chicago," or "company formation attorney Chicago"
  • The Avvo profile shows a 7.0 rating ("Very Good") but 0 client reviews and 0 peer endorsements — meaning the firm has attorney credibility but zero social proof from clients
  • No Yelp listing was found for Tax & Contracts or Carlton Law in Chicago — a missing platform that competitors use actively
Blind Spot: The firm operates under two legal identities — "Carlton Law, Ltd." (the entity name used in the email domain) and "Tax & Contracts, A Legal Services Company" (the trade name on the website). This split creates a Name-Address-Phone (NAP) inconsistency across directories that actively suppresses local search rankings. Google's algorithm treats inconsistent business information as a trust signal problem — and the firm may not realize this dual-name situation is costing it search visibility every day.
The Positive
  • UpCounsel profile shows 17 repeat client hires with strong qualitative feedback — proof that the firm delivers results and has a client retention story worth amplifying
The Gap
  • Competitors in the local 3-pack have 100–280+ verified Google reviews — Tax & Contracts has zero confirmed public reviews under the current brand, making it invisible in local pack comparisons
  • Without a cleaned-up GBP under the new brand, the firm cannot qualify for Local Service Ads (LSAs), which appear above every other result on Google
Gordon Law Group — 200+ Google reviews at 4.8–4.9 stars, with a fully optimized GBP that appears at the top of every local tax attorney search in Chicago. They have built a review moat that makes it progressively harder for any new entrant to displace them from the local pack without a deliberate, sustained review acquisition strategy.
Local SEO is not a one-time fix — it is an ongoing competitive asset. Every month without an optimized GBP is a month competitors extend their lead in the exact searches your target clients are using to find an attorney.

Blind Spot: The firm's most powerful competitive differentiators — U.S. Tax Court admission, a "0% failure rate with the IRS," and bilingual service — do not appear to be surfacing in current agency ad campaigns. If the agencies are running generic legal ads without leveraging these specifics, that is likely a primary driver of the catastrophically low 1-in-57 conversion rate.
