Here Is Where Tax & Contracts, A Legal Services Company Stands Today

Tax & Contracts, A Legal Services Company is a Chicago-based boutique law firm led by attorney Chrystan Carlton, serving small business owners, social entrepreneurs, and nonprofits across federal tax disputes, commercial transactions, company formation, and social enterprise consulting from offices in Chicago and Washington, DC. The firm has a small team of four full-time staff, a proven webinar-driven lead engine, and a goal of reaching $6 million in revenue within three years — but the single most critical gap is that the current paid marketing spend across multiple agencies is producing nearly zero client conversions, while Chrystan remains the sole attorney and the singular bottleneck every dollar of revenue flows through.

Stage 6 is where the firm runs itself — where Chrystan's time is truly her own and the revenue she built no longer depends on her being present for every decision, every call, and every matter.

Where You Are Today

Stage 4: Small Business Manager
  • Chrystan is the sole attorney — every dollar of revenue flows through her personally
  • Paying 3–4 marketing agencies simultaneously with no clear attribution or measurable ROI
  • Webinars convert at ~80% of booked attendees but the system is not built for scale
  • All four full-time team members are working 45+ hours per week and the firm is still at capacity

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between where the firm is today and where Chrystan wants it to be is real — but it is not about working harder. It is about building the right systems, with the right marketing, and the right operational foundation to carry the load she has been carrying alone.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Intake Assessment

Tax & Contracts is generating leads through paid advertising and webinars — but the conversion data reveals a critical breakdown: one agency generated 57 leads and converted only 1 booking, a rate so low it indicates the intake process is not built to handle paid traffic at all. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • One paid ad campaign generated 57 leads that yielded only 1 booking — a 1.75% conversion rate that indicates no structured follow-up system is in place to nurture paid leads to a signed consultation
  • No evidence of a defined multi-step intake sequence: same-day acknowledgment, follow-up calls, email touchpoints, and a closing conversation are not confirmed as a standard operating process
  • The firm is evaluating 3–4 marketing agencies simultaneously without a unified intake tracking system — leads from different sources cannot be attributed, measured, or managed consistently
  • Chrystan is the sole attorney, meaning any consultation that requires legal judgment flows through her personally — limiting the volume of intake conversations the firm can handle without her direct involvement
Blind Spot: The 57-lead-to-1-booking data point is being framed as a marketing problem — bad leads from a bad agency. But even with imperfect leads, a structured intake process with fast follow-up, clear qualification scripts, and persistent outreach typically converts 10–15% of paid ad leads. The issue is almost certainly split between lead quality AND intake process — and the firm may be firing vendors for a problem that is partly internal.
The Positive
  • Webinar attendees convert at approximately 80% of booked participants — proof that when prospects are properly warmed up and expectations are set, the firm closes at an exceptional rate
  • Referrals convert at nearly 100% — the attorney's reputation produces the highest-quality leads available, and the close rate reflects that
The Gap
  • At 1.75% conversion from paid ads, the firm is effectively paying acquisition costs 6–8× higher than what a functional intake process would produce — thousands of dollars in ad spend are generating almost no return
  • Without a defined intake sequence, the gap between the webinar experience (warm, educated prospects who convert at 80%) and cold paid ad leads (who need nurturing to get there) will never close
  • Every unbooked lead represents a prospect who was already interested enough to raise their hand — and then was not followed up with consistently enough to become a client
Intake is where the leverage lives — a firm that converts 10% of paid leads instead of 1.75% generates 5× the clients from the same ad spend, which is the fastest path to giving Chrystan her time back without adding more hours.

Chrystan's goal is not just $6M in revenue — it is a firm that generates that revenue profitably, with clear visibility into what each dollar is doing, so she can build real wealth alongside the practice she has already built.
