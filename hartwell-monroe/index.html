Here Is Where Hartwell & Monroe Law Group Stands Today

Sarah Hartwell has built Hartwell & Monroe Law Group over roughly seven years into a four-person family law and estate planning practice in west Austin generating $680,000 in annual revenue — almost entirely on referrals. The reputation is real, but every paid channel competitors use to scale is sitting unused: no Google Ads, no LSA placement, no current Meta presence, and a Google Business Profile sitting at 34 reviews while named local competitors operate at 198–412.

Stage 6 is the stage Sarah needs to reach to finally stop being the one doing intake, take a real vacation without her phone blowing up, and be at her kids' games — and it is exactly where this roadmap is taking her.

Where You Are Today

Stage 3: Solo Practitioner
  • Referrals are the only consistent source of new clients — no paid channel running
  • Sarah personally handles most intake calls — named on the call as a bottleneck
  • Small team of two associates and one paralegal — no dedicated intake or operations role
  • Revenue swings month to month — "some months are great, some are really slow"

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The distance between a $680K referral-only solo practice and a $1.2M firm that runs without Sarah is closed by exactly two things — a predictable lead system, and a trained intake function she no longer has to be part of.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Intake Assessment

Sarah is doing most of the intake herself between cases, which she named on the call as the bottleneck on both her time and her close rate. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
"I want to not be the one doing intake. I want a team that handles that."
The Problem
  • Sarah personally handles most intake calls — no dedicated intake person on the team of four
  • Office hours are 9–5 only — calls outside that window go to voicemail and the prospect often calls a competitor before morning
  • 40% close rate is the ceiling while an attorney is doing intake between cases, instead of a trained intake function working off a defined script and follow-up sequence
  • No stated CRM or systematic follow-up cadence for leads who do not sign on the first contact
Blind Spot: Family law and estate planning searches spike in evenings and weekends — the exact hours Hartwell & Monroe is closed. Every after-hours caller who lands in voicemail is a paid or referred lead competitors can recapture by morning.

  • Every intake call Sarah takes is one she pulls away from her associates, her cases, or her kids — the unit economics of attorney-time-as-intake-time are upside down
  • Without after-hours capture, the most expensive leads to acquire (evening/weekend high-intent searchers) are the ones most likely to be lost
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.
