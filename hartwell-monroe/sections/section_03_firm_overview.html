Here Is Where Hartwell & Monroe Law Group Stands Today

Sarah Hartwell has built Hartwell & Monroe Law Group over roughly seven years into a four-person family law and estate planning practice in west Austin generating $680,000 in annual revenue — almost entirely on referrals. The reputation is real, but every paid channel competitors use to scale is sitting unused: no Google Ads, no LSA placement, no current Meta presence, and a Google Business Profile sitting at 34 reviews while named local competitors operate at 198–412.

Stage 6 is the stage Sarah needs to reach to finally stop being the one doing intake, take a real vacation without her phone blowing up, and be at her kids' games — and it is exactly where this roadmap is taking her.

Where You Are Today

Stage 3: Solo Practitioner
  • Referrals are the only consistent source of new clients — no paid channel running
  • Sarah personally handles most intake calls — named on the call as a bottleneck
  • Small team of two associates and one paralegal — no dedicated intake or operations role
  • Revenue swings month to month — "some months are great, some are really slow"

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The distance between a $680K referral-only solo practice and a $1.2M firm that runs without Sarah is closed by exactly two things — a predictable lead system, and a trained intake function she no longer has to be part of.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Hartwell & Monroe Law Group
Hartwell & Monroe is operating at Stage 3 — a Solo Practitioner whose reputation has outgrown her systems. The single most urgent limiting area is lead generation: with zero paid channels active in a market where named competitors are running Google Ads, sitting in LSA top-three slots, and pushing always-on Meta video, every $1.2M Sarah wants to grow into has to come through a referral pipeline that already swings wildly month to month. Until that pipeline is replaced with predictable, paid lead flow — and intake is taken off Sarah's desk — the firm cannot reach the $1.2M target, and Sarah cannot get her time back with her kids.
