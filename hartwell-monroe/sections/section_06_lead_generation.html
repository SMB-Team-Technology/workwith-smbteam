Lead Generation

Hartwell & Monroe Law Group runs almost entirely on referrals — no Google Ads, no LSA placement, no current Meta campaigns, and a website that is technically live but doing none of the heavy lifting it could. Every paid channel that named Austin family law and estate planning competitors are using to scale is sitting unused.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed scores: Mobile 42/100 — Desktop 78/100. Mobile is well below the 70 benchmark for competitive local ranking, with a 4.8-second Largest Contentful Paint on phones
  • Contact form is buried on a separate /contact page — no above-the-fold form on the homepage, so visitors who decide on the hero have to keep clicking
  • Family Law and Estate Planning share one generic contact form — no separate conversion path for the two practice areas Sarah wants to grow at different speeds
  • Blog last updated February 11, 2026 — three-plus months stale, sending the wrong "are they still active" signal to both Google and prospects
Blind Spot: Austin is ~32% Hispanic and there is no Spanish-language landing page — a sizeable family law and estate planning audience is being handed to bilingual competitors like The Marisol Vega Law Firm before Hartwell & Monroe is even in the consideration set.
The Positive
  • Site is mobile responsive and the three attorney bios are live — the structural bones are workable
  • "Schedule Your Confidential Consultation" CTA does sit above the fold on the homepage
The Gap
  • A 42 mobile score is silently bleeding leads — every second of LCP delay above two seconds drops conversion measurably
  • No geo-targeted pages for Round Rock, Cedar Park, Pflugerville, Georgetown, Lakeway, or West Lake Hills — the entire western Travis County catchment around the Bee Cave Rd office is unaddressed
  • One generic contact form means every estate planning lead is being routed through messaging built for divorce inquiries, and vice versa
A website that converts visitors without Sarah's involvement is what turns marketing spend into kids' games attended — instead of intake calls returned.

Local SEO

  • No Google Posts in the last 12 months and only six photos last uploaded ~9 months ago — the GBP reads as dormant to Google's local algorithm
  • Two unanswered Q&A questions sitting on the GBP — competitors are answering theirs
Blind Spot: Yelp shows the firm as "Hartwell Monroe Law Group" without the ampersand — a small NAP variance that quietly weakens local ranking signals across the citation graph.
The Positive

The Gap
  • Zero 3-pack presence across both practice areas means Hartwell & Monroe is invisible to the searcher who is ready to call today
  • No surrounding-city geo pages means the western Travis County advantage of the Bee Cave Rd office is not being captured anywhere search engines can read it
Ashford & Pace Family Law — same Bee Cave Rd corridor as Hartwell & Monroe, sitting at 198 Google reviews with an active GBP and 3-pack placement for "child custody attorney Austin." They are showing up for the searches Hartwell & Monroe is invisible in.
A firm that ranks locally without Sarah personally maintaining it is a firm that produces leads while she is on vacation.
