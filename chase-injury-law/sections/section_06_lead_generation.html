Lead Generation

Chase Injury Law has been generating website traffic since launch — approximately 200 daily visitors from Google Ads — but has converted zero of those visitors into qualified leads because the keywords being targeted were health insurance terms, not workers' compensation or injury search terms. The result is a marketing investment that is producing noise instead of clients, while every channel that could generate real demand remains either untapped or blocked by an unresolved Google Business Profile verification.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed scores not confirmed — API quota exhausted during research; site is on Squarespace, which typically scores 45–65 mobile / 72–88 desktop. Squarespace's shared infrastructure limits performance optimization, which directly impacts Google Ads Quality Score and organic rankings. Actual scores must be verified before finalizing recommendations.
  • Contact form is not embedded above the fold — visitors must click a CTA button to reach the form, adding friction at the highest-intent moment and likely reducing conversion rates
  • No video on the site — workers' compensation clients are making a high-stakes, high-emotion decision; video of the attorney builds the trust and personal connection that converts browsers into callers
  • Blog content mix includes posts dating to May 2019, likely imported from a prior domain — this creates potential duplicate content signals and may dilute the site's credibility with Google's crawlers
Blind Spot: Squarespace's platform architecture limits the technical SEO improvements that would otherwise be available — faster hosting, schema markup control, and page speed optimization are all constrained by the platform. As ad spend increases, a slower site will cost more per click and convert fewer of the visitors that the ads are buying. This is a platform risk that compounds over time.
The Positive
  • Recently redesigned (May 2026) — clean, modern appearance with a clear headline focused on injured Florida workers and a compelling "You matter. We've got you." brand message
  • Attorney bio is strong — Michelle's 10+ years of experience, former prosecutor background, and no-win no-fee positioning are trust-builders that give prospective clients a reason to choose her over larger, more anonymous firms
The Gap
  • A site that loads slowly on mobile bleeds Quality Score on every Google Ad click — the firm is paying more per visitor because the platform underperforms, and those visitors are more likely to bounce before contacting the firm
  • Every day the contact form stays buried behind a button click, visitors who arrived with intent leave without converting — a form embedded above the fold on the homepage is one of the highest-ROI changes possible without spending an additional dollar on ads
  • The absence of video means the site relies entirely on text to build trust with clients facing one of the most stressful situations of their lives — competitors who feature attorney video outperform text-only sites in time-on-page and conversion rate
A website that converts at a higher rate means more clients from the same ad budget — and more income reaching Portugal without increasing what the firm spends to get there.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Google Business Profile has been stuck in verification since the firm launched in April 2026 — Chase Injury Law does not appear in any local 3-pack search result across six tested queries covering workers' comp and personal injury in Fort Lauderdale, Pompano Beach, and Broward County
  • Zero Google reviews — competitors in the same 3-pack positions hold 75 to 1,696 reviews; without a live GBP, the firm cannot collect a single review regardless of client satisfaction
  • NAP inconsistency: the firm's registered address is Pompano Beach (2335 E Atlantic Blvd Ste 200) but all marketing language and the transcript reference Fort Lauderdale — this split signal suppresses visibility in both cities and confuses Google's local ranking algorithm
  • Not listed on Expertise.com's Pompano Beach workers' compensation attorney rankings — competitors including Lawlor White Murphey (161 reviews, 5.0 stars) and Katz & Katz P.A. (75 reviews, 5.0 stars) appear on the list
Blind Spot: Until the GBP is verified, the firm cannot run Local Service Ads — which appear above every Google Ad and every organic result at the top of the page. Every day the verification is unresolved is a day the firm is locked out of the highest-converting paid position on Google while competitors collect every click from it.
The Positive
  • Geo-targeting landing pages exist for 12 Florida service areas including Miami, Tampa, and Orlando — the structural intent to compete statewide is already in place, even if the content needs strengthening
The Gap
  • An unverified GBP is not just a missed opportunity — it is the single gating factor blocking every other local search improvement; resolving it unlocks 3-pack eligibility, LSA eligibility, and review collection simultaneously
  • Competitors with 100+ reviews have a Google trust signal that takes months of consistent collection to approach — every month without a live GBP widens the review gap further and makes future catch-up more expensive
Anidjar & Levine — 1,696 Google reviews at 4.8 stars, confirmed in the top 3-pack position for "personal injury attorney Fort Lauderdale," "workers compensation attorney Fort Lauderdale," and "work injury lawyer Fort Lauderdale FL." They hold every local search position Chase Injury Law needs to win.
Local SEO is the channel most aligned with location independence — a verified GBP and strong review base generates inbound calls without requiring the owner to be present, visible, or in the same time zone.

Blind Spot: The previous vendor's failure was not that Google Ads do not work for this firm — it is that the wrong keywords were targeted. Health insurance searchers are not workers' compensation clients. Switching to intent-matched keywords on the same platform could transform a campaign that produces zero results into one that produces consistent qualified leads without increasing spend.
