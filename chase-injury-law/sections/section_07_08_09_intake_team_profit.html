Intake Assessment

Chase Injury Law has no intake system — every prospective client who calls or submits a form is relying on Michelle personally being available in real time, from Portugal, during Florida business hours. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Michelle personally handles every intake call — there is no one else in the firm to answer the phone, screen prospects, or move a lead through the intake process when she is unavailable
  • Operating from Portugal (EST +6 hours) creates a structural after-hours gap during Florida's peak inquiry window; a prospect calling at 5 PM Fort Lauderdale time is calling at 11 PM Portugal time
  • No defined intake process, qualification script, or follow-up sequence — there is no system to ensure that a lead who does not convert immediately is contacted again within 24 hours
  • No after-hours coverage mechanism — calls, form submissions, and website inquiries received outside Michelle's available hours have no pathway to a response until she manually addresses them
Blind Spot: Studies on legal intake consistently show that law firms lose more than 40% of leads simply from delayed follow-up — a prospect who submits a workers' comp inquiry will call multiple attorneys simultaneously and sign with whoever responds first. In a market where Anidjar & Levine explicitly advertises "24/7 availability," every hour Chase Injury Law takes to respond is a client that competitor signs instead.
The Gap
  • Every qualified lead that cannot reach Michelle — due to time zone, availability, or lack of follow-up process — represents a potential $25,000 workers' comp case walking out the door to a competitor who answered faster
  • As marketing spend increases and lead volume grows, the intake bottleneck will become the primary limiting factor on revenue — more leads will not help if the system to convert them is not in place first
  • Without intake tracking, there is no way to measure how many leads are being lost or identify where in the process they are dropping off — the firm is operating without visibility into its most immediate revenue lever
An intake system that qualifies, follows up, and books consultations without Michelle personally on every call is the operational foundation that makes location-independent income possible — it is not a nice-to-have, it is what makes this model work.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • The contact form is not embedded on the homepage — visitors must click through a CTA button to access it, which reduces form completion rates among high-intent prospects who expect immediate access
  • No confirmation message or follow-up sequence is observable from the site — a prospect who submits the form receives no indication of when or how they will be contacted, creating uncertainty that increases the likelihood they will also contact a competitor
  • No appointment booking tool is present — giving prospects the ability to schedule a consultation directly from the site removes a friction point that causes many leads to delay or abandon the process entirely

These findings are also referenced in the Website Audit section.

Team Assessment

Chase Injury Law is a one-person operation — Michelle is the firm. Every client interaction, every case decision, every intake call, and every operational task runs through her personally, from a different continent and a six-hour time difference. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Zero staff — no paralegal, no intake coordinator, no virtual assistant; the firm's capacity to serve clients and respond to new leads is limited entirely by Michelle's personal bandwidth
  • All client communication flows through one person operating across a six-hour time zone gap — there is no coverage structure, no delegation, and no redundancy if Michelle is unavailable for any reason
  • No defined roles, org chart, or accountability framework — without these, hiring even a first team member becomes difficult because there is no structured position to hire into
  • The location-independence model this firm is built around requires a team member who can represent the firm locally — handling calls, attending to time-sensitive Florida business, and ensuring no lead is lost due to unavailability
Blind Spot: The assumption that a solo operation can scale to $250K in year one while the owner is six time zones away significantly underestimates what intake and client service require. Workers' compensation clients are often in crisis — they have been injured, their checks have stopped, their medical care has been delayed. They need timely, reassuring human contact. That contact cannot consistently come from someone operating at 11 PM Portugal time.
The Gap
  • Without a team member handling intake and client communication, every dollar spent on advertising will generate leads that the firm lacks the capacity to respond to promptly — undermining the entire marketing investment
  • As long as Michelle is the only person in the firm, her personal availability is the ceiling on the firm's revenue — that ceiling does not allow the firm to reach its $250K year-one target or its $1M+ long-term goal
  • Hiring without systems is hiring into chaos — the first 90 days of building a team must also include building the intake scripts, role definitions, and communication protocols that give a new hire the tools to succeed
The path to living in Portugal with income replacing $400K+ runs through a team — because a firm that only works when its owner is working is not a business, it is a job that follows you to a different country.

Profit Plan Assessment

Chase Injury Law launched in April 2026 with three referral cases and is effectively pre-revenue — there is no recurring income stream, no financial model tracking acquisition cost or case profitability, and a hard 12-month deadline to become self-sustaining. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Firm is effectively pre-revenue — three referral cases since April 2026 with no consistent or predictable income flow established; current ad spend is consuming budget with zero return
  • No financial visibility into cost per acquisition, case profitability, or monthly revenue targets — without knowing what a client costs to acquire, there is no way to evaluate whether any marketing channel is working
  • No break-even model in place — Michelle does not yet have a clear picture of how many cases per month are required to cover operating costs, marketing investment, and living expenses in Portugal
  • The 12-month runway creates a specific, non-negotiable financial deadline: if the firm does not reach a defined monthly case target within that window, the financial sustainability of the Portugal model is at risk
Blind Spot: Workers' compensation cases in Florida often have settlement timelines of 6–18 months from retained to resolved — meaning cases signed today may not generate revenue until well into 2027. A firm targeting $250K in year-one revenue needs to be signing cases now in order to receive settlements within the runway window. Every month without a working lead system pushes the revenue timeline further out.
The Gap
  • Without a break-even model, every marketing decision is made in the dark — the firm cannot evaluate whether its current ad spend is appropriate, insufficient, or excessive relative to what the revenue goal requires
  • The $250K year-one goal and $1M+ long-term profit vision are stated targets, but neither has been translated into a monthly case count, a cost-per-acquisition ceiling, or a revenue milestone timeline — until those numbers exist, the goal is an aspiration rather than a plan
  • Operating expenses plus SMB Team services plus ad spend must be covered before profit is realized — building a clear picture of these costs against projected case revenue is the first step toward knowing whether the plan is viable
The income that replaces $400K+ and sustains life in Portugal is not discovered at year-end — it is engineered from the first week with a model that shows exactly how many cases are needed, at what average value, to hit the number that makes this life possible.
