Lead Generation

Keeling Gutierrez is spending $6–7k every month on Google LSA and PPC with no attribution, no call tracking, and no way to know which campaigns are generating cases — while Allmand Law, a six-month-old Houston presence backed by an exclusive agency, is running a coordinated multi-channel strategy that is eating into the firm's direct mail leads and outranking it on every search term it is paying to compete on. Until the firm can measure what is working, it cannot grow faster — it can only spend more.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Blog last updated October 8, 2025 — seven months without a new post signals low activity to Google and weakens organic ranking potential over time
  • PageSpeed scores were unavailable at time of audit due to a rate-limiting issue — these should be manually verified at pagespeed.web.dev; a slow mobile experience is one of the most common reasons bankruptcy prospects abandon a website before calling
  • The October 2025 rebrand from "Keeling Law" to "Keeling Gutierrez Debt Relief Attorneys" left two active Yelp listings under different names and YellowPages still showing the old firm name — citation inconsistency that works against the firm in local search
  • No evidence of attorney video content — a significant missed trust-building opportunity in a practice area where prospects are emotionally vulnerable and slow to commit
Blind Spot: Competitors may be bidding on the firm's own name in Google Ads right now. When someone who heard of Keeling Gutierrez types the firm's name into Google, they could be seeing a competitor's ad before they see the firm's own listing — and many would click it without knowing the difference.
The Positive
  • Free consultation CTA is confirmed present and prominent — "Call for a free consultation: 713-686-2222" — giving prospects a clear next step
  • Dedicated attorney bios exist for Kenneth A. Keeling and Yolanda Gutierrez — building credibility with prospects who research before calling
  • Multiple practice area landing pages and service area pages for 14 Texas counties — a structural SEO advantage that many competitors lack
  • Bilingual capability is reflected in the site content — a real differentiator in Houston's 45% Hispanic market
The Gap
  • A stale blog and inconsistent NAP citations are slowly eroding the local SEO authority the firm has built over 40 years — each month without action compounds the loss
  • Website visitors who do not call are permanently lost — with no retargeting in place, every prospect who leaves without converting disappears completely and cannot be re-engaged
  • Without video content, the firm is missing the single highest-converting trust signal available online — attorneys who appear on video convert at measurably higher rates than those who do not
A website that cannot be measured, cannot be improved — and a firm that cannot improve its web presence cannot protect a 40-year legacy from a well-funded competitor who is doing exactly that.

Local SEO

  • Firm does not appear prominently in the local 3-pack for "bankruptcy attorney Houston" — the highest-value search query in the market — while Baker & Associates and Allmand Law hold consistent positions
  • The October 2025 rebrand split the firm's citation equity between two brand names: Yelp has two active listings (one under "Keeling Law Firm," one under the new name), and YellowPages still shows the old name — every mismatched citation weakens local search authority
Blind Spot: The rebrand from "Keeling Law" to "Keeling Gutierrez Debt Relief Attorneys" effectively reset the firm's citation footprint in October 2025. Google uses NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency as a ranking signal — every directory still showing the old name is splitting the SEO authority that took 40 years to accumulate. This is not a minor cleanup item; it is a structural drag on every local SEO effort the firm makes.
The Positive
  • 4.9-star Google rating with 120 reviews is a strong foundation — the quality of the reputation is there; the volume is the gap
  • Service area landing page structure covering 14 Texas counties gives the firm a geographic SEO footprint that most competitors lack
  • Kenneth A. Keeling's Board Certification in Consumer Bankruptcy from the Texas Board of Legal Specialization is a rare, high-trust credential that can be leveraged in listings, bios, and ad copy
The Gap
  • Without a systematic review generation program, the gap with Allmand Law grows every month — Allmand is accumulating reviews through a managed process while the firm has no formal process in place
  • Inconsistent citations across Yelp and YellowPages are actively working against the firm's local SEO rankings — every dollar spent on ads competes less effectively until citations are cleaned up
Allmand Law — entered Houston just six months ago and already has 586 Google reviews to the firm's 120. With an exclusive marketing agency managing their online presence, they are widening this gap systematically every month. The LSA algorithm rewards review volume — which means every dollar Keeling Gutierrez spends on LSA is returning less visibility than the same dollar spent by Allmand Law.
Closing the review gap is the single highest-leverage action the firm can take right now — it costs nothing per review and compounds in value with every new case the firm signs.

Google Ads

  • No attribution or ROI tracking is in place — the firm cannot identify which keywords, campaigns, or channels are generating case consultations, which means budget decisions are made without data while a competitor operates with full analytics through an exclusive agency
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Allmand Law — running aggressive PPC through TSG Logic (exclusive agency), using a dual-attorney strategy that places ads under both the Allmand and Southward names simultaneously, effectively occupying two paid search positions on the same results page
  • Eric Southward / Houston Heights Bankruptcy — running paid ads through TSG Logic (the same agency as Allmand), meaning both of the firm's most aggressive local competitors are receiving coordinated, data-driven marketing from the same team
The Opportunity
  • "Chapter 7 bankruptcy attorney Houston," "chapter 13 bankruptcy attorney Houston," and "stop wage garnishment Houston" — high-intent, high-value keywords where the firm's 40-year track record gives it real credibility to convert
  • "File bankruptcy fast Houston," "same day bankruptcy filing Houston" — urgency-driven terms that match the firm's newly planned Glade AI Chapter 7 acceleration capability
  • Spanish-language keywords — "abogado de bancarrota Houston," "capítulo 7 bancarrota Houston" — the firm has two Spanish-fluent attorneys and Houston is 45% Hispanic; this is a virtually uncontested search segment that no competitor is targeting aggressively
  • Competitor name terms — prospects who have heard of Allmand Law but haven't chosen yet are searchable, and a well-crafted comparison ad can redirect that research directly to Keeling Gutierrez
Blind Spot: Allmand Law is running ads under two separate attorney names at the same time — Reed Allmand and Eric Southward — which means they can appear twice on the same search results page. This strategy doubles their paid search real estate and makes it structurally harder for any single competitor, including Keeling Gutierrez, to win an impression share against them without a similarly coordinated approach.

  • Kenneth A. Keeling's Board Certification in Consumer Bankruptcy is a Google Screened trust signal that can differentiate the firm's LSA listing from competitors who lack that credential — but only if it is prominently featured and properly set up in the LSA profile
