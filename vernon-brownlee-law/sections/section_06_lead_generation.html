Lead Generation

The Law Office of Vernon Brownlee generates approximately 30 qualified criminal defense leads per month through organic Google search — a strong organic foundation — but a broken mobile website, an absence from high-value murder and serious felony searches, and zero paid advertising on any channel mean the firm is converting only a fraction of the market opportunity that exists in Prince George's County today.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Navigation, phone number, and CTAs intermittently disappear on mobile — a visitor who cannot find a way to call or contact the firm will call a competitor within seconds
  • PageSpeed scores: Not available for this report — manual run required before presentation. WordPress site confirmed to carry unused JS/CSS inflating page weight and degrading mobile load time; performance is likely in the poor-to-needs-improvement range based on observed technical issues
  • Contact form was broken by a prior agency for months — approximately 50 qualified leads went unanswered; current form status requires immediate verification before any other marketing investment makes sense
  • Facebook link in footer is broken — a visible signal of neglect that erodes trust before a prospect reads a single word of copy
  • Hours listed inconsistently across the site and directories — Google weights inconsistent information as a negative trust signal in local rankings
Blind Spot: Every hour the mobile site fails to display a working phone number, someone in Prince George's County who searched for a defense attorney clicks away and calls a competitor. The loss is invisible — there is no notification, no record, and no way to know it happened. The only way to find out is to fix it and watch what changes.
The Positive
  • Approximately 110 indexed pages including geo-targeted city and practice area pages across Prince George's County, Annapolis, Silver Spring, Laurel, Bowie, and Bethesda — a strong structural SEO foundation already in place
  • Attorney biography page is live with strong credentials: University of Baltimore School of Law, former judicial law clerk, former Assistant State's Attorney in PG County misdemeanor and felony units — this backstory builds extraordinary trust when it can be found
The Gap
  • Mobile UX failures are directly responsible for the estimated 2–5 lost cases per month — at $5,000 average case value, that is $10,000–$25,000 in monthly revenue leaving the firm through a broken user experience, invisibly
  • No Core Web Vitals optimization — Google's ranking algorithm penalizes slow, bloated sites and suppresses the very organic rankings the firm already depends on to generate leads
  • Without a persistent phone number and CTA on every mobile page, traffic and rankings become meaningless — the lead has already decided to call someone else before the page finishes loading
A rebuilt, conversion-optimized website is not a cosmetic upgrade — it is the foundation that makes every other marketing investment pay off, and the infrastructure the firm needs to attract the serious felony clients Vernon is building his reputation on.
