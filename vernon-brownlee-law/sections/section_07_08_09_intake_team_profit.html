Intake Assessment

The Law Office of Vernon Brownlee loses an estimated 2–5 signed cases per month not because of a lack of leads, but because the systems that should convert leads into signed clients are broken or missing entirely. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED — Critical Priority
The Problem
  • Contact form was broken by a prior agency for months — approximately 50 qualified leads received zero response during that period; current form integrity requires immediate verification before any marketing investment has a reliable path to ROI
  • No 24/7 answering service — after-hours criminal defense calls go unanswered despite arrests happening evenings and weekends; the firm is effectively closed for the 16 hours per day that produce the most urgent criminal defense inquiries
  • CallRail is configured for call tracking but not connected to Clio Grow — inbound call leads do not auto-create contact records, and follow-up depends entirely on whoever happens to take the call
  • No automated follow-up sequences — prospects who reach voicemail or submit a form receive no immediate text confirmation, no automated callback trigger, and no scheduled follow-up; the decision to hire typically happens within 24–48 hours of an arrest, and silence during that window means the case goes to whoever responded
Blind Spot: Criminal defense leads are among the most time-sensitive in any practice area. When someone is arrested or charged, they are making their hiring decision within hours — not days. Every missed after-hours call is not a delayed conversation; it is almost certainly a signed client for whoever picked up the phone first. There is no second chance in criminal defense intake.
The Positive
  • CallRail and Clio Grow are already in place — the infrastructure exists and just needs to be properly connected and automated; this is a configuration problem, not a procurement problem
  • Vernon's personal close rate is exceptionally strong — 70% of qualified, able-to-pay prospects sign when Vernon gets them on a call; the intake problem is getting leads to that call, not closing them once they are there
The Gap
  • 2–5 cases per month lost to web and intake failures × $5,000 average case value = $10,000–$25,000 in monthly revenue leaving the firm through entirely preventable gaps
  • ~25% of qualified prospects cannot afford fees with no financing option offered — at 7–8 prospects per month, adding LawPay or Synchrony could convert 2–3 additional cases monthly without a single new lead generated
  • After-hours missed calls likely represent 30–40% of all inbound opportunities in criminal defense — the most urgent, highest-converting clients call when they need help, not during business hours
Fixing intake is the single highest-ROI move available to this firm — it turns the reputation and organic presence Vernon has already built into the steady flow of serious cases he's working toward, without spending another dollar on marketing.

Team Assessment

Vernon Brownlee runs a solo practice with one assistant, handling every intake consultation, every strategy session, every case, and every client interaction personally — a model that produces outstanding individual results but creates a hard ceiling on how much the firm can grow. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER — In Progress
The Problem
  • Solo attorney with one assistant — no delegation framework for intake calls, no overflow answering, no trained support capable of managing new client inquiries independently
  • Vernon personally handles every initial consultation — his calendar is the bottleneck; prospects who cannot reach Vernon quickly call a competitor who answers first
  • No virtual reception or answering service active — the firm is unreachable outside business hours despite criminal defense being a practice area with 24/7 demand by nature
  • During a murder trial (such as the Feb 7–8 trial referenced on the call), intake likely goes dark entirely — the firm's most visible court moments coincide with its most invisible intake gaps
Blind Spot: The single assistant is one departure away from a team of zero. There is no documented intake process, no redundancy, and no ability for the firm to function at normal capacity if Vernon or his assistant are unavailable — even briefly. The firm's entire operational continuity rests on two people.
The Positive
  • One dedicated assistant provides some operational capacity — the team is not at zero, and a foundation for delegation exists
  • Vernon is already evaluating virtual reception options (Smith.ai, Lex Reception) — he understands the gap and is ready to address it; the solution is identified, it just needs to be implemented
The Gap
  • Without 24/7 answering coverage, the firm is effectively closed for the 16 hours per day that produce the most urgent criminal defense calls — and the firm's local ranking reflects it
  • No intake delegation means Vernon's time in court competes directly with intake responsiveness — the more serious the trial, the darker the intake window becomes
  • Growth beyond the current volume ceiling requires either intake automation or additional staff — currently neither is in place, and the ceiling is real
Building the right intake support infrastructure allows Vernon to be fully present in the serious trial work that defines this practice — knowing that every new inquiry is being handled professionally behind him. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Profit Plan Assessment

The Law Office of Vernon Brownlee generates an estimated $600,000–$720,000 annually with a solo attorney and one assistant — strong revenue for the structure — but approximately 25% of qualified leads leave unconverted due to affordability, there are no financing options offered, and no formal financial tracking system was referenced on the call. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER — In Progress
The Problem
  • No client financing options offered — approximately 7–8 qualified leads per month cite cost as the barrier to signing; at $5,000 average case value, these represent $35,000–$40,000 in monthly unconverted revenue that could be partially recovered with LawPay, Synchrony, or Affirm
  • Revenue of $600K–$720K is being generated without a clear profit plan — growth in case volume could mean significantly more work without meaningfully more take-home income if margins are not tracked
  • No visibility into cost per acquisition — without knowing what it costs to generate a signed client, marketing investment decisions are made by instinct rather than math, which is why two years of prior agency fees produced unclear ROI
  • Prior agency fees of $2,000–$2,400 per month delivered results Vernon described as unsatisfactory — there was no attribution framework to identify what was working, making the spend effectively unaccountable
Blind Spot: The DUI and misdemeanor cases Vernon is deliberately stepping away from may have been subsidizing the serious felony work financially. Without case-type financial tracking through Clio Manage, it is impossible to know whether narrowing to serious felonies increases or decreases overall profitability — and whether the firm can sustain that shift without a compensating increase in case value or volume.
The Positive
  • Revenue volume ($600K–$720K estimated) and case economics ($5,000 average blended) are strong for a solo practice — the raw material for a high-margin, profitable firm is already present
  • Clio Manage is already in place and supports financial reporting, billing, and case revenue tracking — the infrastructure for financial visibility exists, it just needs to be fully utilized
The Gap
  • The $35,000–$40,000 per month in unconverted revenue from prospects who can't afford fees is the fastest-access profit lever available — adding financing options requires no new leads, no new ads, and no new marketing spend; just a decision to offer it
  • No defined monthly revenue goal or growth plan — Vernon mentioned needing 3–5x ROI on SMB fees, but without an underlying profit plan that measurement cannot be made with confidence
  • Without financial visibility, investment decisions (technology, staffing, advertising) are made reactively — which is the exact pattern that led to two years of unsatisfactory agency relationships
The path to Vernon being Prince George's County's most respected serious felony trial attorney requires a firm financially healthy enough to invest in the positioning, infrastructure, and marketing that builds that reputation — and a profit plan that ensures the firm keeps what it earns. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.
