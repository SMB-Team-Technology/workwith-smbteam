Lead Generation

JJ Law has built a genuine reputation in personal injury law across two states — but reputation alone does not generate the 10 cases per month the firm is targeting, and every channel that could make that happen systematically is either absent in Houston or underperforming in Oklahoma. The firm has the brand, the credentials, and the story to dominate digital search in both markets; what it does not yet have is the machine to make it happen.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed scores could not be verified during research — pagespeed.web.dev returned access errors and scores should be confirmed live before the presentation; mobile performance is assumed to be adequate based on site structure but remains unconfirmed
  • Two active domains — 855getjjlaw.com (primary) and jjacksonlawoffice.com (secondary) — serve the same firm with similar content, splitting domain authority and diluting the SEO value of every backlink, review, and citation the firm has earned
  • NAP inconsistency: an old address (9898 Bissonnet Suite 112) remains indexed in the iBegin directory versus the current address (6944 Cypress Creek Pkwy #200), creating conflicting location signals that suppress local rankings
  • No active blog or content publishing observed — the site has FAQ pages but no ongoing content strategy to capture the long-tail informational searches that educated PI prospects use before they call anyone
Blind Spot: The two-domain problem may feel harmless, but search engines treat each domain as a separate authority score. Every backlink, citation, and directory listing split between 855getjjlaw.com and jjacksonlawoffice.com builds two weak authorities instead of one strong one. The firm's $70M+ recovered reputation and 174+ Google reviews are not fully working for it because authority is divided.
The Positive
  • Geo-targeted landing pages are already built for Houston, Oklahoma City, and Tulsa across multiple practice areas — this is a significant SEO infrastructure advantage that many competing firms have not built; it just needs traffic directed at it
  • Jonathan Jackson's unique story — NFL career, Thurgood Marshall Law School, federal clerkship, "The Sneaker Lawyer" — is present on the site and is a genuine differentiator that no competitor can replicate
The Gap
  • Every link, citation, and review split between two domains is half the ranking authority it should be — consolidating to one domain would immediately improve the primary site's ranking potential with zero new content required
  • Until mobile PageSpeed scores are confirmed, there is no way to know how many of the firm's mobile visitors — the majority of PI search traffic — are abandoning the site before converting
  • Without active content publishing, JJ Law is invisible for the informational searches that come before the call: "what to do after a car accident in Houston," "how long does a PI case take in Texas" — competitors with active blogs are capturing those prospects first
A single, authoritative web presence is the foundation that every other digital channel is built on — right now the firm is building on two foundations that compete with each other instead of one that compounds.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • JJ Law does not appear in the local 3-pack for any core PI search term in Houston — "personal injury lawyer Houston," "car accident lawyer Houston Texas," and "Houston personal injury attorney" are all dominated by Zehl & Associates, Sutliff & Stout, and Arnold & Itkin
  • JJ Law does not appear in the local 3-pack for core PI searches in Oklahoma City either — Cain Law, McIntyre Law (292 reviews, 4.7★), and Tawwater Law hold the visible positions in the market where the firm currently spends its entire digital budget
  • Approximately 174 Google reviews — while Zehl & Associates holds 1,922 reviews (11x the social proof) and Cain Law holds 355 reviews (2x the social proof); the firm is 26 reviews away from the 200-review threshold that triggers measurable improvement in local pack visibility
Blind Spot: The NAP inconsistency — old address still indexed in the iBegin directory, and two active domains — is actively suppressing the firm's local pack rankings in both markets. Google uses consistent NAP signals as a trust indicator for local rankings. Every conflicting citation tells Google this firm's information is unreliable, and Google resolves uncertainty by ranking firms it trusts more — which right now means every competitor with clean citation data.
The Positive
  • The firm's geo-targeted landing pages for Houston, Oklahoma City, and Tulsa practice areas are a meaningful SEO head start — when rankings improve, these pages will begin capturing organic PI traffic without requiring additional content work
The Gap
  • Being invisible in the local 3-pack means competitors are getting hundreds of free organic clicks per month for Houston and OKC PI searches that JJ Law cannot reach — these are cases that cost competitors nothing to acquire
  • The firm is 26 reviews away from 200 — a structured review generation outreach to its 260 active cases and satisfied past clients could close this gap within weeks and immediately improve LSA and local pack ranking signals without spending a dollar on ads
Zehl & Associates — 1,922 Google reviews at 4.9★, dominating the Houston PI 3-pack for every major search term while JJ Law's Houston presence relies entirely on referrals. Zehl's review volume alone makes them nearly impossible to displace from the map pack without a sustained review generation and local SEO campaign.
Local SEO is the only channel where appearing at the top of Google costs nothing per click — but right now every competitor is taking those free clicks while JJ Law stays invisible in both of its primary markets.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • JJ Law does not appear to be running Google search ads in Houston — the firm's primary and largest market has zero paid digital presence while major PI competitors run campaigns spending an estimated $25,000–$75,000+ per month in this market
  • In Oklahoma City, the firm is running approximately $10.5k/month in total digital spend through a third-party agency that includes some PPC — but results are unclear, the firm is not appearing in the local 3-pack, and attribution data has not been confirmed
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Sutliff & Stout (Houston) — Board-Certified Personal Injury Trial Law firm running aggressive Houston PPC bidding consistently at the top of paid results; 13 consecutive years of top-ranked recognition in Houston PI
  • Arnold & Itkin LLP (Houston) — nationally recognized firm with a reported $25B+ recovered, running one of the largest PI ad budgets in the Houston market; dominates both paid and organic search with broad and deep keyword coverage
  • Cain Law Office (Oklahoma City) — confirmed active Google Ads presence in OKC capturing PI leads with professionally managed campaigns while JJ Law's digital budget in the same market is split across multiple channels and vendors
The Opportunity
  • Houston PI search — "car accident lawyer Houston," "personal injury attorney Houston," "truck accident lawyer Houston" — currently uncontested by JJ Law while competitors spend six figures per month; a properly managed $10,000–$15,000 monthly Houston campaign would put the firm in front of high-intent prospects for the first time in its home market
  • Oklahoma City consolidation — replacing the current fragmented agency-managed spend with a professionally managed SMB Team campaign targeting the highest-ROI PI keywords across OKC and Tulsa, with clear attribution showing cost per signed case
  • Spanish-language campaigns — "Se habla español" is confirmed on the website and Houston has a 45%+ Spanish-speaking population; Spanish-language PI clicks cost significantly less than English-language equivalents while competing for a high-value, underserved segment that aligns perfectly with the firm's existing positioning
  • Competitor name bidding — appearing when prospects search for Zehl & Associates, Sutliff & Stout, or Cain Law by name captures comparison-shoppers at the exact moment they are evaluating options
Blind Spot: The firm's $10.5k/month Oklahoma City digital spend through an outside agency may be producing fewer results than expected because it is split across LSAs, PPC, and radio without a clear attribution framework. The same budget, properly consolidated and managed with transparent reporting, could produce meaningfully more signed cases from the same investment.

Local Service Ads

Competitor LSA Landscape

  • Cain Law Office (Oklahoma City) — active LSA presence with 355 reviews powering their visibility in OKC; consistently appearing in the LSA pack for PI searches in the market where JJ Law is spending its digital budget
The Opportunity

  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • The 26-review gap to 200 reviews is the single fastest improvement available to LSA performance: a structured review outreach to the firm's 260 active cases could close this gap in weeks, immediately improving LSA ranking in both Houston and Oklahoma City without requiring additional ad spend

This is not a long-term initiative; it is a 30-day sprint that requires nothing more than a structured outreach to current and past clients asking for a Google review.
