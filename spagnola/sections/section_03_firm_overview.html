Here Is Where The Spagnola Law Firm Stands Today

Samuel Spagnola is a Board Certified Family Law Specialist in Greensboro, NC, with over 35 years of practice and recognition as a North Carolina Top 100 Family Lawyer every year from 2022–2026 — a level of credential most attorneys never achieve. He runs the firm as a solo practitioner at a $400,000 annual ceiling, and the single most important finding from this audit is this: the firm's growth has stalled not because the market is too competitive, but because every lead, every consult, and every case runs through Samuel personally — and that structure cannot scale.

Stage 6 is the stage where the firm runs without you — where revenue is no longer tied to your hours, and the business you have spent 35 years building finally earns for you, whether you are working or not.

Where You Are Today

Stage 4: Small Business Manager
  • Running LSA at $3,000/month but personally managing it — generating 21 leads per 2 weeks with only 1 consult booked
  • No intake process, no intake team — Samuel fields every inquiry himself alongside his full caseload
  • Capped at approximately 4 contested cases per month and $400,000 annually because there are no more hours left
  • Cannot take time off — any absence directly halts revenue and disrupts active client matters

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between where you are and where you want to be is not about your credentials or your reputation — it is about building the systems and the team that let the firm work for you instead of the other way around.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
The Spagnola Law Firm
Samuel is operating at Stage 4 — a Small Business Manager who has invested in digital lead generation but has not yet built the intake and team infrastructure to convert that investment into consistent growth. The single most urgent gap is intake: a 5% LSA-to-consult conversion rate and no intake team means the firm is paying for leads it cannot catch. Until a trained intake coordinator handles consult scheduling and follow-up, the $1 million goal Samuel has stated cannot be reached — not because the leads aren't there, but because there is no system to turn them into cases.
