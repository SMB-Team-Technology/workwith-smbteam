Lead Generation

The Spagnola Law Firm is currently spending $6,000–$7,000 per month on digital marketing through Matador and self-managed LSA — but one channel is underperforming severely, one has been dormant for two years, and one has never been activated at all. The result is a firm paying for a system that is capturing a fraction of the available market.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • No contact form anywhere on the website — the only conversion path is a phone call, which means any visitor who is not ready to call immediately has no way to engage and will likely leave and call a competitor
  • PageSpeed scores could not be retrieved during this research session due to API rate limits — actual mobile and desktop scores must be manually verified at pagespeed.web.dev before the audit report is finalized
  • No video content on the site — attorney video is one of the strongest trust-building tools available, and Samuel's 35+ years of experience and board certification are credentials that would convert well in video format
  • Geographic coverage pages exist for Greensboro and High Point but not for Burlington, Winston-Salem, or Kernersville — cities the firm serves per the transcript
Blind Spot: The phone-only contact model means the website is not a lead capture tool — it is a brochure. Every prospect who arrives after hours, on a weekend, or who simply is not ready to call yet has zero way to raise their hand. Competitors like Robertson with Clio booking are capturing those leads 24/7.
The Positive
  • Modern, professionally designed website with a clear headline ("Greensboro Family Law Attorney") and a strong attorney bio that communicates Samuel's board certification and 35+ year history
  • Dedicated geo-landing pages for Greensboro (multiple practice areas) and High Point (8 practice area pages) — a structural SEO investment Matador has built that provides geographic targeting many solo practitioners lack
  • Active blog with recent content (June 2026 post confirmed) — providing ongoing organic SEO signal
The Gap
  • No contact form means the website cannot generate leads outside of business hours — a critical gap when Robertson is open 6:30 AM to 9:00 PM seven days a week
  • No video and no social proof format beyond text reviews means the site is not competing with firms that use attorney video to pre-sell prospects before the first call
  • Missing geo pages for Burlington, Winston-Salem, and Kernersville means Triad-area organic search traffic from those markets is going to competitors by default
A website that converts visitors into leads — 24 hours a day, seven days a week — is one of the most important infrastructure pieces Samuel needs to build a firm that generates revenue without his constant presence.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • 121 Google reviews at 4.5 stars vs. Kreider's 648 at 5.0 stars — a 5x review gap that directly suppresses local pack rankings; Google Maps favors volume and recency, and Kreider is winning on both
  • GMB is not set to "open 24/7" — confirmed by Samuel on the call as a visibility problem; when prospects search for a family law attorney on a Friday night or Saturday morning, this firm's GMB listing loses algorithmic priority during those peak emotional search moments
  • NAP inconsistency across major directories: three different phone numbers appear on FindLaw/LawInfo (336-790-1764), Avvo (336-814-2904), and BBB/website (336-373-8469) — fragmenting Google's entity verification and suppressing local authority
  • No confirmed active GBP posts — a missed ranking signal that competitors with content teams are consistently activating
Blind Spot: The three phone number variants across directories are likely the result of call tracking numbers from different providers over the years — each one never updated when the tracking number changed. Google sees inconsistent contact information and lowers its confidence in the listing, which directly reduces local pack visibility. This is fixable in a matter of weeks and has no ongoing cost.
The Positive
  • GBP is claimed and active with at least 11 photos confirmed; 4.5-star rating with 121 reviews represents genuine social proof and a foundation to build from
  • Listed in every major legal directory — Avvo 10.0 Superb rating, Martindale Distinguished, Super Lawyers 2022–2026, Top 100 Family Lawyers in NC — creating a strong citation profile that supports overall authority
The Gap
  • At the current review acquisition pace, the gap with Kreider (648 reviews) will not close on its own — without a systematic review generation program, Kreider's structural maps advantage compounds every month
  • NAP inconsistency across FindLaw, Avvo, and BBB suppresses local search authority; every month without a fix is a month of wasted organic ranking potential
Kreider Attorneys at Law — 648 Google reviews at 5.0 stars, active blog (posts dated June 23, 2026), and a multi-practice digital presence that gives them dominant local pack positioning. Kreider is not just winning on reviews — they are winning on content velocity, review recency, and GMB activity simultaneously.
Closing the review gap and fixing NAP inconsistencies are foundational to every other lead generation effort — because every ad campaign, every blog post, and every SEO investment performs better when the local listing signals are clean and authoritative.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • The Spagnola Law Firm does not appear to be running Google search ads for divorce attorney, family law attorney, or child custody terms in Greensboro — consistent with Samuel's confirmation on the call that PPC has not been active for approximately two years
  • No ads were observed for High Point family law searches either — the geographic expansion opportunity to that market is currently unaddressed in paid search
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Kreider Attorneys at Law — marketing posture (648 reviews, active blog through June 2026, multi-practice scope) strongly suggests active PPC investment; their conversion-optimized digital presence is consistent with firms running aggressive Google Ads campaigns
  • Law Office of Stephen E. Robertson — website structure with Clio booking integration, 7-day hours, and multilingual capability indicates a firm optimized for paid traffic conversion; PPC is likely part of their lead acquisition stack
The Opportunity
  • Targeted contested divorce and custody campaigns using HHI (household income) demographic targeting — exactly the high-value case profile Samuel specified on the call, with negative keywords for low-fee and child support-only matters
  • High Point campaign targeting — the firm has 8 dedicated geo-pages for High Point but no paid ads amplifying them; a targeted High Point campaign faces less competition than primary Greensboro searches
  • Competitor conquest campaigns — bidding on searches like "Kreider Law attorney review" or "Woodruff Family Law alternative" captures prospects who are researching but have not yet committed to a firm
  • Evening and weekend ad scheduling — the same hours where Samuel's GMB visibility drops are peak emotional search windows for family law prospects facing weekend custody disputes or just-served divorce papers; paid ads can fill the visibility gap
Blind Spot: Samuel's stated skepticism about PPC is based on a prior campaign that ran without HHI targeting, negative keywords, or conversion optimization. The version of PPC that failed two years ago is not the version being proposed — a properly structured family law campaign with household income targeting, tight keyword intent, and an optimized landing page is a fundamentally different product.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • The firm is currently running Google LSA at approximately $3,000/month, self-managed by Samuel — confirmed active on the call
  • LSA conversion rate: approximately 21 leads per 2 weeks with 1 consult booked — a roughly 5% LSA-to-consult rate that Samuel himself identified as poor; industry-performing LSA programs convert at 20–30% to consult
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Kreider Attorneys at Law — 648 Google reviews at 5.0 stars gives them the strongest LSA ranking signals in the Greensboro family law market; their ad likely appears first and gets the most impressions
  • Law Office of Stephen E. Robertson — 262 reviews at 4.8 stars and 7-day response hours means faster LSA response times, which Google's algorithm rewards with higher placement and more leads
The Opportunity
  • Professional LSA management — improving bid strategy, response time protocols, and lead quality filtering — could realistically move conversion from 5% to 20%+ on the same $3,000/month spend, turning 1 consult per 2 weeks into 4–6
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • Accelerating Google reviews toward Kreider/Robertson levels is also an LSA optimization strategy — LSA ranking is directly tied to review count and rating, meaning every new review improves LSA visibility at no additional ad cost
Blind Spot: The current Answering Legal service handles overflow calls but cannot close paid consultation bookings — it can only take a message. That means even the 5% of LSA leads who call back may not be successfully converted to a paid consult if they reach the answering service instead of Samuel. The conversion bottleneck is not just lead volume — it is the entire response and close process.
