Pillar Health Scorecard

Lead Generation

  • All growth to date is referral-only, with 60–70% of revenue concentrated in just 1–2 client relationships — a single departure could erase $540,000+ in annual revenue overnight
  • No confirmed Google Business Profile, no Google Ads, no Local Service Ads, and no Meta Ads across any of the firm's three practice areas or four geographic markets
  • Competitor The Epstein Law Firm has 169 Google reviews while Kreiner Burns has no confirmed GBP presence — a gap that takes 12–24 months to close even with an active campaign starting today
Without a predictable lead generation system, the $2 million goal and the associate hires that come with it remain entirely contingent on whether the right referrals happen to arrive.

Intake

  • Website contact form leads are routing to an unconnected third party who does not forward them — an unknown number of qualified prospects have already been lost with no awareness
  • Partners handle all intake personally with no formal process — no intake script, no qualification criteria, no defined follow-up sequence
  • No after-hours coverage and no scheduling widget on the website — prospects who cannot reach someone during business hours have no path to engagement
Every untracked form submission and every delayed partner follow-up is a qualified commercial or employment client who found a competitor instead — revenue already being generated and being lost.

Self-Managing Team

  • 8 attorneys across 4 states is a meaningful structural foundation — but all intake, business development, and firm management decisions still flow through the founding partners
  • No dedicated intake, marketing, or operations staff identified — all client-facing and administrative responsibilities run through Seth, Peter, and Tom
  • Without defined accountability structures, adding the associates needed to reach $2 million will increase partner workload rather than reduce it
Kreiner Burns has the attorney headcount to scale — but without an accountability infrastructure, adding revenue just means more work for the partners, not more freedom.

Profit

  • Revenue has grown from $700,000 to $900,000 — real momentum — but 60–70% concentration in 1–2 clients makes that progress as fragile as a single client relationship
  • No profit margin, overhead breakdown, or cost-per-acquisition data was available — the firm does not yet have the financial visibility to plan growth investments confidently
  • The $2 million revenue goal is specific and well-articulated, but the financial roadmap — which practice areas, which markets, what cost structure — has not been documented
Without financial visibility and a documented path from $900K to $2M, the revenue goal is a destination without directions — and the firm cannot make confident hiring or investment decisions to get there.
