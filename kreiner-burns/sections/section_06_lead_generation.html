Lead Generation

Kreiner Burns has built a $900,000 practice on the strength of referrals alone — a testament to the firm's legal reputation, but a fragile foundation for a firm targeting $2 million. Across every digital channel — Google Ads, Local Service Ads, Meta, SEO, and directory presence — the firm has no confirmed footprint while competitors compound their online authority month after month.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Contact form is buried in the footer — no lead capture above the fold; visitors who are not ready to call leave without converting
  • PageSpeed mobile and desktop scores could not be obtained at time of research (Google PageSpeed API rate limited); scores should be verified live before the client meeting
  • No blog and no video content — two of the highest-performing SEO and trust-building assets for law firms competing in multi-practice-area markets
  • No city-specific landing pages for any of the firm's four markets — the site cannot rank for "commercial litigation attorney Yardley PA," "employment lawyer Hoboken NJ," or any other geo-specific searches that convert at the highest rates
Blind Spot: The website's headline — "National Business, Financial, and Litigation Counsel" — is accurate but invisible to local search. Google rewards local specificity, not national positioning. Every practice area search in the firm's four markets is rewarding locally-optimized competitors, not Kreiner Burns.
The Positive
  • Modern 2025 redesign with clean layout, mobile-responsive navigation, attorney bios with photos, client testimonials, and a client logo section — a professionally credible foundation
  • Multiple practice area pages exist covering commercial litigation, employment law, real estate, IP, and general counsel — relevant content is present and can be optimized
The Gap
  • With no form above the fold and no scheduling widget, visitors who arrive via Google or paid ads must actively hunt for the contact mechanism — most will not
  • The 6 on-site client testimonials are invisible to Google — with no pathway to Google reviews, the social proof is trapped on the website and cannot influence local search rankings
  • No city-specific pages means the site ranks for nothing location-specific in Hoboken, Yardley PA, Boca Raton FL, or Baltimore MD — four markets producing zero organic search traffic
A website that converts visitors into consultations is the foundation Kreiner Burns needs before any paid advertising can deliver consistent results at the scale required to reach $2 million.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • No confirmed Google Business Profile presence — the firm does not appear in local 3-pack results for commercial litigation, employment law, or family law in any of its four geographic markets
  • Critical NAP inconsistencies across major directories: three different addresses (Hoboken NJ, Millburn NJ 307 Bloomfield Ave, and 150 Essex St Millburn) and two different firm names ("Kreiner & Kreiner" on Yelp, "Kreiner Burke Burns" on Alignable) — Google's algorithms penalize inconsistent business data and actively suppress local rankings as a result
  • Competitor review gaps: The Epstein Law Firm has 169 Google reviews, Smith Eibeler has 117, Frank P. Marciano has 61 — Kreiner Burns has no confirmed GBP review count, likely under 20 based on complete absence from aggregator data
Blind Spot: The old Yelp profile for "Kreiner & Kreiner" at 150 Essex St, Millburn — the predecessor firm's listing — may be receiving brand searches for the current firm and routing those prospects to a dead profile. Every person searching for Kreiner Burns by name who lands on that page gets a dead end instead of a consultation.
The Positive
  • The website was rebuilt in 2025 and is mobile-responsive — a technically sound foundation that SEO investment can build on, without needing to overcome a legacy platform penalty
The Gap
  • With no confirmed GBP and inconsistent NAP data across LawInfo, Yelp, Alignable, FindLaw, and Super Lawyers, the firm is actively penalized in local search across all four of its geographic markets simultaneously
  • Competitors with 60–169 Google reviews have built review authority that takes 12–24 months to replicate even with an active review generation campaign — every month of inaction extends that timeline
The Epstein Law Firm, P.A. — 169 Google reviews at 4.8 stars, Super Lawyers recognition, and a dominant local search presence in NJ commercial litigation. They have built the kind of Google authority that takes years to accumulate — and they did it while Kreiner Burns was not in the game at all.
Claiming, optimizing, and building a Google presence in each of the firm's four markets is not optional for a firm targeting $2 million — it is the prerequisite for every marketing investment that follows.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Kreiner Burns does not appear to be running Google Search ads for commercial litigation, employment law, or family law keywords in any of its four markets — no firm ads were observed across multiple keyword combinations during research
  • With no paid search presence, the firm is completely absent from the top of Google results pages where high-intent prospects — those actively searching for a commercial or employment attorney — are making their decision
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • The Epstein Law Firm, P.A. — active digital marketing presence across NJ business litigation keywords; confirmed digital investment through Birdeye review data and web authority
  • Genova Burns LLC (Newark, NJ) — established digital presence in NJ employment law; Chambers USA-ranked firm with 28 Google reviews and consistent online visibility across employment and commercial practice areas
The Opportunity
  • "Commercial litigation attorney Hoboken NJ," "breach of contract lawyer New Jersey," "business attorney Yardley PA" — high-intent keywords with zero current Kreiner Burns presence and real competitor gaps in smaller markets like Yardley
  • "Employment attorney employer defense NJ," "workplace discrimination defense New Jersey" — employment law expansion keywords in a growth area where Smith Eibeler is dominant but the market is large enough for a well-funded challenger
  • PA and FL market keywords: "business litigation attorney Yardley PA" and "employment attorney Boca Raton" — smaller markets where competition is lower, CPL is more favorable, and Kreiner Burns' existing office presence gives immediate geographic credibility
  • Competitor conquest targeting: bidding on Genova Burns, Scarinci Hollenbeck, and Smith Eibeler brand keywords captures clients who are already researching alternatives and are one step from signing

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • Kreiner Burns does not appear to be enrolled in Google Local Service Ads for employment law, family law, or business law in any of its four markets — no LSA listing was observed during research
  • The firm's low confirmed review count (likely under 20) would limit immediate LSA performance, but the enrollment and review-building process can begin now so the firm is competitive within 3–6 months
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • The Epstein Law Firm, P.A. — 169 Google reviews makes them a strong candidate for a Google Screened badge and top LSA placement in NJ commercial litigation and business law searches
  • Smith Eibeler LLC — 117 Google reviews and established NJ employment law presence position them as a likely active LSA competitor for the practice area Kreiner Burns most wants to expand into
The Opportunity
  • Employment law, family law, and business law are all Google LSA-eligible practice areas as of 2026 — Kreiner Burns qualifies to enroll across all three simultaneously
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • With offices in four states (NJ, PA, FL, MD), Kreiner Burns can run LSA campaigns across multiple geographic markets simultaneously — a structural advantage most single-office firms cannot replicate
