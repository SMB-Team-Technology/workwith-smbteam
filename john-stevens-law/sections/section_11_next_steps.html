Lead Gen Quick Win
Why It Matters
John Stevens Law has 18 Google reviews. The top local competitor has 4,077. Google review count is the single most heavily weighted signal in local pack and LSA rankings — and this gap is visible to every prospect who compares options before calling.
Who Is Doing It Better
Richard Schwartz & Associates — built 4,077 Google reviews through a systematic post-case review request process. This is an operational procedure, not luck, and it runs after every successful case outcome.

A structured review ask — sent by text and email after every resolved case — can generate 3–5 new Google reviews per month. Reaching 100 reviews within a year materially changes the firm's visibility in local search and LSA rankings across all three practice areas.

Lead Gen Quick Win
Why It Matters
Every high-intent search in Jackson — "personal injury attorney Jackson MS," "car accident lawyer Jackson," "workers comp attorney Jackson" — is currently sending cases to competitors. The firm does not appear to be capturing any of this paid search traffic.

Fix NAP Inconsistency Across Directories
Lead Gen Quick Win
Why It Matters
Two different phone numbers appear across directories — 601-366-1900 on the website, FindLaw, and Justia; and 601-708-9662 on Avvo. Conflicting NAP data tells Google it cannot trust the firm's location information, which actively suppresses local pack rankings — including for searches of the firm's own name.
The Opportunity
Aligning the phone number and contact data across all major directories is a zero-cost local SEO improvement that can take effect within weeks and remove a ranking suppression signal that is currently working against the firm every day.
After-Hours Lead Capture System
Intake Quick Win
Why It Matters
PI and WC prospects call immediately after an injury — evenings, weekends, holidays. A call that goes to voicemail at 7pm frequently becomes a signed case for the competitor who answered first. After-hours calls are some of the highest-intent contacts in legal marketing.
The Opportunity
A virtual intake service available after hours captures, qualifies, and schedules every inbound lead regardless of when it arrives — converting calls that currently go unanswered into cases that show up in the morning already warm and ready to sign.
Intake Follow-Up Protocol
Intake Quick Win
Why It Matters
At a 15% default close rate, 85% of inquiries are not becoming clients. Most of those are not unqualified — they are simply not followed up with systematically. A structured sequence of calls, texts, and emails recovers leads who didn't sign on first contact and keeps the firm top of mind until they are ready to commit.
The Opportunity
Moving the close rate from 15% to 20% on current lead volume adds 1–2 cases per month without spending another dollar on marketing. When paid advertising launches and lead volume doubles, the same protocol scales to protect every new marketing dollar spent.

Team Long-Term Play

The "autopilot and profit" model he described is not possible without a documented accountability structure that lets the team run without him in the room.
Who Is Doing It Better
Richard Schwartz & Associates — operates 7 offices across Mississippi without the founder personally managing daily decisions. Their multi-office scale required building exactly the kind of ops and accountability structure that allows the firm to grow beyond one person's capacity.
The Opportunity
Defining clear roles, decision rights, and accountability for the associate attorney and senior support staff reduces the decisions that land on John and begins building the infrastructure for a firm that runs without him — which is the prerequisite for the retirement plan he described.
Case Type Profitability Analysis
Profit Quick Win
Why It Matters
PI, WC, SSDI, and insurance claims cases have different time requirements, settlement timelines, and fee structures. Without knowing which case types generate the best return on time invested, both case selection and marketing spend allocation are based on habit rather than data.
The Opportunity
A simple analysis of fees earned versus time invested by case type identifies where to focus marketing spend and where to selectively decline cases — shifting the firm's mix toward higher-margin work without adding a single case to the caseload.
