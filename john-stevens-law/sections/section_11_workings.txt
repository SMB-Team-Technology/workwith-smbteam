1. Pillar: Lead Gen | Title: Google Review Generation Campaign | Timeline: Quick Win
   Why: 18 Google reviews vs. competitors with 4,000+. Review count is the
   #1 local ranking signal and LSA ranking factor.
   Competitor: Richard Schwartz & Associates — 4,077 reviews through systematic
   post-case review requests.
   Opportunity: A structured review ask after every successful case outcome
   can add 3–5 reviews per month. 50+ reviews within a year changes the firm's
   visibility in local search dramatically.

2. Pillar: Lead Gen | Title: Launch Google Ads for PI + WC Keywords | Timeline: Quick Win
   Why: Every PI and WC search in Jackson is going to Richard Schwartz, Morgan &
   Morgan, and other competitors. This firm does not appear in paid results.
   Competitor: Morgan & Morgan — national advertiser dominating Jackson paid results.
   Opportunity: Getting into paid search for "personal injury attorney Jackson MS,"
   "car accident lawyer Jackson," and "workers comp attorney Jackson MS" captures
   high-intent clients immediately.

3. Pillar: Lead Gen | Title: Fix NAP Inconsistency Across Directories | Timeline: Quick Win
   Why: Two different phone numbers across directories hurts local SEO trust
   signals and suppresses Google Maps rankings.
   No named competitor (internal fix).
   Opportunity: Aligning NAP data across Avvo, Justia, FindLaw, and GBP sends
   a clean consistency signal to Google and can improve local pack position.

4. Pillar: Intake | Title: After-Hours Lead Capture System | Timeline: Quick Win
   Why: PI and WC prospects often call immediately after an injury — evenings
   and weekends. A call that goes to voicemail at 7pm is a case that signs with
   a competitor by 9am.
   No named competitor (internal process).
   Opportunity: A virtual assistant intake service available after hours can
   capture and qualify every inbound lead regardless of when it arrives —
   converting calls that currently go unanswered into signed cases.

5. Pillar: Intake | Title: Intake Follow-Up Protocol | Timeline: Quick Win
   Why: At a 15% default close rate, 85% of inquiries are not converting.
   A structured follow-up sequence — call, text, email — recovers leads who
   did not sign on first contact.
   No named competitor.
   Opportunity: Even moving close rate from 15% to 20% on current lead volume
   adds 1–2 cases per month without any additional marketing spend.

6. Pillar: Team | Title: Define Team Accountability Structure | Timeline: Long-Term Play
   Why: John is currently the decision point for everything — which means he
   cannot step back without the firm slowing down.
   Competitor: Richard Schwartz & Associates — 7-office operation runs with
   multiple managers; founder is not personally involved in daily decisions.
   Opportunity: Defining clear roles and accountability for the associate and
   senior support staff reduces the decisions that require John's attention
   and begins building the infrastructure for a self-managing firm.

7. Pillar: Profit | Title: Case Type Profitability Analysis | Timeline: Quick Win
   Why: PI, WC, SSDI, and insurance claims have different timelines, effort
   levels, and fee structures. Without knowing which case types produce the
   best return on time, case selection is not strategic.
   No named competitor.
   Opportunity: A simple case-type profitability analysis identifies where the
   firm should focus its marketing spend — and where to say no to cases that
   consume time without generating proportional revenue.

8. Pillar: Lead Gen | Title: Geo-Targeting Landing Pages (Metro Jackson Suburbs) | Timeline: Long-Term Play
   Why: The firm has no pages targeting Ridgeland, Brandon, Pearl, Madison,

   pages targeting every major MS city and suburb.
   Opportunity: 5–7 suburb-specific landing pages targeting "personal injury
   attorney Brandon MS," "workers comp lawyer Madison MS," etc. can generate
   organic and paid traffic at lower cost than core Jackson terms.
