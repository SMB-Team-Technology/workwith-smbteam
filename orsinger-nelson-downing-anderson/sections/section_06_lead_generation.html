Lead Generation

Jeff Anderson has spent 30 years building one of the strongest reputations in DFW family law — but his individual practice is now generating leads almost entirely from a declining organic search profile, with no paid advertising running and a Google review count that is effectively keeping him out of the local map pack for the searches his ideal clients conduct every day. The credibility is unmatched; the digital system to turn that credibility into a consistent flow of high-value clients has been failing.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed scores: Unable to obtain — Google's API returned HTTP 429 errors on multiple attempts and the web UI did not load results; manual verification at pagespeed.web.dev is strongly recommended before the audit call
  • Site was redesigned approximately one year ago on WordPress — without dedicated maintenance and performance optimization, WordPress sites accumulate technical debt quickly and often suppress organic rankings below what the site's authority level should produce
  • Contact form is buried on a separate page (/contact-us/) and is not visible above the fold — requiring an extra navigation step for converting visitors at the moment they are most likely to take action
  • No video content detected on the homepage — a critical trust-building gap for high-stakes family law clients who are making decisions about who will handle some of the most important moments of their lives
Blind Spot: The primary site (JeffAndersonFamilyLaw.com) and the ONDA firm site (ondafamilylaw.com) list different suite numbers for the same Frisco building — Suite 210 vs. Suite 200 at 2600 Network Blvd. This NAP inconsistency is invisible from inside the firm but is actively suppressing rankings across both properties, and it is compounded by different phone numbers listed for the same physical locations across platforms.
The Positive
  • Attorney bio is comprehensive — 30+ years of experience, board certifications, and 19 consecutive years of D Magazine Best Lawyers recognition build immediate credibility for high-stakes family law clients the moment they land on the page
  • Above-fold CTA with a prominent phone number and "Schedule Appointment" button provides a clear conversion path for visitors who arrive ready to contact the firm
The Gap
  • Without an above-fold contact form or embedded scheduling, visitors in the active decision phase must navigate to a separate page — research consistently shows that most conversion decisions happen on the first page view, and every extra step reduces the likelihood of contact
  • The absence of video content is a measurable credibility gap in high-net-worth divorce: clients evaluating a $50,000+ legal matter want to see and hear the attorney before they call — and Jeff's credentials and calm authority are exactly the kind of presence that converts on video
  • WordPress performance issues, if present, will suppress organic rankings and load times — particularly on mobile, where the majority of family law searches now originate — costing Jeff placements he has earned through years of SEO investment
A website that converts sends Jeff the right clients — clients who match his expertise and are ready to engage — rather than just visitors who leave without calling.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Jeff Anderson has only 22 Google reviews at 4.8 stars — the highest average rating in his competitive set, but a volume nearly 10x lower than Coker Robb's 226 reviews, directly suppressing his placement in the Frisco map pack for high-intent divorce and custody searches
  • Organic and Google Maps visibility have been declining for approximately 2.5 years with no recovery — this is not a short-term fluctuation, it is a structural failure that the previous vendor was unable to address
  • NAP inconsistencies across platforms (Suite 210 on JeffAndersonFamilyLaw.com vs. Suite 200 on ONDA/FindLaw, plus different phone numbers across properties for the same locations) are actively fragmenting the local ranking signals that Google uses to determine map pack eligibility
Blind Spot: Jeff's FindLaw profile shows zero reviews, yet FindLaw drives significant referral and direct-search traffic for family law attorneys in DFW. Competitors who actively manage their FindLaw profiles are capturing attorney-referral and direct-search inquiries that Jeff is currently not receiving.
The Positive
  • Jeff Anderson's 4.8-star average rating is the highest in his competitive set — every review added at this rating level carries proportionally more ranking power than reviews added by lower-rated competitors, meaning a review generation campaign here has outsized returns
The Gap
  • Without map pack visibility for "divorce attorney Frisco TX" and "child custody attorney Frisco TX," Jeff is invisible to the highest-intent searchers in his primary market — prospects ready to call an attorney today, who are instead clicking on Coker Robb, Hargrave, and Moore Family Law
  • At 22 reviews vs. Coker Robb's 226, closing even half the gap would produce a measurable shift in map pack position — but no active review generation strategy is currently in place to begin building toward that threshold
Coker, Robb & Cannon — 226 Google reviews at 4.4 stars with a dedicated Frisco office, holding dominant map pack positions for Collin County family law searches while Jeff's 4.8-star average has been suppressed by volume for over two years.
Recovering map pack visibility is the highest-ROI move Jeff's practice can make right now — it directly converts to client inquiries from prospects who are already looking for exactly what he offers, without requiring any additional ad spend.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Jeff Anderson's individual practice does not appear to be running Google Search ads for divorce, custody, or protective order searches in Frisco or Dallas — the previous Google LSA campaign was discontinued after generating many low-quality leads, and no PPC campaigns were mentioned as active on the call
  • With no paid search presence across any practice area or geography, the firm has zero visibility in the paid positions above the map pack and organic results — the positions competitors are actively occupying every day
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Coker, Robb & Cannon — consistent paid search presence inferred from their dominant review volume, multi-office structure, and prominence across Frisco family law search results; their investment in digital marketing extends well beyond organic into paid channels
  • McClure Law Group Dallas — prominent in Dallas divorce attorney searches with PPC-optimized ad copy ("highly acclaimed," "protect your fortune") that speaks directly to the high-net-worth clients Jeff specializes in serving
  • Moore Family Law (Frisco) — "100+ 5 Star Reviews" messaging across Frisco divorce and custody searches is consistent with an active PPC campaign targeting the same board-certified family law market
The Opportunity
  • High-intent keywords such as "board-certified divorce attorney Frisco TX," "complex asset divorce Dallas," and "high-net-worth divorce attorney Collin County" would target exactly the sophisticated clients Jeff serves — and his board certifications give him a genuine competitive advantage in ad copy that competitors cannot match
  • Geographic targeting across Frisco, McKinney, Allen, Plano, and the broader Collin County area captures the clients most likely to hire a Frisco-based attorney for complex divorce matters — without wasting budget on unrelated Dallas metro searches
  • Competitor name targeting: prospects searching for "Coker Robb divorce attorney" or "Moore Family Law Frisco" can be served Jeff's ads before they contact a competitor — this non-obvious opportunity is rarely utilized by family law firms and can intercept high-intent prospects mid-decision
  • Tightly structured negative keywords — filtering out searches for low-cost divorce, self-represented litigants, and price-sensitive terms — directly addresses the poor lead quality that caused the previous LSA experience and would produce materially higher-value inquiries from the start
Blind Spot: The previous LSA campaign failed because of broad targeting and a pay-per-lead model that attracts volume rather than quality — not because paid advertising does not work for Jeff's practice profile. A well-structured Google PPC campaign with tight targeting and negative keywords would produce fundamentally different results than that LSA experience, but the failed LSA is creating hesitancy about paid advertising as a category, which is the real risk going forward.

  • With only 22 Google reviews, Jeff's LSA profile would currently rank below competitors with significantly higher review volumes — review count is the primary ranking signal within the LSA format, meaning re-entry today without first building review volume would replicate the same visibility challenges
Competitor LSA Landscape

  • Albin Oldner Law (Frisco) — board-certified with 66 Google reviews; positioned competitively for Frisco custody and divorce LSA placements with a profile that outpaces Jeff's on volume
The Opportunity

  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • Re-entering LSA after a review-building campaign, with board certification credentials qualifying Jeff as "Google Screened," would position him above competitors who have volume but not his level of formal recognition — creating a paid presence that complements rather than competes with his organic recovery
