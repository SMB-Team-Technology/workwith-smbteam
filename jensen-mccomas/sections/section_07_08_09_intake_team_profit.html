Intake Assessment

With admin staff already supporting multiple attorneys each at max capacity and no dedicated intake role, Jensen & McComas has no consistent way to convert the leads its partner outreach already generates. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
"a non-existent marketing engine and a leaky intake process"
The Problem
  • Admin staff already support multiple attorneys each at max capacity — there is no bandwidth left to build a dedicated intake function
  • No CRM or defined intake tracking process exists today
  • New leads from partner referrals and conferences have no structured, delegated follow-up sequence
  • Kristine is personally building the firm's future intake system in addition to running daily practice — the same instinct that built the firm to $1.2M is now a bottleneck
Blind Spot: Every hour spent building your own intake tool personally is an hour not spent on the $85K corporate matters and $20K estate engagements that actually generate revenue today.
The Positive
  • You have already correctly diagnosed intake — not marketing — as the more urgent constraint, which is rare foresight at this stage
  • An in-house AI intake pilot is already planned for a beta launch by the end of August 2026
The Gap
  • Every referral or conference contact that is not followed up on quickly is a lead the firm already earned and then lost
  • Building a homegrown intake tool without a defined process behind it risks automating a broken workflow instead of fixing it
  • The five planned new hires will inherit today's leaky process unless it is fixed first
The fastest path to your $3M goal is not more leads — it is making sure the ones you already have stop leaking out.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • No contact form is visible on the homepage — it is buried on a separate page, adding friction for a visitor ready to reach out
  • No live chat or immediate-response option exists on the site today
  • Both above-the-fold CTAs route to the About page rather than a contact or consultation-request path

Team Assessment

At 16 people — 6 attorneys, 3 paralegals, and roughly 7 admin and operations staff — Jensen & McComas has real headcount, but no one owns operations, and the managing partner is personally building the firm's next intake system on top of running the practice. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No dedicated ops, marketing, or intake team member has been identified — every new initiative currently runs through the managing partner personally
  • Admins already support multiple attorneys each at max capacity, with no clear plan yet for how two more admins will be onboarded and trained
  • A 30% headcount increase (5 new hires) is planned with no operational structure in place to manage it
  • No leadership layer exists yet between the founding partners and the rest of the 16-person team
Blind Spot: The fact that you are personally building the firm's intake tooling is the clearest sign operations does not have an owner yet — and it is exactly the kind of work that quietly consumes the time you are trying to free up by growing.
The Positive
  • You already have a specific, scoped hiring plan — a senior associate, a junior associate, an additional attorney, and two admins — rather than a vague headcount goal
The Gap
  • Every system built personally by the managing partner is a system that depends on her to maintain it
  • Hiring five people onto an undefined operational structure risks recreating today's capacity problem at a larger scale
  • Without a dedicated operations lead, growth adds work for leadership instead of freedom
Freedom does not come from adding headcount — it comes from adding headcount onto a structure that can absorb it without needing you in the middle of every decision.

Profit Plan Assessment

No profitability, cost-per-case, or client acquisition data was discussed on the call, even as the firm plans to double revenue and add 30% more headcount within a year. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No profitability or margin data was shared for either practice area
  • Corporate (~$85K avg. case value) and Trusts & Estates (~$20K avg. case value) carry very different cost structures and staffing needs, with no visibility into which is more profitable
  • The planned 30% headcount increase has no attached profit or margin plan
  • No client acquisition cost tracking exists, since there is currently no paid marketing to measure against
Blind Spot: Growing revenue and headcount at the same rate can feel like progress while margin quietly erodes underneath it — without a profit plan, you will not know which is happening until it already has.
The Gap
  • Without cost-per-case visibility, the firm cannot tell which practice area is actually funding the other's growth
  • Hiring ahead of a profit plan risks growing the top line while take-home stays flat
  • No acquisition cost benchmark exists to judge whether future marketing spend actually pays off
Reaching $3M only matters if what is left after the new hires, new systems, and new overhead is more than what you keep today.
