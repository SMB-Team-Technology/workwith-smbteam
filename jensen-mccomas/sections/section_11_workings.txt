--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Practice-area proxy mapping: Corporate/M&A → "Business Law" row; Trusts &
Estates → "Estate Planning" row (closest matches in the approved tables).

Methodology note / judgment call: Corporate/M&A work at this firm carries a
transcript-stated $85K average case value and is, per the transcript's own
description of current lead sources, won through partner relationships and
conference networking (Dealmakers, Chambers 100) — not through paid digital
channels. Modeling paid-ad ROI against an $85K corporate case value would
overstate returns dramatically (mechanical run using the blended ~$50K
transcript figure across both practice areas produced return multiples over
50x on a modest ad budget — not credible for a client-facing report). Digital
paid channels are modeled against Trusts & Estates only (the practice area an
individual actually searches for online), using the transcript-stated $20K
average case value. This is a disclosed methodological choice, not a silent
deviation.

Conservative scenario:
- Ad spend: $5,500/mo (Estate Planning row: Google PPC min $3,500 + LSA min
  $2,000; absolute paid-ads floor of $3,000/mo is cleared)
- Blended CPL: PPC weight 63.6% x $135 midpoint + LSA weight 36.4% x $65
  midpoint = $109.60; +20% conservative cushion = ~$131.50
- Leads: $5,500 / $131.50 ≈ 42/mo
- Cases: 42 x 15% default close rate (not stated in transcript) ≈ 6/mo
- Revenue: 6 x $20,000 = ~$120,000/mo
- Return: ~21.8x

Aggressive scenario — 20% rule vs. reverse math:
- 20% rule: $3M revenue goal x 20% / 12 = $50,000/mo x Tier 4 (Baltimore
  region, no Tier 1-3 match) 1.0x multiplier = $50,000/mo; no Spanish
  campaign; minus Growth-tier mgmt fee $7,497/mo = $42,503/mo available ad
  spend if a marketing package were active.
- Reverse math: ($3M / 12) / $20,000 case value = 12.5 cases/mo needed /
  15% close rate = 83.3 leads/mo x $109.60 CPL (no cushion) ≈ $9,133/mo.
- Per instructions, use whichever is higher — mechanically that is the 20%
  rule ($42,503/mo). Flagging per escalation rules ("Revenue goal exceeds
  projected ad revenue → paid ads team review"): $42,503/mo of Estate
  Planning-only paid spend is disproportionate to what a single-office,
  boutique Trusts & Estates practice in Towson/Lutherville-Timonium can
  realistically absorb — running the CPL math forward at that budget implies
  ~390 leads/mo and ~58 cases/mo, far beyond what a 6-attorney firm could
  service. Presenting a market-realistic aggressive figure instead, and
  flagging the 20%-rule/reverse-math gap for sales/paid-ads team review
  before any Phase 2 marketing proposal is finalized.
- Aggressive figure used in the client report: $8,500/mo (adds Estate
  Planning Meta lead-gen budget on top of conservative PPC+LSA; roughly
  1.5x conservative spend — realistic for this market size)
- Blended CPL (no cushion): ~$109.60
- Leads: $8,500 / $109.60 ≈ 78/mo
- Cases: 78 x 15% ≈ 11/mo (rounded down from 11.7 for a conservative display)
