Intake Assessment

OEB Law already has AI chat infrastructure installed sitewide through Intaker, but with the video greeting turned off and no confirmed follow-up process, it's unclear how consistently that infrastructure converts visitors into signed clients. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Intaker's AI chat video greeting is currently turned off, reducing the tool's ability to build trust with visitors before they ever speak to a person
  • The website's "Free Case Evaluation" contact form sits mid-to-lower on the page — the more scrolling required, the more prospects abandon before submitting
  • No documented after-hours or weekend intake process was confirmed on the call
  • No defined follow-up sequence was described for leads who inquire but do not sign immediately
Blind Spot: You may already be paying for a tool — Intaker — that could be capturing more trust and more signed cases today, simply by turning the video greeting back on.
The Positive
  • Intaker AI chat is already installed sitewide — most firms this size have not made this investment yet
  • The "Standing By 24/7" messaging above the fold sets the right expectation, even where the process behind it has not been fully confirmed
The Gap
  • Every visitor who leaves without reaching a live person or a working video greeting is a lead OEB Law already paid to generate — now lost for free
  • Without a defined follow-up cadence, leads who don't convert on first contact may never hear from the firm again
  • An unclear after-hours process risks losing the exact urgent, "just had an accident" moment Personal Injury leads are searching in
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Team Assessment

Marketing and growth decisions at OEB Law currently run through DJ Pendergrass, while Managing Partner Tim Elrod — the firm's ultimate decision maker — is often unreachable due to court commitments. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Tim Elrod, the firm's Managing Partner and final decision maker, is consistently hard to reach due to court commitments — a bottleneck for any decision that needs his sign-off
  • Team size was not confirmed on the call; unverified external directory sources reference "8 attorneys," but this has not been validated directly with the firm
  • No coaching, leadership training, or accountability structure was mentioned as being in place beneath Tim
  • Growth and marketing decisions currently depend on informal alignment between DJ and Tim rather than a defined process
Blind Spot: DJ already handling marketing evaluation on Tim's behalf is a genuine asset — but without a formal system, that delegation depends entirely on DJ's personal bandwidth, not a process that would survive her being out or moving on.
The Positive
  • DJ Pendergrass is already handling day-to-day marketing and operations evaluation — proof the firm is capable of delegating beyond the Managing Partner
  • DJ has already begun connecting Google and review workflows through Intaker, showing a willingness to adopt new systems rather than resist them
The Gap
  • Every decision that requires Tim personally is a decision that waits on a courtroom schedule to clear
  • Without a formal team structure, growth work rests on one person's goodwill and bandwidth rather than a repeatable system
  • The firm cannot scale marketing, intake, or client experience further without first building a leadership layer underneath Tim
Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Profit Plan Assessment

No revenue, case value, or close-rate figures were shared on the call — without this data, OEB Law has no way to know whether its marketing dollars, past or future, are actually profitable. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Annual revenue was not disclosed or discussed on the call
  • Average case value and close rate were not stated — both required to know the actual return on any ad dollar spent
  • No profitability claims were made in either direction, suggesting profit is not currently tracked as a metric distinct from revenue
  • Historical "millions" in TV and billboard spend were never evaluated against the revenue they generated, based on what was discussed
Blind Spot: Without knowing your average case value and close rate, it is currently impossible to know whether the firm's past "millions" in ad spend actually paid for itself — or whether referrals alone would have produced similar results for free.
The Gap
  • Without visibility into case value and close rate, any future ad spend recommendation has to lean on industry defaults instead of OEB Law's actual numbers
  • Not knowing profit margins means revenue growth could just as easily mean growth in stress without growth in take-home pay
  • The firm cannot currently answer the most basic profitability question a growing law firm needs to answer: what does a new case actually cost to acquire, and what does it actually return
Real freedom requires knowing the numbers behind the growth — not just growing and hoping the numbers work out.
