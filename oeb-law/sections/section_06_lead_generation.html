Lead Generation

OEB Law built its reputation over decades of heavy TV and billboard investment under the "wreck into a check" brand — but that paid presence has been dark for roughly six years, leaving referrals and residual brand recall as the firm's only consistent sources of new clients. Meanwhile, digitally-native competitors with a fraction of OEB Law's history have taken over the exact searches where injured Knoxville residents are looking for help right now.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed mobile and desktop scores could not be verified live in this pass (Google's PageSpeed API returned a quota/rate-limit response on repeated attempts) — this should be captured with a direct pagespeed.web.dev check before any performance number is quoted to the client
  • Attorney bios are not visible directly on the homepage — visitors have to click through to a separate "Attorneys" page to see who they would be working with
  • The "Free Case Evaluation" contact form sits mid-to-lower on the page, not visible without scrolling past the full homepage
  • No video content appears on the homepage, despite the firm's own stated interest in a stronger video presence
Blind Spot: Your website footer lists your office as 625 Market Street, 3rd Floor — but the firm's actual office is on the 6th floor of the same building. If this discrepancy is repeated across other directories, it can quietly undermine Google's confidence in your address and hurt local rankings. Past marketing vendors have also made foundational SEO mistakes that likely contribute to OEB Law's current absence from local search — the fixes below are foundational, not cosmetic.
The Positive
  • A clear, benefit-driven headline and a prominent 24/7 call-or-text CTA appear above the fold
  • Intaker AI chat is already installed sitewide — the infrastructure for instant engagement already exists, even where it isn't fully utilized yet
The Gap
  • Every visitor who cannot quickly find the "who you'd be working with" story is a visitor more likely to leave for a competitor's site instead
  • A buried contact form means visitors who do not call directly may abandon the page before ever converting
  • Unresolved page-speed and address-consistency questions could be quietly suppressing organic and local rankings without OEB Law ever seeing it happen
Every visitor the website fails to convert is a case Tim has to make up for somewhere else — usually with his own time.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • OEB Law does not appear in the local 3-pack or first-page organic results for "personal injury lawyer Knoxville TN," "car accident lawyer Knoxville TN," or "truck accident attorney Knoxville TN" — the three highest-intent searches in its own core practice area
  • Google Business Profile shows roughly 105 reviews at 4.5 stars — solid, but far behind the market leader and not something recent review velocity appears to be building on
  • No dedicated landing pages exist for the surrounding East Tennessee markets the firm says it serves — Maryville, Oak Ridge, and Sevierville all have zero OEB Law-specific content
Blind Spot: Strong brand recognition offline does not automatically transfer to Google rankings — Google has no way to "remember" that OEB Law has been a trusted Knoxville name for decades. Every ranking has to be earned again, digitally, from scratch.
The Positive
  • OEB Law's roughly 105 Google reviews at 4.5 stars already outpace at least one named competitor (Hodges, Doughty & Carson, roughly 78 reviews) — the review foundation to build from is already there, and the team has already expressed interest in a structured review-generation program to grow it further
The Gap
  • Every month OEB Law is absent from the local 3-pack is a month Garza Law Firm, NST Law, and Brown and Roberto capture searchers who would otherwise have called OEB Law first
  • Missing city-specific landing pages mean the firm is invisible to prospects searching from Maryville, Oak Ridge, and Sevierville — markets it says it already serves
Garza Law Firm — roughly 1,418 reviews at 4.7–4.8 stars and consistently the top organic result for all three of OEB Law's core Personal Injury search terms in Knoxville.
Local SEO is the channel most likely to bring in cases without Tim lifting a finger — right now, it is bringing in none.

Google Ads

  • Per the discovery call, the firm has pulled back from paid media entirely for roughly six years, after historically spending "millions" on TV, billboards, and advertising
Competitor Ad Landscape

  • Brown and Roberto — heavy authority-based messaging suggests an active, well-funded marketing program worth confirming with a live search
The Opportunity
  • "personal injury lawyer Knoxville TN" and "car accident lawyer Knoxville TN" — the exact terms with the highest local search intent
  • "truck accident attorney Knoxville" and "slip and fall lawyer Knoxville" — case types the firm already handles per directory research
  • Competitor conquesting on "Garza Law Firm," "NST Law," and "Brown and Roberto" — capturing searchers already comparing firms by name
  • Geo-expansion terms for Maryville, Oak Ridge, and Sevierville, where no organic presence currently exists
Blind Spot: Given OEB Law's strong offline brand recall, competitors may already be bidding on "OEB Law" or "wreck into a check" directly — capturing people who are searching for this firm by name. Separately, the goal here is not to outspend national firms like Morgan & Morgan on expensive head-to-head keyword bidding — it is to win the local, review-driven, and Local Service Ads channels below where OEB Law can compete on trust and locality instead.
