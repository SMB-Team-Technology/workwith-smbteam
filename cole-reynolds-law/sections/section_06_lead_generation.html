Lead Generation

Cole Reynolds Law generates every one of its leads through organic "Cincinnati SEO success" — with no paid advertising running on any channel and no local map-pack presence to fall back on. That single-channel dependence leaves the firm exposed to competitors like Kinetic Law and Manley Burke, who are out-publishing and out-reviewing the firm by a wide margin.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Contact form is buried — not present on the homepage, reachable only through CTA buttons or footer links to a dedicated Contact page
  • PageSpeed scores could not be obtained this session — Google's PageSpeed Insights API returned a hard daily quota block on three separate attempts. Real mobile/desktop scores are pending manual confirmation before delivery, not estimated here
  • No live chat and no video content — visitors get no immediate engagement option beyond a buried contact form
  • Blog has not published new content in over 20 months — most recent post dated 11/11/24
Blind Spot: A stale blog signals inactivity to both Google's ranking algorithm and prospective clients actively comparing you against firms that publish regularly — it quietly undercuts the one lead channel the firm depends on entirely.
The Positive
  • Site is mobile responsive with a clear "GET STARTED" call-to-action above the fold
  • Full attorney bios are present, including a five-attorney team page with credentials — a trust signal many competitor sites lack
The Gap
  • Every visitor who can't quickly find a way to reach you is a visitor who calls Kinetic Law next
  • Without confirmed PageSpeed numbers, mobile experience issues could be quietly costing organic rankings on the one channel the firm relies on for 100% of its leads
  • A stale blog undercuts the SEO value of that same channel — fresh content is one of the clearest signals Google rewards for local relevance
Your website should be working as hard as your fee structure already does — right now, it isn't.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Google review footprint is directionally very low — an estimated 5–7 reviews across all platforms, versus Kinetic Law's 36 and Manley Burke's 43
  • Firm did not surface among top-cited results for "business attorney Cincinnati" or "mergers and acquisitions attorney Cincinnati"
  • No geo-targeting landing pages exist for Charleston or Charlotte, despite both being named as active expansion targets
Blind Spot: Without dedicated landing pages for Charleston and Charlotte, any organic momentum you build in those markets has nowhere to send prospects before you actually open there.
The Positive
  • NAP (name, address, phone) is fully consistent across every directory checked — Avvo, Yelp, Birdeye, Super Lawyers, LinkedIn — a foundation many firms get wrong
The Gap
  • Competitors capturing organic visibility for "business attorney Cincinnati" and "M&A attorney Cincinnati" are reaching prospects before they ever find your site
  • A thin review count signals lower trust at the exact moment prospects are comparing you side-by-side with Kinetic Law and Manley Burke
Kinetic Law — surfaces consistently in "startup lawyer Cincinnati" searches with 36 reviews at a 4.7 rating, and is actively publishing client testimonials that the firm's stale blog cannot currently match.
Closing the review and visibility gap with Kinetic Law and Manley Burke means the firm's growth stops depending entirely on people already knowing your name.

  • LSAs are less commonly used by transactional business/M&A attorneys than by PI, family, or real estate practices, so this gap is less diagnostic than it would be for a consumer-facing practice area — but it remains an open, unused opportunity
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Named competitor LSA presence could not be confirmed with available tools this session — flagged for manual live check
The Opportunity
  • A Google Screened badge would add a credibility signal precisely where the firm's thin review count is currently working against it
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • The pay-per-lead model lets the firm test a second lead channel without committing to full ad-spend risk upfront
Blind Spot: Because LSAs are typically underused by business/transactional firms in this market, appearing in even a handful of searches could make Cole Reynolds Law stand out precisely where competitors aren't looking.
