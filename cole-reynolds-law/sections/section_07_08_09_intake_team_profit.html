Intake Assessment

With no support staff of any kind, every inbound inquiry at Cole Reynolds Law lands directly on a partner-attorney's desk alongside their billable deal work. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • No support staff exists to handle intake — attorneys personally field every inquiry
  • No defined intake process, CRM, or lead-tracking system was described on the call
  • No after-hours coverage — the firm's single lead channel can generate an inquiry at any hour, but no process exists to catch it overnight
  • Contact form is buried on a separate page, adding friction before an inquiry is even captured
Blind Spot: Every hour a partner isn't immediately available to respond, an SEO-driven inquiry researching Kinetic Law and Manley Burke in the same search session may sign with whichever firm answers first.
The Gap
  • Every inquiry lost to slow response is a client the firm already invested time and SEO effort to attract
  • With only one lead source, a lost inquiry can't be offset by volume from other channels
  • Partner time spent on manual intake is time not spent on billable M&A and GC work
Every minute you spend personally triaging intake is a minute not spent on deal work — or on the time off this business was supposed to buy you.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact form is not visible on the homepage — reachable only through CTA buttons or footer links
  • No live chat is present anywhere on the site
  • No confirmation exists of average response time to form submissions

These findings are also referenced in the Website Audit section.

Team Assessment

Every function outside of practicing law — business development, intake, marketing, and operations — runs through one of five partner-attorneys, with zero dedicated support staff to absorb any of it. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Zero non-attorney staff — no ops, marketing, or intake team member exists at the firm
  • All five attorneys personally handle both revenue-generating deal work and every operational task
  • No manager or leadership layer exists between the partners and day-to-day execution
  • The planned acquisition is framed as extending the lean model — absorbing another firm's tech stack and staff — rather than as a step toward building real delegation inside Cole Reynolds Law itself
Blind Spot: Staying lean has made the firm excellent at cost control — but it's also the reason none of the five partners can take real time away from the firm.
The Positive
  • The stated interest in a Fractional CTO and owned AI agents to "offload operational tasks completely" shows the partners are already thinking about delegation — the intent is there
The Gap
  • Owner and partner time capacity directly caps the firm's deal volume — there is no team to absorb overflow
  • No accountability structure exists if a partner is unavailable during travel, illness, or a deal crunch
  • Growth today means more hours for the same five people, not more leverage
Until people other than the partners themselves are running day-to-day operations, more revenue will always mean more hours — not more freedom.

Profit Plan Assessment

Cole Reynolds Law already runs at a lean 10–20% overhead against an industry norm of 40–60% — real financial discipline that most firms never achieve. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: GREEN
"10–20% overhead" — versus an industry norm the firm itself cites at "40–60%."
The Problem
  • No explicit revenue goal has been set — this audit defaults to 2x current revenue ($2M) absent a stated target
  • Average case value is not tracked or was not stated on the call, making case-level profitability hard to see practice-area by practice area
  • The lean-overhead model is instinctive, not documented as a formal profit plan with specific targets
  • No confirmation was given on customer acquisition cost tracking
Blind Spot: Not tracking average case value by practice area means the firm can't tell which of transactional work, M&A, or fractional GC retainers is actually its most profitable line — you're optimizing overhead without yet optimizing mix.
The Positive
  • Firm can state its overhead ratio precisely — a level of financial visibility most law firm owners never reach
  • The lean structure was deliberately engineered to maximize partner profit, not just to avoid cost — a strategic choice, not an accident
The Gap
  • Without a stated revenue goal, growth has no specific target to plan spend or hiring against
  • Without average case value or acquisition cost data, it's hard to know which growth investment will actually pay back fastest
The financial discipline is already there — the next step is pointing that same precision at growth, not just at overhead.
