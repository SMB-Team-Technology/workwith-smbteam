Here Is Where Krause Law, PLLC Stands Today

Benjamin Krause is a disabled Air Force veteran and VA benefits attorney who has spent more than a decade building one of the most trusted and widely read veterans advocacy platforms in the country — DisabledVeterans.org — while simultaneously practicing law through Krause Law, PLLC in Minneapolis. The firm handles VA and DoD benefit appeals exclusively, with a tight focus on cases where the VA has already said no at least once. The single most important finding from this audit: Benjamin has built extraordinary organic authority and a loyal audience of veterans that most competitors could never replicate — but he has no paid lead generation system to capture the demand he is already generating.

Stage 6 is where Benjamin can step fully into the role he was built for — the most recognized veterans attorney in America, with a law firm that runs without him — and that is exactly where we are taking him.

Where You Are Today

Stage 4: Small Business Manager
  • All leads come from organic sources — DisabledVeterans.org, YouTube, and search — with zero paid advertising in any channel
  • Benjamin functions as both the firm's lead attorney and its primary content creator, splitting his time between casework and audience building
  • Director of Operations is in place, but key strategy and firm decisions still require Benjamin's direct involvement
  • AI technology tools are being explored but not yet deployed across intake and case management — the efficiency gain is available but untapped

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between where Benjamin is today and where Krause Law, PLLC needs to be is not as wide as it feels — but closing it requires pairing the extraordinary authority he has built with the marketing engine, AI infrastructure, and coaching system that have been missing.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

  • No automated follow-up sequence confirmed for leads who don't sign immediately — veterans who are weighing their options need consistent, empathetic follow-up, not silence

Profit Plan Assessment

Krause Law, PLLC operates in a specialized niche where successful appeals can produce substantial retroactive benefit awards — and attorney fees that reflect the real value delivered to veterans. But without a clear financial reporting system tied to case acquisition cost and case lifecycle profitability, it is difficult to know exactly which activities, channels, and case types are driving the most profitable growth. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Cost per acquisition not systematically tracked against marketing channels — organic traffic and content production bring clients, but without measurement tied to each source, optimization is guesswork
  • No confirmed financial reporting framework for tracking case profitability by type, acquisition source, or case timeline — making it impossible to know which case types and which channels produce the best return
  • As paid advertising comes online, tracking ROI per channel is essential infrastructure — without this in place first, ad spend decisions are made blind and scaling becomes risky rather than confident
  • DisabledVeterans.org and the law firm have intertwined audiences and partially intertwined operations — separate financial tracking for each entity is needed for clear profit visibility on the legal practice alone
Blind Spot: VA disability appeals can produce retroactive benefit awards of $50,000–$100,000 or more — and attorney fees on those cases are accordingly significant. But only if the firm tracks which case types yield the best fee-to-effort ratio. Without that data, Krause Law may be spending the same time on cases that pay $3,000 as on cases that pay $20,000 — with no system to prioritize the highest-value work.
The Positive
  • Appeals-only practice model eliminates the inefficiency of handling initial claims — every case has already been through the VA process at least once, creating a more predictable case lifecycle and revenue profile than mixed-practice firms
  • Specialized niche with high-value outcome potential — successful VA appeals with large retroactive awards generate attorney fees that reward quality work at a level most legal niches do not offer
The Gap
  • Without financial visibility into cost per acquisition and case profitability, scaling paid advertising carries unnecessary risk — there is no baseline ROI to validate against as new channels come online
  • The path to profitably serving thousands of veterans requires knowing exactly what it costs to acquire and win a case — that measurement infrastructure does not yet exist and needs to be built before ad spend scales
  • As AI workforce investment increases, tracking the ROI of those tools against case throughput and profitability will be critical — AI tools should produce measurable efficiency gains that show up on the bottom line
The more veterans Benjamin helps, the more the firm should earn — but that outcome is not automatic without a profit plan that ensures every new case, every new channel, and every new AI tool adds to the bottom line rather than just adding complexity.
