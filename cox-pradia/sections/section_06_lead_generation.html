Lead Generation

The Cox Pradia Law Firm has built a recognized brand in Houston personal injury law over more than two decades — but without a paid advertising engine, the firm is effectively invisible to the thousands of people searching Google every month for exactly the cases it handles. The leads are in this market. The system to capture them is not yet in place.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed scores: Not verified — the PageSpeed API was unavailable during research. Scores must be confirmed at pagespeed.web.dev prior to client presentation. Qualitative observation suggests a media-rich site that may score in the 40–65 mobile range, which would be a meaningful conversion liability in a market where page speed is a ranking signal.
  • Contact form is buried in the footer — a visitor who arrives on the homepage and does not scroll to the very bottom has no way to submit their information without calling
  • No video content anywhere on the homepage — personal injury is a high-emotion, high-stakes practice area where attorney video builds the trust that converts a casual visitor into a consultation
  • No geo-targeting landing pages for surrounding Houston suburbs — Katy, Sugar Land, The Woodlands, Pasadena, and Pearland all generate significant PI search volume with no dedicated pages found
Blind Spot: Competitors may be bidding on "Cox Pradia" or the partners' names in Google Ads, capturing prospects who already know the firm and search for them by name. Without running branded campaigns, those high-intent searchers are being handed to a competing firm.
The Positive
  • Modern, mobile-responsive design estimated at 2022–2024 vintage — clean layout that communicates professionalism and is not a barrier to conversion
  • Active blog with 2025–2026 posts on specific case types (TBI, 18-wheeler, brain injury) — shows an SEO content strategy is at least partially in place
  • Attorney bios for both Jonathan Cox and Troy Pradia are accessible from the main navigation — a trust signal that many PI sites lack
The Gap
  • A buried contact form and no after-hours chat means every evening and weekend visitor who does not call is a lost lead — in PI, the first firm to respond often gets the case
  • No video means the firm relies entirely on text and photos to earn trust — competitors running attorney video ads on Facebook and YouTube are building familiarity before a prospect ever visits the site
  • No suburb landing pages means the firm cannot rank organically or convert paid traffic from Katy, Sugar Land, The Woodlands, or other high-volume Houston suburbs
A website that converts visitors into consultations is the foundation that makes every dollar of advertising the firm invests work harder — and every lead the firm earns actually count.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • 51 Google reviews at 4.4 stars — the firm's rating is lower than all four major competitors surveyed (4.6–5.0 stars), and the review gap is severe: Zehl & Associates has 24.7× more reviews, Simmons and Fletcher has 9.8× more, and even the smallest competitor checked has nearly 2× more
  • Significant NAP inconsistency across directories: the firm's website lists 2402 Rosedale Street as the address while Yelp (updated February 2026), LawInfo, Yahoo Local, and BizRatings all list 1415 North Loop West — with three different phone numbers appearing across these sources
  • Firm does not appear in the local 3-pack for any primary search term tested — "personal injury lawyer Houston," "car accident attorney Houston," "18 wheeler accident lawyer Houston," or "wrongful death attorney Houston TX" — meaning zero free local visibility for the searches that matter most
  • FindLaw lists two attorney names (Blanton, Marshall) that appear outdated — not the current Cox/Pradia partners — further fragmenting the firm's online authority
Blind Spot: Two different street addresses appearing in active directories may be splitting the firm's local SEO authority across two distinct map locations — which is worse than a simple citation error. Google's algorithm may be unable to confidently associate all reviews and citations with a single verified business, suppressing 3-pack eligibility even further than the review gap alone would explain.
The Positive
  • The firm is listed across all major legal directories — FindLaw, Avvo, Justia, Yelp, Super Lawyers, and LawInfo — providing a citation base to build from once NAP consistency is corrected
The Gap
  • 3-pack invisibility means the firm earns zero free leads from Google Maps — the placement that captures the highest-intent "near me" and location-specific searchers
  • Every month without a review velocity system, competitors extend their review lead — at the current gap (51 vs. 1,262), catching up requires both speed and volume
Zehl & Associates — 1,262 reviews at 5.0 stars, Google Screened badge, and dominant 3-pack presence for every major Houston PI keyword. They have turned their review volume into an insurmountable organic advantage that gets stronger every week.
Fixing local SEO — starting with NAP consistency and review velocity — is the only way to reclaim the free traffic that competitors are capturing from the firm's own market every single day.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Cox Pradia Law Firm does not appear to be running Google search ads for "car accident attorney Houston," "personal injury lawyer Houston," "18 wheeler accident lawyer Houston," or related terms — the firm may not have active campaigns configured, or may be getting outbid entirely by competitors with significantly larger advertising budgets
  • This absence extends across all observed practice areas including truck accidents, wrongful death, and traumatic brain injury — leaving the firm with no paid visibility at the top of the page for any high-intent search
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Zehl & Associates — estimated $913,000+ annual Google Ads spend; headlines emphasize "Undefeated" and specific recovery statistics; dominant presence across car accident, 18-wheeler, and all major PI terms in Houston
  • Simmons and Fletcher, P.C. — estimated $384,000 annual Google Ads spend; messaging built around "Award-Winning Houston Truck Accident Lawyer | NO Fees Unless We Win" and 45+ year track record; active across car accident, truck accident, and slip and fall
  • Terry Bryant Accident & Injury Law — estimated $272,000 annual Google Ads spend; "$1 Billion Recovered" headline messaging; active in Houston PI search results
The Opportunity
  • "Car accident attorney Houston" and "18 wheeler accident lawyer Houston" — the highest-volume, highest-intent terms where competitors are spending the most; this is where the first ads should run
  • Specific case-type terms: "traumatic brain injury lawyer Houston," "wrongful death attorney Houston," "nursing home negligence lawyer Houston" — lower competition than broad PI terms, higher average case value
  • Spanish-language campaign targeting — Houston's population is 44%+ Hispanic, yet no Spanish-language content was found on the Cox Pradia site; this is a significant untapped segment that competitors are not fully serving
  • Competitor name bidding — targeting searches for Zehl & Associates, Simmons and Fletcher, and Terry Bryant captures prospects who are already researching PI attorneys and have not yet made a decision
Blind Spot: Houston personal injury CPCs range from $50–$500+ per click — among the most expensive legal markets in the country. Entering this market without a properly structured campaign (strong Quality Scores, dedicated landing pages, conversion tracking) means paying top-market CPCs for traffic that does not convert. The entry cost matters less than the conversion infrastructure behind it.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • Cox Pradia Law Firm does not appear to hold an active Google Screened Local Service Ads listing — no LSA placement was observed for any practice area or search term during research
  • With 51 Google reviews, the firm has enough verified reviews to qualify for LSA setup — the barrier is not eligibility, it is activation
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Zehl & Associates — Google Screened badge with 1,262+ reviews powering their LSA placement; appearing at the very top of results above all paid ads for Houston PI searches
  • Simmons and Fletcher — Google Screened with active LSA presence for personal injury results; 502 reviews reinforcing trust at the top placement
  • Terry Bryant Accident & Injury Law — active in LSA results for Houston PI searches; even smaller competitors have secured this placement ahead of Cox Pradia
The Opportunity
  • LSAs appear above everything on Google — above regular paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — making this the highest-visibility placement available for PI searches in Houston
  • LSAs are pay-per-lead, not pay-per-click — the firm pays only when a verified prospect calls directly through the ad, not for clicks from researchers or competitors
  • The Google Screened badge functions as an independent third-party trust signal — in a high-stakes PI market where consumers are choosing who to trust with a serious injury claim, that badge changes the conversion equation

Competitor Ad Landscape

  • Zehl & Associates — comprehensive digital marketing presence across all channels; their scale of Google Ads investment typically correlates with active Meta retargeting and lead gen campaigns
The Opportunity

  • Video ads featuring the attorneys — builds trust before a prospect ever calls. Jonathan Cox and Troy Pradia have compelling professional stories and credibility (Top 50 Black Attorneys in Houston) that translate powerfully to video
  • Educational content ads — capture leads earlier in the decision process before they run a Google search and find a competitor
  • Spanish-language targeting — with Houston's 44%+ Hispanic population and no Spanish content observed on the Cox Pradia site, Meta's audience targeting tools offer a direct path to an underserved segment with high PI case volume
