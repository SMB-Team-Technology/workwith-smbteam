Intake Assessment

Because all operations flow through Lorena, intake at this firm depends entirely on her availability — meaning every lead that arrives when she is in a consultation, managing staff, or handling case work is a lead that may never convert. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED — Critical Priority
The Problem
  • No dedicated intake process or intake staff — with all 6 support staff as generalists and no defined intake role, new prospect calls are handled inconsistently, if at all
  • The website contact form is buried behind a CTA link to a separate page — prospects who arrive at the site after hours, from mobile, or in an emotional state are not met with an immediate, frictionless path to start a conversation
  • No after-hours intake coverage observed — calls and form submissions that arrive outside business hours are not being captured before the prospect finds another attorney
  • No defined follow-up sequence — leads who do not sign immediately are likely falling out of the pipeline entirely, with no systematic re-engagement
Blind Spot: Immigration prospects are often reaching out during a crisis — a family member detained, a visa denial, an employer threatening deportation. These calls do not wait until 9 AM. Every hour of delay in response is a case this firm worked to earn and then handed to a competitor who answered faster.
The Gap
  • The transcript identified an estimated $360,000/year in missed case opportunity — a significant portion of that loss happens not because the cases do not exist, but because intake is failing to convert the leads that are already arriving
  • At a default 15% close rate, every 100 leads generates 15 cases; improving intake conversion to 20% on the same lead volume adds 5 cases per 100 leads — at an average case value of $4,500, that is an additional $22,500 in revenue without any added marketing spend
  • Without a defined intake process, adding more marketing spend will multiply the problem — more leads arriving into a broken intake system means more missed cases, not more revenue
Fixing intake is not a marketing problem — it is an operational one. And it is the fastest path to revenue growth that does not require Lorena to personally work more hours.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • The website's primary CTA ("Si! Quiero mas informacion!") links to a separate contact page rather than opening an embedded form — each additional click in the conversion path reduces form completions, particularly on mobile devices
  • No live chat functionality exists on the site — for a practice serving clients navigating urgent, emotionally charged immigration situations, real-time communication is a conversion differentiator that competitors are increasingly offering
  • No inline contact form is present above the fold on the homepage — the most valuable real estate on the site is not being used to capture leads

Team Assessment

The Law Office of Lorena Ibarra has a team of 6 support staff — but every one of them reports directly through Lorena, who by her own description is the "chief everything officer." There is no management layer, no accountability structure, and no system that allows the team to operate without constant direction from the founder. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED — Critical Priority
The Problem
  • All operations and decisions flow through Lorena — she is simultaneously the attorney, the manager, the HR department, and the operations director, with no structure that functions without her direct involvement
  • Two employees were recently terminated for performance issues and creating a toxic environment — this is not a personnel problem, it is a systems problem: without clear hiring criteria, onboarding protocols, and performance standards, bad hires are inevitable and recurring
  • The current coaching program recommended implementing KPIs but provided no guidance on how to build them — meaning the team has no performance benchmarks, no accountability metrics, and no way for Lorena to manage by data instead of by presence
  • Without a designated operations leader, every process improvement, every personnel issue, and every system breakdown lands on Lorena — and there is no one else to catch it
Blind Spot: The fact that Lorena has been able to grow to $1.3M–$1.5M while managing everything personally has masked how fragile the structure actually is. One extended absence — illness, a family emergency, a vacation — and the firm has no mechanism to continue operating at full capacity. The current structure is not just a growth ceiling; it is a single point of failure.
The Positive
  • Lorena has successfully retained 6 staff members and grown the team beyond solo practitioner scale — she has demonstrated the ability to hire and manage people, which means the infrastructure to build on already exists
The Gap
  • The estimated $360,000/year in missed cases is not a marketing gap — it is a team and operations gap. Cases are slipping because there is no system to capture, process, and convert them when Lorena is occupied elsewhere
  • Without a Fractional COO or operations leader to build systems and manage staff directly, every marketing investment will be undercut by operational chaos — more leads without better operations just creates more stress, not more revenue
  • The burnout trajectory described on the call is not sustainable at $1.3M–$1.5M. It becomes catastrophic at $2.6M–$3.0M without a fundamentally different operational structure in place first
The firm Lorena wants to have — one that runs without her constant presence — will not exist until someone else is responsible for making sure it runs. That is the role of a Fractional COO. And that is exactly what this plan starts with.

Profit Plan Assessment

At $1.3M–$1.5M in annual revenue, the Law Office of Lorena Ibarra generates substantial income — but with no financial visibility systems identified, no cost-per-case tracking, and a $360,000/year operational drag quantified on the call, it is not clear how much of that revenue is becoming profit. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER — Needs Attention
The Problem
  • No mention of financial visibility tools, cost-per-case tracking, or profit margin awareness — without this data, decisions about hiring, marketing spend, and pricing are made on intuition rather than on numbers
  • The $360,000/year missed-case opportunity represents a direct, quantifiable profit gap — cases that were within reach but not captured due to operational bottlenecks are revenue that was earned and then lost
  • The current coaching contract (with 6 months remaining) represents an investment in high-level strategy that the firm cannot yet implement — money spent without a return is a profit drain, not a growth driver
  • No mention of understanding client acquisition cost — without knowing what it costs to get a new client, it is impossible to evaluate whether a marketing investment is worth making or what the true ROI of growth is
The Positive
  • Revenue of $1.3M–$1.5M provides a meaningful financial foundation — the firm has the scale to afford real operational investment and to see a genuine return on it
The Gap
  • Doubling revenue to $2.6M–$3.0M without a profit plan in place risks doubling overhead, doubling complexity, and doubling stress — while the owner's take-home income stays flat or declines as a percentage of revenue
  • Without cost-per-case data, the firm cannot identify which case types are most profitable, which marketing channels produce the best ROI, or where operational inefficiency is eroding margin
  • The absence of profit planning means growth is unpredictable — revenue may be going up while profitability is going sideways
The goal is not just to grow the firm — it is to grow the firm in a way that pays Lorena more while requiring less of her time. That requires a profit plan, not just a revenue target.
