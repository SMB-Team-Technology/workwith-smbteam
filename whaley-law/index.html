Here Is Where Whaley Law Stands Today

Stewart Whaley runs a solo criminal defense and traffic law practice out of two Arkansas offices — Little Rock and Stuttgart — handling traffic tickets, professional license defense, DWI, and felony criminal defense entirely on his own, without a single support staff member. Revenue has doubled year-over-year to $30,000 per month, driven by referrals, a Justia directory listing, and a single paid ad in the Arkansas State Board of Nursing periodical. The single most urgent problem is that Stewart's website is completely offline due to an expired SSL certificate — making the firm invisible to Google, inaccessible to new clients, and unable to capture any of the digital demand that competitors are actively harvesting.

Stage 6 is where the firm generates income whether Stewart is in the office or not — and reaching it is exactly what this roadmap is designed to do, so he can work a normal week, take real vacations, and stop carrying the financial weight of the practice entirely on his own.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • The website is completely inaccessible — whaley.law returns an "SSL certificate has expired" error on both HTTP and HTTPS, blocking all visitors and preventing Google from crawling or indexing the site
  • PageSpeed scores: Could not be run — pagespeed.web.dev returns an error state because the site is unreachable. Scores must be re-evaluated after the SSL is restored. A site that Google cannot crawl is effectively unranked.
  • No confirmed contact form, live chat, or above-the-fold CTA is visible — the site's conversion infrastructure is unknown and unverifiable in its current state
  • No blog content, no geo-specific landing pages, and no video content were observed in any cached or indexed content — the site has no content engine producing organic search signals
Blind Spot: An expired SSL certificate does not just block visitors — it signals to Google that the site may be abandoned or compromised. Every day the SSL remains expired, the firm's organic rankings erode further and the crawl debt compounds. What was a quick fix in week one becomes a months-long recovery project if ignored.
The Positive
  • Stewart has an established domain (whaley.law) with some indexed content and directory backlinks — once the SSL is restored, the existing authority can be built on rather than starting from scratch
  • Attorney bio and contact information appear consistently in directory listings and search snippets — the brand foundation exists even while the website is down
The Gap
  • Every potential client who tries to visit whaley.law sees a security warning and leaves — these are warm leads that the firm is actively losing to competitors right now
  • Without a functioning website, no paid advertising can launch — landing pages, tracking pixels, and conversion forms all require a live, secure site
  • Dallas County and Clark County were identified on the call as underserved geographic markets with few attorneys — the firm has no landing pages targeting these areas, leaving a geographic opportunity completely uncaptured
A website that works is not optional — it is the foundation that every other marketing system depends on, and without it, the path to the financial stability and freedom Stewart is building toward cannot even begin.

Profit Plan Assessment

Stewart's revenue is growing — doubling year-over-year is a real achievement for a solo practitioner — but the firm is leaving significant money on the table through pricing that is far below market rate for its highest-value cases. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Stewart charges $7,000 flat fee for felony criminal defense cases where peers in the same market charge $30,000 — this is not a competitive pricing strategy, it is a cash-flow-driven underpricing that limits income without adding clients
  • With multiple practice areas (traffic tickets at $1,100, license defense at $2,500–$4,000, and felony defense at $7,000), there is no visibility into which case type generates the best return per hour invested
  • The firm operates without a formal financial plan — no defined profit targets, no expense benchmarks, and no system for projecting revenue based on case mix and volume
  • Household financial pressure (supporting a spouse's medical practice launch) means the firm's cash flow decisions are being driven by immediate need rather than long-term strategy
Blind Spot: At $7,000 for felony defense, Stewart may be attracting clients who can only afford $7,000 — rather than clients who value the work at its actual worth. Pricing communicates positioning. Moving felony fees toward even $12,000–$15,000 (still well below market) would double felony revenue without adding a single new case, and would attract clients who expect to pay for quality rather than just the lowest price.
The Positive
  • Revenue doubled year-over-year from $15,000/month in June 2025 to $30,000/month in June 2026 — this trajectory, if sustained, reaches $60,000/month within another year without any additional marketing investment
The Gap
  • Felony case underpricing at $7,000 versus a market rate of $30,000 means every felony case Stewart takes is generating 23 cents on the dollar of its potential value — closing that gap even partially would dramatically accelerate the path to $1M annual revenue
  • Without knowing the profit per case type, it is impossible to prioritize the right practice areas, set the right marketing budget, or make intelligent decisions about which clients to take and which to refer out
  • The $1M annual revenue goal requires a plan — not just more clients, but a clear picture of average case value, case volume targets, and a pricing structure that reflects the quality of work being done
Getting to $1M is not just about more cases — it is about knowing what the work is worth, charging accordingly, and building a financial structure that turns revenue into the real stability and freedom Stewart and his family are working toward.
