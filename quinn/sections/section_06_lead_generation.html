Lead Generation

Quinn Law Group has built genuine credibility in the Philadelphia personal injury market — a 4.9-star rating, Sean Quinn's 25-year track record, and seven office locations across Pennsylvania and New Jersey give the firm a foundation that most competitors cannot match. But the firm does not appear to be converting that credibility into owned digital lead generation: no Google Ads, no LSA, and no Meta Ads observed across any of the firm's three practice areas in one of the most competitive — and most expensive — legal advertising markets in the country.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Blog content effectively inactive since 2014 — a seven-office PI firm operating in one of the most competitive search markets in the country has no active content marketing, ceding organic rankings and AI-generated search visibility to competitors who publish consistently
  • PageSpeed scores: Mobile 73/100 — Desktop 100/100. The mobile score is in the "needs improvement" range — a conversion risk in a market where the majority of personal injury searches happen from mobile devices, often at the scene of an accident
  • Contact form is buried on a separate Contact page — not embedded on the homepage where most visitors decide whether to reach out or leave; every extra step costs conversions
  • No video content present on the site — in a market where competing firms use attorney video to establish trust before a prospect ever calls, Quinn's nationally recognized trial lawyer background has no visual presence online
Blind Spot: Mobile traffic to Quinn's website likely spikes on evenings and weekends — exactly when after-hours intake is most manual and least effective. A faster mobile site with an embedded contact form would generate more after-hours inquiries that the current intake system is already struggling to capture.
The Positive
  • Strong homepage headline ("YOUR VOICE. YOUR ADVOCATE.") with a prominent FREE CONSULTATION CTA above the fold — the firm's brand positioning is clear and trust-building
  • Active social media presence (Facebook: 1,457 likes; Instagram @quinnlawyers: 1,580 followers, 241 posts) — organic audience already exists and is waiting to be activated with paid content
The Gap
  • A mobile PageSpeed score of 73 means Quinn is losing cases to competitors with faster mobile sites — in Philadelphia PI, where a prospect who hits a slow page will call the next attorney in the search results within seconds
  • With no location-specific landing pages for Pittsburgh, Bethlehem, Hoboken, Princeton, or Cherry Hill, five of Quinn's seven offices generate zero local SEO traffic despite representing active practice locations across two states
  • Competitors investing in ongoing blog and FAQ content will consistently outrank Quinn in Google AI Overviews and organic search results — content velocity is a compounding disadvantage that grows every month it goes unaddressed
A website built for conversion — with an embedded contact form, mobile performance above 90, and landing pages for every office location — turns Quinn's existing reputation into consistent inbound leads across all seven markets simultaneously.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Quinn Law Group holds 168 Google reviews at 4.9★ — a strong rating, but behind key competitors in review volume: MyPhillyLawyer (304), The Levin Firm (227), and Zavodnick & Lasky (181) all hold more reviews, directly impacting local pack ranking for high-intent PI searches
  • Quinn does not appear to hold a dominant 3-pack position for highest-volume searches like "personal injury attorney Philadelphia" — competitors with higher review volumes are favored by the algorithm for these placements
  • No geo-targeted landing pages exist for five of seven office locations — Pittsburgh, Bethlehem, Hoboken, Princeton, and Cherry Hill have no dedicated web pages or optimized GBP profiles to capture local searches in those markets
Blind Spot: With seven offices across PA and NJ, Quinn Law Group has the footprint to appear in local packs across six distinct metro areas simultaneously — but without optimized GBP profiles and geo-targeted content for each location, the firm is functionally competing as a single-location practice while paying the overhead of seven.
The Positive
  • 4.9★ Google rating is the strongest in the competitive set — outperforming MyPhillyLawyer (4.7★) and Ostroff Injury Law (4.5★); the rating quality is there, the gap is in review volume
  • Sean Quinn holds an Avvo 10.0 (Superb) rating with a PRO designation — a credibility signal that cross-references positively with Google reviews and strengthens the firm's overall digital authority
The Gap
  • Every month without a structured review acquisition program is a month that competitors close the gap — MyPhillyLawyer's lead of 136 reviews translates directly into higher local pack visibility for the searches that produce the highest-value PI cases
  • Without location-specific landing pages and optimized GBP profiles for all seven offices, the firm cannot capture local search intent in its own markets — Pittsburgh PI searches, Cherry Hill injury attorneys, and Hoboken workers comp are all going to competitors
MyPhillyLawyer — holds nearly 2× the Google review volume (304 vs. 168) and is actively appearing in organic results for the highest-volume Philadelphia PI search terms. A firm with double the review count and an active digital presence will consistently outperform Quinn in local pack rankings until the review volume gap is closed.
With a structured review acquisition program and location-specific SEO for all seven offices, Quinn Law Group can appear in multiple local packs simultaneously — multiplying the firm's digital footprint to match its physical one.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Quinn Law Group does not appear to be running Google search ads for personal injury, workers compensation, or medical malpractice in Philadelphia — the firm may not have active campaigns configured, or may not be competing in a market where CPCs range from $100 to $140 per click
  • With no confirmed Google Ads presence across all three practice areas, the firm is currently absent from the highest-intent, highest-converting digital lead generation channel available to Philadelphia PI attorneys
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • MyPhillyLawyer — prominently appearing in personal injury search results and actively competing for the highest-volume PI keywords in Philadelphia
  • Morgan & Morgan — national advertiser running extensive Philadelphia-area campaigns at a scale that individual local firms cannot match without their own active, well-managed campaigns
The Opportunity
  • "Personal injury attorney Philadelphia" and "car accident lawyer Philadelphia PA" are the highest-value search terms Quinn is not capturing — while competitors are currently taking those calls
  • Workers compensation and medical malpractice present lower-competition opportunities at lower CPCs — Quinn can capture meaningful volume in these practice areas for less than a broad PI campaign would cost
  • Competitor name campaigns and geographic targeting for Pittsburgh, Cherry Hill, and Hoboken would capture leads in markets where Quinn has offices but no observed digital presence — markets where competition is less saturated than center-city Philadelphia
  • Defensive branded campaigns ("Quinn Law Group") protect the firm's name from being captured by national advertisers who bid on attorney names to redirect traffic to their own intake lines
Blind Spot: Morgan & Morgan and national advertisers dominate broad Philadelphia PI terms with budgets that local firms cannot match head-to-head. But specificity wins: medical malpractice in Philadelphia, workers comp in South Jersey, and location-specific PI searches are far less saturated and can produce significantly lower CPLs — this is the strategy that lets a local firm with Quinn's reputation compete on efficiency, not just volume.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence

Competitor LSA Landscape
  • MyPhillyLawyer — 304 Google reviews would give this firm a strong LSA profile; appearing prominently in organic results immediately adjacent to where LSA listings show
  • Morgan & Morgan / Sokolove Law — national PI advertisers with Philadelphia-area presence are known to maintain active LSA programs in competitive markets, occupying top positions before local firms even appear
The Opportunity
  • With 168 Google reviews at 4.9★, Quinn Law Group has the review foundation to qualify for and compete strongly in the LSA space — the rating is stronger than most local competitors
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • A Google Screened LSA profile at 4.9★ would immediately position Quinn above firms like MyPhillyLawyer in the most visible placement on the results page — even though they currently have more reviews, Quinn's higher rating gives the firm an advantage in impression quality once LSA is active
Blind Spot: Every day Quinn Law Group is absent from LSA in Philadelphia PI, a competitor is paying $210–$260 per inbound call that could be going to Quinn — and with Quinn's 4.9★ rating, the conversion rate on those calls would be among the highest in the market.
