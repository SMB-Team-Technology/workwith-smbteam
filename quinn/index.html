Here Is Where Quinn Law Group Stands Today

Quinn Law Group is a Philadelphia-based personal injury firm led by nationally recognized trial lawyer Sean Quinn, with 25 years of practice and seven office locations spanning Pennsylvania and New Jersey — handling PI, workers compensation, and medical malpractice cases with a $15,000 minimum case value that reflects the seriousness of the work. The firm has built exceptional credibility: a 4.9-star Google rating and strong brand recognition across two states. The single most important finding is that Quinn Law Group does not appear to be running any owned paid digital marketing — no Google Ads, no LSA, and no Meta Ads — across any of its three practice areas, while well-funded local and national competitors actively capture every high-intent search in the Philadelphia market.

Stage 6 is where Quinn Law Group's seven offices become a coordinated revenue engine that generates cases consistently — where Sean is reviewing growth metrics instead of managing intake processes, and where the firm wins market share because its systems are faster and smarter than the competition's.

Where You Are Today

Stage 4: Small Business Manager
  • Seven office locations across PA and NJ — but no owned digital marketing system generating inbound leads across any of them
  • After-hours intake is entirely manual — losing 2–3 confirmed qualified leads per month ($45,000/month) in a market where leads cost $100–$140 per click
  • No Google Ads, LSA, or Meta Ads running for PI, workers comp, or medical malpractice — while better-funded competitors dominate every digital channel
  • Follow-up requires 2–3 hours of intake manager time per day, and leads with no response within two weeks are dropped with no automated re-engagement

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between where Quinn Law Group is today and where it needs to be is not a credibility gap — the reputation is already there; it is a systems and marketing gap, and that kind of gap closes faster than most people expect when the right infrastructure is in place.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Intake Assessment

Quinn Law Group has a dedicated Intake Manager actively managing follow-up — but the system behind that manager is almost entirely manual, consuming hours each day and converting at a fraction of what it could if automated. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • After-hours case signing requires a computer — meaning cases cannot be signed digitally on mobile after hours, making after-hours conversion impractical in a market where accidents and injuries happen around the clock
  • Follow-up requires 3–6 manual touchpoints per lead, consuming 2–3 hours of the Intake Manager's time every day — before any new volume from a paid marketing campaign is added to the workload
  • Leads are dropped after two weeks with no contact — in Philadelphia PI, where prospects evaluate multiple firms, a two-week drop with no automated re-engagement means those leads have already signed elsewhere
  • No after-hours coverage for inbound inquiries — the majority of personal injury incidents happen in the evenings and on weekends, exactly when the current intake process is least able to respond
Blind Spot: The 2–3 confirmed lost leads per month is almost certainly understated — it reflects only the leads the team knows they lost. After-hours website visitors who never submitted a form, callers who left voicemail and moved on, and prospects who submitted an inquiry after hours and received no response until the following business day are not captured in that figure.
The Positive
  • A dedicated Intake Manager exists and is actively managing follow-up — the role structure is in place; what is missing is the automation that would allow that role to scale
  • The firm's existing technology stack (Nextiva phone, Case Status case management, DocuBe document automation) shows a willingness to invest in legal technology and provides the integration points needed to build automated intake workflows
The Gap
  • At a $15,000 minimum case value, the 2–3 confirmed lost after-hours leads per month represent a minimum of $30,000–$45,000/month in revenue the firm cannot capture under the current process — that is not a future problem
  • A manual follow-up process consuming 2–3 hours per day leaves the Intake Manager with less than half a workday for everything else — and that capacity disappears entirely when a paid marketing campaign doubles or triples inbound lead volume
  • The current intake system cannot scale: adding 50–75 new cases in six months means significantly more leads to manage manually, creating a conversion bottleneck before the marketing campaign has a chance to produce results
Every system the firm builds to convert after-hours leads and automate follow-up is a direct investment in the 50–75 case goal — it is not overhead, it is the engine that makes the goal achievable.

Profit Plan Assessment

Quinn Law Group operates seven offices in one of the most expensive PI markets in the country, with active lead acquisition costs and a case value floor that signals serious, high-stakes work — yet no financial visibility data was shared on the discovery call. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Annual revenue was not disclosed on the call — in a multi-office firm actively spending on leads in a market with $100–$140 CPCs, knowing cost-per-acquisition is not optional; it is the difference between a profitable marketing campaign and a costly one
  • No mention of profit planning, financial reporting, or margin visibility — growth without financial infrastructure can mean increasing revenue while owner take-home does not improve proportionally
  • Active spending on pay-per-lead sources in a saturated market without knowing exact cost-per-acquisition makes it impossible to evaluate which lead sources are generating profit and which are subsidizing losses elsewhere
  • Seven offices across two states create overhead (rent, staffing, technology licenses) by location that requires financial tracking to identify where the firm's true profit centers are — and where resources might be better deployed
Blind Spot: Quinn's $15,000 case value floor is one of the strongest in the local PI market — but without practice-area-level financial tracking, it is impossible to know whether workers compensation and medical malpractice cases are generating the same return as core PI, or whether certain office locations carry overhead that outpaces their revenue contribution.
The Positive
  • $15,000 minimum case value is well above the PI market average — the firm is not chasing low-value cases, and that case economics discipline is the most important profitability signal possible
  • Existing investments in Case Status and DocuBe demonstrate operational efficiency thinking — the tools to track case-level data and financials are partially in place
The Gap
  • Without knowing cost-per-acquisition, every new lead source added — including a paid marketing campaign — carries unknown profitability that cannot be evaluated until the financial systems to track it are in place
  • The goal of 50–75 new cases in six months needs a financial framework defining what success means — not just more cases, but more profitable cases that translate to Sean taking home meaningfully more
  • Without bookkeeping and financial reporting tied to the firm's KPIs, growth will look like success on the surface while owner income may not reflect the firm's revenue growth
Building a profit plan alongside the marketing campaign means Sean will know exactly what 50–75 new cases are worth to him personally — not just to the firm — and can make every investment decision with that number in mind.
