Lead Generation

Lasser Law Group has been practicing New York real estate and commercial litigation law for over two decades — but its entire client pipeline depends on referrals and reputation in a market where every competitor is actively investing in digital channels to capture the clients who are searching for attorneys right now. No paid ads, no Local Service Ad presence, and no local pack visibility means the firm is invisible at precisely the moment prospects are ready to hire.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Blog content has stalled — the most recent post observed was May 2025, a 12+ month gap that signals inactivity to both Google and prospective clients
  • PageSpeed scores: Unable to retrieve (API rate-limited during research) — based on the site's WordPress architecture with video embeds and multi-office pages, mobile performance is likely in the 40–60 range, which falls below the threshold needed to compete in Google's mobile-first ranking environment
  • Contact form is not above the fold — visitors arriving from search must navigate to find it, and most will not
  • No geo-targeted landing pages for Westchester or Long Island markets despite having offices in both — the firm is missing organic and paid traffic from those metro-adjacent markets
Blind Spot: The firm's video podcast is a genuine content asset that most competitors do not have — but with no paid amplification and stalled publishing, it is building almost no new audience. A few hundred dollars in video promotion per month could turn this existing asset into a lead generation engine without creating any new content.
The Positive
  • Active video podcast ("New York Real Estate Video Podcast") with multiple episodes — a differentiated content asset that builds credibility and trust before a prospect ever calls
  • 12 attorney profiles with photos, credentials, and individual bios — the kind of transparent team presentation that converts hesitant prospects into inquiries
The Gap
  • A stalled blog and unoptimized mobile site means Google is not surfacing the firm for the hundreds of real estate and landlord-tenant searches happening in Manhattan every week
  • No geo-targeted landing pages means the Westchester and Long Island offices generate no organic search traffic — three offices, but only Manhattan gets any digital exposure
  • The contact form placement is costing the firm real conversions: visitors who arrive from any ad or organic source and do not immediately see a way to reach out will leave within seconds
Building a website that works as hard as the attorneys inside it is the first step toward a firm that generates new client inquiries around the clock — not just when someone makes a personal recommendation.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Lasser Law Group does not appear in the local 3-pack for any of its core practice areas — not for landlord-tenant, condo/co-op, HOA, or construction litigation searches in Manhattan
  • Google Business Profile shows 24 reviews at 3.6 stars — the most recent reviews are predominantly negative, including a critical review from late May 2026 citing unprofessional conduct. A sub-4.0 rating actively discourages new clients from calling
  • FindLaw profile is incomplete (no phone number, no practice areas listed) and Yelp incorrectly categorizes the firm under "Bankruptcy Law" — these NAP inconsistencies undermine the local SEO signals Google uses to rank attorneys
Blind Spot: A 3.6-star average is not neutral — it actively costs cases. Studies consistently show that law firm prospects choose attorneys with 4.0+ ratings, and that a single additional star can increase conversion rates by 5–9%. The most urgent action is not generating new reviews — it is addressing the pattern in recent reviews and rebuilding the rating above 4.0 as quickly as possible.
The Positive
  • Stephen Lasser has been recognized by Super Lawyers 2023–2025 — a credentialing asset that should be prominently featured in the GBP profile and all directory listings to offset the current rating gap
The Gap
  • Not appearing in the 3-pack for Manhattan real estate and landlord-tenant searches means the firm misses the primary organic channel that turns Google searches into phone calls
  • Without a systematic review generation process, the firm's rating will continue to be pulled down by sporadic negative reviews from dissatisfied clients while happy clients say nothing
Goldberg & Lindenberg PC — 57 Google reviews at 4.2 stars, appearing consistently in landlord-tenant search results across multiple Manhattan keyword searches where Lasser does not place. The review gap alone is enough to lose the comparison for any prospect who checks both firms online before calling.
Owning the local search results for Manhattan real estate law is not a long-term aspiration — it is the infrastructure that makes brand dominance possible, and the clock is running.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Lasser Law Group does not appear to be running Google search ads for landlord-tenant, condo/co-op, construction litigation, or commercial real estate attorney searches in Manhattan — the firm may not have active campaigns configured for these terms, or may be getting outbid by larger competitors
  • Every high-intent search — "landlord attorney Manhattan," "NYC condo board lawyer," "construction litigation New York" — is going uncaptured by the firm right now
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Gary Wachtel Law / Outerbridge Law P.C. — active search visibility in landlord-tenant attorney searches, capturing the bottom-of-funnel traffic from prospects ready to hire
  • Belkin Burden Goldman LLP / Braverman Greenspun — dominant market presence in co-op and condo attorney searches, with brand recognition and marketing budgets that allow them to maintain visibility across multiple practice areas simultaneously
The Opportunity
  • High-intent search terms: "landlord attorney Manhattan," "NYC landlord-tenant lawyer," "co-op board attorney New York," "condo board representation NYC," "construction dispute attorney Manhattan"
  • Practice-specific terms: "commercial lease attorney NYC," "construction defect lawyer New York," "HOA attorney Manhattan," "zoning attorney New York"
  • Non-obvious opportunities: Competitor name bidding on Goldberg & Lindenberg and Belkin Burden Goldman searches — capturing prospects comparing firms. Westchester and Long Island geographic variations to leverage the firm's existing office presence
  • The video podcast creates a retargeting pool of engaged visitors who have already spent time on the site — a warm audience that Google Ads can convert at significantly lower cost than cold search traffic
Blind Spot: Manhattan real estate law Google Ads are expensive — but the average case value for commercial real estate litigation and condo board representation can be $10,000–$30,000+. Even at a $300–$500 cost per lead, the ROI on a single signed case is 20–60x. The question is not whether paid search is affordable — it is whether the firm can afford to keep giving this traffic to competitors.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • Lasser Law Group does not appear to be enrolled in Google Local Service Ads for any of its practice areas — the Google Screened badge has not been observed for the firm
  • LSA enrollment is currently unavailable to the firm, meaning every prospect who triggers an LSA result for Manhattan real estate attorney searches is directed to a competitor
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Outerbridge Law P.C. / NY Lawyers Team — active LSA presence in Manhattan landlord-tenant searches, capturing the premium placement above organic results with Google Screened verification
  • Competing real estate and condo/co-op attorneys — multiple firms in the Manhattan market are enrolled in LSA and holding spots that convert at the highest rate of any digital channel for attorneys
The Opportunity
  • LSA enrollment would immediately place Lasser Law Group above all paid search ads, above the map pack, and above all organic results — the most visible position on Google, appearing before a prospect sees a single competitor
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • The Google Screened badge that comes with LSA enrollment signals to clients that the firm has passed Google's verification process — a trust signal that directly addresses the firm's current review rating concern and creates credibility before a prospect ever visits the website

Facebook & Instagram Ads

Ad Presence
  • No active Facebook or Instagram ad campaigns have been observed for Lasser Law Group — the firm has a confirmed Facebook page but does not appear to be using it for paid advertising
  • The firm's video podcast content, attorney profiles, and real estate expertise would translate directly into high-performing Meta ad formats — but that content is currently reaching only organic followers
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Belkin Burden Goldman LLP — confirmed social presence and brand investment; with 60 attorneys and Chambers-ranked status, the firm has both the budget and the content to run sustained Meta campaigns targeting property owners, board members, and landlords across the NYC metro area
  • Braverman Greenspun — LinkedIn and social presence confirmed; a firm representing 300+ buildings in the tri-state area has institutional relationships that social advertising reinforces by keeping the brand in front of property managers and board members continuously
The Opportunity
  • Retargeting — website visitors who did not convert are leaving with no follow-up
  • Video ads featuring the attorney — builds trust before a prospect ever calls
  • Educational content ads — capture leads earlier in the decision process
  • The existing New York Real Estate Video Podcast is purpose-built for Meta video ads — short clips from existing episodes can be repurposed as targeted ads reaching landlords, property managers, and board members in the NYC metro area without creating any new content
