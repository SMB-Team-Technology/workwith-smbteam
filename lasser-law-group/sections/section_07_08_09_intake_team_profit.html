Intake Assessment

Lasser Law Group handles complex real estate and commercial litigation matters — the kind of cases where a prospect has a real problem right now and is looking for someone to pick up the phone. Without a defined intake process, a visible contact path on the website, or after-hours coverage, the firm is generating friction at the exact moment a prospect is ready to engage. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No live chat observed on the website — a landlord facing an urgent tenant dispute or a property owner dealing with a construction defect needs to reach someone immediately, and the site offers no real-time contact option
  • Contact form is not visible above the fold — the primary conversion mechanism on the website is buried below multiple content sections, requiring visitors to scroll to find it
  • No confirmed intake process or intake team for handling web inquiries, phone calls, or after-hours leads — with nine attorneys and multiple staff, there is team capacity, but no confirmed protocol for ensuring rapid follow-up
  • No CRM or intake tracking observed — leads that arrive from multiple channels (phone, web form, referral) likely have no centralized tracking, making follow-up inconsistent
Blind Spot: Real estate and commercial litigation clients are often in an active, urgent situation — a tenant in violation, a construction dispute escalating, a closing that has gone sideways. A prospect in that state who calls after 5pm and reaches a voicemail will call the next firm on the list within minutes. The gap between the end of the business day and the start of the next one is where cases are lost — and most firms never see it happening.
The Positive
  • The firm has nine attorneys and three staff members — there is real team depth to delegate and systematize intake if a protocol is put in place
The Gap
  • Every lead that arrives after hours, on a weekend, or at a moment when the contact form is not visible is a potential case handed to a competitor — in Manhattan's competitive real estate law market, that is a meaningful and ongoing revenue loss
  • Without a defined intake process, the quality and speed of follow-up depends entirely on which attorney or staff member happens to see the inquiry first — inconsistent response is invisible to the firm but decisive for the prospect
  • A firm investing in paid advertising without a tight intake system will generate leads that do not convert at the rate they should, making every marketing dollar less effective
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • The primary contact mechanism is the "Contact Us" page linked from navigation — it is not visible without clicking away from the homepage, which means any visitor who does not look for it will not find it
  • No live chat widget is present anywhere on the site — the fastest-converting contact option for law firm websites is absent entirely
  • For a firm serving real estate clients who may have time-sensitive disputes, the absence of an immediate contact pathway on the homepage is a conversion gap that compounds with every new visitor who arrives from any source

Team Assessment

Lasser Law Group has 12 professionals across three offices — more team infrastructure than most firms at this stage, with real capacity to grow. But size without structure means the managing partner remains in the center of business development, client relationships, and firm operations simultaneously, which is a ceiling. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No confirmed management layer or operations leader — with nine attorneys across three locations, someone needs to own accountability structures and day-to-day operations so the managing partner can focus on growth
  • No dedicated marketing, intake, or business development staff observed — attorneys are likely handling client acquisition tasks that could be delegated or systematized
  • Three offices create coordination complexity without a confirmed operations infrastructure to manage consistency across locations
  • No confirmed performance metrics, team accountability systems, or structured onboarding processes for new hires — the foundations that allow a team to run without constant oversight
Blind Spot: A managing partner who is still the primary driver of business development and client relationships is also the primary constraint on how large the firm can grow. The attorneys may be excellent — but without a structure where someone else owns operations, marketing, and intake, the firm can only grow as fast as one person can manage everything. That is a ceiling that feels normal from inside it.
The Positive
  • Nine attorneys with individual profiles, credentials, and practice area depth means the firm has genuine capacity to handle significantly more volume — the talent infrastructure exists, it just needs operational structure around it
  • Three offices suggest the managing partner has already made at least partial moves toward distributed capacity — Westchester and Long Island presence is a meaningful foundation to build on
The Gap
  • Without an operations or accountability layer, adding marketing and lead generation will create more work, not more growth — the managing partner absorbs the demand rather than a system handling it
  • Three offices with no confirmed coordination infrastructure means each location is likely operating independently, missing the consistency and cross-referral potential that a unified firm structure creates
  • The firm cannot be sold, acquired, or valued as a business asset without a documented, replicable operational structure — and that structure does not exist yet
Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Profit Plan Assessment

Lasser Law Group operates a multi-attorney firm with three offices in one of the most expensive legal markets in the country — rent, salaries, and overhead for a firm this size are substantial. Without confirmed financial visibility into cost per case, profit margin by practice area, and monthly targets, revenue growth does not reliably translate into the owner taking more home. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No confirmed financial tracking system or visibility into cost per case, revenue per attorney, or margin by practice area — without this data, the firm cannot make informed decisions about where to invest or which cases to prioritize
  • Three-office overhead structure (Manhattan, Westchester, Long Island) creates significant fixed costs that must be offset by consistent revenue — without a profit plan, the firm is one slow quarter away from a cash flow problem
  • Practice area mix spans high-complexity, high-value matters (construction litigation, commercial leasing) and volume-based work (landlord-tenant disputes) — without cost-per-matter tracking, the firm may be spending significant attorney time on lower-margin work at the expense of higher-margin opportunities
  • No confirmed financial benchmarks or monthly revenue targets — growth without targets means success is undefined and hard to measure
Blind Spot: In a firm with nine attorneys and three offices, the most profitable work is often not the most visible work. Without practice area profitability data, a managing partner may be optimizing for revenue while the firm's highest-margin cases — the large construction disputes and commercial litigation matters — are getting less attention and less marketing investment than they deserve.
The Gap
  • A firm investing in marketing and new leads without first establishing profit visibility risks growing revenue while margins stay flat — more cases, more attorneys, more overhead, same net result for the owner
  • Without financial benchmarks, it is impossible to know whether a marketing investment is generating a return or just covering its own cost — and in a market this competitive, every investment needs to be accountable
  • The goal of dominating New York real estate law requires not just more cases but more profitable cases — and that requires knowing the numbers well enough to choose which work to pursue and which to refer out
Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity — and in a three-office, nine-attorney firm in Manhattan, knowing exactly where every dollar goes is not optional, it is the foundation of everything else the firm is trying to build.
