Intake Assessment

Grabb Law already runs a 24/7 answering service with immediate follow-up on intake — a real foundation many firms at this stage do not have. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No CRM or intake tracking software was mentioned on the call, so there is no visibility into how leads move from first call to signed case
  • Case selection criteria (soft tissue injury with more than $1,500 in vehicle damage) adds a qualifying step to intake, but it is unclear how quickly borderline cases get a decision
  • No defined follow-up sequence was described for leads who do not sign on the first call
  • Website contact form submissions were not confirmed to route into the same 24/7 intake process as phone calls
Blind Spot: Every lead the $25,000/month marketing budget generates is only as good as what happens in the first few minutes after it arrives — without tracking that handoff, it's difficult to know how many are being lost after the phone rings.
The Positive
  • 24/7 answering service is already in place
  • Immediate follow-up on intake is already an established standard, not just an aspiration
The Gap
  • Without tracking, there is no way to measure how many of the leads generated by $25,000/month in marketing spend are actually converting to signed cases
  • Selective case criteria protect quality but risk losing borderline cases to a competitor who responds faster
  • No follow-up sequence means cases that are already paid for through marketing spend can quietly slip away
Tightening intake means the firm's stated goal of 5–10 additional quality cases a month starts with converting more of what it already has — not spending more to generate it.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact form is above the fold on the homepage, with a second form accessible via modal — a strong starting point for capture
  • No live chat is present, so visitors with a question outside business hours have no way to get an immediate response online
  • It is not confirmed whether website form submissions route into the same 24/7 answering process used for phone intake

Team Assessment

Grabb Law runs on a six-person team — two attorneys and four paralegals — with case-quality standards that currently still run through Robert. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No named office manager or intake lead was mentioned — case selection criteria and marketing vendor oversight appear to sit with Robert directly
  • With $25,000/month spread across TV, radio, social, and website vendors, there was no mention of who manages or evaluates those vendor relationships day to day
  • No accountability structure was described for how the paralegal team tracks or reports on case status
  • No mention of cross-training or backup coverage if a team member is out
Blind Spot: Being the one who sets case-quality standards personally is also what keeps you tied to every intake decision — the firm cannot scale past what you personally have time to review.
The Positive
  • A six-person team with two attorneys and four paralegals is a real structure to build management layers on top of — many firms at this stage are running with far less
The Gap
  • Every vendor decision and case-quality judgment call that requires Robert directly is time he cannot spend elsewhere
  • Without a manager layer, growth in case volume means growth in Robert's day-to-day workload, not less of it
A team that can apply your case-quality standards without you in the room is what turns a six-person staff into a firm you can eventually step back from.

Profit Plan Assessment

Grabb Law's own annual revenue and marketing return were not available on the call — confirming them with Robert was left as a follow-up action item. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Annual revenue was not available on the call — even internally, this required a follow-up with Robert after the meeting
  • Roughly $25,000/month is committed to marketing vendors with no stated system for tracking return by channel
  • The stated goal — increase profit by adding 5–10 quality cases a month — has no attached profit plan or per-case cost tracking discussed on the call
  • Average case value was not stated as a dollar figure, only case-quality criteria, making it difficult to project return on the firm's own marketing spend
Blind Spot: Without knowing what a single case is actually worth on average, it's impossible to know whether the $25,000/month going out the door is producing a return — or just producing activity.
The Positive
  • The stated goal is explicitly framed around quality cases and profit — not just case volume — which shows the right instinct even without the numbers to back it yet
The Gap
  • Without a confirmed revenue baseline, it's not possible to know whether the current $25,000/month marketing spend is a sound investment or a drag on margin
  • Without average case value, the firm cannot know its true cost-per-case or return on marketing spend by channel
A real profit plan turns "increase profit by adding 5–10 cases a month" from a goal into a number Robert can track every month.
