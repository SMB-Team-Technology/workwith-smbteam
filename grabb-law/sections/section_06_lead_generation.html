Lead Generation

The firm did not surface among the named competitors in any of six local search tests run across its core practice areas.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed mobile and desktop scores could not be confirmed in this research pass — the public API returned a rate-limit error; these must be manually verified at pagespeed.web.dev before this proposal is finalized
  • No attorney biography is present — a professional photo appears, but no credentials, experience, or personal story is shown to build trust before a call comes in
  • Design patterns (modal contact form, stock-style layout) suggest the site is likely several years old, though an exact redesign date could not be confirmed
  • Mobile responsiveness could not be confirmed directly in this research pass — flagged for a manual spot-check
Blind Spot: The domain that routes to your firm — grabblaw.com — redirects to a site publicly branded "Grabb & Durando." That kind of identity mismatch between the name prospects search for and the name they land on is exactly what erodes trust signals in local search and confuses people cross-checking you across multiple sources before they call.
The Positive
  • The contact form and a second modal form are both above the fold on the homepage — a genuinely conversion-friendly structure many competitors do not have
  • A blog section exists, showing some ongoing content investment
The Gap
  • Without confirmed PageSpeed performance, there is real risk visitors abandon before the above-the-fold contact form ever fully loads
  • Without an attorney bio, prospects comparing multiple firms have nothing personal to differentiate Grabb Law from the next search result
  • The brand mismatch between "Grabb Law" and "Grabb & Durando" likely costs the firm searches from people looking specifically for the name they were referred to
Fixing the website's trust signals and performance is the fastest way to make the $25,000/month already being spent actually convert into cases.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Directory-aggregated data shows an estimated ~3.9 stars across ~46 reviews (a live GBP pull is recommended to confirm) — well below the 92–348 reviews held by the firm's three closest named competitors
  • Grabb Law did not surface among the named firms in any of six local search tests run for car-accident, personal-injury, and motorcycle-accident keywords in Tucson
  • A NAP (name/address/phone) inconsistency exists across the firm's own website and directory listings — three different phone numbers and two different addresses appear across Google-indexed sources
Blind Spot: Prospects doing due diligence before hiring an injury attorney often cross-check a firm across multiple directories. A phone number or address that doesn't match from one listing to the next can read as a red flag at the exact moment a prospect is deciding whom to call.
The Positive
  • Robert Grabb holds an Avvo Client's Choice Award — a credibility signal not yet reflected anywhere in the firm's higher-traffic search presence
The Gap
  • Every search where a competitor's name surfaces instead of Grabb Law's is a lead going straight to Karnas, Zanes, or Goldberg & Osborne
  • The NAP inconsistency is actively working against the firm's own local search rankings, functioning as an unforced trust penalty search engines can detect
Karnas Law Firm, PLLC — holds the highest review volume observed in this market (348 on Birdeye, 4.9 rating) and has built dedicated landing pages specifically for car-accident and motorcycle-accident search traffic — the exact keyword sets Grabb Law is not appearing for.
Closing the local SEO gap means Grabb Law starts showing up in the searches its own $25,000/month should already be winning.
