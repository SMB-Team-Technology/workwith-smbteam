Lead Generation

Fabbrini Law Group has 25 years of reputation and a strong review base behind it, but no confirmed paid advertising, no Local Service Ads presence, and zero organic visibility across six local searches tested for personal injury and criminal defense. Right now, new cases depend entirely on word of mouth in a market where several competitors are actively investing in every digital channel available.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed mobile and desktop scores could not be retrieved during this research pass (the PageSpeed Insights API returned a rate-limit error on repeated attempts) — flagged for a live manual check at pagespeed.web.dev before any final performance claims are made
  • No video content found anywhere on the site — video is one of the fastest ways to build trust with an injured or accused prospect before they ever call
  • Contact and consultation scheduling sits behind a link/section rather than a static form immediately visible above the fold on the homepage
  • No geo-targeted landing pages exist for surrounding suburbs (Naperville, Cicero, Oak Park, Evanston) — only general practice-area pages
The Positive
  • Strong, credentialed attorney bio above the fold — Jim's 25 years, DePaul Law credentials, and 100+ jury trials are clearly presented
  • Actively maintained blog with recent, relevant posts (truck, motorcycle, and car accident topics) — a real content asset most firms this size do not have
The Gap
  • A site with strong bio and content credibility but unverified performance and no video is likely converting fewer of the visitors it does get than it should
  • Every prospect who lands on the site and cannot immediately find a way to reach the firm is a prospect who may call the next name in their search results instead
A website that converts on its own is one less thing standing between Jim and a firm that runs without him in every conversation.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Two distinct, active Google Maps listings exist under the "Fabbrini Law Group" name at different addresses (161 N Clark St 17th Floor, and 6268 1/2 S Archer Ave) — splitting reviews and ranking signals across profiles instead of consolidating them
  • NAP data is inconsistent across at least 4 sources: the firm's own website (181 W Madison St / 312-625-5357, plus a second location at 5524 W. Belmont Ave / 312-900-8519), FindLaw/Avvo (321 N. Clark St / 312-494-3131), and Yelp (181 W Madison St / 312-494-3131)
  • Zero organic appearances across all 6 local-visibility searches run for personal injury and criminal defense terms in Chicago
Blind Spot: Two unmerged Google Business Profile listings do not just look messy — they actively split the review signals and search authority that should be consolidated under one profile, which can suppress both listings from ranking well instead of boosting either.
The Positive
  • Underlying review sentiment is strong — cross-referenced aggregators show a 4.7–4.8 star range across roughly 90–150 reviews, a solid foundation once the profile fragmentation is fixed
The Gap
  • A firm with 25 years in practice and strong reviews should be a top local result — instead, prospects searching for personal injury or criminal defense help in Chicago never see Fabbrini Law Group at all
  • Every one of those searches is currently being won by a named competitor instead
Disparti Law Group — dominant organic visibility across personal injury searches, backed by a review volume (~3,400+) an order of magnitude larger than Fabbrini's, in part because their profile and NAP data are consolidated and consistent.
Fixing NAP consistency and consolidating your Google Business Profile is one of the fastest, lowest-cost ways to start converting your reputation into visibility — and visibility into cases you no longer have to win by referral alone.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Personal Injury: this firm does not appear to be running Google search ads for personal injury terms — they may not be configured to show for these terms, or may be getting outbid by better-funded competitors
  • Criminal Defense: this firm does not appear to be running Google search ads for criminal defense/DUI terms either — no conversion tags (gtag/AW- IDs) or Google Tag Manager container were found on the homepage, consistent with no active paid search campaign
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Rosenfeld Injury Lawyers — heavy content and SEO footprint typical of a large personal injury PPC investment
  • Zayed Law Offices — multi-channel presence typical of PPC-heavy personal injury firms
  • Chicago DUI Lawyers / G&S DUI Attorneys and David L. Freidberg — both surface prominently with free-consultation ad-style messaging for criminal defense/DUI terms, suggesting active paid investment
The Opportunity
  • "Chicago car accident lawyer" and "chicago personal injury lawyer" — high-intent, currently uncontested by Fabbrini
  • "chicago criminal defense attorney" and "dui lawyer chicago" — the firm's website already confirms DUI and drug crime work, but no ad presence backs it up
  • Competitor-name conquesting (e.g. bidding around Disparti, Salvi Schostok & Pritchard) to intercept prospects already comparing firms
  • Neighboring suburb + practice area terms (Naperville, Cicero, Oak Park) where no geo-targeted content or ads currently exist
Blind Spot: With zero paid search presence confirmed across two practice areas, every single lead currently flowing to Fabbrini Law Group is coming from referral and reputation alone — meaning there is effectively no floor under new case volume if referrals slow down.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • No LSA presence was confirmed for the firm in this research pass — this reflects an opportunity gap, not a confirmed absence, since a live rendered SERP was not accessible in this environment
  • Google Screened status could not be confirmed either way and should be checked live before finalizing outreach
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • No competitor could be confirmed holding a Google Screened badge with the tools available in this research pass — LSAs are a well-established, competitive channel for personal injury and DUI work in Chicago generally, so this should not be read as an open field
The Opportunity
  • With a 4.7–4.8 star reputation already in place, Fabbrini Law Group is well-positioned to qualify for and benefit from Google Screened status once LSAs are set up
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • A pay-per-lead model gives Jim predictable cost-per-case data — exactly the kind of KPI he is trying to build a dashboard to track
