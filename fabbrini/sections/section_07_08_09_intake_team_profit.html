Intake Assessment

Fabbrini Law Group has one dedicated receptionist as its confirmed first point of contact, with no after-hours coverage or defined follow-up sequence identified. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No after-hours coverage was identified — calls that come in outside business hours have no confirmed path to a live response
  • No defined follow-up sequence for leads who do not sign immediately was discussed on this call
  • A single receptionist is the only confirmed intake resource for a firm running two practice areas across two locations
  • Intake capacity has never been tested against a real influx of digital leads, because no paid or organic lead system currently exists
The Positive
  • The firm already delegates first contact to a dedicated receptionist rather than routing every call to Jim personally — a real foundation to build a stronger intake process on
The Gap
  • Every new lead generation investment is at risk if intake cannot absorb the additional volume it is designed to produce
  • Without after-hours coverage, prospects searching for help outside business hours may simply call the next firm on the list
  • No follow-up sequence means leads who do not decide on the first call may never be recontacted
A strategic, data-driven ownership role depends on knowing every lead is captured and tracked — not on hoping the front desk catches everything.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Consultation scheduling is accessible via a link/section rather than a static form immediately visible above the fold
  • No live chat is present on the site, so visitors browsing after hours have no way to start a conversation in the moment
  • No video content is present to build trust with a visitor before they decide to call or submit a form

Team Assessment

Fabbrini Law Group has five dedicated staff handling casework, but no leadership layer exists to run the business side — Jim is personally building the firm's own AI tools and KPI dashboard in addition to practicing law. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
"escape daily legal work and focus on business strategy"
The Problem
  • No ops or leadership role exists to absorb the business, technology, or strategic work Jim is currently doing himself
  • Jim is personally building the firm's AI automation tools (medical record timelines, daily briefings, an in-progress firm dashboard) on top of his caseload
  • He has raised direct concern about the quality and vetting of outside help after prior pitches from other providers — a trust gap that needs to be addressed directly
  • No accountability structure was described beyond Jim overseeing the team's casework directly
Blind Spot: Being technically capable enough to build your own tools can make it harder to hand that work off — the very skill that got the firm this far is now one of the things standing between Jim and the strategic role he says he wants.
The Positive
  • Five dedicated specialists (2 paralegals, a medical records specialist, a liens specialist, and a receptionist) already handle casework — a real team, not a solo operation
  • Jim has already built working AI tools himself using Claude and Zapier — a level of technical sophistication uncommon among firm owners, and a strong signal of genuine readiness to adopt new systems
The Gap
  • Every hour Jim spends building tools or managing the tech stack is an hour not spent on casework, strategy, or the free time he is working toward
  • Without a leadership layer, growth in case volume means growth in Jim's personal workload, not less of it
  • A five-person team can absorb more casework only if someone besides Jim is running the operational side
The strategic, KPI-driven ownership role Jim described is only possible once someone else is running the day-to-day — not before.

Profit Plan Assessment

No centralized KPI or financial dashboard exists at Fabbrini Law Group today — Jim is building one himself from scratch because the firm's current tech stack does not provide one. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • No centralized dashboard exists to track firm performance — Jim confirmed he is building a firm dashboard himself, in progress, because one does not currently exist
  • The current tech stack (Lawmatics, Filevine, M365, Zapier, Trustbooks, Bench.ca) is siloed, with no unified view connecting case data to financial performance
  • Profitability and margin data were not part of this conversation — consistent with the absence of a system that would surface that data in the first place
  • Marketing and case-acquisition costs cannot be evaluated against returns without a working KPI view in place
Blind Spot: Without a centralized dashboard, it is difficult to know whether the firm's most time-consuming cases are actually its most profitable ones — growth could be adding work without adding proportional profit.
The Gap
  • Without financial visibility, Jim cannot confidently evaluate whether new marketing investment is paying off
  • Decisions about staffing, case mix, and growth are currently being made without the numbers to back them up
  • Building a dashboard alone, on top of casework, means this gap will not close on its own timeline
A strategic, data-driven ownership role starts with the data actually existing somewhere Jim can see it — not with him building it himself, one tool at a time.
