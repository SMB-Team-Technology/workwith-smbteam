Here Is Where Fabbrini Law Group LLC Stands Today

Jim Fabbrini has practiced law in Chicago for 25 years, building Fabbrini Law Group around personal injury work — medical record analysis, lien negotiation, demand packets, and settlement breakdowns — alongside an active criminal defense practice. He has a five-person team of dedicated specialists and a strong reputation, but the firm is invisible in the digital channels that drive new cases today: it did not surface in a single one of six local-visibility searches run across both practice areas, and its own contact information is fragmented across at least four address variants online.

Stage 6 is the stage where Jim finally trades daily casework for the strategic, KPI-driven ownership role he described wanting — and it is exactly where this roadmap is built to take him.

Where You Are Today

Stage 3: Solo Practitioner
  • New case flow depends on 25 years of reputation and referrals — no paid or organic digital system currently produces leads
  • The firm does not appear in local search results for personal injury or criminal defense anywhere in Chicago
  • NAP data is fragmented across at least four address variants and multiple phone numbers, actively working against local visibility
  • Jim is personally building the firm's AI tools and KPI dashboard himself, on top of running cases — there is no operational layer absorbing that work

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between a firm built on reputation and a firm built on systems is exactly what stands between Jim and the strategic, data-driven role he says he wants — closing it is a matter of building the visibility and leadership layers that do not exist yet, not of earning more trust in the market.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed mobile and desktop scores could not be retrieved during this research pass (the PageSpeed Insights API returned a rate-limit error on repeated attempts) — flagged for a live manual check at pagespeed.web.dev before any final performance claims are made

The Positive
  • Strong, credentialed attorney bio above the fold — Jim's 25 years, DePaul Law credentials, and 100+ jury trials are clearly presented
  • Actively maintained blog with recent, relevant posts (truck, motorcycle, and car accident topics) — a real content asset most firms this size do not have
The Gap
  • A site with strong bio and content credibility but unverified performance and no video is likely converting fewer of the visitors it does get than it should
  • Every prospect who lands on the site and cannot immediately find a way to reach the firm is a prospect who may call the next name in their search results instead
A website that converts on its own is one less thing standing between Jim and a firm that runs without him in every conversation.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Two distinct, active Google Maps listings exist under the "Fabbrini Law Group" name at different addresses (161 N Clark St 17th Floor, and 6268 1/2 S Archer Ave) — splitting reviews and ranking signals across profiles instead of consolidating them
  • NAP data is inconsistent across at least 4 sources: the firm's own website (181 W Madison St / 312-625-5357, plus a second location at 5524 W. Belmont Ave / 312-900-8519), FindLaw/Avvo (321 N. Clark St / 312-494-3131), and Yelp (181 W Madison St / 312-494-3131)

Blind Spot: Two unmerged Google Business Profile listings do not just look messy — they actively split the review signals and search authority that should be consolidated under one profile, which can suppress both listings from ranking well instead of boosting either.
The Positive

The Gap
  • A firm with 25 years in practice and strong reviews should be a top local result — instead, prospects searching for personal injury or criminal defense help in Chicago never see Fabbrini Law Group at all
  • Every one of those searches is currently being won by a named competitor instead
Disparti Law Group — dominant organic visibility across personal injury searches, backed by a review volume (~3,400+) an order of magnitude larger than Fabbrini's, in part because their profile and NAP data are consolidated and consistent.
Fixing NAP consistency and consolidating your Google Business Profile is one of the fastest, lowest-cost ways to start converting your reputation into visibility — and visibility into cases you no longer have to win by referral alone.
