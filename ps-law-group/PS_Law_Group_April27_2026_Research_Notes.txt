STEP 10 — COMPETITIVE URGENCY SCORE
===================================================================

Score: 5 out of 10

Rationale:
PS Law Group operates in a highly specialized B2B niche — licensing and regulatory law for cannabis, hemp, kratom, supplements, and other regulated industries. This is not a consumer-facing practice; clients are businesses, not individuals. As a result, the traditional local 3-pack / Google Maps competitive dynamic matters less than brand authority, content presence, referral networks, and national SEO.

However, several meaningful competitive threats exist:

1. Greenspoon Marder LLP (Fort Lauderdale / National)
   The most significant competitive threat. Ranked Band 1 nationally in cannabis law by Chambers USA since 2019. Over 25 dedicated cannabis/hemp attorneys. National footprint with FL office. Significantly more online credibility, directory presence, and brand authority than PS Law Group. Clients researching cannabis licensing attorneys are far more likely to find Greenspoon Marder first.

2. Mr. Cannabis Law / Dustin Robinson (Boca Raton, FL)
   Active content marketing, podcasts, multi-state licensing work, and deep community ties (NORML, Florida Hemp Council). Visible in searches for Florida cannabis/hemp law. Positions himself as a practitioner-entrepreneur — similar archetype to Paula — but has invested more in digital presence and personal branding.

3. GrayRobinson PA (Statewide FL)
   Richard Blau leads one of the most recognized cannabis practices in Florida. National Chambers USA rankings. Multi-state regulated industries expertise. Well-resourced statewide firm.

While these competitors are not aggressively running paid ads in this niche (cannabis advertising restrictions limit paid search), they have meaningful advantages in organic authority, directory presence, content, and name recognition in the regulated industries legal space. PS Law Group has a word-of-mouth-only acquisition model, minimal digital presence, no verified GBP reviews surfaced, minimal Avvo profile, and no content or paid ad strategy. The gap is real — but this market has not yet been dominated by a single omnipresent digital player, which creates a window.
