Lead Generation

Rex Legal is a solo PI firm in one of Colorado's most competitive legal markets, currently operating with no active paid advertising, no LSA presence, no content marketing, and a Google Business Profile that tells potential clients the firm is closed on weekends — when accidents are most likely to happen. Every new case Michael gets right now comes from referrals alone, and that means every day without a lead system is a day the gap between Rex Legal and its competitors grows wider.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • The website returned HTTP 403 errors to all automated research tools — meaning search engine crawlers and performance testing tools may face the same access restrictions, limiting indexability and technical SEO performance
  • PageSpeed Insights scores were not retrievable at time of research due to access restrictions — these must be run manually at pagespeed.web.dev before the proposal meeting; slow mobile load times are among the top reasons PI prospects abandon a site and call a competitor
  • No blog content visible in Google's index — zero published articles, guides, or FAQs that could rank for long-tail PI and TBI search terms where a solo attorney with a personal story can outperform large firms
  • No geo-targeted landing pages confirmed for surrounding Denver suburbs (Aurora, Lakewood, Arvada, Westminster, Englewood, Thornton) — each suburb represents significant additional PI case volume that the site is not positioned to capture
  • A URL typo was found in one practice area page: "lytf-accidents" instead of "lyft-accidents" — a technical SEO error that prevents the page from ranking for Lyft accident searches
Blind Spot: Rex Legal appears to operate a secondary domain (rexlegaldenver.com) that returns a connection error — if this domain was ever used or linked to, it may be sending broken link signals that dilute the primary domain's authority. Additionally, a third domain (changemytrajectory.com) appeared connected to the firm in search results and also returned access errors, creating potential duplicate authority issues that should be audited and resolved.
The Positive
  • The site has dedicated practice area pages for a wide range of PI case types — including TBI, paralysis, amputations, and burn injuries — giving Google content signals for high-value catastrophic injury searches
  • Michael's personal TBI story is present on the site and gives Rex Legal a compelling, authentic differentiator that large competing firms simply cannot replicate
The Gap
  • A site that search engines and performance tools cannot reliably crawl will not rank — every day without a properly optimized, accessible site is a day competitors capture the organic traffic Rex Legal should own
  • No blog means no long-tail SEO and no AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) content for AI platforms like ChatGPT and Gemini — the very strategy discussed on the call as the foundation for a cheaper, more sustainable lead source
  • Without geo pages for Aurora, Lakewood, and surrounding suburbs, Rex Legal is invisible to PI prospects in the areas that generate a significant share of Denver metro accident cases
A website that converts visitors into consultations is the foundation of every other marketing investment — without it, ad spend generates clicks that go nowhere, and the path to 25 consistent cases stays blocked.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Google Business Profile shows only 41 reviews at 4.9 stars — a quality score that ranks among Denver's best, but a volume that makes Rex Legal nearly invisible in the local 3-pack, where competitors with 407–1,926 reviews dominate every high-intent PI search
  • GBP hours show the firm closed on Saturdays and Sundays — the transcript specifically identified Friday and Saturday nights as prime accident times; closed hours actively depress GBP ranking for off-hours searches
  • NAP inconsistency detected across platforms: the official website lists Suite 1515B, Birdeye lists #416, and Nextdoor shows no suite number — inconsistent NAP signals directly reduce local SEO trust and 3-pack eligibility
  • Rex Legal does not appear in the local 3-pack for "personal injury lawyer Denver," "car accident attorney Denver," or "personal injury attorney Denver Colorado" — the firm is functionally invisible to the majority of PI prospects conducting local searches
Blind Spot: Google's local 3-pack algorithm heavily weights review volume, proximity, and GBP completeness — including business hours. A profile showing the firm closed on weekends signals limited availability, which Google interprets as lower relevance for emergency-type searches like car accident attorneys. Simply switching GBP hours to "Open 24 hours" (paired with a 24/7 virtual receptionist to actually answer) can produce a measurable increase in 3-pack impressions within 30–60 days at no advertising cost.
The Positive
  • 4.9-star average rating on 41 reviews is exceptional — every existing reviewer is a raving advocate, and the quality of those reviews gives Rex Legal a conversion advantage when prospects do find the listing
The Gap
  • At 41 reviews, Rex Legal is not competitive in the local 3-pack for any broad PI keyword in Denver — reaching 100–150 reviews would meaningfully shift 3-pack visibility and is the single highest-leverage short-term SEO action available
  • Without consistent NAP signals across all directories, Google's local algorithm cannot confidently serve Rex Legal's listing for geo-specific searches — fixing the suite number discrepancy alone is a foundational local SEO requirement
Bachus & Schanker — 1,926 Google reviews at 4.7 stars with a fully optimized, multi-location GBP presence, ensuring they appear in the 3-pack for virtually every PI search in Denver. Their review volume alone makes them the default choice for prospects who rely on Google to compare attorneys.
Every month without a review-building system and a corrected GBP is a month Bachus & Schanker, Fuicelli & Lee, and Zaner Law extend their lead — and a month the consistent case flow that funds Michael's path to $500k personal income gets further away.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Rex Legal does not appear to be running Google search ads for "personal injury lawyer Denver," "car accident attorney Denver," or related PI terms at this time — the firm may be getting outbid by significantly better-funded competitors, or ads may not currently be configured for these terms
  • The prior LSA campaign with a previous provider was stopped after spending $120k and generating only $160k in revenue — a 25% margin that left Michael deeply skeptical of paid advertising; the issue was a combination of insufficient budget for the Denver market ($8k/mo vs. the ~$12k/mo needed for results) and poor lead quality (property damage calls rather than injury cases)
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Bachus & Schanker — confirmed running aggressive Google Ads campaigns including bidding on competitor keywords (subject of active litigation), TV commercial campaigns, and YouTube ads — operating on a marketing budget that dominates every paid channel in the Denver PI market
  • Fuicelli & Lee — established 20+ year firm with marketing infrastructure consistent with active Google search campaigns; their long domain history and high review count gives their Quality Score a structural advantage over newer competitors
  • Zaner Law — prominent firm with significant verdicts record; likely running search ads targeting catastrophic injury and TBI keywords where Rex Legal has the strongest competitive differentiator but no paid presence
The Opportunity
  • High-intent keyword targets: "car accident lawyer Denver," "personal injury attorney Denver," "motorcycle accident lawyer Denver," "TBI attorney Colorado" — terms where a well-targeted, properly budgeted campaign can generate qualified injury leads within the first 30 days
  • Case-type specific targeting: "truck accident attorney Denver," "slip and fall lawyer Denver," "wrongful death attorney Colorado" — longer-tail terms with lower competition where Rex Legal can get quality clicks at lower cost-per-click than broad PI terms
  • TBI differentiation targeting: "traumatic brain injury attorney Denver" and "brain injury lawyer Colorado" — where Michael's personal TBI story and dedicated TBI page give him a conversion advantage that no large competing firm can match; this is the keyword category where ad spend works hardest for Rex Legal specifically
  • Suburb targeting: "personal injury attorney Aurora CO," "car accident lawyer Lakewood," "injury attorney Englewood CO" — neighboring city terms where competition is lower and CPLs are more manageable for a solo firm entering paid advertising
Blind Spot: The prior campaign failed not because Google Ads don't work for PI — they absolutely do for every competitor running them in this market — but because the budget was below the Denver market floor, the targeting was too broad (capturing property damage calls), and there was no SEO foundation driving organic quality signals that improve ad performance. A properly structured campaign with injury-specific negative keywords, the right budget, and a website optimized to convert would produce fundamentally different results than the prior campaign.
