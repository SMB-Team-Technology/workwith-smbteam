Intake Assessment

Rex Legal currently has no intake infrastructure — Michael personally handles every call, consultation, and follow-up, with a Google Business Profile that tells prospects the firm is closed on weekends, the exact hours when car accidents, motorcycle accidents, and slip-and-fall injuries are most likely to occur. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Michael personally handles every intake call — there is no delegation, no answering service, and no coverage when he is in a deposition, on a call, or unavailable after 5pm on weekdays
  • Google Business Profile shows the firm closed on Saturdays and Sundays — Friday and Saturday nights are when Denver's accident volume peaks, and any prospect who calls during those hours reaches no one
  • No virtual receptionist or 24/7 answering service in place — the transcript explicitly identified this gap and flagged it as a recommended fix, yet it has not been implemented
  • No defined follow-up sequence for prospects who contact the firm but do not immediately schedule — leads who reach voicemail or a contact form and hear nothing quickly move on to the next attorney they find
Blind Spot: PI prospects make the decision to call within hours of an accident — they are in pain, scared, and searching. The attorney who answers first has an enormous advantage. Studies consistently show that law firm leads that are not contacted within five minutes convert at a fraction of the rate of those contacted immediately. Every missed weekend call is not just a lost lead — it is a case that likely goes to Bachus & Schanker, which operates with a full intake team and 24/7 coverage.
The Positive
  • Michael's 4.9-star review average suggests that when prospects do reach him, the client experience is exceptional — the intake quality problem is about availability and coverage, not about what happens once contact is made
The Gap
  • At 15–20 current cases, even one or two missed weekend calls per month could represent $40,000–$80,000 in lost annual revenue at estimated case values — calls that were never answered, never followed up, and never converted
  • Without after-hours coverage, any investment in paid advertising will generate leads that call at night or on weekends and reach voicemail — converting ad spend into lost opportunities rather than cases
  • Without a structured follow-up system, the gap between a prospect's first contact and signing is left entirely to chance — a solo attorney cannot be expected to remember and chase every inquiry while managing an active caseload
Michael's path to the 25-case threshold that triggers the admin hire — and the $500k personal income that represents financial security — runs directly through a 24/7 intake system that captures every lead he is currently paying to generate or missing entirely.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • A contact page exists at rex-legal.com/contact-us/ — but whether a contact form is present above the fold on the homepage (where most conversions happen) could not be confirmed due to site access restrictions
  • No live chat or chat widget was observed on the site — prospects who land on the website at night or on weekends have no immediate way to reach anyone, which dramatically reduces conversion rates for after-hours traffic
  • The website's contact infrastructure should be reviewed and strengthened to ensure a prominent, above-fold call-to-action and a contact form visible on every key practice area page — particularly the TBI and car accident pages that are most likely to attract high-intent injury prospects

These findings are also referenced in the Website Audit section.

Team Assessment

Rex Legal is a firm of one — Michael Rex is the attorney, the intake coordinator, the marketer, the case manager, and the client relationship. Every hour he spends on administrative tasks is an hour not spent on casework or business development, and the firm has a hard physical ceiling on how many cases it can handle without a hire. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Michael is the sole employee — no admin, no paralegal, no receptionist, and no support staff of any kind; the firm's capacity is capped entirely by one person's available hours
  • Every non-billable function — phone calls, scheduling, paperwork, follow-up, marketing decisions — consumes hours that could be spent on casework, client relationships, or building revenue-generating activities
  • The firm has a demonstrated ceiling around 20 active cases — Michael confirmed that hitting 25+ cases consistently would trigger the admin hire, but without a lead system that reliably hits that threshold, the hire never happens
  • There is no accountability structure, no delegation framework, and no process documentation — everything lives in Michael's head, which means nothing can scale without him being personally involved
Blind Spot: The admin hire is not the destination — it is the unlock. Michael correctly identified that hiring an admin at 25 cases would free up capacity for 50+ cases. But the bigger blind spot is that the admin hire itself changes Michael's relationship with the firm: once someone else handles intake, scheduling, and administrative work, Michael's hours go entirely to the highest-value activities — client work, case strategy, and referral relationships — which is what drives revenue toward $500k personal income. The hire is worth far more than the capacity it creates.
The Gap
  • The absence of any team means the firm cannot handle increased lead volume even if a marketing campaign produces it — a surge in calls with no one to answer them, schedule them, or follow up with them converts into frustration, not cases
  • Without delegation, Michael cannot step back from the day-to-day without the firm stalling — he cannot take time off, cannot be unavailable, and cannot focus exclusively on the high-value work that moves the firm forward
  • A solo attorney approaching 20 cases is at or near capacity — adding marketing without adding support risks burning out the attorney, degrading client service, and ultimately limiting the firm's ability to grow past the very threshold it is trying to reach
The first hire is not an expense — it is the investment that makes every other investment pay off. Without an admin, Michael cannot reach 50 cases, cannot reach $500k personal income, and cannot stop trading hours for dollars.

Profit Plan Assessment

Rex Legal generated $370k in 2025 revenue, yet Michael's stated goal is $500k in personal income — a gap that cannot be closed by revenue alone without a clear profit plan that defines what comes in, what goes to overhead, and what Michael actually takes home. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • At $370k in firm revenue, Michael's personal income is meaningfully below his $500k personal income target — that gap cannot be closed by more cases alone without understanding and controlling margin
  • The prior $120k LSA campaign returned only $160k in revenue at 25% margin — Michael correctly identified this as a failure, but without a framework for evaluating marketing ROI going forward, the same mistake can happen again with any new campaign
  • No stated financial visibility beyond top-line revenue — no tracking of cost per acquired case, no understanding of which case types are most profitable, no clear view of what marketing spend should return to be considered a success
  • Michael's personal income goal ($500k) is framed in isolation from a revenue and margin plan — knowing the income target is step one, but without modeling the revenue and overhead structure needed to hit it, it remains an aspiration rather than a plan
Blind Spot: Not all PI cases are equally profitable. A $10k soft-tissue car accident case and a $200k TBI case both take time and attention, but the TBI case may require more upfront work while delivering dramatically better long-term margin. Without tracking revenue by case type, Michael may be optimizing his marketing and intake for case types that are not his highest-margin opportunities — including TBI and catastrophic injury cases where his personal story gives him a unique competitive advantage.
The Positive
  • Michael clearly understands his revenue figure and has the self-awareness to articulate the gap between $370k revenue and $500k personal income — this financial clarity is the starting point for building a real profit plan
  • Operating as a solo practitioner with no payroll overhead keeps margins structurally high relative to multi-attorney firms — every dollar of revenue increase flows directly toward personal income in a way that would not be possible at a larger firm
The Gap
  • Without knowing his cost per acquired case, Michael cannot evaluate whether any marketing investment is performing — he can see revenue, but he cannot see the profit contribution of each marketing channel
  • The $500k personal income goal requires not just more revenue but a clear model: how many cases at what average value, at what margin, with what overhead, produces $500k in personal income — that math has not been done
  • A coaching relationship that helps Michael model his profit plan and track it monthly would give him the clarity to make marketing and operational decisions based on real data, rather than gut feel and past experience with a failed campaign
The $500k personal income goal Michael defined is not just a revenue target — it is the specific number that means financial security. Getting there requires not just more cases, but a clear profit plan that maps revenue, margin, and overhead to the take-home number that changes Michael's life.
