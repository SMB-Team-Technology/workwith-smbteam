Here Is Where Law Offices of Ron Feinman Stands Today

Ron Feinman has spent decades building one of Lynchburg's most trusted elder law and estate tax planning practices — a firm on track to reach $1.25M in revenue in 2026 entirely on the strength of its reputation and referrals. The single most important finding: the team needs to be rebuilt before the firm can scale — and without the right systems in place to attract and retain A-players, the bottleneck will only deepen.

Stage 6 is where the firm runs itself — where Ron can pursue the "family office" expansion and delegate 3–4 hours of daily work without the whole operation depending on him personally.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Profit Plan Assessment

The Law Offices of Ron Feinman is on track to generate $1.25M in 2026 and has set an ambitious $2.5M target for 2027 — but no financial dashboard, profit tracking system, or cost-per-matter analysis was referenced on the call. The Problem

The Positive
  • Revenue is growing and on track for $1.25M in 2026 despite a significant staffing disruption — the firm's underlying economics are sound

  • Marketing investment, hiring costs, and practice area expansion all need to be sized against the firm's capacity to absorb them — without financial modeling, these decisions are made on instinct rather than data
  • The freedom Ron wants — delegating work, expanding into "family office," not being the bottleneck — requires a firm that is profitable enough to fund its own growth without Ron carrying the weight personally
