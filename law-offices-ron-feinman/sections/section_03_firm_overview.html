Here Is Where Law Offices of Ron Feinman Stands Today

Ron Feinman has spent decades building one of Lynchburg's most trusted elder law and estate tax planning practices — a firm on track to reach $1.25M in revenue in 2026 entirely on the strength of its reputation and referrals. The firm is productive and profitable, but Ron's ability to grow further is being held back by a staffing gap that is forcing him to manage the business instead of lead it. The single most important finding: the team needs to be rebuilt before the firm can scale — and without the right systems in place to attract and retain A-players, the bottleneck will only deepen.

Stage 6 is where the firm runs itself — where Ron can pursue the "family office" expansion and delegate 3–4 hours of daily work without the whole operation depending on him personally.

Where You Are Today

Stage 4: Small Business Manager
  • Revenue is growing — $1.25M projected for 2026 — but the firm recently lost two attorneys and is operating short-handed
  • Hiring is managed personally using Indeed and LinkedIn with poor results; finding attorneys for complex elder law work in Lynchburg is the firm's primary bottleneck
  • New clients come through referrals — there is no paid advertising, no local SEO presence, and no system for generating leads beyond word of mouth
  • The firm's online presence (4 Google reviews, invisible in local search) dramatically understates its credibility in the market

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between where the firm is today and where Ron wants it to be is not about talent or reputation — it is about systems, and systems can be built.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Law Offices of Ron Feinman
Ron is operating at Stage 4 — a Small Business Manager running a respected practice that is productive on the strength of its reputation, but not yet built to scale without his direct involvement. The single most urgent gap is the team: the recent loss of two attorneys has left Ron managing caseload, hiring, and operations simultaneously, which is unsustainable and directly blocks the path to $2.5M in 2027. Until the team is rebuilt with the right systems for attracting and retaining A-players, Ron cannot delegate, cannot pursue the "family office" expansion he envisions, and cannot reclaim the 3–4 hours of daily work he wants to give back to himself.
