Website Audit

The Problem
  • Website is estimated 5–8 years old — mid-era WordPress template design that does not reflect the caliber of a 39-year criminal defense practice or inspire confidence in first-time visitors
  • PageSpeed scores: not obtainable via automated tools during research — manual verification recommended at pagespeed.web.dev. The site uses a 301 redirect from the primary domain (arandallhaas.com → fortlauderdalecriminaldefenselawyer.us), which adds page load latency and typically depresses mobile scores; sites of this type and age typically score 30–55 mobile / 55–75 desktop
  • Contact form is buried on the /contact/ page only — there is no form or callback option visible on the homepage where most visitors land and make their decision to stay or leave
  • No video content anywhere on the site — criminal defense clients make highly personal, high-stakes decisions and video is one of the most powerful tools for building the trust that converts a visitor into a call
Blind Spot: The primary domain arandallhaas.com redirects via a 301 to fortlauderdalecriminaldefenselawyer.us — a keyword-stuffed domain that is not memorable and creates a branding disconnect. Visitors who search for Randall Haas by name land on a URL that looks like a generic directory listing. This undermines brand trust at the exact moment a prospect is trying to confirm they have found the right attorney.
The Positive
  • Attorney bio page is substantive — 39 years of experience, former Assistant Public Defender, Nova Southeastern law background, and a compelling personal philosophy create strong credibility anchors for the right visitor
  • Blog is active with recent, specific, keyword-relevant content on sex offender statutes and Florida criminal law — this signals topical authority to Google when properly optimized
The Gap
  • A slow, outdated site with a buried contact form means the traffic the firm does receive is converting at a fraction of what a modern, conversion-optimized site would produce — costing the firm cases every month without a dollar spent on ads
  • The domain redirect from the primary brand domain to a keyword URL is likely suppressing both SEO performance and direct type-in traffic from satisfied clients referring the firm to others
  • No case results, no video, and no testimonials on the homepage mean first-time visitors have no immediate reason to call — they will find a competitor who makes the decision easier
A rebuilt, conversion-optimized website is the foundation that makes every other investment in this audit work — it is where leads turn into clients, and where Randall's reputation finally matches the platform he deserves.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Firm does not appear to be in the Google local 3-pack for any observed criminal defense search in Fort Lauderdale — including DUI, criminal defense attorney, sex crimes, drug charges, and federal crimes
  • Confirmed NAP inconsistency: Yellow Pages lists a different address (633 SE 3rd Ave Suite 301) and a different phone number (954-388-9001) than the website — two separate NAP conflicts that directly suppress Google local rankings
  • No geo-targeting landing pages for surrounding cities (Hollywood, Pompano Beach, Davie, Coral Springs, Miramar) — missed organic traffic from the firm's stated service area of South Florida
  • 133 Google reviews at 5.0 stars is a genuine asset, but the most recent review is from August 2025 — a 9-month gap in review velocity that signals to Google a decline in activity
Blind Spot: Google's local ranking algorithm heavily weights NAP consistency across the web. When the firm's address and phone number appear differently on Yellow Pages than on the website, Google cannot confidently confirm which location and contact information is correct — so it depresses rankings for all of them. These inconsistencies may also exist on Yelp, Bing, Apple Maps, and other platforms that were inaccessible during research. Every directory that shows conflicting data is an anchor pulling the firm down in local search.
The Positive
  • 133 Google reviews at 5.0 stars is a strong baseline — particularly meaningful in a high-stakes practice area where trust is the deciding factor. This puts the firm ahead of Todd Onore (37 reviews) and Kenneth Padowitz (50 FindLaw reviews) in raw review volume
The Gap
  • Without 3-pack presence, the firm is invisible to the estimated 70–80% of legal service searches that begin on Google — these are clients who are actively searching right now, ready to hire, and finding a competitor instead
  • Rossen Law Firm's 338 Google reviews and 1,000+ cross-platform reviews create a review gap that will become increasingly difficult to close without a systematic review-generation program
Rossen Law Firm — 338 Google reviews at 5.0 stars, 1,000+ total reviews across all platforms, plus dedicated practice area landing pages for every search term Haas should be ranking for. They dominate the 3-pack and page one for virtually every criminal defense keyword in this market.
Getting into the Google 3-pack for criminal defense in Fort Lauderdale means the phone rings without Randall having to chase every client — that is the system his 39-year reputation deserves to be connected to.
