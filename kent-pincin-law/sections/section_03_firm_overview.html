Here Is Where Kent Pincin Law Stands Today

Kent Pincin Law has grown from two attorneys to six in a single year, built almost entirely on high-quality employment law and personal injury referrals — 90% of cases arrive as pre-screened referrals, with zero paid marketing or advertising in place. That growth has outpaced the firm's operational systems: Michael Kent personally handles roughly 90% of case acquisition and intake himself, and the firm is now turning away referral volume — including live transfers — simply because it lacks the capacity to process it.

Stage 6 is where Michael and Emily stop being the single point of failure for every case, every hire, and every piece of IT infrastructure — and finally become pure CEOs, managing managers instead of managing everything.

The gap between six attorneys and the 12-15 attorney firm Michael and Emily are building is not a marketing gap first — it is an operational one, and closing it is what makes the $5M+/year personal distribution goal achievable for each partner.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Kent Pincin Law
Kent Pincin Law is operating at Stage 4 — a Small Business Manager with real momentum but no systems built to match it. The single most urgent gap is not demand: it is capacity. The firm is already turning away qualified referral volume because attorney-led intake cannot scale past what Michael Kent can personally review, and there is no delegation structure — no case-assignment system, no dedicated ops leader — to relieve that bottleneck as headcount grows toward 12-15 attorneys. Until that operational foundation exists, the firm cannot safely add the paid lead generation that would otherwise be the obvious next step, and the partners' goal of becoming "pure CEOs managing managers" will remain out of reach.
