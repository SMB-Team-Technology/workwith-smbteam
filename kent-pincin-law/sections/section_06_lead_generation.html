Lead Generation

Kent Pincin Law has built a fast-growing employment law and personal injury practice entirely on referrals — 90% of cases arrive as pre-screened introductions, with no paid advertising or SEO strategy ever deployed. That means every lead the firm gets today, it gets by chance rather than by design.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed scores could not be independently obtained at time of this audit due to tooling rate limits — recommend a manual re-check before finalizing any website scope, though the site's modern build suggests this is a lower-priority concern than lead generation itself
  • Contact form is not visible above the fold — the full intake form only appears mid-page, under a dedicated "Contact Us" section
  • No live chat is present to capture visitors who are ready to act right now
  • Blog exists but shows no visible post dates, making it impossible to confirm the content is actively maintained
Blind Spot: The website itself is not the constraint holding this firm back — it is mobile responsive, has real attorney bios, and a clear above-the-fold CTA. The real risk is investing in a rebuild before fixing lead generation and intake, which would spend budget on the wrong problem first.
The Positive
  • Site is mobile responsive with a clear "Get Help Now" call-to-action above the fold
  • Dedicated attorney bio pages exist for both founders and additional team members — a trust signal most growing firms lack
The Gap
  • A mid-page contact form means visitors who decide quickly have to scroll and search before they can act
  • No live chat means after-hours visitors have no way to start a conversation immediately
  • An unclear blog cadence signals to Google — and to prospects — that the site may not be actively maintained
A website that converts faster is only worth building once there is a real flow of paid traffic to send to it — for now, the same site can absolutely support the growth Michael and Emily are targeting.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Best-available Google Business Profile figures show only 8-11 reviews, despite a 5.0-star rating
  • No geo-targeted landing pages exist for San Diego, San Francisco, Torrance, or Downtown LA — 4 of the 5 markets the firm's own marketing claims presence in — the site is organized entirely by practice area
  • Two separate, duplicate Yelp listings exist at the same Redondo Beach address, splitting review signal across two profiles instead of consolidating it
Blind Spot: Claiming a five-market footprint without any geo-specific content means Google has no signal to actually rank Kent Pincin Law in San Diego, San Francisco, Torrance, or Downtown LA searches — the firm may be paying for the perception of a five-city presence without the SEO infrastructure to be found there.
The Positive
  • Existing reviews carry a perfect 5.0-star rating, and the firm is listed across a wide range of legal directories (Chamber of Commerce, LawInfo, Super Lawyers, AttorneyAtLaw.com, and more)
The Gap
  • Fewer reviews than every named competitor means lower trust signal at the exact moment a prospect is comparing firms
  • No geo-pages for 4 of 5 claimed markets means near-zero organic visibility in those searches today
Miracle Mile Law Group — maintains dedicated landing pages for Los Angeles, Oakland, and San Diego, backed by 117 Yelp reviews at 4.4 stars, a level of multi-market content investment Kent Pincin Law has not yet made in any of its five claimed markets.
Closing the review and geo-visibility gap is what turns a five-city claim into a five-city reality — and a reality that starts feeding the firm leads instead of just its reputation.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Employment Law: Kent Pincin Law does not appear to be running Google search ads for employment discrimination, wrongful termination, or workplace harassment terms in the Redondo Beach/Torrance area
  • Personal Injury: does not appear to be running ads for personal injury or car accident searches in Redondo Beach
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Miracle Mile Law Group — maintains dedicated landing pages for Los Angeles, Oakland, and San Diego, a level of multi-market content investment consistent with active paid acquisition in employment law
  • Dolan Law Firm — a national firm with a dedicated Redondo Beach office page and "$1 billion+ recovered" marketing claims, consistent with heavy paid acquisition investment in personal injury
The Opportunity
  • Obvious: "employment discrimination lawyer Redondo Beach," "wrongful termination attorney Torrance CA," "car accident lawyer Redondo Beach"
  • Obvious: practice-area + city combinations for San Diego, San Francisco, Torrance, and Downtown LA to match the firm's actual claimed footprint
  • Non-obvious: competitor-name conquesting against Miracle Mile Law Group and The Simon Law Group in their own service areas
  • Non-obvious: urgency-driven terms like "workplace retaliation lawyer near me" that capture prospects ready to act immediately
Blind Spot: With zero paid marketing today, every one of these searches is currently being won by someone else — Kent Pincin Law is invisible in exactly the moments prospective clients are actively looking for the services it already provides at a high level.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • Kent Pincin Law does not appear in Local Service Ads or as Google Screened for either practice area at this time
  • Google has expanded legal-category LSAs into both personal injury and employment law in the LA/South Bay market, making this a live opportunity rather than a settled one
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Named competitor LSA presence — not independently confirmed via available tools; worth a manual check ahead of the proposal call given Google's recent expansion of legal LSAs into this market
The Opportunity
  • Kent Pincin Law's 5.0-star rating is an ideal foundation for LSA — Google Screened status leans heavily on review quality, not just quantity
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • Pay-per-lead pricing fits a firm whose intake team needs to stay selective about which cases to take, rather than paying for clicks that never convert
