Here Is Where Sivia Business & Legal Services Stands Today

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Intake Assessment

Sivia Business & Legal Services has a Clio Grow intake integration active on its website — which is a meaningful head start compared to firms with no intake infrastructure at all — but with seven offices handling multiple practice areas, the demands on intake are significantly more complex than a typical single-location firm. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No discovery call transcript was available for this audit — intake process details including follow-up speed, staff delegation, and conversion rates could not be assessed and must be confirmed on the June 10 proposal call
  • No observed after-hours intake capability — with seven offices and website visitors arriving at all hours, any gap in evening or weekend response is multiplied across the entire geographic footprint
  • Website CTA directs to an external Clio Grow form at sivialaw.cliogrow.com — introducing an extra click and page load between the visitor's intent and the intake capture, which reduces conversion rate compared to an embedded form
  • No AI-assisted or automated initial follow-up observed — in a multi-location firm with diverse practice areas, speed-to-response is critical, and manual follow-up across seven offices creates inconsistency in how leads are handled
Blind Spot: At a 7-office, multi-practice firm, intake is not a single process — it is seven separate processes happening in parallel. Without a standardized, monitored intake protocol across all locations, some offices may be converting well while others are silently losing leads with no visibility into the problem.
The Positive
  • Clio Grow intake integration is confirmed and active — the firm has invested in practice management software that provides a structured intake pathway and lead tracking capability
  • A prominent "Get Started Here" CTA is positioned above the fold on the homepage — intake capture is a clear priority in the website design
The Gap
  • A prospect who submits a Clio Grow form on a Friday evening and does not hear back until Monday morning has almost certainly contacted two or three other firms in that window — at seven offices, this scenario plays out at scale every week
  • Without a defined, monitored intake protocol across all locations, it is impossible to know what percentage of web inquiries are being converted to consultations and where in the funnel the losses are occurring
  • The more traffic and leads a paid advertising campaign drives to the website, the more costly this intake gap becomes — turning on the lead generation faucet without fixing the intake funnel first means paying for leads that will not convert
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Profit Plan Assessment

Sivia Business & Legal Services operates a multi-location, multi-practice law firm with seven offices, four attorneys, and overhead across two states — a structure that requires precise financial management to ensure that revenue growth translates into actual profit growth. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No discovery call transcript available — revenue, margins, profitability by location or practice area, and financial visibility could not be assessed and must be confirmed on June 10
  • Seven offices across two states means seven separate rent obligations, payroll allocations, and operational cost centers — without location-level profit tracking, it is impossible to know which offices and practice areas are generating returns versus consuming them
  • A firm that grows primarily on referrals without a financial dashboard may not have clear visibility into client acquisition cost, revenue per attorney, or profit margin — making strategic decisions about where to invest and where to cut difficult
  • Without a formal profit plan, revenue growth can mask a situation where expenses expand in proportion — the firm gets busier without necessarily getting more profitable
