Lead Generation

Sivia Business & Legal Services has built a multi-location practice across seven offices in two states — but that footprint is not yet backed by a digital lead generation system that brings clients in consistently without relying on Todd's personal network and referrals. The firm's competitors are investing heavily in paid search, local SEO, and online reviews, and the gap in digital visibility is widening.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed scores could not be retrieved during this research session due to API rate limiting — these must be manually verified at pagespeed.web.dev before the proposal call on June 10
  • Blog content is dormant — last published post was August 25, 2025, nine months before this audit; no fresh content signals to Google that the site is active and authoritative
  • Website testimonial section is sparsely populated — only one client testimonial observed on the homepage ("Kevin G."), with a Reviews & Testimonials page that appears to contain placeholder slots rather than real client feedback
  • No geo-targeted landing pages observed for surrounding cities in the firm's service area — Troy, Collinsville, O'Fallon, Belleville, Highland, and Glen Carbon represent significant missed organic search opportunities for a firm with 7 offices
Blind Spot: The firm operates under two names — "Sivia Business & Legal Services" and "Sivia Law" — which appear interchangeably across the website and likely across directory listings. This dual-name usage creates NAP (Name, Address, Phone) inconsistency that can dilute local SEO rankings and confuse Google about which entity to surface in search results.
The Positive
  • Website design is modern and professionally executed — estimated redesign within the last 2–3 years, with clean typography, clear navigation, and mobile-responsive layout
  • Clio Grow intake integration is active and connected to a prominent above-the-fold CTA ("Get Started Here") — the firm has done the work to set up a structured intake pathway from the website
  • Attorney bios are present for all four active attorneys — a credibility signal that helps prospects evaluate the firm before calling
The Gap
  • A dormant blog with no fresh content makes it significantly harder to rank for informational searches — prospects researching estate planning, business formation, or elder law do not find Sivia when they are still in the decision-making phase
  • With only one visible testimonial on the homepage, the firm is not using its client relationships to build the social proof that converts website visitors into calls — competitors with dozens of reviews have a built-in advantage at the moment of decision
  • Without geo-targeted landing pages, the 7-office geographic reach is invisible to local search — someone searching "estate planning attorney O'Fallon IL" will not find Sivia, even though the firm serves that area
A website that does not generate consistent inbound inquiries means Todd will always need to personally generate new business — and that is the definition of a firm that cannot run without him.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Google Business Profile review count and star rating could not be directly verified during this research session — manual verification at Google Maps is required before June 10; Avvo shows only 4 reviews for Todd Sivia (rating 7.8) compared to 52 reviews for Blake Behme Links Raney and 79 for Wendler & Zinzilieta
  • Local 3-pack visibility across practice area searches (business law, estate planning, personal injury) could not be confirmed — firm likely does not appear in the top pack positions based on directory ranking data, which shows Blake Behme consistently ahead
  • No geo-targeted landing pages observed for surrounding communities — the multi-location footprint is not supported by location-specific content that would help Google rank each office in its local market
  • Dual branding ("Sivia Business & Legal Services" vs. "Sivia Law") creates NAP inconsistency risk across directory listings and GBP entries, which can suppress local rankings
Blind Spot: With seven offices, Sivia should have seven separate GBP listings each optimized with location-specific content, photos, services, and Q&A entries. If those listings are incomplete or inconsistently managed, Google will not rank them well — and the geographic advantage of seven locations becomes a liability instead of an asset.
The Positive
  • Multiple GBP listings are confirmed across office locations — the firm has a local search infrastructure in place that can be optimized rather than built from scratch
The Gap
  • Every month that competitors accumulate new Google reviews and the firm does not is a month the review gap widens — with Blake at 52+ Avvo reviews and likely significantly more on Google, Sivia's review count makes it harder to rank in the local 3-pack for the highest-intent searches
  • Seven offices without seven fully-optimized GBP listings means the firm is not extracting local ranking value from the geographic footprint it has already built
Blake Behme Links Raney, P.C. — Consistently ranked #1 in Edwardsville directory searches across estate planning, business law, and real estate, with an Avvo rating of 10.0 and 52 reviews — 13 times Sivia's observed review count. Blake's review volume and tenure make it the default credibility anchor for prospects searching these practice areas in this market.
A local SEO strategy that matches the firm's 7-location footprint is one of the highest-leverage, lowest-cost ways to generate consistent inbound leads without requiring Todd's personal involvement in business development.

Blind Spot: Every month that established competitors like Wendler and TorHoerman run personal injury ads without Sivia in the auction is a month that the PI search market in Metro East is being carved up and claimed — and in paid search, the firms with more data and more history have an advantage that compounds over time. The longer the delay, the steeper the ramp to compete.
