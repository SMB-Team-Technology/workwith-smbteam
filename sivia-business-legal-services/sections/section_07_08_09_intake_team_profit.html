Intake Assessment

Sivia Business & Legal Services has a Clio Grow intake integration active on its website — which is a meaningful head start compared to firms with no intake infrastructure at all — but with seven offices handling multiple practice areas, the demands on intake are significantly more complex than a typical single-location firm. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No discovery call transcript was available for this audit — intake process details including follow-up speed, staff delegation, and conversion rates could not be assessed and must be confirmed on the June 10 proposal call
  • No observed after-hours intake capability — with seven offices and website visitors arriving at all hours, any gap in evening or weekend response is multiplied across the entire geographic footprint
  • Website CTA directs to an external Clio Grow form at sivialaw.cliogrow.com — introducing an extra click and page load between the visitor's intent and the intake capture, which reduces conversion rate compared to an embedded form
  • No AI-assisted or automated initial follow-up observed — in a multi-location firm with diverse practice areas, speed-to-response is critical, and manual follow-up across seven offices creates inconsistency in how leads are handled
Blind Spot: At a 7-office, multi-practice firm, intake is not a single process — it is seven separate processes happening in parallel. Without a standardized, monitored intake protocol across all locations, some offices may be converting well while others are silently losing leads with no visibility into the problem.
The Positive
  • Clio Grow intake integration is confirmed and active — the firm has invested in practice management software that provides a structured intake pathway and lead tracking capability
  • A prominent "Get Started Here" CTA is positioned above the fold on the homepage — intake capture is a clear priority in the website design
The Gap
  • A prospect who submits a Clio Grow form on a Friday evening and does not hear back until Monday morning has almost certainly contacted two or three other firms in that window — at seven offices, this scenario plays out at scale every week
  • Without a defined, monitored intake protocol across all locations, it is impossible to know what percentage of web inquiries are being converted to consultations and where in the funnel the losses are occurring
  • The more traffic and leads a paid advertising campaign drives to the website, the more costly this intake gap becomes — turning on the lead generation faucet without fixing the intake funnel first means paying for leads that will not convert
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Website CTA routes to an external Clio Grow form hosted at sivialaw.cliogrow.com — the redirect adds friction compared to an embedded form; prospects who abandon at this step are lost without capture
  • No live chat or chat widget observed on the website — after-hours visitors who are not ready to fill out a form have no way to engage and may leave without providing contact information

Team Assessment

Sivia Business & Legal Services has built a team of four active attorneys and one of-counsel across seven offices — a meaningful achievement that shows Todd's ability to recruit and retain legal talent. But the firm's public presence, brand, and new business relationships still appear to flow through Todd personally, which means his time and attention remain the primary constraint on how fast the firm can grow. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No discovery call transcript available — the team's internal structure, accountability systems, delegation practices, and management depth could not be assessed and must be confirmed on June 10
  • Todd is the named CEO and the visible face of the firm — all professional branding, credentials (JD, CEPA, CExP), and thought leadership appear tied to him personally, making the firm's reputation dependent on his continued involvement
  • No observed management structure, COO function, or operational leadership layer that would allow Todd to step back from day-to-day decisions across seven offices
  • With no digital lead generation systems in place, new client relationships are likely initiated through Todd's personal referral network — which is not a system that can be delegated or scaled
Blind Spot: The fact that the firm has successfully grown to 7 offices over 20 years may be masking a dependency problem — Todd has built a firm that runs with him at the center, and every additional office makes that dependency more complex rather than less. The very growth that looks like success from the outside may be making it harder to step back.
The Positive
  • Four active attorneys plus of-counsel demonstrates meaningful legal team depth — Todd is not the only attorney delivering work, which is the foundation of a firm that can eventually run without the owner doing everything
  • Multi-location expansion over 20 years shows Todd's capacity for organizational thinking beyond a solo practice — he has already made the shift from lawyer to business builder in meaningful ways
The Gap
  • A firm where the owner is the primary rainmaker is a firm that cannot take a two-week vacation without new business slowing — at 7 offices, that dependency is amplified, not reduced
  • Without accountability systems, scorecards, or a defined management layer, the team's performance across seven locations likely varies in ways that are difficult to see and address without Todd being personally involved in each one
  • The transition from "firm that runs because Todd is involved in everything" to "firm that runs because the systems and people are in place" is the single biggest leverage point available to Sivia right now — and it requires intentional structure, not just more hiring
Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Profit Plan Assessment

Sivia Business & Legal Services operates a multi-location, multi-practice law firm with seven offices, four attorneys, and overhead across two states — a structure that requires precise financial management to ensure that revenue growth translates into actual profit growth. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No discovery call transcript available — revenue, margins, profitability by location or practice area, and financial visibility could not be assessed and must be confirmed on June 10
  • Seven offices across two states means seven separate rent obligations, payroll allocations, and operational cost centers — without location-level profit tracking, it is impossible to know which offices and practice areas are generating returns versus consuming them
  • A firm that grows primarily on referrals without a financial dashboard may not have clear visibility into client acquisition cost, revenue per attorney, or profit margin — making strategic decisions about where to invest and where to cut difficult
  • Without a formal profit plan, revenue growth can mask a situation where expenses expand in proportion — the firm gets busier without necessarily getting more profitable
Blind Spot: In a multi-practice firm handling business law, estate planning, elder law, personal injury, and real estate simultaneously, case profitability varies significantly by practice area. Without tracking cost and revenue per case type and per location, it is possible to be working hardest on the least profitable cases without knowing it.
The Positive
  • Clio practice management software is in active use — a strong foundation for financial reporting, matter tracking, and billing visibility if configured for profit analysis
  • Twenty years of multi-location operation suggests the firm has some financial infrastructure and experience managing overhead across locations — the question is whether that infrastructure delivers visibility, not just bookkeeping
The Gap
  • Adding paid advertising and new lead generation without a clear profit plan means it is difficult to set the right marketing investment level — the right number depends on knowing what each new client is actually worth after all costs
  • Without profit visibility by location, the firm cannot make informed decisions about which offices to invest in, which to consolidate, and which are generating the best return on Todd's time and capital
  • The combination of seven locations, multiple practice areas, and no confirmed financial dashboard creates the conditions for a classic "growing broke" scenario — revenue climbing while profit per dollar of effort quietly declines
Todd has invested 20 years and built a 7-office firm — a profit plan ensures that the next 20 years of that investment actually compounds into wealth, not just revenue.
