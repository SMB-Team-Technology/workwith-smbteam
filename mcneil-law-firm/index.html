Stage 6 is where the feast-and-famine cycle ends — where the pipeline is full months in advance, payroll is never in doubt, and John McNeil leads a firm with financial certainty instead of financial anxiety.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Intake Assessment

McNeil Law Firm is generating 75–125 leads per month and converting 26% of those who qualify — but there is no dedicated intake staff, no defined follow-up sequence, and no after-hours coverage to capture the prospects who cannot reach anyone during business hours. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No dedicated intake staff — leads arriving by phone or contact form are handled by operations and bookkeeping staff who are not structured or trained for lead conversion
  • No after-hours coverage — the website has no mechanism to capture evening and weekend inquiries; voicemail without a defined follow-up protocol means after-hours leads often call a competitor before morning
  • No attribution at intake — because the firm cannot identify which leads came from paid ads versus organic, there is no way to optimize or even measure intake conversion by lead source
  • Contact form buried below the fold on the homepage — reducing form submission volume before prospects even reach the intake step
Blind Spot: At a 26% close rate, 74% of qualified leads are choosing another firm or not signing — and research shows that responding to an inquiry within 5 minutes versus 1 hour increases close rate by over 21×. Small improvements to speed-to-lead alone, without any additional marketing spend, could add multiple cases per month.
The Positive
  • 26% close rate on qualified leads indicates that when the firm does engage prospects in a consultation, it converts at a reasonable rate — the intake process itself is functional, even if it is not systematized
  • A team of four provides enough staffing capacity to delegate intake responsibilities without adding headcount
The Gap
  • At 38–63 qualified leads per month and a 26% close rate, 28–46 qualified prospects are choosing another firm each month — at a blended case value of approximately $4,100, that represents $115,000–$189,000 in monthly uncaptured revenue the firm already paid to generate
  • Without a defined follow-up sequence for prospects who do not sign on the first contact, cases that require multiple touchpoints are being abandoned prematurely — especially common in contested divorce and custody matters where the decision timeline is longer
  • No process for lead capture outside business hours creates a systematic revenue leak that compounds every week — the family law prospect who calls at 8pm on a Tuesday and gets voicemail will often call the next firm on their Google results list before 9am the next morning
A stronger intake system is the highest-ROI investment the firm can make right now — not because more leads are needed, but because the firm is already generating leads it is not fully converting, and every unconverted qualified prospect is revenue the system left behind.

  • No automated contact form acknowledgment or response protocol confirmed — prospects who submit a form may not receive a response until the next business day, creating a response gap that competitors with faster follow-up will close before the firm ever calls back

  • Every time John McNeil takes time off or steps away from the firm — even for a week — revenue generation stops entirely; there is no production redundancy and no safety net if he is unavailable
