Pillar Health Scorecard

Lead Generation

  • Google PPC and LSAs running in-house, but no attribution — the firm cannot tell which spend is generating cases and which is waste
  • Does not appear in the local 3-pack for any primary family law keyword; firm self-reports ranking on page 2 for "family law Raleigh"

Without a measured, accountable marketing system, every dollar spent on ads is a guess — and guessing is what is keeping the pipeline unpredictable.

Intake

  • 26% close rate on qualified leads — meaning 74% of prospects who engage are choosing another firm or walking away
  • No dedicated intake staff; no defined follow-up sequence; contact form buried below the fold on the website
  • No after-hours coverage — evening and weekend prospects cannot reach anyone and are likely calling a competitor first
Every unconverted qualified lead is revenue the firm already paid to generate — improving intake converts more of that spend into cases without spending a dollar more on marketing.

Self-Managing Team

  • John McNeil is the sole attorney — all legal production requires his direct involvement, creating a hard ceiling on case volume
  • Needs at least one paralegal hire before the $1.5M revenue goal becomes achievable; no hire made yet
  • Julia spans operations and bookkeeping without a dedicated manager layer — no org structure to support scaling
A firm where one attorney handles every matter cannot grow to $1.5M without John working more hours — and more hours is not the plan.

Profit

  • Revenue has declined approximately 30% year-over-year — from just over $1M to $720,000 — with no formal reversal plan
  • All bookkeeping handled manually with inadequate software; no budget, no financial projections, no cost-per-acquisition tracking
  • No visibility into which practice areas or lead sources are profitable — John McNeil cannot make confident hiring or marketing investment decisions
Until the numbers are visible and plannable, the 6-week holiday slow season will keep being a crisis instead of a managed dip — and the path to financial certainty stays out of reach.
