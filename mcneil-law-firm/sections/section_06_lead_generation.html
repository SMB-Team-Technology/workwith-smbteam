Lead Generation

McNeil Law Firm is running Google PPC and Local Service Ads in-house — generating an estimated 75–125 leads per month — but without the attribution infrastructure to know what is working, what is wasted, and what each case is actually costing to acquire. While the ad spend is active, the firm ranks on page 2 for its primary keywords and holds 67 Google reviews at a time when the local 3-pack is controlled by competitors at 100+ reviews.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Contact form is buried below the fold — the single most important conversion action on the homepage requires a visitor to scroll to find it, introducing friction at the exact moment intent is highest
  • PageSpeed scores: not retrievable via automated tool — manual verification at pagespeed.web.dev is recommended before the proposal call; site page size is approximately 616 KB with a 0.4s TTFB suggesting moderate mobile load performance
  • No video content present — family law prospects are making a high-stakes, emotional decision; attorney video builds the trust that converts browsers into callers before they ever pick up the phone
  • No blog, no content marketing, and no geo-targeted landing pages for surrounding cities (Cary, Durham, Chapel Hill, Wake Forest, Apex, Garner) — the firm is invisible to prospects searching from outside its immediate street address
Blind Spot: Without city-specific landing pages for Wake County's surrounding communities, the firm is missing an entire layer of search demand from qualified family law prospects who search "divorce attorney Cary NC" or "child custody lawyer Durham NC" — searches that competitors with multi-location pages are capturing automatically.
The Positive
  • Modern, mobile-responsive website design estimated at 2–3 years old — no major rebuild required, optimization is sufficient
  • Attorney bio with headshot featured for John McNeil; 12 practice area pages covering the full range of family law services are in place
The Gap
  • A buried contact form means after-hours website visitors — who often make the highest-intent inquiries — have no low-friction way to connect, making them likely to call a competitor who makes it easier
  • No testimonials, case results, or social proof above the fold means the first 10 seconds of a visit do not answer the most important question a family law prospect has: can I trust this attorney with the hardest moment of my life?
  • Without geo-targeted landing pages, every dollar spent on ads driving traffic for surrounding city keywords lands on a generic homepage — reducing relevance scores, raising cost-per-click, and lowering conversion rates simultaneously
Every friction point on this website is a prospect who paid to arrive, could not easily connect, and called a competitor instead — fixing these issues converts more of the marketing spend that is already running into cases that actually pay.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • 67 Google reviews at 4.4 stars — below the 100+ review threshold that drives consistent 3-pack and LSA visibility in competitive family law markets; most recent review is approximately 4 months old (February 2026)
  • Firm does not appear in the local 3-pack for any primary keyword — "family law attorney Raleigh NC," "divorce attorney Raleigh NC," "child custody attorney Raleigh NC," or "family law Raleigh" — confirmed in research and self-reported as page 2 by the firm
  • Avvo profile shows one client review (1-star negative) and zero peer endorsements — a publicly visible credibility gap that high-value prospects researching attorneys will find before they ever visit the firm's website
Blind Spot: Review velocity matters as much as review count — Google's local ranking algorithm weighs recency heavily, and with the most recent review from February 2026 (four months ago), the firm's profile is signaling low engagement to the algorithm at exactly the time it is trying to recover lost ground.
The Positive
  • Consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) across Justia, Avvo, website, and primary directories — no cleanup required before launching a citation-building campaign
The Gap
  • The review gap with McIlveen Family Law Firm (102 vs. 67) is not cosmetic — it is an algorithmic ranking signal that directly determines which firm appears in the 3-pack and LSA results when a prospect searches "family law attorney Raleigh"
  • Without an active review acquisition strategy, the gap widens every month as competitors continue generating reviews from their client base while McNeil Law Firm's profile stays static
McIlveen Family Law Firm — 102 Google reviews and a multi-location NC presence, consistently appearing above McNeil Law Firm in the local 3-pack for every primary family law and divorce keyword. Their review volume and location authority are the direct algorithmic reasons they rank where McNeil does not.
Building the review count past 100 and activating a consistent review acquisition process is the most direct path to organic 3-pack visibility — which means cases the firm gets without paying for a single ad click.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • McNeil Law Firm confirmed running in-house Google PPC campaigns — ads did not appear during this research session, which may indicate the firm is being outbid on high-competition queries, daily budget was exhausted, or targeting settings are limiting visibility to certain times or match types
  • The firm self-reports an attribution gap: clients say they "found us on Google" but the firm cannot distinguish paid search leads from organic visits — confirming that campaigns are running without proper conversion tracking or Google Analytics integration
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • McIlveen Family Law Firm — visible in paid results context for family law and divorce searches in Raleigh; with 102 Google reviews, their Quality Scores are likely higher, meaning they pay less per click for the same keyword positions McNeil is bidding on
  • Charles R. Ullman & Associates — specialist-certified family law firm active in Raleigh paid search for over 25 years; a dedicated firm with a focused PPC strategy and deep history on competitive terms
  • Gailor Hunt Davis Taylor & Gibbs PLLC — prominent in search results across family law, divorce, and alimony queries; brand authority reinforces paid performance and likely depresses click-through rates for ads appearing below them
The Opportunity
  • High-intent keyword targeting with proper conversion tracking: "family law attorney Raleigh NC," "divorce lawyer Raleigh," "child custody attorney Wake County" — the same terms currently running but with attribution attached to every conversion
  • Case-type-specific ad groups: uncontested divorce (flat fee — $3,000 average value), contested divorce, emergency custody, domestic violence restraining orders — each requiring different ad copy and landing page messaging
  • Competitor name bidding: prospects searching "McIlveen family law" or "Gailor Hunt divorce attorney" are high-intent prospects who have already decided they need a family law attorney — capturing that traffic before they click the competitor result is a non-obvious but high-converting opportunity
  • Surrounding city expansion: "divorce attorney Cary NC," "family law attorney Durham," "child custody lawyer Wake Forest" — markets where McNeil can rank in paid search today while building organic authority takes longer
Blind Spot: Running Google Ads without conversion tracking means the firm may be spending the most on keywords that generate the fewest cases — or paying premium CPCs for traffic that bounces at the buried contact form. A properly tracked campaign often reveals that 20% of keywords drive 80% of signed cases, allowing immediate budget reallocation that lowers cost per acquisition without reducing lead volume.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • McNeil Law Firm confirmed running LSAs — ads did not appear during this research session; at 67 Google reviews, the firm may be appearing below the visible top positions in the LSA auction, where review count is a primary ranking signal
  • Google Screened status not confirmed during research — an unverified or inactive Google Screened badge means the firm does not display the trust indicator that differentiated LSA results carry at the top of the search page
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • McIlveen Family Law Firm — with 102 Google reviews, they rank above lower-review firms in the LSA auction for family law searches, appearing in top positions where the per-lead cost is lowest and the visibility is highest
  • Parker Bryan Family Law — 9 Super Lawyers-recognized attorneys and strong brand authority signals across the Raleigh legal market; positioned for strong LSA performance on high-value contested family law searches
The Opportunity
  • Growing the Google review count from 67 toward 100+ is the single highest-leverage action for improving LSA ranking — it improves both the auction position and the click-through rate of the LSA ad itself
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • Properly structured LSA bid management with active account monitoring would surface the firm on more high-intent family law queries at a predictable cost per lead — replacing the current unmanaged setup where bid floor and review count are likely limiting visibility
