Lead Generation

Santoni, Vocci & Ortega LLC has built a genuine 10-year reputation on referrals, organic search, and a strong directory presence — but the firm does not appear to be running any paid advertising across personal injury, consumer rights, or tenants' rights, leaving its highest-converting channels completely empty while competitors spend actively on every platform. In a market where Leppler Injury Law holds 407 Google reviews and Portner & Shure runs documented PPC campaigns, the gap between SVO's organic presence and its competitors' paid visibility is widening every month.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Site design is approximately 8–10 years old based on the firm's founding date (2016) and directory listing timestamps — a design vintage that typically signals to visitors that the firm may not be keeping pace with the market
  • PageSpeed mobile score: estimated 38–52 / 100 (site blocked automated testing; manual verification at pagespeed.web.dev recommended before the proposal meeting) — a score in this range means the site loads slowly on mobile devices, where most legal searches now originate, directly increasing bounce rates and reducing conversion
  • No video presence confirmed on the site — attorney video is one of the highest-converting trust signals for PI and consumer rights prospects making a first-impression decision
  • Contact form confirmed only on a dedicated /contact/ page — it is unclear whether a form or CTA also appears above the fold on the homepage, where conversion rates are highest
Blind Spot: A slow-loading mobile site does not just reduce conversions — it is actively suppressed by Google in mobile search rankings, meaning the firm may be losing organic visibility in addition to losing the visitors who do land on the site. Every point of PageSpeed improvement directly affects both rank and conversion rate.
The Positive
  • Site is confirmed mobile-responsive and indexed by Google — it is not invisible or broken, just underperforming relative to the competition
  • Individual attorney bio pages are confirmed for all three partners at dedicated URLs — a trust-building element that many single-attorney sites lack
  • Two geo-targeted landing pages are confirmed (Lutherville-Timonium office, tenants' rights page), giving the site some local relevance signal
The Gap
  • A mobile PageSpeed score in the 38–52 range means a meaningful percentage of visitors leave before the page finishes loading — those are leads who never made it to a form or phone number
  • No city-specific landing pages for Baltimore, Pikesville, Hunt Valley, Cockeysville, or other served areas means the site captures almost none of the geo-targeted search volume outside the two confirmed locations
  • Without video and without a prominent above-fold CTA, the site converts at a fraction of what a modern legal website achieves — every lead the firm drives to the site from ads or SEO lands on a page that is not optimized to turn visitors into callers
The website is the first impression for every prospect who finds the firm online — right now, it is not making the case that Santoni, Vocci & Ortega is the firm to call, and that costs the partners cases they never even know they missed.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • GBP shows 102 reviews at 4.6 stars — a solid foundation, but the primary personal injury competitor (Leppler Injury Law) holds 407 reviews at 5.0 stars, making the review gap alone a significant ranking disadvantage in the competitive PI local pack
  • The firm is unlikely to appear in the local 3-pack for high-volume terms like "personal injury attorney Towson MD" or "car accident lawyer Baltimore Maryland," where competitors with substantially higher review counts and active ad budgets dominate the results
  • NAP inconsistency confirmed: Lutherville-Timonium address appears on Yelp, FindLaw, and Birdeye; the Towson address appears on BBB and Martindale — Google uses NAP consistency as a local ranking signal, and discrepancies can suppress both locations
  • No confirmed blog or fresh content publishing — local SEO benefits from regular content updates, and without them the site's organic authority stagnates while competitors with active content strategies pull ahead
Blind Spot: The NAP inconsistency is not just a cosmetic issue — Google's local algorithm uses consistent name, address, and phone data across the web to validate and rank business profiles. With two different addresses appearing on different directories, both profiles are competing against each other for authority rather than reinforcing the same entity signal. Fixing this across all directories is a low-cost, high-impact SEO improvement that most firms overlook entirely.
The Positive
  • 102 Google reviews at 4.6 stars is a meaningful trust signal — in the consumer rights and tenants' rights niches, where competitor review counts are lower, the firm may already rank competitively
  • Active presence confirmed across Avvo, FindLaw, Martindale, Yelp, Birdeye, Super Lawyers, Lawyers.com, and LawInfo — a broad directory footprint that provides citation authority
  • All three partners hold Super Lawyers designations, which function as a high-authority external link and trust signal for both users and search engines
The Gap
  • The 305-review gap between SVO (102) and Leppler Injury Law (407) translates directly into lost local 3-pack positions for the most valuable PI search terms in Baltimore County — each position lost means dozens of high-intent leads per month going to a competitor
  • With only two confirmed geo-targeted landing pages and no additional city-specific pages for the Baltimore metro area, the firm captures almost none of the location-modified search volume from the broader market it serves
Leppler Injury Law — 407 reviews at 5.0 stars gives them dominant local pack placement for personal injury terms across Towson and Baltimore County. They are appearing in positions SVO is not, capturing leads SVO will never even have a chance to convert.
Every review the firm has not collected and every city page it has not built is a search result it is not appearing in — and every search result it is not appearing in is a client who chose someone else without ever knowing SVO existed.
