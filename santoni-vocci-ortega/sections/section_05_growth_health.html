Pillar Health Scorecard

Lead Generation

  • No paid search, LSA, or Meta advertising observed — all three paid channels are currently uncaptured while competitors spend actively on each
  • 102 Google reviews trail primary PI competitor Leppler Injury Law (407 reviews) by more than 300, limiting 3-pack visibility for high-volume searches
  • NAP inconsistency across directories (two different addresses listed on different platforms) creates local SEO confusion and may suppress rankings
Without a reliable inbound system, the firm's ability to serve more clients who need consumer advocacy is limited by how many cases happen to come in on their own.

Intake

  • No live chat or after-hours response mechanism observed on the website — prospects who reach out outside business hours receive no immediate engagement
  • No LSA enrollment means the firm is not receiving Google-verified leads routed directly to the phone, even when it does appear in relevant searches
  • With three active litigating partners and two staff, intake likely depends on attorneys fielding calls directly, limiting capacity and speed of follow-up
Every unanswered inquiry is a client who needed help and found another firm instead — and revenue the firm earned but never converted.

Self-Managing Team

  • Three equity partners handle both legal work and firm operations across two office locations — there is no confirmed separation between ownership and management
  • Two associates/staff for a multi-practice, two-location firm suggests every partner absorbs significant operational and intake responsibilities
  • No evidence of a dedicated intake coordinator, operations manager, or marketing staff member who could own functions the partners currently handle personally
A team where all three partners must be present for the firm to run means none of the three can ever fully step back — regardless of revenue.

Profit

  • Contingency-based PI and consumer rights cases create unpredictable revenue timing — income arrives in large, irregular settlements rather than a steady monthly stream
  • Without a confirmed marketing ROI framework or case acquisition cost tracking, reinvestment decisions are made without knowing what each case actually costs to acquire
  • A growing firm without a profit plan risks scaling overhead faster than it scales income — more cases can mean more work without meaningfully more profit per partner
Growing revenue without growing what each partner keeps is a treadmill — and without a profit plan, faster growth often just means a faster treadmill.
