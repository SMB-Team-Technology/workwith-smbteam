Here Is Where Sun State Legal PLLC Stands Today

Nate Hill is a 15-year employment law veteran who left a long-tenured partner role in late 2024 to launch Sun State Legal PLLC — a solo practice serving both employers and employees across the Phoenix metro on workplace discrimination, wrongful termination, wage disputes, and employment agreements. The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
"2–3 voicemails per day; 4–5 per week worth screening. Uses transcripts to ignore obvious non-fits; lacks system to handle qualified quickly."
The Problem
  • Nate personally reviews voicemail transcripts and decides which leads to follow up — there is no delegation, no intake coordinator, and no defined criteria for what qualifies a lead for callback
  • No intake script exists — when Nate does respond, the conversation is unstructured, with no consistent framework for qualifying the case, anchoring the relationship, or moving toward a signed engagement
  • No tracking system for leads — there is no record of who called, who was contacted back, who was not, or what happened to any prospective client after initial contact
  • No after-hours coverage — employment matters are time-sensitive; a wrongful termination victim who calls at 7pm on a Friday and reaches voicemail will call the next attorney on their list by Saturday morning
Blind Spot: Because Nate does not track leads, he has no visibility into how many qualified prospects call, fail to hear back quickly, and hire a competitor. The leakage is invisible — which is exactly why it has not been addressed.
The Positive
  • Nate already uses voicemail transcription to do basic filtering — a rudimentary triage instinct is in place, even without a formal system
The Gap
  • At 4–5 qualified leads per week with an average case value of $5,000–$15,000+, even a 30% improvement in intake conversion rate represents $30,000–$100,000+ in additional annual revenue from leads already coming in
  • Without a fast-response protocol, Sun State Legal is competing against firms that have dedicated intake teams returning calls within minutes — at that speed disadvantage, qualified leads consistently choose the firm that responded first
  • The absence of any intake tracking means Nate cannot measure his close rate, identify patterns in the types of cases he is winning or losing, or make informed decisions about where to invest his follow-up time

Profit Plan Assessment

Sun State Legal is generating approximately $500,000 in annual revenue with no KPIs, no financial tracking beyond top-line income, and no defined profit target — revenue is growing, but there is no plan that connects that revenue to what Nate is actually taking home or where the business is headed financially. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
"No formal plan; operates to meet household floor; prioritizes lifestyle events; acknowledges flaw and seeks guidance."
The Problem
  • No matter-level tracking — Nate cannot identify which case types (hourly employer matters vs. contingency employee claims) produce the highest profit relative to time invested
  • No KPIs and no dashboard — there is no regular review of revenue per case, hours invested, realization rate, or any other financial metric that would inform staffing and investment decisions
  • Time entry is inconsistent — on hourly matters, unbilled time goes directly to zero revenue, and without disciplined tracking, it is impossible to know how much is being lost
  • No revenue goal beyond a personal income floor — the business has no financial north star to steer hiring, marketing investment, or operational decisions toward
Blind Spot: The mix of hourly and contingency matters creates a financial blind spot that is particularly risky — contingency cases consume real time with no guaranteed return, and without tracking time investment per case type, Nate cannot know whether the contingency work in his pipeline is subsidizing his practice or draining it.
The Positive
  • Revenue is growing year-over-year (~$450k to ~$500k pace) with no marketing investment — this means the underlying practice economics are healthy and there is genuine upside that a profit plan can unlock
The Gap
  • Without knowing which cases produce the best margin per hour, Nate cannot make confident decisions about what types of cases to prioritize, what to refer out, and where to invest marketing dollars for the highest ROI
  • No financial visibility means there is no defensible basis for setting a hiring budget, a marketing budget, or a personal income target — decisions are being made on intuition rather than data
  • A firm heading toward $700k–$1M in revenue without a profit plan will eventually confront a moment where revenue is high but take-home is flat — because costs grew in proportion to revenue without any plan to protect margin
Knowing your numbers is what separates building wealth from building a busy practice — and a profit plan is the tool that turns revenue growth into the financial freedom you described wanting for yourself and your son.
