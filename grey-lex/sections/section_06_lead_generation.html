Lead Generation

Grey Lex is generating leads through Google PPC and SEO but doing so inconsistently, with a sporadic ad cadence, miscategorized Google Business Profiles, and no digital strategy yet built for property damage — the firm's highest-value practice area. The infrastructure to grow is present; the configuration and consistency to scale it are not.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • The Lafayette office address across the website reads "109 Stewart St, Baton Rouge, LA 70501" — the city is listed as Baton Rouge when it should be Lafayette, LA — an active NAP error that undermines local SEO and confuses potential clients
  • PageSpeed scores could not be retrieved automatically — scores must be verified manually at pagespeed.web.dev and should be addressed if mobile score is below 60
  • No blog, no video content, and no educational resources — the site provides no reason for prospects to return or to trust the firm before they call
  • Website meta title reads "Business Lawyer | Grey Lex, LLC" rather than reflecting bankruptcy or property damage — misaligned with the firm's actual revenue drivers and SEO targets
Blind Spot: The Lafayette address error is not just a website issue — it propagates into Google Maps, Google Business Profile, and any citation that pulls from the website. Every month this goes uncorrected, it is actively suppressing local search rankings for the Lafayette market where Clay does the majority of his business.
The Positive
  • Consultation request form is visible above the fold — removing friction for prospects ready to take action
  • Site is mobile responsive with clean navigation — no fundamental usability issues observed
The Gap
  • The address error is actively suppressing local rankings for Lafayette — the city where Clay operates his primary office and generates most of his business
  • Without practice-area-specific landing pages for bankruptcy and property damage, the site cannot compete for high-intent keyword searches against firms that have dedicated, optimized pages
  • No attorney video or personal story means visitors who arrive from ads leave without a trust signal — lower conversion rates and higher cost per case
A website that ranks well and converts well means Clay gets more qualified calls without spending more time on marketing — and that is what creates the space for him to step back from the day-to-day.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Both Google Business Profiles (Lafayette and Baton Rouge) appear to be set to "Business Lawyer" as the primary category — not "Bankruptcy Attorney" — limiting visibility for the firm's highest-volume search terms
  • Grey Lex does not appear in the local 3-pack for core bankruptcy keywords in Lafayette; Simon Fitzgerald LLC, Weinstein & St. Germain, and Richoux Law Firm consistently dominate those results
  • Multiple NAP inconsistencies across directories: Avvo lists a different address, Lawyers.com lists Clay under Denham Springs instead of Lafayette, and Yellow Pages categorizes the firm as "Family Law Attorney"
  • No geo-targeting landing pages exist for any city beyond Lafayette and Baton Rouge — no pages for the North Louisiana markets Clay identified as expansion targets
Blind Spot: Grey Lex has 31 Google reviews at 4.8 stars — a strong rating that most established competitors cannot match. But that credibility is invisible to anyone searching for a bankruptcy attorney because the GBP category mismatch means the firm simply does not appear for those searches. Fixing the category could unlock visibility that is already earned.
The Positive
  • 31 Google reviews at 4.8 stars — stronger than Weinstein & St. Germain (8 reviews, 5.0) and competitive with Richoux Law Firm (23 reviews, 4.7) in the bankruptcy space; this is a real asset that is currently being undersold
The Gap
  • Correcting the GBP primary category from "Business Lawyer" to "Bankruptcy Attorney" is a five-minute fix that could immediately improve visibility for the firm's most-searched terms — this is the single highest-leverage quick win in local SEO
  • Cleaning up NAP inconsistencies across Avvo, Lawyers.com, and Yellow Pages would strengthen the firm's citation authority and improve local ranking signals across both markets
Simon Fitzgerald LLC — Louisiana's oldest bankruptcy firm, 253 aggregated reviews, multi-location statewide presence, deeply entrenched in Lafayette's local pack. Their brand and citation authority have been compounding for over 100 years. The only way to close that gap is to outperform them on recency, reviews, and category relevance — which starts with fixing the GBP category today.
Owning the local 3-pack for bankruptcy in Lafayette means a steady stream of calls that come in without Clay having to generate them himself — and that is the beginning of the firm running without him.

Google Ads

Bankruptcy — Ad Presence
  • Grey Lex is running Google PPC for bankruptcy through Convertit Marketing — confirmed by Clay on the call; current spend is approximately $1,000–$2,000/month with a $500/month management fee
  • Campaign cadence is inconsistent: heavy spend in the first week of each month with a mid-month restart added later — no always-on strategy that maintains visibility across the full billing cycle
Bankruptcy — Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Weinstein & St. Germain LLC — running dedicated landing pages with "do you qualify" CTAs and multi-channel marketing including YouTube and Facebook; highly targeted bankruptcy funnel built specifically to intercept Clay's core clients
  • Lisa W. Thomas (bankruptcylawlafayette.com) — owns an exact-match bankruptcy domain name; the domain alone signals aggressive search intent targeting and strong intent to dominate bankruptcy keywords in Lafayette
  • Menard Law Firm — dedicated website (menardlawfirm.com) appearing in bankruptcy search results; offers both Ch. 7 and Ch. 13, covering case types Grey Lex currently declines
Bankruptcy — The Opportunity
  • "Bankruptcy attorney Lafayette LA" and "chapter 7 attorney Lafayette" — high-intent terms where Grey Lex is currently competing at a fraction of what competitors are spending
  • "File bankruptcy Louisiana" and "stop wage garnishment Lafayette" — urgency-driven searches capturing prospects in financial crisis who are ready to act immediately
  • Neighboring market targeting — New Iberia, Opelousas, Crowley, and Morgan City keywords capture South Louisiana clients in Clay's stated expansion territory without requiring new offices
  • Foreclosure defense and non-bankruptcy debt relief terms — practice areas listed on the Grey Lex website that could capture additional case types from the same bankruptcy audience
Property Damage — Ad Presence
  • Grey Lex does not appear to be running a dedicated property damage ad campaign at this time — Clay confirmed PD cases are in the pipeline primarily from contingency referrals rather than paid advertising
  • There is a property damage practice page on the website, but no evidence of an active PPC campaign driving traffic to it
Property Damage — Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Gordon McKernan Injury Attorneys — the "Get Gordon" brand runs aggressive statewide advertising with 5,000+ reviews and 55+ attorneys; dedicated hurricane damage and property damage landing pages for the Lafayette market
  • Laborde Earles Injury Lawyers — 1,161 Google reviews, heavy statewide advertising, dedicated property damage and hurricane claims practice running full digital campaigns across search and social
  • Galloway Jefcoat, Injury Attorneys — 269 reviews, dedicated hurricane damage landing pages with 24/7 CTA, active in Lafayette property damage search results
Property Damage — The Opportunity
  • "Property damage attorney Lafayette" and "hurricane damage lawyer Louisiana" — high-value searches with $10,000–$20,000 cases that Clay confirmed as a major revenue opportunity
  • Pre-storm and post-storm timing campaigns — property damage volume in Louisiana spikes around hurricane season; a campaign ready to scale when weather events occur could generate significant contingency fee pipeline
  • Homeowner insurance dispute and roof damage keywords — first-party property claims that match the firm's stated focus and are less dominated by the large personal injury brands
Blind Spot: Clay's current ad spend is $1,000–$2,000/month — below the threshold where Google's algorithm optimizes effectively for attorney keywords. At this spend level, the campaigns may not be generating enough conversion data for Google to find the right audience, which means the cost per lead is likely higher than it needs to be. Increasing to a consistent $3,000–$5,000/month would allow the algorithm to optimize properly and drive a substantially better return.
