Pillar Health Scorecard

Lead Generation

  • Google PPC is running via Convertit but at a sporadic cadence — heavy first week only, inconsistent mid-month restarts
  • Both Google Business Profiles are miscategorized as "Business Lawyer" instead of "Bankruptcy Attorney," reducing visibility for the firm's highest-volume practice
  • No digital advertising strategy exists yet for property damage, the firm's highest-fee practice area
Until lead generation is consistent and properly targeted across both practice areas, Clay cannot step back — he is still personally driving every deal that comes in.

Intake

  • 50% of all inbound leads receive no follow-up contact after the initial call — half of the firm's paid marketing spend is being wasted
  • Clay is personally handling all consultations; when he is in legal work, no one is closing new clients
  • Town Square Media CRM is in place but largely unused — there is no automated drip, no no-show rescue, and no re-engagement sequence
A firm that runs without Clay starts with intake that runs without Clay — and right now, nothing in intake moves without him.

Self-Managing Team

  • Two staff members (paralegal and case manager) are in place but have no authority over intake, sales, or firm management
  • No management layer, no KPIs, no accountability structure — Clay is running the day-to-day for three people
  • No hiring playbooks or onboarding process; adding a Ch. 13 attorney or VA requires Clay to design the role from scratch
Clay told us he wants to be the manager, not the doer — but right now there is no management infrastructure for him to step into.

Profit

  • Revenue is growing (~$300k last year, tracking higher in 2026) but Clay is doing his own bookkeeping in QuickBooks — no financial dashboards or KPIs
  • No visibility into cost per lead, cost per case, or profitability by practice area — growth decisions are being made without the numbers
  • Significant revenue is being left on the table by declining all Ch. 13 cases (~$5,000 court-set fee) due to the absence of a second attorney
Hitting $1M requires knowing exactly where each dollar is coming from and what it costs to generate it — that clarity does not exist yet.
