Here Is Where Grey Lex, LLC Stands Today

Clay Hyde launched Grey Lex, LLC 18 months ago in Lafayette and Baton Rouge, building a bankruptcy and property damage practice from scratch to roughly $300,000 in annual revenue using Google PPC, strong personal sales skills, and an MBA-backed approach to the business. The single most important finding in this audit is that Clay is simultaneously the firm's best closer and its only attorney — a dual bottleneck that caps revenue at whatever one person can physically handle, and already causes half of all inbound leads to go cold without ever receiving a second contact.

Stage 6 is where the firm runs without Clay — where he steps out of both the legal work and the sales floor and leads the business from above — and that is exactly where he said he needs to go.

Where You Are Today

Stage 4: Small Business Manager
  • Clay is personally handling both client consultations and all legal work — neither can happen without him in the room
  • Google PPC is running at a sporadic cadence; 50% of all leads receive no follow-up after the first call
  • Two staff members (paralegal and case manager) exist but have no authority over intake or business development
  • Revenue is on pace to exceed $300k but there is no operating system, no KPIs, and no path to $500k–$1M without adding more of Clay's time

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between a $300k practice that needs Clay for everything and a $1M firm that runs without him is not as wide as it feels — but it requires systems, staffing, and structure that do not yet exist, and this audit shows exactly where to start.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Grey Lex, LLC
Clay is operating at Stage 4 — a Small Business Manager who has built a real firm with real revenue but is personally indispensable to every critical function inside it. The single most urgent gap is intake and capacity: with no follow-up system for uncontacted leads and no delegation of the consultation and close process, the firm is leaking revenue it has already paid to generate. Until Clay can hand off intake and eventually some of the legal work to a structured team, his stated goal of $500k–$1M this year will remain out of reach regardless of how much he grows the marketing.
