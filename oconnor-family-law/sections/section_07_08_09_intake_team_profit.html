Intake Assessment

O'Connor Family Law has built one of the most operationally sophisticated intake processes in the Massachusetts family law market — rapid MQL screening, VA-led follow-up, a non-attorney sales team, and a 96.7% consult show rate. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: GREEN
"We text instantly, and we're calling within five to ten minutes. The VAs do the MQL screen — geography and case type — and then non-attorney sales can close certain case types. The complex ones go to a paid strategy session."
The Problem
  • Answering Legal, the firm's overflow answering service, is underperforming — specifically during the 11am–2pm midday window when internal staff are at capacity and incoming calls most need coverage
  • When overflow calls are not answered and booked in real time, prospects in an emotional, urgent decision moment call the next firm on the list — and that firm is often Callahan | Inman or Burke Levy
  • Pre-consult nurture between booking and the consult date is not formalized — prospects who book and then shop are not receiving content that reduces comparison behavior before they sit down with an attorney
Blind Spot: The intake SLA is excellent for inbound calls — but there is no mechanism to capture high-intent website visitors who arrive outside business hours and cannot reach a person. An Intaker video greeting with a structured booking path would convert a meaningful portion of after-hours traffic that currently bounces with no follow-up.
The Positive
  • Text response within minutes and callback within 5–10 minutes — a genuine competitive advantage in a market where most firms take hours or never respond to new inquiries
  • 96.7% consult show rate, Lawmatics CRM, CallRail tracking, and a structured escalation path from VA to non-attorney sales to paid attorney consult — the infrastructure to handle 40–50 new clients per month is already built
  • Paid attorney strategy sessions at $475/hour for complex cases qualify and warm the highest-value prospects before retainer conversations begin
The Gap
  • Every midday overflow call that goes unanswered or handled poorly by Answering Legal is a prospect who may never call back — at a $7,500 average retainer, even two missed converts per month represents $15,000 in lost revenue
  • Without pre-consult nurture content (case preparation guides, what to expect, firm story), prospects who book often continue comparison shopping — reducing show rate and sign rate on calls the firm has already paid to generate
  • The gap between what the intake team can close and what the marketing is delivering is the entire growth constraint — this team is ready for more qualified volume right now
An intake engine this strong only reaches its potential when the leads flowing into it are qualified — fixing the upstream lead quality problem is what turns a good intake system into a $5–7M firm engine.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact form confirmed at /contact/ — placement above the fold on the homepage could not be verified due to bot-blocking; given the firm's intake speed, a visible above-fold form would capture intent before a prospect decides to call a competitor
  • Basic AI chatbot is live on the site but the firm is actively evaluating Intaker — a structured video greeting from Heather O'Connor paired with an intake capture form would convert high-intent after-hours visitors who currently bounce with no follow-up
  • A booking link embedded directly into the answering service overflow flow — so that when Answering Legal takes a call, the prospect is immediately offered a 10-minute intake appointment on the firm's calendar rather than a callback promise — is the highest-leverage conversion upgrade available to the intake system

These findings are also referenced in the Website Audit section.

Team Assessment

O'Connor Family Law has a real team — 13 attorneys, 25 employees, a Director of Operations, VAs, and a non-attorney sales force — but the Director of Operations is currently absorbing workload that belongs to the marketing partner, not to her. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
"We provide signed, won, lost, and qualification data every month. We feel like they're not acting on it — no exclusions, no keyword pruning, nothing."
The Problem
  • Caitlyn Fletcher (Director of Operations) is spending bandwidth auditing agency performance reports and manually separating qualified leads from raw call counts — work that a properly configured marketing partner should be doing independently
  • The firm has been providing monthly signed-case feedback to Empirical 360 for 8–10 months with no visible optimization action — meaning O'Connor's ops leadership is producing data that is not being used, adding friction without adding value
  • No fractional COO advisor or strategic operations partner to architect the systems needed to move from $4M to $5–7M without simply adding more work to Caitlyn's plate
  • The owner, Heather O'Connor, was not on the discovery call — a sign of healthy delegation — but the firm's growth ceiling will compress back onto leadership if the marketing system does not become self-correcting
Blind Spot: The current dynamic — where ops leadership compensates for a broken marketing system — is invisible until it is named. Caitlyn is carrying a burden that should not exist. Every hour she spends auditing agency reports is an hour she is not spending on hiring, attorney development, or the operational improvements that scale the firm to $7M.
The Positive
  • A Director of Operations exists and is clearly capable — the firm has already delegated operational leadership in a meaningful way, which is a Stage 5 signal most firms at this revenue level have not achieved
  • 13 attorneys with a remote-first structure and a functioning non-attorney sales team demonstrate that the firm has built real organizational capacity beyond the founder
The Gap
  • Every month Caitlyn's bandwidth is consumed by agency oversight is a month the firm's operational growth work gets deferred — and the path to $5–7M requires operational leadership, not agency management
  • Without a fractional COO advisor, the firm has no external strategic operations resource to blueprint the systems, accountability structures, and leadership scaffolding needed to scale without proportionally scaling Caitlyn's hours
  • A self-managing marketing system — one that reports on qualified leads and signed cases automatically and adjusts campaigns accordingly — is what frees Caitlyn to do the job she was hired to do
The goal is not just a bigger firm — it is a firm where Heather can step further back, Caitlyn can lead forward, and the systems do the work that currently requires a person to catch every mistake.

Profit Plan Assessment

O'Connor Family Law has strong financial awareness — the leadership team knows case values, retainer averages, monthly volume targets, and annual revenue to the dollar — but the firm is spending $12–15k per month on marketing without clear attribution to what that spend is actually producing in signed cases. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
"2025 revenue just over $4 million. 2026 target is $5 to $7 million."
The Problem
  • $10,000 per month in LSA spend with approximately 50% spam lead rate means the true cost per qualified client acquired is unknown — and almost certainly materially higher than the agency's reported cost-per-lead suggests
  • No fractional CFO in the current stack — growth from $4M to $5–7M without financial planning means adding attorneys and overhead without a corresponding strategy for how revenue translates to owner take-home
  • The $5–7M revenue target is ambitious and achievable, but without per-channel signed-case attribution, there is no way to know which marketing investment is actually driving the cases the firm is closing
  • Case duration of 9–18 months means revenue recognition lags significantly behind client acquisition — the firm needs to be tracking leading indicators (new signed clients/month) with precision to forecast where revenue will land 12 months out
The Positive
  • Lawmatics CRM and CallRail are in place — the data infrastructure to build signed-case attribution already exists; it simply is not being used by the current marketing partner
  • Average case value of $15,000 and initial retainer of $7,500 are known and consistent — this is a firm that understands its unit economics, which is the starting point for real profit planning
The Gap
  • Without knowing the true cost per signed client by channel, the firm cannot make informed decisions about where to increase or decrease marketing investment — it is optimizing blind
  • Growing from $4M to $7M without a fractional CFO means adding $3M in revenue without a plan for how margins, attorney compensation, overhead, and owner distributions scale alongside it
  • The gap between what the firm is spending on marketing ($17–20k/month all-in) and what it can attribute to signed cases is the largest single profit leak in the business — closing it does not require more spend, it requires better measurement
When every marketing dollar is tied to a signed case, the path from $4M to $7M stops being a goal and starts being a calculation — and that is exactly the kind of clarity that lets Heather decide how to grow without guessing.
