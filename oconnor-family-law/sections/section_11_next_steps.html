Dispute LSA Junk Leads for Credit Recovery
Profit Quick Win
Why It Matters
The firm is spending $10,000/month on LSA with approximately 50% of reported calls being spam or unqualified leads. Google's LSA dashboard allows disputed leads to be reviewed and credited — but the current agency has not been disputing them systematically. Every undisputed junk lead is money the firm has paid for a call it could not use.
The Opportunity
Systematic monthly dispute submissions — targeting every lead that did not meet geography or case-type criteria — can recover meaningful credit and reduce the true cost per qualified lead without changing the budget. The infrastructure to track this data already exists in Lawmatics and CallRail.
Replace Midday Answering Service Coverage
Intake Quick Win
Why It Matters
Answering Legal is underperforming specifically during the 11am–2pm window when internal staff are at capacity and incoming call volume is highest. A prospect who calls during this window and reaches a poor experience does not call back — at a $7,500 average retainer, two missed conversions per month is $15,000 in lost revenue the firm has already paid to generate.
The Opportunity
Replacing or supplementing Answering Legal with a higher-quality legal intake service — LexReception or Smith.ai, both of which the firm is already evaluating — and embedding a direct calendar booking link into the overflow flow converts prospects from a callback promise to a confirmed appointment. The intake engine is already built to close; it just needs every qualified call to reach it.

Rebalance PPC vs. LSA Budget Allocation
Lead Gen Long-Term Play
Why It Matters
LSA structurally cannot exclude "cheap," "free," or "pro se" divorce intent — that is a platform limitation, not an optimization problem. At $10,000/month in LSA and only $2–3,000/month in PPC, the budget ratio is inverted from what produces the best qualified-lead economics for a high-conflict, high-asset ICP. PPC allows income demographic layering, negative keyword exclusions, and tailored landing pages — capabilities that eliminate the junk volume at the source.
Who Is Doing It Better
Callahan | Inman — running high-budget PPC across all Worcester County and MetroWest divorce terms with the brand authority to compound paid performance with organic trust signals. Their budget weight is almost certainly heavier on PPC than LSA, giving them targeting precision O'Connor's current ratio does not match.
The Opportunity
Shifting $3,000–$5,000/month from LSA to PPC — while keeping LSA for its top-of-page visibility advantage on high-intent terms — allows income demographic layering (household income top 30%) in MetroWest and South Shore zip codes, negative keyword exclusions that eliminate junk volume at the source, and separate campaigns for Westborough and Hanover with landing pages built for each market. Same budget, cleaner leads.
