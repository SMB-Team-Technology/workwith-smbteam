Lead Generation

O'Connor Family Law is running paid ads on multiple channels and has the spending power, the CRM, and the intake team to generate 40–50 new clients per month — but the campaigns are producing dirty data instead of qualified leads, and the current agency has not acted on eight months of signed-case feedback the firm has been providing. The problem is not the budget. It is who the budget is producing and whether anyone is optimizing to what gets signed.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Homepage is positioned as a "Family Law Firm" rather than a "Divorce Lawyer" — lower-converting search intent that attracts broader, less-motivated traffic instead of the high-conflict, high-asset ICP the firm targets
  • PageSpeed scores could not be captured via automated tools due to bot-blocking on the site — scores must be verified live at pagespeed.web.dev; agency-managed sites commonly score 55–75 mobile / 75–90 desktop, ranges that directly impact Quality Score and paid ad costs
  • Basic AI chatbot is live but the firm is evaluating Intaker as an upgrade — without a video greeting or structured intake capture on the site, high-intent visitors who arrive outside business hours have no path to book a consult
  • Contact form is confirmed at /contact/ but above-fold placement could not be verified — every extra click a high-conflict divorce prospect has to make to reach a form is a prospect lost to a competitor whose form is visible immediately
Blind Spot: The firm's homepage keyword framing ("Family Law") is competing for lower-urgency searches. Prospects in the middle of a high-conflict divorce are searching "divorce lawyer" or "divorce attorney" — not "family law attorney." Aligning the homepage H1, title tag, and GBP primary category to "Divorce Lawyer" is one of the highest-leverage SEO moves available at no cost.
The Positive
  • Active blog with current, relevant content (ChatGPT privacy in divorce, social media impact, gambling addiction and divorce) — demonstrates topical authority and keeps organic traffic flowing
  • Dedicated video page, team bio page, and client portal confirm a modern, content-rich site that builds trust at multiple stages of the prospect journey
  • Geo-targeted landing pages confirmed for Hanover and Westborough — foundational local SEO structure is in place for both markets
The Gap
  • Every paid click landing on a page that says "Family Law Firm" instead of "Divorce Lawyer" pays a conversion penalty — lower Quality Score means higher CPCs and fewer ad impressions for the same budget
  • Without a video greeting on key landing pages, the firm is not differentiating Heather O'Connor's story — the personal founding mission that makes this firm different from every other divorce attorney in the market
  • If PageSpeed mobile scores are below 70, Google's ranking algorithm is suppressing organic visibility and inflating paid ad costs every day the issue goes unresolved
A website that converts at the rate this firm's intake team closes means every dollar of ad spend produces a qualified consult — not a click that bounces to Callahan | Inman.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • GBP primary category is "Divorce & Family Law" rather than "Divorce Lawyer" — the highest-converting primary category for the firm's core ICP, as confirmed during the discovery call
  • Both GBP profiles (Westborough and Hanover) list hours as Monday–Friday 9am–5pm only — closed hours on Google suppress map pack visibility; firms that list 24/7 hours receive more map impressions even if staff are not actually available around the clock
  • Hanover GBP listing was last updated September 2025 — a 7-month gap compared to Westborough (updated April 2026) suggests the Hanover profile is being maintained less actively, reducing its ranking signals in the South Shore market
Blind Spot: The firm's strongest review asset — approximately 136 Google reviews at 4.6 stars — is not being maximized because the primary category is wrong. Switching to "Divorce Lawyer" as the primary GBP category while retaining "Family Law Attorney" and "Child Custody Attorney" as secondaries is one of the fastest ranking improvements available, requiring no new content and no ad spend.
The Positive
  • Approximately 136 Google reviews at 4.6 stars — the strongest review volume in the Westborough family law market based on available data, a significant trust advantage when it appears in the map pack
  • Geo-targeted landing pages confirmed for both Westborough and Hanover (/family-law-in-hanover/, /family-law-in-hanover/child-custody-in-hanover/) — solid local SEO architecture supporting both office markets
The Gap
  • Every month the GBP primary category stays as "Family Law" rather than "Divorce Lawyer" is a month the firm's map pack appearances are weighted toward lower-converting searches
  • The South Shore / Hanover market is the firm's growth frontier — but the Hanover GBP profile is receiving less active management, reducing ranking signals exactly where expansion visibility matters most
South Shore Divorce & Family Law Center — operates a dedicated South Shore brand with a domain (ssdflc.com) and GBP profile built entirely around South Shore divorce identity, giving it inherent local-keyword authority in the Hanover/Norwell/Hingham market that O'Connor's current GBP setup does not match.
Fixing GBP category, hours, and Hanover profile activity costs nothing and takes days — and every local pack impression earned is a qualified prospect who did not click to a competitor instead.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • O'Connor Family Law is confirmed running Google PPC campaigns at approximately $2,000–$3,000 per month, managed by Empirical 360 — confirmed on the discovery call
  • The PPC budget is significantly underfunded relative to LSA ($10k/month) — in a market where PPC allows granular negative keyword control, income demographic layering, and tailored landing pages, the ratio is inverted from what would produce the best qualified-lead economics for the firm's high-conflict ICP
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Callahan | Inman — operating as the largest family law firm in Massachusetts with 21 attorneys, almost certainly running high-budget PPC across all Worcester County and MetroWest divorce terms; their brand authority compounds paid performance with organic trust signals
  • Bergeron | Burgess, PLLC — Super Lawyers Rising Stars-listed firm in Westborough with an active web presence and SEO footprint; likely running PPC targeting the same MetroWest high-asset divorce searcher that is O'Connor's ideal client
  • Logan & Logan LLP — South Shore-focused firm with a Google-verified 5-star rating and active digital presence; likely competing for Hanover/South Shore paid search placement where O'Connor is growing its office footprint
The Opportunity
  • Rebalancing budget toward PPC and away from LSA allows exclusion of "cheap," "free," "pro se," and "do it yourself" divorce intent keywords — the source of the ~50% junk lead volume the firm currently experiences on LSA
  • Income demographic layering (household income top 30%) applied to MetroWest and South Shore zip codes narrows the audience to the 45–60, business-owner, RSU-holder ICP the firm has defined as its highest-value target
  • Competitor name bidding on Callahan, Bergeron, and Burke Levy branded terms captures prospects already in the decision funnel who are evaluating multiple firms — lower CPL, higher intent
  • Separate campaigns for Westborough (MetroWest) and Hanover (South Shore) with tailored landing pages aligned to each market's terminology and case types — currently O'Connor runs undifferentiated campaigns that do not reflect the two distinct markets it serves
Blind Spot: The current agency has received eight months of signed/won/lost and qualification feedback from O'Connor and has not adjusted zip code exclusions or keyword negatives. This is not a budget problem — it is an optimization philosophy problem. A partner who optimizes to signed-case data rather than raw click volume would produce meaningfully different results from the same or lower spend.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • O'Connor Family Law is confirmed running LSA at approximately $5,000 per office per month — $10,000 per month total across both Westborough and Hanover — confirmed on the discovery call
  • The firm has been in LSA for 8–10 months but the current agency has not optimized bids by lead quality, excluded poor-performing zip codes, or used Google's dispute mechanism to push back on unqualified leads — resulting in approximately 50% of reported calls being spam or junk
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Callahan | Inman — almost certainly in LSA given firm size and marketing sophistication; their review volume gives them strong LSA ranking signals in the Westborough/Worcester County market
  • Burke Levy, P.C. — 4.9-star average rating is a direct LSA ranking advantage; Google's LSA algorithm weights review quality heavily, meaning Burke Levy likely appears ahead of O'Connor in LSA results despite having significantly fewer total reviews
The Opportunity
  • LSA leads that are marked as "not a good lead" in the dashboard are eligible for credit — the current agency is not systematically disputing junk calls, which means O'Connor is paying for leads it cannot use
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly; optimized properly, this is the highest-visibility lead source available
  • Pairing LSA with a properly configured Intaker video greeting means high-intent prospects who find the firm via LSA land on a page with Heather O'Connor's personal story — reducing the "just calling around" behavior that inflates unqualified consult volume
Blind Spot: LSA structurally cannot exclude "cheap" or "free" divorce intent — that is a platform limitation, not a solvable problem through negative keywords. The correct response is to shift budget weight from LSA toward PPC, where targeting precision is significantly higher, and use LSA for its top-of-page visibility advantage on high-intent terms only.

  • Educational content ads — capture leads earlier in the decision process
  • Heather O'Connor's founding story (left an unhealthy marriage in 2016, built this firm from that experience) is one of the most compelling attorney brand narratives in the Massachusetts family law market — video content built around this story converts far better than generic "call us" ads, and the firm has a YouTube channel already proving the appetite for this content
