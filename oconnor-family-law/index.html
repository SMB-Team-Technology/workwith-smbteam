Here Is Where O'Connor Family Law Stands Today

O'Connor Family Law is a 13-attorney, 25-employee family law firm founded by Heather O'Connor in 2016, operating out of offices in Westborough and Hanover and serving clients across Massachusetts, Southern New Hampshire, and Rhode Island. The firm generated just over $4 million in 2025 revenue and has set an ambitious $5–7 million target for 2026, backed by one of the most sophisticated intake operations in the market — fast response SLAs, VA-led MQL screening, non-attorney sales capability, and 96.7% consult show rates. The single most urgent problem standing between where the firm is and where it wants to be is a marketing partner that reports raw call counts instead of signed-case economics, leaving the operations team manually compensating for what the campaigns should be doing automatically.

Stage 6 is where the marketing engine runs itself on signed-case data, where Caitlyn's bandwidth goes toward growth instead of agency auditing, and where $5–7 million becomes a starting point — not a ceiling.

Where You Are Today

Stage 5: Law Firm CEO
  • Spending $12–15k/month on ads that generate ~50% spam leads, with no optimization to signed-case data
  • Director of Operations manually reviewing agency reports to separate qualified leads from raw call counts
  • Intake infrastructure is strong and ready for 40–50 new clients/month — but starved of qualified volume from upstream
  • Revenue at $4M with a clear $5–7M goal, but current marketing partner cannot close the gap between those two numbers

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The infrastructure is already here — the team, the process, the CRM, the intake SLAs. What is missing is a marketing partner who optimizes to what matters: signed clients, not raw call volume.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Intake Assessment

O'Connor Family Law has built one of the most operationally sophisticated intake processes in the Massachusetts family law market — rapid MQL screening, VA-led follow-up, a non-attorney sales team, and a 96.7% consult show rate. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: GREEN
"We text instantly, and we're calling within five to ten minutes. The VAs do the MQL screen — geography and case type — and then non-attorney sales can close certain case types. The complex ones go to a paid strategy session."
The Problem
  • Answering Legal, the firm's overflow answering service, is underperforming — specifically during the 11am–2pm midday window when internal staff are at capacity and incoming calls most need coverage
  • When overflow calls are not answered and booked in real time, prospects in an emotional, urgent decision moment call the next firm on the list — and that firm is often Callahan | Inman or Burke Levy
  • Pre-consult nurture between booking and the consult date is not formalized — prospects who book and then shop are not receiving content that reduces comparison behavior before they sit down with an attorney
Blind Spot: The intake SLA is excellent for inbound calls — but there is no mechanism to capture high-intent website visitors who arrive outside business hours and cannot reach a person. An Intaker video greeting with a structured booking path would convert a meaningful portion of after-hours traffic that currently bounces with no follow-up.
The Positive
  • Text response within minutes and callback within 5–10 minutes — a genuine competitive advantage in a market where most firms take hours or never respond to new inquiries
  • 96.7% consult show rate, Lawmatics CRM, CallRail tracking, and a structured escalation path from VA to non-attorney sales to paid attorney consult — the infrastructure to handle 40–50 new clients per month is already built
  • Paid attorney strategy sessions at $475/hour for complex cases qualify and warm the highest-value prospects before retainer conversations begin
The Gap
  • Every midday overflow call that goes unanswered or handled poorly by Answering Legal is a prospect who may never call back — at a $7,500 average retainer, even two missed converts per month represents $15,000 in lost revenue
  • Without pre-consult nurture content (case preparation guides, what to expect, firm story), prospects who book often continue comparison shopping — reducing show rate and sign rate on calls the firm has already paid to generate
  • The gap between what the intake team can close and what the marketing is delivering is the entire growth constraint — this team is ready for more qualified volume right now
An intake engine this strong only reaches its potential when the leads flowing into it are qualified — fixing the upstream lead quality problem is what turns a good intake system into a $5–7M firm engine.
