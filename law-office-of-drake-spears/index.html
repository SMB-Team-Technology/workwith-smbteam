We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Lead Generation

Intake

Profit Plan Assessment

As a firm that opened in October 2024, the Law Office of Drake Spears has not yet had the opportunity to establish the financial tracking and profit planning infrastructure that sustainable growth requires. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: RED — Critical Priority
The Problem
  • No confirmed bookkeeping system, profit and loss tracking, or financial visibility infrastructure for the law firm LLC — the firm is operating without an established profit baseline or cost structure
  • Revenue is entirely tied to Drake's personal billable hours — no scalable income streams, no recurring revenue model, and no analysis of which case types generate the most margin per hour invested
  • No defined case intake criteria based on profitability — taking every inquiry regardless of case type or revenue potential creates the risk of maximum hours for minimum take-home income
  • Without an established retainer structure and billing rate benchmarks, Drake may be underpricing relative to the Hendricks County market and leaving significant revenue on the table from day one
Blind Spot: A new firm that grows rapidly without financial infrastructure often discovers that "more revenue" translated into more expenses and more personal hours — with no corresponding increase in take-home pay. Building the profit plan now, before growth accelerates, is how you ensure that the clients and cases you invest in bringing in actually show up in your bank account.
The Gap
  • Without knowing the cost per case type, Drake cannot make intelligent decisions about which cases to accept, which to refer out, and how to price retainers to reflect the true value of the outcome he delivers
  • Without a bookkeeping and financial reporting system, tax preparation becomes reactive and expensive, and bank balance becomes the only financial metric — which is not a management tool
  • The window to build good financial habits is always now — the longer the firm operates without financial infrastructure, the more disorganized the retroactive cleanup becomes and the harder it is to reconstruct an accurate picture of the business
Building your own firm means you get to decide how much you keep — but only if you have the financial visibility to make that decision with confidence.
