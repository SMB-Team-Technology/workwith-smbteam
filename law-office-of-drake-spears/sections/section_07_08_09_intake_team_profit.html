Intake Assessment

As the firm's sole attorney and only staff member, Drake currently handles every aspect of client intake personally — every call, every consultation, every follow-up. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED — Critical Priority
The Problem
  • All intake handled personally by Drake — no delegation, no system, and no staff available to respond when he is in a hearing, deposition, or client consultation
  • No confirmed after-hours intake coverage — family law clients frequently call in crisis moments (evenings, weekends) when Drake is unavailable, and those inquiries receive no immediate response
  • No intake tracking system or CRM confirmed — leads from directories, referrals, and the website appear to be managed manually with no systematic follow-up process for prospects who do not immediately commit
  • No defined intake qualification script or protocol — each inquiry is likely handled differently, creating inconsistent conversion outcomes and no data to improve against
Blind Spot: Research consistently shows that the first law firm to respond to an inquiry wins the client the majority of the time — and in family law, where clients are often calling during a personal crisis, a delayed response does not just mean losing a lead. It means that person went to a competitor at the moment they needed someone most. Every hour without an intake system is an hour that converts at a lower rate than it should.
The Gap
  • Every hour Drake spends personally answering intake calls is an hour not spent on billable casework, business development, or building the firm — intake handled by the attorney is the most expensive call in the building
  • Without a defined follow-up sequence, leads who inquire but do not immediately sign are being lost — there is no system bringing them back after a first contact
  • Every uncaptured inquiry represents potential revenue that has already cost Drake something in referral goodwill, directory presence, or future marketing investment
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Team Assessment

The Law Office of Drake Spears is a one-person firm — Drake is the attorney, the intake coordinator, the administrator, and the business owner simultaneously, with no capacity to delegate any function. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED — Critical Priority
The Problem
  • Fully solo — no paralegal, no legal assistant, no intake coordinator, no administrative staff of any kind; Drake is the only person in the firm
  • Drake is responsible for all client service, all intake calls, all case filings, all follow-up, and all business operations — no function can be delegated because there is no one to delegate to
  • No accountability structure, no org chart, and no systems for any task to run without the owner present — everything stops the moment Drake is unavailable
  • Hard capacity ceiling is immediate: Drake can only personally handle a fixed number of active cases, which limits both revenue potential and the ability to scale without degrading service quality
Blind Spot: The assumption that "I'll hire when I can afford to" tends to work in reverse for solo attorneys. The reason the revenue is not there yet is that Drake is at capacity doing work that a legal assistant at $15–$20/hour could handle — administrative tasks, intake calls, document organization — which means he cannot take on the additional cases that would fund the hire. The first hire enables the growth; it does not result from it.
The Gap
  • Without at least a part-time paralegal or legal assistant, Drake's calendar is consumed by tasks that do not require a law degree — time that should be spent on billable work, client relationships, and business development
  • The absence of any team structure means Drake cannot accept more clients without immediately degrading service quality for existing clients — sustainable growth is physically impossible at the current structure
  • A solo firm has no operational resilience: a single illness, a family obligation, or any unexpected disruption stops the entire business and affects every active client simultaneously
Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Profit Plan Assessment

As a firm that opened in October 2024, the Law Office of Drake Spears has not yet had the opportunity to establish the financial tracking and profit planning infrastructure that sustainable growth requires. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: RED — Critical Priority
The Problem
  • No confirmed bookkeeping system, profit and loss tracking, or financial visibility infrastructure for the law firm LLC — the firm is operating without an established profit baseline or cost structure
  • Revenue is entirely tied to Drake's personal billable hours — no scalable income streams, no recurring revenue model, and no analysis of which case types generate the most margin per hour invested
  • No defined case intake criteria based on profitability — taking every inquiry regardless of case type or revenue potential creates the risk of maximum hours for minimum take-home income
  • Without an established retainer structure and billing rate benchmarks, Drake may be underpricing relative to the Hendricks County market and leaving significant revenue on the table from day one
Blind Spot: A new firm that grows rapidly without financial infrastructure often discovers that "more revenue" translated into more expenses and more personal hours — with no corresponding increase in take-home pay. Building the profit plan now, before growth accelerates, is how you ensure that the clients and cases you invest in bringing in actually show up in your bank account.
The Gap
  • Without knowing the cost per case type, Drake cannot make intelligent decisions about which cases to accept, which to refer out, and how to price retainers to reflect the true value of the outcome he delivers
  • Without a bookkeeping and financial reporting system, tax preparation becomes reactive and expensive, and bank balance becomes the only financial metric — which is not a management tool
  • The window to build good financial habits is always now — the longer the firm operates without financial infrastructure, the more disorganized the retroactive cleanup becomes and the harder it is to reconstruct an accurate picture of the business
Building your own firm means you get to decide how much you keep — but only if you have the financial visibility to make that decision with confidence.
