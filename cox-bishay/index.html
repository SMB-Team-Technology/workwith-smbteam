Here Is Where Cox & Bishay, LLP Stands Today

Cox & Bishay, LLP has practiced exclusively in family law from West Chester, Pennsylvania since 1988 — a 35-year boutique firm built on peer recognition, community trust, and a track record that includes Super Lawyers, Best of the Main Line honors, and pro bono service awards. With two partners and a paralegal, the firm is operating as a Stage 3 Solo Practitioner: highly regarded in the Chester County legal community, but running entirely on referrals while competitors invest in digital marketing, local SEO, and paid advertising to capture the very clients Cox & Bishay's experience could win.

Stage 6 is where 35 years of earned reputation becomes a firm that attracts the right clients, runs without consuming every hour, and builds a legacy that outlasts any single case — and that is exactly where we are taking Cox & Bishay, LLP.

Where You Are Today

  • Referrals and word-of-mouth are the only source of new clients — no paid or organic digital lead generation in place
  • Not ranking in Google's local 3-pack for any family law search term in West Chester or Chester County
  • Website has no contact form, no CTAs, and no lead capture — visitors cannot inquire outside business hours
  • Partners personally carry all casework and client communication with support from a single paralegal

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Intake Assessment

Cox & Bishay operates with no online intake path — no contact form, no chat, and no after-hours coverage — meaning any prospective client who cannot reach the firm by phone during business hours has no way to start a conversation. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • No contact form exists on the website — the firm's only inquiry path is a direct phone call to one of the attorneys' extension numbers, with no intake staff to intercept calls and no way to submit an inquiry online
  • No after-hours or weekend coverage — any prospect who reaches out outside of office hours receives no response until the next business day, at which point most will have already called a competitor who was available
  • No evidence of a defined intake process or follow-up sequence — without a structured system, there is no way to ensure leads are being responded to consistently or tracked for follow-up
  • No intake delegation observed — with two partners and one paralegal, all new client communication likely falls directly to the attorneys, creating a bottleneck that limits both responsiveness and capacity
Blind Spot: Most family law clients make the decision to reach out during moments of emotional distress — often evenings and weekends. A firm that can only receive inquiries during business hours is not just missing calls; it is systematically handing those prospects to any competitor with after-hours availability.
The Gap
  • Every inquiry that arrives after hours and receives no response represents a case that walked out the door — at a $10,000 average case value, even two lost consultations per month equals $20,000 in forfeited revenue
  • Without an intake process, both partners are required for every first conversation — adding marketing will immediately expose this bottleneck and make the workload unsustainable before it becomes profitable
  • The absence of an online inquiry form means the website cannot function as a lead generation tool even when advertising drives traffic to it — every dollar spent on ads produces a visit, not a lead
Building an intake system that answers prospects before a competitor does is not just a revenue move — it is what frees Rami and Kimberly from being personally required for every first conversation.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • No contact form exists anywhere on coxbishay.com — the firm's "Information" page lists direct phone extensions for each attorney and a fax number; there is no online submission path for prospective clients

Status: AMBER
The Problem

  • Both partners appear to function as both practitioners and business owners simultaneously, with no division of responsibilities and no management layer to absorb volume when lead flow increases
  • No evidence of performance tracking, KPI monitoring, or accountability structure — without this infrastructure, neither partner can step away and still know how the business is performing
  • The firm has sustained a small-team model for an extended period; adding marketing without first scaling team capacity will create overwhelm before it creates growth

The Positive
  • The two-partner model provides some distribution of senior-level workload — Rami and Kimberly sharing cases is meaningfully more sustainable than a true solo practice with no peer to carry the load
  • The firm has maintained consistent quality and community reputation for 35 years, demonstrating that the partners have built reliable working habits and client service instincts even without formal systems
The Gap
  • Without dedicated intake staff, every new client relationship requires a partner's personal time — this bottleneck will surface immediately once marketing begins driving consistent lead volume
  • Neither partner can take extended time away, delegate strategically, or eventually transition the practice without a team structure below them that can operate without constant personal involvement
  • There is currently no path to the firm running without Rami and Kimberly personally present — and that is the only barrier between where the firm is now and what it could become
Building a team with a trained intake coordinator and clear operational roles is the only path to growing Cox & Bishay without both partners giving more of themselves in the process.

Profit Plan Assessment

Cox & Bishay has not disclosed revenue, financial structure, or profitability — leaving the firm's capacity for growth investment, long-term planning, and true net earnings as open questions. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Revenue was not disclosed — the firm's current financial baseline, margins, and realistic capacity for growth investment are unknown, making it impossible to plan confidently
  • No evidence of a formal financial management structure — no CFO, no financial dashboard, and no visible infrastructure for tracking overhead, case profitability, or cost per client acquisition
  • Without data on cost per case type or case margin by matter, the partners cannot know which practice areas and client profiles are driving profitability and which are consuming the most time for the least return
  • A referral-only practice has a natural revenue ceiling set by the size of the referral network — without active client acquisition, growth has likely plateaued at whatever the partners' professional network can sustain
Blind Spot: Many boutique family law firms have higher gross revenue than they realize — but lower net profitability than they expect. Without case-level cost tracking, time-intensive high-conflict matters consume the same firm resources as faster, more profitable ones, making the true profit picture invisible until year-end.
The Positive
  • A 35-year track record with no visible signs of financial distress suggests a stable, fundamentally sound practice — the firm has survived and maintained quality through multiple economic cycles
  • A two-partner boutique with one paralegal carries inherently lean overhead compared to a larger multi-attorney firm — meaning profit margins, once visible, may be stronger than expected
The Gap
  • Without financial clarity, the firm cannot make confident decisions about growth investment — every dollar spent on marketing is a bet made without data about what the return needs to be
  • No evidence of a profit plan means the partners are discovering what they earned at year end rather than planning and engineering what they will earn throughout the year
  • Until revenue, case value, and profitability by matter type are tracked, the firm cannot identify its most valuable clients — and cannot make smart decisions about which practice areas to scale
A clear profit plan does not just show what the firm earns — it shows what is possible, what to invest in next, and whether the growth Rami and Kimberly want is financially within reach.
