Here Is Where Elder Legal Solutions Stands Today

Elder Legal Solutions is a three-attorney elder law practice serving clients across Florida, North Carolina, South Carolina, and Washington D.C., with Medicaid and VA asset protection work driving 60–70% of revenue alongside estate planning and probate. The firm has grown revenue to roughly $740,000 a year on referrals alone — but nearly all of that growth runs through one person: partner Tamr Tadros personally handles all legal ops for the firm's core practice area, and owner Neal Rice personally manages an intake process buried under more than 10,000 unread emails. The single most important finding: this is a firm whose case load has outgrown its systems, with no formal financial reporting, no paid lead generation, and one partner acting as an unmanaged single point of failure.

Stage 6 is where Tamr's raise gets funded by the firm's systems instead of his own overtime — and where Neal stops being the bottleneck standing between a full inbox and a signed client.

Where You Are Today

STAGE 3: SOLO PRACTITIONER
  • New client flow depends on referral partners, not any owned lead generation system
  • Neal personally handles intake out of an inbox with 10,000+ unread emails
  • Tamr personally runs all legal ops for the practice's core Medicaid/VA work — if he is out, that work stops
  • No formal KPIs or financial reporting — Neal categorizes transactions for Bench once a year

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between a $740K referral-only practice and a firm that runs on its own systems is not a talent gap — Neal and Tamr have already proven they can win cases and grow revenue. It is a systems gap, and closing it is what lets Tamr's raise come from structure instead of stretching two people thinner.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Elder Legal Solutions

Until that changes, the stated goal — an extra $200K in revenue to fund Tamr's $50K raise — will keep asking two already-overloaded partners to simply work harder, not smarter.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Intake Assessment

Neal personally manages intake by hand, with an inbox of more than 10,000 unread emails standing between the firm's referral relationships and a signed client. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
"10,000+ unread emails"
The Problem
  • Neal personally handles all intake — there is no delegation, and the inbox backlog shows it
  • No CRM or intake tracking system exists — there is no way to know how many leads are being lost before they are ever followed up
  • Close rate swings 25–50% with no defined process in place to explain or close that gap consistently
  • No after-hours or overflow coverage — inquiries that arrive outside business hours have no defined path to a fast response
Blind Spot: every day that inbox backlog sits unread, a family that is already comparing Elder Legal Solutions to a referral or a competitor is deciding based on who responds first — and right now, that is rarely you.
The Positive

The Gap
  • Every unread email is a potential client sitting un-contacted — and because so much of the firm's volume comes from referral partners, a slow response risks the referral relationship itself, not just the case
  • Raising the close rate from its current 25–50% range toward the stated 50–75% goal would let existing referral volume work harder toward the $200K revenue target — without spending a single ad dollar
  • Without visibility into where in the process leads go cold, the same gaps repeat referral after referral
Fixing intake is the fastest way to move toward Tamr's raise, because it converts business the firm has already earned instead of asking either partner to work more hours.
