Here Is Where Elder Legal Solutions Stands Today

Elder Legal Solutions is a three-attorney elder law practice serving clients across Florida, North Carolina, South Carolina, and Washington D.C., with Medicaid and VA asset protection work driving 60–70% of revenue alongside estate planning and probate. The firm has grown revenue to roughly $740,000 a year on referrals alone — but nearly all of that growth runs through one person: partner Tamr Tadros personally handles all legal ops for the firm's core practice area, and owner Neal Rice personally manages an intake process buried under more than 10,000 unread emails. The single most important finding: this is a firm whose case load has outgrown its systems, with no formal financial reporting, no paid lead generation, and one partner acting as an unmanaged single point of failure.

Stage 6 is where Tamr's raise gets funded by the firm's systems instead of his own overtime — and where Neal stops being the bottleneck standing between a full inbox and a signed client.

Where You Are Today

STAGE 3: SOLO PRACTITIONER
  • New client flow depends on referral partners, not any owned lead generation system
  • Neal personally handles intake out of an inbox with 10,000+ unread emails
  • Tamr personally runs all legal ops for the practice's core Medicaid/VA work — if he is out, that work stops
  • No formal KPIs or financial reporting — Neal categorizes transactions for Bench once a year

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between a $740K referral-only practice and a firm that runs on its own systems is not a talent gap — Neal and Tamr have already proven they can win cases and grow revenue. It is a systems gap, and closing it is what lets Tamr's raise come from structure instead of stretching two people thinner.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Elder Legal Solutions
Elder Legal Solutions is operating at Stage 3 — a Solo-Practitioner-style firm in structure even though three attorneys and five office locations sit behind it, because every part of the client journey still funnels through one of two people. The single most urgent gap is intake and legal-ops capacity: Neal cannot keep up with inbound volume, and Tamr cannot be the only person capable of running the firm's primary practice area without capping how far the firm can grow. Until that changes, the stated goal — an extra $200K in revenue to fund Tamr's $50K raise — will keep asking two already-overloaded partners to simply work harder, not smarter.
