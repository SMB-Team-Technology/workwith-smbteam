Lead Generation

Elder Legal Solutions has grown to roughly $740K a year serving Medicaid & VA asset protection, estate planning, and probate clients across four states — entirely on referral relationships, with no paid advertising history and no presence in the directories where prospective elder-law clients actually search.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • No individual attorney bios are published on the homepage — only a team photo of Neal Rice, Andrew Rosenberg, and Tamer Tadros, with no credentials or personal story to build trust before a call
  • PageSpeed mobile and desktop scores could not be captured live this research pass (Google's PageSpeed API returned repeated rate-limit errors) — this must be spot-checked live before final delivery, but is flagged here rather than estimated
  • No blog and no dated content — a "Resources" nav item and a podcast reference exist in the footer, but there is no ongoing content presence to support organic search
  • No contact form was found directly on the homepage, adding friction for a visitor ready to reach out
Blind Spot: the firm has an 8-month website revamp, SEO, and AEO contract already underway — meaning the site prospective clients see today is the version about to be replaced, and every week before that relaunch is a week competitors' geo-targeted content keeps compounding.
The Positive
  • The homepage has a clear headline ("Protecting Your Legacy, Planning Your Future") with two above-the-fold calls to action — "Schedule Consultation" and "Our Services"
  • A website revamp, SEO, and AEO engagement is already under contract, showing the firm is investing in this exact gap
The Gap
  • Visitors researching a firm before calling have no attorney credibility content to review, which pushes them toward competitors who publish it
  • Every visitor who doesn't immediately find a way to reach out is a lead lost before intake ever sees it
  • A revamp in progress means the current site is actively costing leads today, while the fix is still months from live
A website that builds trust and converts on its own is one less thing standing between Elder Legal Solutions and the revenue that funds Tamr's raise.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Elder Legal Solutions does not appear in Avvo's Fort Lauderdale elder-law attorney listing at all — a directory that lists 20+ competing attorneys, including 13 lawyers specifically under "Medicaid & Medicare"
  • No dedicated Yelp business listing was found for the firm, while named competitors Siegel Law Group and Elder Needs Law both have active, reviewed Yelp profiles
  • Live GBP star rating, total review count, and local 3-pack placement could not be confirmed this session and are flagged for live verification before this report is finalized
Blind Spot: the firm operates 5 office locations across Florida, North Carolina, South Carolina, and Washington D.C. — multi-location firms are especially prone to citation drift, and a live NAP (name/address/phone) audit across all 5 locations should be a priority once local SEO work begins.
The Gap
  • Prospective clients researching options in Avvo or Justia currently cannot find Elder Legal Solutions at all — they only find the competitors listed there
  • Absence from the directories where elder-law clients compare firms means every one of those searches is a lead going to a competitor by default
The Siegel Law Group, P.A. — runs a full network of city-specific landing pages ("Medicaid Lawyer in Fort Lauderdale," "Boca Raton Elder Lawyer," "West Palm Beach Elder Lawyer," "Broward Elder Lawyer") that Elder Legal Solutions' own site has no equivalent of, despite serving more states.
Local SEO is how Elder Legal Solutions gets found by the next Medicaid or VA client before that family ever calls a referral source — reducing how much of the firm's growth depends on relationships Tamr and Neal have to personally maintain.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Elder Legal Solutions does not appear to be running Google search ads for Medicaid & VA asset protection or elder law searches in Fort Lauderdale — they may not be configured to show for these terms, or simply have never run paid search
  • The same holds for estate planning and probate searches — no sponsored placements were observed for the firm in this practice area either
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • The Siegel Law Group, P.A. — was not observed running active paid search this pass either, instead winning visibility organically through its geo-targeted landing page network
  • Elder Needs Law, PLLC — active ad status was not confirmed live this session and should be re-checked before campaigns launch, but the firm's broader multi-platform presence suggests active marketing investment
The Opportunity
  • Obvious terms: "Medicaid planning attorney Fort Lauderdale," "VA benefits attorney Fort Lauderdale," "elder law attorney Fort Lauderdale"
  • Obvious terms: "estate planning attorney Fort Lauderdale," "probate attorney Fort Lauderdale," "special needs planning attorney Fort Lauderdale"
  • Non-obvious: competitor-name and neighboring-market terms (Boca Raton, West Palm Beach, Broward) where Siegel Law Group currently owns the geo-targeted content the firm has none of
  • Non-obvious: urgency-driven terms tied to nursing-home placement and VA claim deadlines, moments when families search immediately rather than wait for a referral
Blind Spot: with no paid search history in any practice area, Elder Legal Solutions has no data on cost-per-lead or close rate from ads — the 25–50% close rate on referral leads may not transfer directly to cold paid-search leads, and campaigns should be built to test and refine this rather than assume parity.
