Intake Assessment

Neal personally manages intake by hand, with an inbox of more than 10,000 unread emails standing between the firm's referral relationships and a signed client. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
"10,000+ unread emails"
The Problem
  • Neal personally handles all intake — there is no delegation, and the inbox backlog shows it
  • No CRM or intake tracking system exists — there is no way to know how many leads are being lost before they are ever followed up
  • Close rate swings 25–50% with no defined process in place to explain or close that gap consistently
  • No after-hours or overflow coverage — inquiries that arrive outside business hours have no defined path to a fast response
Blind Spot: every day that inbox backlog sits unread, a family that is already comparing Elder Legal Solutions to a referral or a competitor is deciding based on who responds first — and right now, that is rarely you.
The Positive
  • When a lead does get worked personally by Neal or Tamr, close rates climb as high as 50% — the legal and sales skill is clearly there, the system to apply it consistently is not
The Gap
  • Every unread email is a potential client sitting un-contacted — and because so much of the firm's volume comes from referral partners, a slow response risks the referral relationship itself, not just the case
  • Raising the close rate from its current 25–50% range toward the stated 50–75% goal would let existing referral volume work harder toward the $200K revenue target — without spending a single ad dollar
  • Without visibility into where in the process leads go cold, the same gaps repeat referral after referral
Fixing intake is the fastest way to move toward Tamr's raise, because it converts business the firm has already earned instead of asking either partner to work more hours.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • No contact form was found directly on the homepage — intake friction starts before a visitor ever reaches Neal's inbox
  • No live chat is present, so after-hours visitors have no way to start a conversation in the moment they are most engaged
  • With intake already backlogged, every extra step required to reach the firm compounds a bottleneck that already exists

Team Assessment

Tamr Tadros is the only person who runs legal ops for the firm's core Medicaid & VA asset protection practice — a dependency the firm itself has already identified as a ceiling on growth. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
"handles all legal ops for the main practice area, creating a single point of failure and preventing scalability"
The Problem
  • Tamr is the only person who can run legal ops for the firm's core Medicaid/VA practice — if he is out, that revenue line stops
  • No management layer exists between the two partners and the casework — every file still funnels through one of them personally
  • The paralegal hire identified as needed for intake and drafting support has not yet been made
  • No formal org structure, defined roles, or accountability system exists across the firm's 5 office locations
Blind Spot: the fact that Tamr has made himself indispensable to the firm's biggest revenue line feels like reliability today — but it is the single ceiling on how much bigger this firm can get without him personally working every case.
The Positive
  • Neal and Tamr have already identified the exact fix themselves — a paralegal hire for intake and drafting support — which means the firm isn't blind to the problem, only behind on solving it
The Gap
  • Neither partner can take meaningful time away from the firm without core practice work stalling — the owner cannot have more free time until this changes
  • Growth in case volume currently means growth in Tamr's personal workload, not the firm's capacity
  • Without a second person trained on core Medicaid/VA workflows, a single vacation or health issue is a real business risk
Until Tamr stops being the only person who can run the firm's core practice, more revenue just means more hours for him — not the raise it is meant to fund.

Profit Plan Assessment

Elder Legal Solutions has grown revenue $100K–$120K a year with no formal financial reporting behind that growth. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
"No formal KPIs or strategic financial reporting. Neal manually categorizes transactions for Bench once a year."
The Problem
  • No formal KPIs or financial reporting exist — transactions are categorized for the bookkeeper only once a year
  • There is no visibility into which practice area — Medicaid/VA asset protection or estate planning/probate — actually drives the most profit, only revenue share
  • The $200K revenue goal tied to funding Tamr's $50K raise has no supporting financial plan behind it — only a target
  • No tracking of cost per case or client acquisition cost exists to know what growth is actually working
Blind Spot: growing revenue without growing visibility means the firm could hit its ~$940K goal and still not know whether it actually funded the $50K raise it was meant to pay for.
The Positive
  • Revenue has grown steadily and organically — $100K to $120K a year — even without any formal financial tracking in place, which points to a healthy underlying business once visibility catches up to it
The Gap
  • Without margin visibility by practice area, the firm cannot tell whether its fastest-growing work is also its most profitable work
  • A revenue goal with no financial plan behind it risks becoming $200K of extra revenue that produces no extra take-home for either partner
  • Once-a-year bookkeeping means problems are caught 12 months late, not in time to fix them
A real profit plan is what turns "$200K more revenue" into an actual, funded $50K raise for Tamr — instead of just a number that sounds good on paper.
