Here Is Where Irvine Legal Stands Today

Joshua Irvine has built Irvine Legal into a genuinely differentiated practice — a subscription-based outsourced general counsel firm serving Utah's construction industry, with 800 registered agent clients and 20–25 active litigation cases running simultaneously. Generating approximately $780,000 per year entirely on referrals, the firm has real momentum and a team of 8–9 staff; the single most important finding is that without a digital marketing system, Joshua is one referral drought away from a growth ceiling that no amount of effort can overcome.

The firms that take that Himalaya trip — and fund their nonprofits from a distance — are the ones that stopped depending on referrals and built a machine that works whether they are in the office or not.

Where You Are Today

Stage 4: Small Business Manager
  • 100% of new business comes from referrals — no digital marketing system exists
  • The firm stalls within 1–2 weeks when Joshua steps away — still the linchpin for contract reviews and litigation deadlines
  • Zero Google reviews and no local pack visibility mean competitors are capturing every high-intent construction law search in the area
  • The subscription general counsel model is growing, but growth is limited by how many referrals happen to arrive each month

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between referral-dependent growth and a self-sustaining firm is not that wide — but it requires a deliberate system, not more waiting for the next referral to come in.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Irvine Legal
Irvine Legal is operating at Stage 4 — a Small Business Manager who has built real infrastructure (a team, a sales process for subscription clients, a strong referral network) but remains personally essential to the firm's day-to-day survival. The single most urgent gap is the complete absence of a digital lead generation system: with zero Google reviews, no paid advertising, and no local pack visibility, every construction company owner in Davis and Weber counties who searches online is finding competitors — not Joshua. Until a predictable digital marketing engine is in place, Joshua's goal of a self-sustaining firm that can fund his nonprofit mission and give him six weeks in the Himalayas will remain just that — a goal.
