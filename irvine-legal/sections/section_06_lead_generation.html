Website Audit

"We have 600 articles on the site — we've been doing that for about 5 years. But we haven't really optimized them technically."
The Problem
  • Site design vintage is 5+ years old with logo assets dating to 2016 — an outdated presentation that erodes trust in a professional services context before a prospect even reads a word
  • PageSpeed scores: Unable to retrieve at time of analysis — site uses dynamic rendering that prevented score capture; a technical performance audit is needed before launch
  • No attorney biography or personal credentials visible on the homepage — visitors cannot immediately connect with Joshua or understand why his construction law expertise is unique
  • Contact form is absent from the homepage — only an email link and phone number are available above the fold, creating friction at the moment a prospect is ready to reach out
  • No geo-targeted landing pages for Salt Lake City, Weber County, Park City, or Arizona — the firm's geographic ambition is not matched by its SEO infrastructure
  • Old immigration content still live on the site creates confusion about the firm's actual focus areas and may attract unqualified inquiries
Blind Spot: The 600+ blog articles represent a genuine content asset — but without technical SEO optimization, they are not ranking or driving traffic. These articles could be systematically improved to capture searches like "construction contract attorney Utah," "mechanic's lien help Davis County," and "outsourced general counsel for contractors" — searches that nobody in the market is aggressively targeting right now. This is a white-space SEO opportunity sitting unused inside content the firm already created.
The Positive
  • 600+ blog articles built over five years creates a substantial content library that can be technically optimized rather than rebuilt from scratch
  • Construction law niche is clearly communicated — the site's practice area pages cover preliens, liens, foreclosure protection, contract drafting, bid management, and compliance, which is differentiated content most general business law firms do not have
The Gap
  • Every week without an optimized homepage is a week where construction company owners who found the site leave without contacting the firm — and call a competitor who made it easier
  • No city-specific landing pages means the firm is invisible to geo-modified searches ("construction attorney Layton Utah," "general counsel Salt Lake City") that drive the highest-intent local traffic
  • The planned WordPress redesign is not yet live — until it is, the firm is competing with a five-year-old site against firms that have invested in modern conversion infrastructure
A rebuilt, conversion-optimized website is the foundation everything else runs on — without it, ads drive traffic to a leaky bucket.

Local SEO

  • Irvine Legal does not appear in the local 3-pack for any construction or business law search observed — including "construction attorney Utah," "construction attorney Ogden Utah," and "business attorney Layton Utah"
  • NAP inconsistency across directories: the Layton address (2297 N Hill Field Rd, Suite 102) and the old Ogden address (2650 Washington Blvd, Ste 103) both appear active across Avvo, Yelp, FindLaw, and LawInfo — this citation conflict suppresses local rankings
  • GBP primary category appears incorrectly listed as "Employment Law" on Yelp cross-reference — a miscategorization that reduces relevance for construction and business law searches
Blind Spot: "Outsourced general counsel for construction companies" is a near-zero-competition keyword in the Utah market. No local competitor appears to be targeting this exact phrase with landing pages or GBP optimization. Irvine Legal could own this search category entirely — but only if the GBP is optimized, the NAP is unified, and a dedicated landing page is created for this specific service.
The Positive
  • AGC Utah membership and Super Lawyers Rising Stars recognition provide legitimate credibility signals that can be prominently featured in GBP posts and directory listings
The Gap
  • Every search for "construction attorney Ogden" or "business lawyer Davis County" currently sends prospects to SSHK (22 reviews) or Froerer & Miles — while Irvine Legal is invisible
  • The NAP inconsistency is actively suppressing whatever organic authority the site has built — unifying citations across all directories is a prerequisite for any ranking improvement
SSHK Lawyers — 22 Birdeye reviews at 4.3 stars, active blog, and free consultation CTAs position them to hold the Ogden and Davis County map pack positions that Irvine Legal should own. Their review volume alone is enough to displace any firm with zero reviews.
Owning the local map pack for construction law in Davis and Weber counties — with zero local competition for the outsourced general counsel keyword — is a first-mover advantage that will not exist forever.
