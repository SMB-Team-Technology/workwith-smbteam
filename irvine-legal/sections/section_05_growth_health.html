Pillar Health Scorecard

Lead Generation

  • 100% referral-dependent — no digital marketing system of any kind in place
  • Zero Google reviews and zero local 3-pack visibility for any construction or business law search in the market
  • Competitors with 8–22 reviews are actively capturing the construction attorney searches Irvine Legal should be winning
Without a digital lead system, the Himalaya trip stays a wish — the firm can only grow as fast as the next referral arrives.

Intake

  • The existing sales team closes subscription clients independently — a genuine strength for referral-sourced leads
  • No intake process exists for ad-generated leads, which behave differently and require faster, structured follow-up
  • No embedded contact form on the homepage — the first digital touch for a new prospect has friction
A strong referral intake process is not the same as a digital intake process — and building one now determines how many ad-generated leads the firm actually converts.

Self-Managing Team

  • Team of 8–9 handles legal assistant, paralegal, and consultation work, but Joshua remains essential for contract reviews and litigation — the firm stalls within 1–2 weeks of his absence
  • No litigation attorney in place — Joshua personally handles all 20–25 active cases, making his availability the firm's single biggest bottleneck
  • No documented operational structure or accountability layer that would allow leadership to run without constant owner input
A 6-week hiking trip requires a firm that can operate independently for 6 weeks — and that does not happen without a clear leadership structure and a second attorney in place.

Profit

  • Revenue is growing at ~$780K/year with a clear goal of $100K/month — the trajectory is right, but no profit plan has been named
  • Subscription model creates predictable recurring revenue, but margin visibility by practice area (subscription vs. litigation vs. registered agent services) has not been established
  • Long-term goal of $3M owner benefit requires a financial architecture that does not yet exist — growing revenue without a profit plan risks growing expenses at the same rate
The nonprofit work and personal missions Joshua wants to fund require profit discipline — not just revenue growth.
