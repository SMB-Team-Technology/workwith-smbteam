Intake Assessment

Danton Legal has no digital intake infrastructure — no website contact form, no phone number displayed online, no after-hours coverage, and all intake handled personally by M. Danton Richardson. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • No website means no contact form, no online consultation booking, and no digital path for a prospective client to reach the firm — without already having Richardson's personal phone number
  • All intake handled personally by the attorney — no delegation, no process, and no structured follow-up system when a prospect first makes contact
  • No after-hours or weekend coverage — IP and entertainment clients frequently have time-sensitive matters (cease and desist letters, contract disputes, urgent trademark filings) that cannot wait until Monday morning
  • No CRM or lead tracking system visible — prospects who express interest but do not sign immediately have no structured follow-up path and are likely lost
Blind Spot: IP clients often have genuinely urgent matters — a competitor using their trademark, a studio threatening litigation, a patent deadline approaching. A slow or inaccessible intake response in this practice area does not just lose a case; it loses the client permanently to whichever attorney picked up the phone first.
The Gap
  • Every potential client who searches online and cannot find a contact method converts to a competitor — at a $7,500+ average case value, each missed intake opportunity represents significant revenue that leaves the firm permanently
  • With no intake system, the attorney is the personal bottleneck — the practice cannot serve more clients than Richardson can personally respond to in a given day, which caps growth regardless of how much marketing is running
  • Any competitor with a 24/7 intake solution — even just a contact form and a clear callback promise — wins the client who calls after hours, which is a growing proportion of legal inquiries
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • No website contact form exists — dantonlegal.com is a blank placeholder with no form, no phone number, and no call-to-action of any kind
  • There is no way for a website visitor to initiate contact with the firm through the current site — every potential lead who visits dantonlegal.com has no option but to leave
  • Once a website is built, adding a consultation request form with immediate confirmation and a defined follow-up sequence is one of the highest-ROI intake improvements the firm can make

Team Assessment

M. Danton Richardson is a solo practitioner with no visible staff — every case, every client call, every intake interaction, and every administrative task falls on the attorney personally. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Sole practitioner with no associates, paralegals, or administrative support — the firm's capacity is capped at what one attorney can personally do in a day
  • No delegation structure means every new client or new case competes with every other task for the same finite block of Richardson's time
  • No operational accountability — without a team, there are no checks on whether client follow-up happens on time, deadlines are tracked, or new leads receive consistent communication
  • The firm cannot take on more business than Richardson personally has capacity for, which creates a hard ceiling on revenue growth that no marketing investment can break
Blind Spot: Running solo may feel like a cost savings — but the true cost is every high-value IP case that cannot be taken because capacity is already full, every client who goes underserved because the attorney is stretched, and every hour spent on administrative work that displaces billable time for a 39-year veteran whose expertise commands premium rates.
The Gap
  • Without a team, M. Danton Richardson cannot step away — vacation, illness, or simply taking a day off creates a direct gap in client service and follow-up that the firm has no way to cover
  • Growth from marketing investment creates a paradox without team support: more leads require more of Richardson's time for intake and client management, which displaces the IP work that generates the revenue
  • Every hour spent on administrative tasks, scheduling, or follow-up calls is an hour not spent on the high-value IP and entertainment work that justifies the firm's premium positioning
Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Profit Plan Assessment

As a newly launched solo brand with no marketing systems and no consistent lead flow, Danton Legal's revenue is referral-dependent and variable — income arrives when relationships produce it, not when the firm plans for it. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Revenue is entirely dependent on referrals and personal relationships — making income unpredictable from month to month with no reliable floor
  • No financial infrastructure visible — no tracking of client acquisition cost, case profitability by type, or monthly margins against fixed overhead
  • No marketing spend means no measurable return on investment to optimize — the firm cannot systematically grow revenue because there is no data to learn from
  • As a solo practice, fixed overhead (office rent, bar dues, professional liability insurance) continues in slow referral months whether revenue supports it or not
Blind Spot: IP and entertainment matters vary enormously in value and duration — a trademark registration may be a $3,000 flat fee while an IP litigation matter could run $50,000 or more. Without tracking which case types are most profitable relative to the time they consume, the firm may be allocating the most hours to the least profitable work without realizing it.
The Positive
  • IP, trademark, and entertainment law carry significantly higher average case values than most practice areas — with Richardson's credentials representing Fortune 500 clients, the ability to command premium rates is there when a consistent pipeline exists to support it
The Gap
  • Without a financial baseline, any marketing investment cannot be evaluated for ROI — the firm has no way to know whether new clients are covering their acquisition cost or contributing to profit
  • Income volatility from referral dependency makes sound business planning extremely difficult — team hires, office decisions, and technology investments all require a predictable revenue foundation
  • Profit cannot be planned without knowing what it costs to acquire a client, what each case type earns net of time invested, and what the firm's monthly break-even looks like
M. Danton Richardson has spent 39 years building the expertise to command premium fees — but without a profit plan, those fees cannot reliably build the independent, self-sustaining practice he is launching Danton Legal to become.
