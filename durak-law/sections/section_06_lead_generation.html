Lead Generation

Durak Law has built a credible digital foundation with geo-targeted landing pages, an active blog, and a 5.0-star Google Business Profile — but there is no observed paid lead generation system in place, meaning every client the firm does not reach organically is being captured by a competitor who is paying to be there. The firms competing for the same clients in Franklin are not waiting for organic traffic.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed scores could not be verified during initial research — manual verification at pagespeed.web.dev is required before the next conversation; scores below 50 on mobile are a conversion killer
  • The website is estimated at 5–6 years old (logo dated 2020) and has not been redesigned for current conversion best practices — trust signals, booking integration, and social proof placement have all evolved since the last major update
  • No video content detected on the site — attorneys who appear on video before a prospect calls convert at measurably higher rates, and this is a gap every competitor can exploit
  • No after-hours or instant-response mechanism visible on the homepage — a visitor who cannot reach anyone immediately will call the next result on their list
Blind Spot: Avvo lists Durak Law at 725 Cool Springs Blvd., Suite 600, while the website and current address show 381 Riverside Drive, Suite 440. This NAP (Name, Address, Phone) inconsistency across major directories — Avvo, Chamber of Commerce — is actively suppressing the firm's local search authority. Google uses directory consistency as a trust signal, and every mismatch chips away at the ranking equity the blog and landing pages are working to build.
The Positive
  • Active blog with 2026 posts on Franklin-specific family law topics demonstrates consistent content effort and local keyword relevance
  • Geo-targeted practice area pages (/franklin/, /franklin/family-law-attorney/, /nashville/) show intentional SEO investment and a foundation worth building on
The Gap
  • A website without speed optimization and conversion rate testing loses a measurable percentage of every organic visitor it receives — those visitors are going to competitors with faster, more conversion-focused sites
  • The NAP inconsistency across directories is undermining all local SEO investment already made, suppressing rankings that the content work should be supporting
  • Without video, booking integration, or an immediate response option, the site is capturing attention but losing conversions — especially for visitors who are comparing multiple firms at once
A website that converts three times better does not require three times more traffic — it just requires the right structure and speed, and that is exactly what SMB Team builds.
