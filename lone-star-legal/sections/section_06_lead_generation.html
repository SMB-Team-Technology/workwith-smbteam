Lead Generation

Lone Star Legal has built a strong offline reputation in Georgetown — but without paid advertising on any channel, its digital footprint does not match the firm's credentials, and that gap is being filled every day by well-funded regional competitors. The firm does not appear to be running Google Ads, Local Service Ads, or Meta campaigns for any of its Georgetown personal injury practice areas, leaving the highest-intent traffic in the market entirely uncaptured.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Contact form is buried near the bottom of the homepage — website visitors who arrive from Google, social media, or a referral and do not scroll fully to the bottom have no visible way to reach the firm online, and the friction reduces conversion rates significantly
  • PageSpeed scores were not retrievable during research (tool required JavaScript rendering that was unavailable) — scores must be verified at pagespeed.web.dev before the proposal call; a mobile score below 50 would indicate a significant rebuild priority given that the majority of PI prospects search on mobile after an accident
  • No live chat or chatbot observed — prospects who visit the site outside office hours have no immediate way to self-qualify or ask a question, making the firm invisible to the after-hours search behavior that is common for accident victims
  • The site lists over a dozen practice areas but the homepage headline focuses on "Texas Personal Injury Attorneys with A National Reach" — a broad message that may not resonate with Georgetown-area residents searching for local representation after a car accident
The Positive
  • Website was redesigned in 2024–2025 with a modern, professional layout — clean typography, gradient styling, and mobile-responsive framework mean the site does not need a rebuild, just optimization
  • Active blog with location-specific content (Georgetown car accident posts as recently as April 2026), attorney bios, credentials, and video in the hero section — all elements that support trust and SEO
The Gap
  • A buried contact form means every dollar spent on paid ads sends traffic to a page where prospects have to search to reach the firm — conversion rates drop with every additional step required to make contact
  • Without a clear mobile PageSpeed benchmark, it is not possible to know how much site speed is costing in both Google Quality Score (which affects ad cost) and direct conversions from mobile users who bounce before the page loads
  • Without after-hours contact options, the site is effectively closed from 5 PM to 9 AM — and that is when many Georgetown-area accidents happen and victims are searching for help
A website that converts is the foundation everything else is built on — before any paid traffic is sent to lonestarlegal.com, the contact path needs to be clear and immediate, or every dollar spent on ads is partially wasted.

Local SEO

The Problem

  • Google Business Profile review count could not be directly verified — but the firm's absence from ranking directories alongside competitors with 142–790+ reviews indicates a significant review gap that is suppressing local map rankings
  • NAP inconsistency confirmed: FindLaw lists the former address (205 East University Avenue, Suite 402) while the current address is 285 SE Inner Loop, Suite 106 — Google uses NAP consistency across directories as a local ranking signal, and this discrepancy is actively working against the firm's local SEO
Blind Spot: The firm operates under two names — Kelly Law Firm, P.C. and Lone Star Legal — across different directory listings. Inconsistent business name usage across directories compounds the NAP problem: Google may treat these as two separate entities rather than one firm, splitting the authority that should be consolidated behind one strong GBP listing.
The Positive
  • The firm is listed in SuperLawyers, FindLaw, LawInfo, Yelp, and Hello Georgetown directories — a presence that, once cleaned up and made consistent, provides a strong foundation for local SEO recovery
The Gap
  • Georgetown personal injury searches drive hundreds of high-intent local prospects every month — without local 3-pack visibility, Lone Star Legal is invisible to the majority of those prospects at the exact moment they are ready to hire an attorney
  • The review gap between Lone Star Legal and competitors like Angel Reyes (790+) and The Carlson Law Firm (408) is the primary driver of 3-pack suppression — Google uses review count and recency as a core ranking factor for local results
Angel Reyes & Associates — maintains a dedicated Georgetown landing page and 790+ Google reviews, making it the dominant result for Georgetown personal injury searches even though their primary office is in Austin. Lone Star Legal is the only Georgetown-based firm with a Board Certified PI attorney on staff — and it should be winning that 3-pack position.
Owning the Google local 3-pack for Georgetown personal injury is not just a traffic win — it is a market dominance signal that changes how the entire Georgetown legal community perceives Lone Star Legal's position.
