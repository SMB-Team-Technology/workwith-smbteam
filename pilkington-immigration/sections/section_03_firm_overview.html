Here Is Where Pilkington Immigration Stands Today

Pilkington Immigration is a multi-location Canadian immigration law firm generating approximately $3 million USD annually across five offices in Guelph, Toronto, Burlington, Kitchener, and Orlando, Florida — with practice areas spanning Canadian immigration, U.S. business visas, spousal sponsorship, citizenship, and U.S. entry waivers. The firm has grown to 30 employees and 8–9 attorneys, recently acquired a U.S. law firm, and built an impressive organic search presence across 55 geo-targeted microsites — but a Google algorithm update in summer 2024 broke the paid ad channel that once drove significant revenue, and the owner, Andrew Maloney, is still deeply embedded in day-to-day operations despite the firm's scale.

Stage 6 is where the firm runs without Andrew — where the revenue keeps growing, the team keeps moving, and he gets to decide each morning how he wants to spend his time; that is the goal, and it is closer than it looks.

Where You Are Today

Stage 4: Small Business Manager
  • $3M in annual revenue but growth has flatlined for nearly two years since a Google algorithm update disrupted the firm's paid advertising results
  • Google Ads are active at $10K/month but generating negative ROI — the firm is currently losing money on its primary paid channel
  • No dedicated intake or sales team — attorneys handle consultations and conversion, with uneven results across the team
  • Andrew remains the operational hub of a 30-person firm with 5 offices and 55 geo-targeted microsites — all marketing and key decisions route through him

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
Pilkington Immigration has the revenue, the team, and the infrastructure to become a fully owner-independent firm — what is missing is the right systems, the right marketing fix, and a structured plan to get Andrew out of the day-to-day and into the CEO seat he has earned.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Pilkington Immigration
Pilkington Immigration is operating at Stage 4 — a multi-location firm with real scale but owner-dependent operations that have not yet caught up to the firm's ambitions. The single most urgent limiting factor right now is a broken paid advertising channel: $10,000 per month in Google Ads spending that was generating 1.5:1 ROI a year ago is now returning below 0.5:1, and without a fixed marketing engine the firm cannot meaningfully grow past its current plateau. Andrew wants to exit operations and lead from the owner level — but that transition requires both a reliable growth machine and the operational infrastructure to support it.
