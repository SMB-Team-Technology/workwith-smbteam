Pillar Health Scorecard

Lead Generation

  • Organic search is the firm's strongest channel — nearly double the next lead source — but it is not growing fast enough to offset broken paid ad performance
  • Google Ads are active at $10K/month but now generating below 0.5:1 ROI; the channel that once produced 1.5:1 returns has been severely degraded since summer 2024
  • No Local Service Ads and no paid social advertising of any kind — two high-converting channels completely untapped while competitors advance on both
Until the paid channels are rebuilt and the firm has multiple reliable growth engines running, Andrew will remain tied to managing the marketing personally — and growth will stay flat.

Intake

  • No dedicated intake or sales team — attorneys personally handle consultations, with significant variation in closing ability across the team
  • Some attorneys are described as strong closers; others are not suited to sales conversations, meaning lead quality is irrelevant if the conversion process is inconsistent
  • No defined intake process, follow-up sequence, or after-hours coverage — leads generated by marketing are converting at whatever rate the individual attorney achieves that day
A broken intake process means every dollar spent on marketing is underperforming — and Andrew cannot step back from operations while conversion depends on who picks up the phone.

Self-Managing Team

  • Andrew is still the operational center of a 30-person, 5-office firm — all marketing decisions, digital strategy, and key business direction route through him personally
  • Brian Gill handles client relations and day-to-day marketing functions, but there is no management layer between Andrew and the broader team that allows the firm to run independently
  • Andrew has never worked with a business coach or outside advisor — the firm has grown entirely through his personal effort, which is exactly what is limiting it now
Andrew has built a firm that is far too large for one person to manage — but it is still being run that way, and that gap is what stands between him and the CEO role he is trying to step into.

Profit

  • Revenue has been flat at approximately $3M USD for nearly two years — the firm is not losing ground, but it is not gaining any, and the trajectory reversed a prior growth path of $2.2M → $2.4M → $3M
  • The firm is actively burning $10,000/month in Google Ads at sub-0.5:1 ROI — that is a confirmed loss of roughly $5,000+ per month in wasted ad spend on the current channel
  • No profitability tracking by practice area is in place, meaning it is unclear whether the growth in E-Visa revenue ($325K YTD) is compensating for the sharp decline in U.S. Entry Waiver revenue (formerly $750K/yr, now roughly $160K YTD)
A $3M revenue ceiling with active losses on the paid channel is not a sustainable place to build the owner-independent firm Andrew is working toward — profit clarity is the foundation everything else is built on.
