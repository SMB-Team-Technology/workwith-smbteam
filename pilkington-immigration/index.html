Here Is Where Pilkington Immigration Stands Today

Pilkington Immigration is a multi-location Canadian immigration law firm generating approximately $3 million USD annually across five offices in Guelph, Toronto, Burlington, Kitchener, and Orlando, Florida — with practice areas spanning Canadian immigration, U.S. business visas, spousal sponsorship, citizenship, and U.S. entry waivers. The firm has grown to 30 employees and 8–9 attorneys, recently acquired a U.S. law firm, and built an impressive organic search presence across 55 geo-targeted microsites — but a Google algorithm update in summer 2024 broke the paid ad channel that once drove significant revenue, and the owner, Andrew Maloney, is still deeply embedded in day-to-day operations despite the firm's scale.

Stage 6 is where the firm runs without Andrew — where the revenue keeps growing, the team keeps moving, and he gets to decide each morning how he wants to spend his time; that is the goal, and it is closer than it looks.

Where You Are Today

Stage 4: Small Business Manager
  • $3M in annual revenue but growth has flatlined for nearly two years since a Google algorithm update disrupted the firm's paid advertising results
  • Google Ads are active at $10K/month but generating negative ROI — the firm is currently losing money on its primary paid channel
  • No dedicated intake or sales team — attorneys handle consultations and conversion, with uneven results across the team
  • Andrew remains the operational hub of a 30-person firm with 5 offices and 55 geo-targeted microsites — all marketing and key decisions route through him

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
Pilkington Immigration has the revenue, the team, and the infrastructure to become a fully owner-independent firm — what is missing is the right systems, the right marketing fix, and a structured plan to get Andrew out of the day-to-day and into the CEO seat he has earned.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Lead Generation

Pilkington Immigration has one of the most sophisticated organic search infrastructures of any immigration firm in Canada — 55 geo-targeted microsites, an active blog dating to 2014, and a toll-free presence across multiple Canadian provinces and U.S. states. The problem is that the paid channel that was once responsible for a significant portion of growth is now generating negative returns, and paid social advertising remains completely untouched while competitors advance on all fronts.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • No attorney photos or individual attorney bios on the homepage — the firm's most valuable trust signals (8–9 attorneys, professional credentials) are invisible to a first-time visitor evaluating whether to call
  • PageSpeed scores were not retrievable during research (API rate-limited) — manual verification at pagespeed.web.dev is required before this call; sites running 55 WordPress microsites are often significantly impacted by load time issues across the network
  • Contact form is not inline on the homepage — visitors must click through to a separate consultation page to initiate contact, adding friction at the highest-intent moment
  • No video on the homepage — immigration decisions are high-stakes and personal; video is the fastest way to build the trust that converts a skeptical visitor into a consultation request
Blind Spot: Managing 55 WordPress microsites manually is a significant content maintenance liability — the transcript confirms this is a "cumbersome" process, and a single plugin update or security issue affecting the network can suppress rankings across dozens of local pages simultaneously. This infrastructure risk is compounding over time.
The Positive
  • The site is mobile responsive with a modern design (estimated 2022–2024) and professional credential badges displayed prominently — Law Society of Ontario, American Immigration Lawyers Association, and College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants
  • Active blog dating back to 2014 with a recent post (May 2026) on E-1 Treaty Trader Visas demonstrates consistent content marketing; the blog archive is an organic ranking asset most competitors do not have at this depth
The Gap
  • Visitors who arrive without knowing the firm need a reason to trust — no attorney photos, no bios, and no video means the site reads as a directory rather than as a professional relationship, reducing consultation request rates
  • The 55-microsite infrastructure is a competitive advantage only as long as each site is maintained, optimized, and load-speed compliant — an unverified PageSpeed score across a 55-site network is an unknown risk that needs to be addressed before ad spend is increased
  • A contact form buried behind a click is a conversion leak — each additional step between a visitor and a form submission costs the firm consultation requests it has already paid to earn through organic rankings
A website that converts visitors into consultations at a higher rate is one of the fastest ways to grow revenue without spending more on marketing — and it is the foundation everything else in this plan is built on.

Intake Assessment

Pilkington Immigration generates strong lead volume through organic search and referrals — but without a dedicated intake team or defined conversion process, those leads are being converted by whoever happens to answer, at whatever rate they personally achieve that day. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • No dedicated intake or sales team — attorneys personally handle all consultations, including the 20–25 paid Zoom consultations per month at $300 each, with conversion rates that vary significantly by individual attorney
  • Some attorneys are described as strong closers; others are explicitly not suited to sales conversations — the French-speaking attorney is noted as not well-suited to the sales side of consultations, meaning language-specific leads are likely converting at a lower rate than English-language cases
  • No documented intake process, no defined follow-up sequence, and no tracking of lead-to-case conversion rate — without knowing the current close rate, it is impossible to know how many leads are being lost between inquiry and signed engagement
  • No after-hours coverage mechanism — the firm's geographic network spans Ontario and Florida, meaning time-zone gaps and after-hours inquiries from U.S.-based clients (E-visa, EB-5, family sponsorship) are almost certainly going unaddressed until the next business day
Blind Spot: The 20–25 paid Zoom consultations per month at $300 each generate roughly $6,000–$7,500/month in consultation revenue — but they also create a hidden opportunity cost. Attorneys spending time on $300 consultations that do not convert into cases are not available for billable case work. A trained intake specialist handling pre-qualification would free attorneys to spend their time on the highest-value cases only.
The Positive
  • The paid consultation model ($300 Zoom) is a meaningful filter that pre-qualifies serious prospects before an attorney's time is committed — this is a structural advantage most firms do not have
  • The firm handles 140–160 invoices per month with approximately 75% new clients, suggesting strong market demand — the intake process does not need to create volume, it needs to convert the volume that already exists more reliably
The Gap
  • If the firm is generating 140–160 new client engagements per month at an undocumented close rate, even a 10-percentage-point improvement in consultation conversion would represent dozens of additional cases per month across practice areas averaging $2,000–$7,500 in case value
  • Attorney-handled intake at a $3M firm is a Stage 3 behavior in a Stage 4 body — it is the single most direct constraint on Andrew's ability to step back from daily operations, because the revenue generating process requires him and his attorneys to be personally present for every conversion
  • Without after-hours coverage, every overnight inquiry from a U.S. client (E-visa or family-based sponsorship) that reaches voicemail is a lead that will call the next firm on the search results page by morning
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Profit Plan Assessment

Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Revenue has flatlined at approximately $3M USD for nearly two years — the firm's prior growth trajectory ($2.2M → $2.4M → $3M) has stalled, and without a clear financial diagnostic it is difficult to identify whether the issue is acquisition volume, conversion rate, case mix, or overhead structure
  • $10,000 per month is being spent on Google Ads at below 0.5:1 ROI — this represents a confirmed monthly loss of roughly $5,000+ in wasted advertising spend that is not generating returns; this money is not reaching the firm's revenue, it is leaving the firm entirely
  • The U.S. Entry Waiver practice area, formerly generating $750,000 per year in revenue, is now on track for approximately $160,000 YTD — a decline of over $590,000 annualized. Without per-practice-area profitability tracking, it is unknown whether the growth in E-Visa ($325K YTD) and other areas is fully compensating for this structural revenue loss
  • No profitability tracking by practice area was mentioned — meaning the firm does not know its cost to acquire a client by case type, its margin contribution by practice area, or whether the current mix of cases is more or less profitable than it was when revenue was last growing
Blind Spot: The shift in practice area mix — from U.S. Entry Waivers (lower case value, $1,500–$2,000) toward E-Visas (higher case value, $7,000–$8,000) — could actually represent a margin improvement even if total volume has declined.   • The firm has capacity for approximately 30% more clients without new hires — meaning growth does not require a proportional increase in overhead, and improved marketing performance will flow directly to the bottom line
The Gap
  • Knowing revenue by practice area is the starting point for financial clarity — but without cost allocation by case type, the firm cannot identify its highest-margin work and deliberately shift marketing investment toward it
  • A $120,000/year loss on broken Google Ads (12 months × $10K/month at sub-0.5:1 ROI) represents a profit leak that, once fixed, immediately improves the firm's effective margin without changing any other cost structure
  • Without a CFO-level view of the P&L — tracking both the Canadian operation and the newly acquired Orlando firm as integrated entities — Andrew cannot make strategic decisions about where to grow next, how much to invest in each market, or what the combined operation is actually worth
The path to the CEO role Andrew is building toward is built on financial clarity — knowing exactly what the firm earns, where it earns it, and how to grow the number that matters most.
