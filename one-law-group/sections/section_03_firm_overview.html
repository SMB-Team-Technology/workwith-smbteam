Here Is Where One Law Group Stands Today

Emanuel Abrishami built One Law Group from zero into a $2.4M personal injury firm in Beverly Hills — entirely on the strength of his skill as a litigator and the referrals that skill generates, without a single paid advertisement. He is a Super Lawyers honoree for 11 consecutive years, a KABC Radio legal correspondent, and a Pepperdine mentor — and yet he spends the majority of his week managing payroll, accounting, HR, and admin instead of doing the work he is exceptional at. The firm's single most urgent challenge is building the marketing and operational infrastructure that matches the caliber of attorney already at its center.

Stage 6 is the stage where Emanuel gets to be what he is genuinely exceptional at — the rainmaker, the litigator, the face of One Law Group — while the firm operates, grows, and generates revenue without him in the weeds managing payroll and HR.

Where You Are Today

Stage 4: Small Business Manager
  • Every client comes from Emanuel's personal referral network — no paid marketing system exists to generate leads independently of his relationships
  • Emanuel personally manages payroll, accounting, HR, and admin — consuming 50 to 70 hours per week and leaving only 5 hours for rainmaking
  • Three office locations (Beverly Hills, Long Beach, Santa Clarita) but no digital presence to capture the PI prospects actively searching in those markets
  • The $10M revenue goal requires a system the firm does not yet have — One Law Group cannot reach $10M on referrals alone

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between Stage 4 and the $10M firm Emanuel is capable of building is not a talent gap — it is a systems gap, and closing it starts here.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
One Law Group
Emanuel is operating at Stage 4 — a Small Business Manager who has proven beyond any doubt that he can generate and deliver results, but who has yet to build the infrastructure that lets the firm grow without him doing everything himself. The single most urgent constraint is the complete absence of any scalable lead generation system: One Law Group's growth is 100% dependent on Emanuel's personal relationships, and every competitor in Beverly Hills is spending aggressively to intercept the cases that should come to him first. Until a predictable client acquisition system is in place and the firm's operations are managed by someone other than the attorney, the $10M goal remains out of reach — regardless of how talented that attorney is.
