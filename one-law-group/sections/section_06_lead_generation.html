  • PageSpeed scores: Not available — Google PageSpeed API returned rate limit errors during research; scores must be confirmed manually before the proposal call

Blind Spot: Two different phone numbers appear across directories — (310) 273-2733 on the website versus (310) 923-9420 on FindLaw, Yelp, and others — and the suite number varies between "Penthouse," "Suite 702," and no suite depending on the listing. This NAP inconsistency is an active signal to Google that the firm's information is unreliable, suppressing local rankings even when other signals are strong.
The Positive

The Gap
  • Without a compelling differentiator above the fold, visitors who find the site have no clear reason to choose One Law Group over Omega Law Group or The Dominguez Firm — they convert to whoever looks most authoritative or responds first
  • A dormant blog means zero organic content generation and no compounding SEO value — while competitors publish new PI content consistently, One Law Group's domain authority stagnates
  • Inconsistent contact information across directories tells Google the firm is unreliable, suppressing 3-pack rankings regardless of review quality — a technical problem with a direct revenue cost
A website that converts at scale and a content strategy that builds authority are the foundations Emanuel needs before paid advertising can work at its full potential — and before his reputation can attract cases from people who haven't yet met him.
