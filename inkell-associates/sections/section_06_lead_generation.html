Lead Generation

The Inkell Firm is actively running both Google PPC and Local Service Ads, generating real lead volume across its personal injury and medical malpractice practice areas. But high volume has not translated into high-quality signings — the firm's own team describes a steady stream of leads who are already represented elsewhere or simply comparison-shopping firms.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed scores were not obtainable this session (tooling quota exhausted) — mobile and desktop scores should be manually pulled from pagespeed.web.dev before final delivery so a real number can be shown to the firm
  • No dedicated service-area landing pages exist for surrounding cities (Newark, Dover, New Castle County) — a /service-areas/ page returns a 404
  • Contact form is not present above the fold on the homepage — visitors are routed to a separate contact page before they can reach out
  • Mobile responsiveness could not be independently confirmed this session and should be manually spot-checked
Blind Spot: Rhoades & Morrow — a named local competitor with a far thinner review profile (4.5★/15) — has built out dedicated city and practice-area landing pages that Inkell's site does not have. A weaker reputation with stronger site architecture can still out-rank a stronger reputation with thinner architecture.
The Positive
  • Full attorney bio pages exist for the team, including Joshua J. Inkell and Tiffany Shrenk, with a complete team directory — a real trust asset most competitors do not match
  • A clear above-the-fold CTA ("Schedule a Free Consultation") is present on the homepage, and the blog is actively maintained (most recent post July 1, 2026)
The Gap
  • Every visitor routed off the homepage to find a contact form is a visitor who may not complete that extra step — particularly on mobile
  • Without dedicated geo-targeted landing pages, the firm is leaving long-tail local search terms entirely to competitors who have built that structure
  • Without a confirmed PageSpeed benchmark, there is no way to know how much mobile load time may already be costing the firm in abandoned visits
A website that converts on its own — without Griffin or his team manually chasing down every visitor — is a foundational piece of the systems-driven firm Griffin says he wants.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • The firm did not appear in top organic results this session for "personal injury lawyer Wilmington DE," "car accident lawyer Wilmington Delaware," or "medical malpractice attorney Wilmington DE" — despite holding the strongest review profile of any firm checked
  • BBB and Birdeye still list the firm under its prior name, "Hudson, Castle & Inkell, LLC," stranding 219 reviews under an un-migrated listing separate from the current 324-review profile
  • No geo-targeted service-area pages exist for Newark, Dover, or New Castle County, unlike at least one named competitor
Blind Spot: The rebrand from Hudson, Castle & Inkell to The Inkell Firm is not just a naming change on the site — it has fragmented the firm's local citation and review signal across two un-reconciled listings at exactly the moment competitors are consolidating theirs.
The Positive
  • Google Business Profile shows 4.9★ across 324 reviews — stronger on both rating and volume than every named competitor checked (Silverman, McDonald & Friedman: 4.8★/313; Pratcher Krayer: 4.8★/89; Rhoades & Morrow: 4.5★/15)
The Gap
  • A 4.9★/324 review profile is a significant asset that is currently not converting into top organic visibility for the firm's core practice area terms
  • Every month the legacy listing stays un-migrated is a month of stranded review signal that could otherwise be reinforcing the current brand
Silverman, McDonald & Friedman — consistently surfaces in organic results for the firm's core terms despite a weaker review profile, likely reflecting stronger on-page and citation consistency.
A review profile this strong should be doing the selling on its own — right now it is an underused asset instead of a systemized one, which is exactly the kind of gap Griffin cannot close by working harder personally.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • The firm's own team confirms active PPC campaigns for personal injury / car accident (Q1 2026: $644 cost per lead, $1,500 cost per signed case) — ads were not observed in this session's search snapshot, which is consistent with normal bidding and dayparting variation rather than an inactive campaign
  • No ads were observed for medical malpractice search terms this session
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Emery Ledger Esq. — appeared with a toll-free tracking number in car accident search results, consistent with an active paid placement
  • Martindale-sponsored placements — aggregator listings surfaced ahead of organic results for "personal injury lawyer Wilmington DE," competing for the same click real estate
The Opportunity
  • Tighter targeting and quality-focused optimization of existing PPC campaigns — the firm's own stated goal — rather than expansion into new channels
  • Medical malpractice search terms currently show no distinct paid competitor presence — an open lane for a firm already handling this practice area
  • Filtering out already-represented and non-serious inquiries at the ad and landing-page level, directly addressing the "high volume, low quality" pattern the firm describes
  • Geographic conquesting around Newark, Dover, and New Castle County, where no dedicated landing pages currently exist to support paid traffic

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • The firm confirms active LSA spend (Q1 2026: $252 cost per lead, $1,500 cost per signed case) — this is a known, active channel even though the live unit could not be visually reconfirmed in this session
  • Google Screened status was not confirmed this session and should be manually verified from a Delaware-based IP
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Named competitor LSA presence — not confirmed via available research tools this session; recommend a live manual check before the proposal call if this data point is needed for the pitch
The Opportunity
  • LSA already carries the lowest cost per lead of the firm's two active channels ($252 vs. $644 for PPC) — tightening quality here has the fastest path to a better blended cost per signed case
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • A 4.9★/324 review profile positions the firm to compete strongly for LSA placement once Google Screened status and lead quality controls are confirmed and optimized

Facebook & Instagram Ads

Ad Presence

  • The firm's stated goal is to optimize its existing PPC and LSA channels for quality, not to expand into new channels at this time
Competitor Ad Landscape

The Opportunity

  • Educational content ads — capture leads earlier in the decision process
  • Meta is intentionally not part of the near-term recommendation given the firm's stated priority of optimizing its existing PPC and LSA spend first — worth revisiting once quality issues on those two channels are resolved
