Pillar Health Scorecard

Lead Generation

  • Both PPC and LSA are active with real, meaningful spend (Q1 2026: PPC $644 cost per lead, LSA $252 cost per lead) — this is not a firm starting from zero
  • Lead volume is high, but quality is inconsistent — a meaningful share of leads are already represented by another firm or are shopping around
  • No dedicated city or practice-area landing pages exist, while at least one named competitor (Rhoades & Morrow) has built exactly that structure
Fixing lead quality — not just lead volume — is what lets Griffin stop treating marketing spend as a gamble and start treating it as a predictable system he does not have to manage personally.

Intake

  • Current close rate is roughly 50% against the firm's own 80% target
  • Sales scripts exist but are not consistently used, and follow-up is inconsistent across the intake pipeline
  • An estimated one-third of qualified opportunities are mishandled somewhere between first contact and signed case
Every mishandled opportunity is revenue the firm already paid to generate — and every one of them currently lands back on Griffin's desk to catch.

Self-Managing Team

  • All operational knowledge and problem-solving runs through Yuri Griffin — there is no independent management layer that functions without him
  • The 24-person team has real structure (overseas intake specialists, pre-litigation paralegals, a demand/negotiation department, a litigation team) but no accountability system independent of Griffin
  • The litigation team is missing its own monthly complaint-filing target (6/month) and pre-lit paralegals are pushing back on 70-80 case caseloads — both signs of a team without a self-correcting management structure
Griffin's own stated goal — "an autonomous firm where systems drive performance" — cannot happen while every escalation, every metric miss, and every accountability gap still routes through him personally.

Profit

  • Revenue is growing fast — 2025 actual $2.2M, 2026 projected >$5M, well ahead of the firm's own $3M goal
  • Financial visibility currently lives in a local RStudio dashboard that Griffin maintains himself — the broader team does not have access to it
  • The firm is shifting strategy toward litigation cases (roughly double the revenue-per-case-day of pre-litigation work: $40.22 vs. $34.33) without a firm-wide profit plan tracking the transition
Revenue outrunning visibility means growth is currently making Griffin more essential to the business, not less — the opposite of the freedom he says he wants.
