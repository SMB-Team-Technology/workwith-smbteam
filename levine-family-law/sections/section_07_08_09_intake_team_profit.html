Intake Assessment

Levine Family Law has no answering service, no structured intake process, and no after-hours coverage — calls not answered by the shared building receptionist are forwarded to Scott's personal cell, and after-hours callers reach no one. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No dedicated intake person or answering service — calls are handled by a shared building receptionist with no training in legal intake, and overflow goes to Scott's personal cell
  • No structured intake script or process — each prospect call is handled differently, with no consistent qualification questions, no follow-up protocol, and no urgency to schedule a consultation
  • No after-hours coverage of any kind — calls after office hours reach no one; family law prospects in crisis do not wait until morning to find an attorney
  • No email or text follow-up sequence for leads who express interest but do not sign immediately — warm leads go cold with no systematic re-engagement
Blind Spot: Scott's current Google Ads spend — even at its modest level — is generating some inbound calls. Without tracking which calls came from ads versus referrals, and without a consistent intake process, there is no way to know how many of those paid leads converted versus how many were lost to slow response, missed calls, or voicemail.
The Positive
  • Scott updated his Google Business Profile hours to 8:30 AM–8:30 PM during the discovery call — a proactive step that shows willingness to optimize visibility and demonstrates he is already thinking about accessibility
  • Scott's wife is actively interested in the operations and intake plan — having a motivated operational partner is a strong foundation for implementing intake improvements quickly
The Gap
  • At $90–$180/click for Google Ads traffic, every unanswered call represents $200–$500 in paid advertising that produced a lead and then immediately lost it — intake failure is a direct multiplier on marketing waste
  • Family law prospects in the middle of a divorce or custody situation will call another attorney within the hour if their call goes unanswered; unlike a product purchase, there is no waiting around — the urgency is real and immediate
  • Without a booking link or structured follow-up, there is no way to convert leads who are not ready to hire on the first call — a cohort that represents a significant share of any referral or paid traffic stream
Fixing intake does not require a full hire — a professional answering service and a 10-minute booking link would immediately capture every lead the firm is already generating; that alone is enough to fund the paralegal Scott wants to hire next.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact form is present mid-page with the heading "We Can Help. Fill This Out To Get Started." — visible but not prominently positioned above the fold where first-time visitors are most likely to engage
  • No live chat or chat widget of any kind on the website — a significant gap given that many prospects prefer to initiate contact via text-style messaging before committing to a phone call
  • Phone number is in the CTA above the fold ("Call for a Confidential Consultation") — good placement for visitors who are ready to call; the gap is for visitors who are not yet ready, who currently have no low-friction conversion option

These findings are also referenced in the Website Audit section.

Team Assessment

Scott A. Levine is the entire firm — he does the legal work, prepares for trials, handles intake calls from his personal cell, oversees the marketing vendor, and manages every client relationship simultaneously. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Zero support staff — no paralegal, no legal assistant, no dedicated intake person, and no office manager; Scott is the only person running the firm
  • Trial-heavy months ($40,000–$45,000 revenue) are described as unsustainably intense — the ceiling on Scott's growth is the ceiling on Scott's personal capacity and endurance
  • No delegation path exists for any function — every client interaction, every court appearance, every marketing decision runs through Scott personally
  • Scott has identified adding 5 new matters per month as the trigger to make a first hire economically viable — but without a structured intake system generating consistent leads, that trigger point cannot be reached reliably
Blind Spot: The pattern of solo practitioners waiting until they feel "ready" to hire is one of the most common and costly traps in law firm growth — by the time it feels urgent, months of revenue that should have funded the hire have already been left on the table. Building the hiring framework before it feels necessary is what separates the firms that scale from the ones that plateau.
The Gap
  • Without a paralegal or support hire, Scott cannot increase case volume without proportionally increasing his own working hours — there is no leverage, only substitution
  • The second Daytona/Space Coast location Scott has envisioned requires someone managing Fort Lauderdale operations independently; that person does not exist yet
  • Every hour Scott spends on intake calls, marketing vendor management, and administrative tasks is an hour he is not doing the $400–$500/hour legal work that generates the firm's revenue
The first hire — a full-time paralegal — does not just add capacity; it creates the leverage Scott needs to grow volume without growing his personal hours, and sets the foundation for every subsequent step in the firm he has described.

Profit Plan Assessment

Scott knows his monthly revenue numbers in broad strokes — low months around $21,000–$22,000, typical months around $30,000, and high trial months at $40,000–$45,000 — but has no confirmed financial tracking system, no cost-per-acquisition data, and no defined profit target. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Revenue fluctuates dramatically by month — $21K–$45K+ swings — with no planned smoothing mechanism; profit targets are not set and financial decisions are reactive rather than strategic
  • Marketing spend of $1,500–$2,000/mo has no confirmed ROI measurement; Scott cannot confirm how many clients came from Google Ads, what they cost to acquire, or whether the spend is generating a positive return
  • No stated awareness of cost per lead, cost per signed case, or marketing ROI as a tracked metric — growth decisions cannot be data-driven without these numbers
  • Moving to a $5,000 initial retainer floor (from a previous $3,500 minimum) is a positive margin move, but without tracking which case types generate the best lifetime value, retainer pricing is being set without full information
Blind Spot: Scott mentioned that occasional high-value cases reach $85,000–$95,000 in total billing — but without tracking which client sources, practice area subtypes, or initial retainer levels predict these cases, there is no way to intentionally attract more of them. The data to answer this question is already in Scott's billing system; it just has not been analyzed.
The Positive
  • Scott has a clear sense of his revenue range and has already begun moving toward a higher retainer floor ($5,000+) — this shows financial self-awareness and a deliberate move toward margin improvement
The Gap
  • Without knowing cost per signed case from each lead source, Scott cannot tell whether his current marketing spend is generating a profit or a loss — and cannot make a rational decision about increasing it
  • Dramatic monthly revenue swings make it impossible to plan a hire, a rent increase, or any fixed cost investment with confidence; smoothing revenue requires either retainer structures, a pipeline system, or both
  • Without a profit target and monthly financial review, growth in revenue can be entirely consumed by growth in expenses — more revenue does not automatically mean more take-home
A simple monthly financial dashboard — revenue by source, cost per lead, cost per case, and profit margin — turns every decision Scott makes about hiring, marketing spend, and retainer pricing from a guess into a calculation.
