Lead Generation

Levine Family Law currently generates clients almost entirely through referrals and a severely underfunded Google Ads campaign managed by a non-professional vendor — leaving Scott invisible on every digital channel where today's family law clients actually search. In Fort Lauderdale's highly competitive paid search market, this is not a minor gap: competitors are spending $5,000–$15,000+/month to capture the exact clients Scott has the credentials and experience to serve.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • H1 and title tag mismatch confirmed — homepage title uses "Fort Lauderdale Divorce and Family Law Attorney | Broward County Divorce Lawyer" while the primary H1 reads "Divorce and Family Law Attorney" with a secondary H1 on "Addressing the Needs of Family Law Clients"; Scott acknowledged this keyword cannibalization issue in a prior conversation and it continues to suppress both Maps and organic visibility for primary Fort Lauderdale searches
  • PageSpeed scores: Manual verification required — automated retrieval via PageSpeed Insights API was rate-limited during research and exact scores could not be confirmed; SMB Team's technical audit will produce real numbers for this site
  • NAP inconsistency — the website "About" page lists 500 E Broward Blvd, Suite 900, Fort Lauderdale, FL 33394 while all directory listings (Avvo, Yelp, inferred GBP) show 6550 N Federal Hwy, Suite 220, Fort Lauderdale, FL 33308; this directly suppresses Google Business Profile ranking
  • No video content, no chat widget — design is professional but not conversion-optimized for 2026 standards; vendor-maintained and estimated several years without a full redesign
Blind Spot: The NAP inconsistency between the website's About page and every directory listing (Avvo, Yelp, GBP) is likely costing more in local search rankings than any single SEO improvement would gain — and it is a one-time fix. Every day it goes uncorrected, Google's local algorithm continues to penalize the firm's Maps visibility.
The Positive
  • Active blog with a recent post dated June 21, 2026 — consistent publishing is a positive organic SEO signal that puts the site ahead of many competitor sites with stale content
  • Strong attorney bio covering education, Broward County Public Defender background, 45+ jury trials, Florida Bar committee memberships, and Supreme Court Certified Mediator credential — builds immediate trust above the fold
The Gap
  • H1/title mismatch and keyword cannibalization mean Fort Lauderdale divorce and family law searches are being diluted across multiple URL signals — Google cannot determine which page to rank for which term
  • NAP inconsistency means any GBP optimization work will have a reduced ceiling until the address mismatch is resolved across the website and all directory listings
  • Without geo-targeted landing pages for specific Broward County cities beyond Hollywood and Margate, the site misses local search traffic from Coral Springs, Pompano Beach, Deerfield Beach, and other high-population surrounding markets
A website that ranks — and converts — is the foundation for everything Scott wants to build; fixing these structural issues is the first step toward a pipeline that runs without him chasing it.

A professional agency with a proper $3,500/month media budget — separate from management fees — would generate approximately 25–30 leads per month at Fort Lauderdale's average family law CPL; the current setup generates 5–10 clicks, not leads.
