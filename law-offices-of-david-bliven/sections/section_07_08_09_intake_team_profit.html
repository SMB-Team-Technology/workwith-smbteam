Intake Assessment

Law Offices of David Bliven is generating more than enough leads to grow — the marketing is working. The crisis is at intake: with no-show rates running 40–73% over four consecutive months and a 15% lead-to-client conversion rate, the firm is losing the majority of the revenue its marketing budget is paying to create. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
"Sep: 68 set/30 completed; Oct: 42/15; Nov: 43/14; Dec: 73% no-show"
The Problem
  • No-show rates of 40–73% over four consecutive months — for every 10 consultations scheduled, as few as 3 actually occur; the trend worsened from September through December with no apparent intervention
  • 15% lead-to-client conversion rate — 85 out of every 100 interested prospects do not become paying clients, meaning the firm closes fewer than 1 in 6 leads even when they do show up
  • No defined KPIs for intake performance — no benchmark targets for show rate, response time, or conversion rate; no alert system when intake metrics decline month over month
  • No confirmed after-hours intake coverage — prospects who contact the firm outside business hours have no immediate response option and may book a consultation with a competitor before the following morning
Blind Spot: The no-show problem is most likely a confirmation and reminder workflow gap — most no-shows happen when a prospect books a consultation but receives no automated reminder in the 24–48 hours before. Clio Grow supports automated follow-up sequences natively; if this is not currently configured, it is a same-week fix that could recover 20–30% of current no-shows within 30 days.
The Positive
  • Clio Grow is already in place as the intake and lead management platform — the infrastructure to track, follow up, and manage the intake pipeline exists; it needs to be configured and systematized
  • Intake volume is strong: 42–68 consultations scheduled per month means the marketing is generating genuine interest at meaningful scale; the opportunity is entirely in converting what is already arriving
The Gap
  • At a $25,000 average case value, a single 10-percentage-point improvement in show rate recovers approximately $100,000–$150,000 in annual revenue — the current 40–73% no-show rate represents $500,000+ in uncaptured annual revenue from consultations that were already scheduled
  • Without a KPI dashboard, the intake team has no visibility into whether performance is improving or declining — the September-to-December trend shows a steady worsening from 44% to 27% show rate with no evidence of an organized response
  • Without a defined follow-up sequence for no-shows and leads who do not sign immediately, prospects who miss a consultation are permanently lost — many are still in the market and would return with a single well-timed text or call
Fixing intake doesn't just improve revenue — it means David can stop relying on increased ad spend to compensate for conversion losses, restore the cash cushion, and start paying himself what this firm has already earned.

Team Assessment

David Bliven has assembled a team of five — two senior paralegals, two virtual assistants, and himself as the sole attorney — but the firm is operating without the accountability structure, KPIs, or delegation framework needed for that team to run independently. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No KPIs, no accountability metrics, and no performance benchmarks for any team member — paralegals, VAs, and the incoming associate will all operate without measurable targets or a feedback loop
  • Two senior paralegals billing only ~10 hours per week at an average of $300/hr while drawing combined salaries of over $200,000 annually — they are generating approximately $26,000/month in billings against payroll that should require 3–4x that output
  • David remains the de facto operations manager, intake supervisor, and sole attorney simultaneously — every process question, personnel decision, and workflow issue still runs through him personally
  • An associate attorney is being hired in August 2026 with no onboarding framework, KPI structure, or management layer in place — adding a team member without infrastructure multiplies complexity and pulls David further into operations, not further away
Blind Spot: The associate hire is both the most important growth decision and the highest operational risk David faces right now. Without an office manager, a KPI framework, and a defined onboarding process already in place before August, the new attorney will be managed by David personally — making him more of an operator, not less, at exactly the moment he is trying to scale toward $2M.
The Positive
  • The team has been stable since late January — two VAs are in place and the firm is no longer in a hiring emergency; the foundation exists to build systems on top of
  • David has identified the exact gap: he knows he needs an office manager, KPIs, and COO-level structure before the associate hire; the awareness is there — the plan and execution are what is missing
The Gap
  • Underutilized paralegals represent an immediate $50,000+ monthly revenue gap — at $300/hr and 10 hrs/week each, they are billing approximately 40% of a reasonable utilization target; closing that gap requires a work assignment workflow, not additional hiring
  • Without a management layer in place before August, adding the associate means David's bandwidth goes further into operations at exactly the moment he needs to be focused on scaling toward $2M–$2.5M by 2027
  • As long as the firm runs on David's individual attention rather than documented systems and KPIs, it cannot be valued, sold, or handed off — and the personal freedom he wants will remain contingent on him showing up in every role, every day
Building a self-managing team is not optional for David — it is the prerequisite for the $2M revenue goal, the August associate hire, and the ability to finally step back from daily firefighting and practice the law he built this firm to practice.

Profit Plan Assessment

Law Offices of David Bliven is generating meaningful revenue — but the combination of uncollected AR, an owner who is underpaying himself, paralegals billing below capacity, and no financial KPI visibility means the firm's financial health does not reflect what the billing should support. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
"AR >$100k outstanding; estimates ~$50k collectible; many debtors have assets/on-books jobs."
The Problem
  • $100,000+ in outstanding AR with approximately $50,000 identified as collectible — with no systematic collections process or retainer replenishment trigger in place, this balance grows with every new client added
  • Owner is underpaying himself despite growing revenue — cash cushion is drained, creating financial stress that limits investment decisions, hiring confidence, and long-term planning
  • Two senior paralegals billing only ~10 hours per week at $300/hr each are currently cost centers rather than revenue drivers — combined payroll of over $200,000/year is not being matched by proportionate billing output
  • No financial KPI dashboard — no visibility into cost per client acquired, profit per practice area, paralegal utilization rates, or monthly margin trends; the firm's financial picture is assembled reactively, not managed proactively
Blind Spot: The underutilized paralegals are not just a team management problem — they are the most direct and immediate profit lever available right now. At $300/hr, raising each paralegal from 10 to 25 billable hours per week adds approximately $36,000/month in billings against zero additional payroll. That is more than $430,000/year in recoverable revenue sitting inside the team David already has.
The Positive
  • $20,000–$30,000 average case value with a $10,000 initial retainer represents strong unit economics — the firm's fee structure is positioned for healthy margins once billing discipline and utilization are brought into alignment
  • Clio Manage is already in place for case management and billing — the infrastructure exists; AR management and utilization tracking need to be built on top of what is already running
The Gap
  • Uncollected AR of approximately $50,000 represents money already earned and not yet received — without a defined collections follow-up workflow tied to Clio Manage, this balance grows with the firm's caseload rather than shrinking
  • Without a retainer replenishment trigger, active cases run past their retainer balance without prompt — creating both AR risk and cash flow unpredictability that directly contributes to the owner underpaying himself
  • Without monthly financial KPIs (cost per signed client, margin by practice area, payroll-to-revenue ratio, paralegal utilization rate), growth toward $2M–$2.5M will be financially opaque — David will continue discovering what he made each quarter instead of deciding what he makes
Fixing the profit picture is what lets David restore his cash cushion, pay himself consistently what this firm earns, and make every growth decision from a position of financial confidence — not financial stress.
