Intake Assessment

Hayoon Kane Law Firm has an intake specialist on staff, but the firm's 20% booked-to-hired rate signals a deeper structural problem: Meta ads are delivering low-intent leads who are not ready to hire, and every consultation still requires Hayoon's personal time — meaning the bottleneck is not just conversion, it is capacity. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
"20% book-to-hire conversion — and I do every consultation myself."
The Problem
  • Hayoon personally handles every consultation — there is no Consultation Specialist in place, so every new lead that books a meeting requires her direct time, regardless of lead quality
  • 20% booked-to-hired rate is the firm's stated conversion — for every 5 booked consultations, 4 result in no hire, meaning 80% of Hayoon's consultation time generates no revenue
  • Lead quality is the root cause: Meta ads attract social media scrollers who are in awareness mode, not decision mode — resulting in low-intent consultations that do not convert at the rate that high-intent Google searchers would
  • Contact form is buried behind a modal link — visitors who arrive from ads and cannot immediately see a way to reach the firm leave before booking, meaning the intake problem starts at the website level before anyone even calls
Blind Spot: Adding more leads without fixing intake capacity — specifically, without hiring a Consultation Specialist — will not increase revenue. It will increase the number of consultations Hayoon has to personally conduct, increasing her hours without a proportional increase in signed cases. The firm can absorb more leads starting in July 2026, but only if the Consultation Specialist hire happens in parallel with the campaign launch.
The Positive
  • An intake specialist is already on staff — the foundational role exists, meaning the firm has a starting point for intake process improvement rather than building from zero
  • The firm is aware of its conversion gap and has already identified the two hires needed (Declaration Writer and Consultation Specialist) — this is a self-aware team that knows where the problems are
The Gap
  • At $11,000 per T-Visa case and a 20% booked-to-hired rate, the firm is converting roughly 1 in 11 callers into a paying client — improving that rate to 25–30% through higher-intent Google leads and a structured follow-up process represents 3–5 additional cases per month without increasing ad spend
  • Without after-hours engagement capability — no chat, no automated response — leads who reach out on evenings and weekends go unanswered until the next business day; in immigration cases where families are in urgent situations, that delay means a competitor gets the call first
  • Without a Consultation Specialist, the firm's growth is directly capped by Hayoon's personal schedule — adding 50 new Google leads per month does not help if there is no capacity to convert them
Getting intake right — higher-intent leads from Google, a Consultation Specialist to handle them, and a structured follow-up process — is the most direct path to the revenue growth Hayoon needs to purchase that home in San Francisco.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact form is accessible via a modal link — not immediately visible above the fold; visitors who arrive from paid ads and do not immediately see a contact path leave at higher rates, reducing the ROI on every marketing dollar spent
  • No live chat present on the site — for immigration clients facing urgent situations (visa deadlines, detention fears, trafficking recovery), the inability to reach someone immediately via chat or after-hours messaging is a conversion killer
  • The firm does offer multiple contact points (contact@hklaw.us, greencard@hklaw.us, phone) — but these are passive channels; proactive conversion tools (prominent forms, chat, callback widgets) are missing from the homepage experience

These findings are also referenced in the Website Audit section.

Team Assessment

Hayoon Kane Law Firm has built a 5-person team — an associate attorney, paralegal, intake specialist, and admin are all in place — but the owner is still personally required for every consultation, every new T-Visa case evaluation, and every high-stakes client decision. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
"Difficulty finding qualified, bilingual staff — English and Korean."
The Problem
  • Hayoon is the consultation bottleneck — she conducts every initial consultation personally, which means the firm's revenue ceiling is directly tied to how many hours she can work each week
  • The firm has identified the two key hires needed (Declaration Writer and Consultation Specialist) but has not been able to fill them due to difficulty finding bilingual English/Korean candidates in Las Vegas
  • An associate attorney (Yeonjung Waller) and paralegal (Heesun Lim) are in place, but the team structure does not include a dedicated consultation or client development role — meaning case generation and case delivery both depend on the owner
  • Without an accountability structure or documented process for consultations, adding staff without a system means inconsistent client experience and missed conversions — even if the hire is made
Blind Spot: The firm's bilingual staffing challenge is real — but it is also solvable with the right hiring approach. A Consultation Specialist does not need to be a licensed attorney; they need to speak Korean, understand the firm's case types, and follow a structured consultation script. SMB Team's coaching infrastructure includes intake and consultation systems that allow a trained non-attorney staff member to handle initial consultations and significantly improve conversion rates.
The Positive
  • A 5-person team is already in place — the firm is not starting from zero; it has the operational foundation to add a Consultation Specialist role without restructuring everything
  • The associate attorney and paralegal handling case work means Hayoon is not doing every casework task alone — there is capacity to delegate operational work, freeing Hayoon's time for consultations and firm leadership once the consultation role is filled
The Gap
  • Every consultation Hayoon conducts is an hour she cannot spend on firm strategy, case oversight, or the personal priorities — like planning the San Francisco home purchase — that revenue growth is supposed to enable
  • Without a Consultation Specialist, the firm cannot responsibly absorb the increased lead volume that Google Ads and LSA will generate — the leads will arrive and Hayoon will have no choice but to work more hours or let leads go cold
  • Without a documented consultation process, any future hire will require Hayoon to train them from scratch, extending the timeline to true delegation and keeping her in the bottleneck longer
Solving the team gap is what makes everything else work — a Consultation Specialist and a documented process are what let Hayoon step back from the intake chair and into the firm-builder role that reaches $1.5M.

Profit Plan Assessment

Hayoon Kane Law Firm is generating approximately $700K per year — real revenue with a clear goal to reach $1.2–$1.5M — but without a structured profit plan, growing revenue without growing take-home income is entirely possible: more cases, more staff, more overhead, same margin. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No profitability metrics were shared on the discovery call — profit per case type, cost per acquisition, or margin data were not referenced, suggesting financial clarity is not yet part of the firm's management rhythm
  • The firm is spending $9,000 per month on Meta ads generating leads that convert at 20% booked-to-hired — without a cost-per-acquired-case calculation, it is impossible to know whether that $9k is producing a positive return or consuming margin
  • Revenue goal of $1.2–$1.5M is clear, but without a documented profit plan, hitting $1.4M in revenue does not guarantee hitting the personal financial milestone — buying a home in San Francisco requires a specific amount of take-home income, not just gross revenue
  • Planned hires (Declaration Writer, Consultation Specialist) will increase payroll — without a profit model in place, those hires could grow revenue while compressing margins, leaving Hayoon in the same financial position at $1.2M that she is in at $700K
Blind Spot: T-Visa cases at $11,000 each are the firm's highest-value work — but the actual profit on those cases depends on how much time Hayoon and the team invest per case. Without tracking time and cost per case type, the firm may be prioritizing T-Visa cases for revenue reasons without knowing whether they are also the highest-margin cases. A profit plan answers this question and lets the firm choose cases strategically.
The Positive
  • Revenue at $700K with a clear $1.2–$1.5M growth target means the firm is in a growth orientation — the owner is not satisfied with the status quo and is actively looking to build toward a specific personal financial milestone
  • A defined average case value ($11,000 per T-Visa) gives the firm a clear unit economics foundation — most firms at this stage do not know their case value; knowing it makes ROI calculation and profit planning much more straightforward
The Gap
  • Without knowing the profit per case, the firm cannot make strategic decisions about which cases to prioritize, how many cases are needed to hit the $1.5M goal, or how much of that revenue will actually be available as personal income for Hayoon
  • The San Francisco home purchase is a specific financial target — without a profit plan that connects revenue goals to take-home income to personal savings, the firm can hit $1.4M in revenue and still find that the purchase is out of reach
  • As the firm adds staff to scale, without a profit model, every new hire is a guess about whether the revenue they generate will exceed their cost — a simple profit framework turns those guesses into data-driven decisions
A profit plan is what ensures that reaching $1.5M in revenue actually means reaching the personal milestone Hayoon is working toward — the San Francisco home is funded by take-home income, not gross revenue.
