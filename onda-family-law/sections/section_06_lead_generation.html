Lead Generation

Jeff Anderson Family Law, PLLC has built a nationally recognized reputation in Texas family law — but the digital systems that should be driving a predictable flow of high-value clients have eroded significantly over the past two and a half years, leaving the firm reliant on a declining organic channel and an unstructured referral network in a market where competitors are actively investing across every paid and organic channel.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Website vendor errors include typos, wrong phone numbers on location pages, and cross-contamination of Frisco and Dallas location content — creating confusion for prospects and negative signals for local SEO
  • PageSpeed scores: not obtained prior to this report — manual run required at pagespeed.web.dev; based on WordPress site profile with known plugin and malware issues, mobile scores are expected below 50
  • Malware incident discovered late — extent of lingering technical damage unknown; site security and page performance may still be compromised
  • NAP inconsistencies persist across directories: Frisco suite 210 (personal site) vs. 200 (ONDA/FindLaw); Frisco phone (972) 963-5464 (personal site) vs. (972) 963-5459 (ONDA/FindLaw); Dallas suite 808 vs. 800 (FindLaw); phone missing entirely on Avvo
Blind Spot: The ONDA umbrella firm website and Jeff Anderson Family Law, PLLC are competing for local search authority under related but different brand names and addresses — Google may be splitting local relevance signals between the two instead of consolidating authority under Jeff's personal practice.
The Positive
  • Website was redesigned approximately one year ago — the basic structure is current and includes dedicated location pages for both Frisco and Dallas
  • Strong above-the-fold CTA with phone numbers for both locations, an appointment scheduling button, and a honeypot-protected contact form
  • Attorney bio is extensive and trust-building: 30+ years, Board Certified, Top 100 TX, AAML Fellow, D Magazine Hall of Fame — credentials that most competitors cannot match
The Gap
  • Vendor-introduced errors and NAP mismatches are actively eroding the local authority Jeff's credentials should be building — every inconsistency is a negative signal to Google's local algorithm
  • Without confirmed PageSpeed scores and resolved site security issues, any future Google Ads campaigns will suffer from lower Quality Scores and higher CPCs — making marketing more expensive than it needs to be
  • The firm has no landing pages for San Antonio, no surrounding city pages (Plano, Allen, McKinney, Denton), and no dedicated practice area pages — leaving an enormous long-tail keyword footprint uncaptured
A website that matches Jeff Anderson's credentials — fast, accurate, and built to convert — is the foundation every other marketing investment depends on.

  • GBP Maps visibility declined after Google algorithm updates per the call — primary GBP category (Family Law vs. Divorce Attorney) is unresolved and may be limiting ranking for the highest-volume search terms
  • Five confirmed NAP inconsistencies across Avvo, FindLaw, and ONDA directories weaken the local authority signals Google uses to rank GBP listings in both Frisco and Dallas
Blind Spot: The ONDA umbrella firm has its own separate GBP (87 reviews, 3.9 stars) that may be competing directly with Jeff Anderson Family Law's GBP — Google may be splitting local authority between the two profiles rather than consolidating it under Jeff's personal practice brand.
The Positive
  • Both Frisco and Dallas GBPs are active with a 4.8-star rating — a strong credibility foundation once review volume is built up to match
  • Jeff is listed on Super Lawyers (annually since 2003), Best Lawyers in America (2009–2026), Avvo 10.0, LawInfo, and State Bar of Texas — a citation profile most competitors cannot replicate
The Gap
  • 22 reviews vs. 240–317 for top competitors means Jeff's Maps ranking will remain suppressed regardless of any other optimization — Google's local algorithm rewards review volume, and Jeff is at a structural disadvantage until that gap closes
  • San Antonio office has no GBP, no website landing page, and no directory listings — an entire active office location is completely invisible to Google and to any prospect searching in that market
Pfister Family Law — 240+ Google reviews at 4.8 stars, Board Certified, actively dominates the Frisco divorce 3-pack. Their review volume creates a self-reinforcing Maps advantage: more reviews → higher ranking → more calls → more reviews. Jeff is outside that loop entirely right now.
Building local Maps dominance is the fastest way to capture the high-intent leads Jeff has already earned the right to attract — prospects who are ready to hire, searching right now, and currently finding a competitor.

Blind Spot: Without CallRail or equivalent call tracking in place before ads launch, Jeff cannot know which campaigns are generating calls, which keywords are converting, or whether any ad spend is working — making optimization impossible from day one.

  • The prior negative experience was likely a result of broad targeting without tighter qualifier filters — the recommended path forward is smart PPC with CallRail and precise negative keyword management rather than an immediate LSA restart
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Pfister Family Law — positioned to capture LSA real estate in Frisco family law; 240+ reviews make their LSA profile highly competitive and credible to prospective clients
  • Ramage Law Group — 317+ reviews position them as a top LSA contender across the DFW family law corridor, including Jeff's core Frisco market
The Opportunity

  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • Once review volume grows from the current 22 toward 75–100+, revisiting LSA as a supplemental channel becomes a viable second-phase investment — particularly for high-intent divorce searches in Frisco
Blind Spot: The competitors who held LSA spots while Jeff's account was inactive have been building their review counts and credibility — Pfister and Ramage's review moats are partially a product of the time Jeff was out of those positions. Rebuilding competitive LSA standing requires review generation to come first.
