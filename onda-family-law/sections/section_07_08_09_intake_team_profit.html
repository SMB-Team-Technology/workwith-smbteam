Intake Assessment

Jeff Anderson Family Law, PLLC recently added Blazio live chat — a smart initiative Aleshia drove that has already shown early positive results in call volume. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No call tracking in place — without CallRail or an equivalent system, the firm cannot attribute which marketing channels are generating calls, which keywords are converting, or whether any future ad spend is actually working
  • NAP inconsistencies across directories (wrong phone numbers on some platforms, missing phone on Avvo) mean some prospects who find Jeff's name online may be calling wrong numbers or inadvertently reaching the ONDA umbrella firm instead
  • Small team limits structured follow-up for leads who inquire but do not immediately book — no defined process for re-engaging warm leads who need a second touchpoint before signing
  • No confirmed after-hours intake coverage beyond Blazio live chat, which cannot independently close a case — divorce and custody prospects often reach out during emotional moments in evenings and on weekends
Blind Spot: Every prospect who finds Jeff's name on Avvo (where his phone number is not listed) or on a directory showing the wrong phone number is a warm lead that never reaches the firm — those losses are invisible without a tracking system, but they are happening every day.
The Positive
  • Blazio live chat recently added by Aleshia — early signals of improved call volume confirm that website traffic is converting at a measurable rate when a contact option is present
  • Homepage contact form with honeypot spam protection is well-positioned above the fold — lowering friction for prospects who prefer to initiate contact via form
The Gap
  • Without CallRail, every dollar spent on Google Ads goes into a black box — no way to confirm which campaigns generate calls, which go nowhere, or what the true cost per lead actually is
  • Prospects who inquire at night or on weekends but cannot reach anyone may call a competitor within hours — in divorce and custody matters, the first attorney who responds has a significant closing advantage
  • Jeff's Board Certified reputation means prospects already have a level of trust before they call — a fast, professional intake process would convert a meaningfully higher percentage of inquiries without any additional lead generation investment
Better intake means Jeff's credentials do the work of closing more clients — every inquiry that gets a fast, professional response converts more of the leads the firm has already earned the right to attract.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact form is present above the fold on the homepage with honeypot spam protection — well-positioned to capture visitors who want to initiate contact without calling
  • Blazio live chat was recently installed by Aleshia — improved call volume and perceived ranking lift per the call; no quantified attribution metrics available yet without call tracking
  • Phone numbers for Frisco and Dallas are visible on the homepage, but NAP inconsistencies across external directories mean some prospects who find Jeff through search or a directory may be dialing incorrect or missing numbers

Jeff Anderson Family Law, PLLC has Aleshia Anderson actively managing digital and ops initiatives — a meaningful first step toward delegation that shows real initiative. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER

  • Aleshia is driving digital and marketing efforts without a formal ops or marketing infrastructure to support her — well-intentioned individual initiative without a systemic framework
  • No defined org structure or accountability system for managing the planned expansion (space + staff in ~6 months) — growth without structure typically creates cost without proportional capacity

Building the right team structure is how Jeff goes from being a lawyer who runs a practice to an owner who leads a firm — and that is when the scale he is planning for becomes real.

Profit Plan Assessment

Jeff Anderson Family Law, PLLC generates premium-value cases in one of Texas's most competitive family law markets — but without explicit financial visibility into revenue goals, cost per acquisition, or case economics, growth strategy is being built on instinct rather than data. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Annual revenue was not stated on the call — no confirmed financial baseline against which to measure growth, size marketing investment proportionally, or validate ROI projections
  • Revenue goal was not stated — "wants +20% lead growth now, more later" is aspirational but is not connected to a defined revenue or profit target
  • No marketing attribution or cost-per-case tracking — impossible to determine which channels produce the best return or which case types are most profitable to invest in acquiring
  • Prior vendor marketing spend may have produced little measurable return during the period when the website carried malware and content errors — unrecoverable cost that underscores the need for tracked investment going forward
Blind Spot: High-net-worth divorce cases command exceptional average values — but they also carry higher acquisition costs and longer sales cycles. Without tracking cost per acquisition by case type, Jeff may be allocating effort toward volume when optimizing for margin and case type fit would be substantially more profitable.
The Gap
  • Without a revenue baseline, marketing investment recommendations are proportional estimates — accurate planning and ROI measurement require knowing what the firm generates today
  • Expansion investments (new space, additional staff, Dallas and San Antonio growth) can erode profit margins quickly without financial planning and clear targets — growth that costs more than it earns is a common and avoidable trap at this stage
  • The planned +20% lead growth goal has no financial model behind it — without defining what each new case is worth and what it costs to acquire, hitting the lead target may not produce the financial outcome Jeff is actually working toward
A clear profit plan is how Jeff ensures that scaling to additional staff and new markets actually results in more money home — not just more work at the same rate.
