Lead Generation

Beacon Legacy Group has built a dominant Cookeville reputation through live seminars, but that single channel is declining — and across all three locations, the firm has almost no digital lead infrastructure generating inbound estate planning and probate clients outside the event calendar.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Homepage title tag reads "Estate Planning Attorney in Cookeville, TN" — the firm's two other markets (Crossville and Mt. Juliet) are entirely absent from the site's primary SEO signal
  • No location-specific content silos exist for Crossville or Mt. Juliet — no dedicated practice area pages by location, meaning Google cannot rank the firm for estate planning or elder law searches in those markets
  • The estate planning practice page H1 reads "Establish Your Legacy" — a branding phrase, not a keyword-matched heading for the searches the firm needs to rank for
  • The Initial Consultation page (/initial-consultation/) has a confirmed broken video embed and minimal page content — one of the most important conversion pages on the site is sending warm prospects to a dead end
  • PageSpeed scores: Manual verification required at pagespeed.web.dev before presenting — automated tools were unable to retrieve scores at time of report generation
  • Contact form is buried — accessible only via "Start Now" button or "Connect" link, not visible above the fold; no schema markup detected across key pages; duplicate navigation menus appear twice in page markup
Blind Spot: The legacy email address info@lannomlaw.com still appears on third-party directories from the firm's earlier branding as "Lannom Law." When Google indexes inconsistent business information across the web, it erodes trust in the GBP listing — which directly affects map pack rankings. This needs to be cleaned before extending GBP hours to 24/7.
The Positive
  • The website has a modern design with 2024-era updates, professional photography, and all three attorney and staff bios — a strong first impression for referred visitors and seminar attendees who look the firm up
  • The site is mobile-responsive with a clean navigation structure, and the Meta Pixel is installed — remarketing audiences can be activated immediately without a site rebuild
The Gap
  • Without location-specific SEO architecture, every Crossville and Mt. Juliet prospect searching Google for estate planning finds competitors — not Beacon Legacy Group — regardless of how good the attorneys are
  • The broken Initial Consultation page is a silent revenue leak: prospects who have already decided to explore the firm arrive at the site, find a broken experience, and leave without converting
  • Until the homepage title structure, practice area H1s, and content depth reflect three markets, the firm's SEO ceiling is limited to Cookeville alone
A rebuilt, multi-location website is what transforms the firm's online presence from a Cookeville-only brochure into a statewide lead engine that works around the clock — without Tyler or Victoria having to be in a seminar room.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Beacon Legacy Group does not appear to be running Google search ads for estate planning, elder law, or probate in Cookeville, Crossville, or Mt. Juliet — confirmed on the call as "minimal/no PPC running." The firm may have been outbid on some searches or simply not configured for Google paid search yet.
  • Tyler confirmed on the call that he observes "very low competition" in his markets for PPC, and that probate in particular appears to be an essentially uncontested paid search opportunity — meaning competitors are not actively claiming this space either.
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Crabtree & Patterson (Cookeville) — Probate-focused firm serving Putnam, Cumberland, and surrounding counties; not confirmed running Google Ads but represents the most direct competitor for probate/conservatorship PPC keywords if the firm enters that space
  • Vanderpool Law (Wilson County) — Has dedicated estate planning landing pages for Mt. Juliet; unclear if running PPC but the page infrastructure suggests active SEO investment that could extend to paid search
The Opportunity
  • Probate and conservatorship PPC — Tyler confirmed this is a near-empty paid search landscape in Middle Tennessee; a focused campaign at even $1,500–$2,000/month would capture the highest-intent probate queries in the state with minimal competition
  • Estate planning PPC for Crossville and Mt. Juliet — neither office has any paid digital coverage, and both markets have low-to-moderate PPC competition; targeted campaigns for each office location would immediately generate local leads that organic SEO will take months to build
  • Remarketing — the Meta Pixel is already installed on the site, meaning Display and YouTube remarketing can be activated immediately to stay in front of website visitors who do not convert on first visit (the "omnipresence" strategy Tyler mentioned on the call)
  • Income and age targeting for estate planning PPC — Google allows demographic targeting by estimated household income and age; narrowing PPC to 45+ audiences in higher-income ZIP codes filters for the $7k–$11k estate planning buyer and reduces wasted spend on clicks from non-buyers
Blind Spot: Because PPC competition is low right now in these markets, the cost-per-lead for probate and estate planning keywords is significantly lower than in larger metros. That pricing window will not stay open indefinitely — as other firms discover the same whitespace, CPL will rise. The first firm to establish conversion-optimized campaigns in this territory sets the anchor that future competitors have to outbid.
