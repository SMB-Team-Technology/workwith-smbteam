Here Is Where Beacon Legacy Group Stands Today

Tyler and Victoria Lannom co-founded Beacon Legacy Group as one of Tennessee's most specialized estate planning and elder law firms — now operating out of three locations in Cookeville, Crossville, and a newly launched Mt. Juliet office, with roughly $1.4M in 2024 gross revenue and a 9-person team behind them. The most important finding from this audit: the firm has an extraordinary Cookeville reputation — roughly 200 Google reviews at 5.0 stars — but almost no digital infrastructure to match it across the two newer locations, and a primary lead gen channel (seminars) that has been declining in performance for 12 months.

Stage 6 is where Beacon Legacy Group needs to be — where all three locations are generating consistent, predictable inbound leads without Tyler and Victoria having to be on a stage every weekend to keep the revenue coming in.

Where You Are Today

Stage 4: Small Business Manager
  • Seminar events are the primary — and declining — lead source, with ROAS dropping over the last 12 months
  • Three-office footprint has no corresponding SEO silos, and only Cookeville has an active (just-launched) LSA
  • Both attorney-owners are still personally running the seminar circuit while managing a growing 3-location operation
  • Revenue is flat year-over-year despite team growth, signaling that the current channel cannot scale to the $2–3M goal

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap is not about the quality of your work — roughly 200 Cookeville reviews at 5.0 stars prove your clients love what you do — the gap is about building the digital systems that make all three locations produce leads whether or not you are in the room.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Beacon Legacy Group
Beacon Legacy Group is operating at Stage 4 — a well-built small business with a strong Cookeville reputation and a capable team, but still dependent on a single energy-intensive lead channel that is actively declining. The most urgent growth constraint is the absence of a multi-location digital presence: two of three offices are essentially invisible on Google, with no LSAs, no location-specific SEO, and near-zero review counts outside Cookeville. Until the firm builds a predictable digital lead engine across all three markets, reaching the $2–3M revenue goal Tyler described means running more and bigger seminars — the exact grind this audit is designed to replace.
