Your Firm at a Glance

Beacon Legacy Group

Beacon Legacy Group is a specialized estate planning and elder law firm operating across three Tennessee markets — Cookeville, Crossville, and Mt. Juliet — with $1.4M in gross revenue, a nine-person team, and a dominant Cookeville reputation built through live seminars. The firm’s most urgent challenge is that its primary revenue engine (the seminar channel) has been declining for 12 consecutive months while its two newest offices have virtually no digital presence to replace it.

Stage 6 is where Beacon Legacy Group needs to be — where all three locations are generating consistent, predictable inbound leads without Tyler and Victoria having to be on a stage every weekend to make it happen.
Stage 4: Small Business Manager
Your Current Reality
  • The seminar/Meta ad channel generates approximately $30k/month in revenue but has declined for 12 consecutive months — the firm’s primary lead engine is losing pressure with no digital replacement yet in place
  • Crossville and Mt. Juliet offices have zero LSA coverage, near-zero Google reviews, and no location-specific SEO — both satellite markets are effectively invisible to anyone searching for estate planning help online
  • Both attorney-owners are still personally running the seminar circuit, managing three locations, and making every strategic call — the business cannot grow faster than their personal bandwidth, which is already stretched
  • Combined marketing and coaching spend exceeds $18,000/month across three separate vendors with no single view of which channel is generating closed cases or driving revenue
Here is where Beacon Legacy Group is headed — and what is standing in the way.

Beacon Legacy Group is operating at Stage 4: the firm has real organizational depth, a strong local brand in Cookeville, and a team structure that goes beyond just the owners — but both attorneys are still essential to every revenue-generating activity, and the digital infrastructure to support a $2–$3M firm simply does not exist yet. The most urgent gap is the absence of a multi-location digital presence: without LSA coverage, local SEO, and review velocity across all three markets, Crossville and Mt. Juliet are revenue gaps dressed up as offices.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Homepage title tag reads “Estate Planning Attorney in Cookeville, TN” — the firm’s two other markets (Crossville and Mt. Juliet) are entirely absent from the site’s primary SEO signal
  • No location-specific content silos exist for Crossville or Mt. Juliet — no dedicated practice area pages by location, meaning Google cannot rank the firm for estate planning or elder law searches in those markets
  • The estate planning practice page H1 reads “Establish Your Legacy” — a branding phrase, not a keyword-matched heading for the searches the firm needs to rank for
  • The Initial Consultation page (/initial-consultation/) has a confirmed broken video embed and minimal page content — one of the most important conversion pages on the site is sending warm prospects to a dead end
  • PageSpeed scores: Manual verification required at pagespeed.web.dev before presenting — automated tools were unable to retrieve scores at time of report generation
  • Contact form is buried — accessible only via “Start Now” button or “Connect” link, not visible above the fold; no schema markup detected across key pages; duplicate navigation menus appear twice in page markup
Blind Spot: The legacy email address info@lannomlaw.com still appears on third-party directories from the firm’s earlier branding as “Lannom Law.” When Google indexes inconsistent business information across the web, it erodes trust in the GBP listing — which directly affects map pack rankings. This needs to be cleaned before extending GBP hours to 24/7.
The Positive
  • The website has a modern design with 2024-era updates, professional photography, and all three attorney and staff bios — a strong first impression for referred visitors and seminar attendees who look the firm up
  • The site is mobile-responsive with a clean navigation structure, and the Meta Pixel is installed — remarketing audiences can be activated immediately without a site rebuild
The Gap
  • Without location-specific SEO architecture, every Crossville and Mt. Juliet prospect searching Google for estate planning finds competitors — not Beacon Legacy Group — regardless of how good the attorneys are
  • The broken Initial Consultation page is a silent revenue leak: prospects who have already decided to explore the firm arrive at the site, find a broken experience, and leave without converting
  • Until the homepage title structure, practice area H1s, and content depth reflect three markets, the firm’s SEO ceiling is limited to Cookeville alone
A rebuilt, multi-location website is what transforms the firm’s online presence from a Cookeville-only brochure into a statewide lead engine that works around the clock — without Tyler or Victoria having to be in a seminar room.

Intake Assessment

Beacon Legacy Group has dedicated intake staff in Hannah Hydrick and June Ashby — which puts it ahead of many firms at this revenue level — but the intake process is largely built around a seminar funnel that pre-qualifies leads before they hit the office, not an inbound digital system that converts cold prospects into consultations at $7k–$11k price points. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • The intake process is built around seminar attendees who arrive warm and already educated — it has not been pressure-tested against cold inbound digital leads who search Google, find the firm, and call or submit a form with no prior relationship
  • The contact form is buried behind a button click — a prospect arriving from a Google search or LSA who is ready to act must navigate to find any contact mechanism, and friction at this stage loses high-value leads before intake staff ever see them
  • No confirmed after-hours coverage for inbound calls or web form submissions — estate planning prospects often research attorneys in evenings and on weekends; a lead who cannot reach anyone and gets voicemail is very likely to call the next firm on the list
  • With a three-location expansion underway, intake workflows optimized for one-location volume may not scale cleanly to handle leads from all three markets without dedicated tracking of which location, channel, and source generated each consultation
Blind Spot: Because the seminar funnel pre-selects and pre-educates prospects, the firm may not fully see how it performs with genuinely cold inbound leads. A prospect who finds Beacon Legacy Group through a Google search or LSA call has no prior exposure to Tyler or Victoria — converting them requires a faster response, a stronger intake script, and a clearer path to scheduling than a seminar attendee who already attended an event and trusts the firm.
The Positive
  • Having dedicated Client Engagement Specialists (Hannah Hydrick and June Ashby) handling intake means attorney time is not consumed by initial prospect calls — a real structural advantage over solo practitioners competing in the same space
The Gap
  • Every high-intent estate planning prospect who lands on the website after hours, cannot reach a person, and moves on to another firm represents a $7k–$11k missed opportunity — at the firm’s case value, even one missed case per month is $84k–$132k in annualized lost revenue
  • Without lead source tracking by channel and location, it is impossible to know whether Crossville or Mt. Juliet digital leads are being followed up consistently or falling through the cracks of a process built for Cookeville seminar volume
  • A digital lead generation expansion without a corresponding intake upgrade is the fastest way to spend more on marketing while returning the same closing rate — the two must be built together
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

  • The Initial Consultation page (/initial-consultation/) — the most logical destination for a prospect ready to take action — has a broken video embed and minimal text content, making it one of the weakest conversion points on the site rather than one of the strongest
  • No live chat widget is present; for a high-ticket estate planning firm, a real-time scheduling widget or contact form embedded directly on high-traffic practice area pages would capture after-hours intent without requiring a live person to respond immediately

Profit Plan Assessment

At $1.4M in 2024 gross revenue, Beacon Legacy Group has real scale — but revenue has been flat year-over-year despite adding a third office and growing the team, and marketing spend now exceeds $18,000 per month across multiple vendors with no single view of ROI by channel. Status: AMBER
“2024 gross ≈$1.4M; 2025 run-rate similar month over month.”
The Problem
  • Revenue is flat at $1.4M even as the firm opened a second satellite office and grew its team — fixed costs have increased while top-line revenue has not, which means margins are compressing in real terms
  • Combined marketing spend across the seminar agency (~$10k/month), SMB AI/avatar program (~$5k+/month), and Frank’s coaching (~$3k+/month) exceeds $18,000/month; without per-channel revenue attribution, it is impossible to know which of these is actually driving closed cases
  • The seminar channel’s ROAS has declined for 12 consecutive months but remains the primary revenue driver — the firm is investing the most in the channel that is performing the worst, partly because there is no alternative channel built to replace it yet
  • No mention of per-location profitability tracking — as a three-location firm, Cookeville likely subsidizes the newer offices; without that clarity, it is impossible to know the real return on opening Crossville and Mt. Juliet
The Positive
  • Tyler is actively tracking revenue and marketing spend at a high level — he knows gross revenue ($1.4M), the seminar channel’s ROAS (2.2x average), and the rough monthly cost of each vendor relationship, which is a better financial awareness baseline than most firms at this stage
The Gap
  • If the seminar channel continues its 12-month ROAS decline without a replacement pipeline in place, the firm faces a revenue cliff — flat growth today can become contraction if the primary channel deteriorates further without digital substitution
  • Adding a third office, launching LSAs, and beginning Google PPC all require upfront investment before the revenue appears — without a profit plan that maps those investments against expected cash flow, the growth period between now and $2M could create more financial stress than clarity
  • Every dollar spent on a vendor or channel that cannot be attributed to closed cases is a dollar not working for the firm’s growth — the path to $2–$3M requires financial visibility by channel and by location, not just total gross revenue
A clear profit plan is what turns the $2–$3M goal from a number on a whiteboard into a funded, predictable path — so Tyler and Victoria know exactly how to get there and exactly what it should cost them to do it.
