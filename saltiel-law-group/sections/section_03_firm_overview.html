Here Is Where Saltiel Law Group Stands Today

Saltiel Law Group is a bilingual English-Spanish business law firm based in Coral Gables, Miami, serving entrepreneurs and business owners — primarily from the Latin American community — with corporate formations, M&A, contract work, and cross-border transactions. The firm is generating strong inbound interest with roughly 83 leads per month, yet converting only about 9 into paying clients — an 11% conversion rate that is the single biggest constraint on reaching Moisés's $6M revenue goal.

Stage 6 is the stage where the firm runs on systems — not on Moisés — freeing him to focus on the high-value cross-border work he loves while revenue grows past $6M without him being the ceiling.

Where You Are Today

Stage 4: Small Business Manager
  • Generating ~83 leads per month through organic search and referrals, but converting only ~11% — leaving the majority of qualified prospects unconverted
  • Moisés personally handles every consultation and closes every client, making him the single point of failure in the entire revenue pipeline
  • Camila manages intake alone with no automation, no after-hours coverage, and no structured follow-up sequence beyond Clio Grow
  • No paid advertising has ever been run — the firm has never tested Google Ads, LSA, or Meta Ads while direct competitors are actively investing in all three channels

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between where Saltiel Law Group is today and where it needs to be is not about generating more leads — it is about building the systems to convert the leads already coming in and the paid channels to accelerate that growth to $6M and beyond.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Saltiel Law Group
Saltiel Law Group is operating at Stage 4 — Small Business Manager — with a defined team and strong brand credibility in the Miami bilingual business law market, but Moisés remains the centerpiece of every client conversion. The most urgent gap is a combination of broken intake conversion (11% close rate) and zero paid lead generation infrastructure, while well-funded competitors like EPGD Business Law and Trembly Law Firm are dominating both paid search and content authority in the same market. Until intake is automated and paid campaigns are running, the firm cannot grow past its current ceiling regardless of how strong the brand becomes.
