Lead Generation

Saltiel Law Group is generating roughly 83 leads per month through organic search, referrals, and its content presence — a meaningful volume built without a single dollar of paid advertising. The firm's entire lead flow rests on one channel, and while it is working today, competitors are actively buying the keywords Saltiel ranks for organically, putting every future lead at risk.

Website Audit

"We have a Webflow website... it's bilingual, English and Spanish... We have a blog, it's quite robust... the design is clean and professional."
The Problem
  • PageSpeed scores could not be retrieved during this audit — a manual PageSpeed check at pagespeed.web.dev is strongly recommended before launch, as Webflow sites can have significant mobile performance gaps that suppress Google rankings
  • The site's blog — which drives meaningful organic traffic — has not been updated in approximately one year (last posts dated around April/May 2025), signaling to Google that the site is not actively maintained and allowing competitors to overtake existing keyword rankings
  • No dedicated intake-optimized landing pages exist for paid campaigns — launching Google Ads or Meta Ads directly to the homepage will produce significantly lower conversion rates than campaign-specific landing pages
  • The firm's website is built in Webflow, which limits the ability to add conversion tracking pixels, server-side GTM, and advanced CRO elements without developer involvement at every step
The Positive
  • The bilingual English-Spanish website is a meaningful differentiator in the Miami market — very few business law competitors offer the same depth of Spanish-language content, and it directly serves the 78% of clients who are Latin or Spanish-speaking
  • The site has attorney bio pages, practice area pages, and a content blog — all of which contribute to Google's E-E-A-T signals and support organic search visibility
The Gap
  • Without dedicated paid landing pages, launching any ad campaign today means paying for clicks that convert at homepage rates — typically 1–3% — instead of the 8–15% conversion rates a purpose-built intake page can achieve
  • A blog that has not been updated in a year is actively losing organic rankings to competitors who publish weekly — every month without new content is a month EPGD extends its content authority lead
  • The absence of performance data (PageSpeed, Core Web Vitals) means the firm is flying blind on a major Google ranking factor that directly affects how many leads the site generates
A rebuilt, conversion-optimized site with bilingual paid landing pages is what turns Saltiel's existing traffic into a predictable client pipeline — and gives Moisés the data to know exactly what each new client costs to acquire.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Saltiel Law Group's Google Business Profile review count could not be independently verified during this audit — the firm's exact review volume and recency need to be confirmed, as both are primary ranking factors for local 3-pack placement
  • Yelp profile shows only 2 reviews at 5.0 stars — an extremely thin review footprint on a platform that surfaces prominently in voice search and for prospects who do not use Google as their primary discovery channel
  • Facebook Business page shows 0 reviews — a missed trust signal for the large segment of the Latin American prospect base that uses Facebook as a primary discovery and referral tool
  • The local 3-pack for "business attorney Miami" and related terms is dominated by competitors with review counts of 200–342+ verified reviews, creating a significant perceived credibility gap at the exact moment a prospect is choosing who to call
Blind Spot: With 78% of your clients being Spanish-speaking, Google searches in Spanish — "abogado de negocios Miami," "abogado corporativo Coral Gables" — represent a separate local SEO battlefield where your bilingual GBP content gives you a structural advantage that competitors cannot easily replicate. This is an untapped opportunity to own Spanish-language local pack results that most Miami business law firms are not even competing for.
The Positive
  • The firm has an established Google Business Profile with a Coral Gables address and practice area categories that support local pack eligibility — the infrastructure is in place, it just needs to be actively built upon
The Gap
  • Firms with 200+ reviews consistently outrank firms with fewer reviews for competitive local searches — until Saltiel's review count is competitive, it will struggle to hold 3-pack position for high-intent terms like "business attorney Miami" or "corporate lawyer Coral Gables"
  • Without an active review-generation system, the firm depends on clients proactively leaving reviews — a passive approach that will never close the gap against competitors running systematic review campaigns
EPGD Business Law — holds 342+ verified Birdeye reviews at 4.7–4.8 stars and has built the deepest bilingual content library in the Miami business law market, making them the default authority brand in both English and Spanish local search results.
Owning the Spanish-language local pack for business law in Miami is an asymmetric opportunity — it plays directly to Saltiel's bilingual strength — and it is how the firm captures the exact clients it is already best positioned to serve, without fighting EPGD on English-language terms where they have a 342-review head start.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Saltiel Law Group does not appear to be running Google Search ads for business law terms in Miami — no paid ad placements were observed for core keywords during this audit
  • Hector confirmed on the call that the firm has never run paid advertising of any kind, relying entirely on organic search and referrals for all lead generation
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Trembly Law Firm — confirmed running Google Search ads with paid URL parameters targeting "corporate attorneys miami" at ad position 1 — they are intercepting high-intent prospects at the top of the page before Saltiel's organic listing appears
  • EPGD Business Law — dominates organic results and likely supplements with paid search; their volume of content and reviews makes them a full-funnel competitor across paid and organic simultaneously
The Opportunity
  • High-intent English terms: "business attorney Miami," "corporate lawyer Coral Gables," "M&A attorney Miami," "contract attorney Miami" — prospects actively searching for representation right now
  • High-intent Spanish terms: "abogado de negocios Miami," "abogado corporativo Miami," "abogado para empresa Miami" — a largely uncontested paid search lane that directly targets the firm's core demographic
  • Competitor name targeting: bidding on "Trembly Law Firm attorney" and "EPGD business law" captures prospects who have found a competitor but are still in the comparison phase — a non-obvious tactic that converts at high rates
  • Cross-border and Latin American client terms: "international business lawyer Miami," "cross-border transaction attorney Florida" — specialty terms with low competition and high case values aligned exactly with Saltiel's positioning
Blind Spot: Competitors may already be bidding on "Saltiel Law Group" and "Moisés Saltiel" as brand keywords — capturing prospects who have already heard of the firm and are looking it up directly. This is a low-cost campaign the firm can run today to protect its own brand traffic from being siphoned to Trembly or EPGD.
