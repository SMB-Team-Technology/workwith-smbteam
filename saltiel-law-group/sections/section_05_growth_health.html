Pillar Health Scorecard

Lead Generation

  • Organic search and referrals are generating roughly 83 leads per month — a meaningful volume, but built on a single channel with no paid infrastructure as a backstop
  • No Google Ads, Local Service Ads, or Meta campaigns have ever been run — every lead the firm receives today could disappear if Google's algorithm shifts
  • Direct competitors including Trembly Law Firm are actively running paid search campaigns for the exact keywords Saltiel relies on organically, putting the firm's lead volume at continuous risk
Layering paid campaigns on top of organic would compound the firm's existing lead volume — creating the predictable, scalable pipeline Moisés needs to reach $6M without depending on algorithm luck.

Intake

  • Only about 11% of inbound leads are converting to clients — meaning 89% of the leads the firm already paid to generate are walking out the door unconverted
  • Camila handles all intake alone with no automated follow-up, no after-hours coverage, and no structured nurture sequence — leads that come in at night or on weekends go cold
  • Moisés personally closes every single client matter, making him the bottleneck in the entire revenue pipeline and preventing the firm from scaling beyond what one person can handle
Fixing intake is the fastest path to more revenue without spending another dollar on marketing — and it is the prerequisite for Moisés stepping back from the day-to-day close.

Self-Managing Team

  • The firm has defined roles — Hector in marketing, Camila in intake, Yvonne in admin — but Moisés remains the central decision-maker and sole closer, which ties revenue directly to his personal hours
  • No intake team member has been trained to conduct consultations or close matters independently, which means no one can cover when Moisés is unavailable
  • The team structure exists in name but not in function — there is no accountability system, no documented process, and no manager who can keep operations moving without owner involvement
Building a team that can close clients and run operations without Moisés is what turns this firm from a job into a business — and makes the $6M goal achievable without him working more hours.

Profit

  • Revenue is estimated at $3–5M annually, which indicates the firm has genuine scale — but no mention was made of a formal profit plan, a known acquisition cost, or a target take-home percentage
  • With an 11% intake conversion rate, every lead that goes unconverted represents direct profit leakage — fixing intake before scaling spend is critical to protecting margin
  • The firm has never invested in paid advertising, so there is no data yet on cost per lead or cost per acquisition — making it difficult to forecast the return on any marketing investment
A clear profit plan will show Moisés exactly what it costs to acquire each client and how much he keeps from every dollar of revenue — the foundation for scaling to $6M with intention, not accident.
