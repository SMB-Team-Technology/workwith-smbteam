Intake Assessment

Saltiel Law Group is converting only about 11% of its inbound leads to signed clients — meaning roughly 74 out of every 83 prospects who reach out each month leave without becoming clients. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
"We have 83 leads and we're getting 9 clients... our close rate is probably around 11%... we know there's a problem there."
The Problem
  • Moisés personally conducts every initial consultation and closes every client matter — there is no trained intake person or associate attorney who can qualify, consult, or close in his place
  • Camila is the sole intake coordinator with no documented follow-up sequence, no automated reminders, and no after-hours coverage — leads that come in evenings or weekends wait until the next business day, and many go cold
  • Clio Grow is being used as a reporting dashboard rather than an active intake automation tool — the CRM has the capability to send automated follow-up sequences, schedule consultations, and track lead status, but these features are not being used
  • There is no defined multi-touch follow-up sequence — if a lead does not book a consultation on the first contact attempt, there is no structured process to re-engage them over the following days and weeks
Blind Spot: At an average case value of roughly $10,000, each percentage point of improvement in conversion rate is worth approximately $83,000 per year in additional revenue. Moving from 11% to 20% conversion on the same 83 leads per month would generate roughly 9 additional clients — close to doubling revenue growth without a single additional marketing dollar.
The Positive
  • Clio Grow is already in place — the infrastructure for automated intake exists and does not need to be built from scratch, only properly configured and activated
  • With 83 leads coming in monthly, there is significant volume to optimize against — even modest improvements in conversion rate will produce meaningful revenue gains immediately
The Gap
  • At the current 11% conversion rate, the firm is leaving approximately $740,000 per month in potential revenue on the table — leads that have already been generated and are already interested but are not being converted
  • Because Moisés closes every client personally, intake capacity is capped by his availability — the firm literally cannot close more clients than Moisés has hours to consult, which makes scaling to $6M structurally impossible without changing this
  • Without after-hours coverage or automated response, any lead who reaches out outside business hours — which is a significant portion of business owner prospects who research services after working hours — is at high risk of calling a competitor before Camila can follow up in the morning
Fixing intake is the single highest-ROI action available to Saltiel Law Group right now — it converts more of the investment that has already been made into clients, and it frees Moisés from being the only person who can close a deal.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • The Webflow website does not appear to have a prominent, above-the-fold contact form on the homepage — prospects who arrive with intent to inquire must actively navigate to find a way to reach the firm, which increases drop-off before any intake contact is made
  • No scheduling widget or calendar booking tool was observed on the site — prospects cannot self-schedule a consultation, which means every booking requires a manual back-and-forth through Camila, adding friction and delay to what should be an instant action
  • Without an intake calendar embedded on the site, late-night and weekend visitors — who represent a substantial portion of business owner web traffic — have no way to take action, and the opportunity is lost by morning

Team Assessment

Saltiel Law Group has a defined team with clear roles, but every revenue-generating function — consultation, closing, and client relationship — still runs through Moisés personally, making him the ceiling on how large the firm can grow. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
"Moisés is the one doing all the consultations... he's the one closing every client."
The Problem
  • Moisés is personally responsible for every client consultation and every close — no associate attorney, intake specialist, or trained team member can conduct a consultation or sign a client without him
  • There is no documented closing process, consultation script, or intake training guide that would allow another person to conduct consultations to a consistent standard — the entire close exists in Moisés's head
  • The team's roles (Hector in marketing, Camila in intake, Yvonne in admin) are operational and support-oriented, not revenue-generating — there is no one on the team whose primary function is to turn a lead into a signed client without owner involvement
  • There is no accountability structure, no weekly team meetings with KPI reviews, and no manager who can identify performance gaps and address them without Moisés being involved in the conversation
Blind Spot: The risk is not just that the firm can't scale — it is that it is one emergency away from a revenue disruption. If Moisés is unavailable for two weeks for any reason, intake stalls, consultations stop, and new client revenue drops to near zero. The entire firm's cash flow depends on one person's availability.
The Positive
  • The firm has already defined specialized roles and hired for them — this is further along the team-building journey than many firms at a similar stage, and it creates a foundation to build a self-managing structure on top of
  • Hector as a dedicated marketing team member is a genuine differentiator — most law firms at Stage 4 do not have in-house marketing support, which gives Saltiel a strategic advantage once the right campaigns are running
The Gap
  • As long as Moisés closes every client, the firm's maximum revenue is constrained by his available working hours — there is a mathematical ceiling to how many consultations one person can conduct per week, which means growth past a certain point is literally impossible without delegation
  • Without a documented closing and consultation process, training a replacement is not just difficult — it is impossible, because the process does not exist outside of Moisés's experience
  • The absence of accountability systems means the firm cannot identify and fix performance problems until they become crises — by the time Moisés notices an issue, revenue has already been lost
Building a team that can close clients without Moisés is not optional — it is the prerequisite for every other goal, including the $6M revenue target and the ability to step back from day-to-day operations.

Profit Plan Assessment

Saltiel Law Group is generating strong revenue in the $3–5M range, but no structured profit plan, no known client acquisition cost, and no defined take-home target were discussed — meaning growth decisions are being made without the financial data needed to make them well. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • The firm does not have a documented cost per lead or cost per client acquisition figure — without this number, it is impossible to evaluate whether any marketing investment is profitable or to predict the return on future spend
  • No profit plan or target take-home percentage was discussed, which means profit is discovered at year-end rather than engineered throughout the year — a pattern that leads to high revenue but lower-than-expected personal income
  • With an 11% conversion rate and no financial model tying lead volume to revenue, the firm cannot forecast how much additional revenue a given increase in marketing spend will produce — growth decisions are made on instinct rather than data
  • The absence of a Fractional CFO or financial accountability structure means there is no one who is systematically reviewing margins, identifying cost overruns, or planning for profitability as the firm scales toward $6M
Blind Spot: At $3–5M revenue with a one-person intake function and an owner closing every deal, the firm's overhead structure may not scale linearly to $6M — the team investments required to reach that goal could compress margins significantly if not modeled in advance. Knowing your numbers before you scale is what protects profitability during growth, not after.
The Positive
  • Revenue at $3–5M indicates strong case values and healthy billing — the firm is not a profitability turnaround story, it is a structure-and-visibility story, which is a much stronger starting point
The Gap
  • Without a known acquisition cost, the firm cannot confidently approve any marketing budget — every spending decision is a guess rather than a calculated investment with a projected return
  • Without a take-home target and a plan to hit it, Moisés is working at the pace required to generate revenue, not at the pace required to generate wealth — the distinction matters more at $5M than it did at $1M
  • Scaling to $6M without a financial model in place risks adding team, overhead, and marketing costs that erode margin — the goal is not just more revenue, it is more profit per dollar of revenue earned
A clear profit plan is what transforms the $6M goal from an aspiration into a number Moisés can build a specific, funded plan around — and it is what ensures that the journey to $6M makes him meaningfully wealthier, not just busier.
