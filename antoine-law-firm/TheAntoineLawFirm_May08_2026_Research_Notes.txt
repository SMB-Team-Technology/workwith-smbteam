Jacob Meissner has identified FCFO (Fractional CFO) + Coaching as the
recommended package for this client.

RATIONALE (drawn from transcript findings):

1. FRACTIONAL CFO (FCFO):
   Wilson's personal distribution was only $60k on ~$492k in gross revenue —
   a gap that Wilson himself attributes to "bookkeeping issues." He has no
   clear visibility into where his money is going, no financial reporting
   systems, and no ability to set a compensation plan that achieves his
   $250k personal distribution goal. The FCFO engagement addresses the
   exact pain point: installing financial infrastructure, cleaning up
   bookkeeping, clarifying profitability by practice area (Public Entity
   is likely the most profitable at $20k–$50k/matter; PI may be
   underperforming), and building a plan to get Wilson to his $250k
   distribution target.

2. COACHING:
   Wilson is the "Chief Everything Officer" — the firm's primary bottleneck.
   He spends 50–60% of his time on admin and only 5% on business development.
   His Smokeball implementation is stalled. His associate needs system
   training he can't provide while managing everything else. Coaching
   addresses the leadership and delegation gap: helping Wilson transition
   from operator to CEO, build accountability in his team, and create the
   management infrastructure that allows him to step back. His vision of
   "unofficially retiring" is a Coaching engagement outcome, not just a
   systems outcome.

COMBINED IMPACT:
FCFO gives Wilson financial clarity and a path to the $250k distribution.
Coaching gives him the leadership infrastructure to stop being the bottleneck.
Together, they address the two root causes of his firm's underperformance:
(1) he doesn't know where the money is going, and (2) he is doing everything
himself. These two services, implemented together, create the conditions for
his legacy vision — a self-running, multi-state firm that outlasts his
day-to-day involvement.
