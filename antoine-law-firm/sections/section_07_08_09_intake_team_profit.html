Intake Assessment

The Antoine Law Firm's intake process is held together by Wilson's personal presence — the paralegal handles PI pre-suit, but Wilson personally manages all other intake, and even the PI process is inconsistent enough to create quality and malpractice risk concerns. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Wilson personally handles all intake outside of PI pre-suit — employment discrimination, business litigation, and public entity inquiries all route through him, making him a single point of failure for every non-PI case type
  • The paralegal handling PI pre-suit intake was hired in Fall 2024 and requires significant training to meet quality standards — the intake process is not yet reliable or documented
  • A reactive workflow documented in the call is causing missed deadlines and creating malpractice risk — there is no systematic follow-up process for prospects who do not retain immediately
  • No intake tracking system is in place — cases slip through without a follow-up protocol, and there is no visibility into how many prospects are contacting the firm and what is happening to them
Blind Spot: A firm that relies on word-of-mouth for 100% of its cases cannot afford to lose any of the leads that do come in. If even 2 prospects per month call and are not followed up with effectively, and each case is worth $8,000, that is $192,000 per year in cases the firm is paying nothing to generate and still losing.
The Positive
  • The paralegal has Haitian Creole language capability through the legal assistant — a meaningful community connection that could dramatically improve PI intake quality for a significant segment of the Newark population
The Gap
  • Every prospect who calls after hours, gets routed to Wilson's voicemail, and does not hear back before morning is a case that calls the next firm on their list — and right now there is no after-hours coverage or follow-up protocol in place
  • Without a documented intake SOP, training the paralegal to a consistent standard is impossible — the quality problem will persist until there is a written process to train against
  • Wilson's personal involvement in all non-PI intake means that as PI volume grows (now 40–60 active cases), the non-PI intake quality will continue to degrade simply because his time is stretched thinner
The firm that Wilson wants to build — one that generates income while he is not in the office — cannot exist until intake is a system, not a person. This is the first domino.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • A dedicated contact page (/contact-2) exists but no above-fold contact form was observed on the homepage — visitors who are not determined enough to navigate to a separate page are leaving without engaging
  • No live chat or chatbot was observed on the site — after-hours visitors have no way to initiate a conversation, leaving the firm dark during evenings and weekends when many prospective clients are actively searching
  • These findings are directly connected to the intake gaps above — even when the website generates a lead, there is no mechanism to capture it efficiently outside of business hours

Team Assessment

Wilson built a team — a paralegal, legal assistant, and associate — but has not yet built a firm that can run without him. He is spending 50–60% of his time on low-value admin while only 5% on business development, which means the more the firm grows, the more trapped he becomes. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Wilson is the "Chief Everything Officer" by his own description — every critical decision, every non-routine task, and every quality check routes through him, which caps the firm's capacity at whatever Wilson personally can handle
  • The associate hired in March 2025 needs training on basic firm systems like date stamping — foundational operations have not been documented or delegated, leaving Wilson responsible for teaching things that should already have written SOPs
  • The Smokeball case management system implementation is stalled — data from the old system (Simple Law) is not fully imported, blocking key features including conflict searches, which creates liability exposure and prevents the team from working efficiently
  • Contractors (clerical assistant at 30+ hours per month, per-diem attorneys and paralegals) are expensive, have limited availability, and lack litigation experience — the firm is paying for temp solutions instead of building durable capacity
Blind Spot: Wilson's instinct to train by doing — to be present for everything — is the exact behavior that keeps him trapped. Every time he handles something himself instead of building a system for someone else to handle it, he is choosing short-term efficiency over long-term freedom. The "Chief Everything Officer" role is not a title; it is a trap, and it compounds with every hire.
The Positive
  • Wilson has assembled the right talent profile — a PI-experienced associate, a Haitian Creole-speaking legal assistant, and a paralegal managing pre-suit intake. The raw ingredients of a high-functioning team are present.
  • Wilson recognizes the problem and named it clearly — he knows he is the bottleneck, which is the first and most important step toward changing it
The Gap
  • Until Wilson has a delegation framework — a documented map of every task he is personally handling and a trained person accountable for each — he will remain the constraint no matter how many people he hires
  • Without completing the Smokeball migration, the team cannot operate efficiently or independently — a stalled technology implementation is costing Wilson time every day because the team cannot self-serve on basic operational tasks
  • The $250K personal distribution goal is not achievable while Wilson is doing $25/hour admin work — the opportunity cost of his time spent on admin versus business development and client strategy is the hidden revenue gap in this firm
The legacy firm Wilson envisions — multi-state, self-running, a pipeline for underrepresented talent — begins with Wilson learning to lead instead of do. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Profit Plan Assessment

The Antoine Law Firm generated approximately $492,000 in gross revenue in 2023 — but Wilson took home only $60,000 in personal distributions, and the $432,000 gap cannot be explained because the bookkeeping is not clean enough to trace. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Wilson's 2023 personal distribution was $60,000 on ~$492,000 in gross revenue — a distribution rate of approximately 12%, far below the 30–40% a healthy law firm should be paying its owner
  • The gap between revenue and distributions is unexplained due to "bookkeeping issues" — there is no financial reporting system in place to identify where overhead is going, which practice areas are profitable, or what the firm's real margin is
  • There is no profit plan — the $250,000 annual personal distribution goal has no roadmap, no timeline, and no identified levers to achieve it
  • With 2022 revenue of ~$900,000 inflated by one large PI case and 2023 normalizing to $492,000, revenue is highly volatile — which means personal income planning is impossible without a more stable revenue mix and financial tracking infrastructure
Blind Spot: Public Entity defense cases worth $20,000–$50,000 each — generating $204,000 in 2023 from a small number of recurring clients — may be the most profitable practice area by case, but without cost-per-case tracking, it is impossible to know whether PI or employment cases are subsidizing that segment or vice versa. Without this data, Wilson cannot make intelligent decisions about where to invest his time and marketing dollars.
The Positive
  • The Public Entity practice represents a stable, recurring revenue stream — $204K in 2023 from established client relationships at $20K–$50K per matter. This is predictable income that anchors the firm's cash flow.
The Gap
  • Wilson cannot reach his $250,000 personal distribution goal without first understanding where the current $432,000 gap is going — and that requires clean books, not just higher revenue
  • Without practice area profitability data, Wilson may be allocating his most valuable resource (his time) to the least profitable case types — spending hours on commercial litigation that generates $38K per year while Public Entity cases at $20K–$50K each sit underserved
  • Growing revenue without a profit plan will produce more gross income but the same take-home experience — the only way to reach $250K in personal distributions is to design it deliberately, not discover it accidentally
Financial clarity is not just an accounting exercise — it is the prerequisite for the freedom Wilson is describing. You cannot design a firm that funds your retirement if you do not know where the money is going today.
