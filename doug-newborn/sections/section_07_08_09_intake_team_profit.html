Intake Assessment

Doug Newborn Law Firm has a structured Probate/EP intake operation with dedicated personnel, but Personal Injury intake is less systematized, the website contact form is buried below the fold, and no close rate tracking exists by practice area to identify where prospects are being lost. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Contact form is buried at the bottom of the homepage — visitors who do not scroll to the bottom never find a submission option, and mobile users (the majority of legal searchers) rarely scroll that far
  • No confirmed after-hours coverage for web inquiries — a lead submitted at 9pm on a weeknight from the Probate PPC campaign could wait until the next business morning to receive a response, during which time the prospect calls a competitor
  • No close rate tracking by practice area — without this data there is no way to identify whether the PI intake funnel is converting 15% of inquiries or 5%, and no way to target the right fix
  • PI intake lacks the dedicated pipeline structure that Probate/EP has — no confirmed separate intake personnel for injury cases beyond Doug and wife personally handling prospects alongside active caseloads
Blind Spot: Every PPC dollar spent driving a visitor to the homepage who encounters no immediate contact option above the fold is a dollar that drives a prospect to a competitor who has a sticky header form and an immediate callback promise — the buried form is not a cosmetic issue, it is a conversion rate issue that compounds with every lead campaign run.
The Positive
  • Probate/EP intake has dedicated "two salespeople" personnel with a structured pod — this is a genuine operational strength in the firm's primary practice area and demonstrates that Doug understands the intake model
  • 850+ five-star reviews and Doug's veteran brand story do significant pre-qualification work before a prospect ever calls — prospects arrive with high trust, which increases conversion rate when intake process delivers
The Gap
  • Each untracked lead that receives a delayed or missed follow-up costs approximately $900 in CAC (the firm's confirmed probate acquisition cost) with zero revenue in return — at even 5 missed leads per month, that is $4,500 in marketing spend delivering nothing
  • Without close rate tracking by practice area, the firm has no visibility into whether the PI intake process is the primary bottleneck to PI revenue growth — or whether the problem is upstream at lead volume
  • After-hours inquiry coverage represents the highest-ROI intake fix available — most legal decisions happen in the evenings when a prospect is researching at home, and the first firm to respond typically wins the client
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact form is buried below the fold on the homepage — the "Contact Us Today!" section with the submission form appears only at the very bottom of the page, invisible to visitors who do not scroll
  • No live chat widget detected on the website — prospects who want to ask a quick question after hours leave without any conversion path available to them
  • Two different phone numbers displayed across the site create confusion: (520) 355-1161 in the header and (520) 585-5525 on the contact page and in external directories

Team Assessment

Doug Newborn Law Firm has 25 staff across two distinct practice pods with a clear structural foundation — but no KPIs, no utilization targets, no weekly accountability cadence, and no operational executor to drive projects between Doug's biweekly Atticus sessions. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
"Main constraint is time; operations need support more than top-line growth."
The Problem
  • Doug is the primary escalation point for firm-wide fires — he cannot consistently drive operational projects, and critical initiatives stall each time his attention moves to a more urgent matter
  • No KPIs, billable hour targets, or utilization tracking for any of the 7 attorneys — four litigators billing at $350/hour have no performance benchmarks, and the firm cannot identify underperformance or optimize case profitability without this data
  • No weekly operational cadence (huddles, 1:1s, dashboards) between strategic Atticus sessions — strategic clarity exists every two weeks but day-to-day execution gaps persist in the intervals
  • A $8–12K Clio Draft project has been paid and stalled for over a year — it requires an owner to create urgency and drive the rollout to completion, and that owner does not currently exist inside the firm
Blind Spot: The biweekly Atticus coaching cadence is building the right strategic vision, but without someone executing the KPIs, accountability systems, and implementation playbook week-over-week between those sessions, the firm will continue operating at Doug's bandwidth ceiling — no matter how clearly the strategy is defined on the call.
The Positive
  • 25-person team with specialized pods — non-litigation Probate/GC pod, PI litigation team, and estate planning attorney — shows a clear structural foundation for a self-managing firm that is already partially in place
  • Active Atticus coaching relationship demonstrates Doug's commitment to building a business, not just practicing law, and provides a strategic framework that a Fractional COO can execute against
The Gap
  • Without utilization targets for 4 litigation attorneys at $350/hr billing rate, the firm is likely leaving significant recoverable revenue on the table each month — and has no mechanism to identify or recover it
  • Every stalled project like Clio Draft represents compounding operational debt — every month without that automation means staff continues spending time on tasks that could be eliminated, and the $8–12K already invested generates zero return
  • Without a defined management layer executing the weekly accountability infrastructure, growth in revenue will not translate into growth in Doug's time freedom — it will just mean more fires and more context-switching at the same bandwidth ceiling
Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Profit Plan Assessment

Doug Newborn Law Firm has improved its financial discipline significantly over the past two quarters — stricter AR enforcement has reduced the PI-to-Probate subsidy from $400K/year to near zero — but there is still no formal profit plan, no weekly P&L dashboard, and no utilization tracking for attorneys. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
"Historically moved ~$400K from PI to cover Probate/Title 14 annually."
The Problem
  • Probate/Title 14 historically operated at a loss requiring ~$400K/year in PI subsidies — while recent AR enforcement has reduced this, the underlying visibility problem (no weekly margin tracking by practice area) that allowed this to persist for years has not been solved
  • No billable hour targets or utilization KPIs for any of the 7 attorneys — at $350/hr billing rates for litigators earning $100–120K, untracked underutilization is the most direct recoverable profit leak in the firm
  • No formal financial dashboard or weekly P&L review — profit is discovered at the end of a quarter, not managed throughout, meaning problems surface too late to course-correct
  • The $8–12K Clio Draft investment is generating zero ROI after a year of stalling — automated document generation would directly reduce labor cost per probate matter, but the investment is sunk without returns until someone drives implementation
Blind Spot: Without tracking cost-per-case by case type, the firm cannot determine whether high-complexity litigation cases at $350/hr are actually more profitable than flat-fee non-litigated probate matters at $7K — it is possible that certain litigation profiles are profit-negative after attorney time, paralegal support, and overhead allocation, and the firm would have no mechanism to identify this without utilization data.
The Positive
  • Probate CAC efficiency is exceptional — $900 to acquire a case generating $7,000 in fees with ~$2,500 in internal costs means a $4,500 contribution margin per non-litigated matter before overhead; this is a highly scalable, profitable case type when volume is optimized
  • Recent financial improvement through stricter AR enforcement and strategic case selection demonstrates Doug's capacity to execute financial discipline when the priority is clear — Q2 2026 PI subsidy to Probate is at zero
The Gap
  • Without a weekly dashboard tracking gross margin by practice pod, the next $400K annual subsidy situation can develop again before anyone notices — profit surprises are a symptom of financial invisibility, not bad luck
  • Utilization blind spot across litigation attorneys creates potential for significant monthly recoverable billing that is currently untracked and unmanaged — this is the highest-leverage profit improvement opportunity available without spending a dollar on marketing
  • The Clio Draft stall means ongoing staff labor cost for document generation that could be automated — every month without implementation means the firm pays more in labor than it should for non-litigation probate case processing
Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity — and until Doug has a clear weekly view of what each practice pod earns and keeps, growth will just mean more moving pieces to manage.
