Here Is Where Balboa Law Stands Today

Balboa Law is a personal injury firm being built from the ground up by Gerald Balboa — targeting a September 2026 launch in the Houston, TX market with a bilingual English/Spanish brand, a high-value catastrophic injury and wrongful death focus, and a referral-fee business model designed to keep his wife's attorney role as frictionless as possible. The firm enters one of the most competitive PI advertising markets in the country with zero digital infrastructure, zero reviews, and zero established lead flow — but with a powerful structural advantage: it is being built AI-first, bilingual from day one, with no legacy systems to undo and no reason to protect the status quo.

Stage 6 is where the firm runs without either of you — generating consistent income from a system, not from Gerald's personal effort every day — and that is exactly the version of Balboa Law worth building toward.

Where You Are Today

Stage 3: Solo Practitioner
  • Pre-launch firm with no website, no GBP, no reviews, and no active lead sources
  • Gerald handles all operations, marketing, and business development personally
  • Wife's attorney role is intentionally minimal — intake and delivery gaps must be solved by automation, not people
  • Revenue goal of $300K–$1M net profit year one with no established infrastructure to support it yet

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between where Balboa Law is today and where it needs to be is significant — but this firm has one enormous advantage that no established competitor can replicate: it is starting clean, and the window to build it right is open right now.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Balboa Law
Balboa Law is at Stage 3 — a pre-launch solo practice with the right vision but no operational infrastructure yet in place. The single most urgent gap is the complete absence of digital presence: no website, no Google Business Profile, no reviews, and no active advertising in a market where the leading competitors have accumulated 1,000–2,400 reviews and have been spending aggressively on every channel for years. Gerald's September launch target is achievable — but only if the infrastructure that takes most firms years to build is built before or concurrent with launch. Every month of delay in a market this competitive widens the gap that Balboa Law will have to close.
