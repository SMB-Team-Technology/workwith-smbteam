Intake Assessment

With two attorneys and no dedicated intake staff, every incoming inquiry at Dickey Law Group competes directly with casework, client meetings, and firm operations for James and Mireya's personal attention — and estate planning inquiries rarely wait. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • No dedicated intake staff — all incoming inquiries are fielded by the attorneys personally, creating a bottleneck at the highest-cost point in the firm
  • Contact form is near the footer rather than above the fold — an unknown percentage of visitors never see the primary call to action before leaving
  • No after-hours coverage mechanism observed — estate planning prospects making decisions in evenings or on weekends have no immediate way to connect with the firm
  • No CRM or intake tracking confirmed — without a system, there is no visibility into how many inquiries convert, how many are lost, or where in the process prospects drop off
Blind Spot: A significant share of estate planning decisions are triggered by life events — a health scare, a new baby, a parent's diagnosis — that happen outside business hours. Prospects in these moments search at night and call the first firm that responds. Without an after-hours intake mechanism, every one of those calls goes to the next firm on the list.
The Positive
  • The "Schedule a Free Consultation" button is prominently placed above the fold — the primary call to action is in the right location
  • 5.0 rating across 32 reviews confirms that clients who do connect with the firm have an excellent experience — the intake problem is at the top of the funnel, not in the quality of service
The Gap
  • Every consultation that is not booked outside business hours is a lead that went cold — with a two-attorney firm, there is no capacity to chase after-hours inquiries the next morning before a competitor already responded
  • Without intake tracking, the firm has no data on conversion rates, lead volume, or where the funnel breaks down — making it impossible to improve what cannot be measured
  • If inquiry volume grows with new marketing spend, the current intake structure will become a bottleneck that wastes every dollar invested in lead generation
Fixing intake is not just about revenue — it is about making sure that every family in The Woodlands who finds Dickey Law Group actually gets served, without requiring James or Mireya to be available every hour of every day.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact form is in the footer area with fields for name, email, phone, and inquiry type — functional but positioned where many visitors will not reach it before making a decision
  • No live chat observed on the website — after-hours and weekend visitors have no real-time way to connect with the firm
  • The "Schedule a Free Consultation" CTA above the fold is the strongest intake entry point — but it is a link rather than an embedded booking tool, requiring visitors to take an additional step to schedule

Team Assessment

Dickey Law Group runs on two attorneys with no support staff observed anywhere on the website or in public records — James and Mireya Dickey are simultaneously the lawyers, the intake team, the marketers, and the firm administrators. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • No support staff listed — the firm has no documented capacity to delegate non-legal work to anyone other than the two attorneys
  • Both attorneys are handling all functions simultaneously — there is no specialization of role, no coverage structure, and no room for either partner to step back
  • No documented operations, processes, or standard operating procedures that a future hire could follow without intensive personal training
  • The firm's capacity is capped at what two people can handle personally — adding clients without adding infrastructure means less time, more stress, and declining quality
Blind Spot: Many two-person firms believe they do not need staff "yet" — but the moment marketing brings in more volume, adding people without systems in place creates chaos rather than growth. Building the systems before hiring is what separates firms that scale cleanly from firms that grind through growth and burn out.
The Positive
  • The co-founder partnership structure gives James and Mireya built-in coverage for each other — a redundancy that solo practitioners never have
The Gap
  • Without staff, every additional client means less personal time — growth comes at a direct personal cost to James and Mireya
  • There is no path to the firm running during a vacation, a health event, or a planned leave without both attorneys being present or clients going unserved
  • Without documented systems, any future hire will require intensive training rather than stepping into a functioning operation — slowing the firm down before it can speed up
A firm that can serve its clients without both founders in every meeting is the foundation of the financial independence and personal freedom James and Mireya are building toward.

Profit Plan Assessment

With no transcript available, detailed financial information for Dickey Law Group could not be confirmed — but as a firm under five years old with two attorneys and no financial staff, formal profit planning is likely limited. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Revenue not confirmed — estimated at $300K–$600K annually based on team size and practice area, but actual margins, owner compensation, and expenses are unknown
  • No financial staff or dedicated profit planning process identified — profitability is likely assessed at tax time rather than managed on a rolling basis
  • No visibility into case value or margin by practice area — estate planning packages, probate matters, and LLC formation each have different time requirements and profit profiles
  • Owner compensation structure unknown — a common issue in young firms where the owners take "whatever is left" rather than paying themselves a planned salary first
Blind Spot: Estate planning firms often face unpredictable cash flow — clients pay once and may not return for years. Without a profit plan that accounts for seasonality and client lifecycle, the owners may experience feast-or-famine income even as revenue grows steadily.
The Positive
  • An active blog and strong review base represent compounding brand equity — the kind of long-term asset that eventually reduces the cost of client acquisition
The Gap
  • Growing revenue without a profit plan often means expenses grow proportionally — and the take-home does not keep pace with the work
  • Without visibility into cost per case by practice area, the firm may be allocating the most attorney time to the least profitable work
  • No confirmed financial planning means James and Mireya may not be compensating themselves at the level the firm's revenue could support
Financial independence does not come from earning more revenue — it comes from understanding where every dollar goes and making sure enough of it ends up where James and Mireya actually want it.
