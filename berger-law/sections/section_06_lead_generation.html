Lead Generation

Berger Law is spending approximately $5,000 per month on marketing — $2,200 on SEO and website, $2,800 on PPC and Local Service Ads — and generating 15–20 new cases per month, but the firm's own assessment is that the current agency's AI SEO optimization is underperforming competitors and the paid results are inconsistent. The issue is not whether the firm is investing; it is whether that investment is being managed with the precision and accountability that a competitive criminal defense market demands.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Website launched in 2021 — the site's age and the firm's own concern about underperforming AI SEO suggest the content strategy may not be keeping pace with how competitors are optimizing for current search algorithms
  • PageSpeed scores: Not retrieved during research — site blocks automated tools. Scores should be verified manually at pagespeed.web.dev before this report is presented, as slow mobile load times are one of the most common causes of lost leads in criminal defense
  • No blog or news section found — the site relies on practice area landing pages for content, which limits fresh signals to Google and the ability to capture prospects earlier in the decision process
  • No live chat widget found — for criminal defense, many prospects reach out at night or on weekends after an arrest; if they cannot reach anyone and there is no immediate response option, they call the next firm on the list
Blind Spot: In rural markets like the U.P., standard call tracking numbers with non-local area codes actively reduce answer rates. The transcript confirmed this is already happening — prospects associate non-906 area codes with out-of-town firms and are less likely to pick up or trust the call. This is a lead generation leak that has nothing to do with ad spend or SEO.
The Positive
  • The site has dozens of indexed practice area and geo-targeting pages — Marquette, Iron Mountain, Upper Peninsula-wide, and county-level pages — which is a genuine SEO asset that most small criminal defense firms do not have
  • Individual attorney bio pages exist for all four attorneys, which builds trust signals for both visitors and Google
The Gap
  • Without a blog or content calendar, the site is not capturing prospects who are researching before they are ready to call — those prospects are finding Grabel & Associates and other statewide firms that publish regularly
  • No after-hours capture mechanism (live chat or AI response widget) means every contact attempt outside business hours is either lost or routed to a slow follow-up, compounding the 20-minute response time problem
  • Without verified PageSpeed scores, the firm cannot know whether slow load times are costing it Google Ad Quality Score points — which directly affects cost-per-click and ad placement
A website that cannot be measured, cannot capture after-hours leads, and is not producing fresh content is not an asset that supports a self-running firm — it is a liability that requires constant manual attention to produce inconsistent results.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Google Business Profile shows approximately 88 Google reviews at 5.0 stars — but GBP post activity and the recency of the most recent review could not be confirmed, and stale GBP signals reduce local pack visibility over time
  • Current SEO agency's AI optimization strategy was described on the call as underperforming competitors despite a $2,200/month investment — which suggests either the wrong strategy, poor execution, or both
  • Call tracking numbers with non-local area codes are actively undermining local credibility in searches — prospects who call and see an unfamiliar area code on callbacks are less likely to respond, creating a gap between lead volume and actual contact
The Positive
  • With approximately 88 Google reviews at 5.0 stars, Berger Law has significantly more reviews than any identified local competitor — this is a real advantage that, if actively maintained and highlighted, drives both local pack rankings and conversion trust
The Gap
  • Competitors with decades in the market have legacy referral networks that feed GBP reviews organically — without a systematic review-generation process built into the firm's intake and case-close workflow, the review count advantage can erode
  • The local pack for criminal defense and DUI in Marquette is competitive, with Andriacchi Law, Numinen DeForge & Toutant, Casselman & Henderson, and Hyde & Swajanen all appearing in searches — if Berger Law's GBP activity drops, the 3-pack position is not guaranteed
Grabel & Associates — maintains a dedicated Marquette County criminal defense landing page with targeted content for U.P. searches, effectively competing for organic rankings in Berger Law's home market from a statewide budget that far exceeds what any local firm can match on SEO spend alone.
Local SEO is the channel most likely to produce self-sustaining, predictable lead flow — the kind of lead generation that keeps working at 3am while Alex is not in the office, which is exactly the system needed to get to 10 hours a week.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Berger Law is confirmed running Google PPC and LSA ads — approximately $2,800/month combined — covering criminal defense, DUI, and related practice area terms across the Upper Peninsula market
  • The call identified the current paid campaign as underperforming: leads are coming in but at an inefficient cost, and there is no confirmed cost-per-signed-case tracking to evaluate true ROI — meaning the firm is spending without knowing what it is getting back
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Grabel & Associates — a statewide criminal defense firm with a dedicated Marquette County landing page that appears in searches for U.P. criminal defense terms; their statewide ad budget means they can sustain higher CPCs without the same pressure a local firm feels on ROI
  • Andriacchi Law — has built out dedicated pages for DUI, drug crimes, and theft defense in Marquette with SEO optimization suggesting they may also be running targeted PPC for these terms; their broader personal injury practice gives them budget flexibility Berger Law does not currently have
The Opportunity
  • High-intent keyword targeting: "criminal defense attorney Marquette," "DUI lawyer Upper Peninsula," "drug charges attorney Michigan UP" — these terms convert at high rates because the searcher is in immediate need
  • Practice area specificity: dedicated ad groups for DUI, drug possession, sex crimes, gun charges, and federal criminal defense allow each campaign to match ad copy to the exact search, improving Quality Score and reducing cost-per-click
  • Competitor name targeting: searchers who look up Grabel, Numinen, or Andriacchi by name are already decided on criminal defense — reaching them with a Berger Law ad when they search a competitor is one of the highest-ROI moves available in this market
  • Surrounding county and city targeting: Berger Law serves all 15 U.P. counties, but ad spend concentrated only on Marquette leaves Iron Mountain, Houghton, Escanaba, and other U.P. cities largely unchallenged — each represents incremental case volume at lower CPCs than the primary market
Blind Spot: Without cost-per-signed-case tracking, Berger Law cannot tell the difference between a campaign that is generating profitable cases and one that is generating low-quality leads that waste intake time. The $2,800/month currently being spent may be generating significantly better or worse ROI than assumed — and without measurement, it cannot be optimized.
