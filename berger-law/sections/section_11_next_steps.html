Quick Wins for Berger Law

Replace Non-Local Tracking Numbers with 906 Area Code Numbers
Lead Gen Quick Win
Why It Matters
In the Upper Peninsula, a non-local area code on a callback is a trust signal killer — prospects associate out-of-area numbers with downstate or national firms and are measurably less likely to answer. Berger Law is paying to generate leads and then losing them at the callback stage because the number looks wrong.
The Opportunity
Switching all tracking and callback numbers to 906 area codes is a same-week fix that can immediately improve callback answer rates and contact-to-consultation conversion — at zero additional ad spend.
Implement a 5-Minute Lead Response Protocol
Intake Quick Win
Why It Matters
Berger Law's average lead response time exceeds 20 minutes. Research consistently shows that contact rates drop by more than 80% after 5 minutes — meaning a significant portion of the firm's leads are being lost not to competitors' marketing, but to the firm's own response lag.
The Opportunity
A defined response protocol — with clear ownership, an escalation sequence, and a 5-minute SLA — can be built in days. Combined with the AI call system already in place, this creates an immediate lift in the contact-to-consultation rate without changing the ad spend.
Build and Deploy a Consultation Conversion Script
Intake Quick Win
Why It Matters
The consultation-to-hire rate has dropped to 51%. The identified cause is attorney performance during consultations — meaning prospects are arriving ready to hire and leaving unsigned. At an estimated $4,000 average case value, recovering even 10 percentage points of close rate generates approximately $6,000–$8,000 in additional monthly revenue from the same lead volume.
The Opportunity
A proven consultation framework with scripted responses to common objections, a structured discovery sequence, and closing language specific to criminal defense — deployed across all four attorneys with consistent training — can reverse the close rate decline within 60 days.
Automate KPI Tracking and Performance Dashboard
Team Quick Win
Why It Matters
Manual KPI reporting is consuming Alex's time every month and making it impossible to delegate performance management. If Alex has to compile the numbers, he cannot hold anyone accountable without being the bottleneck — which is the opposite of a self-managing team.
The Opportunity
Automating KPI tracking into a dashboard visible to the team removes Alex from the reporting loop and makes performance bonuses self-calculating. This is the foundational step toward accountability without owner intervention — and it makes the 2029 goal structurally possible.
Establish Cost-Per-Signed-Case Tracking Across All Marketing Channels
Profit Quick Win
Why It Matters
Berger Law is spending $5,000 per month on marketing with no confirmed cost-per-signed-case metric. Without it, the firm cannot know which channels are profitable and which are generating leads that waste intake time — which means the $5,000 spend is effectively unmanaged.
The Opportunity
Installing cost-per-signed-case tracking across Google PPC, LSA, SEO, and referrals reveals which channels deserve more investment and which should be cut — and immediately improves the ROI of the current marketing budget without increasing it.
Launch Targeted Google Ads for Surrounding U.P. Counties
Lead Gen Long-Term Play
Why It Matters
Berger Law serves all 15 Upper Peninsula counties but paid ad coverage is concentrated in Marquette — leaving Iron Mountain, Houghton, Escanaba, Newberry, and other U.P. cities largely uncontested. Competitors bidding in Marquette face CPCs that increase with competition; surrounding counties offer higher case volume per ad dollar.
Who Is Doing It Better
Grabel & Associates — their statewide campaign structure allows them to efficiently cover all Michigan markets including the U.P. with geo-targeted landing pages, capturing cases in markets where local firms have not invested in paid visibility.
The Opportunity
Expanding paid coverage to Iron Mountain, Houghton, and Escanaba creates new case volume at lower CPCs than the Marquette market — and reinforces the firm's position as the U.P.'s criminal defense firm, not just Marquette's.
Build a Consistent Review Generation Process into Case Close
Lead Gen Long-Term Play
Why It Matters
Berger Law's approximately 88 five-star Google reviews are the firm's single strongest competitive advantage — significantly more than any identified local competitor. But that advantage erodes without a systematic process to keep new reviews coming in, because Google local pack rankings weight review recency alongside total count.
The Opportunity
A simple, automated review request workflow built into the case-close process — triggered when a case resolves favorably — compounds the review advantage over time and protects the firm's local SEO position against competitors who are actively building their review counts.
Define Roles and Build an Org Chart That Reflects the 2029 Target Structure
Team Long-Term Play
Why It Matters
A self-managing firm requires defined roles before it can hire into them. Without an organizational chart that reflects where the firm needs to be in 2029, every hire is reactive — filling gaps as they become painful rather than building toward a deliberate structure.
The Opportunity
Building the 2029 org chart now — including the management layer, intake coordinator role, and operational oversight position — creates a hiring roadmap that makes each new hire a step toward the goal rather than a response to a crisis.
