Here Is Where Berger Law Stands Today

Berger Law is Michigan's Upper Peninsula criminal defense firm — the only practice in the U.P. focused exclusively on criminal law — handling DUI, drug crimes, sex offenses, gun charges, and federal cases for clients across all 15 U.P. counties. Founded in 2021, the firm has built a genuine market position: 4 attorneys, 88 five-star Google reviews, and 15–20 new cases coming in every month. The single most important finding is that Berger Law is quietly losing an estimated $5,000–$10,000 per month in revenue it already earned — not because of a lack of leads, but because a consultation-to-hire rate that has slipped to 51% and a lead response time exceeding 20 minutes are sending signed cases to competitors.

Stage 6 is where a firm runs without its owner — and it is exactly the destination Alex described: working 10 hours a week, strategic not operational, with income that no longer depends on him personally being there.

Where You Are Today

Stage 4: Small Business Manager
  • Marketing is running but underperforming — $5k/month spent with inconsistent results and no clear cost-per-signed-case tracking
  • Lead response averages more than 20 minutes, and the intake-to-hire conversion has dropped to 51% — a gap the transcript estimates at $5k–$10k/month in lost revenue
  • KPI tracking is manual and time-consuming; holding the team accountable for performance metrics requires direct owner involvement
  • Alex is working 30–50 hours per week and cannot step back without the firm stalling

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between where Berger Law is today and the 10-hour-a-week life Alex described for 2029 is real — but it is a systems gap, not a talent gap, and that is exactly the kind of gap SMB Team closes.

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Lead Generation

Intake

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Website launched in 2021 — the site's age and the firm's own concern about underperforming AI SEO suggest the content strategy may not be keeping pace with how competitors are optimizing for current search algorithms
  • PageSpeed scores: Not retrieved during research — site blocks automated tools. Scores should be verified manually at pagespeed.web.dev before this report is presented, as slow mobile load times are one of the most common causes of lost leads in criminal defense
  • No blog or news section found — the site relies on practice area landing pages for content, which limits fresh signals to Google and the ability to capture prospects earlier in the decision process
  • No live chat widget found — for criminal defense, many prospects reach out at night or on weekends after an arrest; if they cannot reach anyone and there is no immediate response option, they call the next firm on the list
Blind Spot: In rural markets like the U.P., standard call tracking numbers with non-local area codes actively reduce answer rates. The transcript confirmed this is already happening — prospects associate non-906 area codes with out-of-town firms and are less likely to pick up or trust the call. This is a lead generation leak that has nothing to do with ad spend or SEO.
The Positive
  • The site has dozens of indexed practice area and geo-targeting pages — Marquette, Iron Mountain, Upper Peninsula-wide, and county-level pages — which is a genuine SEO asset that most small criminal defense firms do not have
  • Individual attorney bio pages exist for all four attorneys, which builds trust signals for both visitors and Google
The Gap
  • Without a blog or content calendar, the site is not capturing prospects who are researching before they are ready to call — those prospects are finding Grabel & Associates and other statewide firms that publish regularly
  • No after-hours capture mechanism (live chat or AI response widget) means every contact attempt outside business hours is either lost or routed to a slow follow-up, compounding the 20-minute response time problem
  • Without verified PageSpeed scores, the firm cannot know whether slow load times are costing it Google Ad Quality Score points — which directly affects cost-per-click and ad placement
A website that cannot be measured, cannot capture after-hours leads, and is not producing fresh content is not an asset that supports a self-running firm — it is a liability that requires constant manual attention to produce inconsistent results.

Intake Assessment

Berger Law is generating 15–20 new cases per month — a real achievement in a rural market — but a 51% consultation-to-hire rate and an average lead response time exceeding 20 minutes mean the firm is converting fewer than it should from the leads it is already paying to generate. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Average lead response time exceeds 20 minutes — the research benchmark is 5 minutes or less; beyond that, contact rates fall sharply and the probability of signing the case drops significantly
  • Consultation-to-hire rate has declined to 51%, down from historical highs — the call identified attorney performance during consultations as the primary cause, meaning the conversion problem is a coaching and scripting gap, not a lead quality problem
  • AI answers all incoming calls, but the human follow-up system that should close the loop is not functioning fast enough — the AI call handler creates a lag between first contact and a real conversation with a qualified person
  • A disconnect exists between the intake process and attorneys: attorneys are unaware of marketing efforts and what prospects have been told before a consultation, which undermines the ability to run a consistent, repeatable sales process
Blind Spot: A 51% consultation-to-hire rate feels like a marketing problem — not enough qualified leads. In reality, it is almost always a sales process problem. The prospects sitting in a consultation with a Berger Law attorney have already decided they need criminal defense help. They are not evaluating whether to hire a lawyer; they are evaluating whether to hire this lawyer. Without a defined consultation framework and coached response to common objections, that evaluation is entirely dependent on how each attorney happens to feel that day.
The Positive
  • The firm is already using an AI call answering system — the infrastructure for 24/7 response exists; what is needed is the human follow-up layer and the intake script that converts the AI-captured contact into a booked consultation
  • With 15–20 cases per month coming in, the firm has enough lead volume that even modest improvements to conversion will produce meaningful revenue gains without any increase in marketing spend
The Gap
  • At a 51% close rate on 15–20 monthly consultations, the firm is losing 7–10 cases per month it paid to generate — at an estimated $4,000 average case value, that is $28,000–$40,000 per month in revenue the firm earned and then gave away
  • Without a multi-channel follow-up cadence (text, email, call) for prospects who do not sign at the initial consultation, a significant portion of lost cases could be recovered with structured follow-up alone
  • The absence of intake-attorney communication means consultations are going in cold — prospects arrive with expectations set during the AI intake that attorneys do not know about, creating disconnects that reduce trust and conversion
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing. For Alex, that means more revenue from the same effort, which is the exact financial foundation that makes a 10-hour-a-week role possible.
