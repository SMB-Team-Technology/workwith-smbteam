Lead Generation

Shark Law has built its growth primarily on referrals and LSAs — a foundation that has worked well enough to reach $800K, but one that is fragile, unpredictable, and increasingly outpaced by a competitor who is systematically dominating every digital channel where DUI clients look for help. The opportunity in front of this firm is not incremental — it is transformational.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • PageSpeed scores could not be retrieved at research time (API quota exceeded) — manual verification required at pagespeed.web.dev; Scorpion-built sites commonly score 45–65 on mobile, which means most DUI prospects on phones are waiting for a slow site to load before they can even reach a contact form
  • The homepage headline focuses on attorney credentials and experience rather than the client's immediate need — a DUI prospect at midnight is not looking for "22 years of experience," they are looking for someone who can help them right now
  • No video content confirmed on the site — competitors who feature video of the attorney above the fold convert at a materially higher rate for high-trust practice areas like DUI defense
  • Geo-specific landing pages exist only for Hamilton County — no confirmed pages for Mason, West Chester, Blue Ash, Anderson Township, or Norwood, leaving significant suburban search traffic unaddressed
Blind Spot: Scorpion's AI answering is separate from the website's contact form — which means there is a gap between what a late-night website visitor sees and what actually happens when they reach out. If the form goes to Scorpion's queue and the AI notification takes 15–20 minutes, a prospect who fills out a form at midnight and gets no immediate response will call a competitor before morning.
The Positive
  • The site is active, mobile-responsive, and appears to be well-maintained by Scorpion — practice area pages are comprehensive, covering every OVI/DUI sub-type
  • The blog is genuinely active with recent posts in January 2026, December 2025, and September 2025 — this provides consistent fresh content signals to Google
The Gap
  • Without service-area pages for surrounding suburbs, Shark Law is invisible in searches like "OVI attorney Mason Ohio" or "DUI lawyer West Chester" — cities where Hamilton County DUI arrests happen regularly
  • Without video content, the site cannot differentiate Shane's personality and courtroom presence — which is his single biggest competitive advantage over high-volume firms like Suhre
  • A slow mobile site in a practice area where 70%+ of traffic comes from phones means bounce rates that cost cases before a single call is made
A website that reflects Shane's actual trial record and converts after-hours DUI traffic is the difference between getting the calls and letting competitors answer them — and right now, the site is not doing that job.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Shark Law holds approximately 104 Google reviews at 4.8 stars — while Suhre & Associates holds approximately 399 reviews at 4.9 stars; this review gap directly maps to the ~11% vs. ~96% Google Maps visibility gap confirmed on the call
  • Three different phone numbers appear across directories (main website: (513) 924-4378 | Yelp: (513) 910-2196 | Justia: (513) 532-5555) — NAP inconsistency confuses Google's local ranking algorithm and suppresses GBP authority
  • The firm operates under two names in the market ("Herzner Law" and "Shark Law") without a unified GBP strategy — citation fragmentation across Avvo, Justia, FindLaw, Yelp, and LinkedIn further dilutes local signal
  • No confirmed geo-targeted pages beyond Hamilton County — competitors with suburb-specific content rank for searches Shark Law does not even appear for
Blind Spot: The "DUI attorney" Google Maps market in Cincinnati is highly concentrated. When one firm holds 96% of map pack appearances for the primary search terms, they are effectively capturing first-call advantage for an enormous percentage of all DUI arrests in the metro. Every week without a review generation system and NAP cleanup is another week that gap widens — and review velocity compounds over time, making it harder to close.
The Positive
  • Shark Law holds a 4.8-star rating — the quality of reviews is strong; the problem is volume, not sentiment
The Gap
  • At current review velocity, Shark Law would need to generate 20–30 new Google reviews per month for 12–18 months to approach parity with Suhre — that does not happen without a systematic post-case review request process
  • Without NAP cleanup across all major directories, GBP authority remains suppressed regardless of how many new reviews are generated
Suhre & Associates DUI and Criminal Defense Lawyers — approximately 399 Google reviews at 4.9 stars vs. Shark Law's ~104 at 4.8; dominant 3-pack presence for "DUI attorney Cincinnati," "OVI attorney Cincinnati," and related terms; multi-location footprint across Ohio, Kentucky, and Indiana continuously compounds their local authority.
Dominance on Google Maps is not a vanity metric — it is the difference between being the attorney 89% of DUI prospects never find and being the one they call first. That gap is closable, but it requires a plan and it requires starting now.
