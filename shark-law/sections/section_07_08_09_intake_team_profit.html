Intake Assessment

DUI defense is one of the most time-sensitive practice areas in law — a client arrested at 11pm needs to speak with an attorney within the hour, and the firm that picks up first almost always gets the case. Shark Law's current intake system has a 15–20 minute AI notification delay and relies entirely on Shane to close every consultation. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
"The main issue is slow notifications — 15 to 20 minutes — which hurts speed-to-lead in DUI."
The Problem
  • Scorpion AI answering has a 15–20 minute notification lag after missed calls and after-hours inquiries — in DUI defense, where prospects are often in or near custody and making decisions under acute stress, 15 minutes is long enough to lose the case to a competitor who picks up immediately
  • Shane personally handles every intake consultation and every close — there is no delegation, no backup, and no system for when Shane is in court, in trial, or unavailable
  • Scorpion-to-Clio Grow auto-creation frequently produces incomplete lead records, often missing the prospect's name — without a complete record, structured follow-up is impossible and leads who do not sign on the first call are effectively abandoned
  • Booking workflow is not fully implemented through the AI answering system — prospects who interact with the AI answering service may not always be directed to schedule a consultation, creating a gap between first contact and actual booked appointment
Blind Spot: DUI clients almost never call back on their own if they do not hear from you within the first hour. They are in a high-stress, time-sensitive situation — they will call the next firm on the list, and that firm will get a paying client that should have been yours. The 15–20 minute lag is not an inconvenience — it is a direct, measurable revenue leak that compounds with every DUI arrest in Cincinnati every night.
The Positive
  • Clio Grow is in use as the intake CRM — the infrastructure for tracking and managing leads already exists; the gaps are in process and integration, not in platform selection
  • CallRail is integrated for call tracking and recording — this gives Shane data on call volume and outcomes that most firms at this stage do not have
The Gap
  • At a $5,000–$8,000 average case value, losing even one DUI case per month to a slow intake response costs the firm $60,000–$96,000 in annual revenue — and in a market where the competitor's primary differentiator is 24/7 human availability, this is not a hypothetical
  • Without a follow-up sequence for leads who do not sign immediately, the firm has no system for the 60–70% of prospects who need nurturing before they commit — these are cases Shark Law is generating the lead for and then handing to competitors
  • Shane closing every case himself caps the firm's capacity at his personal bandwidth — no matter how much marketing improves, revenue cannot scale past the number of consultations Shane can personally conduct
Fixing intake is the fastest path to more revenue from the marketing dollars already being spent — and it is the first step toward building a firm that can close cases when Shane is in trial, not just when Shane is free to answer the phone.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • A contact page exists at herznerlaw.com/contact/ — but the position of the contact form relative to the homepage fold was not confirmed; in DUI defense, a form that requires navigation to find is a form that does not get filled out at midnight
  • No live chat widget was confirmed on the site — Scorpion AI answering functions as an after-hours call handler, not a real-time on-site engagement tool; prospects who land on the site after hours and want to take action immediately have limited options
  • The gap between website form submission and intake notification (15–20 min AI lag) means that even when prospects do reach out via the site, the response delay creates a window where the prospect can reconsider or contact a competitor

Team Assessment

Shark Law has a 4-person team — Shane, one associate, one of-counsel, and an office manager — but Shane splits his time approximately 50% court and 50% administration, and he personally handles every intake call and every close. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
"Shane splits time roughly 50% admin and 50% court. He does all intakes and closing. He prefers trials but wants to choose cases and reduce routine court."
The Problem
  • Shane is the only person in the firm who conducts intake consultations and closes cases — this means every new revenue dollar must pass through his calendar, creating a hard ceiling on how much the firm can grow without growing Shane's working hours
  • Approximately 50% of Shane's time goes to administrative work that an office manager or paralegal could handle — time that currently cannot go to trials, business development, or strategic growth
  • No intake specialist or dedicated closer on staff — the firm cannot build a predictable intake process that functions independently of Shane's personal availability
  • The associate and of-counsel handle court coverage but are not structured to take on revenue-generating responsibilities — their role is execution, not business development or client acquisition
Blind Spot: Shane wants to take only the cases he loves — DUI jury trials and high-value felony OVI. But as long as he is the only closer, he has to take every intake call to keep the pipeline full. The firm cannot become selective until it builds the infrastructure to generate, qualify, and close cases without Shane's personal involvement at every stage.
The Positive
  • A real team structure already exists — the firm has moved past solo practice and has people who can cover court when Shane is unavailable
  • Shane has clarity on what he wants the firm to look like — he knows he wants to do trials and choose his cases, which means there is a clear direction to build toward
The Gap
  • Every hour Shane spends on intake is an hour he cannot spend in court — at his current case value ($5,000–$10,000 per case), misallocating trial-ready hours to intake calls is one of the most expensive uses of his time in the firm
  • Without an intake specialist to handle first-contact qualification, adding more leads from improved marketing will only create more intake pressure on Shane — not more revenue per hour of his time
  • Without a closer who can handle consultations when Shane is in trial, the firm cannot serve inbound DUI prospects the same day — and in DUI defense, same-day matters
The firm that Shane wants to run — one where he chooses his trials, delegates the rest, and leaves court knowing the business is growing without him — gets built by systematically removing himself from the intake and close process, starting now.

Profit Plan Assessment

Shark Law is growing — $700K last year, $800K pace this year, fees recently raised with zero pushback — but without clear visibility into cost per lead, cost per signed case, or which marketing channels drive the best margins, growth is happening without a plan. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
"About $700K last year. Pacing about $800K this year. Goal to surpass $1M and keep growing."
The Problem
  • No confirmed visibility into cost per lead, cost per acquired client, or contribution margin by case type — Scorpion dashboard and Clio Grow are only partially integrated and used occasionally for reporting
  • LSA and PPC spend through Scorpion is not fully tracked against signed-case outcomes — the firm does not currently know the return on its marketing investment at the case level
  • Fee structure was recently raised (from $4,000 flat to $5,000–$10,000 by offense) with no client pushback — but there is no systematic data on how fees affect conversion rates at each case type, which means pricing decisions are being made by instinct rather than evidence
  • The path to $1M is clear as a goal but not built as a plan — without knowing the number of cases needed per month, the leads required to generate them, and the cost to acquire each case, there is no way to build backwards from the goal to the daily actions that get there
Blind Spot: Shane recently raised fees and found that clients did not push back — which means the market is telling him that Shark Law is underpriced relative to its reputation. A competitor charges $7,500 for a first OVI; Shane charges $5,000. Every case signed at $5,000 that could have been signed at $7,000 is $2,000 in margin left on the table. Without data on conversion rates by fee level, this pricing gap will never be optimized.
The Positive
  • Revenue trajectory is strong and accelerating — $700K to $800K to $1M+ is a realistic path given the market opportunity
  • Fees were raised successfully without any pushback — this is strong evidence that the market values Shark Law's results above what it is currently charging, and that further increases are likely possible
The Gap
  • Without tracking cost per signed case across each lead channel, Shane cannot make confident decisions about where to increase marketing investment and where to reduce it — which means he may be funding underperforming channels while underfunding the ones that work
  • The jump from $800K to $1M requires either more leads, a higher close rate, a higher average case value, or some combination of all three — but none of those levers can be pulled confidently without data on where the current funnel is leaking
  • As the firm scales toward $1M and beyond, undocumented financials and informal reporting will create an increasingly large blind spot that prevents accurate forecasting and strategic investment decisions
Shane has already proven he can win cases — the next step is building the financial visibility to know exactly which activities produce the most profitable ones, so that every growth decision is backed by data and every dollar invested in marketing has a measurable return.
