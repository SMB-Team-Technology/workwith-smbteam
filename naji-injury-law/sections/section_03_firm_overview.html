Here Is Where Naji Injury Law Stands Today

Naji Injury Law is a second-year St. Louis personal injury firm built fast — a six-person team recruited from larger firms, an 88% conversion rate on qualified leads, and a clear ambition to grow past $3M and eventually $7M in revenue. But roughly three out of every four new leads are rented from a single third-party vendor that has produced zero conversions since July 3, and the firm does not appear anywhere in local search against the incumbents it is competing with every day.

Stage 6 is where Naji Injury Law owns its market instead of renting it one lead at a time — the systems in place, brand equity building with every case, and Jamil's hours no longer the ceiling on how big this firm can get.

Where You Are Today

STAGE 4: SMALL BUSINESS MANAGER
  • About 75% of new leads come from a single third-party vendor that has produced zero conversions since July 3 — a rented pipeline that builds someone else's brand, not Naji Injury Law's
  • A 6-month SEO campaign and a 30-day PPC/LSA test both underperformed and were shut down, and the firm does not appear in any of the local search terms tested against established St. Louis PI firms
  • A six-person team recruited from larger firms is already in place, converting 88% of qualified leads — but Jamil is still personally involved in decisions as fundamental as the next marketing hire
  • Stated goals of $3M and eventually $7M in revenue sit well ahead of the lead generation systems currently in place to reach them

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
Naji Injury Law already has the team and the intake performance most second-year firms would envy — what it is missing is a lead engine it owns, and closing that gap is exactly what stands between today and the $3M firm Jamil is building toward.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
