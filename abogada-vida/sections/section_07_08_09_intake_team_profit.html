Intake Assessment

Abogada Vida has built a three-person intake team with tracked performance metrics — a real differentiator at this revenue stage — but a 40-point gap in booking rates between the top and bottom performers, combined with a 20% overall lead-to-consultation conversion rate, means the firm is leaving a significant volume of already-generated leads unconverted every month. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
"Karen (Lead Intaker) Performance (May): 66% booking rate, 50% signing rate" and "Javier booked 13.5% of May's consultations and closed two cases (approx. $10.5k generated) in 5 days"
The Problem
  • The overall lead-to-consultation booking rate is 20% for the year — meaning 4 out of every 5 leads who contact the firm do not book a consultation, and the gap between Karen (66% booking) and Gabby (26.7% booking) shows how much of that loss is a coaching and process problem, not a lead quality problem
  • 41 of 177 May leads were classified as dead — without a systematic follow-up sequence and lead nurturing process, leads that are not ready to book immediately are being discarded rather than converted over time
  • No contact form is visible above the fold on the website, and no after-hours coverage was confirmed — prospective clients who search after 5pm and cannot reach anyone are calling a competitor before morning
  • New intaker Javier, while sales-focused and promising, booked only 13.5% of consultations in his first month — without a structured onboarding and performance coaching process, new hires ramp slowly and conversion rates suffer during the transition period
Blind Spot: At a 40.7% case signing rate on consultations, every additional consultation that gets booked generates roughly $2,100 in expected revenue (using $5,250 average case value × 40.7% close rate). Closing the gap between Gabby's 26.7% booking rate and Karen's 66% rate — on the same volume of leads — represents roughly 70 additional consultations per month and $147,000 in potential monthly revenue from leads already being generated.
The Positive
  • Karen's 66% booking rate and 50% signing rate are genuinely strong performance metrics — the firm has a proven intake model; the opportunity is to train the full team to Karen's standard
  • Performance is already tracked by intaker — booking rates, signing rates, and consultation counts are logged, which means the data exists to coach, correct, and improve without guesswork
The Gap
  • If the full intake team matched Karen's 66% booking rate on May's 177 leads, the firm would have booked approximately 117 consultations instead of the reported number — at a 40.7% signing rate and $5,250 case value, that is a substantial revenue gap driven entirely by intake performance, not lead volume
  • The "dead lead" classification for 41 of 177 May leads may be premature — leads that are not ready to sign in week one are often ready in weeks three or four with structured follow-up; without a nurture sequence, those 41 leads are revenue that disappears permanently
  • Without after-hours coverage, the firm is unreachable during the hours when many immigrant families — especially those working full-time — have time to search for and contact an attorney
Getting the entire intake team to Karen's level, adding a structured follow-up sequence for non-converting leads, and ensuring the firm is reachable after hours are the fastest paths to more revenue from the leads Abogada Vida is already generating — and they are the foundation Mahsa needs to justify the marketing investment that gets the firm to $1M.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • The primary website conversion path is a "Schedule a Consultation" CTA button — no inline contact form is visible above the fold, meaning visitors who are ready to inquire but not ready to call are given no easy alternative capture point
  • No live chat or chatbot is confirmed on the website — for an immigration firm serving a community that may prefer text-based communication or has language questions, an accessible chat alternative to calling can meaningfully increase lead capture from website traffic
  • The Clio Grow intake platform is confirmed (vida.cliogrow.com) — the infrastructure for structured intake management is in place; the opportunity is connecting the website's conversion paths more directly to that intake system to reduce drop-off between website visit and consultation booking

Team Assessment

Abogada Vida has a nine-person team with documented SOPs, Airtable automations, and a remote operations model that is genuinely impressive at this revenue stage — but Mahsa remains the firm's only attorney, which means every consultation, every legal decision, and every case acceptance ultimately flows through her. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
"Mahsa has not opened client emails for three months due to effective case management" and "Established 400+ pages of Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)"
The Problem
  • Mahsa is the only attorney in the firm — she personally handles every consultation and provides all legal advice, meaning the firm's case capacity is capped at whatever she can personally handle in a week
  • Approximately 30% of inbound leads are for removal and detainee cases that must be referred out because no second attorney exists — the firm is actively generating demand it cannot serve and paying for those leads in marketing costs
  • The planned attorney hire ($140K salary, ~$200K total cost) adds significant overhead before the hire produces enough revenue to cover their cost — without a structured onboarding plan and case assignment model, this is a high-risk transition
  • There is no confirmed manager layer between Marlen (remote operations) and the paralegal/case manager/intake teams — as the firm grows toward $1M and beyond, the operational structure will need a layer of leadership that does not yet exist
Blind Spot: The fact that Mahsa has not opened client emails in three months is a strong signal that case management is working — but it does not mean the firm can operate without her. The bottleneck has simply moved upstream to consultations and legal decisions, which are still 100% dependent on her presence. The firm is operationally effective at its current size but structurally fragile above it.
The Positive
  • 400+ pages of documented SOPs and Airtable-based automations represent a level of operational infrastructure most firms at this revenue stage do not have — Marlen's remote systems work is a genuine asset that makes the second-attorney hire and team scaling far more manageable
  • The firm uses AI for document generation and is actively building AI agents — this positions Abogada Vida to scale case processing capacity without proportional headcount growth
The Gap
  • Without a second attorney, the firm's revenue ceiling is Mahsa's personal bandwidth — adding marketing and getting more consultations does not translate to more revenue if Mahsa is already at capacity for consultations per week
  • Referring out 30% of leads is not just a revenue problem — it is a brand problem; clients who came to Abogada Vida for removal help and are referred elsewhere may not come back for future immigration needs, and they will not refer others
  • A single-attorney firm is fundamentally vulnerable to health, personal commitments, or unexpected life events — without a second attorney who can cover, any disruption to Mahsa's availability halts the entire firm
The attorney hire is the single decision that unlocks Mahsa's path from Stage 4 to Stage 5 and beyond — the moment a second attorney is handling their own caseload, Mahsa's ceiling rises from her personal hours to the firm's total capacity, and the $3M and $7M stages of her Vivid Vision become structurally possible.

Profit Plan Assessment

Abogada Vida tracks monthly revenue and has a clear annual goal of $1M in Year 1 — but with May at $66K, June running at $38K (partial), and January at $36K, the firm is averaging well below the $83K per month needed to hit that target, and the revenue swings suggest the firm is not yet at a predictable income level. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Monthly revenue ranged from $36K (January) to $66K (May) against a $100K monthly target — a swing of $30K per month suggests the firm does not yet have the consistent lead flow and intake conversion needed to produce predictable revenue
  • No profitability data by practice area was shared — without knowing the margin on family immigration versus humanitarian versus removal cases, scaling decisions may be directing resources toward the firm's least profitable work
  • A planned attorney hire at approximately $200K total annual cost represents roughly one-third of the firm's current annualized revenue — without a profit plan that shows the breakeven timeline, this hire carries meaningful financial risk
  • No explicit mention of profit visibility tools (profit-first methodology, CFO reporting, margin-by-case-type tracking) — the firm is managing revenue without confirmed visibility into what it actually keeps
Blind Spot: The $250 consultation fee is a revenue line the firm likely does not think of as a strategic lever — but at 177 leads per month and a 20% booking rate, every 1% improvement in lead-to-consult conversion generates approximately $350 in additional consultation fees plus the downstream signing revenue. Consultation fees are not just revenue; they are a filter for serious prospects and a signal of case quality.
The Positive
  • Monthly revenue is actively tracked and discussed — the firm knows it missed the $100K May goal by $33K and is not operating blind to its numbers
  • The three-year Vivid Vision ($1M, $3M, $7M) provides a clear directional framework — goals this specific create accountability and allow for backward planning on what monthly revenue targets need to look like at each stage
The Gap
  • The gap between the $100K monthly target and the $38K–$66K actual range is primarily a lead generation and intake conversion problem — but without a profit plan, it is easy to mistake a revenue gap for a cost problem and cut expenses that are actually investments
  • Without case-type-level profitability data, the firm cannot know whether serving more removal cases (once the second attorney is hired) will move the profit needle faster than adding more family immigration volume — strategic hiring decisions require this data
  • Scaling to $1M without a defined profit plan means the risk of reaching $1M in gross revenue while working more hours for the same or lower take-home — the goal is not $1M, it is $1M with healthy margins and a firm that does not consume Mahsa in the process
A profit plan is what transforms the $1M Vivid Vision from an aspirational target into a month-by-month execution plan — one that shows Mahsa exactly what revenue she needs, what she keeps, and what each hire and marketing dollar returns on the path to Year 2's $3M goal.
