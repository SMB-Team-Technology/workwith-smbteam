Intake Assessment

Abogada Vida PLLC has built a three-person intake team with documented performance KPIs — a genuine operational asset that most firms at this revenue level do not have. But with an average booking rate of 20% on all inbound leads and a 39-point performance gap between the top and lowest intakers, the firm is converting fewer than 1 in 5 prospects who contact it. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • The firm's overall booking rate averages 20% on all inbound leads — meaning 80% of every prospect who contacts Abogada Vida does not become a consultation; in May, 177 leads produced approximately 35 consultations
  • Significant performance variation across the intake team: Karen books 66% of her leads while Gabby books only 26.7% — a 39-point gap that indicates no standardized intake script or call process holding all three intakers to a consistent standard
  • Javier, the new sales-focused intaker hired for conversion, booked only 13.5% of May's consultations in his first partial month — suggesting onboarding and scripting are not yet systematized for new intake hires
  • The $250 consultation fee (standard) and $500 fee (detainee cases) create a gatekeeping moment in the intake call that, without the right script, can lose a prospect before the value of the consultation is communicated
Blind Spot: Gabby's signing rate on the consultations she does book matches Karen's exactly at 50% — meaning Gabby can close as well as the firm's top performer. The gap is not her ability to convert prospects; it is her ability to book them. A focused intake script improvement for Gabby could double her booking rate and produce an immediate revenue lift from leads already coming in.
The Positive
  • A dedicated three-person intake team with documented KPI tracking (booking rate, signing rate per intaker) is a genuine operational foundation — most firms at this stage have the attorney answering intake calls personally
  • Karen is performing at an elite level (66% booking rate, 50% signing rate) and represents a proven model that the rest of the team can be trained to replicate
The Gap
  • If the firm's average booking rate moved from 20% to Karen's 66% across May's 177 leads, that would produce 117 consultations instead of 35 — at a 40.7% signing rate, that is 47 cases instead of 14, a 33-case monthly increase worth approximately $173,000 in additional monthly revenue potential at an average case value of ~$5,250
  • Without a standardized intake conversion process, every new intaker hire (like Javier) restarts from scratch on training — preventing the predictable performance scaling required to sustain $1 million and beyond
  • Inconsistent booking rates mean the ROI on any marketing investment is highly variable — adding ad spend to a team converting at 20% average produces unpredictable returns; the intake floor must be raised first
Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing. For a firm targeting $1 million in year one, this is where the fastest gains are.

  • No live chat confirmed on the website — prospects who visit outside business hours have no way to initiate contact or leave a message in real time, and Spanish-speaking visitors on mobile may not call immediately

Team Assessment

Abogada Vida PLLC has more team infrastructure than most firms at this revenue level — over 400 pages of documented SOPs, Airtable automations, a dedicated case manager, three paralegals, and an operations manager in Marlen who has taken client email management off Mahsa's plate entirely. The single hard constraint is one that infrastructure cannot solve: Mahsa is the only licensed attorney. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Mahsa is the sole licensed attorney — every consultation involving legal advice, every case strategy decision, every filing, and every court appearance requires her personally; there is no partner, associate, or contractor who can handle legal matters in her absence
  • Approximately 30% of all inbound leads — removal and detainee cases, which carry consultation fees of $500 versus the standard $250 — are referred out to competitors because no attorney with that specialty is in place; these are the firm's highest-value leads going directly to Lahoud Law Group and Albarelli & Harrington
  • The firm operates entirely remote except for Mahsa and one on-site admin, and the office build-out in Schnecksville has been delayed by permits and contractors with no expected completion before end of summer — limiting the professional physical presence that immigration clients and the court system sometimes require
  • The attorney hire is projected at $140K salary plus benefits totaling approximately $200K annually — a commitment that only makes financial sense once the firm's lead volume and intake conversion rate are generating enough cases to fully utilize a second attorney's capacity
Blind Spot: The operations infrastructure Mahsa has built — SOPs, Airtable automations, Clio Grow, Marlen's case management — means the firm is ready to hand off operational work to a second attorney immediately. The bottleneck is not readiness; it is having enough predictable case volume to justify the hire. Marketing must come first to make the second attorney economically viable.
The Positive
  • 400+ pages of documented SOPs with Airtable automations and Clio Grow integration — the operational documentation is in place to onboard and manage a second attorney without Mahsa rebuilding the system from scratch
  • Marlen Islas (Client Success & Legal Operations Manager) is managing client communication and case flow well enough that Mahsa has not opened client emails in three months — an operational win that frees Mahsa's time for the legal work only she can do
The Gap
  • With only one attorney, the firm cannot accept all the work it generates — every referred-out removal and detainee case is a case worth $2,000–$8,000+ going directly to a competitor, and there is no current timeline for stopping that revenue loss
  • Without a second attorney, Mahsa cannot reduce her hours, take time off, or step back from any legal decision without the firm's case work pausing — the $1M, $3M, and $7M Vivid Vision requires an attorney team, and right now it has one person
  • The $200K attorney hire requires case volume to justify it — which requires a predictable lead flow the firm does not yet have — meaning the team bottleneck and the marketing gap are the same problem, not separate ones
Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Profit Plan Assessment

Abogada Vida PLLC is generating meaningful revenue — tracking toward an annualized run rate of approximately $550K–$650K — but monthly revenue fluctuates significantly with no clear framework connecting performance to the $1 million year-one goal. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Monthly revenue varies dramatically — May at $66,625 (missing a $100K monthly goal), June at $38,720 (as of June 29), January at $36,125 (lowest of the year) — with no clear explanation for the swings or a system driving performance consistently toward the $83K/month average needed to hit $1M in year one
  • No profitability framework, gross margin by practice area, or cost-per-case analysis was in place at the time of this audit — making it impossible to evaluate whether growth is producing proportional take-home income or just more case volume and overhead
  • A $200K attorney hire is the firm's most important near-term financial decision, but without a revenue model showing which case types justify that cost and at what volume, the timing and ROI of the hire cannot be calculated
  • The firm's three-year goal ($1M → $3M → $7M) requires a compounding growth plan — but without knowing current margins, the financial requirements of each milestone cannot be validated or planned for
Blind Spot: Every referred-out removal and detainee case carries an acquisition cost (intake time, consultation scheduling, attorney review) with zero revenue. These cases are not just lost revenue — they are subsidizing competitor business while adding overhead cost to the firm. Quantifying the fully-loaded cost of referred-out cases makes the attorney hire ROI case immediately clearer.
The Positive
  • The firm is already tracking intake KPIs (booking rate, signing rate by intaker) and monthly revenue figures — this data discipline creates the foundation to build a proper profit model without starting from scratch
  • May's $66,625 month demonstrates what is possible when intake performs well — this is a real benchmark that can be modeled, not a one-time aberration
The Gap
  • Without knowing gross margin by practice area, it is impossible to determine whether growing from $600K to $1M increases profit proportionally or simply adds more overhead — particularly with a $200K attorney hire and new marketing spend in the plan
  • The $36K–$66K monthly revenue range means the firm is operating well below its stated $100K monthly goal with no financial model explaining the cause — case volume problem, fee structure problem, case mix problem, or intake problem — or a combination
  • Investing $8,047/month in SMB Team services plus $3,000–$12,000 in ad spend represents a meaningful share of monthly revenue (13–31% at current levels) — without a profit plan, the firm cannot assess whether this investment is sustainable at current revenue or only justified at the $1M target
Revenue without a profit plan just means more cases — building to $1 million in year one and $7 million in year three requires knowing exactly what each case type earns, what the overhead structure requires, and how profit grows as the team does.
