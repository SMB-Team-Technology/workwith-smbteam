Lead Generation

  • No Google Ads, LSA, or Meta advertising confirmed active — competitors with downtown Allentown addresses are capturing the firm's highest-intent searches
  • Firm does not appear in the local 3-pack for "immigration lawyer Allentown PA" or "immigration attorney Lehigh Valley PA" — Schnecksville location limits geographic relevance for the market's anchor searches
  • No geo-targeted landing pages for Allentown, Bethlehem, or Easton — the firm is invisible for location-modified searches it could rank for
Without a predictable paid lead system, the path to $1 million this year and $3 million next year depends entirely on referrals and organic reach — neither of which scales fast enough to meet the Vivid Vision timeline.

Intake

  • The firm's average booking rate on all inbound leads is 20% — meaning 80% of every prospect who contacts the firm does not become a consultation
  • A 39-point performance gap between the top intaker (Karen at 66%) and the lowest (Gabby at 26.7%) indicates no standardized intake process holding performance to a consistent floor
  • In May, the firm received 177 leads but converted only ~35 to consultations — the intake system is leaving the majority of paid-for leads unconverted
Intake conversion is the fastest lever available — improving from 20% to 50% booking rates on existing leads does not require a single additional marketing dollar, and it doubles the cases the firm closes from the same traffic.

Self-Managing Team

  • Mahsa is the sole licensed attorney — every consultation involving legal advice and every case requires her personally, creating an absolute ceiling on case volume that no amount of marketing can break through
  • Approximately 30% of inbound leads (removal and detainee cases) are referred out to competitors because no second attorney with that specialty is in place
  • The operations layer (400+ SOPs, Airtable automations, Marlen managing case flow) is strong enough that Mahsa has not opened client emails in three months — but the legal decision bottleneck remains entirely hers
The team systems are working — but building a firm that runs at $1M, $3M, and $7M without Mahsa as the single point of failure requires adding licensed capacity before the growth ceiling becomes a crisis.

Profit

  • Monthly revenue is highly variable — May at $66,625 (missing a $100K goal), June at $38,720, January at $36,125 — with no clear framework explaining the swings or driving them toward the $83K/month needed to hit $1M in year one
  • A $200K attorney hire is the firm's most important near-term decision, but without a profit model showing which case types cover that cost and at what volume, the timing and ROI of the hire cannot be evaluated
  • No profitability framework, cost-per-case-type analysis, or financial planning model was in place at the time of this audit
Revenue without a profit plan just means more cases — building to $1M and beyond requires knowing exactly what each case type earns, what the overhead covers, and how the attorney hire changes the math.
