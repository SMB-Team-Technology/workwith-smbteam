  • PageSpeed scores: Mobile score unavailable due to API quota limits — raw indicators (TTFB 5.63 seconds, 14-second load time) strongly indicate a poor score; scores should be verified at pagespeed.web.dev before the proposal call

  • No geo-targeting landing pages for Allentown, Bethlehem, Easton, or other Lehigh Valley cities — the firm's Schnecksville address creates a geographic relevance gap for searches originating in the larger surrounding market
Blind Spot: The firm's bilingual website is a genuine competitive asset — but without landing pages that specifically target "abogada de inmigración Allentown PA" or "abogado de deportación Lehigh Valley," that bilingual advantage is invisible to the Spanish-speaking clients actively searching in the market's largest cities.
The Positive
  • Modern, clean design estimated 2023–2024 with full bilingual English/Spanish functionality and a language toggle — the visual brand communicates credibility and cultural connection
  • Active blog with recent immigration content (May 2026 and March 2026 posts) — a foundation for organic SEO that most small immigration firms do not have
The Gap
  • A 14-second mobile load time means the majority of mobile visitors — who make up most local search traffic — are bouncing before they ever read the firm's credentials or see the 96-review track record
  • Without city-specific landing pages, the firm is effectively invisible in organic and paid search for anyone searching from Allentown, Bethlehem, or Easton — the largest population centers in the firm's service area
  • No above-fold contact form means the firm is losing leads who are interested but not yet ready to pick up the phone — a streamlined form above the fold alone can meaningfully increase lead capture
A fast, bilingual, conversion-optimized website with city-specific landing pages is the foundation every other marketing investment depends on — without it, ad spend drives traffic to a site that does not convert.
