Intake Assessment

David handles every intake call personally — including calls at 2 a.m. — because there is no one else on the team he can trust to do it correctly. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
"disorganized intake missing weekend PI/criminal cases"
The Problem
  • David personally handles all intake calls, including 2 a.m. calls — there is no delegation structure, no intake protocol, and no staff member trained or empowered to handle an initial consultation
  • No after-hours coverage — weeknight and weekend calls either reach David directly at any hour or go unanswered; no answering service or backup protocol exists
  • Office manager currently out sick with no coverage — the firm's primary administrative support is unavailable and there is no one designated to cover, exposing just how fragile the intake infrastructure actually is
  • No defined intake process or follow-up sequence — leads who do not sign on first contact are likely lost because there is no system to track, follow up, or re-engage them
Blind Spot: Every weekend arrest call that reaches voicemail instead of a live voice is a signed retainer walking to Seth Okin or Hassan, Hassan & Tuchman. Criminal defense clients rarely wait — they call the next name on the list. In PI, the family that cannot reach an attorney at a critical moment calls one that answers. This is not hypothetical; it is happening every weekend.
The Positive
  • David is personally committed to client service — his willingness to take 2 a.m. calls demonstrates that responsiveness is a genuine priority; that commitment just needs to be transferred to a trained team member, not abandoned
The Gap
  • 150 active PI cases are already in the pipeline — but every new inbound lead that arrives during a case-heavy period risks being missed or delayed, costing cases the firm has effectively already earned through reputation and referral
  • Every PI and criminal defense case that misses the weekend intake window is a case value, a referral relationship, and a client given to a competitor who answered the phone
  • Without a defined intake process, no lead gen investment — Google Ads, LSA, or otherwise — can perform at its potential; leads generated will leak out of a system with no documented process to capture them
An intake system that works 24/7 is what allows David to stop being the emergency line for his own firm — and start having evenings, weekends, and eventually the Maine home and restaurant he is working toward.

Team Assessment

David described his current team as a "C or D team" — and the evidence supports it. The office manager is out sick, part-time staff are unreliable, and David is personally carrying 60–70 hours of work per week because there is no one else he trusts to handle it. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
"C or D team"
The Problem
  • David estimates 80% of his current tasks are delegable — but without a capable team in place, all of that work falls on him, keeping him at 60–70 hours per week with no path to reduction
  • The office manager — described as David's one reliable admin — is currently out sick with no backup coverage or protocol; this single point of failure is exposing the entire operational structure
  • No management layer exists — every client question, case status inquiry, and operational issue escalates directly to David, making it structurally impossible for the firm to function without his daily presence
  • Part-time staff are described as unreliable — and a firm managing 150 active PI cases alongside criminal defense matters cannot advance those cases or serve clients properly with a "C or D team"
Blind Spot: The 38 years of experience and Supreme Court victories that make David exceptional in the courtroom are the same qualities that make delegation feel impossible — because no one on the team can do it the way he would. But "do it the way David would" and "do it well enough that clients are served and cases advance" are not the same standard. A team does not need to be David. It needs to be capable, trained, and accountable.
The Positive
  • David has already identified approximately 80% of his workload as delegable — this level of self-awareness is the first step; the gap is execution, not insight
The Gap
  • Without a self-managing team, the Maine home is a vacation David schedules around cases, not a lifestyle he can access at will — the team problem is the life problem
  • The firm's growth ceiling is David's personal hourly capacity — no amount of marketing spend, case volume, or revenue goal changes that ceiling until there is a team to process it
  • 150 active PI cases in the backlog are not being advanced at the rate they should be — the revenue is already in the door, but the team to extract it is not in place
The A team David described wanting is not a wish — it is the mechanism that turns 38 years of reputation into a firm that runs without him in the room.

Profit Plan Assessment

David's monthly revenue swings from $15,000 to $70,000 with no predictable pattern — making it nearly impossible to pay himself consistently, plan investments, or build toward any financial goal with confidence. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
"Highly variable — $15K–$70K/month currently"
The Problem
  • Monthly revenue ranges from $15,000 to $70,000 — a $55,000 swing that makes personal financial planning, team investment, and business growth planning effectively impossible on a consistent basis
  • No stated tracking of profitability by practice area, case type, or productivity metric — without this data, it is impossible to know which work generates the best return on time invested
  • The firm's revenue is structurally tied to case settlements, which are unpredictable by nature — without a profit plan that accounts for this variability, David cannot build toward any financial goal with confidence
  • 150 active PI cases represent significant uncollected revenue — but without visibility into case progress, settlement timing, and profitability per case, planning for that revenue is guesswork
Blind Spot: In PI practice, case mix matters enormously — a traumatic brain injury case and a minor fender bender both arrive as "a PI case," but their revenue potential and time investment are wildly different. Without tracking by case type, David cannot identify which matters to prioritize in marketing, intake screening, and attorney time — and the $100K/month goal has no clear mechanism attached to it.
The Positive
  • David has a concrete revenue goal ($100,000 per month) and understands the approximate mechanism (the 150-case backlog) — most owners at this stage have no financial target at all; this is a meaningful starting point for a profit plan
The Gap
  • Without a profit plan, the $100K/month goal is a wish, not a projection — there is no roadmap connecting the 150-case backlog to a specific revenue timeline or monthly cash flow target
  • Revenue variability makes it impossible to build a reliable team compensation structure — and without reliable compensation, building the capable team David needs becomes even harder
  • Revenue growing is not the same as profit growing — without tracking expenses and margins, David could reach $100K/month in collections while still not taking home significantly more than he does today
A profit plan is what allows David to see exactly when the Maine home and the restaurant become affordable — not someday, but on a specific date with a specific number attached.
