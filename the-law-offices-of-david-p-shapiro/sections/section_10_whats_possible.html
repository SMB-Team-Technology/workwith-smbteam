Here's What's Possible for The Law Offices of David B. Shapiro

David has spent 37 years building a reputation in Baltimore that most attorneys never come close to — a Supreme Court argument, a legislative career, decades of PI and criminal defense work, and a community footprint that extends far beyond the courtroom. The question is not whether the credibility is there. It is whether the team, the systems, and the digital presence exist to convert that credibility into the life David described wanting: a firm that runs without him, summers in Maine, the restaurant on his own terms. They are not in place yet. But they can be built — and faster than David may think.

1
David's phone stops ringing at 2 AM because a trained A-team handles intake around the clock — criminal arrests and car accidents on Saturday night get answered by a prepared intake specialist, not the attorney. The 150-case PI backlog is being moved systematically by an associate and a support team David trusts, and $100,000 months are no longer a goal but a floor.
2
David spends July and August at the Maine house, and the firm is generating more revenue than it did when he was there 60 hours a week — because the digital marketing engine is running in the background, LSA leads are coming in every week, and the team is closing cases without him. He checks in twice a week. That is enough.
3
Shapiro's Café gets the attention it deserves — not stolen hours on a Tuesday when the office is slow, but real time, real energy, and real ownership — because the law firm no longer requires David's daily presence to function. He built one institution. Now he can build another.

This is not a fantasy. SMB Team can make this a reality for The Law Offices of David B. Shapiro.
