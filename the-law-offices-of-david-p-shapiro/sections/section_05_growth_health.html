Until there is a system to attract and convert new clients independently of referrals, David cannot step back — because the moment he does, the pipeline stops.

Intake

  • David personally handles all intake, including calls at 2 AM — there is no delegation or coverage system in place, and when the office manager is out sick, coverage drops further
  • Weekend PI and criminal defense cases are confirmed as a recurring intake gap — the highest-urgency prospective clients (arrest calls, accident victims) are not consistently reached in the critical first hour
  • The website has no confirmed contact form, no after-hours intake pathway, and no chat — digital inquiries arriving outside business hours have no way to be captured
Every missed after-hours call is a case that becomes someone else's signed retainer — and fixing this is the fastest way to generate more revenue without spending more on marketing.

Self-Managing Team

  • David described his support team as "C or D team" — one good admin, a few part-time staff, and an office manager currently out sick; none of the current staff is at a level David can fully delegate critical tasks to
  • Solo practitioner structure means all legal work, client relationships, intake, and business decisions flow through David personally — the firm stops when he stops
  • No management layer, no accountability structure, and no defined performance standards were described — without these, building toward the A-team David named as his goal has no foundation to build on
Until David has a team he trusts to run without him, the Maine home and the restaurant will stay a plan — because the firm will always need him more than he can afford to be away.

Profit

  • Revenue swings between $15,000 and $70,000 per month depending on case settlements — this is not a revenue problem, it is a predictability problem driven by a 150-case PI backlog that cannot be moved without more team capacity
  • No financial visibility was described — no tracking of profit by practice area, no owner compensation structure, and no mechanism for distinguishing which cases are most profitable
  • The firm's single largest revenue lever — resolving the existing 150-case PI backlog — is currently blocked by a team capacity constraint, not a market demand problem
The money is already in the caseload — what is missing is the team and the financial system to convert backlog into predictable, bankable profit that David can count on every month.
