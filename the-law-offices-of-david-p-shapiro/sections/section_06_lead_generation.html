Lead Generation

The Law Offices of David P. Shapiro has built a 38-year career almost entirely on referrals — with no paid advertising, no LSA, no active SEO strategy, and a website that actively warns potential clients away with a browser security error. The firm has both the credentials and the case volume to compete aggressively online; what is missing is the digital infrastructure to do it.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • www.davidshapiro.com has an expired or misconfigured TLS certificate — any visitor arriving via an HTTPS link in 2026 sees a browser security warning before reading a single word of the firm's content, and many will leave immediately
  • PageSpeed scores could not be determined due to the TLS error — a manual run at pagespeed.web.dev is required before delivery; based on site age and the content indicators below, mobile performance is expected to be below 60
  • Website copy references "over 22 years of combined litigation experience" — content that has not been refreshed in years and signals to prospective clients that the firm is not actively maintaining its digital presence
  • No confirmed above-the-fold call to action, contact form, or click-to-call button — the path from website visit to consultation is not visible on arrival
Blind Spot: The TLS certificate error is more than a cosmetic issue — Google treats HTTPS as a local ranking signal. A site generating a security warning is being penalized in organic search rankings and actively losing referral traffic every day a visitor's browser redirects them away. Every directory listing, email signature, and business card pointing to www.davidshapiro.com is sending prospects to a blocked door.
The Positive
  • Attorney bio page confirmed at /attorney-profile/ — David's Supreme Court admission, Maryland House of Delegates service, and 38-year record are documented credentials that, once the TLS issue is resolved, differentiate this firm from every mass-market PI shop in Baltimore
  • Testimonials page exists at /testimonials/ — the social proof infrastructure is in place and can be rebuilt into a conversion asset once the site is functioning correctly
The Gap
  • The TLS error alone is sufficient to disqualify the website from converting any paid or organic traffic — a $7,500–$25,000/month ad spend directed to a site with a browser security warning is wasted investment until this is fixed
  • Outdated content signals to prospective clients that the firm is not actively invested in its digital presence — in a high-stakes legal decision, that signal loses consultations
  • Without a confirmed mobile-responsive design and above-the-fold intake path, the majority of prospects arriving on mobile have no clear immediate way to contact the firm
A functioning, conversion-optimized website is not optional for a firm trying to reach $100,000 per month — it is the first requirement before any paid marketing dollar is spent.
