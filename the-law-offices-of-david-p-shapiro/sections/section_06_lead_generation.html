Lead Generation

The Law Offices of David P. Shapiro has built a recognizable brand through radio advertising, strong directories, and organic SEO — but in a market where competitors are running active Google Ads, Local Service Ads, and paid social campaigns, the firm does not appear to be competing for the highest-intent digital searches that generate immediate retainers. The opportunity to accelerate growth through digital paid channels is significant and largely untapped.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Contact form is buried at the bottom of the homepage with no above-the-fold conversion path — a prospect who lands on the site at 2 a.m. after an arrest has no immediate way to reach out other than calling a number, which may go unanswered
  • PageSpeed scores: Not confirmed — API quota exceeded during research; manual run at pagespeed.web.dev required before audit delivery. If mobile score falls below 50, a rebuild priority flag applies.
  • No video on the homepage — an attorney who has been on Padres radio broadcasts and earned 12 consecutive Super Lawyers designations should have video above the fold building trust before the phone rings
  • The trigger file listed www.davidshapiro.com as the firm's URL — that domain routes to an entirely different attorney in Baltimore, Maryland. Any prospect who received that URL via business card, radio mention, or referral ends up on an unrelated firm's website or gets a TLS certificate error.
Blind Spot: The davidshapiro.com URL mismatch is an active, ongoing lead loss event — not a past issue. Every time someone hears "David Shapiro" on the radio or receives an informal referral and types davidshapiro.com, they land on a Baltimore attorney or get a browser error. This is not a branding problem; it is a plumbing problem that can be fixed immediately with a redirect or clarified in all marketing materials.
The Positive
  • Modern, contemporary website design (circa 2022–2024) on WordPress with mobile responsiveness — the site presents a professional, credible first impression consistent with the firm's 16-year track record
  • Active blog with 14+ pages of practice-area content, dedicated geo-targeting pages for 18+ San Diego County cities, and a substantial case results page — strong foundational SEO architecture already in place
  • Attorney bios for all three attorneys plus four support staff, a case results library, a client testimonials section, and community involvement content (Leukemia Scholarship, Padres sponsorship) — all trust-building elements competitors may lack
The Gap
  • Without an above-the-fold contact form or online intake path, the website is not converting all the traffic it already receives — prospects who do not want to call at odd hours will leave without contacting the firm
  • The URL mismatch means a measurable percentage of referred or radio-generated prospects are being lost before they ever see the correct website
  • Missing homepage video means the site cannot communicate the level of trust and authority that a 16-year Super Lawyers attorney deserves — competitors with simpler credentials but better video presence may close those prospects first
A website that converts as well as this firm's reputation deserves is not a cosmetic upgrade — it is the difference between brand awareness that turns into signed retainers and brand awareness that turns into missed calls.

Emergency urgency terms like "arrested tonight San Diego" and "need DUI lawyer now" capture high-intent prospects at peak desperation.

Blind Spot: In San Diego's criminal defense market, the firms spending $5,000–$12,000 per month on Google Ads are not necessarily better attorneys than this firm — they just appear first when someone searches at 2 a.m. after an arrest. The quality gap favors this firm. The visibility gap does not.
