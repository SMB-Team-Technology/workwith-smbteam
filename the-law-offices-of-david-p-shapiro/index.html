Here Is Where The Law Offices of David B. Shapiro Stands Today

David B. Shapiro has practiced law in Baltimore for over 37 years — handling personal injury, criminal defense, DUI/DWI, and medical malpractice cases as a solo practitioner out of a single downtown office. The most important finding is this: David is carrying 150 active PI cases and working 60–70 hours per week, but almost none of that work is being supported by a team strong enough to delegate to — which means the revenue sitting in that backlog cannot be unlocked until the team problem is solved, and every new client who calls after-hours or on a weekend is a case the firm risks losing before David even answers the phone.

Stage 6 is where David stops being the firm and starts owning it — spending summers in Maine, running the restaurant on his terms, and showing up only when he chooses to, while the firm generates revenue with or without him.

  • 100% referral-dependent with no paid advertising and no scalable lead generation system in place — LegalMatch contract signed but not yet activated

  • Support team described as "C or D team" — one good admin, a few part-time staff, and an office manager currently out sick — nothing in place that David can fully trust to run without him
  • Revenue swings from $15K to $70K per month depending on case settlements — profit is discovered when it arrives, not planned in advance

Where You Need To Be

  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.
