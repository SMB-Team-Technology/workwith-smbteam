Intake Assessment

McFall Legal's only confirmed intake channel is a single consultation form on the website, with no visible process behind it once a prospect submits. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • With only Alan and Jason handling casework, new consultation requests likely have to compete with active case deadlines rather than flow to a dedicated intake process
  • No CRM or intake tracking system was identified — there is no confirmed way to know how many form submissions turn into signed clients
  • No live chat or after-hours contact option exists on the site, so evening and weekend inquiries have no path to a fast response
  • No defined follow-up sequence was identified for prospects who do not sign after their first consultation
The Gap
  • Every lead that goes unanswered after hours is a prospect who may call the next firm on their list — potentially Daly or Ceraul, both of whom show up in the same searches
  • Without tracking, McFall Legal cannot know its true close rate or which practice area converts best
  • Referral clients naturally get a warm reception — but leads generated through new marketing efforts need the same speed and structure to convert at all
A defined intake process means every consultation request gets Alan and Jason's full reputation behind it, without either of them having to personally chase it down.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • A dedicated "Contact Us" page hosts a named "Legal Consultation Form" — present, but its position above or below the fold was not independently confirmed this session
  • No live chat widget was identified anywhere on the site
  • No confirmed after-hours or weekend contact path exists beyond the standard form

Team Assessment

McFall Legal has already taken the first real step toward a self-managing team by bringing Jason on in 2023, but the firm still runs on two attorneys with no confirmed dedicated marketing, operations, or intake staff. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Only two attorneys currently handle all casework, client relationships, and — by default — intake
  • No dedicated marketing, operations, or intake team member was identified
  • No leadership or management structure beyond the two attorneys has been identified
  • Alan still appears to be the central point of contact for the practice five years after founding it solo
The Positive
  • Adding Jason in 2023 shows a real, deliberate move toward building a team rather than staying a single-attorney practice
The Gap
  • With no delegated systems, growth today means more hours for Alan and Jason, not less
  • A firm built around two attorneys' personal availability cannot scale past what those two people can physically handle
  • There is no confirmed path yet for the practice to run smoothly during a vacation, illness, or eventual transition
Building real structure around Jason's role now is what turns "bringing my son into the practice" into an actual succession plan — not just a second name on the letterhead.
