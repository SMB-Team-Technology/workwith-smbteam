Lead Generation

McFall Legal has built a content-rich website and a genuine local reputation — 4.8 stars and 21 reviews, ahead of both same-town competitors on review count — but has no confirmed paid advertising, no geo-targeted landing pages, and no repeatable system generating new estate planning or real estate clients beyond referrals.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • The current site launched with the 2021 rebrand — roughly 5 years old, which is typically when law firm sites start falling behind on design and conversion best practices
  • PageSpeed mobile and desktop scores could not be measured live this session (the public API was rate-limited on repeated attempts) — flagged for a manual pagespeed.web.dev check rather than an estimated number
  • No live chat was identified on the site
  • Mobile responsiveness and above-the-fold CTA placement were not independently confirmed this session and should be verified before the proposal call
The Positive
  • A dedicated "Our Legal Team" page features both attorneys' bios — a real trust-building asset many solo firms lack
  • An active "Insights & News" blog exists, with recent content including a post on electronic signature wills
  • A dedicated "Contact Us" page hosts a named "Legal Consultation Form"
The Gap
  • Without confirmed PageSpeed and mobile performance data, it is unknown how many visitors are dropping off before ever reaching the consultation form
  • Real content assets — the blog and attorney bios — are not currently connected to any paid promotion or geo-targeted landing page structure that would actively drive new traffic to them
A website built to convert is the foundation of a lead engine that runs without Alan fielding every call personally — freeing up exactly the time he is trying to create for himself and for Jason.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Google Business Profile shows an estimated 4.8 stars and 21 reviews via the Birdeye aggregator (a strong proxy for the Google listing, though not independently confirmed on Google itself this session)
  • Live map-pack position could not be rendered this session — organic and directory visibility is the best available proxy for competitive standing
  • Avvo, FindLaw, and Yellow Pages still list the firm under the pre-2021 legacy name "McFall, Layman & Jordan," diluting the current "McFall Law, LLC" brand across exactly the directories prospective clients cross-check before calling
  • No geo-targeted landing pages were identified for surrounding towns such as Wind Gap or Easton
Blind Spot: Three major directories are quietly telling prospective clients you may not be the same firm they found on Google — a five-year-old rebrand that still has not fully reached the sources people check before they call.
The Positive
  • At 21 reviews and 4.8 stars, McFall Legal already exceeds Daly Law Offices (13 reviews) and Ceraul Law Offices (15 reviews) — this firm is not losing the local reviews race
The Gap
  • Without geo-landing pages, McFall Legal is ceding ground to a competitor's built-out page structure for exactly the searches prospective clients are running today
  • The NAP inconsistency across Avvo, FindLaw, and Yellow Pages may be quietly suppressing the firm's local ranking authority
Daly Law Offices — has built a full geo + practice-area landing page structure (dedicated Bangor and Easton pages for estate planning, elder law, wills & trusts, and probate) that McFall Legal does not currently match, despite having fewer reviews.
Fixing the directory and landing-page gaps means McFall Legal's real reputation — already ahead of its closest local competitors — finally shows up everywhere a prospective client looks.
