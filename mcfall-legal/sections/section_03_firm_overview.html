Here Is Where McFall Legal Stands Today

Alan McFall built McFall Law, LLC in 2021 after a partner's retirement ended his prior firm, McFall, Layman & Jordan, P.C. — and in 2023 his son Jason joined the practice, handling estate planning, probate, and real estate matters for clients in Bangor and Northampton County, PA. The firm holds real assets: a content-rich website, a 4.8-star reputation, and more reviews than its two closest same-town competitors combined — but three major legal directories still list the firm under its old pre-2021 name, and no system exists yet to turn that reputation into a predictable, referral-independent flow of new clients.

Stage 6 is the stage that turns McFall Law into a firm that runs itself — one Alan can eventually hand to Jason with real stability, not just a client list and a phone that only rings when someone remembers to refer a friend.

Where You Are Today

STAGE 3: SOLO PRACTITIONER
  • New client flow depends on referrals and reputation, not a built system
  • No confirmed paid advertising or geo-targeted landing pages driving inbound leads
  • Five years after the 2021 rebrand, Avvo, FindLaw, and Yellow Pages still list the firm under the retired legacy name "McFall, Layman & Jordan"
  • Two attorneys (Alan and Jason) with no confirmed dedicated intake, marketing, or ops staff

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between Stage 3 and Stage 6 is not a talent gap — Alan has already built the credibility that most firms spend decades chasing. It is a systems gap, and closing it is exactly what puts a stable, transferable firm in Jason's hands instead of leaving growth to chance.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
McFall Legal
McFall Law, LLC is operating at Stage 3 — a Solo Practitioner model where Alan and Jason's genuine credibility (a 4.8-star reputation and more reviews than either same-town competitor) has never been matched by a system to generate leads independent of referrals. The single biggest gap limiting growth right now is local visibility infrastructure: no confirmed geo-targeted landing pages while a direct Bangor competitor has built exactly that, and a five-year-old rebrand three major directories still haven't caught up to. Until that infrastructure exists, McFall Legal will keep winning on reputation alone in a market where competitors are actively building the systems to out-position them.
