Here Is Where McFall Legal Stands Today

Alan McFall built McFall Law, LLC in 2021 after a partner's retirement ended his prior firm, McFall, Layman & Jordan, P.C. — and in 2023 his son Jason joined the practice, handling estate planning, probate, and real estate matters for clients in Bangor and Northampton County, PA. The firm holds real assets: a content-rich website, a 4.8-star reputation, and more reviews than its two closest same-town competitors combined — but three major legal directories still list the firm under its old pre-2021 name, and no system exists yet to turn that reputation into a predictable, referral-independent flow of new clients.

Stage 6 is the stage that turns McFall Law into a firm that runs itself — one Alan can eventually hand to Jason with real stability, not just a client list and a phone that only rings when someone remembers to refer a friend.

Where You Are Today

STAGE 3: SOLO PRACTITIONER
  • New client flow depends on referrals and reputation, not a built system
  • No confirmed paid advertising or geo-targeted landing pages driving inbound leads
  • Five years after the 2021 rebrand, Avvo, FindLaw, and Yellow Pages still list the firm under the retired legacy name "McFall, Layman & Jordan"
  • Two attorneys (Alan and Jason) with no confirmed dedicated intake, marketing, or ops staff

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between Stage 3 and Stage 6 is not a talent gap — Alan has already built the credibility that most firms spend decades chasing. It is a systems gap, and closing it is exactly what puts a stable, transferable firm in Jason's hands instead of leaving growth to chance.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

McFall Legal's only confirmed intake channel is a single consultation form on the website, with no visible process behind it once a prospect submits. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating. This is where the biggest immediate ROI lives.

Status: RED

  • With only Alan and Jason handling casework, new consultation requests likely have to compete with active case deadlines rather than flow to a dedicated intake process
  • No CRM or intake tracking system was identified — there is no confirmed way to know how many form submissions turn into signed clients

  • No defined follow-up sequence was identified for prospects who do not sign after their first consultation
The Gap
