The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Intake Assessment

For a firm whose entire value proposition is protecting clients from the risks they never saw coming, the intake process carries extra weight — a prospect who calls about an asset protection matter and reaches voicemail, or submits a form and waits days for a response, has already experienced the firm as unprepared. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • No support staff or intake team identified — every consultation request and incoming inquiry must be routed to Jedediah personally, creating a delay between interest and contact that costs conversions
  • No after-hours intake mechanism observed — the strategy session CTA on the homepage captures interest but no automated response, booking confirmation, or after-hours coverage exists to bridge the gap between submission and contact
  • No live chat or chat widget is present on the website — a prospect researching estate planning options at 10pm has no way to engage in real time, and the moment of intent passes without capture
  • No intake software, CRM, or follow-up sequence was observed — without a tracking system, leads that do not convert on the first call are likely lost entirely rather than nurtured to a close
Blind Spot: Business owners and high-net-worth individuals — the firm's primary target clients — are disproportionately likely to research attorneys outside of normal business hours. The firm's core audience often makes purchasing decisions in the evening or on weekends, when no intake coverage exists. Every night without after-hours follow-up is a night a competitor with faster response wins the consultation.
The Gap
  • At a $5,000 average engagement value, every two consultations lost to slow follow-up or after-hours gaps represents $10,000 in revenue that paid for the leads but failed to convert them — intake improvement has the highest immediate ROI of any investment the firm can make right now
  • A prospect who submits a strategy session request and does not hear back within the same business day is statistically more likely to fill out the next attorney's contact form — speed to first response is the intake metric that matters most in a consultative practice
  • Without a defined intake process, Jedediah cannot delegate the consultation-booking function even if he hires support staff — every team member hired needs a system to follow, not just a phone to answer
Jedediah's expertise is in making sure his clients never lose what they built — but if the intake process is losing clients before they ever become clients, that expertise never gets the chance to protect anyone.

Status: RED
The Problem

  • There is no accountability structure, no defined role separation, and no manager between the attorney and the day-to-day operations — meaning the firm cannot run for a single day without him present

  • Without a team, the firm has no succession plan — the same risk Jedediah helps his clients avoid (a business that cannot survive without its founder) is the exact risk present in the firm today
  • Competing firms like Foster & Buick (8 attorneys) and Klein Stoddard (5 attorneys) can handle volume, offer coverage, and serve clients across 8 counties simultaneously — a structural capacity advantage that a solo practitioner cannot match without building a team

Profit Plan Assessment

Supernus serves clients who need clear, structured financial plans to protect what they have built — but the firm's own financial picture remains unconfirmed, with no revenue data available from the discovery process and no visible profit tracking system in place. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No annual revenue data was confirmed — without a known revenue baseline, it is impossible to calculate profit margins, set growth targets, or make data-driven decisions about marketing investment and team expansion
  • Referral-dependent revenue means income fluctuates based on the unpredictable volume and timing of word-of-mouth introductions — not a managed, planned profit stream that can be projected or optimized
  • No visibility into cost per client acquisition or revenue by practice area — asset protection engagements, estate planning packages, and second-marriage planning work likely have different margins and time requirements that should inform where the firm invests its marketing and time
  • A solo practice with no financial tracking infrastructure cannot identify which engagements are most profitable, which clients are most valuable, or where overhead is consuming margin that should be kept
Blind Spot: The firm's highest-value practice area — asset protection for business owners — likely commands significantly higher engagement fees than standard estate planning work, but without tracking revenue by matter type, it is impossible to confirm whether marketing investment is being directed toward the most profitable work or simply the most common inquiries.
The Positive
  • As an attorney with a deep background in business strategy, entrepreneurship, and financial planning for clients, Jedediah has the intellectual framework to understand and implement a profit plan — this is not a knowledge gap but a prioritization gap
The Gap
  • Without knowing the current revenue baseline, any growth investment — in marketing, team, or systems — is made without a benchmark to measure return against; every dollar spent on growth should have a measurable revenue target attached
  • A referral-only revenue model cannot be forecasted, budgeted, or scaled — the transition from unpredictable word-of-mouth income to planned, marketing-driven revenue is the single most important financial structural change the firm can make
  • Without a profit plan, adding clients and revenue through paid marketing could increase gross revenue while actually compressing net margins — knowing the numbers before scaling is what separates a profitable growth story from a busy but unprofitable one
The irony is that Jedediah's clients hire him specifically because he gives their finances structure, protection, and a plan — and the firm he has built deserves that same clarity applied to its own numbers.
