Here Is Where Conley Law Office Stands Today

Ginny Conley has built Conley Law Office into a respected solo practice in Parkersburg, WV, handling family law, criminal defense, and personal injury matters — backed by 12 years as a prosecuting attorney and a stated philosophy of always sharing the truth with clients. The firm is now expanding into North Carolina, a market with stricter trust-account regulations, at the same time its own bookkeeping still runs on a manual QuickBooks workaround because its practice management platform was never built for trust accounting. The single most important finding: Conley Law's growth ambitions — including a stated 5-year plan to position the firm for a sale — are running ahead of the operational and financial systems needed to support them.

Stage 6 is where a firm becomes truly sellable — running on systems and structure instead of Ginny's day-to-day involvement — and that is exactly the value she needs to build before any sale.

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you

Closing the gap to Stage 6 starts with the same discipline Ginny is already bringing to her finances — extending that same structure to lead generation, intake, and team so the firm's value keeps growing toward a sale, not just its revenue.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
Conley Law Office
Conley Law Office is operating at Stage 3 — a respected solo practice whose credibility, built on 12 years as a prosecuting attorney and a multi-practice-area book of business, outpaces the systems behind it. The most urgent limiting factor is financial and operational visibility: without a clean trust ledger and predictable bookkeeping, it is difficult to know what the firm is actually worth or how to grow that value ahead of a sale. Layered on top of that is a lead generation and intake system built almost entirely on reputation and directory presence, with no paid or trackable channel driving new business — a gap that matters even more as Ginny works toward a 5-year exit.
