Lead Generation

Conley Law Office relies almost entirely on its website, directory listings, and word-of-mouth reputation to generate new clients — no paid Google Ads, Local Service Ads, or Meta Ads activity was found for any of its three practice areas. That leaves a 12-year prosecutor turned family law, criminal defense, and personal injury attorney competing for visibility against firms that are already spending real budget on paid channels.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Contact form is buried on a separate Contact Us page — not visible on the homepage
  • PageSpeed scores: Mobile UNABLE TO OBTAIN — Desktop UNABLE TO OBTAIN (Google PageSpeed Insights API quota was exhausted across repeated attempts; verify actual scores manually before the proposal call)
  • No blog and no video content anywhere on the site — competitors building trust through content are capturing prospects earlier in their search
  • Site is structured entirely around practice areas, with no geo-targeted pages for surrounding markets or the planned North Carolina expansion
Blind Spot: FindLaw lists a different office address ("610 Market Street, Suite 3") than the firm's own website and BBB listing ("1130 Market Street") — a NAP inconsistency that can quietly suppress local search rankings without ever showing up as an obvious problem.
The Positive
  • Mobile responsive design with a confirmed working viewport, and a dedicated attorney bio page highlighting 12 years as a prosecuting attorney
The Gap
  • Every visitor who can't find the contact form quickly on mobile is a lead lost to a competitor's site, not a lead lost to "no interest"
  • Without working PageSpeed data, it is impossible to know how many visitors are abandoning the site before it even loads
  • The NAP inconsistency may already be quietly costing local search visibility that has never been diagnosed
A website that quietly loses leads is a website that quietly caps what this firm is worth at sale — and neither can be fixed without first measuring it.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • GBP star rating shows 3.7 (per third-party aggregator) — Conley's own live Google review count could not be verified, while competitors like Klie Law Offices (4.5–4.8) and Summers & Associates (3.9 / 210 reviews) show far larger, more current review bases
  • NAP inconsistency: FindLaw lists "610 Market Street, Suite 3" while the website and BBB list "1130 Market Street, Parkersburg, WV 26101"
  • Several directory listings (Avvo, Yelp) could not be confirmed as claimed or complete, and Hub.biz shows an entirely unclaimed listing inviting anyone to claim it
Blind Spot: An unclaimed Hub.biz listing means a third party — or a competitor — could claim it first and control what shows up under Conley Law's name.
The Positive
  • BBB shows an A+ rating, and Martindale-Hubbell rates the firm "Distinguished" with a 4.5/5.0 peer rating
The Gap
  • Competitors with stronger, more current review volume are more likely to win the local pack for the same divorce, custody, and criminal defense searches Conley Law needs
  • An unclaimed or inconsistent directory presence makes it harder for prospective clients — and a future buyer's due diligence — to trust what they find online
Klie Law Offices — multi-office presence with dedicated location pages across four cities, directly relevant given Conley Law's own planned expansion into North Carolina.
Fixing local SEO now builds the kind of visible, verifiable reputation that supports both new clients today and a stronger valuation at sale.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • This firm does not appear to be running Google search ads for divorce, custody, or family law terms in Parkersburg — automated tools could not confirm live SERP ad slots, so this should be read as an opportunity gap, not a confirmed absence
  • The same applies to criminal defense and personal injury search terms — no ad presence was observed across any of the firm's three practice areas
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Summers & Associates — most likely paid-search spender in this market given multi-office scale and review volume, though no specific ad copy was captured this pass
  • Klie Law Offices — likely paid-search spender given its multi-office footprint; no specific ad copy was captured this pass, recommend a live SERP check before the proposal call
The Opportunity
  • "divorce lawyer Parkersburg WV" and "family law attorney Parkersburg WV" — direct, high-intent keywords the firm is not currently bidding on
  • "criminal defense attorney Parkersburg WV" and "DUI lawyer Parkersburg WV" — the same gap in a second practice area
  • Competitor-name bidding — capturing searches for "Klie Law Offices" or "Summers & Associates" from prospects who are comparison-shopping
  • Neighboring-market terms tied to the North Carolina expansion, so the firm can start building visibility there before it opens
Blind Spot: Because no ad presence was found for any of the three practice areas, competitors bidding on "Conley Law Office" or "Ginny Conley" by name could be capturing prospects who are already searching for this firm specifically.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • No Local Service Ads or Google Screened badge presence was found for Conley Law Office — LSA rendering requires a live, interactive SERP session that available research tools could not perform, so this is an opportunity gap, not a confirmed absence
  • Recommend a manual LSA check across all three practice areas before the proposal call
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Not confirmed live this pass for any named competitor — flagged for manual check
  • Klie Law Offices and Summers & Associates are the most likely LSA holders given their review volume, but this needs to be confirmed directly on a live SERP
The Opportunity
  • Google Screened status would pair well with Ginny's genuine credibility — 12 years as a prosecuting attorney — to build trust before the first call
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • With no verified Google review count yet, LSA's pay-per-lead model is a lower-risk way to test paid visibility while the review base is being built up
Blind Spot: Google Screened badges are becoming a trust signal prospects specifically look for before calling — competitors who earn it first may capture leads even without a stronger review base.
